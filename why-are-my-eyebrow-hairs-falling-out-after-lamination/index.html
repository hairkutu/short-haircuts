Why Are My Eyebrow Hairs Falling Out After Lamination

Written by Bardi Jul 17, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you recently got your eyebrows laminated and noticed that your eyebrow hairs are falling out, you're not alone. Many people experience this issue after getting their eyebrows laminated. In this article, we'll explore why this happens and provide some tips on how to prevent it.

Description

Eyebrow lamination is a beauty treatment that has gained popularity in recent years. It involves applying a chemical solution to the eyebrows to straighten and set the hairs in place. The result is a fuller, more defined brow that can last for up to two months.

While eyebrow lamination can be a great way to achieve your desired look, it can also lead to some unwanted side effects. One of the most common issues is eyebrow hair loss, which can be alarming for those who have invested time and money into the treatment.

How to Prevent Eyebrow Hair Loss After Lamination

If you're concerned about eyebrow hair loss after lamination, there are a few things you can do to prevent it:

Choose a Professional Technician

The first step is to choose a reputable and experienced eyebrow lamination technician. A skilled technician will use high-quality products and techniques that minimize the risk of hair loss.

Do Your Research

Before booking your appointment, do your research on the technician and the salon. Look for reviews and testimonials from other clients to get an idea of their experience and the quality of their work.

Avoid Overprocessing

Overprocessing is one of the leading causes of eyebrow hair loss after lamination. If the chemical solution is left on for too long or applied too frequently, it can weaken and damage the hair follicles, leading to hair loss. Make sure to follow the technician's instructions carefully and avoid overprocessing.

Use a Gentle Cleanser

After getting your eyebrows laminated, it's important to use a gentle cleanser to avoid irritating the hair follicles. Avoid using harsh chemicals and scrubbing too hard, as this can lead to further hair loss.

Step by Step Guide to Eyebrow Lamination

If you're considering getting your eyebrows laminated, here's a step-by-step guide to the process:

Step 1: Cleanse the Brows

The first step is to cleanse the eyebrows to remove any dirt, oil, or makeup. This ensures that the chemical solution can penetrate the hair shaft and provide maximum results.

Step 2: Apply the Chemical Solution

The technician will apply a chemical solution to the eyebrows to soften and straighten the hairs. The solution is left on for a few minutes before being removed with a damp cloth.

Step 3: Set the Hairs in Place

After the chemical solution is removed, the technician will use a spoolie brush to comb the hairs into place. A setting solution is then applied to keep the hairs in the desired position.

Step 4: Neutralize the Chemicals

The final step is to neutralize the chemicals with a neutralizing solution. This ensures that the hairs do not continue to over-process and become damaged.

Tips for Caring for Your Laminated Eyebrows

To keep your laminated eyebrows looking their best and prevent hair loss, follow these tips:

Avoid Touching Your Brows

Avoid touching or rubbing your eyebrows as much as possible, as this can cause the hairs to fall out.

Avoid Getting Them Wet

Avoid getting your laminated eyebrows wet for the first 24-48 hours after the treatment. This allows the solution to fully set and ensures the best results.

Avoid Oily Products

Avoid using oily products on your eyebrows, as this can cause the hairs to become greasy and weigh them down.

Avoid Heat and Sun Exposure

Avoid exposing your laminated eyebrows to heat and sun as much as possible. This can cause the hairs to become dry and brittle, leading to hair loss.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is eyebrow lamination safe?

Yes, eyebrow lamination is generally safe when done by a professional technician. However, as with any beauty treatment, there is a risk of side effects, such as hair loss.

How long does eyebrow lamination last?

Eyebrow lamination can last for up to two months, depending on how well you care for your eyebrows and how quickly your hair grows.

What should I do if my eyebrow hairs are falling out after lamination?

If you're experiencing hair loss after eyebrow lamination, it's best to consult with your technician or a dermatologist. They may be able to recommend treatments or products to help promote hair growth and prevent further loss.

Hair Care for Laminated Eyebrows

Hair care is an essential part of maintaining healthy and full eyebrows. Here are some tips for caring for your laminated eyebrows:

Brush Your Brows Regularly

Use a spoolie brush to comb your eyebrows regularly, as this helps to distribute natural oils and stimulate hair growth.

Apply Castor Oil

Castor oil is a natural remedy that can help promote hair growth and thickness. Apply a small amount to your eyebrows before bed and leave it on overnight.

Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a healthy and balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help promote hair growth and health.

Overall, eyebrow lamination can be a great way to achieve fuller, more defined eyebrows. However, it's important to be aware of the potential side effects, such as hair loss, and take steps to prevent them. By choosing a reputable technician, following aftercare instructions, and caring for your eyebrows properly, you can enjoy the benefits of eyebrow lamination without any negative effects.
