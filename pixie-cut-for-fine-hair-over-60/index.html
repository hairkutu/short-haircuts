Pixie Cut For Fine Hair Over 60 A Guide To Flattering Short Haircuts

Written by Oliver Aug 09, 2023 Â· 13 min read

Are you over 60 with fine hair and looking for a stylish and low-maintenance haircut? A pixie cut may be the perfect solution for you! This short and chic hairstyle can make you look younger and more vibrant, while also enhancing your natural features and texture. In this guide, we'll show you how to choose the right pixie cut for your face shape, hair type, and personal style, as well as how to care for and style your new 'do. Let's get started!

How to Choose the Right Pixie Cut for Fine Hair Over 60

Before you book a salon appointment or grab the scissors, take some time to consider which type of pixie cut will suit your face shape, hair texture, and personal preferences. Here are some factors to keep in mind:

Face Shape

The shape of your face can influence which pixie cut will flatter your features and balance your proportions. Here are some general guidelines:

  • If you have a round face, opt for a pixie cut with volume on top and tapered sides to elongate your face and create angles.
  • If you have a square face, go for a layered and textured pixie cut with soft edges to soften your jawline and add movement.
  • If you have an oval face, you're lucky! You can pull off almost any pixie cut, from a sleek and polished style to a tousled and messy one.
  • If you have a heart-shaped face, try a pixie cut with side-swept bangs and a tapered nape to balance your forehead and chin.

Hair Texture

The thickness, density, and curl pattern of your hair can also influence which pixie cut will work best for you. Here are some tips:

  • If you have fine hair, choose a pixie cut with layers and choppy ends to create the illusion of volume and texture.
  • If you have thinning hair, opt for a pixie cut with a longer top and shorter sides to cover any bald spots and add fullness.
  • If you have curly hair, embrace your natural texture and choose a pixie cut with layers and definition to enhance your curls.
  • If you have straight hair, play with asymmetry and angles to add interest and dimension to your pixie cut.

Personal Style

Finally, think about your lifestyle, fashion sense, and personality when choosing a pixie cut. Do you want a classic and elegant look, or a bold and edgy one? Do you prefer low-maintenance or high-maintenance hairstyles? Do you need to consider any dress codes or occasions? Here are some ideas:

  • If you're a fan of the classic Audrey Hepburn style, try a sleek and tapered pixie cut with long bangs and a deep side part.
  • If you're a rebel at heart, go for a spiky and textured pixie cut with a shaved side or back.
  • If you're a bohemian spirit, opt for a messy and tousled pixie cut with a tousled fringe and natural waves.
  • If you're a busy and practical person, choose a low-maintenance pixie cut with a simple shape and easy styling options.

Once you've decided on your ideal pixie cut, it's time to find a hair stylist who specializes in short haircuts and can bring your vision to life. You can ask for recommendations from friends, read online reviews, or check out social media profiles of local salons and stylists.

Step-by-Step Guide to Getting a Pixie Cut

Now that you've found a hairstylist you trust and have a clear idea of what you want, it's time to prepare for your pixie cut appointment. Here's what to expect:

  • Wash your hair the day before your appointment, but don't apply any styling products or oils.
  • Bring a few pictures of your desired pixie cut, from different angles and with different hair colors if applicable.
  • Wear comfortable and loose-fitting clothes that are easy to change in and out of.
  • Communicate with your hairstylist about your hair type, face shape, and personal preferences, and ask for their advice and feedback.
  • Trust the process and be open to minor adjustments and tweaks during the cutting and styling process.
  • Enjoy your new and improved pixie cut and don't forget to take selfies!

Styling Tips for Pixie Cut for Fine Hair Over 60

One of the benefits of a pixie cut is that it requires minimal styling time and effort, but you still have some options to customize your look and enhance your natural beauty. Here are some ideas:

  • If you want a sleek and polished look, apply a small amount of smoothing serum or wax on your hair and use a flat iron or a round brush to smooth the ends and create shine.
  • If you want a textured and messy look, use a texturizing spray or a dry shampoo to add volume and grip to your hair, and use your fingers to tousle and separate the strands.
  • If you want a voluminous and bouncy look, use a volumizing mousse or a root-lifting spray on your damp hair, and blow-dry it with a round brush or a diffuser attachment.
  • If you want a playful and romantic look, add some accessories such as hair pins, ribbons, or headbands to your pixie cut, and experiment with different parting and styling techniques.

Hair Care Tips for Pixie Cut for Fine Hair Over 60

Even though a pixie cut is low-maintenance, you still need to take care of your hair to keep it healthy, shiny, and vibrant. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days with a mild shampoo and conditioner that suit your hair type and texture.
  • Avoid using hot tools such as flat irons, curling irons, or hot rollers too often, as they can damage your hair and make it brittle.
  • Protect your hair from sun, wind, and pollution by wearing a hat, a scarf, or a hair serum with UV filters.
  • Trim your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and avoid split ends.
  • Experiment with different hair products such as serums, oils, masks, or leave-in conditioners to find the ones that work best for your hair.

Frequently Asked Questions About Pixie Cut for Fine Hair Over 60

Q: Will a pixie cut make me look older or younger?

A: A well-executed pixie cut can actually make you look younger and more vibrant, as it can highlight your cheekbones, eyes, and jawline, and add texture and volume to your hair. However, it's important to choose a style that suits your face shape, hair texture, and personal style, and to maintain it properly with regular trims and hair care.

Q: Can I dye my hair after getting a pixie cut?

A: Yes, you can dye your hair after getting a pixie cut, but you should wait at least a week or two to let your hair and scalp recover from the cutting process. Also, keep in mind that short hair tends to fade faster than long hair, so you may need to touch up your color more often or choose a low-maintenance shade.

Q: Can I grow out my pixie cut if I change my mind?

A: Yes, you can grow out your pixie cut if you decide you don't like it or want to try a different style. However, it may take several months or even a year to reach your desired length, and you'll need to go through some awkward stages of uneven hair and awkward layers. To make the transition smoother, you can ask your hairstylist for tips on how to style your growing hair, use hair accessories such as headbands and clips, and trim the sides and back regularly to maintain a neat shape.
