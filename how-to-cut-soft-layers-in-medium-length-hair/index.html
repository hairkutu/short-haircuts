How To Cut Soft Layers In Medium Length Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Sep 02, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Medium length hair is a versatile length that can be styled in a variety of ways. One popular style is soft layers, which add movement and texture to the hair. If you're looking to cut soft layers in your medium length hair, this step-by-step guide will help you achieve a beautiful, natural-looking result.

Description

Soft layers are typically cut into the hair using scissors, and are designed to create a natural-looking, textured effect. Unlike blunt cuts, which can look harsh and angular, soft layers create a more relaxed and effortless vibe. Soft layers can be cut into any length of hair, but they work particularly well in medium length hair, which falls between the shoulders and the collarbone.

How to Cut Soft Layers in Medium Length Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to gather the right tools. You'll need a good pair of hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to hold sections of hair in place. Once you have your tools, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will help you see the natural texture and movement of your hair, which will make it easier to cut soft layers.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, divide your hair into four sections: two at the front and two at the back. Clip each section in place.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Starting with the back sections, take a small section of hair and comb it straight. Hold the hair between your middle and index fingers, and use your scissors to cut into the hair at a slight angle. This will create a soft, textured effect. Repeat this process on each section of hair, moving from the back to the front of your head.

Step 4: Check Your Work

Once you've cut all of your hair into soft layers, check your work in the mirror. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure that your layers are even and look natural.

Tips for Cutting Soft Layers in Medium Length Hair

Cutting soft layers can be tricky, so it's important to follow these tips to achieve the best results:

  • Start with small sections of hair, and work your way up to larger sections as you get more comfortable.
  • Cut into the hair at a slight angle, rather than straight across, to create a natural-looking effect.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, as this can lead to uneven layers.
  • If you're unsure about how much hair to cut, start with a small amount and gradually cut more as needed.

FAQ

What is the difference between soft layers and blunt cuts?

Soft layers are cut into the hair at an angle, which creates a natural-looking, textured effect. Blunt cuts, on the other hand, are cut straight across the hair, which can look harsh and angular.

Can I cut soft layers into my own hair?

While it's possible to cut soft layers into your own hair, it can be tricky unless you have experience. We recommend visiting a professional stylist for the best results.

Hair Care for Soft Layers in Medium Length Hair

After cutting soft layers into your hair, it's important to care for your hair properly to maintain the look. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using too much heat when styling your hair. Consider using a texturizing spray to enhance the natural movement and texture of your hair.

Cutting soft layers in medium length hair is a great way to add movement and texture to your hair. With the right tools, techniques, and care, you can achieve a beautiful, natural-looking result that will make you feel confident and stylish.
