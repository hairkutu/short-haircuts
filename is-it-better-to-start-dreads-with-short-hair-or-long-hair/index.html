Description

Dreadlocks or dreads are a popular hairstyle among people of all ages and cultures. They are low maintenance and give a unique look to the hair. However, one of the biggest questions that people often ask is whether it is better to start dreads with short hair or long hair.

There is no straightforward answer to this question. It depends on various factors such as the type of hair, the desired look, and the amount of time and effort one is willing to put into maintaining the dreads.

How to Start Dreads with Short Hair or Long Hair

The process of starting dreads is the same for both short hair and long hair. However, the length of the hair affects the outcome of the dreads. Short hair creates thinner dreads, while long hair creates thicker dreads.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to start dreads with short hair or long hair:

  1. Cleanse your hair thoroughly with a residue-free shampoo to remove any dirt, oil or hair products.
  2. Section your hair using a comb or your fingers. The size of the sections will determine the size of the dreads.
  3. Apply a dreadlock wax or gel to each section of hair. This will help the hair lock together and form dreads.
  4. Twist each section of hair in a clockwise motion until it starts to coil and form a dread. Use a rubber band or hair tie to secure the end of each dread.
  5. Repeat the twisting process for each section of hair until all sections have formed dreads.
  6. Let the dreads dry completely before removing the rubber bands or hair ties. The dreads will tighten and lock together as they dry.
  7. Use a dreadlock wax or gel to maintain the dreads and prevent them from unraveling.
  8. Start a regular maintenance routine for your dreads, including washing, conditioning, and re-twisting to maintain their shape and prevent them from becoming too loose or too tight.

Tips for Starting Dreads with Short Hair or Long Hair

Here are some tips for starting dreads with short hair or long hair:

  • Short hair produces thinner dreads, while long hair produces thicker dreads. Keep this in mind when deciding on the size of the sections.
  • Use a dreadlock wax or gel to help the hair lock together and form dreads.
  • Be patient. It takes time for dreads to form and mature. Don't expect perfect dreads overnight.
  • Maintain your dreads regularly to prevent them from becoming too loose or too tight.

FAQs

Q: Can I start dreads with relaxed hair?

A: Yes, but it may take longer for the hair to form dreads because relaxed hair is more straight and less textured than natural hair.

Q: Can I start dreads with dyed hair?

A: Yes, but it is best to wait a few weeks after dyeing your hair before starting dreads to give your hair time to recover.

Q: How often should I wash my dreads?

A: It is recommended to wash your dreads every 1-2 weeks to prevent buildup and keep them clean.

Hair Care for Dreads

Once you have started your dreads, it is important to take proper care of them to keep them healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Condition your dreads with a leave-in conditioner to keep them moisturized.
  • Avoid using heavy hair products that can cause buildup and make your dreads feel heavy.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent your dreads from drying out and breaking while you sleep.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your dreads, as they can cause damage.

Overall, whether you start dreads with short hair or long hair, the key to achieving great dreads is patience, proper maintenance, and care.
