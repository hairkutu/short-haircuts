Why Do I Have A Circle Bald Spot On My Head

Written by Eveline Mar 16, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you experiencing hair loss in a circular pattern on your scalp? This condition is known as alopecia areata, and it affects millions of people worldwide. Alopecia areata is an autoimmune disorder that causes the immune system to attack hair follicles, leading to hair loss. In this blog post, we'll discuss why you might have a circle bald spot on your head, how to manage it, and some tips for caring for your hair.

Description

Alopecia areata is a common hair loss condition that typically starts as small, circular bald patches on the scalp. The bald spots can appear anywhere on the scalp and can be the size of a coin or larger. The condition can occur in anyone, regardless of age or gender, but it is most commonly diagnosed in children and young adults.

How to Diagnose Alopecia Areata

If you notice a circle bald spot on your head or any other areas of hair loss, it is essential to visit a dermatologist or hair specialist. They will examine your scalp and may perform a scalp biopsy, blood test, or other diagnostic tests to confirm the diagnosis of alopecia areata.

Step by Step Guide to Manage Alopecia Areata

Step 1: Seek Medical Advice

If you suspect that you have alopecia areata, it is crucial to see a dermatologist or hair specialist. They can confirm the diagnosis and recommend the best course of treatment.

Step 2: Use Topical Treatments

Topical treatments such as corticosteroids, anthralin, and minoxidil can help promote hair regrowth in the affected areas. These treatments are applied directly to the scalp and can help stimulate hair growth in the bald patches.

Step 3: Try Injection Therapy

If topical treatments are not effective, your doctor may recommend injection therapy. This involves injecting corticosteroids directly into the affected area to help promote hair growth.

Step 4: Consider Oral Medications

If injection therapy is not effective, your doctor may prescribe oral medications such as methotrexate, cyclosporine, or biologics to help manage the condition.

Tips for Caring for Your Hair

If you have alopecia areata, it is essential to care for your hair properly. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid harsh chemicals and treatments that can damage your hair
  • Choose gentle hair care products that won't irritate your scalp
  • Use a soft-bristled brush to prevent further hair loss
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair and cause further damage

FAQs

Q: Will my hair grow back?

A: In most cases, hair will regrow in the affected areas within a year. However, it is possible for the condition to recur, and hair loss may be permanent in some cases.

Q: Is alopecia areata contagious?

A: No, alopecia areata is not contagious. It is an autoimmune disorder that is caused by a malfunctioning immune system.

Q: Can stress cause alopecia areata?

A: Stress can trigger alopecia areata in some people, but it is not the primary cause of the condition.

Conclusion

If you have a circle bald spot on your head, it is essential to seek medical advice to diagnose alopecia areata. With proper treatment and hair care, you can manage the condition and promote hair regrowth in the affected areas. Remember to be gentle with your hair and avoid harsh treatments or products that can further damage your hair.
