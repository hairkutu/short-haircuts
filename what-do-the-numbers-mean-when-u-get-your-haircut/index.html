What Do The Numbers Mean When You Get Your Haircut

Written by Oliver Jul 13, 2023 · 6 min read

Have you ever been to a barbershop or hair salon and heard the stylist ask you what number you want for your haircut? If you’re not familiar with the numbers, it can be confusing. In this article, we will explain what the numbers mean and how they affect your haircut.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How To
  • Step By Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

The numbers in a haircut refer to the length of the hair that will be left after cutting. The numbers usually range from 0 to 8, with 0 being the shortest and 8 being the longest. The number system is used on clippers and trimmers to ensure that the length of the hair is consistent throughout the haircut.

The numbers are also used for different types of haircuts. For example, a buzz cut usually requires a number 0 or 1, while a crew cut may require a number 2 or 3. The length of the hair can also be adjusted by using different clipper guards or by using scissors.

How To

To determine which number to use for your haircut, you should consider your hair type, face shape, and the style you want. If you’re not sure, you can ask your stylist for recommendations.

If you’re using clippers at home, start with a longer guard and work your way down. It’s always better to start longer and go shorter, as you can’t put hair back once it’s been cut.

Step By Step

1. Start with clean, dry hair

2. Choose the appropriate clipper guard or length

3. Begin at the sides and work your way up to the top

4. Use scissors to trim any longer hairs or to create texture

5. Finish with a trim around the neckline and ears

Tips

- Always use sharp clippers or scissors to ensure a clean cut

- Use a comb to guide the clippers and ensure an even length

- Don’t be afraid to ask your stylist for advice or recommendations

- If you’re doing it yourself, start with a longer guard and work your way down

- Use a mirror to check the back of your head and ensure a symmetrical cut

FAQ

Q: What’s the difference between a buzz cut and a crew cut?

A: A buzz cut is usually shorter and requires a number 0 or 1 guard, while a crew cut is slightly longer and may require a number 2 or 3 guard.

Q: Can I use the same number all over my head?

A: It depends on the style you want. If you want a more uniform look, you can use the same number all over. If you want a more textured or layered look, you may need to use different numbers or scissors to achieve the desired effect.

Hair Care

After getting a haircut, it’s important to take care of your hair to maintain the style and prevent damage. Here are some tips:

- Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner

- Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers or straighteners too often

- Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends

- Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes

- Protect your hair from the sun and wind with a hat or scarf

Now that you know what the numbers mean when you get a haircut, you can confidently choose the right length for your style. Whether you’re going for a buzz cut or a crew cut, the number system ensures that your haircut is consistent and tailored to your preferences.
