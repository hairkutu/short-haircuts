How Best To Sleep With Long Hair Tips For A Restful Night

Written by Luffy Jun 10, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have long hair, you know that sleeping can be a challenge. Tossing and turning can lead to tangled hair and a frizzy mess in the morning. But don't worry, we've got you covered! In this article, we'll discuss how best to sleep with long hair, from how to style it before bed to the best sleeping positions to avoid hair damage.

How to Prepare Your Hair Before Bed

One of the best ways to ensure that you wake up with healthy, tangle-free hair is to prepare it before bed. Here are a few tips to help you get started:

Brush Your Hair

Before hitting the sack, make sure to brush your hair thoroughly. This will help to remove any tangles and prevent knots from forming while you sleep.

Apply a Leave-in Conditioner

Applying a leave-in conditioner or hair oil can help to keep your hair soft and moisturized while you sleep. This will prevent it from becoming dry and brittle, which can lead to breakage and split ends.

Style Your Hair

There are many ways to style your hair before bed to prevent tangles and damage. Here are a few options to consider:

  • Braids: Braiding your hair before bed can help to prevent tangles and keep it in place while you sleep.
  • Bun: Tying your hair up in a loose bun can help to prevent damage and keep it from getting tangled.
  • Silk scarf: Covering your hair with a silk scarf can help to prevent frizz and breakage.

The Best Sleeping Positions for Long Hair

Your sleeping position can also have an impact on the health of your hair. Here are a few sleeping positions to consider:

Sleeping on Your Back

Sleeping on your back is one of the best positions for long hair. This allows your hair to flow freely and prevents it from getting tangled or damaged.

Sleeping on Your Side

If you prefer to sleep on your side, consider tying your hair up in a loose bun or braid to prevent it from getting tangled.

Sleeping on Your Stomach

Sleeping on your stomach can be damaging to your hair, as it can cause it to become tangled and matted. If you do sleep on your stomach, consider tying your hair up in a loose bun or braid to prevent damage.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair While You Sleep

In addition to preparing your hair before bed and choosing the best sleeping position, there are a few other tips to keep in mind to maintain healthy hair while you sleep.

Use a Silk Pillowcase

Using a silk pillowcase can help to prevent frizz and damage to your hair while you sleep. The smooth surface of the silk allows your hair to glide freely, preventing tangles and breakage.

Avoid Using Hair Ties

Hair ties can cause damage and breakage to your hair, especially if they're too tight. Instead, consider using a scrunchie or hair clip to tie your hair up before bed.

Wash Your Hair Regularly

Regular washing can help to keep your hair healthy and prevent tangles and breakage. Make sure to use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid harsh chemicals or heat styling tools.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I wash my hair?

It's best to wash your hair every two to three days to keep it healthy and prevent damage.

Can I sleep with wet hair?

It's best to avoid sleeping with wet hair, as it can cause tangles and breakage. Instead, try to allow your hair to air dry or use a low-heat setting on a hair dryer.

What should I do if my hair still gets tangled while I sleep?

If your hair still gets tangled while you sleep, try using a detangling spray or serum before brushing it out in the morning.

Hair Care for a Restful Night's Sleep

Sleeping with long hair can be a challenge, but with the right preparation and care, you can wake up with healthy, tangle-free locks. Remember to brush your hair before bed, apply a leave-in conditioner, and choose a comfortable sleeping position. Use a silk pillowcase, avoid using hair ties, and wash your hair regularly to keep it healthy and prevent damage. With these tips, you'll be able to enjoy a restful night's sleep and wake up with beautiful, healthy hair.
