How To Dye Black Male Hair Blonde A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia May 07, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you a black male who wants to change up his look and go blonde? Dyeing your hair can be a fun way to experiment with your style and express yourself. However, if you have dark hair, the process can be a bit tricky. In this guide, we’ll walk you through how to dye black male hair blonde, including tips and tricks to ensure a successful outcome.

Table of Contents

  • How to Dye Black Male Hair Blonde
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Dyeing Black Male Hair Blonde
  • FAQs

How to Dye Black Male Hair Blonde

Dyeing black hair blonde involves lifting the color from your hair shaft and then depositing the blonde color on top. This process can be damaging to your hair, so it’s important to take precautions and use high-quality products.

Before getting started, it’s important to gather all of the necessary materials:

  • Blonde hair dye
  • Bleach powder
  • Developer
  • Gloves
  • Plastic mixing bowl
  • Applicator brush
  • Old T-shirt or cape

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have your materials gathered, let’s get started with the dyeing process:

  1. Put on your gloves and old T-shirt or cape to protect your skin and clothing.
  2. Mix the bleach powder and developer in the plastic mixing bowl. Follow the instructions on the packaging for the correct proportions.
  3. Divide your hair into four sections using hair clips.
  4. Starting from the back, apply the bleach mixture to your hair using the applicator brush. Be sure to saturate your hair evenly.
  5. Once all sections are covered, wait for the bleach to develop. This can take anywhere from 20-45 minutes, depending on the color of your hair and the strength of the developer.
  6. Rinse out the bleach mixture thoroughly with cool water.
  7. Next, mix the blonde hair dye according to the instructions on the packaging.
  8. Apply the blonde hair dye to your hair, starting from the back and working your way forward.
  9. Once all sections are covered, wait for the dye to develop. This can take anywhere from 20-45 minutes, depending on the desired shade of blonde.
  10. Rinse out the dye thoroughly with cool water.

Tips for Dyeing Black Male Hair Blonde

Dyeing black male hair blonde can be a tricky process, so here are some tips to help you achieve the best results:

  • Use a high-quality bleach powder and developer to ensure even and thorough lightening.
  • Start with a lower volume developer and gradually increase if necessary to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Do a strand test before dyeing your entire head to ensure you like the color and your hair can handle the process.
  • Consider going to a professional stylist for your first time dyeing your hair to avoid any mishaps.
  • Use a toner after dyeing your hair to neutralize any unwanted brassy tones.

FAQs

Can I dye my hair blonde without bleaching it?

If you have dark black hair, it’s unlikely that you’ll be able to achieve a blonde shade without bleaching your hair first. However, if you have lighter black hair, you may be able to use a high-lift blonde hair dye without bleach.

How long will my blonde hair last?

Blonde hair dye typically lasts 4-6 weeks before fading. To prolong the life of your color, use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner and avoid excessive heat styling.

Can I dye my hair blonde if I have a relaxer?

Yes, you can dye your hair blonde if you have a relaxer. However, it’s important to wait at least two weeks after relaxing your hair before dyeing it to avoid damaging your hair.

Hair Care After Dyeing

Once you’ve successfully dyed your hair blonde, it’s important to take care of it properly to maintain the color and health of your hair:

  • Use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner to prevent fading.
  • Avoid excessive heat styling, as this can dry out and damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of your haircut.
  • Consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

With these tips and tricks, you’ll be able to successfully dye your black male hair blonde and rock a new, bold look.
