How Do You Identify Your Hair Type

Written by Robby Mar 30, 2023 · 7 min read

Knowing your hair type is essential in determining the right products and care routine for your hair. Identifying your hair type can be a challenge, especially if you are new to the natural hair community. In this guide, we will discuss how to identify your hair type and provide tips on how to care for it.

Table of Content

  • How to Identify Your Hair Type
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Hair Care
  • FAQ

How to Identify Your Hair Type

The first step in identifying your hair type is to understand the different hair textures. There are four main hair types, which are:

  • Type 1: Straight Hair
  • Type 2: Wavy Hair
  • Type 3: Curly Hair
  • Type 4: Coily Hair

Each hair type has a unique texture, curl pattern, and level of porosity. Understanding your hair type can help you choose the right products and styling techniques that work best for your hair.

Type 1: Straight Hair

Type 1 hair is straight and lacks any curl pattern. It is often shiny and smooth, and it's the easiest hair type to style. This hair type has the lowest porosity level, which means it doesn't absorb moisture quickly. It is also the most oil-prone hair type.

Type 2: Wavy Hair

Type 2 hair has a slight wave pattern, and it's often described as beachy waves. This hair type has more volume than straight hair and can hold curls well. It has a medium porosity level, which means it can absorb moisture well.

Type 3: Curly Hair

Type 3 hair has a more defined curl pattern and can range from loose to tight curls. It often has a lot of volume and can be prone to frizz. This hair type has a high porosity level, which means it absorbs moisture quickly but also loses moisture quickly.

Type 4: Coily Hair

Type 4 hair has a tight coil pattern and can range from fine to coarse. It often has a lot of shrinkage and can be prone to dryness. This hair type has the highest porosity level, which means it absorbs moisture quickly but also loses moisture quickly.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to identify your hair type:

  1. Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Avoid using any styling products to get an accurate reading of your hair's natural texture.
  3. Let your hair air dry without using any heat styling tools.
  4. Observe your hair's natural texture, curl pattern, and level of frizz.
  5. Compare your hair's texture to the hair types mentioned above to determine your hair type.

Tips for Hair Care

Once you have identified your hair type, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Use hair products that are specific to your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools frequently.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to maintain its moisture balance.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals like sulfates and parabens on your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about identifying hair type:

1. Can my hair type change over time?

Yes, your hair type can change over time due to factors like hormonal changes, age, and chemical treatments.

2. How often should I wash my hair?

It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. Generally, it's recommended to wash your hair every 2-3 days, but some people may need to wash their hair more frequently.

3. Can I have more than one hair type?

Yes, it's possible to have more than one hair type on your head. For example, you may have wavy hair in the front and curly hair in the back.

Identifying your hair type is the first step in taking care of your natural hair. By understanding your hair's texture, curl pattern, and level of porosity, you can choose the right products and styling techniques that work best for your hair. Remember to take care of your hair properly by using gentle products, deep conditioning regularly, and protecting it from the sun and wind.
