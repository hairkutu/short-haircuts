What To Do For Oily Thin Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Petter Apr 22, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Oily thin hair can be a frustrating problem, leaving your hair looking limp and lifeless. But don't worry, there are plenty of things you can do to help combat oily hair and add volume to your locks. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about what to do for oily thin hair, including how to care for your hair, step-by-step instructions, and frequently asked questions. So let's get started!

Table of Contents

  1. Hair Care
  2. How To: Step by Step
  3. Tips and Tricks
  4. Frequently Asked Questions

Hair Care

The first step in dealing with oily thin hair is to make sure you're taking care of your hair properly. Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind:

Washing Your Hair

When it comes to washing your hair, less is more. Washing your hair too frequently can strip it of its natural oils, causing it to produce more oil to compensate. Try washing your hair every other day or every three days if possible. When you do wash your hair, use a gentle shampoo that's designed for oily hair, and focus on your scalp rather than your ends.

Conditioning Your Hair

When it comes to conditioning your hair, you'll want to avoid applying conditioner to your scalp. This can weigh down your hair and make it look even more oily. Instead, focus on applying conditioner to the mid-lengths and ends of your hair, where it's needed most.

Drying Your Hair

When it comes to drying your hair, avoid using high heat. High heat can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage. Instead, try using a cool or warm setting on your hair dryer, and use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair while it's still damp.

How To: Step by Step

Now that you know how to care for your hair, let's take a look at some steps you can take to help combat oily thin hair:

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

Make sure your hair is clean and dry before you begin. This will help ensure that any products you use will be effective.

Step 2: Apply Dry Shampoo

Dry shampoo is a great way to absorb excess oil and add volume to your hair. Apply dry shampoo to your roots, focusing on the areas that tend to get the most oily.

Step 3: Tease Your Hair

Teasing your hair can help add volume and lift to your roots. Use a teasing comb or a fine-tooth comb to gently backcomb your hair at the roots.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Finally, style your hair as desired. You can use a curling iron or straightener to add texture and definition, or simply leave your hair as is for a more natural look.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to help you deal with oily thin hair:

Use a Clarifying Shampoo

Using a clarifying shampoo once a week can help remove buildup from your hair and scalp, leaving your hair feeling clean and refreshed.

Avoid Touching Your Hair

Touching your hair can transfer oil from your hands to your hair, making it look even more oily. Try to avoid touching your hair as much as possible.

Avoid Heavy Products

Avoid using heavy products like mousses and gels, which can weigh down your hair and make it look even more oily.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about dealing with oily thin hair:

Is it okay to brush oily hair?

Yes, it's okay to brush oily hair. In fact, brushing your hair can help distribute the oil throughout your hair, making it look less oily.

How often should I wash my hair if it's oily?

It's generally recommended to wash oily hair every other day or every three days if possible.

Can I use a hair mask on oily hair?

Yes, you can use a hair mask on oily hair. Just be sure to apply the mask to the mid-lengths and ends of your hair, rather than your scalp.

Dealing with oily thin hair can be a challenge, but with these tips and tricks, you can help combat oily hair and add volume to your locks. Remember to take care of your hair properly, use the right products, and avoid heavy styling products. With a little bit of effort, you can have the beautiful, voluminous hair you've always wanted.
