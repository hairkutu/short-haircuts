Will Curls Suit Me Everything You Need To Know

Written by Robby Aug 22, 2023 Â· 10 min read

If you've ever considered getting curls, you might have wondered if they would suit your face shape, hair type, and personal style. It's a common concern, but the good news is that there's a curl for everyone. In this article, we'll answer the question "will curls suit me?" and provide tips on how to choose the right type of curl for you.

Description

Curls are a versatile hairstyle that can be worn by people of all ages and ethnicities. They can add volume, texture, and movement to any hair type, whether it's straight, wavy, or curly. However, not all curls are created equal, and what looks good on one person might not work for another. It's important to consider your face shape, hair type, and lifestyle when choosing a curl style.

How to Choose the Right Curl Style

Choosing the right curl style depends on several factors, including your face shape, hair type, and personal style. Here are some tips to help you choose the perfect curl:

Know Your Face Shape

The first step in choosing a curl style is to determine your face shape. There are six basic face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and oblong. Each face shape has its own unique characteristics, and certain curl styles can complement or accentuate these features.

Consider Your Hair Type

Another important factor to consider when choosing a curl style is your hair type. Curls can be achieved with a variety of tools, including curling irons, rollers, and braids. However, the type of curl you choose should be based on your hair's natural texture and thickness.

Think About Your Lifestyle

Your lifestyle also plays a role in choosing the right curl style. If you have an active lifestyle or work in a professional setting, you may prefer a low-maintenance curl that requires minimal styling. On the other hand, if you enjoy experimenting with different styles and have time to dedicate to your hair, you may opt for a more intricate curl style.

Step-by-Step Guide to Getting Curls

If you've decided that curls are right for you, here's a step-by-step guide to getting them:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Before you start curling your hair, you'll need to prepare it by washing and conditioning it. You may also want to apply a heat protectant to prevent damage from hot styling tools.

Step 2: Choose Your Curling Tool

Next, choose the curling tool that best suits your hair type and desired curl style. Curling irons, rollers, and braids are all popular methods for creating curls.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small sections, starting at the back of your head and working your way forward. This will help ensure that each section is curling evenly.

Step 4: Curl Your Hair

Starting at the roots, wrap each section of hair around the curling tool, holding it in place for a few seconds before releasing. Repeat this process for each section of hair, working your way around your head.

Step 5: Finish Your Curls

Once you've curled all of your hair, you can finish your curls with hairspray or other styling products. This will help hold your curls in place and prevent them from falling flat throughout the day.

Tips for Maintaining Your Curls

Now that you've got your curls, it's important to know how to maintain them. Here are some tips for keeping your curls looking their best:

Use a Wide-Toothed Comb

When combing your curls, use a wide-toothed comb to avoid pulling or tugging on your hair. This will help prevent breakage and maintain the shape of your curls.

Avoid Over-Washing

Washing your hair too frequently can strip it of its natural oils, leaving it dry and frizzy. Try to wash your hair every 2-3 days, or use dry shampoo in between washes to keep your curls looking fresh.

Protect Your Curls While Sleeping

Sleeping with your hair loose can cause it to become tangled and frizzy. To protect your curls while sleeping, try tying your hair up in a loose bun or braid before bed.

FAQs

Q: Will curls suit me if I have straight hair?

A: Yes, curls can be achieved on straight hair using a curling iron, rollers, or braids. However, it's important to consider your hair's natural texture and thickness when choosing a curl style.

Q: Will curls suit me if I have a round face?

A: Yes, curls can complement a round face by adding volume and texture to the hair. However, it's important to choose a curl style that elongates the face, such as loose waves or a side-swept fringe.

Q: Will curls suit me if I have fine hair?

A: Yes, curls can add volume and body to fine hair. However, it's important to use a curling tool that won't damage or weigh down your hair, such as a ceramic curling iron or foam rollers.

Hair Care for Curls

Caring for curly hair requires a different approach than caring for straight hair. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy, beautiful curls:

Moisturize Your Hair

Curly hair is prone to dryness, so it's important to keep it moisturized. Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to hydrate your curls and prevent frizz.

Avoid Heat Damage

Excessive heat can damage your hair and cause your curls to lose their shape. Try to limit your use of hot styling tools, and use a heat protectant when you do use them.

Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular trims can help keep your curls looking healthy and prevent split ends. Aim to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks, or as needed.

Use the Right Products

Choosing the right products for your hair type and curl style can make a big difference in how your curls look and feel. Look for products that are designed for curly hair, such as curl-enhancing shampoos and styling creams.

Now that you know the answer to "will curls suit me," it's time to try them out for yourself. With the right curl style and hair care routine, you can achieve beautiful, healthy curls that suit your face shape, hair type, and personal style.
