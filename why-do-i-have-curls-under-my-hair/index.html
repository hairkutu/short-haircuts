Why Do I Have Curls Under My Hair

Written by Christine Apr 08, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Have you ever noticed that underneath your straight hair, there are curls? It can be frustrating to deal with, especially if you want to maintain a sleek and straight hairstyle. In this article, we'll explore why you might have curls under your hair, how to deal with them, and some tips for caring for your hair.

Description

The curls that you might see under your hair are often referred to as "hidden curls" or "underneath curls." They are typically found in people with naturally curly or wavy hair, but they can also occur in people with straight hair who have undergone chemical treatments such as relaxers or keratin treatments.

Underneath curls are caused by a combination of factors, including genetics, hair texture, and environmental factors. While some people may have more prominent curls than others, everyone has some degree of curliness underneath their hair.

How to Identify Underneath Curls

If you're not sure if you have curls under your hair, there are a few ways to identify them. One way is to wash your hair and let it air dry without any styling products. You may notice that as your hair dries, curls or waves start to form underneath your straight hair.

Another way to identify your underneath curls is to use a hair straightener. After straightening your hair, run your fingers through your hair, and you may notice curls or waves underneath the straightened hair.

Step by Step: How to Deal with Underneath Curls

If you're looking to maintain a straight hairstyle, dealing with underneath curls can be a challenge. Here are some steps you can take to manage them:

Step 1: Use a Straightening Shampoo and Conditioner

Start by using a shampoo and conditioner that is designed to straighten hair. These products can help smooth out your hair and reduce the appearance of curls or waves.

Step 2: Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow-drying your hair can help straighten it out and reduce the appearance of curls. Use a round brush to help smooth out your hair as you blow-dry it.

Step 3: Use a Flat Iron

Using a flat iron can help further straighten out your hair. Take small sections of hair and run the flat iron over them to help smooth out any curls or waves.

Step 4: Finish with a Straightening Product

Finish off your hairstyle with a straightening product, such as a serum or cream. These products can help keep your hair straight and smooth throughout the day.

Tips for Caring for Your Hair

Whether you have curls or straight hair, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips for caring for your hair:

Tip 1: Use a Heat Protectant

When using heat styling tools, such as a flat iron or blow dryer, be sure to use a heat protectant to prevent damage to your hair.

Tip 2: Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular trims can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Tip 3: Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Using a deep conditioning treatment once a week can help keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

FAQs

Q: Can I permanently straighten my hair?

A: Yes, there are several permanent hair straightening treatments available, such as a Brazilian blowout or Japanese straightening. However, these treatments can be expensive and may require regular touch-ups.

Q: Will my hair grow back curly if I straighten it?

A: No, straightening your hair will not change its natural texture or curl pattern. However, if you have undergone chemical treatments such as relaxers, your hair may grow back with a different texture.

Hair Care: Final Thoughts

While dealing with underneath curls can be frustrating, it's important to remember that they are a natural part of your hair's texture. By using the right products and styling techniques, you can manage your hair and achieve the look you want.
