How To Thin Your Hair With Normal Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Jun 23, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Thinning your hair can be a great way to remove excess bulk and create a more manageable style. While many people opt to use thinning shears or visit a salon for this service, it is possible to thin your hair at home using normal scissors. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to thin your hair with normal scissors, as well as provide tips and advice for maintaining your new look.

How to Thin Your Hair with Normal Scissors

What You'll Need:

- Normal scissors
- A comb
- Hair ties or clips
- A mirror (preferably one with a magnified side)

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with your favorite shampoo and conditioner. Towel dry your hair until it is damp, but not dripping wet.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Using your comb, divide your hair into sections. Start by parting your hair down the middle and then dividing each side into three sections (top, middle, and bottom). Clip or tie each section out of the way, leaving the bottom section loose.

Step 3: Begin Thinning Your Hair

Using your normal scissors, begin thinning your hair from the bottom section. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Starting at the ends, make small snips along the length of the hair. Be careful not to cut too much at once, as you can always go back and thin more if needed.

Step 4: Blend Your Layers

Once you have thinned the bottom section, let down the middle section and repeat the process. Be sure to blend the layers together by snipping at an angle, rather than straight across. Continue thinning each section until you've achieved your desired look.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

As you work, use your mirror to check your progress from different angles. Pay attention to any uneven areas or spots that need more thinning.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Once you've finished thinning your hair, you can style it as desired. You may find that your hair is easier to manage and style with the excess bulk removed.

Tips for Thinning Your Hair with Normal Scissors

- Start by thinning a small section of hair to get a feel for the process before moving on to larger sections.
- Be patient and take your time. It's better to thin slowly and carefully than to rush and make a mistake.
- Use a sharp pair of scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
- Avoid thinning your hair too much, as this can lead to split ends and damage.
- If you're unsure about how much to thin, start with less and then thin more as needed.

FAQ About Thinning Hair with Normal Scissors

Can I use any kind of scissors to thin my hair?

It's best to use scissors specifically designed for cutting hair, as these are sharper and more precise. However, if you don't have hair scissors, you can use normal scissors as long as they are sharp and in good condition.

Can I thin my hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can thin curly hair. However, be careful not to thin too much, as this can disrupt the curl pattern and lead to frizz.

How often should I thin my hair?

This will depend on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Generally, it's recommended to thin your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain your style and remove excess bulk.

Hair Care After Thinning

After thinning your hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

- Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
- Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible
- Use a heat protectant spray if you must use heat styling tools
- Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends
- Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine with a hat or swim cap

By following these tips, you can keep your hair looking and feeling its best after thinning.

Thinning your hair with normal scissors can be a great way to achieve a more manageable style. By following these simple steps and tips, you can thin your hair at home and maintain a healthy, stylish look.
