How to Cut Women's Medium Short Hair

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's define what medium short hair is. Medium short hair is hair that falls between the chin and shoulder length. It's a versatile length that can be styled in many ways, from a sleek bob to messy waves.

Now, let's get into how to cut women's medium short hair:

1. Section the Hair

Start by sectioning the hair into four parts: the crown, left side, right side, and back. Use hair clips to hold each section in place.

2. Cut the Back

Starting at the nape of the neck, cut the hair in a straight line across. Use the length of the hair in the crown as a guide for the length in the back.

3. Cut the Sides

Starting at the front of the left side, hold the hair at a 45-degree angle and cut in a straight line towards the back. Repeat on the right side.

4. Cut the Crown

Starting at the front of the crown, hold the hair at a 45-degree angle and cut in a straight line towards the back. Use the length of the hair in the back as a guide for the length in the crown.

5. Blend the Layers

Using scissors or a razor, blend the layers together to create a seamless look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a more detailed step-by-step guide on how to cut women's medium short hair:

1. Wash the Hair

Wash the hair with shampoo and conditioner to remove any dirt, oil, or product buildup.

2. Section the Hair

Section the hair into four parts: the crown, left side, right side, and back. Use hair clips to hold each section in place.

3. Cut the Back

Starting at the nape of the neck, comb the hair straight down and cut in a straight line across. Use the length of the hair in the crown as a guide for the length in the back.

4. Cut the Sides

Starting at the front of the left side, hold the hair at a 45-degree angle and cut in a straight line towards the back. Repeat on the right side. Use the hair in the back as a guide for the length in the front.

5. Cut the Crown

Starting at the front of the crown, hold the hair at a 45-degree angle and cut in a straight line towards the back. Use the length of the hair in the back as a guide for the length in the crown.

6. Blend the Layers

Using scissors or a razor, blend the layers together to create a seamless look. Start at the back and work your way towards the front.

7. Texturize the Hair

Use scissors or a razor to add texture to the hair. This will help create movement and volume.

8. Style the Hair

Style the hair as desired using a blow dryer, straightener, or curling iron.

Tips for Cutting Women's Medium Short Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting women's medium short hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up.
  • Check the length of the hair frequently to ensure symmetry.
  • Blend the layers well to avoid a choppy look.
  • Use a razor to add texture to the hair.

FAQs

1. Can I cut my own medium short hair?

While it's possible to cut your own hair, we recommend going to a professional hairdresser to ensure a clean and even cut.

2. How often should I cut my medium short hair?

We recommend getting a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and health of your hair.

3. What kind of scissors should I use?

Use sharp, professional-grade scissors for the best results. Avoid using regular household scissors, as they can damage the hair.

Hair Care Advice

Here are some tips to keep your medium short hair healthy and shiny:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage the hair.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish and strengthen the hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray.
