How To Cut Men s Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel May 18, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune on haircuts for men? Learning how to cut men's hair can save you a lot of money in the long run. In this article, we'll go over the basics of how to cut men's hair, including the tools you'll need, the different types of haircuts, and some tips for making the process as smooth as possible.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Different Types of Haircuts
  • How to Cut Men's Hair: Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Men's Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting men's hair, it's important to have the right tools on hand. Here's what you'll need:

  • Clippers
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Cape or towel
  • Haircutting cape or smock
  • Hair products (optional)

It's important to invest in quality tools, as they will make the process much easier and produce better results.

Different Types of Haircuts

There are many different types of haircuts for men, from short buzz cuts to longer layered styles. Here are a few of the most popular:

  • Buzz Cut: A very short, all-over cut.
  • Crew Cut: Short on the sides and back, slightly longer on top.
  • Fade: Gradually fades from short to longer hair.
  • Undercut: Short on the sides and longer on top, with a sharp contrast between the two.
  • Layered Cut: Longer hair on top, layered to create volume and texture.

How to Cut Men's Hair: Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have your tools and know what type of haircut you want to give, it's time to get started. Here's a step-by-step guide:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Wet hair can be more difficult to cut accurately.
  2. Put the haircutting cape or smock on the client to protect their clothing.
  3. Use the clippers to cut the sides and back of the hair, using a guard to achieve the desired length.
  4. Blend the sides and top by using different guard sizes to fade the hair gradually.
  5. Use the scissors to cut the top of the hair, using the fingers to measure the desired length.
  6. Blend the top with the sides and back, using the scissors to remove any excess hair.
  7. Cut the neckline and sideburns to create a clean, polished look.
  8. Style the hair using products if desired.
  9. Remove the haircutting cape or smock and clean up any stray hairs from the client's neck and shoulders.
  10. Admire your handiwork!

Tips for Cutting Men's Hair

Here are a few tips to make the process of cutting men's hair easier and more successful:

  • Start with a longer guard size and work your way down, rather than starting short and trying to fix mistakes.
  • Use a comb to lift the hair and cut it at an angle, creating a natural-looking shape.
  • Take breaks to step back and assess your progress, rather than getting too focused on one area.
  • Be patient and take your time, especially if you're new to hair cutting.

FAQs

Here are a few common questions about cutting men's hair:

Do I need to go to cosmetology school to cut men's hair?

No, you don't need to have a cosmetology license to cut men's hair. However, it's important to do your research and practice before cutting someone's hair.

How often should men get their hair cut?

It depends on the person and their hair type, but every 4-6 weeks is a good rule of thumb for maintaining a haircut.

What's the best way to learn how to cut men's hair?

Practice, practice, practice! Start by practicing on friends and family members, and watch tutorials online to learn different techniques.

Hair Care Tips

Finally, here are a few tips for keeping men's hair healthy and looking great:

  • Wash hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Use conditioner to keep hair hydrated and soft.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and straighteners too often, as they can damage hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain a healthy cut.

Learning how to cut men's hair can be a fun and rewarding skill to have. With the right tools, techniques, and practice, you can give great haircuts from the comfort of your own home.
