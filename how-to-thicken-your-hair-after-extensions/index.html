How To Thicken Your Hair After Extensions

Written by Daniel Jun 04, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you struggling with thinning hair after removing your hair extensions? You're not alone. Hair extensions can cause damage to your natural hair, leading to thinning and breakage. But don't worry, there are ways to thicken your hair after extensions. In this blog post, we'll go over some tips and tricks on how to achieve thicker, healthier hair.

  1. Hair Care
  2. How To Thicken Your Hair
  3. Step By Step
  4. Tips

Hair Care

Before we dive into how to thicken your hair, it's essential to take care of your hair. Here are some basic hair care tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools daily.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using heat styling tools.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair, such as braids and ponytails.
  • Get regular haircuts to remove split ends and promote healthier hair growth.

By following these hair care tips, you can help prevent further damage and breakage to your hair.

How To Thicken Your Hair

Now that you've got the basics down let's talk about how to thicken your hair after extensions. Here are some effective ways to achieve thicker, healthier hair:

1. Use Hair Growth Products

There are many hair growth products available that can help stimulate hair growth and promote thicker hair. Look for products that contain ingredients such as biotin, keratin, and vitamins B and E.

2. Eat a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet is essential for healthy hair growth. Make sure to eat foods that are rich in vitamins and minerals, such as leafy greens, nuts, and fish.

3. Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can help improve blood circulation and promote hair growth. Use your fingertips to massage your scalp for a few minutes every night.

4. Use Hair Extensions with Caution

If you're going to use hair extensions, make sure to use them with caution. Choose high-quality extensions that won't damage your natural hair, and avoid leaving them in for too long.

Step By Step

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to thicken your hair after extensions:

  1. Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Apply a hair growth serum to your scalp and massage it in.
  3. Eat a healthy diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals.
  4. Avoid using heat styling tools daily.
  5. Use a heat protectant spray before using heat styling tools.
  6. Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair.
  7. Get regular haircuts to remove split ends and promote healthier hair growth.
  8. Use hair extensions with caution.
  9. Consider using hair thickening products, such as volumizing shampoos and conditioners.
  10. Be patient. It takes time to thicken your hair, but with consistent effort, you'll see results.

Tips

Here are some additional tips on how to thicken your hair after extensions:

  • Don't brush your hair when it's wet, as it's more prone to breakage.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair gently.
  • Consider taking hair growth supplements, such as biotin or folic acid.

FAQ

1. Can hair extensions cause hair loss?

Yes, hair extensions can cause hair loss if they're not installed correctly or if they're left in for too long. It's essential to use high-quality extensions and to remove them regularly to prevent damage to your natural hair.

2. How long does it take to thicken hair after extensions?

It can take several months to thicken your hair after extensions, depending on the extent of the damage. Consistency is key, so make sure to follow a healthy hair care routine and use hair growth products regularly.

3. Can diet affect hair thickness?

Yes, a healthy diet is essential for healthy hair growth. Make sure to eat foods that are rich in vitamins and minerals, such as leafy greens, nuts, and fish.

Thick, healthy hair is achievable, even after extensions. By following these tips and tricks, you can promote healthy hair growth and achieve the luscious locks you've always wanted.
