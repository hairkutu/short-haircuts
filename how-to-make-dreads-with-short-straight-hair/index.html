Are you tired of your short straight hair and want to try something new? Dreadlocks are a great option, but you may be wondering if they are possible with short straight hair. The answer is yes! How to Make Dreads with Short Straight Hair

Before we begin, it's important to note that making dreads with short straight hair may take a bit more effort and time than if you have curly or wavy hair. However, it's still possible and can look amazing!

Here are the steps to make dreads with short straight hair:

1. Start with Clean Hair

Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to make sure it's clean and free of any products or build-up. This will help the dreads form better.

2. Section Your Hair

Use a comb to section your hair into small squares or rectangles. The size of the sections will depend on the thickness of your hair and how big you want your dreads to be.

3. Backcomb Your Hair

Take one section of hair and backcomb it using a fine-tooth comb. This means combing the hair towards your scalp in short, quick strokes. Do this until the hair begins to tangle and form a dread.

4. Roll Your Hair

Roll the dread between your palms to smooth it out and compact the hair. Do this for each section until all your hair is in dreads.

5. Set Your Dreads

Use a hair tie or rubber band to secure the end of each dread. You can also use a dread wax or locking gel to help set the dreads and keep them in place.

Step by Step Guide

For a more detailed guide on how to make dreads with short straight hair, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start with clean hair by washing it with a clarifying shampoo. This will remove any products or build-up in your hair that can make it difficult to form dreads.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use a comb to section your hair into small squares or rectangles. Use hair clips or rubber bands to keep each section separated.

Step 3: Backcomb Your Hair

Take one section of hair and hold it taut. Use a fine-tooth comb to backcomb the hair towards your scalp. Take short, quick strokes to create tangles and knots. Do this until the hair begins to form a dread.

Step 4: Roll Your Hair

Roll the dread between your palms to smooth it out and compact the hair. Do this for each section until all your hair is in dreads.

Step 5: Set Your Dreads

Use a hair tie or rubber band to secure the end of each dread. You can also use a dread wax or locking gel to help set the dreads and keep them in place.

Tips for Making Dreads with Short Straight Hair

Here are some tips to help make the process of making dreads with short straight hair easier:

  • Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any product build-up in your hair.
  • Backcomb your hair in small sections to create more defined dreads.
  • Roll your dreads between your palms to smooth them out and compact the hair.
  • Use a hair tie or rubber band to secure the end of each dread.
  • Use a dread wax or locking gel to help set the dreads and keep them in place.

FAQ

Can you make dreads with short straight hair?

Yes, you can make dreads with short straight hair. It may take more effort and time than if you have curly or wavy hair, but it's still possible.

How long does it take to make dreads with short straight hair?

The time it takes to make dreads with short straight hair will depend on the thickness of your hair and how big you want your dreads to be. It can take anywhere from a few hours to a full day.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Once you have your dreads, it's important to take care of them properly. Here are some tips for hair care for dreadlocks:

  • Wash your hair with a residue-free shampoo once a week.
  • Use a hair conditioner or oil to keep your scalp and hair moisturized.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Avoid using heavy products or oils, as they can cause build-up in your dreads.

With these tips and steps on how to make dreads with short straight hair, you can confidently try out this new hairstyle. Remember to take care of your dreads properly to keep them healthy and looking great.
