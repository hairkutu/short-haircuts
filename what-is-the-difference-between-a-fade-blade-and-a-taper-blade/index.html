What Is The Difference Between A Fade Blade And A Taper Blade

Written by Aprilia Jun 03, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Welcome to our 2023 blog post where we will be discussing the difference between a fade blade and a taper blade. If you're someone who enjoys getting a fresh haircut or is a hairstylist, it's important to know the differences between these two popular blades. We'll be covering the basics, how to use them, tips, and frequently asked questions. So, let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Basics
  • How to Use
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Basics

Both fade and taper blades are used in haircutting to create different looks. A fade blade is typically used to create a fade haircut, where the hair gradually fades from shorter to longer. A taper blade, on the other hand, is used to create a tapered haircut, where the hair is cut shorter towards the nape of the neck and longer towards the top of the head.

The main difference between the two blades is the angle of the blade. A fade blade has a more angled blade, which makes it easier to blend the hair. A taper blade has a straighter blade, which is better for creating clean lines and precise cuts.

How to Use

When using a fade blade, it's important to start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard. This will help create a smooth fade. To use a taper blade, start with a shorter guard and work your way up to a longer guard. This will help create a tapered look.

It's important to use the right technique when using either blade. For a fade, use a circular motion to blend the hair. For a taper, use a straight motion to create clean lines.

Tips

When using either blade, it's important to use a steady hand and take your time. Rushing can lead to uneven cuts and a less-than-desirable result. It's also important to keep your blades well-maintained and sharp. Dull blades can pull on hair and create an uneven cut.

If you're a hairstylist, it's important to communicate with your client about the type of cut they want. Make sure to ask questions and clarify any uncertainties before beginning the cut.

FAQ

What type of clippers should I use with a fade or taper blade?

You can use any type of clippers with a fade or taper blade, as long as the blade fits properly.

Can I use a fade or taper blade on any type of hair?

Yes, you can use either blade on any type of hair. However, it's important to adjust the guard length and technique based on the texture and thickness of the hair.

Do I need to use a guard when using a fade or taper blade?

Yes, it's recommended to use a guard when using either blade. This will help create a consistent cut and prevent any accidental snips.

Hair Care

After getting a fade or taper haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the look. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using excessive heat on your hair. You can also use a styling product to help hold the shape of your haircut. Regular trims every 4-6 weeks will also help keep your hair looking fresh.

We hope this blog post has helped you understand the difference between a fade blade and a taper blade. Remember, practice makes perfect when it comes to using these blades, so don't be afraid to experiment and try different techniques.
