How to Dread Small Hair: A Step-by-Step Guide

If you have short hair, you might think that dreadlocks are out of reach. But don't worry! With a little patience and some know-how, you can create dreads in even the tiniest of locks. In this article, we'll show you how to dread small hair in a relaxed and easy-to-understand way.

How to Dread Small Hair

The process of dreading small hair is similar to dreading longer hair, but there are a few key differences. Here's a step-by-step guide to help you get started:

Step 1: Clean Your Hair

Before you start dreading, it's important to make sure your hair is clean and free of any product buildup. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo and let it air dry.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using a comb or your fingers, separate your hair into small sections. The smaller the sections, the tighter your dreads will be. Use hair ties or clips to keep each section separate.

Step 3: Backcomb Your Hair

Starting at the roots, use a fine-toothed comb to backcomb each section of hair. Hold the hair taut with one hand and use the comb to tease the hair towards the roots. This will create knots and tangles that will eventually form into dreads.

Step 4: Twist and Rip

Once you've backcombed each section, twist the hair tightly in one direction and then rip it apart. This will help to further knot the hair and create more defined dreads.

Step 5: Maintain Your Dreads

Once you've created your dreads, it's important to maintain them. This means washing and conditioning your hair regularly, and using a residue-free dread wax to help hold the dreads together.

Tips for Dreading Small Hair

Here are a few tips to help make the dreading process easier:

  • Use a dreadlock shampoo and conditioner to help keep your hair healthy and your dreads tight.
  • Be patient. Dreading takes time, especially with short hair.
  • Use a crochet hook to tighten up loose hairs and help shape your dreads.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about dreading small hair:

Can you dread hair that's less than an inch long?

Yes, you can dread hair that's less than an inch long. The process will be a bit more challenging, but it's definitely doable.

How long does it take to create dreads in short hair?

It can take anywhere from a few weeks to a few months to create dreads in short hair, depending on how often you maintain them and how quickly your hair knots up.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Here are a few tips for keeping your dreadlocks healthy:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to help keep your dreads moisturized.
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol or other drying ingredients.

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to dread small hair with ease. Remember to be patient and take good care of your dreads, and you'll have a head of beautiful, healthy locks in no time!
