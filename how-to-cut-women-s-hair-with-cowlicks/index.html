How To Cut Women s Hair With Cowlicks A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 18, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're someone who has ever tried to cut women's hair with cowlicks, you know how challenging it can be. Cowlicks are those pesky strands of hair that grow in a different direction than the rest of the hair on your head. They can make styling your hair a nightmare, and cutting them can be even more difficult. In this guide, we'll teach you how to cut women's hair with cowlicks, step-by-step, so you can give your clients the perfect haircut every time.

Description

Before we dive into the how-to of cutting hair with cowlicks, let's first discuss what cowlicks are. Cowlicks are sections of hair that grow in a circular or spiral shape. They can be found on any part of the head, but are most common on the crown of the head or near the hairline. Cowlicks can be a result of genetics, but they can also be caused by the way you sleep, the products you use, or the way you style your hair. Cowlicks can make it difficult to style your hair, and they can also make it tricky to cut hair evenly.

How to Cut Women's Hair with Cowlicks

Now that you understand what cowlicks are, let's get into how to cut women's hair with cowlicks. Follow these steps to give your clients the perfect haircut:

Step 1: Section the Hair

The first step in cutting hair with cowlicks is to section the hair. Use clips to separate the hair into four sections: the top, left side, right side, and back. This will make it easier to work with each section of hair individually.

Step 2: Identify the Cowlicks

Once you have sectioned the hair, identify where the cowlicks are. Cowlicks will usually be located near the hairline or at the crown of the head. Take note of where they are so you can work around them.

Step 3: Work Around the Cowlicks

The key to cutting hair with cowlicks is to work around them. Use your comb to lift the hair and cut around the cowlicks. This will ensure that the hair is cut evenly, and that the cowlicks are not sticking out in odd directions.

Step 4: Use a Razor or Thinning Shears

If the cowlicks are particularly stubborn, you may want to use a razor or thinning shears to help blend them in. This will help to create a more natural look and prevent the cowlicks from sticking out too much.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Once you have cut around the cowlicks, it's time to blend the hair. Use your scissors to cut any stray hairs that may be sticking out, and use your comb to blend the hair together. This will ensure that the haircut looks polished and professional.

Tips

Here are a few tips to help you cut women's hair with cowlicks:

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut
  • Don't cut too much hair at once - it's better to take small sections and work your way around the cowlicks
  • Be patient - cutting hair with cowlicks takes time and precision

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting women's hair with cowlicks:

What if I can't work around the cowlicks?

If you find that the cowlicks are too difficult to work around, you may want to consider using a different haircutting technique. Consult with your client and see if there are any other styles that they may be interested in that will work better with their cowlicks.

Can I cut my own hair with cowlicks?

Cutting your own hair with cowlicks can be tricky, but it is possible. Just be sure to take your time and work slowly, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it.

How often should I cut my hair with cowlicks?

The frequency of haircuts for women with cowlicks will depend on the individual. It's best to consult with your stylist to determine how often you should come in for a trim.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is important when dealing with cowlicks. Here are a few tips to keep your hair healthy and looking great:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair (e.g. blow dryers, straighteners)
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy

Now that you know how to cut women's hair with cowlicks, you can give your clients the perfect haircut every time. Remember to be patient, work slowly, and use the right tools to ensure a clean and polished look. With these tips and tricks, you'll be a pro at cutting hair with cowlicks in no time.
