How To Fix Patchy Bleached Hair At Home A Complete Guide

Written by William Sep 11, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you've recently bleached your hair and ended up with patchy or uneven results, don't worry! You can easily fix this problem at home with a few simple steps. In this guide, we'll walk you through everything you need to know to fix patchy bleached hair at home.

Description

Patchy bleached hair occurs when some parts of your hair are lighter than others after bleaching. This can happen due to various reasons, such as uneven application, overlapping bleach, or different hair textures. Fixing patchy bleached hair at home is possible, but it requires some patience, time, and effort.

How to Fix Patchy Bleached Hair at Home

Step 1: Assess the Damage

The first step in fixing patchy bleached hair is to assess the damage. Take a close look at your hair and identify the areas that are patchy or uneven. This will help you determine the best approach to fixing your hair.

Step 2: Determine the Cause

The next step is to determine the cause of the patchiness. If it's due to uneven application, you may need to re-bleach your hair. If it's due to overlapping bleach, you may need to use a color remover. If it's due to different hair textures, you may need to use a toner or color corrector.

Step 3: Re-Bleach or Color Correct

If you need to re-bleach your hair, make sure to follow the instructions carefully and use a high-quality bleach. Apply the bleach evenly to the areas that need it, and leave it on for the recommended time. If you need to use a color corrector or toner, make sure to choose the right shade and apply it evenly to your hair.

Step 4: Deep Condition Your Hair

After bleaching or color correcting your hair, it's essential to deep condition it. This will help restore moisture to your hair and prevent further damage. Use a high-quality deep conditioner and leave it on for at least 30 minutes before rinsing it off.

Tips for Fixing Patchy Bleached Hair at Home

Here are some tips to help you fix patchy bleached hair at home:

  • Use a high-quality bleach or color corrector
  • Follow the instructions carefully
  • Apply the product evenly
  • Don't leave the product on for too long
  • Deep condition your hair regularly
  • Avoid heat styling and chemical treatments until your hair has fully recovered

FAQ About Fixing Patchy Bleached Hair at Home

Here are some frequently asked questions about fixing patchy bleached hair at home:

Can I fix patchy bleached hair without re-bleaching it?

Yes, you can use a color corrector or toner to fix patchy bleached hair without re-bleaching it. However, if the patchiness is severe, you may need to re-bleach your hair to achieve an even color.

How often should I deep condition my hair?

You should deep condition your hair once a week or more, depending on how damaged it is. Deep conditioning will help restore moisture to your hair and prevent further damage.

Can I use home remedies to fix patchy bleached hair?

While some home remedies may help improve the condition of your hair, they are not a substitute for professional hair care products. It's best to use high-quality, salon-grade products to fix patchy bleached hair.

Hair Care Tips for Bleached Hair

If you have bleached hair, here are some hair care tips to help keep it healthy:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid heat styling and chemical treatments
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine
  • Deep condition your hair regularly
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends

Fixing patchy bleached hair at home is possible, but it requires some patience and effort. Follow the steps and tips outlined in this guide, and your hair will be looking better in no time!
