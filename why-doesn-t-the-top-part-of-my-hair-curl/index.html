Why Doesn t The Top Part Of My Hair Curl Explained

Written by Eveline Mar 23, 2023 · 7 min read

If you're someone who loves to curl your hair, you've probably experienced the frustration of not being able to get the curls you want at the top of your head. No matter how much you try, the top part of your hair just seems to refuse to curl. This can be especially frustrating if you have a special event or occasion coming up, and you want your hair to look its best. In this blog post, we'll be exploring some of the reasons why the top part of your hair may not be curling, and what you can do to fix it.

Description

When you curl your hair, you're essentially manipulating the hair shaft to create a wave or curl. This is achieved by wrapping sections of hair around a heated curling iron or wand. However, the top part of your hair may not be curling for a number of reasons, including:

How to

Now that we've gone over some of the reasons why your hair may not be curling at the top, let's talk about what you can do to fix it. Here are some tips:

Step by Step

Step 1: Prep your hair
Before you start curling, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Apply a heat protectant to your hair to prevent damage from the curling iron.

Step 2: Section your hair
Separate your hair into sections, starting from the bottom. Use hair clips to hold each section in place.

Step 3: Choose the right curling iron
Make sure you're using a curling iron that's the right size for your hair. If you have shorter hair, choose a smaller curling iron. If you have longer hair, go for a larger one.

Step 4: Curl the bottom sections
Start curling the bottom sections of your hair first. Take a small section of hair and wrap it around the curling iron, holding it for a few seconds before releasing.

Step 5: Move on to the top section
Once you've curled the bottom sections, move on to the top section. Take small sections of hair and wrap them around the curling iron, holding for a few seconds before releasing.

Step 6: Finish with hairspray
Once you've curled all of your hair, finish with a light mist of hairspray to hold the curls in place.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you get the perfect curls:

  • Make sure your curling iron is hot enough. If it's not hot enough, your hair won't curl properly.
  • Don't overload your hair with product. Too much product can weigh down your hair and make it harder to curl.
  • Use a comb or brush to smooth out each section of hair before curling.
  • Try using different curling techniques, such as wrapping the hair around the iron or using a twisting motion.

FAQ

Q: Can I curl my hair if it's wet?

A: No, you should never curl your hair when it's wet. Wet hair is more prone to damage from heat, and the curl won't hold as well.

Q: How long should I hold the curling iron on my hair?

A: This depends on the thickness and texture of your hair. As a general rule, hold the curling iron on your hair for 10-15 seconds.

Q: How often should I curl my hair?

A: It's best to limit heat styling to once or twice a week, to prevent damage to your hair.

Hair Care

Finally, it's important to take care of your hair to ensure it's healthy and able to hold curls. Here are some tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, and always use a heat protectant product.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair and cause damage.

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you should be able to get the curls you want, even at the top of your head.
