How To Repair Chemically Damaged Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Aug 07, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Chemical treatments like hair dyes, perms, and relaxers can give you the look you desire, but they often come at a cost: damaged hair. If your hair is brittle, dry, and prone to breakage, you may be wondering how to repair chemically damaged hair. Luckily, there are steps you can take to restore your hair's health and shine. In this article, we'll walk you through the process of repairing chemically damaged hair, from understanding what causes damage to implementing a hair care routine that will leave your locks looking and feeling their best.

What Causes Chemically Damaged Hair?

Chemically damaged hair is caused by a variety of factors, including:

  • Over-processing with dyes, relaxers, or perms
  • Exposure to harsh chemicals, such as chlorine or bleach
  • Excessive heat styling
  • Poor nutrition or vitamin deficiencies

If you're experiencing chemically damaged hair, it's important to identify the cause so you can take steps to prevent further damage.

How to Repair Chemically Damaged Hair: Step-by-Step

Step 1: Assess the Damage

The first step in repairing chemically damaged hair is to assess the extent of the damage. Look for signs like split ends, dryness, and breakage. You may also notice that your hair is less elastic than usual, meaning it doesn't stretch as easily when pulled. Once you've assessed the damage, you can move on to the next step.

Step 2: Cut Back on Chemical Treatments

If you're experiencing chemically damaged hair, it's important to cut back on chemical treatments like dyes and relaxers. Give your hair a break and let it recover naturally. If you can't resist the urge to color your hair, try using a gentle, ammonia-free dye.

Step 3: Use a Protein Treatment

Protein treatments can help repair chemically damaged hair by strengthening the hair shaft. Look for products that contain ingredients like keratin, collagen, or hydrolyzed silk. Follow the instructions on the product label for best results.

Step 4: Moisturize Your Hair

Dry, brittle hair is a common sign of chemically damaged hair. To combat dryness, use a moisturizing conditioner after every shampoo. Look for products that contain ingredients like shea butter, coconut oil, or argan oil. You can also use a leave-in conditioner for added moisture.

Step 5: Protect Your Hair from Heat

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can further damage chemically treated hair. If you must use these tools, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage. You can also try air-drying your hair or using heat-free styling methods like braids or twists.

Tips for Repairing Chemically Damaged Hair

  • Avoid using harsh shampoos that contain sulfates
  • Limit your use of heat styling tools
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends from spreading
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage

FAQs

Can chemically damaged hair be repaired?

Yes, chemically damaged hair can be repaired with the right hair care routine. Be patient and consistent with your routine, and you should start to see results.

How long does it take to repair chemically damaged hair?

The amount of time it takes to repair chemically damaged hair depends on the extent of the damage. It could take anywhere from a few weeks to several months to see results.

Hair Care Routine for Chemically Damaged Hair

Here's an example of a hair care routine for chemically damaged hair:

  • Wash hair with a sulfate-free shampoo
  • Use a protein treatment once a week
  • Apply a moisturizing conditioner after every shampoo
  • Avoid using heat styling tools
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends

By following these steps and being consistent with your hair care routine, you can repair chemically damaged hair and restore your locks to their former glory.
