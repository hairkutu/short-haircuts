How To Make Your Face Look Less Round With Makeup

Written by Bardi Apr 05, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Do you have a round face shape and want to know how to make it look less round with makeup? Well, you've come to the right place! In this article, we will discuss how to contour your face to make it look slimmer and more defined. We will also provide tips on choosing the right makeup products and techniques to achieve a flattering look. So, let's get started!

Description

A round face shape is characterized by a fuller, wider face with a rounded chin and cheeks. While some people find this shape to be cute and youthful, others may want to create a more defined look. With the right makeup techniques, you can contour your face to make it look slimmer and more angular. This involves using darker shades to create shadows and lighter shades to highlight your features.

How to

Step 1: Prep Your Skin

Before applying makeup, it's important to prep your skin by cleansing, toning, and moisturizing. This will create a smooth base for your makeup and help it last longer. Apply a primer to blur any imperfections and create a smooth canvas for your foundation.

Step 2: Apply Foundation

Choose a foundation that matches your skin tone and apply it evenly all over your face. Use a damp beauty blender or a brush to blend the foundation into your skin. Make sure to blend it well around your jawline and hairline to avoid any harsh lines.

Step 3: Contour Your Cheeks

Take a darker shade of foundation or a contouring powder and apply it to the hollows of your cheeks. Use a fluffy brush to blend it upwards towards your ears. This will create a shadow effect and make your cheeks look more defined.

Step 4: Define Your Jawline

Apply the same darker shade of foundation or contouring powder to your jawline to create a more defined look. Use a small brush to blend it well along your jawline.

Step 5: Highlight Your Features

Take a lighter shade of foundation or a highlighting powder and apply it to the high points of your face, such as your cheekbones, brow bone, and the bridge of your nose. This will create a luminous effect and make your features stand out.

Step 6: Finish with Blush and Lipstick

Apply a natural-looking blush to the apples of your cheeks to add a flush of color to your face. Choose a lipstick shade that complements your skin tone and apply it evenly to your lips to complete the look.

Tips

- Choose makeup products that are specifically designed for contouring and highlighting.

- Use a light hand when applying darker shades to avoid any harsh lines.

- Blend well to create a seamless look.

- Experiment with different shades and techniques to find what works best for your face shape.

FAQ

Q: Can contouring make my face look too thin?

A: While contouring can create a slimmer look, it's important to use it in moderation. You don't want to create a harsh, unnatural look. Use a light hand and blend well to create a natural-looking contour.

Q: What kind of brush should I use for contouring?

A: Use a fluffy, angled brush for contouring. This will help you apply the product precisely and blend it well.

Q: Can contouring work for all face shapes?

A: Yes, contouring can work for all face shapes. The key is to use the right techniques and products to create a flattering look.

Hair Care

When contouring your face, it's important to consider your hair style. If you have a shorter haircut, you may want to create a more defined jawline to balance out your face shape. If you have longer hair, you can play around with different styles to create a more flattering look. Experiment with different haircuts, styles, and colors to find what works best for you.
