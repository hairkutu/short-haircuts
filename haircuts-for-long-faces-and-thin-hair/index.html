Best Haircuts For Long Faces And Thin Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 26, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Long faces and thin hair can be a tricky combination when it comes to finding the perfect haircut. You want to add volume and depth to your hair, but you don't want your face to look even longer. Fortunately, there are plenty of haircuts that can flatter your features and give you the confidence boost you need. In this article, we'll go over some of the best haircuts for long faces and thin hair, as well as tips on how to care for your hair.

Table of Contents:

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Long faces are characterized by a high forehead, elongated chin, and narrow cheekbones. If you have thin hair, your strands may lack volume and fullness, making it difficult to style. The key to finding the right haircut for this combination is to create the illusion of width and volume. This can be achieved through strategic layers, texture, and length.

How to

When it comes to choosing a haircut for long faces and thin hair, it's important to consider your face shape and hair texture. A skilled hairstylist can help you determine the right haircut for your specific features, but here are some general guidelines to follow:

  • Opt for haircuts that add volume and width, such as layered bobs, long layers, and shags.
  • Avoid haircuts that elongate your face, such as blunt bobs or long, straight hair.
  • Add texture to your hair with curls, waves, or choppy layers to create the illusion of fullness.
  • Consider adding bangs to your haircut, as they can help balance out the length of your face.

Step by Step

Here are some step-by-step instructions for achieving some of the best haircuts for long faces and thin hair:

Layered Bob

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Section your hair into four parts: top, bottom, and two sides.
  3. Create a guide by cutting the bottom section to your desired length.
  4. Add long layers to the top sections, starting at the crown of your head.
  5. Blend the layers together by point-cutting with scissors.
  6. Style with a curling iron or wand for added texture.

Long Layers

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Section your hair into four parts: top, bottom, and two sides.
  3. Create a guide by cutting the bottom section to your desired length.
  4. Add long layers to the top sections, starting at the crown of your head.
  5. Blend the layers together by point-cutting with scissors.
  6. Style with a curling iron or wand for added texture.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for caring for your long, thin hair:

  • Use volumizing products, such as mousse or root lift spray, to add fullness to your hair.
  • Avoid using heavy conditioners or oils, as they can weigh down your hair and make it look flat.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends, which can make your hair look even thinner.
  • Consider investing in a high-quality hairbrush, such as a boar bristle brush, to help distribute your scalp's natural oils throughout your hair.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about haircuts for long faces and thin hair:

What is the best haircut for a long face?

The best haircut for a long face is one that adds width and volume, such as a layered bob, long layers, or a shag.

What is the best haircut for thin hair?

The best haircut for thin hair is one that adds texture and fullness, such as choppy layers, curls, or waves.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy, beautiful hair. Here are some tips for caring for your long, thin hair:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping your scalp of its natural oils.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as straighteners or curling irons, every day to prevent heat damage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth and strength.

With the right haircut and hair care routine, you can rock your long face and thin hair with confidence. Consult with a hairstylist to find the best haircut for your unique features and hair texture, and don't forget to follow these tips for maintaining healthy hair.
