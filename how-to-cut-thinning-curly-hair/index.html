How To Cut Thinning Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Sep 04, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you have thinning curly hair, it can be challenging to find the right haircut that complements your face shape and highlights your natural curls. However, with the right technique and tools, you can create a haircut that adds volume and texture to your hair. In this blog post, we will guide you on how to cut thinning curly hair.

How to Cut Thinning Curly Hair

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure it's clean and dry. Comb your hair to remove any tangles and knots. Then, section your hair into four parts, securing each section with a hairclip.

Step 2: Cut Your Hair

Start at the back of your head and work your way to the front. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, following the natural curl pattern. Repeat this process with each section of hair, making sure to cut the hair evenly.

When you reach the front of your head, take smaller sections of hair and cut them at an angle. This will create layers that add volume and texture to your hair.

Step 3: Clean Up

Once you have finished cutting your hair, use a mirror to check for any uneven spots or stray hairs. Use your scissors to trim any uneven spots or stray hairs.

Tips for Cutting Thinning Curly Hair

Cutting thinning curly hair can be challenging, but with these tips, you can create a haircut that adds volume and texture to your hair:

  • Cut your hair when it's dry to see where the curls fall naturally.
  • Use sharp scissors to create clean cuts.
  • Cut your hair in small sections to ensure even cuts.
  • Create layers to add volume and texture to your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How often should I trim my thinning curly hair?

It's recommended to trim your hair every six to eight weeks to prevent split ends and breakage.

Should I cut my hair when it's wet or dry?

It's best to cut your hair when it's dry to see where the curls fall naturally.

Can I cut my hair myself or should I go to a hairstylist?

If you have experience cutting hair, you can cut your hair yourself. However, it's recommended to go to a hairstylist if you're unsure or want a more complex haircut.

Hair Care for Thin Curly Hair

To keep your thinning curly hair healthy and strong, make sure to:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to add moisture and shine.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair to prevent breakage.
  • Limit heat styling tools to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions.

Conclusion

Cutting thinning curly hair can be challenging, but with the right technique and tools, you can create a haircut that adds volume and texture to your hair. Make sure to follow our step-by-step guide and tips to achieve the perfect haircut. Remember to take care of your hair by using the right hair care products and techniques.
