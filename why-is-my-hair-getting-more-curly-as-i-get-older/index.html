Why Is My Hair Getting More Curly As I Get Older

Written by Bardi Aug 26, 2023 · 7 min read

If you’ve noticed that your hair is getting curlier as you age, you’re not alone. Many people experience changes in their hair texture and curl pattern as they get older. In this article, we’ll explore the reasons behind this phenomenon and provide tips on how to care for your curls.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

As we age, our hair goes through a natural aging process that can affect its texture and curl pattern. This is due to changes in the hair follicles, which can cause the hair to become thinner, drier, and more brittle. Additionally, the amount of melanin in the hair decreases as we age, which can cause it to lose its natural color and become gray or white.

These changes can also affect the shape of the hair follicles, which can cause the hair to become more curly or wavy. This is because the hair follicles become more oval-shaped, which can cause the hair to grow in a spiral pattern instead of a straight one.

How to

If you’re experiencing changes in your hair texture and curl pattern, there are several things you can do to care for your curls:

1. Moisturize

Dry hair is more prone to frizz and breakage, so it’s important to keep your curls moisturized. Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner, and consider adding a deep conditioning treatment to your hair care routine once a week.

2. Use a Diffuser

If you’re using a hair dryer to style your curls, use a diffuser attachment to help distribute the heat evenly and prevent frizz. This will also help enhance your natural curl pattern.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and cause it to lose its natural curl pattern. If you must use these tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the heat setting low.

Step by Step

Here’s a step-by-step guide to caring for your curly hair:

1. Shampoo and Condition

Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner to keep your curls moisturized.

2. Towel Dry

Gently towel dry your hair to remove excess water.

3. Apply Product

Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to your hair to help define your curls.

4. Diffuse

Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your curls. Start at the ends and work your way up, using a low heat setting.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for caring for your curly hair:

1. Sleep on a Satin Pillowcase

Satin pillowcases can help reduce frizz and breakage by preventing your hair from rubbing against a rough cotton pillowcase.

2. Don’t Overwash

Curly hair tends to be drier than straight hair, so you don’t need to wash it as often. Aim to wash your hair every two to three days.

3. Embrace Your Curls

Don’t try to fight your natural curl pattern. Embrace your curls and learn to work with them.

FAQ

Q: Can I straighten my curly hair?

A: Yes, you can straighten your curly hair, but be sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the heat setting low to prevent damage.

Q: Will coloring my hair affect its curl pattern?

A: Yes, coloring your hair can affect its texture and curl pattern, so be sure to use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner to keep your curls healthy.

Q: Can genetics affect my hair texture?

A: Yes, genetics can play a role in your hair texture and curl pattern, so if your parents or grandparents have curly hair, you’re more likely to have curly hair as well.

Hair Care

Caring for your curly hair requires a bit of extra effort, but it’s worth it to keep your curls healthy and beautiful. Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner, moisturize your curls regularly, and avoid heat styling tools whenever possible. Embrace your natural curl pattern and learn to work with it, and your hair will thank you.
