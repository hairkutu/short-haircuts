How To Cut Men s Hair With Clippers And Guard

Written by Petter Apr 09, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of spending a lot of money on haircuts? Do you want to learn how to cut men's hair with clippers and guard? If yes, then you are in the right place. In this article, we will guide you on how to cut men's hair with clippers and guard. We will also provide you with some tips and tricks to make the process easier and smoother. So, let's get started!

Description

Cutting men's hair with clippers and guard is a simple and easy process. It requires a little bit of practice and patience, but once you get the hang of it, you will be able to cut hair like a pro. Clippers are an essential tool for cutting hair, and guards are used to adjust the length of the hair. By using different guards, you can achieve different lengths and styles.

How to

Before you start cutting hair, you need to have the right equipment. You will need a clipper, guards, scissors, a comb, a cape, and a spray bottle with water. Once you have all the equipment, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry the Hair

The first step is to wash and dry the hair. Make sure the hair is clean and dry before you start cutting. Wet hair tends to stick together, making it harder to cut evenly.

Step 2: Choose the Right Guard

The second step is to choose the right guard. Different guards are used to achieve different lengths. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard if necessary. It's always better to start with a longer guard and trim the hair gradually than to start with a shorter guard and end up cutting too much hair.

Step 3: Start Cutting

The third step is to start cutting. Begin at the back of the head and work your way up to the crown. Use the clipper in an upward motion and go against the direction of the hair growth. Use the comb to lift the hair and cut it evenly.

Step 4: Trim the Sides and the Top

The fourth step is to trim the sides and the top. Use the same guard that you used for the back of the head and cut the hair evenly. Use the scissors to trim any stray hairs that the clipper missed.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

The fifth step is to blend the hair. Use a longer guard and go over the hair that you just cut to blend it with the longer hair on top. Use the scissors to trim any uneven areas.

Step 6: Finish the Haircut

The sixth and final step is to finish the haircut. Use a shorter guard to trim the hair around the neck and the ears. Use the scissors to trim any stray hairs. Brush the hair and use the spray bottle to wet the hair if necessary.

Tips

Here are some tips to make the process easier:

  • Practice on a mannequin head before cutting someone's hair.
  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard.
  • Use the comb to lift the hair and cut it evenly.
  • Blend the hair to make the haircut look more natural.
  • Use the scissors to trim any stray hairs that the clipper missed.

FAQ

Q: Can I cut my own hair with clippers?

A: Yes, you can cut your own hair with clippers. However, it may be harder to cut the back of the head without help.

Q: What if I accidentally cut too much hair?

A: Don't worry. Hair grows back, and it's better to cut too little than too much. You can always go back and trim more hair if necessary.

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It depends on the length of your hair and the style you want to achieve. Generally, it's recommended to cut hair every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care

After cutting hair, it's essential to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as blow dryers and straighteners.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet and drink plenty of water to promote hair growth.

Cutting men's hair with clippers and guard is a simple and easy process. By following these steps and tips, you can achieve a great haircut. Remember to practice and be patient, and you will be able to cut hair like a pro in no time!
