How To Fix Your Frontal Wig A Step By Step Guide

How to Fix Your Frontal Wig

A frontal wig is a type of wig that has lace at the front, which allows you to part the hair in any direction. Fixing a frontal wig involves securing the wig to your scalp so that it looks natural and stays in place. Here are the steps you need to follow:

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start by preparing your hair. If you have long hair, braid your hair in cornrows or wrap it around your head in a circular pattern. If you have short hair, use a wig cap to flatten your hair.

Step 2: Clean your forehead and hairline with rubbing alcohol to remove any oil or dirt. This will help the wig adhesive stick better.

Step 3: Apply a wig cap over your hair. This will help keep your natural hair in place and prevent it from sticking out from under the wig.

Step 4: Cut the lace on the wig. Use sharp scissors to carefully cut the lace along the hairline of the wig.

Step 5: Apply wig adhesive. Apply a thin layer of adhesive along your hairline, starting from one side and working your way around to the other side. Wait for the adhesive to dry until it becomes tacky.

Step 6: Place the wig on your head. Starting from the front, carefully place the wig onto your head and press it down onto the adhesive. Use a comb or your fingers to smooth out any bumps or bubbles in the wig.

Step 7: Style your wig. Use a flat iron or curling iron to style your wig as desired.

Tips for Maintaining Your Wig

Now that you know how to fix your frontal wig, it's important to know how to care for it to keep it looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your wig regularly using a mild shampoo and conditioner.
  • Store your wig on a wig stand to keep its shape.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage the wig.
  • Avoid sleeping in your wig, as this can cause tangles and damage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long will my wig stay in place?

A: This depends on the type of adhesive you use and how well you apply it. Generally, most adhesives will keep your wig in place for 1-2 weeks, but you may need to reapply the adhesive more frequently if you have oily skin or sweat a lot.

Q: Can I swim or shower with my wig on?

A: It's best to avoid getting your wig wet as much as possible, as this can cause the adhesive to loosen and the wig to slip. If you do need to get your wig wet, use a waterproof adhesive and be sure to dry the wig thoroughly afterwards.

Hair Care Tips

Even though your wig is not your natural hair, it still requires proper care to keep it looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or brush to detangle your wig.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your wig, as this can damage the fibers.
  • Use a wig-specific shampoo and conditioner to keep your wig looking its best.
  • Avoid using regular hair products on your wig, as these can cause buildup and damage the fibers.

And that's it! With these tips, you should be able to fix your frontal wig in no time and keep it looking great for as long as possible. Happy wig-wearing!
