If I Cut My Hair Short Will It Grow Thicker

Written by Eveline Aug 06, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you tired of having thin hair and considering cutting it short in hopes of it growing thicker? Well, you're not alone. Many people believe that cutting their hair short will make it grow thicker, but is this really true? In this article, we will explore the truth behind this common myth and provide you with some tips to help you achieve thicker hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Your Hair Short
  • Step-by-Step Guide on Cutting Your Hair Short
  • Tips for Growing Thicker Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Thicker Hair

Description

The belief that cutting your hair short will make it grow thicker is a common myth. The truth is that cutting your hair short does not affect the thickness of your hair. Your hair's thickness is determined by your genetics, age, and overall health. However, cutting your hair short can make it appear thicker because it removes split ends and damage, giving your hair a healthier and fuller look.

How to Cut Your Hair Short

If you're considering cutting your hair short, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, decide on the length you want and make sure it complements your face shape and features. You may want to consult with a hairstylist to help you choose the right style for you.

Step-by-Step Guide on Cutting Your Hair Short

If you're cutting your hair short yourself, follow these steps:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Section your hair into four parts using clips.
  3. Begin cutting the hair at the back, working your way to the sides and front.
  4. Use scissors to cut the hair to your desired length.
  5. Blend the hair by cutting at an angle to avoid choppy ends.
  6. Style your hair as desired.

Remember, if you're not confident in cutting your hair yourself, it's best to seek the help of a professional hairstylist.

Tips for Growing Thicker Hair

While cutting your hair short won't make it grow thicker, there are some tips you can follow to promote thicker hair growth:

  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is free of harsh chemicals.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, or use a heat protectant spray if you do.
  • Massage your scalp to improve blood circulation and stimulate hair growth.
  • Get enough sleep and manage stress levels.

FAQs

Does cutting your hair short make it grow faster?

No, cutting your hair short does not make it grow faster. Hair growth is determined by your genetics, age, and overall health.

Will shaving my head make my hair grow back thicker?

No, shaving your head does not make your hair grow back thicker. The thickness of your hair is determined by your genetics, age, and overall health.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and free of split ends.

Hair Care for Thicker Hair

Once you've achieved thicker hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and fullness. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to avoid pulling and damaging your hair.
  • Avoid using tight hair ties that can cause breakage.
  • Protect your hair from sun damage with a hat or scarf.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

In conclusion, cutting your hair short will not make it grow thicker, but it can give the illusion of thicker hair by removing split ends and damage. To achieve thicker hair, focus on following a healthy lifestyle and hair care routine. Remember, if you're not confident in cutting your hair yourself, it's best to seek the help of a professional hairstylist.
