How To Cut Layers In Hair Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Are you looking to add some dimension and movement to your hair without breaking the bank? Cutting layers in your hair yourself can be a great way to achieve a fresh look without visiting the salon. In this guide, we will walk you through the step-by-step process of how to cut layers in hair yourself, as well as provide some tips and hair care advice to ensure your new 'do stays looking fresh and fabulous.

How to Cut Layers in Hair Yourself

Before we get started, it's important to note that cutting layers in your hair yourself can be a little intimidating, especially if you're not used to handling scissors. However, with the right tools and a little patience, you can achieve a beautiful layered look.

Tools You Will Need

  • Sharp hair-cutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Sectioning clips or hair ties

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to achieve a layered look:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. It's best to cut hair when it's dry, as wet hair can stretch and make it difficult to achieve an even cut.
  2. Comb your hair straight and divide it into sections. Use sectioning clips or hair ties to keep each section separate.
  3. Determine the length of your layers. Decide how many layers you want and where you want them to start. For a subtle look, start your layers around the jawline. For a more dramatic look, start your layers higher up.
  4. Begin cutting. Take one section of hair at a time and hold it straight up. Use your hair-cutting scissors to cut a small amount at a time. Start at the ends and work your way up. Be sure to cut at an angle to achieve a layered look.
  5. Continue cutting. Work your way through each section of hair, making sure each layer is even and the same length.
  6. Blend your layers. Once you've finished cutting all your layers, use your scissors to blend them together. This will create a natural-looking layered effect.

Tips for Cutting Layers

Here are a few tips to keep in mind as you cut your layers:

  • Start with small cuts. It's easier to cut more hair than it is to add it back.
  • Don't cut straight across. Cutting at an angle will create a natural-looking layered effect.
  • Take your time. Cutting hair can be a slow process, especially if you're new to it. Be patient and take breaks if needed.
  • Use a mirror. It's important to see what you're doing as you cut your hair. Use a handheld mirror or set up a larger mirror in front of you.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting layers in hair yourself:

Can I cut layers in curly hair?

Yes, you can cut layers in curly hair. Keep in mind that curly hair can shrink when it dries, so be sure to cut it a little longer than you want it to be.

How often should I cut my layers?

It's recommended to cut your layers every 8-12 weeks to maintain their shape and prevent split ends.

Can I cut layers in short hair?

Yes, you can cut layers in short hair. However, the layers may be less noticeable than they would be in longer hair.

Hair Care After Cutting Layers

After cutting your layers, it's important to take care of your hair to ensure it stays healthy and looking great:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that's right for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat tools too often, as they can dry out your hair and cause damage.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your layers and prevent split ends.
  • Consider using a hair mask or hot oil treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.

Cutting layers in your hair yourself can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and a little patience, you can achieve a beautiful, layered look that's all your own. Follow these tips and hair care advice to keep your hair looking its best!
