Can Accutane Cause Hair Loss

Written by Luffy Apr 18, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Accutane, also known as isotretinoin, is a medication that is commonly used to treat severe acne. While it is effective in clearing up acne, one of the potential side effects that has been reported is hair loss. In this article, we will explore whether Accutane can cause hair loss, and what steps you can take to prevent or minimize it.

What is Accutane?

Accutane is a medication that is derived from vitamin A. It is prescribed to treat severe acne that has not responded to other forms of treatment. Accutane works by reducing the amount of oil that is produced by the skin's oil glands, which helps to prevent acne from forming. It is typically taken in pill form for a period of four to six months.

Can Accutane Cause Hair Loss?

While hair loss is not a common side effect of Accutane, it has been reported by some users. The exact mechanism by which Accutane causes hair loss is not fully understood, but it is believed to be related to the medication's effect on the sebaceous glands in the scalp. These glands are responsible for producing the oil that keeps hair follicles healthy, and it is possible that Accutane disrupts this process.

How Common is Hair Loss as a Side Effect of Accutane?

Hair loss is not a common side effect of Accutane, but it has been reported in a small percentage of users. According to one study, hair loss occurred in approximately 10% of patients who took Accutane. However, it is important to note that this percentage may be higher in individuals who have a history of hair loss or who are genetically predisposed to hair loss.

How to Prevent Hair Loss While Taking Accutane

If you are concerned about hair loss while taking Accutane, there are some steps you can take to prevent or minimize it:

1. Take a Hair Supplement

Taking a hair supplement that contains vitamins and minerals that are important for hair health, such as biotin and zinc, may help to prevent hair loss while taking Accutane.

2. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Using a gentle shampoo that is free of harsh chemicals and sulfates can help to keep your hair healthy and prevent breakage.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoiding heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can help to prevent damage to your hair that can lead to breakage and hair loss.

4. Be Gentle When Brushing

Being gentle when brushing your hair, and using a wide-toothed comb instead of a brush, can help to prevent breakage and hair loss.

FAQs

1. Will my hair grow back after I stop taking Accutane?

In most cases, hair loss that occurs as a side effect of Accutane is temporary and will resolve on its own once you stop taking the medication. However, it may take several months for your hair to return to its normal thickness and texture.

2. Should I stop taking Accutane if I experience hair loss?

If you are experiencing hair loss while taking Accutane, you should speak to your doctor. In some cases, your doctor may recommend that you stop taking the medication or adjust your dosage.

3. Can women take Accutane if they are pregnant or planning to become pregnant?

No, women should not take Accutane if they are pregnant or planning to become pregnant. Accutane can cause serious birth defects, and women of childbearing age must take precautions to avoid pregnancy while taking the medication.

Hair Care Tips for Healthy Hair

Whether you are taking Accutane or not, there are some steps you can take to keep your hair healthy:

1. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals that are important for hair health, such as vitamin C, B vitamins, and iron, can help to keep your hair strong and healthy.

2. Avoid Harsh Chemicals

Avoiding harsh chemicals and sulfates in your hair care products can help to prevent damage to your hair that can lead to breakage and hair loss.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray can help to protect your hair from sun damage that can lead to dryness, breakage, and hair loss.

4. Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims can help to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends that can lead to breakage and hair loss.

Conclusion

While hair loss is not a common side effect of Accutane, it has been reported in some users. If you are concerned about hair loss while taking Accutane, there are steps you can take to prevent or minimize it, such as taking a hair supplement and using a gentle shampoo. Additionally, following good hair care practices, such as eating a healthy diet and protecting your hair from the sun, can help to keep your hair healthy and prevent hair loss.
