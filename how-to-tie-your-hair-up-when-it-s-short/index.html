How To Tie Your Hair Up When It s Short A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Mar 24, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you struggling to tie your hair up because it's too short? Don't worry, you're not alone. Short hair can be tricky to style, especially when it comes to putting it up. But with the right techniques and some practice, you can create a variety of hairstyles that will keep your hair out of your face and look stylish at the same time.

How to Tie Your Hair Up When It's Short

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's take a look at some popular ways to tie up short hair:

  • Ponytail
  • Bun
  • Braid
  • Half-Up, Half-Down
  • Top Knot
  • Twisted Ponytail

Each of these hairstyles can be modified to suit your hair length and personal style. So, let's get started!

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Ponytail

Start by brushing your hair to remove any tangles. Gather your hair at the back of your head and hold it in place with one hand. Use a hair tie to secure the ponytail in place.

2. Bun

Gather your hair into a ponytail and twist it into a bun shape. Use bobby pins to secure the bun in place. For a messier look, pull out a few strands of hair around your face.

3. Braid

Part your hair down the middle and create two small braids on either side of your head. Secure the ends of the braids with a hair tie and wrap them around the back of your head, securing them in place with bobby pins.

4. Half-Up, Half-Down

Take the top half of your hair and pull it back, securing it in place with a hair tie. Leave the bottom half of your hair loose.

5. Top Knot

Gather your hair into a high ponytail and twist it into a bun shape on the top of your head. Use bobby pins to secure the bun in place.

6. Twisted Ponytail

Create a low ponytail and secure it with a hair tie. Split the hair above the hair tie in half and flip the ponytail up and over the split. Tuck the ends of the ponytail into the split and secure with bobby pins.

Tips for Tying Up Short Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when tying up short hair:

  • Use hair ties that match your hair color to make them less noticeable.
  • Don't pull your hair too tight, as this can cause breakage and damage.
  • Experiment with different styles to find what works best for your hair length and texture.

FAQs

Can I still tie up my hair if it's too short?

Yes, there are plenty of ways to tie up short hair, as we've shown in this guide. You may need to modify the styles slightly to suit your hair length and texture.

Will tying up my hair damage it?

If you tie your hair up too tight or use hair ties that are too small, it can cause breakage and damage. Be gentle when tying up your hair and use hair ties that are the appropriate size and strength for your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Short Hair

Short hair still needs proper care and maintenance to look its best. Here are a few tips to keep your short hair healthy:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage to your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and tidy.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions by wearing a hat or scarf.

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to tie up your short hair in a variety of styles that look great and keep your hair out of your face. Experiment with different styles and have fun with your hair!
