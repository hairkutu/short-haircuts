How To Start Dreads With Straight Hair

Written by Christine Aug 13, 2023 · 9 min read

Welcome to the ultimate guide on how to start dreads with straight hair! If you're someone who has always wanted to rock those cool dreadlocks but don't have naturally curly or kinky hair, don't worry, it's still possible. In this article, we'll walk you through everything you need to know about starting dreads with straight hair, including tips, step-by-step instructions, and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  • How to Start Dreads with Straight Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Starting Dreads with Straight Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care for Dreads

How to Start Dreads with Straight Hair

The first thing to keep in mind is that dreads require time and patience, no matter what your hair type is. With straight hair, it may take longer to form the locks, and you may have to put in a bit more effort to get them started. However, the end result is worth it, and you can achieve great-looking dreads with straight hair.

1. Choose Your Method

There are several methods you can use to start your dreads with straight hair, including:

  • Twist and Rip Method
  • Backcombing Method
  • Crochet Method
  • Natural Method (also known as neglect method)

Each method has its pros and cons, and you should choose the one that works best for your hair type and personal preference.

2. Prepare Your Hair

Before you start your dreads, make sure your hair is clean and dry. You can wash your hair with a residue-free shampoo to get rid of any product buildup or oils. Avoid using conditioner, as it can make your hair slippery and harder to lock up.

3. Section Your Hair

Sectioning your hair is an essential step in starting dreads. You can use hair ties or clips to separate your hair into small sections, depending on how thick you want your dreads to be. Make sure each section is even in size and that you have enough hair in each section to form a lock.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to start dreads with straight hair:

1. Twist and Rip Method

This method involves twisting and ripping each section of hair to create knots that will eventually form into locks. Here's how to do it:

  1. Section your hair into small sections.
  2. Take one section and divide it into two equal parts.
  3. Twist each part in opposite directions until they start to knot up.
  4. Rip the twisted sections apart, creating a knot in the middle.
  5. Continue twisting and ripping until the entire section has knots all the way down.
  6. Repeat the process on all sections of hair.

2. Backcombing Method

This method involves teasing the hair to create knots that will eventually form into locks. Here's how to do it:

  1. Section your hair into small sections.
  2. Take one section of hair and hold it straight up.
  3. Using a fine-tooth comb, backcomb the hair towards your scalp, creating knots.
  4. Continue backcombing until the entire section has knots all the way down.
  5. Repeat the process on all sections of hair.

3. Crochet Method

This method involves using a crochet hook to pull the hair into knots that will eventually form into locks. Here's how to do it:

  1. Section your hair into small sections.
  2. Take one section of hair and insert the crochet hook into the hair at the root.
  3. Twist the hook and pull it through the hair, creating a knot.
  4. Repeat the process on the same section of hair, working your way down to the end.
  5. Repeat the process on all sections of hair.

4. Natural Method

This method involves letting your hair do its thing and form locks naturally over time. Here's how to do it:

  1. Section your hair into small sections.
  2. Let your hair grow and tangle on its own.
  3. Encourage the process by not brushing or combing your hair.
  4. Over time, your hair will form natural locks.

Tips for Starting Dreads with Straight Hair

Here are some tips to help you start your dreads with straight hair:

  • Use a residue-free shampoo to avoid product buildup.
  • Avoid conditioner, as it can make your hair slippery and harder to lock up.
  • Be patient. Dreads take time to form, especially with straight hair.
  • Maintain your dreads regularly to keep them looking neat and healthy.
  • Consider using a locking gel or wax to help your hair lock up faster.

FAQ

Can I start dreads with straight hair?

Yes, you can start dreads with straight hair. It may take longer to form the locks, but it's still possible.

What is the best method for starting dreads with straight hair?

There is no one-size-fits-all answer to this question, as each method has its pros and cons. Choose the method that works best for your hair type and personal preference.

How long does it take for dreads to form with straight hair?

It depends on your hair type and the method you use. With straight hair, it may take several months to a year or more for your dreads to form fully.

Hair Care for Dreads

Once you've started your dreads, it's important to take good care of them to keep them looking neat and healthy. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using heavy products, as they can weigh down your dreads and make them look greasy.
  • Use a locking gel or wax to help your hair lock up faster.
  • Avoid using a hair dryer, as it can cause your dreads to unravel.
  • Regularly palm roll your dreads to help them maintain their shape.

Starting dreads with straight hair may require some effort and patience, but the end result is worth it. With the right method and hair care, you can achieve great-looking dreads that will turn heads wherever you go.
