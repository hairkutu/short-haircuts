How To Get Rid Of Red Undertones In Blonde Hair

Written by Aprilia Jul 30, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Blonde hair with red undertones can be frustrating for many people. You may have tried different hair products and treatments, but nothing seems to work. In this article, we will provide you with tips and tricks to get rid of those pesky red undertones.

Description

Blonde hair with red undertones can happen due to several reasons. It can be a result of genetics, the use of certain hair products, or even exposure to the sun. These red undertones can make your hair look brassy and faded. Therefore, it is essential to know how to get rid of them.

How to Get Rid of Red Undertones in Blonde Hair

Step 1: Use a Purple Shampoo

Purple shampoo is a great option to neutralize red undertones in blonde hair. The purple pigments in the shampoo counteract the red tones, leaving your hair looking bright and fresh. Use it once a week or more often if needed.

Step 2: Try a Toning Treatment

A toning treatment can also help get rid of red undertones in blonde hair. Mix a toning product with a developer and apply it to your hair. Leave it on for the recommended time and rinse it off. This treatment will help neutralize the red tones and give your hair a cooler shade of blonde.

Step 3: Use a Hair Mask

A hair mask is an excellent way to nourish and hydrate your hair while getting rid of red undertones. Look for a hair mask that contains ingredients like keratin, argan oil, or coconut oil. These ingredients will help repair dry and damaged hair while also neutralizing the red tones.

Tips

- Avoid using hot tools like curling irons and straighteners as they can cause further damage to your hair.

- Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools to protect your hair from damage.

- Wash your hair less frequently to prevent it from drying out and becoming brittle.

FAQ

Q: Is it possible to get rid of red undertones in one treatment?

A: It depends on the severity of the red undertones and the type of treatment you are using. Most treatments will require multiple applications to see significant results.

Q: Can I use regular shampoo instead of purple shampoo?

A: Regular shampoo may not be effective in neutralizing red undertones. Purple shampoo is specifically designed to counteract those tones, making it the best option.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is essential to maintain its health and prevent further damage. Use a hair care routine that includes shampoo, conditioner, and a hair mask. Avoid using harsh chemicals or products that can dry out your hair. Lastly, protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.

With these tips and tricks, you can finally get rid of those annoying red undertones in your blonde hair. Remember to take good care of your hair to maintain its health and vibrancy.
