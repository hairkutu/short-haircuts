Starting Dreads With Long Natural Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Frank Aug 07, 2023 · 7 min read

If you’re thinking about starting dreads with long natural hair, you’ve come to the right place. This guide will provide you with all the information you need to know about starting your locs journey, including how to do it, step by step instructions, tips, FAQs, and hair care tips.

Description

Starting dreads with long natural hair is a process that can take anywhere from a few months to a few years, depending on your hair type and the method you choose. However, the end result is a beautiful and unique hairstyle that is low maintenance and can be styled in a variety of ways.

How to Start Dreads with Long Natural Hair

There are several methods you can use to start your dreadlocks, including:

Backcombing Method

This method involves teasing your hair backwards to create knots that will eventually turn into dreadlocks. To use this method, follow these steps:

  1. Separate your hair into sections using clips or hair ties.
  2. Starting at the roots, tease each section of hair backwards using a comb or dread comb.
  3. Once you’ve teased the section, roll it between your palms to create a dreadlock.
  4. Continue this process until you’ve created dreadlocks throughout your entire head.

Twist and Rip Method

This method involves twisting and ripping your hair to create knots that will eventually turn into dreadlocks. To use this method, follow these steps:

  1. Separate your hair into sections using clips or hair ties.
  2. Using your fingers or a comb, twist each section of hair tightly.
  3. Once the section is twisted, pull it apart in opposite directions to create knots.
  4. Continue this process until you’ve created dreadlocks throughout your entire head.

Freeform Method

This method involves letting your hair section itself naturally and allowing the knots to form on their own. To use this method, simply stop combing or brushing your hair and allow it to grow out. Over time, your hair will naturally begin to form knots and dreadlocks.

Step by Step Instructions

Here is a step by step guide for starting dreads with long natural hair:

  1. Choose a method for starting your dreadlocks.
  2. Separate your hair into sections using clips or hair ties.
  3. Begin backcombing, twisting, or allowing your hair to form knots naturally.
  4. Once a section of hair has formed a knot or dreadlock, roll it tightly between your palms to help it hold its shape.
  5. Continue this process until you’ve created dreadlocks throughout your entire head.
  6. Use a locking gel or wax to help your dreadlocks hold their shape and stay in place.
  7. Continue to care for your dreadlocks by washing them regularly and maintaining them with oils and other hair care products.

Tips for Starting Dreads with Long Natural Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when starting your locs journey:

  • Be patient – it can take several months or even years for your hair to fully dread.
  • Use a locking gel or wax to help your hair hold its shape.
  • Be gentle with your dreadlocks – avoid pulling or tugging on them.
  • Wash your dreadlocks regularly to keep them clean and healthy.
  • Use oils and other hair care products to keep your dreadlocks moisturized.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about starting dreads with long natural hair:

Q: How long does it take for dreadlocks to form?

A: It can take anywhere from a few months to a few years for dreadlocks to form, depending on your hair type and the method you choose.

Q: How do I maintain my dreadlocks?

A: You can maintain your dreadlocks by washing them regularly, using oils and other hair care products, and avoiding pulling or tugging on them.

Q: Can I still style my hair with dreadlocks?

A: Yes, you can still style your hair with dreadlocks. There are many different styles you can create, including updos, braids, and twists.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind when starting dreads with long natural hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Use oils and other hair care products to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools on your hair.
  • Be gentle when handling your hair to avoid breakage.

Starting dreads with long natural hair is a commitment, but the end result is a beautiful and unique hairstyle that is sure to turn heads. Use this guide to help you get started on your locs journey and enjoy the ride!
