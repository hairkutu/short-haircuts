How To Cut Medium Short Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jul 20, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're in need of a haircut but don't want to go to a salon, you can cut your medium short hair at home. Cutting your hair at home can be intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be easy and fun. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to cut medium short hair at home, from start to finish.

Table of Contents

  1. Tools You'll Need
  2. How to Cut Medium Short Hair at Home
  3. Tips for Cutting Your Hair at Home
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair at Home

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair at home, you'll need to gather the right tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Scissors: Use hair-cutting scissors, not regular household scissors.
  • Comb: A wide-tooth comb is best for detangling your hair.
  • Clips: Use hair clips to section off your hair.
  • Mirror: Use a mirror or two to see the back of your head.
  • Spray bottle: Fill a spray bottle with water to help wet your hair.

How to Cut Medium Short Hair at Home

Now that you have all the tools you need, it's time to get started. Here's how to cut your medium short hair at home:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. Clean hair is easier to cut, and it will make the process smoother. Once your hair is dry, comb it out to remove any tangles.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use your comb to section off your hair. Start by parting your hair down the middle, then divide each side into two sections. Clip each section so it's out of the way.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Begin cutting your hair by starting at the back. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair, making sure to cut straight across.

Continue cutting small sections of hair, moving from the back of your head to the front. Make sure to cut each section evenly. Use your comb to check that each section is even.

Step 4: Cut the Sides and Bangs

Once you've cut the back of your hair, move on to the sides and bangs. Take small sections of hair and cut them to the desired length. Use your comb to check that each section is even. For bangs, cut them straight across.

Step 5: Check Your Work

Once you're done cutting your hair, use your comb to check that everything is even. If you notice any uneven sections, trim them until they're even.

Tips for Cutting Your Hair at Home

Cutting your hair at home can be challenging, but with these tips, you can make it easier:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Take breaks if you get tired. Cutting your hair can be tiring, so take breaks if you need to.
  • Use a spray bottle to keep your hair wet. Wet hair is easier to cut.
  • Don't cut too much off at once. It's better to cut a little bit at a time than to cut too much off.
  • Be patient. Cutting your hair at home takes time, so be patient and take your time.

FAQs

Can I cut my own hair?

Yes, you can cut your own hair. It can be challenging, but with the right tools and techniques, you can do it.

What kind of scissors should I use?

Use hair-cutting scissors, not regular household scissors. Hair-cutting scissors are sharper and will give you a cleaner cut.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it loses its shape. On average, you should cut your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair at Home

Once you've cut your hair at home, it's important to take care of it. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair soft and shiny.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain the shape.

Cutting your hair at home can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a great haircut without leaving your house. Follow the steps and tips in this guide, and you'll be able to cut your medium short hair at home with ease.
