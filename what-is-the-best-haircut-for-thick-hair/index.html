What Is The Best Haircut For Thick Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Frank Aug 14, 2023 · 8 min read

Thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. On one hand, it’s voluminous and full, but on the other, it can be difficult to manage and style. Finding the right haircut for thick hair can make all the difference in achieving a look that’s both stylish and easy to maintain. In this article, we’ll explore the best haircuts for thick hair, as well as tips for styling and caring for your locks.

What is Thick Hair?

Thick hair is characterized by an abundance of hair follicles on the scalp, resulting in a full and voluminous mane. Thick hair often has a coarser texture and can be difficult to manage, especially in humid weather. However, with the right haircut and styling techniques, thick hair can be transformed into a gorgeous, head-turning look.

Best Haircuts for Thick Hair

The best haircuts for thick hair are those that work with the natural texture of your locks. A layered cut is ideal for thick hair, as it removes excess weight and adds movement and volume. A blunt cut can also work well for thick hair, but it’s important to avoid a one-length cut that can make your hair appear heavy and weighed down. Below are some of the best haircuts for thick hair:

  • Long Layers
  • Choppy Bob
  • Textured Pixie Cut
  • Shaggy Lob
  • Side-Swept Bangs

When deciding on a haircut for thick hair, it’s important to consider your face shape and personal style. Consult with your hairstylist to determine which cut will work best for you.

How to Style Thick Hair

Styling thick hair can be a challenge, but with the right techniques, it can be a breeze. Here are some tips for styling thick hair:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add lift and body to your locks.
  • Blow-dry your hair with a round brush to add volume and movement.
  • Use a texturizing spray or mousse to enhance your hair’s natural texture.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Opt for hairstyles that work with your natural texture, such as beachy waves or messy buns.

Step-by-Step Guide to Haircut for Thick Hair

If you’re interested in cutting your own hair at home or simply want to understand the process for your next salon visit, here’s a step-by-step guide to getting a haircut for thick hair:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Divide your hair into sections using clips.
  3. Start at the back of your head and work toward the front.
  4. Using sharp scissors, trim the ends of each section at a slight angle to create layers.
  5. Continue working around your head, trimming each section to create layers.
  6. Once you’ve finished creating layers, trim any stray hairs to create a cohesive look.
  7. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Thick Hair

Cutting thick hair can be tricky, but with the right tips and techniques, it can be a success. Here are some tips for cutting thick hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to larger ones.
  • Create layers to remove excess weight and add movement to your hair.
  • Avoid cutting your hair when it’s wet, as it can be more difficult to see the true length.
  • Consult with a professional hairstylist for best results.

FAQ

Q: Can I get a short haircut if I have thick hair?

A: Yes, you can still get a short haircut if you have thick hair. A layered pixie cut or bob can work well for thick hair, as it removes excess weight and adds movement.

Q: Will a blunt cut work for thick hair?

A: Yes, a blunt cut can work well for thick hair, but it’s important to avoid a one-length cut that can make your hair appear heavy and weighed down. Instead, opt for a layered or textured blunt cut.

Hair Care

Caring for thick hair is essential to maintaining its health and appearance. Here are some tips for caring for your locks:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Avoid using hot tools on a daily basis to prevent damage.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your locks.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s wet, as it can cause breakage.

With the right haircut, styling techniques, and care, thick hair can be transformed into a gorgeous, head-turning look. Whether you opt for a long layered cut, choppy bob, or textured pixie cut, consult with a professional hairstylist to determine which cut will work best for you.
