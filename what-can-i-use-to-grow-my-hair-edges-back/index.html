What Can I Use To Grow My Hair Edges Back

Written by Christine May 27, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you struggling with thinning or receding hair edges? This is a common problem faced by both men and women, and it can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, stress, and certain hairstyles. Fortunately, there are several products and techniques that can help you grow your hair edges back. In this article, we'll explore some of the best options available.

Table of Contents

  • How to Grow Your Hair Edges Back
  • Products to Help Grow Your Hair Edges
  • Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair Edges
  • FAQs About Growing Hair Edges Back
  • Hair Care Practices for Healthy Edges

How to Grow Your Hair Edges Back

The first step in growing your hair edges back is to identify the underlying cause of the problem. If your edges are thinning due to tight hairstyles, you may need to change your styling routine. If stress is causing your hair to fall out, you'll need to take steps to manage your stress levels. Once you've addressed any underlying issues, you can start using products and techniques to help stimulate hair growth.

Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can help to increase blood flow to your hair follicles, which can stimulate hair growth. You can use your fingers to massage your scalp for a few minutes each day, or you can invest in a scalp massager for more targeted stimulation.

Use Essential Oils

Essential oils like peppermint and rosemary can help to stimulate hair growth when applied directly to the scalp. Mix a few drops of your chosen oil with a carrier oil like coconut or jojoba oil, and massage the mixture into your scalp. Leave it on for at least 30 minutes before washing it out.

Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a diet rich in vitamins and nutrients can help to promote healthy hair growth. Make sure you're getting enough protein, iron, and vitamin D, and consider taking a biotin supplement to support hair growth.

Products to Help Grow Your Hair Edges

There are several products on the market that are specifically designed to help grow hair edges. Here are some of the best options:

Minoxidil

Minoxidil is a topical medication that is FDA-approved to treat hair loss. It works by increasing blood flow to the hair follicles, which can help to stimulate hair growth. You can find minoxidil products at your local drugstore, or you can ask your doctor for a prescription-strength version.

Castor Oil

Castor oil is a popular natural remedy for hair growth. It contains ricinoleic acid, which can help to improve circulation and stimulate hair growth. Apply castor oil to your hair edges and massage it into your scalp for a few minutes each day.

Hair Vitamins

There are several hair vitamin supplements on the market that are designed to support healthy hair growth. Look for products that contain biotin, vitamin C, and other hair-healthy nutrients.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair Edges

Once you've started to regrow your hair edges, it's important to take steps to maintain their health. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight braids, weaves, and other hairstyles can put stress on your hair edges and cause them to thin or recede. Opt for looser styles that don't put as much tension on your hair.

Protect Your Hair at Night

Wearing a silk or satin scarf or bonnet at night can help to protect your hair edges while you sleep. These materials are less likely to cause friction and breakage than cotton or other fabrics.

Avoid Heat Damage

Excessive heat styling can damage your hair and cause it to break and thin. Try to limit your use of heat tools like flat irons and blow dryers, and use a heat protectant spray when you do use them.

FAQs About Growing Hair Edges Back

Q: How long does it take to grow hair edges back?

A: The amount of time it takes to regrow hair edges can vary depending on the individual. It may take several months to see noticeable results.

Q: Are there any side effects to using minoxidil?

A: Some people may experience scalp irritation or itching when using minoxidil. If you experience any side effects, stop using the product and talk to your doctor.

Q: Can stress cause hair loss?

A: Yes, stress can contribute to hair loss. When you're under stress, your body produces hormones that can disrupt the hair growth cycle.

Hair Care Practices for Healthy Edges

In addition to using products and techniques to stimulate hair growth, it's important to take good care of your hair overall. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

Wash Your Hair Regularly

Keeping your hair and scalp clean is essential for healthy hair growth. Wash your hair at least once a week, and use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.

Deep Condition Regularly

Deep conditioning treatments can help to moisturize and strengthen your hair, which can prevent breakage and thinning. Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair healthy and strong.

Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims can help to prevent split ends and breakage. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair looking healthy and full.

By following these tips and using the right products and techniques, you can regrow your hair edges and maintain healthy, strong hair. Remember to be patient, as it may take several months to see results. With persistence and consistency, you can achieve the healthy hair you've always wanted.
