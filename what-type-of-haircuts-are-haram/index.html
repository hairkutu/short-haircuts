What Type Of Haircuts Are Haram

Written by Christine Mar 22, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're a Muslim, you may be wondering what type of haircuts are haram. It's important to understand that certain hairstyles are considered haram in Islam, and it's important to know what they are so that you can avoid them.

What Does Haram Mean?

Haram is an Arabic word that means forbidden or prohibited. In Islam, haram refers to anything that is considered sinful or unlawful.

Why Are Certain Haircuts Haram?

Certain haircuts are considered haram in Islam because they imitate the hairstyles of non-Muslims or are associated with a particular group or lifestyle that goes against Islamic values.

The following are some examples of haircuts that are considered haram in Islam:

1. Shaving the Head Partially

Shaving the head partially is not allowed in Islam. This includes shaving only one side of the head, leaving only a patch of hair at the top, or shaving the hair in a particular design.

2. Long Hair

Islam encourages men to keep their hair short and neat. Long hair is associated with non-Muslims and is considered haram. However, it's important to note that keeping hair long for cultural or medical reasons is allowed.

3. Mohawks and Fauxhawks

Mohawks and fauxhawks are considered haram because they imitate the hairstyles of non-Muslims and are associated with rebelliousness and nonconformity.

4. Ponytails and Braids

Ponytails and braids are considered haram for men because they imitate the hairstyles of women. However, it's important to note that keeping hair tied back for functional reasons, such as during physical activity, is allowed.

How to Choose a Halal Haircut

Choosing a halal haircut is important for Muslims who want to follow Islamic values. Here are some tips on how to choose a halal haircut:

1. Keep it Short and Neat

Islam encourages men to keep their hair short and neat. A simple, short haircut that is easy to maintain is the best option.

2. Avoid Imitating Non-Muslims

Avoid imitating the hairstyles of non-Muslims. This includes hairstyles that are associated with a particular group or lifestyle, such as punk or goth.

3. Consider Cultural and Medical Reasons

It's important to consider cultural and medical reasons when choosing a haircut. Keeping hair long for these reasons is allowed in Islam.

FAQs

1. Can I dye my hair?

Dyeing hair is allowed in Islam as long as it is not done to imitate non-Muslims or to change one's identity.

2. Can I shave my head completely?

Shaving the head completely is allowed in Islam, but it's important to do it for the right reasons, such as during the Hajj pilgrimage or to show solidarity with a sick person.

3. Can I have a beard?

Having a beard is encouraged in Islam, but it's important to keep it neat and trimmed.

Hair Care Tips

1. Wash Your Hair Regularly

Washing your hair regularly is important for maintaining healthy hair and scalp.

2. Use Halal Hair Products

Using halal hair products is important for Muslims who want to follow Islamic values. Look for products that are free from alcohol and animal-derived ingredients.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage the hair.

In conclusion, it's important for Muslims to choose a halal haircut that is in line with Islamic values. By avoiding haram hairstyles and following hair care tips, you can maintain healthy and beautiful hair while also following your faith.
