How To Fix Messed Up Hair Dye A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jun 20, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Have you ever tried to color your hair at home and ended up with a disaster? Don't worry, it happens to the best of us. The good news is that there are ways to fix messed up hair dye without having to go to a salon. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of fixing your hair dye, step-by-step.

Description

Messed up hair dye can happen for a number of reasons. You might have left the dye on for too long, used the wrong color, or not followed the instructions properly. Whatever the reason, the result is usually uneven, patchy or too dark hair color. This can be frustrating and even embarrassing, but don't worry - there are ways to fix it.

How to Fix Messed Up Hair Dye

Before we get started, it's important to note that fixing messed up hair dye is not a one-size-fits-all solution. Depending on the severity of the issue, you may need to follow different steps. However, there are a few things you can do to try and fix the problem:

1. Use a Color Remover

If the issue is that the color is too dark, you can use a color remover to lighten it. This is a more gentle solution than bleach and will help to remove some of the color without damaging your hair. However, it's important to note that color removers can only lighten hair by a few shades, so it may not be enough if your hair is significantly darker than you intended.

2. Apply a Toning Shampoo

If your hair has turned brassy or orange, you can use a toning shampoo to neutralize the color. Look for a shampoo that's specifically designed for your hair color (e.g. purple shampoo for blondes) and apply it to wet hair. Leave it on for a few minutes and then rinse it out. Repeat this process until you see the desired results.

3. Use a Color Corrector

If your hair is patchy or uneven, you can use a color corrector to balance out the color. This is a more targeted solution than a color remover and will help to even out the color without damaging your hair. However, it's important to choose the right color corrector for your hair color and to follow the instructions carefully.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the basics of how to fix messed up hair dye, let's go through the step-by-step process:

Step 1: Assess the Damage

Take a good look at your hair and identify the problem areas. Is the color too dark? Is it patchy? Is it brassy? This will help you to choose the right solution.

Step 2: Choose the Right Solution

Based on your assessment, choose the right solution from the options we discussed above. Make sure to follow the instructions carefully and do a patch test if necessary.

Step 3: Apply the Solution

Apply the solution to your hair, making sure to cover all the problem areas. Follow the instructions carefully and leave the solution on for the recommended time.

Step 4: Rinse and Condition

Rinse the solution out of your hair and apply a deep conditioner. This will help to restore moisture and prevent damage.

Tips to Prevent Messed Up Hair Dye

While it's always possible to fix messed up hair dye, it's better to prevent it from happening in the first place. Here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Read the instructions carefully before you start
  • Do a patch test to check for allergies or adverse reactions
  • Choose the right color for your hair type and skin tone
  • Follow the recommended processing time
  • Don't wash your hair immediately after coloring
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to maintain color

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about fixing messed up hair dye:

Can I fix messed up hair dye without going to a salon?

Yes, you can fix messed up hair dye at home using the methods we discussed above. However, if the issue is severe or you're not confident in your ability to fix it yourself, it's always best to seek professional help.

How long should I wait before trying to fix messed up hair dye?

You should wait at least 72 hours before trying to fix messed up hair dye. This will give your hair time to recover from the initial coloring process.

Can I use bleach to fix messed up hair dye?

Bleach is a harsh chemical that can damage your hair if not used correctly. It's best to avoid using bleach unless you're a professional or have experience using it.

Hair Care After Fixing Messed Up Hair Dye

After you've fixed your messed up hair dye, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent further damage. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible
  • Apply a deep conditioning treatment once a week
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine

With these tips and tricks, you can fix messed up hair dye and keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful.
