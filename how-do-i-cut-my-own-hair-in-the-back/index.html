How Do I Cut My Own Hair In The Back

Written by Bardi Jul 25, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking to save some money on haircuts, or just prefer to do things yourself, cutting your own hair can be a great option. However, one of the trickiest parts of cutting your own hair is the back. It can be difficult to see what you're doing, and it's easy to make mistakes. In this article, we'll go over how to cut your own hair in the back, step by step.

Table of Contents

  • How To Cut Your Own Hair in the Back
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

How To Cut Your Own Hair in the Back

The first thing you'll need is a good pair of scissors. Hair-cutting scissors are designed for this purpose, so it's worth investing in a pair. Once you have your scissors, find a mirror that allows you to see the back of your head. You can use a hand mirror or a larger mirror mounted on the wall. Ideally, you'll also want a second mirror that you can place behind you, so you can see the back of your head from different angles.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's how to cut your own hair in the back:

  1. Start by combing your hair straight down your back. Make sure it's as smooth as possible.
  2. Take a small section of hair from the middle of your head, at the nape of your neck. Hold it between your fingers, and cut it straight across. This will be your guide for the rest of your haircut.
  3. Working in small sections, cut your hair straight across, using the guide you created in step 2. Make sure to cut only a small amount of hair at a time, so you don't accidentally cut too much.
  4. Continue cutting small sections of hair, working your way up your head. Use your mirrors to check your progress from different angles.
  5. Once you've cut all the way to the top of your head, check for any stray hairs or uneven sections. Use your scissors to trim any areas that need it.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind:

  • Start with clean, dry hair. Wet hair is harder to cut, and can result in uneven sections.
  • Don't rush. Take your time, and work in small sections.
  • Use hair clips to section off your hair, so you can focus on one area at a time.
  • If you're not sure how much to cut, start with a small amount. You can always cut more later.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own hair in the back:

Can I use regular scissors?

You can, but it's not recommended. Hair-cutting scissors are designed to make clean, even cuts, while regular scissors can result in jagged edges or split ends.

What if I mess up?

If you accidentally cut too much, don't panic. You can always even it out by cutting the rest of your hair to match. If you're really unhappy with the results, you can always go to a professional for help.

How often should I cut my hair?

This depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. As a general rule, most people should get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

Now that you've cut your own hair in the back, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Shampoo and condition your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair soft and shiny.

With these tips in mind, you should be able to successfully cut your own hair in the back. Remember to take your time, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. Happy cutting!
