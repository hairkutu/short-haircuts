Is Crimping Hair In Style 2021

Description

Crimping hair is a hairstyle trend that has made a comeback in recent years. It involves creating small, zigzag-like waves in the hair to add texture and volume. This style was popular in the 80s and 90s, but it has evolved since then to look more modern and chic.

So, is crimping hair in style in 2021? The answer is yes! Crimped hair has been seen on many celebrities and influencers, and it’s a fun way to switch up your hairstyle.

How to Crimp Hair

Crimping hair can be done with a crimping iron or a flat iron. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to avoid damage from the heat.
  3. Divide your hair into sections. The size of the sections will depend on the thickness of your hair.
  4. Take a section of hair and clamp the crimping iron at the root.
  5. Hold the iron in place for a few seconds, then move it down the section of hair, crimping as you go.
  6. Continue this process until you have crimped all sections of hair.
  7. Finish with a hairspray to hold the style in place.

Step-by-Step Guide to Crimp Hair

Here’s a more detailed step-by-step guide to crimping hair:

  1. Wash and dry your hair thoroughly.
  2. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to protect it from the heat of the iron.
  3. Divide your hair into sections. If you have thin hair, you can divide it into two sections. If you have thick hair, divide it into four or more sections.
  4. Take one section of hair and clip the rest out of the way.
  5. Starting from the root, clamp the crimping iron onto the hair and hold it for a few seconds.
  6. Move the iron down the section of hair, crimping as you go.
  7. Repeat steps 4-6 until you have crimped all sections of hair.
  8. Once you have crimped all sections, brush through your hair gently to soften the crimps.
  9. Finish with a light hairspray to hold the style in place.

Tips for Crimping Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when crimping your hair:

  • Use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage from the heat of the iron.
  • Don’t crimp your hair too close to the roots, as this can create a puffy look.
  • For a more natural look, crimp random sections of hair rather than crimping all of it.
  • Use a light hairspray to hold the style in place without weighing down your hair.

FAQs about Crimping Hair

Q: Can I crimp my hair if it’s already curly?

A: Yes, you can. However, crimping curly hair can make it look too voluminous, so it’s best to crimp only certain sections of your hair.

Q: How long will crimped hair last?

A: Crimped hair can last up to several days, depending on how well you maintain it. It’s best to avoid washing your hair for at least 24 hours after crimping and to use a dry shampoo to refresh your hair in between washes.

Q: Can I crimp my hair if it’s damaged or colored?

A: It’s best to avoid crimping your hair if it’s damaged or colored, as crimping can cause further damage and color fading.

Hair Care Tips after Crimping

After crimping your hair, it’s important to take care of it to maintain the style and prevent damage. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid washing your hair for at least 24 hours after crimping.
  • Use a dry shampoo to refresh your hair in between washes.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and frizz.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid using hair ties that can create dents in your hair.

In conclusion, crimping hair is definitely in style in 2021. It’s a fun and easy way to switch up your hairstyle and add texture and volume to your hair. Follow these tips and instructions to achieve the perfect crimped hair look!
