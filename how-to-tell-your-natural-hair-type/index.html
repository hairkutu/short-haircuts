How To Tell Your Natural Hair Type A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Apr 07, 2023 ¬∑ 6 min read

If you're new to the natural hair community, one of the first things you'll need to know is your hair type. Understanding your hair type is crucial for selecting the right hair care products and styling methods. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps of how to tell your natural hair type.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Tell Your Natural Hair Type
  3. Step by Step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description

Before we get into how to tell your natural hair type, let's define what we mean by "hair type." Hair type refers to the texture and curl pattern of your hair. There are four main hair types: type 1, type 2, type 3, and type 4. Each hair type has subcategories based on the size of the curl pattern.

How to Tell Your Natural Hair Type

The easiest way to determine your hair type is to look at your natural hair when it is completely dry, free of product, and has not been manipulated in any way. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Look at the Shape of Your Curl Pattern

The shape of your curl pattern will determine your hair type. Type 1 hair is straight, while type 2 hair has a slight wave. Type 3 hair has a loose curl pattern, and type 4 hair has a tight curl pattern.

Step 2: Determine the Size of Your Curl Pattern

Once you've determined your hair type, you'll need to determine the size of your curl pattern. Type 2 hair has three subcategories: 2A, 2B, and 2C. Type 3 hair has three subcategories: 3A, 3B, and 3C. Type 4 hair has three subcategories: 4A, 4B, and 4C.

Step by Step

Here is a step-by-step guide to determining your natural hair type:

  1. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup.
  2. Allow your hair to air dry completely without using any styling products or heat tools.
  3. Look at the shape of your curl pattern to determine your hair type.
  4. Determine the size of your curl pattern to determine the subcategory of your hair type.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when determining your hair type:

  • Be patient. It may take a few tries to accurately determine your hair type.
  • Ask for help. If you're having trouble determining your hair type, ask a friend or a hair care professional for assistance.
  • Take note of any changes. Your hair type may change over time due to factors such as age, hormones, and lifestyle changes.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about determining your natural hair type:

What if my hair has multiple curl patterns?

If your hair has multiple curl patterns, choose the one that is most prevalent throughout your hair.

What if I have heat damage or chemical damage?

If you have heat damage or chemical damage, it may be more difficult to determine your natural hair type. In this case, it's best to consult with a hair care professional.

What if I have fine hair?

If you have fine hair, your hair type will still fall within one of the four categories. Fine hair simply refers to the thickness of each individual strand of hair.

Hair Care

Once you've determined your natural hair type, you can select the right hair care products and styling methods to keep your hair healthy and beautiful. Remember to be patient and gentle with your hair, and always use products that are designed for your specific hair type.

Now that you know how to tell your natural hair type, you're one step closer to achieving your healthiest, most beautiful hair yet!
