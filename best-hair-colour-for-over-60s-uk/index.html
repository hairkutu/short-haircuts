The Best Hair Colour For Over 60S Uk A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Sep 06, 2023 Â· 11 min read

Welcome to our comprehensive guide on the best hair colour for over 60s UK. If you are in your 60s or over, you might be wondering what hair colour would suit you best. In this article, we'll explore the different options available and provide tips on how to achieve the perfect look. Whether you want to cover grey hair, add depth and dimension, or simply try something new, we've got you covered. Let's get started!

Description

As we age, our hair naturally loses pigmentation and becomes grey or white. While some people embrace their grey locks, others prefer to cover them up. However, choosing the right hair colour can be tricky. You want something that complements your skin tone, enhances your features, and looks natural. Plus, you don't want to damage your hair or scalp in the process. That's why it's important to understand your options and consult a professional hairdresser if needed.

How to Choose the Best Hair Colour for Over 60s UK

Choosing the best hair colour for over 60s UK depends on several factors, including your skin tone, eye colour, natural hair colour, and personal preference. Here are some tips to help you make the right choice:

Consider Your Skin Tone

Your skin tone can influence which hair colours suit you best. If you have warm undertones, such as yellow or peach, you might look better in golden blondes, caramel browns, or copper reds. If you have cool undertones, such as pink or blue, you might suit ash blondes, chocolate browns, or burgundy reds. If you're not sure about your undertones, ask a professional hairdresser or use online tools to find out.

Think About Your Eye Colour

Your eye colour can also affect which hair colours complement you. For example, if you have blue eyes, you might want to try warm browns, auburns, or honey blondes to bring out the blue. If you have green eyes, you might suit cool browns, ash blondes, or reds with blue undertones. If you have brown eyes, you have more flexibility, as most colours will suit you.

Assess Your Natural Hair Colour

Your natural hair colour can give you clues about what shades will look best on you. If you have cool-toned hair, such as ash blondes or greys, you might want to stick to cool colours to avoid clashing. If you have warm-toned hair, such as golden blondes or chestnut browns, you might want to try warm colours to enhance your natural hue.

Consider Your Lifestyle

When choosing a hair colour, it's important to consider your lifestyle and how much maintenance you're willing to do. If you lead an active lifestyle or don't have time for frequent touch-ups, you might want to choose a low-maintenance colour that grows out gracefully. On the other hand, if you enjoy experimenting with different looks, you might want to try a bold or trendy colour.

Step by Step Guide: How to Dye Your Hair at Home

If you've decided to dye your hair at home, here's a step-by-step guide to help you:

Step 1: Choose the Right Hair Dye

Choose a hair dye that suits your hair type, colour, and desired effect. Read the instructions carefully and do a patch test to check for allergies or irritation.

Step 2: Prepare Your Hair and Workspace

Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and towel-dry it. Put on old clothes and gloves to avoid staining. Cover your workspace with newspaper or plastic to protect it.

Step 3: Mix and Apply the Dye

Follow the instructions to mix the dye and apply it evenly to your hair, section by section. Use a brush or comb to distribute the dye and avoid overlapping.

Step 4: Wait and Rinse

Wait for the recommended time and check your hair regularly to avoid over-processing. Rinse your hair with warm water until the water runs clear. Use a conditioner to restore moisture and shine.

Step 5: Style and Enjoy

Style your hair as usual and enjoy your new look. Take care of your hair by using gentle products, avoiding heat styling, and protecting it from the sun.

Tips for Maintaining Your Hair Colour

Once you've dyed your hair, it's important to maintain the colour and keep it looking fresh. Here are some tips to help you:

Use Colour-Safe Products

Use shampoo and conditioner formulated for coloured hair to prevent fading and dryness. Avoid products containing sulphates, which can strip the colour.

Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools, such as hairdryers, straighteners, or curling irons, as they can damage your hair and fade the colour. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray and low heat settings.

Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or scarf, or using a UV-protectant spray. The sun can bleach your hair and make the colour fade faster.

Touch Up Your Roots

If you have grey hair, you might need to touch up your roots every few weeks to maintain the colour. Use a root touch-up kit or visit a professional hairdresser for a quick fix.

FAQs

What is the Best Hair Colour for Over 60s UK?

The best hair colour for over 60s UK depends on your skin tone, eye colour, natural hair colour, and personal preference. Some popular options include warm blondes, caramel browns, auburns, and burgundy reds.

Can I Dye My Hair at Home?

Yes, you can dye your hair at home, but it's important to choose the right hair dye, prepare your hair and workspace, follow the instructions carefully, and do a patch test first. If you're not confident, it's best to visit a professional hairdresser.

How Often Should I Touch Up My Hair Colour?

It depends on your hair growth rate and the type of hair dye you use. Some dyes last longer than others, and some require more frequent touch-ups. As a general rule, you might need to touch up your roots every 4-6 weeks.

Conclusion

Choosing the best hair colour for over 60s UK can be a fun and rewarding experience. By considering your skin tone, eye colour, natural hair colour, and lifestyle, you can find a colour that suits you and enhances your features. Whether you choose to dye your hair at home or visit a professional hairdresser, remember to take care of your hair and maintain the colour to keep it looking fresh and vibrant. We hope our guide has been helpful, and we wish you all the best in your hair colour journey!
