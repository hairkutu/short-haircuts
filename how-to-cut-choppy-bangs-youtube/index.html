How To Cut Choppy Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Aug 17, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you ready for a change in your hairstyle? Cutting choppy bangs can be a great way to switch up your look and add some edge to your hair. In this article, we will provide a step-by-step guide on how to cut choppy bangs, as well as share some tips and frequently asked questions. Plus, we've included a helpful YouTube tutorial to guide you through the process. Let's get started!

How to Cut Choppy Bangs

Cutting choppy bangs can seem intimidating, but it's actually a simple process that you can do at home. Here's how:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the necessary tools. You will need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • A comb
  • Clips to section off your hair
  • A mirror

Make sure your scissors are sharp to ensure a clean cut.

Step 2: Choose Your Bang Length

Decide on the length of your bangs. Keep in mind that choppy bangs are typically shorter than regular bangs and have more texture. They can be anywhere from eyebrow-length to chin-length.

Step 3: Section Off Your Hair

Section off the hair that you want to cut into bangs. Use clips to hold the rest of your hair out of the way.

Step 4: Cut Your Bangs

Starting in the middle of your forehead, use your comb to pull a small section of hair straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut straight across with your scissors. Repeat this process until you have cut all of your bangs.

For choppy bangs, you can use a technique called point cutting. This involves holding your scissors vertically instead of horizontally and cutting into the hair at an angle. This will create a textured, choppy look.

Step 5: Style Your Bangs

Once you've cut your bangs, style them to your liking. You can blow dry them straight or use a curling iron to add some texture.

Tips for Cutting Choppy Bangs

Cutting your own bangs can be nerve-wracking, but these tips will help you achieve the perfect choppy look:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.
  • Don't cut your bangs too short. You can always go back and cut more later.
  • Use point cutting to create a choppy, textured look.
  • Be patient and take your time. Rushing through the process can lead to mistakes.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting choppy bangs:

How do I know if choppy bangs will look good on me?

Choppy bangs can look great on a variety of face shapes and hair types. If you're unsure, consult with a hairstylist or try on a wig with choppy bangs to get an idea of how they will look on you.

Can I cut choppy bangs if I have curly hair?

Yes! Choppy bangs can look great on curly hair. Just make sure to cut them when your hair is dry, as curly hair can shrink up when it dries.

How often do I need to trim my choppy bangs?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your bangs start to look overgrown. On average, you should trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care for Choppy Bangs

Now that you've cut your choppy bangs, it's important to take care of them. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your bangs regularly to prevent them from becoming oily.
  • Blow dry your bangs after washing them to prevent them from sticking to your forehead.
  • Use a dry shampoo in between washes to keep your bangs looking fresh.
  • Avoid touching your bangs too much, as this can make them look greasy.
