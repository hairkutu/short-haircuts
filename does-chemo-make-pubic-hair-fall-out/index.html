Does Chemo Make Pubic Hair Fall Out

Written by Bardi Jul 14, 2023 Â· 6 min read

When undergoing chemotherapy, it is common for patients to experience hair loss. This can include hair loss on the scalp, eyebrows, eyelashes, and even body hair. But what about pubic hair? Does chemo make pubic hair fall out? In this article, we will explore this question, as well as provide tips for caring for your hair during and after chemotherapy.

Table of Contents

  • FAQ
  • How to Care for Your Hair During Chemo
  • Step-by-Step Guide for Hair Care After Chemo
  • Tips for Hair Care During and After Chemo
  • Conclusion

FAQ

Does chemo make pubic hair fall out?

Yes, chemo can cause pubic hair loss. This is because chemotherapy drugs target rapidly dividing cells, which includes hair follicle cells. The amount of hair loss can vary from person to person, but it is common to experience hair loss in the pubic area, as well as other areas of the body.

When does pubic hair fall out during chemo?

Pubic hair loss typically occurs within the first few weeks of starting chemotherapy. However, it can also occur later on in treatment. The amount of hair loss can also vary depending on the individual and the specific chemotherapy drugs being used.

How to Care for Your Hair During Chemo

During chemotherapy, it is important to take care of your hair to minimize hair loss and promote healthy regrowth. Here are some tips:

  • Use a mild shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using hot styling tools
  • Avoid coloring or perming your hair
  • Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair
  • Consider wearing a wig or scarf to protect your scalp from the sun and cold

Step-by-Step Guide for Hair Care After Chemo

After chemotherapy, your hair may take some time to grow back. Here is a step-by-step guide for caring for your hair after chemo:

  1. Wait for at least two weeks after your last chemotherapy treatment before washing your hair
  2. Use a mild shampoo and conditioner
  3. Gently massage your scalp to promote circulation
  4. Avoid using hot styling tools
  5. Avoid coloring or perming your hair for at least six months
  6. Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair
  7. Consider a short hairstyle to make your hair look thicker and fuller
  8. Wear a hat or scarf to protect your scalp from the sun and cold
  9. Eat a healthy diet to promote hair growth
  10. Be patient - it may take several months for your hair to grow back completely

Tips for Hair Care During and After Chemo

Here are some additional tips for caring for your hair during and after chemotherapy:

  • Use a satin pillowcase to reduce hair breakage
  • Avoid using hair accessories that are tight or pull on your hair
  • Consider using a scalp cooling cap during chemotherapy to minimize hair loss
  • Use a wide-toothed comb instead of a brush to avoid pulling on your hair
  • Use a gentle detangling spray to make brushing easier
  • Stay hydrated to promote overall hair health

Conclusion

While it is common to experience pubic hair loss during chemotherapy, there are steps you can take to minimize hair loss and promote healthy regrowth. By using gentle hair care products, avoiding hot styling tools, and being patient, you can help your hair recover after chemotherapy. If you have any concerns about hair loss or hair care during chemotherapy, be sure to talk to your healthcare provider or a hair care specialist.
