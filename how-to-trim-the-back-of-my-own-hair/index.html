How To Trim The Back Of My Own Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Jul 25, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of spending money on haircuts or relying on someone else to trim the back of your hair? With a bit of practice and the right tools, you can learn how to trim the back of your own hair at home. In this article, we'll walk you through the process step by step and share some tips for success.

Equipment Needed

Before we get started, let's make sure you have the right tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Clippers or scissors
  • Comb
  • Mirror
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Haircutting cape or towel
  • Spray bottle with water

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. Comb out any tangles and make sure your hair is completely dry. If your hair is curly, you may want to straighten it first to make it easier to see and cut.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using hair ties or clips, section off the top of your hair and the sides, leaving only the back section loose. This will make it easier to see what you're doing and prevent you from accidentally cutting too much from the sides.

Step 3: Wet Your Hair

Spray the back section of your hair with water to make it easier to comb and cut.

Step 4: Comb Your Hair

Using a comb, separate a small section of hair from the back of your head. Comb it straight down, away from your head.

Step 5: Trim Your Hair

Using either clippers or scissors, trim the ends of the hair at the desired length. Be sure to cut straight across and avoid cutting too much at once. It's always better to cut too little than too much.

Step 6: Move to the Next Section

Continue working in small sections, combing the hair down and trimming the ends. Work from the bottom of your hairline to the top, using the mirror to check your progress.

Step 7: Check for Evenness

Once you've finished trimming the back of your hair, remove the hair ties or clips and check for evenness. If you notice any uneven areas, make small adjustments until it looks even.

Step 8: Clean Up

Once you're happy with the length and evenness of your hair, remove the haircutting cape or towel and clean up any loose hairs.

Tips

  • Start with small snips and work your way up to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress and make sure your hair is even.
  • Don't rush! Take your time and be patient.
  • If you're using clippers, start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter length.
  • If you're not confident with scissors, try using clippers instead.

FAQ

Can I use regular household scissors to trim my hair?

While you can use regular scissors, we recommend investing in a pair of hair cutting scissors. These are specifically designed for cutting hair and will give you better results.

How often should I trim the back of my hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and the style you're going for. As a general rule, we recommend trimming the back of your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care

Trimming the back of your hair is just one part of a healthy hair care routine. To keep your hair looking its best, be sure to:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo
  • Use conditioner to keep your hair soft and shiny
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as blow dryers and flat irons
  • Eat a healthy diet with plenty of vitamins and minerals
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you'll be able to trim the back of your own hair like a pro. Happy cutting!
