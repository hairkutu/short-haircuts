How To Cut Your Own Hair To Medium Length A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 01, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking to save some money and cut your own hair to medium length, you've come to the right place. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a salon-worthy haircut right in your own home. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of cutting your own hair to medium length, from start to finish. You'll learn what tools you'll need, how to prepare your hair, and step-by-step instructions for cutting your hair. Let's get started!

Tools You'll Need

Before you get started, you'll need to gather a few tools:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Thinning shears (optional)
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • A handheld mirror or a larger mirror you can use to see the back of your head

How to Prepare Your Hair

Before you start cutting, you'll need to prepare your hair:

  1. Wash and dry your hair as you normally would.
  2. Comb your hair to remove any tangles or knots.
  3. Section your hair into four parts: the front, the back, and two sides. Use hair clips to hold each section in place.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Now it's time to start cutting your hair:

  1. Start with the front section of your hair. Take a small section of hair between your fingers, hold it straight up, and cut the ends straight across. Repeat this process for the rest of the front section.
  2. Move to the back section of your hair. Take a small section of hair between your fingers, hold it straight up, and cut the ends straight across. Use a handheld mirror or a larger mirror to check the back of your head and make sure the length is even.
  3. Move to the sides of your hair. Take a small section of hair between your fingers, hold it straight up, and cut the ends straight across. Repeat this process for both sides.
  4. If you want to add some texture or remove some bulk, use thinning shears to cut into the ends of your hair.

Tips

Here are a few tips to help you achieve the perfect medium-length haircut:

  • Start with small sections of hair and cut slowly. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.
  • Use the right tools. Sharp hair cutting scissors and thinning shears will make the job much easier.
  • Check your work in a mirror. Use a handheld mirror or a larger mirror to check the back of your head and make sure the length is even.
  • Don't cut too much at once. It's better to take your time and cut a little bit at a time than to cut too much and have to wait for your hair to grow back.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own hair to medium length:

Can I cut my own hair to medium length if I've never done it before?

Yes, you can! Just take your time, start with small sections of hair, and cut slowly.

Do I need to use thinning shears?

No, thinning shears are optional. They can help to remove bulk and add texture, but you can achieve a great haircut without them.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always go back and fix it later. If you're really unhappy with the results, you can always visit a professional stylist to have your hair fixed.

Hair Care

Now that you've cut your own hair to medium length, it's important to take care of it:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Condition your hair to keep it soft and smooth.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Visit a professional stylist every 6-8 weeks to have your hair trimmed.

Cutting your own hair to medium length can be a great way to save money and achieve a stylish haircut. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a salon-worthy look right in your own home. Just remember to take your time, start with small sections of hair, and cut slowly. Good luck!
