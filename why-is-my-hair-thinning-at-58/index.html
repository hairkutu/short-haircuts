Why Is My Hair Thinning At 58

Written by Christine Jul 15, 2023 Â· 6 min read

As we age, it's not uncommon to notice changes in our hair. For many people, this includes thinning hair. If you're wondering why your hair is thinning at 58, you're not alone. In this blog post, we'll explore some of the reasons why hair thinning happens and offer tips for how to care for your hair as you age.

Age-related hair thinning

One of the most common reasons for hair thinning as we age is simply that our hair follicles shrink over time. This can lead to hair that is less dense and appears thinner. Additionally, as we age, our hair may become more brittle and prone to breakage, further contributing to thinning hair.

Hormonal changes

Changes in hormones can also play a role in hair thinning. As we age, our bodies naturally produce less estrogen and progesterone, which can lead to hair loss. Menopause, in particular, can be a time of significant hormonal changes for women, which may lead to hair thinning or hair loss.

Nutrition and hair health

Good nutrition is essential for healthy hair. As we age, it's important to make sure we're getting enough protein, vitamins, and minerals to support hair growth. A diet that is lacking in these nutrients can contribute to hair thinning and hair loss.

Stress and hair loss

Chronic stress can also contribute to hair loss. When we're under stress, our bodies produce more of the hormone cortisol, which can lead to hair thinning and hair loss. Finding ways to manage stress, such as through meditation or exercise, can help support healthy hair growth.

Genetics and hair loss

Finally, genetics can also play a role in hair thinning. If you have a family history of hair loss or thinning hair, you may be more likely to experience this yourself as you age.

Hair care tips for thinning hair

If you're experiencing hair thinning or hair loss, there are several steps you can take to care for your hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is formulated for thinning hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as curling irons or straighteners, which can damage hair and lead to breakage.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles, which can pull on hair and lead to breakage.
  • Eat a healthy and balanced diet that is rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals.
  • Consider using hair growth products, such as minoxidil, which can help promote hair growth.

FAQs about hair thinning at 58

Q: Is hair thinning at 58 normal?

A: Yes, hair thinning is a common occurrence as we age. It's estimated that around 50% of women over the age of 50 experience some degree of hair thinning or hair loss.

Q: Can hair thinning be reversed?

A: In some cases, hair thinning can be reversed with the use of hair growth products or changes in diet and lifestyle. However, it's important to talk to a healthcare provider to rule out any underlying medical conditions that may be contributing to hair loss.

Q: Are there any medical treatments for hair thinning?

A: Yes, there are several medical treatments for hair thinning, including prescription medications and hair transplant surgery. Talk to a healthcare provider to determine which treatment option may be right for you.


Overall, hair thinning is a common occurrence as we age. By taking steps to care for your hair and overall health, you can promote healthy hair growth and support thicker, fuller hair.
