Which Dog Breeds Shed Less A Guide To Low Shedding Dogs

Written by William May 11, 2023 · 9 min read

Are you tired of constantly vacuuming up dog hair from your floors and furniture? Or maybe you or someone in your household has allergies to dog hair? Whatever your reason may be, finding a low-shedding dog breed can be a game-changer. In this article, we will discuss which dog breeds shed less, how to care for their hair, and answer some frequently asked questions about low-shedding dogs.

Table of Contents

  1. Dog Breeds That Shed Less
  2. How to Care for Low-Shedding Dogs
  3. Step-by-Step Guide to Grooming Low-Shedding Dogs
  4. Tips for Living with Low-Shedding Dogs
  5. FAQs about Low-Shedding Dogs

Dog Breeds That Shed Less

While no dog breed is completely hypoallergenic or non-shedding, there are certainly breeds that shed less than others. Here are some of the most popular low-shedding dog breeds:

1. Poodle

The Poodle is a classic low-shedding dog breed that comes in three sizes: Standard, Miniature, and Toy. Their curly, dense coat requires regular grooming, but it does not shed much. Poodles are also known for being intelligent and easy to train.

2. Bichon Frise

The Bichon Frise is a small, fluffy dog breed that is known for being affectionate and playful. They have a soft, curly coat that does not shed much but requires regular grooming to prevent matting.

3. Shih Tzu

The Shih Tzu is a small, adorable dog breed that is known for their long, silky hair. While they do shed, their hair is so long that it often gets caught in their coat instead of falling out. Regular grooming is necessary to prevent matting and tangling.

4. Maltese

The Maltese is a small, elegant dog breed that is known for their long, white hair. They shed very little but require regular grooming to prevent matting and tangling. Maltese dogs are also known for being affectionate and playful.

5. Portuguese Water Dog

The Portuguese Water Dog is a medium-sized dog breed that is known for their curly, non-shedding coat. They were originally bred for fishing and have webbed feet, making them excellent swimmers. Portuguese Water Dogs require regular grooming to prevent matting.

How to Care for Low-Shedding Dogs

Caring for low-shedding dogs involves regular grooming and coat maintenance. Here are some tips:

1. Brush Your Dog's Coat Regularly

Regular brushing helps to remove loose hair and prevent matting and tangling. The frequency of brushing will depend on your dog's coat type and length. Poodles, for example, may require daily brushing to prevent matting.

2. Bathe Your Dog as Needed

Low-shedding dogs do not need to be bathed as frequently as high-shedding dogs. However, they do still require regular baths to keep their coat clean and healthy. The frequency of bathing will depend on your dog's activity level and lifestyle.

3. Trim Your Dog's Hair

Low-shedding dogs often have long hair that can easily become tangled and matted. Regular trimming helps to prevent this and keeps their coat looking neat and tidy. The frequency of trimming will depend on your dog's coat type and length.

Step-by-Step Guide to Grooming Low-Shedding Dogs

Grooming low-shedding dogs can seem overwhelming, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be easy and enjoyable. Here is a step-by-step guide:

1. Gather Your Tools

You will need a slicker brush, a metal comb, scissors, and clippers if you plan on trimming your dog's hair.

2. Brush Your Dog's Coat

Start by brushing your dog's coat to remove any loose hair and prevent matting. Use a slicker brush for curly coats and a metal comb for straight coats.

3. Trim Your Dog's Hair

If your dog's hair is getting too long, use scissors or clippers to trim it to the desired length. Be careful not to cut too much or too close to the skin.

4. Clean Your Dog's Ears

Use a cotton ball and ear cleaner to gently clean your dog's ears. Be careful not to insert anything into their ear canal.

5. Brush Your Dog's Teeth

Use a dog toothbrush and toothpaste to brush your dog's teeth. This helps to prevent dental problems and bad breath.

Tips for Living with Low-Shedding Dogs

Living with low-shedding dogs can be a joy, but it does require some adjustments. Here are some tips:

1. Invest in a Good Vacuum

Even low-shedding dogs will still shed some hair. Investing in a good vacuum can make cleaning up easier and more efficient.

2. Keep Your Dog's Coat Clean

Regular baths and grooming help to keep your dog's coat clean and healthy, which can reduce shedding.

3. Use Pet-Friendly Cleaning Products

When cleaning your home, use pet-friendly cleaning products that are safe for your dog and the environment.

FAQs about Low-Shedding Dogs

1. Are low-shedding dogs hypoallergenic?

No dog breed is completely hypoallergenic, but low-shedding dogs are less likely to trigger allergies because they shed less.

2. Do low-shedding dogs require less grooming?

No. Low-shedding dogs require regular grooming to prevent matting and tangling.

3. Can low-shedding dogs live in apartments?

Yes. Many low-shedding dog breeds are well-suited for apartment living due to their small size and low exercise requirements.


Overall, low-shedding dogs can make great companions for those looking for a low-maintenance pet. With regular grooming and care, their coats can remain healthy and beautiful while shedding less. Consider one of the breeds mentioned in this article if you are in the market for a low-shedding dog.
