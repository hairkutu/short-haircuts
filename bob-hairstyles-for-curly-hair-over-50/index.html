Bob Hairstyles For Curly Hair Over 50 A Guide To A Chic Look

Written by William May 19, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're over 50 and have curly hair, you might think that there are limited hairstyle options available for you. But worry not, because bob hairstyles are the perfect choice for women with curly hair over 50. Bobs are versatile, easy to maintain, and they can be customized to suit your face shape and personal style. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of choosing and styling a bob hairstyle for your curly hair.

How to Choose the Right Bob Hairstyle for Your Curly Hair

Before going to a salon or picking up those scissors, it's important to choose the right bob hairstyle that complements your curly hair texture, face shape, and lifestyle. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Consider Your Face Shape

Not all bob hairstyles suit every face shape. For instance, if you have a round face, opt for a longer bob that falls just below the chin to create an illusion of length. If you have a square face, go for a layered bob with soft edges to soften the jawline.

Think About Your Hair Texture

Curly hair has varying textures, from loose waves to tight coils. Take into account your hair's texture when choosing a bob hairstyle. For instance, if you have tight coils, a layered bob can add volume and movement to your hair. If you have loose waves, a blunt bob can give your hair structure and definition.

Consider Your Lifestyle

Your lifestyle plays a role in choosing the right bob hairstyle. If you have an active lifestyle, go for a low-maintenance bob that requires minimal styling. On the other hand, if you have more time to spend on your hair, a layered bob with bangs can create a sophisticated look.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Your Bob Hairstyle

Now that you've chosen the perfect bob hairstyle, it's time to style it. Here's a step-by-step guide to achieving a chic look:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products that are suitable for curly hair. Avoid using hot water, as it can dry out your hair and cause frizz. Instead, use lukewarm water to wash your hair.

Step 2: Apply Styling Products

Apply a leave-in conditioner or a curl defining cream to your damp hair to enhance your curls and reduce frizz.

Step 3: Blow-Dry Your Hair

Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair. This will help maintain the natural curl pattern and prevent frizz.

Step 4: Style Your Bob

Once your hair is dry, use a curling iron to define any loose curls or waves. You can also use a straightener to create sleek, straight hair. Experiment with different styles to find what suits you best.

Tips for Maintaining Your Bob Hairstyle

To keep your bob hairstyle looking fresh and stylish, here are some tips to follow:

Get Regular Trims

Regular trims every six to eight weeks will keep your bob looking sharp and prevent split ends.

Protect Your Hair from Heat Damage

Use heat protectant products when using hot styling tools to prevent heat damage.

Use Hair Accessories

Add some flair to your bob hairstyle by using hair accessories such as headbands, clips, or scarves.

FAQs

Can I dye my curly bob hair?

Yes, you can dye your curly bob hair. However, it's important to use hair dye products that are suitable for curly hair and to follow the instructions carefully.

How do I prevent frizz in my curly bob hair?

To prevent frizz, use hair products that are designed for curly hair, such as curl defining creams and leave-in conditioners. Avoid using hot styling tools and minimize touching your hair throughout the day.

What is the best length for a curly bob hairstyle?

The best length for a curly bob hairstyle depends on your face shape and hair texture. Generally, a bob that falls just below the chin or at the shoulder length suits most face shapes and hair textures.

Hair Care for Curly Bobs

Curly hair requires extra care and attention to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy curly hair:

Use Sulfate-Free Shampoo

Sulfate-free shampoo is gentle on curly hair and won't strip away its natural oils.

Deep Condition Regularly

Deep condition your hair once a week to nourish and hydrate your curls.

Avoid Brushing Your Hair

Brushing your curly hair can cause breakage and frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.

Now that you know how to choose, style, and maintain a curly bob hairstyle, it's time to hit the salon and get that chic look you've always wanted. Remember to take care of your hair and enjoy your new hairstyle.
