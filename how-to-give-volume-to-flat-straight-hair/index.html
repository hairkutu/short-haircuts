How To Give Volume To Flat Straight Hair

Written by Aprilia Jul 08, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Do you have flat, straight hair that lacks volume? It can be frustrating to try and style your hair, only for it to fall flat within minutes. But don't worry, there are tricks and techniques you can use to give your hair some much-needed volume. In this article, we will cover how to give volume to flat straight hair, step-by-step instructions, tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Give Volume to Flat Straight Hair
  2. Step-by-Step Instructions
  3. Tips for Adding Volume to Flat Straight Hair
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care Advice for Flat Straight Hair

How to Give Volume to Flat Straight Hair

The first step in giving your flat, straight hair some volume is to start with clean hair. Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add volume and body to your hair. Once your hair is clean, apply a volumizing mousse to your roots. This will help lift your hair and give it some volume.

Next, blow-dry your hair upside down. This will help lift your roots and add volume. Use a round brush to lift your hair and create volume at the crown of your head. Once your hair is dry, use a texturizing spray to add some texture and hold to your hair.

If you have straight hair, you can also use a curling iron to add volume. Take small sections of your hair and curl them away from your face. Once you have curled your entire head, flip your head upside down and shake out your curls. This will give your hair a tousled, voluminous look.

Products to Use:

When trying to add volume to your flat, straight hair, it's important to use the right products. Here are a few products that can help give your hair some volume:

  • Volumizing shampoo and conditioner
  • Volumizing mousse
  • Texturizing spray
  • Dry shampoo
  • Hairspray

Step-by-Step Instructions

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to give volume to flat, straight hair:

  1. Start with clean hair using a volumizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Apply a volumizing mousse to your roots.
  3. Blow-dry your hair upside down using a round brush to lift your hair and create volume at the crown of your head.
  4. Once your hair is dry, use a texturizing spray to add some texture and hold to your hair.
  5. If you have straight hair, use a curling iron to add volume by curling small sections of your hair away from your face.
  6. Flip your head upside down and shake out your curls to give your hair a tousled, voluminous look.
  7. Finish with a light hairspray to hold your style in place.

Tips for Adding Volume to Flat Straight Hair

Here are some tips for adding volume to your flat, straight hair:

  • Blow-dry your hair upside down to lift your roots and create volume at the crown of your head.
  • Use a round brush when blow-drying to add volume and body to your hair.
  • Apply a volumizing mousse to your roots before blow-drying to give your hair some lift.
  • Use a texturizing spray to add texture and hold to your hair.
  • Try using a dry shampoo to add volume and texture to second-day hair.
  • If you have straight hair, use a curling iron to add volume and texture.
  • Flip your head upside down and shake out your curls to give your hair a tousled, voluminous look.
  • Finish with a light hairspray to hold your style in place.

FAQs

Q: Can I add volume to my hair without using heat?

A: Yes, you can add volume to your hair without using heat. Try using a volumizing shampoo and conditioner, a volumizing mousse, and a texturizing spray to add volume and hold to your hair.

Q: How often should I wash my hair to maintain volume?

A: It's best to wash your hair every other day to maintain volume. Washing your hair too often can strip your hair of its natural oils, which can lead to flat, lifeless hair.

Q: Can I use hairspray to add volume to my hair?

A: Yes, you can use hairspray to add volume to your hair. Look for a light-hold hairspray that won't weigh your hair down or make it feel crunchy.

Hair Care Advice for Flat Straight Hair

If you have flat, straight hair, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and prevent breakage. Here are some hair care tips for flat straight hair:

  • Avoid using hot tools on a daily basis, as this can lead to damage and breakage.
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using hot tools to protect your hair from damage.
  • Avoid using heavy conditioners or hair masks, as this can weigh your hair down and make it look flat.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, as this will prevent breakage and damage.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can lead to breakage.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol, as this can dry out your hair and make it look flat.

By following these tips and techniques, you can add volume and body to your flat, straight hair. Experiment with different products and styling techniques to find what works best for your hair type and texture.
