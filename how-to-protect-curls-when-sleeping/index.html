How To Protect Curls When Sleeping Tips And Tricks

Written by Bardi Jul 17, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you have curly hair, you know how important it is to keep your curls intact. But what happens when you go to bed and wake up with flattened or frizzy curls? Don't worry, we've got you covered. Here are some tips and tricks on how to protect your curls when sleeping.

How to Protect Curls When Sleeping

Sleeping can be a nightmare for curly hair. The tossing and turning can create friction and cause your curls to become frizzy or flattened. Here are some ways to protect your curls when sleeping.

Use a Silk or Satin Pillowcase

One of the best ways to protect your curls is to use a silk or satin pillowcase. Unlike cotton, silk and satin are gentle on your hair and won't create friction. This means your curls will stay intact and won't become frizzy or flattened.

Use a Silk or Satin Bonnet

If you don't want to invest in a silk or satin pillowcase, you can use a silk or satin bonnet instead. This will protect your curls from rubbing against your pillow and becoming frizzy.

Pineapple Your Curls

Another way to protect your curls when sleeping is to pineapple them. To pineapple your curls, gather all of your hair on the top of your head and secure it with a scrunchie. This will keep your curls intact and prevent them from getting flattened.

Braid Your Hair

If you have longer hair, you can braid your hair before going to bed. This will keep your curls intact and prevent them from getting flattened. You can also try different braiding techniques, such as French braids or Dutch braids, to create different curl patterns.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step by step guide on how to protect your curls when sleeping.

Step 1: Brush Your Hair

Brush your hair before going to bed to remove any tangles or knots. This will make it easier to style your hair in the morning.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to keep it moisturized and prevent frizz.

Step 3: Choose a Protective Style

Choose a protective style that works best for your hair, such as using a silk or satin pillowcase, wearing a silk or satin bonnet, pineapple your curls, or braiding your hair.

Step 4: Secure Your Hair

Secure your hair with a scrunchie or hair tie to keep it in place.

Step 5: Sleep

Get a good night's sleep and wake up with beautiful, intact curls.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you protect your curls when sleeping.

Tip 1: Use a Microfiber Towel

Use a microfiber towel to dry your hair before going to bed. This will help prevent frizz and keep your curls intact.

Tip 2: Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, before going to bed. This can damage your hair and cause your curls to become frizzy.

Tip 3: Use a Diffuser

If you need to dry your hair before going to bed, use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer. This will help prevent frizz and keep your curls intact.

FAQ

Q: How can I protect my curls when sleeping without using a silk or satin pillowcase?

A: You can use a silk or satin bonnet or pineapple your curls to protect them when sleeping.

Q: Can I use a regular cotton pillowcase?

A: While you can use a regular cotton pillowcase, it can create friction and cause your curls to become frizzy or flattened. This is why a silk or satin pillowcase is recommended.

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type, but most people with curly hair can go 2-3 days between washes.

Q: How can I keep my curls moisturized?

A: Use a leave-in conditioner, deep condition once a week, and avoid using heat styling tools.

Hair Care

Taking care of your curly hair is important to keep your curls healthy and beautiful. Here are some tips for curly hair care.

Tip 1: Use a Sulfate-Free Shampoo

Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, which can cause it to become dry and brittle. Use a sulfate-free shampoo to keep your hair moisturized.

Tip 2: Deep Condition Once a Week

Deep conditioning once a week can help keep your curls moisturized and healthy.

Tip 3: Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage your hair and cause it to become frizzy. Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, as much as possible.

Tip 4: Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush. This will help prevent breakage and keep your curls intact.

Tip 5: Sleep on a Silk or Satin Pillowcase

As we mentioned earlier, sleeping on a silk or satin pillowcase can help protect your curls and prevent frizz.

By following these tips and tricks, you can protect your curls when sleeping and keep them looking beautiful and healthy.
