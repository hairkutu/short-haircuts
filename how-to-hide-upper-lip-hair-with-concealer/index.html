How To Hide Upper Lip Hair With Concealer A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Aug 15, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're someone who has upper lip hair, you know how frustrating it can be. It can be embarrassing and make you feel self-conscious. While there are many ways to remove it, sometimes you need a quick fix. One way to hide upper lip hair is by using concealer. Here's how to do it:

How to Hide Upper Lip Hair with Concealer

Step 1: Choose the Right Concealer

The first step in hiding upper lip hair with concealer is choosing the right product. You'll want to look for a concealer that matches your skin tone and has full coverage. It's best to choose a cream-based concealer as it will be easier to apply and blend.

Step 2: Prep Your Skin

Before applying concealer, you'll want to prep your skin by cleansing and moisturizing it. This will help the concealer go on smoothly and last longer.

Step 3: Apply the Concealer

Using a small brush or your finger, apply the concealer to the area above your lips where the hair is. Be sure to blend it well into your skin. You may need to apply a second layer if the hair is still visible.

Step 4: Set the Concealer

To make sure the concealer stays in place and doesn't smudge, set it with a translucent powder. This will also help it last longer throughout the day.

Tips for Hiding Upper Lip Hair with Concealer

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when using concealer to hide upper lip hair:

  • Use a small amount of concealer at first and build it up as needed.
  • Make sure to blend the concealer well into your skin to avoid a noticeable line.
  • Choose a concealer with a matte finish to avoid any shine.
  • Don't forget to set the concealer with powder to ensure it stays in place.

FAQ

Can I use a concealer that is lighter than my skin tone?

No, it's best to choose a concealer that matches your skin tone as closely as possible. Using a lighter shade may make the hair more noticeable.

Can I use a liquid concealer?

You can use a liquid concealer, but it may not be as effective as a cream-based one. Liquid concealers tend to be more sheer and may not provide full coverage.

How often do I need to reapply the concealer?

This will depend on your skin type and how much you touch your face throughout the day. It's best to check your upper lip area periodically and reapply the concealer as needed.

Hair Care

While using concealer to hide upper lip hair can be a quick fix, it's important to address the root cause of the hair growth. Consider talking to a dermatologist or esthetician about hair removal options that work best for your skin type.

Whether you choose to remove the hair or hide it with concealer, taking care of your skin is important. Be sure to cleanse, moisturize, and protect your skin from the sun to keep it healthy and glowing.
