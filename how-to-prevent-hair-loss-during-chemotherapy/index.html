How To Prevent Hair Loss During Chemotherapy

Written by Robby May 24, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Dealing with cancer can be a difficult time, and chemotherapy can have a significant impact on your body, including your hair. Hair loss during chemotherapy is a common side effect, but there are steps you can take to help prevent or minimize it. Here are some tips on how to prevent hair loss during chemotherapy.

Description

Cancer cells grow quickly, and chemotherapy drugs target rapidly dividing cells, including hair follicles. This results in hair loss, which can be distressing for many people. Hair loss during chemotherapy can occur on the scalp, eyebrows, eyelashes, and other parts of the body. The amount of hair loss can vary from person to person, and it may be temporary or permanent.

How to Prevent Hair Loss During Chemotherapy

1. Cold Cap Therapy

Cold cap therapy involves wearing a special cap that cools the scalp during chemotherapy. The cold temperature constricts blood vessels in the scalp, reducing the amount of chemotherapy drugs that reach the hair follicles. This can help to minimize hair loss during treatment. Cold cap therapy is not suitable for everyone, so talk to your doctor to see if it is an option for you.

2. Scalp Cooling System

A scalp cooling system is a device that circulates cooled liquid through a cap worn on the head during chemotherapy. This can help to reduce the amount of chemotherapy drugs that reach the hair follicles and minimize hair loss. Scalp cooling systems are more effective than cold cap therapy, but they are also more expensive.

3. Minoxidil

Minoxidil is a medication that is used to treat hair loss. It can also help to prevent hair loss during chemotherapy. Minoxidil is applied topically to the scalp, and it works by increasing blood flow to the hair follicles. Talk to your doctor to see if minoxidil is an option for you.

4. Hair Care

During chemotherapy, it is important to take good care of your hair. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools like blow dryers and flat irons. Use a wide-toothed comb to avoid pulling on your hair. Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair, and avoid tight hairstyles like braids or ponytails.

Step by Step

Here are some step-by-step instructions on how to prevent hair loss during chemotherapy:

Step 1:

Talk to your doctor about cold cap therapy or a scalp cooling system to help reduce hair loss during chemotherapy.

Step 2:

If cold cap therapy or a scalp cooling system is not an option for you, ask your doctor about minoxidil to help prevent hair loss.

Step 3:

Take good care of your hair during chemotherapy by using a gentle shampoo and conditioner, avoiding heat styling tools, and being gentle when brushing or combing your hair.

Tips

Here are some additional tips on how to prevent hair loss during chemotherapy:

Tip 1:

Wear a soft hat or scarf to protect your scalp from the sun and keep it warm during chemotherapy.

Tip 2:

Consider cutting your hair short before starting chemotherapy to make it easier to manage.

Tip 3:

If you do experience hair loss, consider wearing a wig or hairpiece to help you feel more comfortable.

FAQ

Q: Will hair loss during chemotherapy be permanent?

A: Hair loss during chemotherapy can be temporary or permanent. It depends on the type and dosage of chemotherapy drugs used.

Q: Can I dye my hair during chemotherapy?

A: It is generally not recommended to dye your hair during chemotherapy, as it can be more fragile and prone to damage. Talk to your doctor before using any hair dyes or other hair treatments.

Q: Is it normal to feel upset about hair loss during chemotherapy?

A: Yes, it is normal to feel upset about hair loss during chemotherapy. It can be a difficult side effect to deal with, but there are resources available to help you cope.

Hair Care

During chemotherapy, it is important to take good care of your hair to minimize damage and prevent hair loss. Use gentle products, avoid heat styling tools, and be gentle when brushing or combing your hair. If you do experience hair loss, consider wearing a wig or hairpiece to help you feel more comfortable. Remember that hair loss during chemotherapy is a common side effect, and there are steps you can take to help minimize it.
