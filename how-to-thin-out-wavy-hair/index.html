How To Thin Out Wavy Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Jul 03, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you struggling with thick, unruly waves that seem impossible to manage? If so, you may benefit from thinning out your hair. Thinning out your wavy hair can help reduce bulk, promote better curl definition, and make styling easier. In this guide, we'll show you how to thin out wavy hair with ease.

  • How to Thin Out Wavy Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Thinning Out Wavy Hair

How to Thin Out Wavy Hair

Thinning out wavy hair is a process that involves removing some of the bulk from your hair strands. This can be done in a number of ways, including:

  • Using thinning shears or scissors
  • Getting a razor cut
  • Using a texturizing technique

Each of these methods works differently and can produce different results. It's important to choose the method that works best for your hair type and desired outcome.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's how to thin out wavy hair using thinning shears:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as you normally would.
  2. Towel dry your hair and comb it out to remove any tangles.
  3. Section off the areas of your hair that you want to thin out. This can be the entire head or just certain areas.
  4. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers.
  5. Using the thinning shears, make small snips along the length of the hair section. Be careful not to cut too much or too close to the scalp.
  6. Move on to the next section and repeat until you've thinned out all the areas you want to address.
  7. Style your hair as you normally would.

Here's how to thin out wavy hair using a razor cut:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as you normally would.
  2. Towel dry your hair and comb it out to remove any tangles.
  3. Section off the areas of your hair that you want to thin out. This can be the entire head or just certain areas.
  4. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers.
  5. Using a razor comb, make small, quick cuts along the length of the hair section. The razor comb will help to thin out the hair without creating blunt lines.
  6. Move on to the next section and repeat until you've thinned out all the areas you want to address.
  7. Style your hair as you normally would.

Here's how to thin out wavy hair using a texturizing technique:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as you normally would.
  2. Towel dry your hair and comb it out to remove any tangles.
  3. Section off the areas of your hair that you want to thin out. This can be the entire head or just certain areas.
  4. Using a texturizing technique, such as twist cutting or point cutting, create texture and remove bulk from the hair. This technique involves making small cuts at an angle to create movement and separation in the hair.
  5. Move on to the next section and repeat until you've thinned out all the areas you want to address.
  6. Style your hair as you normally would.

Tips for Thinning Out Wavy Hair

Thinning out wavy hair can be a tricky process, but these tips can help make it easier:

  • Start with dry hair to get a better idea of how much bulk you need to remove.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress as you're thinning out your hair.
  • Be careful not to cut too much or too close to the scalp, as this can create uneven lines and cause damage to your hair.
  • Take your time and work in small sections to ensure that you don't over-thin your hair.
  • Consult with a professional stylist if you're unsure about how to thin out your hair.

FAQs

Here are some common questions and answers about thinning out wavy hair:

Q: Will thinning out my hair damage it?

A: Thinning out your hair can cause damage if you cut too much or too close to the scalp. It's important to use the right technique and tools to avoid damaging your hair.

Q: How often should I thin out my wavy hair?

A: This will depend on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Some people may need to thin out their hair every few weeks, while others may only need to do it once or twice a year.

Q: Can I thin out my wavy hair at home?

A: Yes, you can thin out your wavy hair at home, but it's important to use the right tools and technique to avoid damaging your hair.

Hair Care for Thinned Out Wavy Hair

Once you've thinned out your wavy hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips for caring for thinned out wavy hair:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage to your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair after washing.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to help keep your hair soft and manageable.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Thinning out wavy hair can be a great way to reduce bulk, promote better curl definition, and make styling easier. By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve the perfect look for your hair type and personal style.
