White Male Fade Haircut A Complete Guide For 2023

Written by Frank Jul 01, 2023 · 8 min read

If you’re a white male looking for a trendy and stylish haircut, look no further than the fade haircut. This classic style has been popular for decades, and it’s still going strong in 2023. In this article, we’ll cover everything you need to know about the white male fade haircut, including how to get it, step-by-step instructions, tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Get a White Male Fade Haircut
  • Step-by-Step Instructions
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Fade Haircut
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Advice for Your Fade Haircut

Description

The white male fade haircut is a popular style that features short hair on the sides and back of the head that gradually gets longer as it moves towards the top. This gradual transition creates a “fading” effect, hence the name. The fade can be high or low, depending on your preference, and the top can be styled in a variety of ways, including slicked back, textured, or messy.

The fade haircut is versatile and can be customized to suit your individual style. It’s a great choice for men who want a low-maintenance haircut that still looks sharp and stylish.

How to Get a White Male Fade Haircut

If you’re interested in getting a white male fade haircut, the first step is to find a skilled barber or stylist who has experience with this style. Look for someone who has a portfolio of fades they’ve done in the past and who can give you advice on which type of fade would work best for your face shape and hair type.

When you go in for your appointment, be prepared to discuss the style you want and bring in a picture if possible. Your barber or stylist will then use clippers to gradually cut your hair shorter on the sides and back, blending it into the longer hair on top. The length of the fade will depend on your preference, but most fades start with a #1 or #2 guard and gradually get longer towards the top.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Here are the step-by-step instructions for getting a white male fade haircut:

  1. Choose a skilled barber or stylist who has experience with fades.
  2. Discuss the style you want and bring in a picture if possible.
  3. Sit down in the chair and let your barber or stylist get to work.
  4. Start with a #1 or #2 guard on the clippers and gradually work your way up, blending the hair on the sides and back into the longer hair on top.
  5. Use scissors or a razor to clean up the edges and create a clean, polished look.
  6. Style the top of your hair as desired using pomade, wax, or gel.

Tips for Maintaining Your Fade Haircut

Here are some tips for maintaining your white male fade haircut:

  • Visit your barber or stylist every 2-3 weeks to keep your fade looking fresh.
  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Experiment with different styles on top, such as a slicked back look, a messy textured look, or a quiff.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about the white male fade haircut:

What is the difference between a high fade and a low fade?

A high fade starts at the temple and gradually gets shorter towards the ear, while a low fade starts lower on the head and gradually gets shorter towards the neck.

Can I get a fade if I have curly hair?

Yes, a fade can work well with curly hair. Talk to your barber or stylist about which type of fade would work best for your hair type.

What products should I use to style my hair?

You can use pomade, wax, or gel to style the top of your hair. Experiment with different products to find what works best for your hair type and the style you want to achieve.

Hair Care Advice for Your Fade Haircut

Here are some additional hair care tips for maintaining your white male fade haircut:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping it of natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using hot tools such as flat irons or curling irons, as these can damage your hair and make it more difficult to style.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner with SPF.

With these tips and advice, you’ll be able to rock a sharp and stylish white male fade haircut in 2023 and beyond.
