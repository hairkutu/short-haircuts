How To Thin Really Thick Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine May 10, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Do you have thick, curly hair that you just can't seem to manage? Does your hair get tangled easily, making it difficult to style? If so, you may benefit from thinning your hair. Thinning your hair can make it easier to manage, reduce tangles, and give you more styling options. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of thinning really thick curly hair.

How to Thin Really Thick Curly Hair

Thinning your hair involves removing some of the bulk to make it more manageable. Here's how to do it:

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair

Before you start thinning your hair, make sure it's clean and dry. Thinning wet hair can result in uneven cuts and a choppy appearance.

Step 2: Section your hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to thin your hair evenly.

Step 3: Choose your thinning shears

Thinning shears are special scissors designed to remove bulk from the hair. You can find them at most beauty supply stores. Choose a pair with teeth that are spaced evenly apart for the best results.

Step 4: Start thinning

Start at the bottom of your hair and work your way up. Hold a section of hair between your fingers and use the thinning shears to remove some of the bulk. Be careful not to cut too much at once.

Step 5: Blend your hair

Once you've thinned out the bottom section of your hair, blend it with the next section. Repeat this process until you've thinned your entire head of hair.

Tips for Thinning Really Thick Curly Hair

Thinning your hair can be tricky, especially if you have thick, curly hair. Here are some tips to help you get the best results:

Tip 1: Use the right tools

Thinning shears are essential for thinning your hair. Make sure you choose a pair with teeth that are spaced evenly apart for the best results.

Tip 2: Take your time

Thinning your hair can be time-consuming, but it's important to take your time to get the best results. Rushing can result in uneven cuts and a choppy appearance.

Tip 3: Work in small sections

Thinning your hair in small sections will help you achieve a more even look. It will also make it easier to blend your hair as you go.

Tip 4: Don't over-thin

It's important not to over-thin your hair. Removing too much hair can result in a thin, wispy appearance that may not be flattering.

FAQ

How often should I thin my hair?

It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. As a general rule, you should thin your hair every 6-12 months.

Can I thin my hair at home?

Yes, you can thin your hair at home with the right tools and technique. However, if you're not confident in your abilities, it's best to visit a professional stylist.

Hair Care

Thinning your hair can make it more manageable, but it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy. Here are some tips for caring for your hair after thinning it:

Tip 1: Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner

Thinning your hair can make it more vulnerable to damage, so it's important to use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep it hydrated.

Tip 2: Avoid heat styling

Heat styling can damage your hair, especially if it's already thin. Try to avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons as much as possible.

Tip 3: Get regular trims

Regular trims can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

Tip 4: Protect your hair from the sun

UV rays can damage your hair, so it's important to protect it from the sun. Wear a hat or use a UV-protectant spray when you're outside for extended periods.

Thinning really thick curly hair can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and technique, it's possible to achieve a more manageable, versatile style. Just remember to take your time, work in small sections, and care for your hair properly after thinning it.
