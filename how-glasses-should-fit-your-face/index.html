How Glasses Should Fit Your Face A Complete Guide

Written by Daniel Sep 04, 2023 Â· 8 min read

When it comes to eyeglasses, finding the perfect frame is just one part of the equation. Ensuring that your glasses fit your face correctly is crucial for both comfort and vision. In this guide, we'll walk you through how glasses should fit your face, common fit issues, and tips for finding the perfect pair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Determine the Right Fit
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Adjusting Your Glasses
  • Tips for Finding the Perfect Pair of Glasses
  • FAQs About Glasses Fit
  • How Glasses Can Affect Your Hair

How to Determine the Right Fit

When it comes to glasses fit, there are a few key measurements to consider:

1. Bridge Width

The bridge width is the distance between the two lenses, where the glasses rest on your nose. It's important to ensure that the bridge width matches the width of your nose, as a too-narrow or too-wide bridge can cause discomfort or slippage.

2. Temple Length

The temple length is the distance from the hinge to the earpiece, and it's crucial to ensure that the temples are long enough to fit comfortably around your ears without pinching or sliding down your nose.

3. Lens Height

The lens height is the distance from the top to the bottom of the lens. It's important to ensure that the lens height is tall enough to accommodate your eyes and provide proper vision, without being too tall or too short.

Step-by-Step Guide to Adjusting Your Glasses

If you've already purchased glasses and are experiencing discomfort or fit issues, there are a few steps you can take to adjust them:

1. Adjust the Nose Pads

If your glasses have adjustable nose pads, try adjusting them up or down to ensure a comfortable fit. You may also want to adjust the angle of the nose pads to match the shape of your nose.

2. Bend the Temple Arms

If your glasses are sliding down your nose, try gently bending the temple arms inward to create a tighter fit. If the temple arms are causing discomfort behind your ears, try bending them slightly outward.

3. Tighten the Screws

If your glasses feel loose or wobbly, try tightening the screws that hold the lenses in place. You can find these screws on the inside of the frame near the lenses.

Tips for Finding the Perfect Pair of Glasses

When shopping for glasses, keep these tips in mind to ensure a perfect fit:

1. Know Your Measurements

Before shopping for glasses, measure your face to determine your bridge width, temple length, and lens height. Many glasses websites and stores offer guides to help you find the perfect measurements.

2. Try Before You Buy

When shopping for glasses in person, be sure to try on multiple pairs to find the perfect fit. Take note of any discomfort or fit issues, and don't be afraid to ask for assistance from a salesperson.

3. Consider Your Lifestyle

When choosing glasses, consider your daily activities and choose a frame that will fit comfortably and securely during all of your activities, from work to exercise.

FAQs About Glasses Fit

1. What if my glasses are too tight?

If your glasses are too tight, try adjusting the nose pads or temple arms. If the glasses still feel uncomfortable, consider getting a new pair with a wider bridge or longer temple arms.

2. What if my glasses are too loose?

If your glasses are too loose, try adjusting the temple arms or tightening the screws that hold the lenses in place. If the glasses still feel too loose, consider getting a new pair with a narrower bridge or shorter temple arms.

3. Can I adjust my glasses at home?

While it's possible to adjust your glasses at home, it's best to have them professionally adjusted by an optician or eyeglasses store. This ensures that the adjustments are made safely and correctly.

How Glasses Can Affect Your Hair

Wearing glasses can have an impact on your hair, particularly if you have longer hair. Here are a few tips for keeping your hair looking great while wearing glasses:

1. Choose the Right Style

Consider your hairstyle when choosing glasses. If you have longer hair, consider frames that sit higher on the nose to prevent tangling with your hair.

2. Use Hair Products

Using hair products like leave-in conditioner or anti-frizz serum can help protect your hair from tangling with your glasses and keep it looking smooth and shiny.

3. Adjust Your Glasses

If you're experiencing hair tangling with your glasses, try adjusting the temple arms to create a better fit. You may also want to consider wearing your hair up or back to prevent tangling.

By following these tips and ensuring that your glasses fit your face correctly, you can enjoy comfortable, clear vision and a stylish look.
