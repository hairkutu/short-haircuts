What Type Of Hair Cut Suits Me

Written by Aprilia May 29, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Choosing a hairstyle that suits your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle can be a daunting task. With so many options to choose from, it can be challenging to determine the best haircut for you. In this article, we will guide you through the process of figuring out what type of hair cut suits you and provide tips on how to maintain your new look.

Description

Before we dive into the different types of haircuts, it's essential to understand your face shape. The four primary face shapes are round, oval, square, and heart-shaped. The shape of your face will play a crucial role in determining which haircut will suit you the best.

How to

The next step is to consider your hair texture. Hair texture can range from fine to thick, curly to straight. Your hair texture will determine how your hair will fall and which haircuts will be easy to maintain.

Straight Hair

For those with straight hair, a blunt bob or long layers can add volume and dimension to your hair. A pixie cut is also an option for those looking for a low-maintenance haircut.

Wavy Hair

Wavy hair looks great in a layered haircut, as it adds movement and texture to your hair. A lob or long layers are also great options for those with wavy hair.

Curly Hair

Curly hair looks best with long layers or a short bob. A curly pixie cut is also a great option for those who want a low-maintenance haircut.

Thick Hair

For those with thick hair, a layered haircut can help to thin out your hair and make it more manageable. A long bob or lob is also a great option for those with thick hair.

Fine Hair

For those with fine hair, a blunt bob or a pixie cut can add volume and make your hair appear thicker. Long layers are also a great option for those with fine hair.

Step by Step

Once you have determined your face shape and hair texture, the next step is to visit a hairstylist. They will be able to recommend the best haircut for you based on your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle.

During your visit to the salon, it's essential to communicate with your hairstylist. Let them know what you're looking for and what your lifestyle is like. This will ensure that they recommend a haircut that suits your needs and is easy to maintain.

After your haircut, it's crucial to take care of your hair. Use high-quality hair products, such as shampoo and conditioner, that are appropriate for your hair type. Regular trims every six to eight weeks will also help to maintain your new haircut.

Tips

  • Consider your face shape and hair texture when choosing a haircut
  • Communicate with your hairstylist to ensure they recommend a haircut that suits your needs
  • Use high-quality hair products that are appropriate for your hair type
  • Schedule regular trims to maintain your haircut

FAQ

What haircut suits a round face?

A long bob or a side-swept bang can help to elongate a round face.

What haircut suits an oval face?

An oval face can pull off almost any haircut, but a shoulder-length lob is a great option.

What haircut suits a square face?

A textured bob or a long layered haircut can soften the angles of a square face.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is essential to maintaining your new haircut. Use high-quality hair products, such as shampoo and conditioner, that are appropriate for your hair type. Regular trims every six to eight weeks will also help to maintain your new haircut.

Avoid using heat styling tools, such as curling irons and straighteners, too often, as they can damage your hair. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and use a low heat setting.

Lastly, protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.

Conclusion

Choosing a haircut can be a daunting task, but by considering your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle, you can determine what type of hair cut suits you. Visit a hairstylist and communicate with them to ensure they recommend a haircut that suits your needs. Take care of your hair by using high-quality hair products, scheduling regular trims, and avoiding heat styling tools. With these tips, you'll be able to maintain your new haircut and look your best.
