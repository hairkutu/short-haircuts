How To Grow My Edges Back From Traction Alopecia

Written by Petter Jun 01, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you are experiencing hair loss around your hairline due to tight hairstyles, you may be suffering from traction alopecia. This condition is caused by prolonged tension on the hair follicles, which can lead to hair breakage and bald patches. Fortunately, there are steps you can take to grow your edges back and prevent further damage.

Description

Traction alopecia is a form of hair loss that occurs when the hair is pulled too tightly for a prolonged period. This tension can damage the hair follicles and lead to hair breakage and bald patches. The condition is common among people who wear tight hairstyles, such as braids, weaves, and extensions. It can also occur from wearing hats, helmets, or headbands that are too tight.

How to Grow My Edges Back from Traction Alopecia

Step 1: Remove the Source of Tension

The first step in growing your edges back is to remove the source of tension. This means avoiding tight hairstyles, hats, and headbands that pull on your hair. You may also need to adjust the way you wear your hair, such as wearing it loose or in a low ponytail. It is important to give your hair a break from tension to allow it to grow back.

Step 2: Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can help stimulate blood flow to the hair follicles and promote hair growth. Use your fingertips to massage your scalp in circular motions for a few minutes each day. You can also use a scalp massager or a soft-bristled brush to help with circulation.

Step 3: Use Natural Oils

Natural oils are a great way to nourish your hair and promote growth. Some oils that are beneficial for hair growth include castor oil, coconut oil, and olive oil. Apply the oil to your scalp and edges and massage it in with your fingertips. Leave the oil on for a few hours or overnight and then wash it out with a gentle shampoo.

Step 4: Take Supplements

Supplements can help promote hair growth and strengthen your hair. Biotin, vitamin D, and iron are all important for healthy hair growth. You can take these supplements in pill form or get them from your diet. Foods that are high in these nutrients include eggs, spinach, and salmon.

Tips

- Be patient. It can take several months to see results from hair growth treatments.

- Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair and slow down growth.

- Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather by wearing a hat or scarf.

FAQ

Q: Can traction alopecia be reversed?

A: Yes, traction alopecia can be reversed by removing the source of tension and promoting hair growth.

Q: How long does it take to grow hair back after traction alopecia?

A: It can take several months to a year for hair to grow back after traction alopecia. This depends on the severity of the damage and how well you take care of your hair.

Hair Care

It is important to take good care of your hair to prevent further damage and promote growth. This includes washing your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo, using a conditioner to nourish your hair, and avoiding harsh chemicals and heat styling tools. You should also trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

By following these steps and taking good care of your hair, you can grow your edges back and prevent further damage from traction alopecia. Remember to be patient and consistent in your hair care routine, and you will see results over time.
