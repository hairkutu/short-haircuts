What Causes Alopecia In Young Females

Written by Oliver Jul 29, 2023 · 7 min read

If you’re a young female experiencing hair loss, you may be wondering what is causing your alopecia. Hair loss can be a distressing experience, and it’s important to understand what may be causing your condition so that you can seek the appropriate treatment. Alopecia can have a number of causes, and in this article, we’ll explore some of the most common reasons why young females may experience hair loss.

Hormone Imbalances

Hormone imbalances can cause hair loss in young females. Hormones such as estrogen, progesterone, and testosterone play an important role in the growth and maintenance of hair. When these hormones are imbalanced, hair loss can occur. Hormone imbalances can be caused by a number of factors, including pregnancy, menopause, and polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS).

Stress

Stress can also cause hair loss in young females. When you’re under stress, your body produces cortisol, a hormone that can cause hair follicles to shrink and stop producing hair. If you’re experiencing a lot of stress, it’s important to find ways to manage it, such as through exercise, meditation, or therapy.

Nutrition Deficiencies

Nutrition deficiencies can also contribute to hair loss in young females. If you’re not getting enough vitamins and minerals, your hair may become thin and brittle. Common nutrient deficiencies that can cause hair loss include iron, vitamin D, and biotin.

Genetics

Genetics can also play a role in hair loss in young females. If hair loss runs in your family, you may be more likely to experience it yourself. Female pattern hair loss is a common genetic condition that can cause hair to thin and fall out.

Autoimmune Disorders

Autoimmune disorders can also cause hair loss in young females. Conditions such as alopecia areata and lupus can cause hair to fall out in patches or all over the scalp. These conditions are caused by the body’s immune system attacking hair follicles.

Medications

Some medications can cause hair loss in young females. Chemotherapy drugs, for example, are known to cause hair loss. Other medications that can cause hair loss include antidepressants, blood thinners, and birth control pills.

Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles such as braids, ponytails, and weaves can also cause hair loss in young females. These hairstyles can pull on the hair follicles and cause them to become damaged over time. If you regularly wear tight hairstyles, it’s important to give your hair a break and wear looser styles.

Hair Products

Some hair products can cause hair loss in young females. Products that contain harsh chemicals, such as hair dyes and relaxers, can damage the hair follicles and cause hair to fall out. It’s important to choose gentle hair products and to avoid overusing products that can damage your hair.

Fungus

Fungal infections can also cause hair loss in young females. Conditions such as ringworm can cause hair to fall out in patches. If you suspect that you have a fungal infection, it’s important to see a doctor for treatment.

Thyroid Problems

Thyroid problems can also contribute to hair loss in young females. The thyroid gland produces hormones that regulate metabolism, and when the gland is underactive or overactive, hair loss can occur. If you’re experiencing hair loss along with symptoms such as fatigue, weight gain, or sensitivity to cold, it’s important to see a doctor to rule out thyroid problems.

Conclusion

If you’re a young female experiencing hair loss, it’s important to understand what may be causing your alopecia. Hair loss can have a number of causes, including hormone imbalances, stress, nutrition deficiencies, genetics, autoimmune disorders, medications, tight hairstyles, harsh hair products, fungal infections, and thyroid problems. By understanding the underlying cause of your hair loss, you can seek the appropriate treatment and take steps to prevent further hair loss.
