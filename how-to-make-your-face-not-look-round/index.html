How To Make Your Face Not Look Round A Guide To Flattering Facial Features

Written by Bardi Apr 16, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you have a round face shape, you may feel self-conscious about its fullness and lack of angles. However, with the right techniques and styling, you can create the illusion of a more sculpted and defined face. In this guide, we will provide you with tips and tricks on how to make your face not look round, including hair care strategies, makeup hacks, and facial exercises.

Description: Understanding Your Face Shape

Before we dive into the nitty-gritty of face contouring, it is important to understand your unique facial features. Face shapes are typically categorized into six main categories: round, oval, square, heart, diamond, and triangle. If you have a round face, your cheeks are the widest part of your face, and your jawline is soft and rounded. While round faces can be cute and youthful, they may lack definition and structure.

How to: Measuring Your Face Shape

To determine your face shape, you can use a tape measure or ruler to take the following measurements:

  • Forehead: Measure across the widest part of your forehead, from the peak of one eyebrow arch to the other.
  • Cheekbones: Measure across your cheekbones, starting and ending at the pointiest part below the outer corner of each eye.
  • Jawline: Measure from the tip of your chin to the point below your ear where your jaw angles upward. Then, double that number.
  • Face length: Measure from the center of your hairline to the tip of your chin.

Once you have these measurements, you can compare them to the ideal proportions for each face shape. A round face typically has similar measurements for the cheekbones and face length, while the forehead and jawline are slightly narrower.

Step by Step: How to Make Your Face Not Look Round

Now that you have a better understanding of your face shape, let's explore some practical tips and techniques for creating more angular and defined features:

1. Contouring with Makeup

One of the easiest and most effective ways to create the illusion of a more chiseled face is through makeup contouring. Using a matte powder or cream that is two shades darker than your skin tone, apply it to the hollows of your cheeks, temples, and jawline. Blend it out with a fluffy brush to avoid any harsh lines. You can also use a highlighter on the high points of your face, such as the cheekbones, brow bone, and center of the nose, to add dimension and glow.

2. Experiment with Hairstyles

Your hair can be a powerful tool for balancing out your face shape. Long, straight hair can elongate a round face, while short, choppy layers can add texture and movement. Side-swept bangs can also help to create the illusion of an elongated face. Avoid blunt, chin-length cuts, as they can emphasize the roundness of your face.

3. Facial Exercises

Facial exercises can help to tone and lift the muscles in your face, creating a more sculpted and defined appearance. Some effective exercises for a round face include chin lifts, cheek lifts, and jaw releases. It is important to do these exercises daily and consistently to see results.

4. Dress for Your Shape

The right clothing can also help to flatter your face shape. V-necklines and scoop necks can elongate your neck and draw attention upwards, while statement earrings can also add vertical lines. Avoid turtlenecks and high-necked tops, as they can make your face look more circular.

Tips: Additional Strategies for a More Defined Face

Here are some bonus tips and tricks for achieving a more sculpted and angular face shape:

  • Drink plenty of water and eat a balanced diet to keep your skin looking healthy and radiant.
  • Avoid excessive salt and alcohol, which can cause water retention and bloating in the face.
  • Consider getting a professional facial or massage to stimulate circulation and promote lymphatic drainage.
  • Invest in a good skincare routine to keep your skin firm and hydrated.
  • Practice good posture to elongate your neck and create a more confident stance.

FAQ: Common Questions About Face Shapes and Contouring

Q: Can face shape change over time?

A: Yes, face shape can change due to factors such as aging, weight gain or loss, and hormonal fluctuations. However, your underlying bone structure will still play a role in determining your overall face shape.

Q: Can I contour my face every day?

A: While it is safe to contour your face every day, it is important to choose products that are non-comedogenic and do not clog your pores. It is also crucial to remove your makeup thoroughly at the end of the day to avoid any breakouts or skin irritation.

Q: Is it possible to have a combination of face shapes?

A: Yes, it is possible to have a hybrid or mixed face shape, such as a round-square or heart-oval combination. In this case, you can use a combination of contouring and styling techniques to balance out your unique features.

Hair Care: Choosing the Right Products for Your Face Shape

Your hair care routine can also play a role in defining your face shape. Here are some tips for choosing the right products for your unique features:

  • For a round face, choose volumizing products that add lift and height to your hair.
  • Avoid heavy, flat-ironed hairstyles that can emphasize the roundness of your face.
  • Opt for soft, wavy textures that add movement and dimension.
  • Use a lightweight, non-greasy serum to add shine and tame frizz.

By incorporating these tips and techniques into your daily routine, you can create a more defined and flattering face shape. Remember, confidence is key - embrace your unique features and rock them with pride!
