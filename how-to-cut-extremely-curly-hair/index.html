How To Cut Extremely Curly Hair Tips And Techniques

Written by Christine Sep 02, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Curly hair can be challenging to cut, especially when it's extremely curly. But with the right techniques and tools, you can achieve a beautiful, bouncy haircut that compliments your natural curls. In this post, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to cut extremely curly hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Extremely Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Process
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care After Cutting

How to Cut Extremely Curly Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a wide-tooth comb, and hair clips to section off the hair. It's also important to have a spray bottle filled with water to keep the hair moist as you cut.

The first step is to determine the length you want to cut. Keep in mind that curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so it's best to err on the side of caution and cut a little less than you think you want.

Start by dividing the hair into sections. Begin at the nape of the neck and work your way up to the crown. Clip each section out of the way as you go. This will make it easier to cut each section evenly.

Step-by-Step Process

Here's a step-by-step process to follow when cutting extremely curly hair:

  1. Wet the hair with a spray bottle filled with water
  2. Divide the hair into sections and clip each section out of the way
  3. Start at the nape of the neck and work your way up to the crown
  4. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight
  5. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut the ends
  6. Continue cutting small sections of hair, working your way around the head
  7. Blend the hair as you go, making sure there are no harsh lines or uneven sections
  8. Use a mirror to check the back of the head and make any necessary adjustments
  9. Once you're happy with the length, use the scissors to add texture and shape to the curls
  10. Style the hair as desired

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to keep in mind when cutting extremely curly hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections
  • Use a comb to tease out any knots or tangles before cutting
  • Don't pull the hair too taut when cutting, as this can cause the curls to bounce up unevenly
  • Use a diffuser when blow-drying to help define the curls and reduce frizz
  • Consider cutting the hair when it's dry, as this will give you a better idea of how the curls will fall

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting extremely curly hair:

Can you cut curly hair with regular scissors?

Yes, you can cut curly hair with regular scissors, but it's best to use a pair of sharp, professional-grade scissors. Dull scissors can cause split ends and damage the hair.

Should you cut curly hair wet or dry?

It's best to cut curly hair when it's wet, as this makes it easier to see the natural shape of the curls. However, some stylists prefer to cut curly hair when it's dry, as this gives them a better idea of how the curls will fall.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting extremely curly hair, it's important to take care of it properly to keep the curls looking their best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils
  • Avoid brushing curly hair, as this can cause frizz and breakage
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle the hair when wet
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define the curls and reduce frizz
  • Avoid heat styling tools, as they can cause damage to the hair

Cutting extremely curly hair can be challenging, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a beautiful, bouncy haircut that compliments your natural curls. Remember to take care of your hair properly after cutting to keep it looking its best.
