How To Know Which Way To Curl Your Hair

Written by Oliver Mar 30, 2023 Â· 10 min read

Curling your hair is a great way to add some volume and texture to your locks. But if you're new to curling, it can be tricky to figure out which way to curl your hair. In this article, we'll give you some tips and tricks on how to know which way to curl your hair so you can rock those curls like a pro.

Table of Contents

  • How to know which way to curl your hair
  • Step-by-Step guide to curling your hair
  • Tips for achieving perfect curls
  • FAQs
  • Hair care tips for healthy curls

How to Know Which Way to Curl Your Hair

Knowing which way to curl your hair depends on the look you want to achieve. Below are some tips to help you determine which way to curl your hair:

1. Decide on the type of curl

Before you start curling your hair, decide on the type of curl you want. Do you want loose waves or tight curls? Different types of curls require different curling techniques, so it's important to know what you're aiming for.

2. Determine your natural hair part

Knowing your natural hair part is crucial when curling your hair. If your hair naturally parts on the left side, you'll want to curl your hair away from your face on the left side. If your hair parts on the right side, you'll want to curl your hair away from your face on the right side. This will help your curls look more natural and cohesive with your hair's natural flow.

3. Consider your face shape

If you have a round face, you'll want to curl your hair away from your face to create a slimming effect. If you have a square face, you can curl your hair inwards or outwards, depending on your preference. If you have an oval face, you can pretty much curl your hair in any direction.

4. Experiment

Ultimately, the best way to figure out which way to curl your hair is to experiment. Try curling your hair in different directions and see what looks best for you. It may take some trial and error, but once you figure out the best way to curl your hair, you'll be able to achieve that perfect curl every time.

Step-by-Step Guide to Curling Your Hair

Now that you know which way to curl your hair, here's a step-by-step guide to curling your hair:

Step 1: Prep your hair

Before you start curling your hair, make sure it's clean and dry. Apply a heat protectant spray to prevent damage from the curling iron. You can also add some volumizing mousse or spray to give your hair some extra volume.

Step 2: Section your hair

Part your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to curl your hair and ensure that you don't miss any sections.

Step 3: Heat up your curling iron

Turn on your curling iron and let it heat up to the desired temperature. For fine hair, use a lower temperature setting, while thicker hair may require a higher temperature.

Step 4: Curl your hair

Start curling your hair section by section. Take a small section of hair and wrap it around the curling iron, holding it for 10-15 seconds. Release the curl and repeat until you've curled all of your hair.

Step 5: Finish with hairspray

Once you've curled your hair, finish with a light mist of hairspray to hold the curls in place.

Tips for Achieving Perfect Curls

Here are some additional tips for achieving perfect curls:

1. Use the right size curling iron

The size of your curling iron will determine the size of your curls. If you want tight curls, use a smaller curling iron. For loose waves, use a larger curling iron.

2. Don't curl your hair all the way to the roots

Curling your hair all the way to the roots can create a dated, '80s look. Instead, start curling midway down the hair shaft for a more modern look.

3. Use a comb to separate your curls

After curling your hair, use a wide-tooth comb to separate your curls and give them a more natural look.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about curling hair:

1. How long do I hold the curling iron on my hair?

Hold the curling iron on your hair for 10-15 seconds, depending on the thickness of your hair.

2. How do I prevent my curls from falling out?

Use a strong hold hairspray to keep your curls in place. You can also curl your hair in smaller sections or use a smaller curling iron to create tighter curls.

3. Can I curl my hair without a curling iron?

Yes, you can curl your hair without a curling iron by using a straightener or curling wand.

Hair Care Tips for Healthy Curls

Curling your hair can be damaging if you don't take proper care of your locks. Here are some hair care tips for healthy curls:

1. Use a heat protectant spray

Before curling your hair, always use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage from the curling iron.

2. Don't overdo it with heat styling

While curling your hair can be fun, it's important not to overdo it with heat styling. Try to limit your heat styling to once or twice a week to prevent damage.

3. Moisturize your hair

Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to moisturize your hair and prevent damage from heat styling.

4. Use a wide-tooth comb

When combing your hair, use a wide-tooth comb to prevent breakage and damage to your curls.

5. Get regular trims

Regular trims will keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends, which can make your curls look frizzy and damaged.

Now that you know which way to curl your hair, put these tips into practice and start rocking those curls!
