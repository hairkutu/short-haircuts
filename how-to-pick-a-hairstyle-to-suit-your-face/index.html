How to Pick a Hairstyle to Suit Your Face

Table of Content: Description, How to, Step by step, Tips, FAQ, Hair Care

Description

Choosing a hairstyle that suits your face shape can enhance your overall appearance. It can create the illusion of balance and proportion on your face, making you look more attractive and confident. However, with so many hairstyle options available, it can be overwhelming to choose the right one for you. In this article, we will guide you on how to pick a hairstyle to suit your face shape.

How to

When picking a hairstyle, it's important to consider your face shape. There are six main face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and oblong. Each face shape has its own unique characteristics, and certain hairstyles can complement or detract from these features. To determine your face shape, pull your hair back and examine the outline of your face in a mirror. Then, follow the guidelines below:

Oval Face Shape

An oval face shape is longer than it is wide, with a gently rounded hairline and jawline. This face shape is considered the most versatile, as it can pull off a variety of hairstyles.

Best Hairstyles: Long layers, beachy waves, side-swept bangs, bob with volume at the crown

Avoid: Short layers that add volume to the sides of the face, blunt bangs

Round Face Shape

A round face shape has soft, curved lines, with a width and length that are almost equal. The cheeks are often the widest part of the face.

Best Hairstyles: Long layers, angled bob, side-swept bangs, voluminous updo

Avoid: Chin-length hair, blunt bangs, straight bob

Square Face Shape

A square face shape has a strong, angular jawline and forehead, with a width and length that are almost equal. The hairline is often straight.

Best Hairstyles: Soft curls, layered bob, side-swept bangs, pixie cut

Avoid: Straight, flat hair, blunt bangs, chin-length hair

Heart Face Shape

A heart face shape has a wider forehead and cheekbones, with a narrow chin and jawline.

Best Hairstyles: Side-swept bangs, layered bob, long waves, pixie cut with volume at the crown

Avoid: Short layers that add volume to the sides of the face, blunt bangs

Diamond Face Shape

A diamond face shape has a narrow forehead and jawline, with wide cheekbones.

Best Hairstyles: Side-swept bangs, layered bob, long waves, pixie cut with volume at the crown

Avoid: Short layers that add volume to the sides of the face, blunt bangs

Oblong Face Shape

An oblong face shape is longer than it is wide, with a straight hairline and jawline.

Best Hairstyles: Long layers, beachy waves, side-swept bangs, bob with volume at the crown

Avoid: Short layers that add volume to the sides of the face, blunt bangs

Step by step

1. Determine your face shape by pulling your hair back and examining the outline of your face in a mirror.

2. Take note of the unique characteristics of your face shape, such as the width of your jawline or the length of your forehead.

3. Research hairstyles that complement your face shape, using the guidelines provided above.

4. Consider other factors, such as your hair texture and lifestyle, when choosing a hairstyle.

5. Consult with a hairstylist to determine the best hairstyle for you.

Tips

- Don't be afraid to experiment with different hairstyles until you find the perfect one for you.

- Consider using hair accessories, such as headbands or clips, to enhance your hairstyle.

- Maintain your hairstyle with regular trims and proper hair care.

FAQ

Q: Can I still wear a certain hairstyle even if it doesn't match my face shape?

A: Yes, you can still wear any hairstyle you like, regardless of your face shape. However, choosing a hairstyle that complements your face shape can enhance your overall appearance.

Q: How often should I get a haircut?

A: It's recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape of your hairstyle and prevent split ends.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining a healthy and beautiful hairstyle. Follow these tips:

- Use a shampoo and conditioner that is appropriate for your hair type.

- Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, too often.

- Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner.

- Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

- Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth and strength.
