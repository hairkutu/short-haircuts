What Causes Dog s Hair To Fall Out

Written by Frank Jul 09, 2023 Â· 6 min read

As a dog owner, you may have noticed that your furry friend is shedding more than usual. Excessive shedding can be a sign of an underlying health issue that needs to be addressed. In this article, we will discuss the common causes of hair loss in dogs, and how you can prevent it from happening.

Table of Contents

  • Genetics
  • Stress
  • Infection
  • Poor Diet
  • Parasites
  • Allergies
  • Hormonal Changes
  • Autoimmune Disease
  • Medications
  • Hair Care

Genetics

Just like humans, some dogs are more prone to hair loss than others due to their genetic makeup. Certain breeds, such as the Chinese Crested, are known for their hairless or low-shedding coats. However, even dogs without a predisposition to hair loss can experience it due to other factors.

Stress

Stress can cause hair loss in dogs just as it can in humans. Dogs that are exposed to stressful situations, such as moving to a new home or being left alone for long periods of time, may experience hair loss as a result. In some cases, stress-induced hair loss can be temporary and hair will grow back once the stressor is removed.

Infection

Various infections can also cause hair loss in dogs. Fungal infections, such as ringworm or yeast infections, can cause hair loss in small patches. Bacterial infections, such as folliculitis, can cause hair loss and skin irritation. Infections can be treated with medication prescribed by a veterinarian.

Poor Diet

Just like humans, dogs need a balanced diet to maintain healthy hair growth. A diet lacking in essential nutrients, such as protein and omega-3 fatty acids, can cause hair to fall out or become thin and brittle. Make sure that your dog's diet is balanced and includes high-quality protein sources.

Parasites

Parasites, such as fleas and ticks, can cause hair loss in dogs. These pesky bugs can irritate the skin and cause your dog to scratch or bite at it, leading to hair loss. Preventing and treating parasite infestations is key to keeping your dog's coat healthy.

Allergies

Dogs can also experience hair loss due to allergies. Allergies to food or environmental factors, such as pollen or dust, can cause skin irritation and hair loss. Identifying and treating the underlying allergy is important to prevent further hair loss.

Hormonal Changes

Hormonal changes can also cause hair loss in dogs. This can occur due to a thyroid imbalance, Cushing's disease, or other hormonal disorders. Your veterinarian can run tests to determine if your dog is experiencing hormonal imbalances and recommend a treatment plan.

Autoimmune Disease

Autoimmune diseases, such as lupus or alopecia areata, can cause hair loss in dogs. These diseases cause the immune system to attack the hair follicles, leading to hair loss. Treatment for autoimmune diseases typically involves medication prescribed by a veterinarian.

Medications

Some medications can cause hair loss as a side effect. If your dog is experiencing hair loss and is currently taking medication, talk to your veterinarian about the possibility of the medication causing the hair loss.

Hair Care

Poor hair care can also contribute to hair loss in dogs. Over-bathing, using harsh shampoos, or not grooming your dog's coat regularly can all cause hair to fall out. Make sure that you are using a gentle shampoo and brushing your dog's coat regularly to prevent hair loss.

Conclusion

There are many reasons why a dog's hair may be falling out. From genetics to poor hair care, it is important to identify the underlying cause and address it to prevent further hair loss. If you are concerned about your dog's hair loss, talk to your veterinarian who can help identify the cause and recommend a treatment plan.
