How To Cut A Short Bob On Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine May 16, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of paying for expensive salon visits to maintain your short bob haircut? With a little bit of courage and the right tools, you can easily cut your own short bob at home. Not only will this save you money, but it will also give you a sense of independence and creativity. In this blog post, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut a short bob on yourself.

Description

A short bob haircut is a timeless and versatile hairstyle that can be worn by women of all ages and hair types. It is a low-maintenance haircut that can be styled in different ways, such as straight, wavy, or curly. The length of a short bob usually falls between the chin and the shoulders, which makes it perfect for those who want a chic and fashionable look without sacrificing too much length.

How to Cut a Short Bob on Yourself

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, you need to make sure that you have all the necessary tools. These include:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair ties
  • Clips
  • Mirror

Make sure that your scissors are specifically designed for cutting hair, and that they are sharp enough to make clean cuts. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to achieve the desired look.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It is important to start with clean and dry hair, as wet hair can be more difficult to cut and style. Use your regular shampoo and conditioner, and let your hair air dry or blow dry it with a hair dryer.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into four sections: two at the front and two at the back. Use hair ties or clips to secure each section.

Step 4: Cut the Back Section

Start with the back section of your hair. Use your comb to create a straight line across the back of your head, and then cut along that line with your scissors. Make sure that your cuts are even and smooth.

Step 5: Cut the Side Sections

Move on to the side sections of your hair. Use your comb to hold the hair straight, and then cut along the length of the hair. Start at the front and work your way to the back, making sure to cut each section evenly.

Step 6: Cut the Front Sections

Finally, it's time to cut the front sections of your hair. This is the trickiest part, so be patient and take your time. Use your comb to create a guide line, and then cut along that line with your scissors. Make sure that both sides are even, and that the length is what you want.

Step 7: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting your hair, use a mirror to check the back and sides. If you notice any unevenness, go back and make small adjustments until you are satisfied with the overall look.

Tips

  • Start with small cuts, and gradually work your way up to the desired length.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, as this can lead to mistakes and unevenness.
  • Use a sharp pair of scissors to make clean cuts, and avoid using regular household scissors.
  • Be patient and take your time, as rushing can lead to mistakes.
  • Watch tutorial videos or ask for help from a friend or family member.

FAQ

Can I cut my own short bob if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your own short bob regardless of your hair type. However, keep in mind that curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so you may want to cut it a bit longer than the desired length.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

Don't panic! Mistakes happen, and they can be fixed. If you accidentally cut too much hair, try to even it out by trimming the other side. If you're unsure of what to do, seek help from a professional hairstylist.

How often should I cut my short bob?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how well you maintain it. Generally, it's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

Hair Care

After cutting your short bob, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and shiny. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using too much heat or styling products. Regularly trim your hair to prevent split ends, and protect it from the sun and harsh weather conditions.

Cutting your own short bob can be a fun and rewarding experience, as long as you follow the right steps and take your time. With practice and patience, you can achieve a beautiful and stylish haircut that suits your personality and lifestyle. Good luck!
