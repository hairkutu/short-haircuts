Do Some Dogs Have Hair Instead Of Fur

Written by William Apr 16, 2023 · 4 min read

If you’re a dog lover, you’ve probably noticed that there are different types of dog coats. Some dogs have long, fluffy fur while others have short, sleek hair. But have you ever wondered if some dogs have hair instead of fur?

What’s the Difference Between Hair and Fur?

Hair and fur are both made of the same protein, called keratin. However, there is a difference between the two. Hair grows continuously and sheds periodically, while fur grows to a certain length and then falls out. Hair is also finer and softer than fur, which is coarser and thicker.

Do Some Dogs Have Hair Instead of Fur?

Yes, some dogs have hair instead of fur. These dogs are often referred to as “hair breeds” and are typically non-shedding. This means that they don’t lose their hair like other dogs do, which can be a plus if you have allergies or don’t want to deal with shedding.

What Breeds Have Hair Instead of Fur?

Some of the most popular hair breeds include:

  • Poodle
  • Bichon Frise
  • Shih Tzu
  • Yorkshire Terrier
  • Afghan Hound

How to Care for Dogs with Hair

While dogs with hair don’t shed like other dogs, they do require regular grooming to keep their coat healthy and tangle-free. Here are some tips for caring for dogs with hair:

  • Brush your dog’s hair daily to prevent mats and tangles
  • Trim your dog’s hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the length and prevent it from getting too long
  • Bathe your dog regularly to keep their coat clean and shiny
  • Use a detangling spray or conditioner to make brushing easier and prevent breakage

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Do hair breeds still shed?

A: Yes, hair breeds still shed, but the shedding is minimal compared to other dogs.

Q: Can you shave a dog with hair?

A: It’s not recommended to shave a dog with hair as it can damage their coat and cause skin irritation.

Q: Do hair breeds have dander?

A: Yes, hair breeds still produce dander, but it’s less than other dogs with fur.

Conclusion

So, yes, some dogs do have hair instead of fur. These hair breeds are a great option for people with allergies or those who don’t want to deal with shedding. However, it’s important to remember that while these dogs don’t shed, they do require regular grooming to keep their coat healthy and tangle-free.
