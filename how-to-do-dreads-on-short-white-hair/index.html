How To Do Dreads On Short White Hair A Step By Step Guide

Are you thinking of getting dreads on your short white hair? Dreads can be a bold and stylish choice, but many people think that it's only possible for those with longer hair. Fortunately, that's not the case. With the right approach, you can get dreads on your short white hair. In this article, we'll guide you on how to do dreads on short white hair.

How to Do Dreads on Short White Hair

Before you start, it's important to know that getting dreads on short hair might take longer than those with longer hair. This is because the hair needs to be long enough to form a stable dreadlock. However, you can still achieve dreads on short hair with patience and the right technique.

1. Clean Your Hair

Make sure your hair is clean and free from any products. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any build-up or dirt that could interfere with dread formation. Rinse your hair thoroughly and let it dry completely before proceeding.

2. Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using a rat-tail comb. The size of the sections will depend on how thick you want your dreads to be. The smaller the sections, the thinner your dreads will be. Use hair ties to secure each section.

3. Backcomb Your Hair

Starting at the roots of each section, backcomb the hair using a dread comb. This involves combing the hair up towards the roots in a back-and-forth motion until it starts to form a knot. Repeat this process until the entire section is knotted.

4. Roll Your Dreads

Once you've backcombed each section, roll them between your palms to smooth them out and encourage them to lock together. This will help to set the dreads and prevent them from unraveling.

5. Secure Your Dreads

Use a hair tie to secure the base of each dread. This will help to keep the knot in place while the dread continues to form. You can remove the hair tie once the dread has locked together.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to do dreads on short white hair:

  1. Clean your hair thoroughly with a clarifying shampoo.
  2. Divide your hair into sections using a rat-tail comb.
  3. Backcomb each section using a dread comb.
  4. Roll each dread between your palms to smooth it out and encourage it to lock together.
  5. Secure the base of each dread with a hair tie.
  6. Repeat the process for each section until you have the desired number of dreads.
  7. Leave your dreads to set for at least a week before washing them.
  8. Use a residue-free shampoo to wash your dreads and avoid using conditioner.
  9. Pat your dreads dry with a towel and let them air dry completely.
  10. Use a dread wax or locking gel to help your dreads lock together and maintain their shape.

Tips for Maintaining Your Dreads

Here are some tips for maintaining your dreads:

  • Avoid using any products that contain alcohol or leave residue on your dreads.
  • Don't wash your dreads too often as this can cause them to unravel.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to avoid frizz and breakage.
  • Keep your dreads dry and avoid swimming or getting them wet for extended periods.
  • Avoid using any heat styling tools on your dreads.

FAQs

How long does it take for dreads to form on short hair?

It can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months for dreads to form on short hair. The length of time depends on the thickness of your hair and how often you maintain and tighten your dreads.

Do I need to cut my hair before getting dreads?

No, you don't need to cut your hair before getting dreads. However, if you have damaged or split ends, it's a good idea to trim them before starting the dread process.

Can I wash my dreads?

Yes, you can wash your dreads, but you should avoid using conditioner as this can cause them to unravel. Use a residue-free shampoo and be gentle when washing your dreads.

Hair Care for Dreads

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy and strong dreads. Here are some tips:

  • Massage your scalp regularly to promote blood flow and encourage hair growth.
  • Avoid using any products that contain harsh chemicals or sulfates.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage.
  • Trim any loose or frayed ends to prevent them from unraveling.
  • Be patient and consistent with your hair care routine.

Now that you know how to do dreads on short white hair, it's time to give it a try. Remember to be patient and take good care of your dreads to keep them looking great.
