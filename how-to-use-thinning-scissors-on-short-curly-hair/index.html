How To Use Thinning Scissors On Short Curly Hair

Written by Daniel Aug 03, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Thinning scissors are a great tool for those with short curly hair who want to add more texture and movement to their locks. However, using thinning scissors can be quite tricky, especially if you're not familiar with how they work. In this blog post, we'll walk you through how to use thinning scissors on short curly hair step by step, as well as provide some tips and tricks along the way. So, let's get started!

  • How to Use Thinning Scissors on Short Curly Hair
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Thinning scissors are a type of scissors that are designed to remove some of the hair's weight without affecting its length. They have a serrated edge that helps to thin the hair out as it's cut, creating a more textured and layered look. Thinning scissors are especially useful for those with thick hair or curly hair, as they can help to reduce bulk and add more movement to the hair.

How to Use Thinning Scissors on Short Curly Hair

Before you start using thinning scissors on your short curly hair, make sure you have a good quality pair of scissors that are sharp and well-maintained. You should also have a comb and some hair clips on hand to help you section off your hair.

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning off your hair into small sections using your clips. This will help you to work on one area at a time and ensure that you don't miss any sections. Make sure to leave a small section at the nape of your neck for last.

Step 2: Comb Through Your Hair

Using your comb, comb through each section of your hair to remove any tangles and make sure that the hair is smooth and even. This will help you to get a more accurate cut.

Step 3: Cut with Thinning Scissors

Hold your thinning scissors at a 45-degree angle and start cutting into the hair, making sure to keep the scissors moving in a back-and-forth motion. The more you cut, the more hair you will remove, so start with small sections and work your way up to larger sections as you become more comfortable.

Step 4: Blend the Hair

Once you've finished cutting with the thinning scissors, use your regular scissors to blend any harsh lines and make the hair look more natural. This will help to ensure that your hair looks even and well-blended.

Step 5: Finish with Styling Products

Once you've finished cutting your hair, apply some styling products to enhance your curls and add some definition. Use a diffuser to dry your hair and create more volume and movement.

Tips

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections as you become more comfortable using the thinning scissors.
  • Use a comb to make sure that the hair is even and smooth before cutting.
  • Use your regular scissors to blend any harsh lines and make the hair look more natural.
  • Apply styling products to enhance your curls and add some definition.
  • Use a diffuser to dry your hair and create more volume and movement.

FAQ

Can I use thinning scissors on my short curly hair if I have fine hair?

Yes, you can use thinning scissors on fine hair, but make sure to use them sparingly to avoid removing too much hair and making your hair look thin and stringy.

How often should I use thinning scissors on my short curly hair?

You can use thinning scissors on your short curly hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and texture of your hair.

Hair Care

After using thinning scissors on your short curly hair, it's important to take good care of your locks to keep them healthy and strong. Make sure to use a good quality shampoo and conditioner that is designed for curly hair. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, and use a heat protectant spray when you do. Finally, get regular trims to keep your hair looking its best!
