How To Cut Short Bangs Choppy A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia May 12, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Description

Short, choppy bangs are a trendy and edgy hairstyle that can add a lot of personality to your look. However, cutting your own bangs can be daunting, especially if you're not a trained professional. In this post, we'll guide you through the process of cutting your short bangs choppy, so you can achieve the perfect look at home.

How to Cut Short Bangs Choppy

Cutting short bangs choppy can be done in just a few simple steps. Here's how to do it:

Step 1: Choose the Right Tools

Before you start cutting your bangs, you'll need the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a mirror. It's also a good idea to have some hair clips on hand to keep your hair out of the way.

Step 2: Section Off Your Bangs

Using your comb, section off the hair that you want to cut into bangs. Make sure the section is straight and even.

Step 3: Cut Your Bangs

Starting at the center of your forehead, use your scissors to make small, diagonal cuts into the hair. This will create a choppy, textured look. Work your way from the center of your forehead out to the sides, making sure to keep the hair length even.

Step 4: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting your bangs, check your work in the mirror. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure that your bangs are even and the length you want.

Tips

- Go slow and be patient - it's better to cut too little at first and then make more cuts, rather than cutting too much and having to wait for your hair to grow back. - Use your fingers to hold your hair in place instead of a comb, as this will give you a more natural, textured look. - If you're not sure how short you want your bangs to be, start by cutting them a little longer than you think you want. You can always cut more later.

FAQ

Can I cut my own bangs?

Yes, you can cut your own bangs! However, it's important to be patient and careful when doing so.

How often should I trim my bangs?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your bangs start to look unkempt. Generally, you should trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake while cutting your bangs, don't panic! You can always go to a professional stylist to have them fix it.

Hair Care

To keep your short bangs choppy looking their best, it's important to take good care of your hair. Here are some tips: - Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. - Use a heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools. - Avoid using too much product in your hair, as this can weigh it down and make it look greasy. - Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Conclusion

Cutting short bangs choppy can be a fun and rewarding experience, as long as you take your time and follow the steps outlined in this post. With a little practice, you'll be able to achieve the perfect choppy bangs at home.
