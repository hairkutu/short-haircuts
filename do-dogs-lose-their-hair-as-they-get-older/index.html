Do Dogs Lose Their Hair As They Get Older

Written by Eveline Sep 02, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're a dog owner, you may have noticed that your furry companion's coat has changed over time. As dogs age, it's not uncommon for them to lose hair, but is this a natural part of the aging process, or is it a sign of a more serious problem? In this article, we'll explore the reasons why dogs lose hair as they get older, how to care for their coat, and when to seek veterinary help.

Table of Contents

  • Reasons Why Dogs Lose Hair as They Get Older
  • Hair Care Tips for Aging Dogs
  • When to Seek Veterinary Help
  • FAQs

Reasons Why Dogs Lose Hair as They Get Older

There are a few reasons why dogs may start to lose hair as they age:

1. Hormonal Changes

One of the most common reasons for hair loss in senior dogs is hormonal changes. As dogs age, their bodies produce less estrogen and testosterone, which can result in hair loss or thinning. This is more common in female dogs, but it can affect males as well.

2. Nutritional Deficiencies

As dogs get older, their nutritional needs may change. They may require more protein, vitamins, and minerals to keep their coat healthy. If their diet is lacking in these nutrients, it can lead to hair loss or a dull coat.

3. Skin Conditions

Senior dogs may be more prone to skin conditions, such as allergies or infections, which can cause hair loss or itching. They may also develop benign growths, such as lipomas or cysts, which can affect their coat.

Hair Care Tips for Aging Dogs

While some hair loss is normal for aging dogs, there are steps you can take to keep their coat healthy:

1. Brush Your Dog Regularly

Regular brushing can help remove loose hair and prevent matting, which can contribute to hair loss. It can also stimulate the skin and promote blood flow, which can improve coat health.

2. Feed Your Dog a Nutritious Diet

Make sure your dog's diet is balanced and provides all the nutrients they need. If you're not sure what to feed them, talk to your veterinarian.

3. Bathe Your Dog as Needed

Regular baths can help keep your dog's skin and coat healthy, but don't overdo it. Too much bathing can strip the skin of natural oils, which can contribute to hair loss.

When to Seek Veterinary Help

If you notice excessive hair loss or other changes in your dog's coat, it's important to seek veterinary care. Your veterinarian can perform a physical exam and recommend tests to determine the underlying cause of the hair loss. They may also recommend treatment, such as medication or dietary changes, to help improve your dog's coat health.

FAQs

Do all dogs lose hair as they get older?

Not all dogs lose hair as they age, but it is common.

Can hair loss in aging dogs be prevented?

While some hair loss is normal for aging dogs, you can take steps to keep their coat healthy, such as regular brushing and a nutritious diet.

Is excessive hair loss in aging dogs a sign of a serious problem?

Excessive hair loss or other changes in your dog's coat can be a sign of a serious problem, such as a medical condition or skin infection. It's important to seek veterinary care if you notice any changes in their coat.

Can medication help with hair loss in aging dogs?

Depending on the underlying cause of the hair loss, medication or dietary changes may be recommended by your veterinarian to help improve your dog's coat health.

How often should I bathe my aging dog?

You should bathe your aging dog as needed, but don't overdo it. Too much bathing can strip the skin of natural oils, which can contribute to hair loss.

Can diet affect my dog's coat health?

A balanced and nutritious diet can help keep your dog's coat healthy. If you're not sure what to feed them, talk to your veterinarian.

As you can see, it's not uncommon for dogs to lose hair as they age. However, with proper care and attention, you can help keep your furry friend's coat healthy and beautiful. If you have any concerns about your dog's coat health or hair loss, don't hesitate to reach out to your veterinarian.
