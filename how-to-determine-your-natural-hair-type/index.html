How To Determine Your Natural Hair Type

Written by Daniel Apr 01, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you struggling to figure out your natural hair type? Don't worry, you're not alone. Determining your hair type is important because it helps you choose the right products and treatments that work best for your hair. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of determining your natural hair type.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Determine Your Hair Type
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Determining Your Hair Type
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Your Hair Type

Description

Your natural hair type is determined by the shape and structure of your hair strands. There are four main hair types: straight, wavy, curly, and coily. Each type has its own unique characteristics and requires different care and styling techniques. Knowing your hair type will help you choose products that work best for your hair and avoid damage caused by using the wrong products and techniques.

How to Determine Your Hair Type

To determine your hair type, you'll need to examine the shape of your hair strands. The easiest way to do this is to take a single strand of hair and examine it closely. Here are the four main hair types:

Straight Hair

Straight hair is characterized by hair strands that are completely straight from root to tip. Straight hair is usually shiny and reflects light easily. It can be difficult to hold a curl and may become oily quickly.

Wavy Hair

Wavy hair is characterized by hair strands that form an "S" shape. It is not completely straight but not curly either. Wavy hair is usually thicker and may be more prone to frizz. It can hold a curl better than straight hair.

Curly Hair

Curly hair is characterized by hair strands that are tightly coiled and form a spiral shape. It can be difficult to comb or brush and may become frizzy easily. Curly hair may also be prone to dryness.

Coily Hair

Coily hair is characterized by hair strands that are tightly coiled and form a zigzag or "Z" pattern. It is the most fragile hair type and may break easily. Coily hair is also prone to dryness and may require more moisture than other hair types.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to determining your hair type:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as you normally would.
  2. Gently towel dry your hair.
  3. Take a single strand of hair from the top of your head.
  4. Examine the shape of the strand:
    • If the strand is completely straight, you have straight hair.
    • If the strand forms an "S" shape, you have wavy hair.
    • If the strand forms a spiral shape, you have curly hair.
    • If the strand forms a zigzag or "Z" shape, you have coily hair.

Tips for Determining Your Hair Type

Here are some tips to help you determine your hair type:

  • Examine your hair when it's wet and dry. Your hair type may look different when it's wet compared to when it's dry.
  • Ask a stylist for help. A professional stylist can help you determine your hair type and recommend products and treatments that work best for your hair.
  • Experiment with different products and techniques. Try different products and styling techniques to see what works best for your hair.

FAQs

What if I have more than one hair type?

It's common to have more than one hair type. For example, you may have wavy hair on top and curly hair on the bottom. In this case, you'll need to use different products and techniques for each hair type.

Can my hair type change over time?

Yes, your hair type can change over time due to hormonal changes, aging, and other factors. It's important to regularly assess your hair type and adjust your hair care routine as needed.

Hair Care for Your Hair Type

Once you've determined your hair type, it's important to choose products and treatments that work best for your hair. Here are some general tips for caring for each hair type:

Straight Hair

  • Use a volumizing shampoo to add body to your hair.
  • Avoid heavy conditioners that may weigh down your hair.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can cause damage.

Wavy Hair

  • Use a lightweight conditioner to control frizz.
  • Use a diffuser when blow drying your hair to enhance your waves.
  • Use a curling iron to add more defined curls.

Curly Hair

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness.
  • Avoid brushing your hair, as it can cause frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.

Coily Hair

  • Use a leave-in conditioner to add moisture to your hair.
  • Protect your hair at night by wearing a silk or satin scarf or bonnet.
  • Avoid using heat on your hair, as it can cause damage.

Now that you know your hair type, you can choose products and treatments that work best for your hair. Remember to regularly assess your hair type and adjust your hair care routine as needed.
