How I Cured My Pcos Hair Loss A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 05, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're reading this, you're probably one of the many women affected by Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS). PCOS is a hormonal disorder that affects up to 10% of women of childbearing age, and one of its most common symptoms is hair loss. I know firsthand how devastating PCOS hair loss can be, but I'm here to tell you that it's possible to recover from it. In this article, I'll share with you the steps I took to cure my PCOS hair loss and regain my confidence.

What is PCOS Hair Loss?

PCOS hair loss, also known as androgenetic alopecia, is a type of hair loss that affects women with PCOS. It is caused by high levels of androgens, which are male hormones that women also produce in small amounts. These androgens cause hair follicles to shrink and produce thinner, shorter hair, which eventually stops growing altogether. PCOS hair loss can occur anywhere on the scalp, but it typically affects the crown and the front of the scalp.

How to Cure PCOS Hair Loss

Curing PCOS hair loss is not a quick fix, but it is possible with a combination of lifestyle changes, medication, and hair care. Here are the steps I took:

Step 1: Consult a Doctor

The first step in curing PCOS hair loss is to consult a doctor. Your doctor will perform a physical exam, order blood tests, and may refer you to a dermatologist or an endocrinologist. They will also evaluate your medical history and any medications you're currently taking. Based on your test results, your doctor may prescribe medication to balance your hormones and prevent further hair loss.

Step 2: Make Lifestyle Changes

Lifestyle changes can also help in curing PCOS hair loss. Eating a healthy diet, exercising regularly, and managing stress can all improve your hormonal balance and reduce hair loss. I started eating more fruits, vegetables, and lean proteins, and cut down on sugar and processed foods. I also started practicing yoga and meditation to manage my stress levels.

Step 3: Use Hair Growth Products

Using hair growth products can also help in curing PCOS hair loss. Look for products that contain ingredients like biotin, zinc, and keratin, which promote hair growth and strengthen hair follicles. I started using a hair growth shampoo and conditioner, as well as a hair growth serum that I applied to my scalp twice a day.

Step 4: Consider Hair Transplantation

If your hair loss is severe, you may want to consider hair transplantation. Hair transplantation is a surgical procedure in which hair follicles from one part of your scalp are transplanted to another part. It's a costly and time-consuming procedure, but it can be effective in restoring hair growth.

Tips for PCOS Hair Care

In addition to the steps above, taking care of your hair can also help in curing PCOS hair loss. Here are some tips:

  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, and avoid brushing it when it's wet.
  • Avoid heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage your hair.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles like ponytails and braids, which can pull on your hair and cause breakage.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals like hair dyes and relaxers, which can weaken your hair.

FAQs about PCOS Hair Loss

Q: Can PCOS hair loss be cured?

A: Yes, PCOS hair loss can be cured with a combination of lifestyle changes, medication, and hair care.

Q: How long does it take to cure PCOS hair loss?

A: It varies from person to person, but it can take several months to a year to see significant improvement in hair growth.

Q: Can hair transplantation cure PCOS hair loss?

A: Hair transplantation can be effective in restoring hair growth, but it's not a cure for PCOS hair loss. You still need to address the underlying hormonal imbalance to prevent further hair loss.

Conclusion

PCOS hair loss can be a frustrating and emotional experience, but it's not a hopeless one. By consulting a doctor, making lifestyle changes, using hair growth products, and taking care of your hair, you can cure PCOS hair loss and regain your confidence. Remember that it takes time and patience, but the results are worth it.
