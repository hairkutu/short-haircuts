How To Protect Hair Fall After Delivery

Written by Luffy Jul 01, 2023 Â· 7 min read

After giving birth, many new mothers experience postpartum hair loss or telogen effluvium. This is a condition where hair falls out in large amounts, leaving new mothers with thinning hair. While this can be a stressful and frustrating experience, there are steps you can take to protect your hair and promote healthy growth. In this article, we will discuss how to protect hair fall after delivery, including tips, FAQ, and hair care.

Table of Contents

  • How to Protect Hair Fall After Delivery
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Preventing Hair Fall
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Regimen

How to Protect Hair Fall After Delivery

The first step in protecting your hair from postpartum hair loss is to maintain a healthy lifestyle. This includes eating a balanced diet, staying hydrated, and getting enough sleep. Additionally, there are several things you can do to prevent hair fall:

1. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles such as braids, buns, and ponytails. These styles can pull on your hair and cause it to break or fall out. Instead, opt for loose hairstyles such as a messy bun or low ponytail.

2. Use Gentle Hair Products

Use gentle hair products that are free from harsh chemicals such as sulfates and parabens. Look for products that are specifically designed for postpartum hair loss, such as those that contain biotin and keratin.

3. Massage Your Scalp

Regularly massage your scalp to increase blood flow and promote hair growth. You can use your fingers or a scalp massager to gently massage your scalp for a few minutes each day.

Step by Step Guide

Follow these steps to protect your hair from postpartum hair loss:

Step 1: Cleanse Your Hair

Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo that is free from harsh chemicals. Massage your scalp while washing to increase blood flow.

Step 2: Condition Your Hair

Use a conditioner that is designed for postpartum hair loss. Apply the conditioner to your hair and let it sit for a few minutes before rinsing thoroughly.

Step 3: Apply a Leave-In Treatment

Apply a leave-in treatment that contains biotin and keratin to nourish and strengthen your hair.

Step 4: Style Your Hair Gently

Avoid tight hairstyles and use gentle styling techniques such as air-drying or using a wide-tooth comb.

Tips for Preventing Hair Fall

In addition to the steps outlined above, here are some additional tips for preventing hair fall:

1. Eat a Balanced Diet

Eat a diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals such as iron, vitamin C, and biotin. These nutrients are essential for healthy hair growth.

2. Stay Hydrated

Drink plenty of water to keep your body and hair hydrated.

3. Get Enough Sleep

Get enough sleep to reduce stress and promote healthy hair growth.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about postpartum hair loss:

1. How Long Does Postpartum Hair Loss Last?

Postpartum hair loss usually lasts for a few months, but can last up to a year.

2. Is Postpartum Hair Loss Permanent?

No, postpartum hair loss is temporary and will eventually grow back.

3. Can I Use Hair Extensions to Hide Hair Loss?

Avoid using hair extensions as they can further damage your hair and exacerbate hair loss.

Hair Care Regimen

Here is a sample hair care regimen for protecting your hair from postpartum hair loss:

1. Shampoo and Conditioner

Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are free from harsh chemicals.

2. Leave-In Treatment

Apply a leave-in treatment that contains biotin and keratin to nourish and strengthen your hair.

3. Scalp Massage

Gently massage your scalp with your fingers or a scalp massager for a few minutes each day.

4. Healthy Lifestyle

Eat a balanced diet, stay hydrated, and get enough sleep to maintain a healthy lifestyle.

By following these tips and incorporating them into your daily routine, you can protect your hair from postpartum hair loss and promote healthy hair growth.
