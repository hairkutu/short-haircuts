What To Ask Your Barber For Curly Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Luffy Aug 09, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of struggling to style your curly hair? Do you feel like your barber never quite understands what you want? You're not alone. Finding the right haircut and style for curly hair can be a challenge, but it's not impossible. In this article, we'll give you tips and tricks on what to ask your barber for curly hair, so you can achieve the look you want.

Table of Contents

  • How to Ask Your Barber for Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Curly Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair

How to Ask Your Barber for Curly Hair

The first step in getting the right haircut for your curly hair is to communicate clearly with your barber. Here are some things you should ask for:

1. Layers

Ask your barber to add layers to your hair, as this will help to create a more defined shape and reduce bulk.

2. Texture

Ask for texture on the top and sides of your hair to create a more natural look. This can be done through techniques such as razor cutting or point cutting.

3. Length

Ask for a length that suits your face shape and hair texture. For example, if you have tighter curls, you may want to keep your hair shorter, while looser curls can handle longer lengths.

4. Shape

Ask for a shape that complements your natural curls. This can be done through techniques such as shaping around the ears and neckline.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on what to ask your barber for curly hair:

1. Start with a Consultation

Before your barber starts cutting your hair, have a conversation about your hair type, the look you want to achieve, and any concerns you have.

2. Ask for Layers

Ask your barber to add layers to your hair to create a more defined shape and reduce bulk.

3. Texture the Hair

Ask for texture on the top and sides of your hair to create a more natural look. This can be done through techniques such as razor cutting or point cutting.

4. Cut the Hair to the Desired Length

Ask for a length that suits your face shape and hair texture. For example, if you have tighter curls, you may want to keep your hair shorter, while looser curls can handle longer lengths.

5. Shape the Hair

Ask for a shape that complements your natural curls. This can be done through techniques such as shaping around the ears and neckline.

6. Finish with Styling Products

Ask your barber to recommend styling products that will work well with your hair type and the style you are trying to achieve.

Tips for Maintaining Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for maintaining your curly hair:

1. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, as this will help to prevent breakage and frizz.

2. Don't Overwash Your Hair

Curly hair can be dry, so it's important not to overwash it. Wash your hair every 3-4 days and use a gentle shampoo and conditioner.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools such as flat irons and curling irons, as these can damage your hair and cause frizz.

FAQ

What Haircuts Work Best for Curly Hair?

Layered haircuts work best for curly hair, as they help to create a more defined shape and reduce bulk.

How Often Should I Get a Haircut?

It's recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and health of your hair.

What Products Should I Use for Curly Hair?

Use products that are specifically designed for curly hair, such as curl-defining creams and gels.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for taking care of your curly hair:

1. Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

2. Sleep on a Silk Pillowcase

Sleep on a silk pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Wear a hat or use a hair sunscreen to protect your hair from the sun's damaging rays.

By following these tips and tricks, you can achieve the perfect haircut and style for your curly hair. Remember to communicate clearly with your barber and take care of your hair at home to maintain its health and beauty.
