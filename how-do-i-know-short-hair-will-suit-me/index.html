How Do I Know Short Hair Will Suit Me

Written by Petter Sep 02, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you thinking about cutting your hair short but not sure if it will suit you? It's a common concern for many people who are considering making a drastic change to their hairstyle. In this article, we'll discuss how to determine if short hair will suit you and provide tips for maintaining and caring for your new look.

Table of Contents

  • How to Determine If Short Hair Will Suit You
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Cutting Your Hair Short
  • Tips for Maintaining Short Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Short Hair

How to Determine If Short Hair Will Suit You

Before you make the decision to cut your hair short, there are several factors to consider to determine if it will suit you:

Face Shape

The shape of your face is an essential factor to consider when choosing a short hairstyle. If you have a round face, a short haircut with layers will help elongate your face. If you have a square face, a short bob with soft layers will soften your jawline. For a heart-shaped face, a pixie cut with side-swept bangs will highlight your cheekbones.

Hair Texture

Your hair texture is another crucial factor to consider when choosing a short hairstyle. If you have thick hair, a layered bob or pixie cut will help remove some of the bulk. If you have fine hair, a blunt bob or pixie cut will create the illusion of fuller hair.

Lifestyle

Your lifestyle is another factor to consider when choosing a short hairstyle. If you're an active person who enjoys outdoor activities, a short hairstyle will be more manageable than long hair. If you're someone who prefers a low-maintenance hairstyle, a short haircut will require less styling time.

Step-by-Step Guide to Cutting Your Hair Short

If you've decided to cut your hair short, here's a step-by-step guide to help you:

Step 1: Find Inspiration

Before you head to the salon, find inspiration for your new hairstyle. Look through magazines or Pinterest for short hairstyles that you like.

Step 2: Consult With a Hairstylist

Consult with a hairstylist to determine the best short hairstyle for your face shape and hair texture. They can also provide advice on how to style and maintain your new look.

Step 3: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair before your appointment to remove any product buildup or oils. This will help your hairstylist see your natural hair texture.

Step 4: Cut Your Hair

Your hairstylist will cut your hair according to the style you've chosen. They may use a razor, shears, or clippers to create the desired look.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Your hairstylist will style your hair and provide tips on how to maintain your new look at home.

Tips for Maintaining Short Hair

Here are some tips for maintaining your short hair:

Tip 1: Use the Right Products

Use products specifically designed for short hair, such as texturizing sprays or pomades, to add volume and texture.

Tip 2: Get Regular Trims

Get regular trims every 4-6 weeks to maintain your short hairstyle's shape and prevent split ends.

Tip 3: Protect Your Hair

Use heat protectant products before using hot tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, to prevent damage to your hair.

FAQs

Q: Will short hair suit me if I have a round face?

A: Yes, a short hairstyle with layers will help elongate your face and create a more balanced look.

Q: Can I style my short hair in different ways?

A: Yes, you can style your short hair in various ways, such as adding waves or curls or creating a sleek, straight look.

Hair Care Tips for Short Hair

Here are some hair care tips for short hair:

Tip 1: Wash Your Hair Regularly

Wash your hair every 2-3 days to prevent buildup of oils and product.

Tip 2: Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

Tip 3: Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Wear a hat or use a UV protectant spray to prevent sun damage to your hair.

Now that you know how to determine if short hair will suit you, you can confidently make the decision to cut your hair short. Follow these tips to maintain and care for your new hairstyle, and enjoy your new look!
