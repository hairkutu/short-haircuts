How To Cut Short Hair At Front A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Apr 14, 2023 · 9 min read

Cutting your hair at home can be intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be a great way to save money and achieve the look you want. If you have short hair and want to learn how to cut the front section yourself, you've come to the right place. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut short hair at the front, along with some tips and FAQs to help you achieve the perfect look.

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips or hair ties
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Make sure your scissors are sharp, as dull scissors can damage your hair and result in uneven cuts.

Prepping Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. If you have curly hair, you may want to straighten it first so you can see the length more clearly. Once your hair is dry, brush it thoroughly to remove any tangles.

Next, dampen your hair by spraying it lightly with water. This will make it easier to cut and help you achieve a more precise cut.

Sectioning Your Hair

The key to cutting short hair at the front is to section it properly. Use your comb to create a part down the middle of your head, from your forehead to the back of your head. Then, create a horizontal part just above your ears, separating the front section from the back section. Clip or tie the back section out of the way.

Next, divide the front section into two equal parts by creating a part down the middle of your head. Clip or tie one of the sections out of the way, and focus on the other section.

Cutting Your Hair

Starting at the center of your forehead, take a small section of hair between your fingers and comb it straight out from your head. Use your scissors to cut the hair to the desired length, keeping the scissors straight and horizontal.

Continue taking small sections of hair and cutting them to the same length, working your way towards the side of your head. Repeat the process on the other side, making sure to keep the length even on both sides.

Remember to cut less than you think you need, as you can always go back and cut more if necessary.

Blending Your Hair

Once you've cut both sides, remove the clip or tie from the other section of the front section. Comb both sections together and check for any unevenness. If you notice any, use your scissors to make small adjustments until the length is even.

To blend the front section with the back section, comb your hair straight down and cut the ends to the desired length. This will create a seamless transition between the two sections and give you a natural look.

Finishing Touches

After you've finished cutting your hair, it's time to style it. Use a blow dryer and brush to create your desired look, or simply let it air dry for a more natural look.

If you notice any stray hairs, use your scissors to trim them away. Remember to take your time and be patient – cutting your own hair can be tricky, but with practice, you'll get better and more confident.

Tips and Tricks

  • Don't rush the process – take your time and make small adjustments as needed.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Comb your hair straight out from your head before cutting to ensure an even cut.
  • Start with a small amount of hair and cut less than you think you need, as you can always go back and cut more if necessary.
  • When blending the front section with the back section, use your scissors to create a slightly angled cut for a more natural look.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut my hair with regular household scissors?

It's not recommended, as household scissors may not be sharp enough to cut hair properly. Invest in a pair of sharp scissors specifically designed for cutting hair.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Generally, it's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and maintain healthy hair.

Can I cut my own hair if it's curly?

Yes, but it may be more difficult to achieve an even cut. Consider straightening your hair before cutting it to make it easier to see the length.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Regular trims can also help keep your hair looking its best.

Conclusion

Cutting short hair at the front can be challenging, but with the right tools and techniques, it's possible to achieve a great look. Follow our step-by-step guide, use our tips and tricks, and remember to take your time and be patient. With practice, you'll soon be a pro at cutting your own hair.
