Best Way To Thin Out Thick Hair

Written by Frank Jun 23, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Do you have thick hair that you find difficult to manage? Do you want to make your hair more manageable without losing its volume? If yes, then you are in the right place. Thick hair can be a blessing, but it can also be a curse if it's unmanageable. This article will guide you through the best way to thin out thick hair and make it more manageable.

Table of Contents:

  • Description
  • How to Thin Out Thick Hair
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Thinning out thick hair is a process of reducing the amount of hair on your head while maintaining its volume. It involves removing some of the hair from the root to make it less dense. Thinning out your hair will make it easier to style and manage, and it will also reduce the weight of your hair, which can relieve tension on the scalp.

How to Thin Out Thick Hair

There are several ways to thin out thick hair, but the best way is to use thinning shears. Thinning shears are scissors with teeth that are designed to remove some of the hair from the root without cutting it completely. This results in a more natural-looking, textured finish.

Step by Step

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to thin out thick hair using thinning shears:

  1. Wash your hair and towel dry it.
  2. Comb your hair to remove any tangles.
  3. Divide your hair into sections using clips.
  4. Start with the bottom section of your hair and work your way up.
  5. Hold a small section of your hair with your fingers and slide the thinning shears down the length of the hair.
  6. Repeat the process on each section of your hair, making sure to blend the cut pieces with the uncut pieces.
  7. Check your hair in the mirror to make sure it's even and well-blended.
  8. Style your hair as desired.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when thinning out your hair:

  • Start with a small amount of hair and gradually increase the amount you cut.
  • Cut the hair in a zigzag pattern to create a more natural-looking finish.
  • Don't cut too close to the roots, as this can cause damage to the hair.
  • Use a comb to guide the thinning shears and to make sure you're cutting the hair evenly.
  • Don't over-thin your hair, as this can make it look stringy and weak.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about thinning out thick hair:

  • Can I use regular scissors to thin out my hair?

    • No, regular scissors can't thin out your hair properly. You need to use thinning shears to get the best results.

  • Will thinning out my hair make it look thinner?

    • No, thinning out your hair won't make it look thinner. It will only make it more manageable and reduce its weight.

  • Will thinning out my hair damage it?

    • No, if you use thinning shears correctly, it won't damage your hair. However, if you over-thin your hair or cut too close to the roots, it can cause damage.

Hair Care

After thinning out your hair, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain its health and volume. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that are gentle on your hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your hair.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as it can cause damage.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized.

Thinning out thick hair is a great way to make it more manageable and reduce its weight. By following the steps and tips in this article, you can achieve a natural-looking, textured finish that will make your hair easier to style and maintain.
