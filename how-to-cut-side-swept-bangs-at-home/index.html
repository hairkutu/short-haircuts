How To Cut Side Swept Bangs At Home

Are you tired of your same old hairstyle and want to switch things up? One simple way to do that is by cutting your own side swept bangs at home. Not only is it a cost-effective option, but it can also be a fun and empowering experience. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut side swept bangs at home, along with some tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

How to Cut Side Swept Bangs at Home

Before you start cutting your bangs, you should have a clear idea of the style and length you want. It's best to start with longer bangs and gradually cut them shorter. Here's how to cut side swept bangs at home:

Materials Needed:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle with water

Step-by-Step Guide:

  1. Start with clean and dry hair. Comb your hair and make sure it's free of tangles.
  2. Section off the hair that you want to cut into bangs. Use hair clips to hold the rest of your hair back.
  3. Spray the section of hair with water. This will make it easier to cut and style your bangs.
  4. Using the comb, comb the section of hair straight down. The comb should be in the center of your forehead, where you want your bangs to start.
  5. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and cut straight across. Start with small snips and work your way up to the desired length.
  6. Create a slight angle by cutting the hair shorter on the sides and longer in the middle. This will give your bangs a natural, side-swept look.
  7. Blend the bangs by cutting into the ends with the scissors pointing upwards. This will soften any harsh lines.
  8. Style your bangs by blow-drying them using a round brush. Brush them to the side and away from your face.
  9. Trim any stray hairs or uneven ends with the scissors.
  10. Finish by applying a small amount of hair wax or pomade to your bangs to keep them in place.

Tips for Cutting Side Swept Bangs at Home

Cutting your own bangs can be a little intimidating, but with these tips, you can achieve a salon-worthy look at home:

  • Take your time and cut small sections at a time. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet. Wet hair can be deceiving, and you may end up cutting your bangs shorter than you intended.
  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and create split ends.
  • When in doubt, cut less. You can always go back and cut more later.
  • Practice makes perfect. Don't worry if your first attempt doesn't turn out perfectly. Keep practicing, and you'll get the hang of it.

FAQs

1. Can I cut my bangs with regular scissors?

While you can technically cut your bangs with regular scissors, it's not recommended. Regular scissors can damage your hair and create split ends. It's best to use sharp hair cutting scissors, which are designed for cutting hair.

2. How often should I trim my bangs?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your bangs start to look overgrown. As a general rule, it's best to trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh.

3. How do I know if side swept bangs will look good on me?

Side swept bangs look good on most face shapes, but it's best to consult with a hairstylist to determine the best bangs for your face shape and hair type.

Hair Care Tips for Side Swept Bangs

Now that you've cut your side swept bangs, it's important to take care of them to keep them looking great:

  • Wash your bangs daily to keep them clean and free of grease.
  • Blow-dry your bangs using a round brush to style them and give them volume.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your bangs, as it can damage the hair and cause split ends.
  • Use a small amount of hair wax or pomade to keep your bangs in place and prevent them from frizzing.
  • Trim your bangs regularly to prevent them from getting too long and obstructing your vision.

Cutting your own side swept bangs at home can be a fun and rewarding experience. With these tips and step-by-step guide, you can achieve a salon-worthy look without breaking the bank. Whether you're looking to switch up your style or just want to try something new, cutting your own bangs at home is a great way to do it.
