How To Fix A Brassy Hair Dye Job Tips And Tricks

Written by Christine Aug 29, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Have you recently dyed your hair and ended up with a brassy tone? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many people experience this issue after dyeing their hair, especially if they have naturally dark hair or have used a cheap dye product. The good news is that fixing a brassy hair dye job is not as difficult as it may seem. In this article, we will discuss some tips and tricks on how to fix a brassy hair dye job at home.

Description

Brassy hair is a common issue that occurs when hair turns yellow or orange after dyeing. This can happen due to various reasons such as using a low-quality dye, leaving the dye on for too long, or using the wrong developer. Brassiness can make your hair look dull and lifeless, and it can be frustrating for anyone who has spent time and money on dyeing their hair.

How to Fix a Brassy Hair Dye Job

If you want to fix a brassy hair dye job, there are a few things you can do at home. Here are some tips:

1. Use a Purple Shampoo

Purple shampoo is the most popular solution for fixing brassy hair. This shampoo contains purple pigments that neutralize the yellow and orange tones in your hair. Use it once or twice a week, and you will notice a significant difference in the tone of your hair.

2. Tone Your Hair

If purple shampoo doesn't work, you can try toning your hair with a toner or hair dye that has blue or violet undertones. This will help neutralize the brassy tones and give your hair a more natural look.

3. Use a Hair Mask

A hair mask can help hydrate and nourish your hair, which can help reduce brassiness. Look for a hair mask that contains ingredients such as argan oil, coconut oil, or shea butter.

4. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage your hair and make brassiness worse. If you want to fix your brassy hair, avoid using heat styling tools such as flat irons or curling irons. Instead, let your hair air dry or use a heat protectant spray.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to fix a brassy hair dye job:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup. Rinse your hair thoroughly and towel dry.

Step 2: Apply Purple Shampoo

Apply purple shampoo to your hair and massage it into your scalp. Leave it on for 5-10 minutes, then rinse thoroughly.

Step 3: Tone Your Hair

If the purple shampoo didn't work, you can try toning your hair with a toner or hair dye that has blue or violet undertones. Follow the instructions on the product and rinse thoroughly.

Step 4: Use a Hair Mask

Apply a hair mask to your hair and leave it on for 10-15 minutes. Rinse thoroughly with water.

Step 5: Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid heat styling tools such as flat irons or curling irons. Let your hair air dry or use a heat protectant spray.

Tips

Here are some additional tips on how to fix a brassy hair dye job:

1. Use a Quality Hair Dye

Invest in a quality hair dye product that doesn't contain harsh chemicals. This will help prevent brassiness in the first place.

2. Don't Overdo It

Don't leave the dye on for too long or use too much product. Follow the instructions on the product carefully.

3. Visit a Professional

If you're not comfortable fixing your brassy hair at home, visit a professional hairstylist who can help you.

FAQs

Q: Can I use regular shampoo instead of purple shampoo?

A: No, regular shampoo won't neutralize the brassy tones in your hair. You need a purple shampoo that contains purple pigments.

Q: How often should I use purple shampoo?

A: Use purple shampoo once or twice a week, depending on how brassy your hair is.

Q: Can I use a hair mask instead of toner?

A: Yes, a hair mask can help reduce brassiness, but toners are more effective.

Hair Care

After fixing your brassy hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some hair care tips:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Use a gentle shampoo that doesn't contain harsh chemicals. This will help keep your hair healthy and prevent brassiness.

2. Condition Regularly

Use a conditioner regularly to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.

3. Protect Your Hair from Heat

Avoid using heat styling tools too often, and always use a heat protectant spray.

Fixing a brassy hair dye job can be frustrating, but with these tips and tricks, you can get your hair looking beautiful and natural again. Remember to take care of your hair properly to prevent brassiness in the future.
