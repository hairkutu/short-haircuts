Can Scalp Psoriasis Make Your Hair Fall Out

Written by Daniel Jun 05, 2023 · 8 min read

Psoriasis is a chronic autoimmune disease that affects the skin and scalp. It causes red, scaly patches on the skin, and in severe cases, it can cause hair loss. Scalp psoriasis is a common form of psoriasis that affects the scalp and hairline. It can be a frustrating and embarrassing condition that can cause a lot of discomfort. If you have scalp psoriasis, you may be wondering if it can make your hair fall out. In this article, we will explore this question and provide you with some tips on how to manage scalp psoriasis and prevent hair loss.

Description

Scalp psoriasis is a skin condition that causes red, itchy, and scaly patches on the scalp. The patches can be small or large, and they can appear on any part of the scalp. Scalp psoriasis can cause a lot of discomfort, and it can also be embarrassing, especially if the patches are visible. People with scalp psoriasis often experience hair loss, which can be caused by the inflammation of the scalp. In some cases, the hair loss can be temporary, but in severe cases, it can be permanent.

How to Manage Scalp Psoriasis

If you have scalp psoriasis, there are several things you can do to manage the condition and prevent hair loss:

  • Use a medicated shampoo: You can use a medicated shampoo that contains salicylic acid, coal tar, or ketoconazole. These ingredients can help to reduce inflammation and control the symptoms of scalp psoriasis.
  • Avoid harsh hair treatments: Avoid using harsh hair treatments, such as chemical relaxers and hair dyes, as they can irritate the scalp and worsen the condition.
  • Keep your scalp moisturized: Use a moisturizing conditioner or oil to keep your scalp moisturized and prevent dryness.
  • Avoid scratching your scalp: Scratching your scalp can worsen the condition and lead to hair loss. Try to avoid scratching and use a gentle touch when washing and styling your hair.

Step by Step

If you are experiencing hair loss due to scalp psoriasis, here are some steps you can take to prevent further hair loss:

  1. Consult a dermatologist: If you are experiencing severe hair loss, it is important to consult a dermatologist. They can prescribe medication or recommend treatments that can help to control the symptoms of scalp psoriasis and prevent further hair loss.
  2. Use a scalp mask: You can use a scalp mask that contains ingredients like tea tree oil, aloe vera, and jojoba oil. These ingredients can help to reduce inflammation and soothe the scalp.
  3. Take supplements: Taking supplements like biotin, vitamin D, and omega-3 fatty acids can help to promote hair growth and strengthen your hair.
  4. Avoid tight hairstyles: Avoid tight hairstyles like braids and ponytails, as they can pull on your hair and cause further hair loss.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you manage scalp psoriasis and prevent hair loss:

  • Wash your hair regularly: Washing your hair regularly can help to remove excess oil and dead skin cells from your scalp.
  • Eat a healthy diet: Eating a healthy diet that is rich in vitamins and nutrients can help to promote healthy hair growth.
  • Reduce stress: Stress can worsen the symptoms of scalp psoriasis and cause hair loss. Try to reduce stress by practicing relaxation techniques like yoga and meditation.

FAQ

Q: Can scalp psoriasis cause permanent hair loss?

A: In severe cases, scalp psoriasis can cause permanent hair loss. However, in most cases, the hair loss is temporary and will grow back once the condition is under control.

Q: Is scalp psoriasis contagious?

A: No, scalp psoriasis is not contagious.

Q: Can I dye my hair if I have scalp psoriasis?

A: It is best to avoid dyeing your hair if you have scalp psoriasis, as the chemicals in hair dye can irritate the scalp and worsen the condition.

Hair Care

If you have scalp psoriasis, it is important to take care of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid using hot tools: Hot tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and worsen the condition of your scalp.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb: Use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair, as a brush can be too harsh on your scalp.
  • Avoid using hair products with alcohol: Hair products that contain alcohol can dry out your hair and scalp, making the condition worse.

In conclusion, scalp psoriasis can cause hair loss, but with proper management and treatment, you can prevent further hair loss and promote healthy hair growth. Consult a dermatologist if you are experiencing severe hair loss or if your symptoms are not improving with home treatments. Remember to take care of your hair and scalp, and avoid harsh treatments that can worsen the condition.
