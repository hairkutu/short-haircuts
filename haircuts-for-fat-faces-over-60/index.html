Haircuts For Fat Faces Over 60 A Guide To Flattering Styles

Written by Robby Aug 11, 2023 · 8 min read

As we age, our face shape may change, and for those with a rounder or fuller face, finding the perfect haircut can be a challenge. If you're over 60 and have a fat face, don't worry – there are plenty of flattering hairstyles that can help you look and feel your best. In this guide, we'll share some tips, tricks, and ideas for haircuts for fat faces over 60.

Description: What is a Fat Face?

A fat face, also known as a round or fuller face, is a face shape where the cheeks and jawline are wider or fuller than the forehead and chin. This can be due to genetics, weight gain, or age-related changes in the face. While there's nothing wrong with having a fat face, some people may want to find a haircut that helps balance their features or creates a more flattering look.

How to Choose the Right Haircut for a Fat Face

When choosing a haircut for a fat face, it's important to consider your face shape, hair texture, and personal style. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

1. Consider a Layered Cut

A layered cut can create movement and texture in the hair, which can help elongate the face and draw attention away from the cheeks. This can be a great option for those with thick or curly hair.

2. Opt for Side-Swept Bangs

Side-swept bangs can help create angles in the face and soften a rounder jawline. They can also draw attention to the eyes and add dimension to the hair.

3. Avoid Blunt Cuts

A blunt cut can accentuate the roundness of the face, so it's best to avoid this style if you have a fat face. Instead, opt for a layered or textured cut.

4. Consider a Bob

A bob can be a great option for those with a fat face, as it can create a sleek and elongated look. You can choose from a variety of bob styles, including a chin-length bob or a longer bob that grazes the shoulders.

Step-by-Step Guide to Getting a Haircut for a Fat Face

Here's a step-by-step guide to getting a flattering haircut for a fat face:

Step 1: Find Inspiration

Before heading to the salon, find some inspiration for your new haircut. Look for styles that complement your face shape and hair texture.

Step 2: Consult with Your Stylist

When you arrive at the salon, consult with your stylist about your desired haircut. They can provide recommendations based on your face shape and hair texture.

Step 3: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Before getting your haircut, your stylist will wash and condition your hair to ensure it's clean and ready for cutting.

Step 4: Get the Haircut

Your stylist will then begin cutting your hair, following the style you discussed in your consultation.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Once your haircut is complete, your stylist will style your hair using products and tools that work best for your hair type and preferences.

Tips for Maintaining and Styling Your Hair

Once you have a flattering haircut for your fat face, it's important to maintain and style your hair to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

1. Use Products that Suit Your Hair Type

Whether you have thick, thin, curly, or straight hair, it's important to use products that are designed for your hair type. This will help keep your hair healthy and looking its best.

2. Embrace Your Natural Texture

Instead of fighting your hair's natural texture, embrace it. This will make styling your hair easier and can also create a more youthful and natural look.

3. Experiment with Accessories

Accessories can be a great way to add interest and personality to your hairstyle. Try adding a headband, clip, or scarf to your hair for a fun and stylish touch.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best haircut for a fat face?

The best haircut for a fat face depends on your face shape, hair texture, and personal style. Some flattering options include layered cuts, side-swept bangs, and bobs.

Can I still have long hair if I have a fat face?

Yes, you can still have long hair if you have a fat face. However, it's important to choose a style that creates movement and texture in the hair to help balance your features.

How often should I get my hair cut?

It's recommended to get a haircut every 4-6 weeks to maintain healthy and well-groomed hair.

Haircuts for Fat Faces Over 60: Final Thoughts

Having a fat face shouldn't hold you back from feeling confident and stylish. With the right haircut and styling, you can embrace your unique features and look your best at any age. Use the tips and ideas in this guide to find the perfect haircut for your fat face over 60.
