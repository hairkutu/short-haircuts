Do Thinning Scissors Damage Your Hair

Written by Oliver Mar 21, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Thinning scissors are a popular tool used by hairdressers to create layers and texture in hair. However, there has been some debate about whether or not thinning scissors can damage your hair. In this article, we will explore this topic in depth and provide you with some tips on how to use thinning scissors safely.

Table of Contents

  • How to Use Thinning Scissors
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Using Thinning Scissors
  • Tips for Using Thinning Scissors Safely
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Using Thinning Scissors

Description

Thinning scissors are a type of hair cutting tool that has teeth or notches on one or both blades. These teeth or notches are designed to remove hair in a way that creates texture and volume. Thinning scissors are often used to create layers in the hair or to thin out thick hair.

While thinning scissors can be a useful tool for hairdressers, there is some concern that they can damage the hair. This is because the teeth or notches on the scissors can create small, micro-cuts in the hair shaft. Over time, these micro-cuts can weaken the hair and cause breakage or split ends.

How to Use Thinning Scissors

If you are going to use thinning scissors on your own hair or someone else's, it is important to use them correctly. Here are some tips:

  • Make sure the hair is clean and dry before using thinning scissors.
  • Only use thinning scissors on the mid-lengths and ends of the hair.
  • Don't use thinning scissors on wet hair.
  • Don't use thinning scissors on hair that is already damaged or weakened.
  • Use a light touch when using thinning scissors. Don't pull or tug at the hair.

Step-by-Step Guide to Using Thinning Scissors

Here is a step-by-step guide to using thinning scissors:

  1. Brush the hair to remove any tangles.
  2. Section the hair and clip it out of the way.
  3. Hold the thinning scissors at a 45-degree angle to the hair.
  4. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers.
  5. Open and close the thinning scissors several times on the section of hair, moving from the mid-lengths to the ends.
  6. Repeat on the other sections of hair.
  7. Blend the sections of hair together with regular scissors, if desired.

Tips for Using Thinning Scissors Safely

Here are some additional tips for using thinning scissors safely:

  • Invest in a high-quality pair of thinning scissors. Cheap scissors can cause more damage to the hair.
  • Don't use thinning scissors too often. Once a month is plenty.
  • Don't use thinning scissors on hair that is already thin or fine.
  • Don't use thinning scissors on curly hair. They can cause the curls to become frizzy or flat.
  • Always clean and disinfect your thinning scissors after each use.

FAQs

Do thinning scissors cause split ends?

Thinning scissors can cause split ends if they are used incorrectly or too often. The micro-cuts created by the scissors can weaken the hair over time and cause it to split.

Can thinning scissors be used on all hair types?

Thinning scissors can be used on most hair types, but they are not recommended for curly or fine hair.

How often should you use thinning scissors?

You should only use thinning scissors once a month at most. Overuse can cause damage to the hair.

Hair Care Tips for Using Thinning Scissors

Here are some additional hair care tips for using thinning scissors:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep the hair healthy and strong.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often. Heat can weaken the hair and cause breakage.
  • Trim the ends of the hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or hair dyes on the hair.

In conclusion, thinning scissors can be a useful tool for creating texture and volume in the hair. However, it is important to use them correctly and to take care of the hair to prevent damage. By following the tips and guidelines in this article, you can safely use thinning scissors to achieve the hairstyle you desire.
