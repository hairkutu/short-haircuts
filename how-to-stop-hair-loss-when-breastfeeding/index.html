How To Stop Hair Loss When Breastfeeding

Written by Oliver May 20, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you experiencing hair loss while breastfeeding? This is a common concern among new moms. The good news is that it’s usually temporary and there are ways to prevent or minimize it. In this article, we’ll discuss how to stop hair loss when breastfeeding.

Table of Contents

  • How to Stop Hair Loss When Breastfeeding
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How to Stop Hair Loss When Breastfeeding

The first step to stopping hair loss when breastfeeding is to identify the cause. Most commonly, it’s due to hormonal changes in the body. When a woman is pregnant, her body produces higher levels of estrogen, which can cause hair to remain in the growth phase for longer than usual. After giving birth, estrogen levels drop, and hair follicles may enter the resting phase, leading to hair loss.

In addition to hormonal changes, other factors that can contribute to hair loss while breastfeeding include stress, poor nutrition, and lack of sleep. Fortunately, there are several steps you can take to minimize hair loss and promote healthy hair growth.

Step by Step

1. Eat a Healthy Diet

Make sure you’re getting enough vitamins and minerals in your diet, especially iron and biotin. Iron is important for healthy hair growth, and biotin helps to strengthen hair. Good sources of iron include lean meats, beans, and leafy green vegetables. Biotin can be found in eggs, nuts, and whole grains.

2. Take a Hair Supplement

If you’re not getting enough nutrients from your diet, consider taking a hair supplement. Look for one that contains biotin, iron, and other vitamins and minerals that are important for healthy hair growth.

3. Manage Stress

Stress can contribute to hair loss, so it’s important to find ways to manage it. Try to get enough sleep, exercise regularly, and practice relaxation techniques like yoga or meditation.

4. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles like braids, buns, and ponytails can put stress on your hair follicles and cause them to break. Try to wear your hair down or in loose styles as much as possible.

5. Be Gentle with Your Hair

Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, and be gentle when brushing or combing it. Use a wide-tooth comb and start at the ends, working your way up to the roots.

Tips

  • Use a mild shampoo that’s gentle on your scalp.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools like flat irons and curling irons.
  • Take breaks between chemical treatments like relaxers and perms.
  • Consider getting a shorter haircut to reduce the weight of your hair and minimize breakage.

FAQ

Can breastfeeding cause hair loss?

Yes, hormonal changes during breastfeeding can cause hair loss. However, it’s usually temporary and should stop once breastfeeding ends.

How long does hair loss last after pregnancy?

Postpartum hair loss usually starts around 3-4 months after giving birth and can last up to a year.

Can I use hair loss treatments while breastfeeding?

It’s best to consult with your doctor before using any hair loss treatments while breastfeeding.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is important for preventing hair loss and promoting healthy hair growth. Make sure to wash your hair regularly with a mild shampoo, and avoid using hot styling tools and harsh chemicals. Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair, and avoid tight hairstyles that can cause stress on your hair follicles.

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can minimize hair loss while breastfeeding and promote healthy hair growth.
