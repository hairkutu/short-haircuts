What Did The Bible Say About Covering Of Hair

Written by Christine May 12, 2023 Â· 7 min read

As Christians, we often turn to the Bible for guidance on various aspects of life, including our appearance. One question that many Christian women have is whether or not they should cover their hair. This article will explore what the Bible has to say about covering of hair and provide some tips for those who choose to do so.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Cover Your Hair
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips for Covering Your Hair
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care for Covered Hair

Description

The Bible mentions covering of hair in several places, including 1 Corinthians 11:6-7, which says, "For if a woman does not cover her head, she might as well have her hair cut off; but if it is a disgrace for a woman to have her hair cut off or her head shaved, then she should cover her head. A man ought not to cover his head since he is the image and glory of God; but woman is the glory of man."

Many interpret this passage to mean that women should cover their hair as a sign of submission to their husbands and to God. However, there is some disagreement among scholars and Christians about the exact meaning of this passage and whether or not it is still applicable today.

How to Cover Your Hair

If you choose to cover your hair as a sign of submission to God and your husband, there are several methods you can use. Here are a few:

Headscarf

A headscarf is a simple and traditional way to cover your hair. You can choose a scarf that matches your outfit or opt for a plain color. Simply wrap the scarf around your head, making sure to cover all of your hair.

Hijab

A hijab is a headscarf that is commonly worn by Muslim women. It covers the hair and neck and can be styled in various ways. If you choose to wear a hijab, make sure to choose a style that is comfortable for you and that matches your outfit.

Hairnet

A hairnet is a small net that is worn over the hair to keep it in place and prevent it from falling out. This is a good option if you want to keep your hair out of your face but still want to cover it.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're new to covering your hair, here's a step-by-step guide to get you started:

  1. Choose your method of hair covering.
  2. Select a scarf or hairnet that matches your outfit.
  3. Wrap the scarf around your head, making sure to cover all of your hair.
  4. Secure the scarf in place with bobby pins or a hair clip.
  5. Adjust the scarf as needed to make sure it is comfortable and secure.

Tips for Covering Your Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when covering your hair:

  • Choose a method that is comfortable for you.
  • Make sure the scarf or hairnet is secure so it doesn't slip off.
  • Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you.
  • Don't feel like you have to cover your hair all the time. It's okay to take a break if you need to.

FAQs

Is covering your hair required by the Bible?

There is some disagreement among Christians about whether or not covering your hair is required by the Bible. Some interpret 1 Corinthians 11:6-7 to mean that women should cover their hair, while others believe that this passage is no longer applicable today.

Do I have to cover my hair all the time?

No, you don't have to cover your hair all the time. Some women choose to cover their hair only during church services or special occasions, while others cover their hair all the time.

Hair Care for Covered Hair

If you choose to cover your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools that can damage your hair.
  • Use a silk or satin scarf or hairnet to protect your hair while you sleep.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

Overall, the decision to cover your hair is a personal one that should be based on your own beliefs and convictions. If you choose to cover your hair, make sure to do so in a way that is comfortable for you and that reflects your personal style.
