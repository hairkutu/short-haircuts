Why Do My Eyebrow Hairs Stick Up

Written by Aprilia Sep 09, 2023 Â· 8 min read

As someone who has experienced this problem, I know how frustrating it can be to have eyebrow hairs that just won't stay in place. It can be a challenge to style your eyebrows or apply makeup when your hairs are sticking up all over the place. But why does this happen?

Description

Before we get into the specifics of why eyebrow hairs stick up, let's talk about what we mean by "stick up." When we say that our eyebrow hairs are sticking up, we're referring to hairs that are standing straight up or at an angle, rather than lying flat against the skin. This can make our brows look unruly and messy, and can be especially frustrating if we're trying to achieve a certain look.

How to

If you're dealing with eyebrow hairs that stick up, there are a few things you can do to help tame them. Here are some tips:

1. Use eyebrow gel

One of the easiest ways to keep eyebrow hairs in place is to use a clear eyebrow gel. This can help to hold your hairs in place throughout the day, and can be especially helpful if you're trying to achieve a certain shape or style.

2. Trim your hairs

If you have particularly long eyebrow hairs, they may be more prone to sticking up. Consider trimming your hairs with a pair of small scissors to keep them at a more manageable length.

3. Use a spoolie brush

A spoolie brush can be a great tool for grooming your eyebrows and keeping your hairs in place. Simply brush your hairs in the direction you want them to go, and they should stay in place.

4. Moisturize your skin

Dry skin can contribute to eyebrow hairs sticking up. Make sure to moisturize your skin regularly to keep it hydrated and healthy.

Step by Step

If you're not sure how to implement these tips, here's a step-by-step guide:

1. Cleanse your skin

Before you start grooming your eyebrows, make sure to cleanse your skin to remove any dirt or oil that may be contributing to your hairs sticking up.

2. Apply eyebrow gel

Using a clear eyebrow gel, brush your hairs in the direction you want them to go. This will help to hold them in place throughout the day.

3. Trim your hairs

If you have particularly long eyebrow hairs, use a small pair of scissors to trim them to a more manageable length.

4. Brush your hairs with a spoolie brush

Using a spoolie brush, brush your hairs in the direction you want them to go. This will help to keep them in place and give you a more polished look.

5. Moisturize your skin

Make sure to moisturize your skin regularly to keep it hydrated and healthy. This can help to prevent dryness that can contribute to eyebrow hairs sticking up.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you keep your eyebrow hairs in place:

- Avoid touching your eyebrows throughout the day, as this can cause your hairs to become disheveled. - Consider using a brow wax or pomade to help keep your hairs in place. - Don't over-pluck your eyebrows, as this can make it harder to achieve the look you want. - Be patient â it may take some time to find the right products and techniques that work for you.

FAQ

Q: Why do eyebrow hairs stick up?

A: There are a few reasons why eyebrow hairs may stick up. One common cause is dry skin, which can cause hairs to become more brittle and prone to sticking up. Additionally, genetics can play a role â some people simply have more unruly eyebrow hairs than others.

Q: Can eyebrow hairs be trained to stay in place?

A: Yes, with regular grooming and the use of products like eyebrow gel or wax, it is possible to train eyebrow hairs to stay in place.

Q: Is it bad to pluck eyebrow hairs that stick up?

A: It's generally not a good idea to over-pluck your eyebrows, as this can make it harder to achieve the look you want. However, if you have a few stray hairs that are bothering you, it's okay to pluck them.

Hair Care

Remember that taking care of your eyebrows is just as important as taking care of the hair on your head. Make sure to keep your skin hydrated, avoid over-plucking, and be patient as you work to train your eyebrow hairs to stay in place. With a little bit of effort and the right tools, you can achieve the look you want.
