Why Are My Curls Going Straight

Written by Oliver Jun 06, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you noticing that your once curly hair is starting to go straight? This can be frustrating, especially if you've spent a lot of time and money trying to maintain your curls. There are a few different reasons why your curls may be losing their shape, and in this post, we'll explore some of the common causes and what you can do to get your curls back.

Cause #1: Heat Damage

One of the most common reasons why curls start to go straight is heat damage. If you've been using hot tools like flat irons or curling irons on a regular basis, this can cause your hair to lose its natural curl pattern. Heat can damage the protein structures that give your hair its shape, so if you're using high heat regularly, your curls may start to lose their bounce.

Another way that heat can damage your curls is by causing split ends. When your ends are split, they won't curl as well, and this can make your hair look straighter overall.

Cause #2: Product Buildup

Product buildup can also contribute to curls going straight. If you're using a lot of heavy products like gels, mousses, or creams, this can weigh down your curls and make them look limp. Over time, this buildup can cause your curls to lose their shape and become straighter.

Another type of buildup that can affect your curls is mineral buildup. If you live in an area with hard water, the minerals in the water can build up on your hair and make it look dull and lifeless. This can also contribute to your curls losing their shape.

Cause #3: Hormonal Changes

Finally, hormonal changes can also affect your curls. When your hormones are out of balance, it can affect the health of your hair, and this can make it harder for your curls to maintain their shape. This is particularly common during pregnancy, menopause, and other times when your hormones are fluctuating.

Solution #1: Lay off the Heat

If heat damage is causing your curls to go straight, the best solution is to lay off the hot tools. Try air-drying your hair instead of using a blow dryer, and avoid using flat irons or curling irons on a regular basis. If you must use a hot tool, use a lower heat setting and always use a heat protectant spray.

Solution #2: Clarify Your Hair

If product buildup or mineral buildup is affecting your curls, try clarifying your hair with a clarifying shampoo. This will help to remove any buildup and restore your hair's natural bounce. You can also try using a chelating shampoo to remove mineral buildup specifically.

Solution #3: See a Doctor

If you suspect that hormonal changes are affecting your curls, it's a good idea to see a doctor. They can help to identify any underlying health issues and recommend treatments to restore your hair's health.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Curls

Regardless of the cause of your curls going straight, there are some tips you can follow to maintain healthy curls:

  • Avoid using hot tools on a regular basis
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using hot tools
  • Avoid using heavy products that can weigh down your curls
  • Clarify your hair regularly to remove any buildup
  • Deep condition your hair at least once a week
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry
  • Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair
  • Sleep on a satin pillowcase to reduce friction
  • Avoid wearing tight hairstyles that can cause tension on your hair

FAQ

Q: Can stress cause curls to go straight?

A: Yes, stress can affect the health of your hair and make it harder for your curls to maintain their shape.

Q: Can coloring or bleaching hair cause curls to go straight?

A: Yes, coloring or bleaching your hair can damage the protein structures in your hair and make it harder for your curls to maintain their shape.

Q: How often should I clarify my hair?

A: It depends on how much product buildup you have, but once a month is a good rule of thumb. If you have hard water, you may need to clarify more often.
