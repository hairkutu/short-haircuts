How To Fix Spotty Bleached Hair At Home A Guide To Diy Hair Repair

Written by Oliver Apr 30, 2023 · 7 min read

If you’ve ever bleached your hair at home, you know that the process can be both exciting and nerve-wracking. But what happens when your hair ends up looking spotty, uneven, or patchy? Don’t panic – there are several things you can do to fix spotty bleached hair at home. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps to DIY hair repair, answer some common questions, and provide tips for keeping your hair healthy.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Fix Spotty Bleached Hair at Home
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for DIY Hair Repair
  4. FAQ
  5. Hair Care Tips

How to Fix Spotty Bleached Hair at Home

Spotty bleached hair can be caused by a number of factors, including uneven application, overlapping bleach, or using too strong of a developer. To fix spotty bleached hair at home, you’ll need:

  • Clarifying shampoo
  • Deep conditioning treatment
  • Color depositing shampoo or toner (optional)
  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Plastic shower cap or plastic wrap

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to fix spotty bleached hair at home:

  1. Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo. This will help remove any buildup or residue from your hair and scalp.
  2. Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair, focusing on the areas that are most damaged or spotty. Use a wide-tooth comb to distribute the conditioner evenly through your hair.
  3. Cover your hair with a plastic shower cap or plastic wrap and leave the conditioner on for at least 30 minutes. You can also leave it on overnight for a more intensive treatment.
  4. Rinse your hair thoroughly with cool water and towel dry.
  5. If your hair is still spotty or uneven, you can use a color depositing shampoo or toner to help even out the color. Choose a shade that matches your hair color or is slightly darker to help cover up any spots or patches.
  6. Apply the color depositing shampoo or toner to your hair, following the instructions on the package. Leave it on for the recommended amount of time and then rinse it out with cool water.
  7. Condition your hair again and rinse with cool water.

Tips for DIY Hair Repair

To prevent spotty bleached hair in the future, follow these tips:

  • Always use a timer when bleaching your hair to ensure even application and processing time.
  • Use a lower volume developer if you have fine or damaged hair.
  • Avoid overlapping bleach on previously bleached or colored hair.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to keep it healthy and hydrated.
  • Use a color depositing shampoo or toner to refresh your color in between salon visits.

FAQ

Here are some commonly asked questions about fixing spotty bleached hair:

Can I fix spotty bleached hair at home?

Yes, you can fix spotty bleached hair at home using a clarifying shampoo, deep conditioning treatment, and color depositing shampoo or toner.

Will a color depositing shampoo or toner damage my hair?

No, color depositing shampoos and toners are designed to be gentle on your hair and won’t cause damage if used correctly.

How often should I deep condition my hair?

You should deep condition your hair once a week if it’s damaged or dry, or once a month if it’s healthy.

Hair Care Tips

To keep your hair healthy and prevent spotty bleached hair, follow these hair care tips:

  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often, as they can cause damage and dryness.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes on your hair.
  • Eat a balanced diet with plenty of protein and vitamins to promote healthy hair growth.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip it of its natural oils.

By following these tips and using the steps outlined in this guide, you can fix spotty bleached hair at home and keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful.
