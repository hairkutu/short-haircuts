How Long Can Army Hair Be

Written by Robby Apr 07, 2023 Â· 6 min read

As an enlisted soldier or officer in the U.S. Army, it is important to adhere to the grooming standards set forth by the military. One aspect of this is the length of one's hair. In this article, we will discuss how long army hair can be for those with relaxed hair.

Description

Army grooming standards are put in place to ensure that all soldiers have a neat and professional appearance. This includes guidelines for hair length, which vary based on gender and hairstyle. For those with relaxed hair, there are specific guidelines to follow.

How To

Before discussing the specific length requirements, it is important to note that all soldiers are expected to maintain good hygiene and grooming habits. This means regularly washing and conditioning hair, as well as keeping it neat and tidy.

For those with relaxed hair, the maximum length allowed is four inches. This is measured from the scalp to the end of the hair when it is in a natural, unstraightened state. If the hair is straightened, it must still fall within the four-inch limit.

It is also important to note that the hair must not touch the collar, ears, or eyebrows when it is dry. This means that even if the hair is under four inches when wet, it must be styled in a way that keeps it from touching these areas when dry.

Step by Step

To ensure that your hair meets the army's grooming standards, follow these steps:

  1. Regularly wash and condition your hair.
  2. Ensure that your hair is no longer than four inches from the scalp when in a natural state.
  3. If straightening your hair, ensure that it still falls within the four-inch limit.
  4. Style your hair in a way that keeps it from touching your collar, ears, or eyebrows when dry.
  5. Regularly trim your hair to maintain the appropriate length.

Tips

Here are a few tips to help you maintain the appropriate hair length:

  • Visit a professional stylist who is familiar with army grooming standards to ensure that your hair is cut to the appropriate length.
  • Invest in quality hair products to keep your hair healthy and manageable.
  • Consider wearing a headband or hair tie to keep your hair away from your face and ensure that it does not touch your eyebrows.

FAQ

Q: What happens if my hair is longer than four inches?

A: If your hair is longer than four inches, you will be required to cut it to meet the army's grooming standards. Failure to do so may result in disciplinary action.

Q: Can I wear my hair in a ponytail or bun?

A: Yes, as long as the style does not cause your hair to touch your collar, ears, or eyebrows when dry.

Q: Can I wear a wig or extensions?

A: Yes, as long as the wig or extensions do not cause your hair to touch your collar, ears, or eyebrows when dry.

Hair Care

Maintaining the appropriate hair length is just one aspect of hair care in the army. It is important to also keep your hair clean, healthy, and styled in a professional manner.

Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and healthy. Avoid using too much heat when styling your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage. Consider wearing a protective style, such as braids or twists, to minimize damage and make styling easier.

Remember, your appearance is a reflection of your professionalism and dedication to the army. By following the grooming standards and taking care of your hair, you can ensure that you are presenting yourself in the best possible light.
