Does Breastfeeding Prevent Postpartum Hair Loss

Written by Eveline Apr 21, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Postpartum hair loss is a common concern for many new mothers. During pregnancy, hormonal changes can lead to thicker and fuller hair. However, after giving birth, hormones return to their pre-pregnancy levels, causing hair to shed at a faster rate than usual. This can lead to noticeable hair loss and thinning, which can be distressing for many women. One popular theory is that breastfeeding can help prevent postpartum hair loss. In this article, we'll explore this theory and provide tips for managing postpartum hair loss.

Does Breastfeeding Really Help Prevent Postpartum Hair Loss?

While there is no definitive answer to whether breastfeeding prevents postpartum hair loss, some studies suggest that it may have a protective effect. One study found that women who breastfed exclusively for six months or more were less likely to experience postpartum hair loss than those who did not breastfeed or who breastfed for a shorter period of time. Another study found that breastfeeding was associated with a lower risk of postpartum hair loss, even after adjusting for other factors such as age, body mass index, and hair length.

There are several possible explanations for why breastfeeding may help prevent postpartum hair loss. One is that breastfeeding causes the body to produce higher levels of prolactin, a hormone that can stimulate hair growth. Another is that breastfeeding may help regulate hormone levels, reducing the sudden drop in estrogen that can trigger hair loss.

How to Manage Postpartum Hair Loss

Whether or not you breastfeed, postpartum hair loss can be a difficult experience. Here are some tips for managing it:

1. Be Gentle with Your Hair

Avoid pulling your hair back tightly or using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools. Instead, opt for gentle hairstyles that don't put too much stress on your hair.

2. Eat a Balanced Diet

Eating a diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals can help support healthy hair growth. Focus on eating plenty of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins.

3. Take Care of Your Scalp

Keeping your scalp healthy can help promote hair growth. Try massaging your scalp with a gentle oil, such as coconut or jojoba oil, and use a gentle shampoo and conditioner.

4. Consider Hair Supplements

There are several supplements that may help promote hair growth, such as biotin, iron, and vitamin D. Talk to your doctor before taking any supplements.

5. Be Patient

Postpartum hair loss is a temporary condition, and most women will see their hair return to its pre-pregnancy state within a year. In the meantime, be patient and kind to yourself.

FAQ

Does breastfeeding cause hair loss?

No, breastfeeding itself does not cause hair loss. However, hormonal changes associated with breastfeeding and postpartum can lead to hair loss.

Is there anything I can do to prevent postpartum hair loss?

While there is no guaranteed way to prevent postpartum hair loss, some women find that breastfeeding, eating a healthy diet, and taking care of their scalp can help.

When will my hair stop falling out after pregnancy?

Most women will experience postpartum hair loss for several months after giving birth. Hair typically starts to grow back within a year.

Hair Care Tips for New Moms

Managing postpartum hair loss can be challenging, but there are steps you can take to promote healthy hair growth. Whether or not you breastfeed, focus on eating a balanced diet, taking care of your scalp, and being gentle with your hair. Remember that postpartum hair loss is a temporary condition, and that your hair will likely return to its pre-pregnancy state within a year. Be patient and kind to yourself as you navigate this challenging time.
