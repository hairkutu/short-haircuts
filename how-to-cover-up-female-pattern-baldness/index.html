How To Cover Up Female Pattern Baldness Tips And Tricks

Written by Bardi Aug 07, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're experiencing female pattern baldness, you're not alone. This common condition affects approximately 40% of women by the time they reach age 50. Female pattern baldness, also known as androgenetic alopecia, is a genetic condition that causes hair loss in women. While there's no cure for female pattern baldness, there are ways to cover up the hair loss and boost your confidence. In this article, we'll give you some tips and tricks on how to cover up female pattern baldness.

How to Cover Up Female Pattern Baldness

Here are some ways to cover up female pattern baldness:

1. Hairstyles

One of the easiest ways to cover up female pattern baldness is to change your hairstyle. A shorter haircut can create the illusion of thicker hair. Side-swept bangs can also help to cover up a receding hairline. Avoid hairstyles that pull your hair back tightly, as this can make your hair loss more noticeable.

2. Hairpieces

Hairpieces, such as wigs and hair extensions, can be a great way to cover up female pattern baldness. Wigs come in a variety of styles, colors, and textures, so you can find one that looks natural and complements your face shape. Hair extensions can add volume and length to your hair, making it look fuller and thicker.

3. Hair Fibers

Hair fibers are tiny, colored fibers that attach to your existing hair to make it look thicker. They work by clinging to your hair with static electricity. Hair fibers are easy to use and can be applied in just a few minutes. They're also waterproof and sweat-resistant, so they won't come off until you wash your hair.

4. Scarves and Headwraps

Scarves and headwraps can be a stylish way to cover up female pattern baldness. They come in a variety of styles and colors, so you can find one that matches your personal style. Scarves and headwraps can also protect your scalp from the sun and wind, which can further damage your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide to Covering Up Female Pattern Baldness

Here's a step-by-step guide to covering up female pattern baldness:

Step 1: Choose a Style

Choose a hairstyle, hairpiece, or headwrap that complements your face shape and personal style.

Step 2: Apply Hair Fibers

If you're using hair fibers, apply them to your existing hair to make it look thicker.

Step 3: Put on Your Hairpiece or Headwrap

If you're using a hairpiece or headwrap, put it on and adjust it so that it looks natural and feels comfortable.

Step 4: Secure Your Hairpiece or Headwrap

Secure your hairpiece or headwrap with bobby pins or combs to ensure that it stays in place.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Style your hair as desired, using hairspray or other styling products to keep it in place.

Tips for Covering Up Female Pattern Baldness

Here are some tips for covering up female pattern baldness:

1. Use Hair Products

Use hair products, such as volumizing mousse or thickening spray, to make your hair look fuller.

2. Protect Your Hair

Protect your hair from further damage by avoiding harsh chemicals, heat styling, and tight hairstyles.

3. Get Regular Haircuts

Get regular haircuts to keep your hair looking healthy and to prevent split ends.

FAQs About Female Pattern Baldness

Q: What Causes Female Pattern Baldness?

A: Female pattern baldness is caused by genetics and hormonal changes.

Q: Is Female Pattern Baldness Treatable?

A: While there's no cure for female pattern baldness, there are treatments that can slow down or stop the hair loss, such as minoxidil and finasteride.

Q: Can Stress Cause Female Pattern Baldness?

A: Stress can contribute to hair loss, but it's not a direct cause of female pattern baldness.

Hair Care for Female Pattern Baldness

If you're experiencing female pattern baldness, it's important to take good care of your hair to prevent further damage. Here are some tips:

1. Use Gentle Hair Products

Use gentle, sulfate-free hair products to avoid further damaging your hair.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid heat styling, such as blow-drying, curling, and straightening, as this can further damage your hair.

3. Protect Your Scalp

Protect your scalp from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.

4. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals, such as iron and biotin, which are essential for healthy hair.


In conclusion, female pattern baldness can be a challenging condition to deal with, but there are ways to cover up the hair loss and boost your confidence. By using hairstyles, hairpieces, and hair fibers, you can create the illusion of thicker hair. Additionally, taking good care of your hair through gentle hair products, avoiding heat styling, and eating a healthy diet can help to prevent further damage. If you're experiencing female pattern baldness, don't hesitate to talk to your doctor or a hair loss specialist for advice.
