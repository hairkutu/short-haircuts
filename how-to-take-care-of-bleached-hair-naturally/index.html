How To Take Care Of Bleached Hair Naturally A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Jun 08, 2023 · 10 min read

Bleaching your hair can give you a whole new look, but it also comes with a lot of maintenance. Bleached hair is more prone to damage and breakage, and it requires special care to keep it healthy and strong. In this guide, we’ll show you how to take care of bleached hair naturally, so you can enjoy your new look without sacrificing the health of your hair.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Take Care of Bleached Hair Naturally
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Maintaining Bleached Hair
  4. Frequently Asked Questions
  5. General Hair Care Tips

How to Take Care of Bleached Hair Naturally

When it comes to taking care of bleached hair, natural products are your best friend. Chemical-laden hair products can further damage your hair and strip it of its natural oils, leaving it dry and brittle. Here are some natural ways to take care of bleached hair:

1. Use Coconut Oil

Coconut oil is a natural elixir for hair. It helps to nourish and protect hair from damage, making it an excellent choice for bleached hair. Apply coconut oil to your hair once a week, focusing on the ends, and leave it on for at least an hour before washing it off with a mild shampoo.

2. Try Apple Cider Vinegar

Apple cider vinegar is a natural conditioner that helps to restore the pH balance of your hair. It also helps to remove buildup from hair products and hard water, which can make bleached hair look dull and lifeless. Mix equal parts of apple cider vinegar and water, and use it as a final rinse after shampooing your hair.

3. Use Aloe Vera

Aloe vera is a natural moisturizer that helps to hydrate and soothe the scalp. It also helps to prevent breakage and promote healthy hair growth. Apply aloe vera gel to your hair once a week, focusing on the scalp, and leave it on for 30 minutes before washing it off with a mild shampoo.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the natural ways to take care of bleached hair, here’s a step-by-step guide to help you incorporate them into your hair care routine:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair with a mild shampoo that’s free of sulfates and parabens. Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, while parabens can cause irritation and damage to the scalp. Rinse your hair thoroughly with lukewarm water.

Step 2: Apply Coconut Oil

Apply coconut oil to your hair, focusing on the ends. Massage it in gently, and leave it on for at least an hour. You can also leave it on overnight for maximum benefits.

Step 3: Rinse with Apple Cider Vinegar

Mix equal parts of apple cider vinegar and water, and use it as a final rinse after shampooing your hair. This will help to restore the pH balance of your hair and remove buildup from hair products.

Step 4: Apply Aloe Vera Gel

Apply aloe vera gel to your hair, focusing on the scalp. Leave it on for 30 minutes, and then wash it off with a mild shampoo.

Tips for Maintaining Bleached Hair

In addition to the natural remedies we’ve mentioned, here are some tips for maintaining bleached hair:

1. Use a Heat Protectant

Heat styling tools can further damage bleached hair, so it’s important to use a heat protectant before using them. A heat protectant will create a barrier between your hair and the heat, preventing damage and breakage.

2. Avoid Over-Shampooing

Shampooing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, leaving it dry and brittle. Try to limit your shampooing to once or twice a week, and use a mild shampoo that’s free of sulfates and parabens.

3. Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

A deep conditioning treatment can help to restore moisture to bleached hair and prevent breakage. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week, focusing on the ends of your hair.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Can I bleach my hair naturally?

Yes, you can bleach your hair naturally using lemon juice or chamomile tea. However, natural bleaching methods can take longer to achieve the desired result and may not be as effective as chemical bleaching.

2. How often should I trim my hair?

You should trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and breakage. Trimming your hair regularly will also help it to grow longer and stronger.

3. Can I use hair masks on bleached hair?

Yes, you can use hair masks on bleached hair to restore moisture and prevent breakage. Look for hair masks that are specifically formulated for bleached hair.

General Hair Care Tips

In addition to the tips we’ve mentioned for taking care of bleached hair, here are some general hair care tips that will help to keep your hair healthy and strong:

1. Eat a Balanced Diet

A balanced diet that’s rich in vitamins and nutrients is essential for healthy hair. Make sure to eat plenty of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins.

2. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

UV rays can damage your hair and cause it to become dry and brittle. Wear a hat or use a hair product that contains SPF to protect your hair from the sun.

3. Don’t Brush Wet Hair

Wet hair is more prone to breakage, so it’s important to be gentle when brushing it. Use a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush to gently remove tangles.

By following these tips and using natural remedies, you can keep your bleached hair healthy and strong. Remember to be gentle with your hair and avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools. With a little extra care, you can enjoy your new look without sacrificing the health of your hair.
