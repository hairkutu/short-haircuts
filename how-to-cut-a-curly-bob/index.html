How To Cut A Curly Bob A Step By Step Guide

Are you tired of your long curly locks and looking for a fresh new look? A curly bob might be just what you need to switch things up. Cutting curly hair can be intimidating, but with the right technique and tools, you can achieve a beautiful new hairstyle.

How to Cut a Curly Bob

The first step in cutting a curly bob is to decide on the length and style you want. A bob is typically chin-length or slightly longer, with layers that add volume and movement to the hair. You can opt for a blunt cut or a more textured look, depending on your preferences.

It's important to note that cutting curly hair when it's dry is recommended, as wet hair can stretch and appear longer than it actually is. This can result in a shorter cut than you intended.

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Start with clean, dry hair.

Wash and dry your hair as you normally would, then let it air dry completely. It's important to start with dry hair to ensure an accurate cut.

2. Section your hair.

Divide your hair into four sections: top, sides, and back. Clip each section out of the way with hair clips.

3. Start cutting.

Start with the back section of your hair, and take a small section of hair to start. Hold the hair between your fingers, and cut the ends straight across. Work your way up the back section, cutting small sections of hair at a time.

4. Move to the sides.

Release the side sections of your hair, and repeat the same process as the back section. Take small sections of hair, and cut the ends straight across. Be sure to blend the side sections into the back section to create a seamless look.

5. Finish with the top section.

Release the top section of your hair, and repeat the same process as the sides and back. Take small sections of hair, and cut the ends straight across.

6. Add texture.

Once you've cut your hair to the desired length, you can add texture to create volume and movement. Use thinning shears to remove bulk from the ends of your hair.

7. Style your hair.

Now that your hair is cut, you can style it as you normally would. Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to enhance your natural curls, or use a curling iron to add more defined curls.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

Cutting curly hair can be challenging, but with these tips, you can achieve a beautiful curly bob:

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Cut your hair when it's dry to ensure an accurate cut.
  • Start with small sections of hair, and work your way up.
  • Blend the sections of your hair to create a seamless look.
  • Use thinning shears to add texture and volume to your hair.

FAQs

How often should I get my curly bob trimmed?

It's recommended that you get your curly bob trimmed every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and style.

Can I cut my own curly bob at home?

While it's possible to cut your own curly bob at home, it's recommended that you go to a professional stylist to ensure an accurate and even cut.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Curly hair requires special care to maintain its shape and style. Here are some tips for caring for your curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it's wet.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.

Cutting a curly bob can be intimidating, but with the right technique and tools, you can achieve a beautiful new look. Follow these steps and tips to get the perfect curly bob for your face shape and hair type.
