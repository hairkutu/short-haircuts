Why Is My Hair Spiky After Haircut

Written by Oliver Aug 10, 2023 · 7 min read

Have you ever experienced getting a haircut and ending up with spiky hair? If yes, then you might be wondering why it happens. This article will explain why your hair can be spiky after a haircut and how you can fix it.

Table of Content:

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Spiky hair after a haircut can happen due to several reasons. One of the most common causes is the use of a new hair product that you're not used to. The product can make your hair stiff and hard, resulting in a spiky appearance. Another reason could be that the hairstylist used a different technique than what you're used to, resulting in a different look than what you were expecting.

It's also possible that your hair is naturally coarse and thick, which can make it more prone to spiking up after a haircut. This happens because your hair is trying to find its natural direction, and it's pushing up and out of the scalp.

How to

If you want to avoid having spiky hair after a haircut, there are a few things you can do. First, make sure to communicate with your hairstylist about the look you're going for, and ask them to use techniques that will achieve that look.

Second, avoid using new hair products before your haircut. Stick to the ones you know and love to avoid any surprises.

Lastly, if you have naturally coarse and thick hair, try using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to soften your hair and make it more manageable.

Step by step

Here are some steps you can take to fix spiky hair after a haircut:

  1. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to soften your hair.
  3. Blow-dry your hair using a round brush to smooth it out.
  4. Style your hair using a pomade or hair wax to achieve the look you want.

Tips

  • Communicate clearly with your hairstylist about the look you want to achieve.
  • Stick to hair products you know and love to avoid any surprises.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to soften your hair if it's naturally coarse and thick.
  • Blow-dry your hair using a round brush to smooth it out.
  • Use a pomade or hair wax to style your hair and achieve the look you want.

FAQ

Q: Can a bad haircut cause spiky hair?

A: Yes, a bad haircut can cause spiky hair. If your hairstylist uses the wrong techniques or cuts your hair too short, it can result in a spiky appearance.

Q: How long does it take for spiky hair to go away?

A: It depends on the cause of your spiky hair. If it's due to product buildup, washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo can remove it. If it's due to a bad haircut, it can take a few weeks for your hair to grow out and regain its natural shape.

Q: Can I fix spiky hair myself?

A: Yes, you can fix spiky hair yourself by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo, using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil, blow-drying your hair with a round brush, and styling it with a pomade or hair wax.

Hair Care

To keep your hair healthy and prevent spiky hair after a haircut, make sure to take good care of your hair. This includes washing it regularly, using a conditioner or hair oil, and avoiding harsh chemicals and heat styling tools.

You can also visit a professional hairstylist regularly for haircuts and treatments to keep your hair looking and feeling its best.

In conclusion, spiky hair after a haircut can happen due to several reasons, including the use of new hair products, different techniques, and naturally coarse and thick hair. To avoid spiky hair, communicate with your hairstylist, stick to hair products you know and love, and use leave-in conditioner or hair oil if necessary. If you end up with spiky hair, you can fix it by washing your hair, using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil, blow-drying it with a round brush, and styling it with a pomade or hair wax.
