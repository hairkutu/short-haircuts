Is My Hair Wavy Or Frizzy

Written by Bardi May 26, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you one of those people who struggle to identify whether their hair is wavy or frizzy? Don't worry; you're not alone. Many people have a hard time telling the difference between the two. But, it's essential to know whether your hair is wavy or frizzy to choose the right hair care routine. In this article, we'll help you identify whether your hair is wavy or frizzy and provide some tips on how to take care of it.

How to Identify Whether Your Hair is Wavy or Frizzy?

Before we dive into the tips, let's identify whether your hair is wavy or frizzy. Wavy hair has a natural S-shape pattern that gives it volume and texture. On the other hand, frizzy hair lacks the defined S-shape pattern and appears dry and rough. To determine whether your hair is wavy or frizzy, you can try the following:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, making it dry and frizzy. Rinse your hair thoroughly, and avoid rubbing it with a towel. Instead, gently squeeze out the excess water.

Step 2: Let Your Hair Air Dry

Air dry your hair without using any styling products. If your hair has a defined S-shape pattern and appears voluminous, it's wavy. But, if your hair appears dry, rough, and lacks a defined pattern, it's frizzy.

Step 3: Pay Attention to Humidity

If your hair becomes more defined and voluminous in humid weather, it's wavy. But, if it becomes more dry and rough, it's frizzy.

Tips for Taking Care of Wavy Hair

If your hair is wavy, you should follow the tips below to maintain its natural texture and volume:

Tip 1: Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it's wet. Brushing your hair can break the natural S-shape pattern and make it frizzy.

Tip 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to keep it moisturized and prevent frizz. Focus on the ends and avoid applying it to the roots.

Tip 3: Use a Diffuser

Use a diffuser attachment on your hairdryer to dry your hair gently. The diffuser helps to maintain the natural S-shape pattern and add volume to your hair.

Tips for Taking Care of Frizzy Hair

If your hair is frizzy, you should follow the tips below to tame it and make it smoother:

Tip 1: Use a Satin Pillowcase

Use a satin pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent breakage. Cotton pillowcases can absorb moisture from your hair, making it dry and frizzy.

Tip 2: Apply a Hair Mask

Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair and prevent frizz. Look for a hair mask that contains moisturizing ingredients like coconut oil, shea butter, or avocado oil.

Tip 3: Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons. Heat styling can further damage your hair and make it more frizzy. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and set them to a low temperature.

FAQ about Wavy and Frizzy Hair

Q1: Can I have both wavy and frizzy hair?

A: Yes, you can have both wavy and frizzy hair. It's common for people with wavy hair to experience frizz, especially in humid weather.

Q2: Can I make my hair wavy or frizzy?

A: No, you can't make your hair wavy or frizzy. It's a natural texture determined by your genetics.

Q3: Can hair products make my hair wavy or frizzy?

A: No, hair products can't change your hair's natural texture. But, they can enhance it and help maintain its natural pattern.

Hair Care Routine for Wavy and Frizzy Hair

Regardless of whether your hair is wavy or frizzy, you should follow a hair care routine that keeps it healthy and moisturized. Here's a basic hair care routine you can follow:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. Rinse your hair thoroughly, and avoid rubbing it with a towel. Instead, gently squeeze out the excess water.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to keep it moisturized and prevent frizz. Focus on the ends and avoid applying it to the roots.

Step 3: Dry Your Hair Gently

Dry your hair gently using a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt. Avoid rubbing your hair with a towel as it can cause frizz.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Style your hair using a hair mousse, gel, or cream that enhances your natural texture. Use a diffuser attachment on your hairdryer to dry your hair gently.

Conclusion

Knowing whether your hair is wavy or frizzy is essential to choose the right hair care routine. Follow the tips and hair care routine mentioned in this article to maintain healthy and beautiful hair. Don't forget to embrace your natural texture and love your hair!
