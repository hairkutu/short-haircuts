How To Know If You Will Suit Short Hair

Written by Aprilia Jun 25, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Short hair is a bold and beautiful look that can completely transform your appearance. However, before you take the plunge and chop off your locks, it's important to consider whether short hair will suit your face shape, hair texture, and personal style. In this article, we'll show you how to know if you will suit short hair and provide tips for caring for your new 'do.

Table of Contents

  • Determining Your Face Shape
  • Assessing Your Hair Texture
  • Considering Your Personal Style
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Choosing Short Hair
  • Tips for Maintaining Short Hair
  • FAQs About Short Hair
  • Hair Care Tips for Short Hair

Determining Your Face Shape

One of the most important factors to consider when deciding whether to get short hair is your face shape. Certain short hairstyles may flatter round faces, while others may look better on heart-shaped or oval faces. To determine your face shape, look in the mirror and observe the following:

  • Measure the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline.
  • Observe the shape of your face: do you have a round, oval, square, heart-shaped, or diamond-shaped face?
  • Consider your facial features: do you have a prominent nose, large ears, or a high forehead?

Once you have determined your face shape, you can research short hairstyles that are best suited for your features.

Assessing Your Hair Texture

Another important factor to consider is your hair texture. Short hair can look great on thick, curly hair, but may not be as flattering on fine, straight hair. Consider the following when determining whether short hair will suit your hair texture:

  • Is your hair thick or thin?
  • Is your hair naturally straight, wavy, or curly?
  • Do you have any hair concerns, such as frizz or breakage?

If you have fine, thin hair, you may want to opt for a shorter layered haircut that adds volume and movement. If you have thick, curly hair, a pixie cut or a bob may be a great option.

Considering Your Personal Style

Lastly, it's important to consider your personal style when deciding whether to get short hair. Short hair can be edgy, playful, sophisticated, or classic, depending on the cut and styling. Ask yourself the following:

  • What is your personal style? Are you more of a trendsetter or a traditionalist?
  • Do you prefer low-maintenance or high-maintenance hairstyles?
  • Are you willing to experiment with styling products and techniques?

If you're more of a low-maintenance type, a short, textured bob may be a great option. If you're willing to experiment, a buzz cut or a sleek, asymmetrical pixie cut may be more your style.

Step-by-Step Guide to Choosing Short Hair

If you're still unsure whether short hair will suit you, follow this step-by-step guide:

  1. Determine your face shape.
  2. Assess your hair texture.
  3. Consider your personal style.
  4. Research short hairstyles that flatter your face shape, hair texture, and personal style.
  5. Consult with a hair stylist to determine which short haircut is right for you.
  6. Bring pictures and examples of hairstyles that you like to your appointment.
  7. Be open to suggestions from your stylist.
  8. Try on a wig or use a virtual hairstyling app to visualize your new look.
  9. Make the chop!
  10. Experiment with styling products and techniques to find your perfect short hair routine.

Tips for Maintaining Short Hair

Short hair requires less maintenance than long hair, but it still requires some TLC. Here are some tips for keeping your short hair looking its best:

  • Get regular trims to maintain your haircut's shape.
  • Invest in quality styling products, such as a good hairspray or a texturizing pomade.
  • Experiment with different styling techniques, such as blow-drying, flat ironing, or curling.
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray or serum.
  • Wash your hair every few days to prevent oil buildup.
  • Consider using a dry shampoo in between washes.

FAQs About Short Hair

Here are some frequently asked questions about short hair:

  • Will short hair make my face look rounder?
  • Can I still wear hair accessories with short hair?
  • Will short hair make me look older?
  • Can I still wear my hair in a ponytail with short hair?
  • How often should I get a haircut with short hair?

Hair Care Tips for Short Hair

Short hair requires different care than long hair. Here are some tips for keeping your short hair healthy and shiny:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner formulated for your hair type.
  • Massage your scalp when washing to stimulate hair growth.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle wet hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools every day to prevent heat damage.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it soft and moisturized.
  • Avoid using too much product, as it can weigh down your hair.

Now that you know how to know if you will suit short hair, it's time to take the plunge and try out a new 'do. Remember to consider your face shape, hair texture, and personal style before making the chop, and don't forget to follow our tips for maintaining and caring for your short hair.
