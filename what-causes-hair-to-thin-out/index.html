What Causes Hair To Thin Out Understanding The Common Causes

Written by Daniel Jul 22, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you experiencing hair thinning or hair loss? If so, you're not alone. Many men and women experience hair loss or thinning at some point in their lives. While genetics and age can play a role, there are other common causes that you should be aware of. In this article, we'll discuss what causes hair to thin out and what you can do about it.

Hormonal Changes

Hormonal changes can be a common cause of hair thinning. This can include changes in estrogen, testosterone, and thyroid hormones. Hormonal imbalances can cause hair to become thinner or fall out. Women may experience hair thinning or loss during pregnancy or menopause due to changes in hormone levels. Men may experience hair loss due to changes in testosterone levels.

Stress

Stress can also be a common cause of hair thinning. Stress can cause hair to enter the resting phase of the hair growth cycle prematurely, which can lead to hair thinning or loss. Stress can also cause hormonal imbalances, which can contribute to hair thinning.

Poor Nutrition

Poor nutrition can contribute to hair thinning. If you're not getting enough nutrients, your hair may become thin or brittle. A lack of protein, iron, and vitamin D can all contribute to hair loss. Eating a balanced diet with plenty of protein, iron, and vitamins can help keep your hair healthy.

Heat Damage

Heat damage can also contribute to hair thinning. Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair if used too frequently or at high temperatures. Over time, this can cause your hair to become thin or brittle. If you must use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant and limit your use.

Genetics

Genetics can play a role in hair thinning. Male and female pattern baldness are genetic conditions that can cause hair to become thinner or fall out. While you can't change your genetics, there are treatments available to help slow or stop hair loss.

Medications

Some medications can cause hair thinning or loss as a side effect. Chemotherapy drugs, blood thinners, and antidepressants are among the medications that can cause hair loss. If you're experiencing hair thinning or loss and are taking medications, talk to your doctor about your options.

Autoimmune Disorders

Autoimmune disorders can also contribute to hair thinning. Conditions like alopecia areata and lupus can cause hair loss or thinning. These conditions occur when the immune system attacks hair follicles. Treatment for autoimmune disorders can help slow or stop hair loss.

Thyroid Conditions

Thyroid conditions can also cause hair thinning. Hypothyroidism and hyperthyroidism can both contribute to hair loss. If you're experiencing hair thinning or loss and have thyroid conditions, talk to your doctor about your options.

Hair Care Practices

The way you care for your hair can also contribute to hair thinning. Tight hairstyles like braids and ponytails can cause hair to become thin or fall out. Chemical treatments like relaxers and perms can also damage hair over time. If you must use chemical treatments or tight hairstyles, be sure to take breaks and care for your hair properly.

Conclusion

While hair thinning or loss can be frustrating, there are many common causes that you should be aware of. Hormonal changes, stress, poor nutrition, heat damage, genetics, medications, autoimmune disorders, thyroid conditions, and hair care practices can all contribute to hair thinning. If you're experiencing hair thinning or loss, talk to your doctor about your options. With the right treatment and care, you can help keep your hair healthy and full.
