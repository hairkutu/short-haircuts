How To Work With Thick Hair Tips Tricks And Hair Care

Written by Aprilia May 04, 2023 · 8 min read

Having thick hair can be both a blessing and a curse. On one hand, it’s full of volume and body. On the other hand, it can be difficult to manage and style. In this article, we’ll share some tips and tricks on how to work with thick hair, as well as some hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  • How to Work with Thick Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care for Thick Hair

How to Work with Thick Hair

Thick hair can be challenging to work with, but with the right techniques and tools, you can make it work for you. Here are some tips:

1. Use the Right Products

When it comes to thick hair, the right products can make all the difference. Look for products that are specifically designed for thick hair, such as volumizing shampoos and conditioners, and styling products that provide hold and control without weighing your hair down.

2. Consider Your Haircut

The right haircut can also make a big difference. Talk to your stylist about a cut that will work well with your hair texture and thickness. Layers can help to reduce bulk and add movement, while a blunt cut can emphasize thickness.

3. Don’t Overwash Your Hair

Thick hair tends to be on the drier side, so avoid overwashing your hair, which can strip it of its natural oils. Aim to wash your hair every other day, or even less frequently if possible.

4. Use Heat Protectant

Thick hair can handle heat styling, but it’s important to use a heat protectant to prevent damage. Apply a heat protectant spray or cream before using a blow dryer, flat iron, or curling iron.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide to styling thick hair:

1. Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Wash your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner, then towel dry your hair and blow dry it until it’s completely dry.

2. Apply Styling Products

Apply a styling product, such as mousse or a styling cream, to your hair from roots to tips. This will help to add volume and control to your hair.

3. Blow Dry Your Hair

Using a round brush, blow dry your hair in sections, lifting your hair at the roots to add volume. Once your hair is dry, use a flat iron or curling iron to add texture and shape.

4. Finish with Hairspray

Finish your style with a light hairspray to hold your hair in place without weighing it down.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks for working with thick hair:

1. Use Sectioning Clips

Use sectioning clips to divide your hair into manageable sections while styling. This will help you to achieve a more polished look.

2. Experiment with Different Styles

Don’t be afraid to experiment with different styles, such as braids, updos, and half-up, half-down styles. With thick hair, the possibilities are endless!

3. Embrace Your Natural Texture

Instead of fighting your natural texture, embrace it! If you have curly or wavy hair, try using a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to enhance your curls.

FAQ

Q: How often should I wash my thick hair?

A: Aim to wash your hair every other day, or even less frequently if possible. Overwashing can strip your hair of its natural oils, which can lead to dryness and damage.

Q: What kind of haircut is best for thick hair?

A: Talk to your stylist about a cut that will work well with your hair texture and thickness. Layers can help to reduce bulk and add movement, while a blunt cut can emphasize thickness.

Hair Care for Thick Hair

In addition to styling tips, it’s important to take care of your thick hair to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some hair care tips:

1. Use a Moisturizing Conditioner

Thick hair tends to be on the drier side, so use a moisturizing conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

2. Avoid Overbrushing

Brushing your hair too often can cause breakage and damage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Just like your skin, your hair can be damaged by the sun’s UV rays. Wear a hat or use a leave-in conditioner with SPF to protect your hair.

By following these tips and tricks, you can learn to work with your thick hair and make it look its best!
