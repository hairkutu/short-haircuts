How To Fix Bleached Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Are you experiencing dry, damaged, and brittle hair due to bleaching? Do you want to know how to fix your bleached hair at home without going to a salon? You've come to the right place! In this blog post, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to repair bleached hair at home. We will also give you some tips and answer some frequently asked questions to help you take care of your hair afterwards.

How to Fix Bleached Hair at Home

Fixing bleached hair at home is possible, but it requires patience and dedication. You need to follow the right steps and use the right products to restore your hair's health and shine. Here are some things you can do to fix your bleached hair at home:

  • Moisturize your hair with deep conditioning treatments
  • Use protein treatments to strengthen your hair
  • Trim your hair to get rid of split ends
  • Avoid heat styling and chemical treatments
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash your hair

Start by washing your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo to remove any build-up and residue from your hair. Rinse your hair thoroughly and towel-dry it gently.

Step 2: Apply a deep conditioning treatment

Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair, focusing on the ends and damaged areas. Leave it on for at least 30 minutes, or longer if your hair is severely damaged. Rinse your hair thoroughly with water.

Step 3: Use a protein treatment

Use a protein treatment to strengthen your hair and prevent breakage. Apply it to your hair, focusing on the most damaged areas. Leave it on for 20-30 minutes, then rinse it off with water.

Step 4: Trim your hair

If your hair has a lot of split ends, it's time to trim it. Use sharp scissors to cut off the damaged ends. Don't cut too much, as you want to keep as much length as possible.

Step 5: Avoid heat styling and chemical treatments

Avoid using heat styling tools such as blow dryers, flat irons, and curling irons. They can further damage your hair and make it more brittle. Also, avoid chemical treatments such as coloring and perming, as they can weaken your hair.

Step 6: Protect your hair

Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather by wearing a hat or scarf. You can also use a leave-in conditioner with SPF to protect your hair from UV rays.

Tips for Hair Care

Here are some tips to help you take care of your hair after fixing it:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid washing your hair too often
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I fix my bleached hair at home?

Yes, you can fix your bleached hair at home. It requires patience and dedication, but it's possible to restore your hair's health and shine.

How long does it take to fix bleached hair?

It depends on the severity of the damage. It can take several weeks or months to fully repair bleached hair.

Can I use hair masks to fix my bleached hair?

Yes, hair masks can help moisturize and repair bleached hair. Look for masks that contain natural ingredients such as avocado, coconut oil, and honey.

How often should I deep condition my hair?

You should deep condition your hair once a week, or more if your hair is severely damaged.

Can I use argan oil on my bleached hair?

Yes, argan oil can help moisturize and repair bleached hair. Apply a few drops to your hair, focusing on the ends and damaged areas.

We hope this guide on how to fix bleached hair at home has been helpful to you. Remember to be patient and consistent in your hair care routine, and your hair will be healthy and shiny in no time!
