How To Cut Short Hair At Home By Yourself

Written by Luffy Aug 02, 2023 · 8 min read

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, but with the right techniques and tools, it can be done successfully. This guide will walk you through the steps on how to cut short hair at home by yourself. Whether you’re looking to save money or simply prefer the convenience of cutting your own hair, this article will provide all the tips and tricks you need to achieve a professional-looking cut.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Cut Short Hair at Home by Yourself
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care After Cutting

How to Cut Short Hair at Home by Yourself:

The first step to cutting your own hair is to gather the right tools. You’ll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips. It’s also important to have a mirror set up in front of you so you can see what you’re doing.

Next, decide on the length you want to cut your hair. It’s always best to start with a longer length and gradually cut more if needed. Remember, you can always cut more but you can’t add hair back once it’s been cut.

Once you have your tools and length in mind, it’s time to start cutting. Follow the step-by-step guide below for best results.

Step by Step Guide:

Step 1: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Wet hair can appear longer than dry hair, so cutting it wet can result in a shorter cut than intended.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Divide your hair into sections using hair clips. Start by sectioning off the top layer of your hair and clipping it on top of your head. This will ensure that you cut the bottom layers evenly.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Starting with the bottom layer of your hair, take a small section and comb it straight down. Hold the section between your fingers and cut in a straight line. Repeat this process for each section of the bottom layer.

Step 4: Cut the Middle Layer

Release the top layer of your hair and clip the bottom layer up. Take a small section of the middle layer and follow the same process as the bottom layer. Be sure to use the bottom layer as a guide for length.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Release the final layer of hair and clip the other layers up. Take a small section of the top layer and use the middle layer as a guide for length. Cut in a straight line, being careful not to cut too much off.

Step 6: Check for Evenness

Check your hair for any uneven sections. If you notice any areas that are longer than others, trim them to match the rest of your hair.

Step 7: Add Texture

If you want to add texture to your hair, take small sections and point cut into the ends. This will create a choppy, textured look.

Tips and Tricks:

  • Don’t cut your hair when you’re in a rush or feeling stressed. This can lead to mistakes and an uneven cut.
  • Start with a longer length and gradually cut more if needed.
  • Use a comb to help guide your scissors and create an even cut.
  • Be sure to cut in a well-lit area so you can see what you’re doing.
  • Remember, it’s always better to take your time and cut less than to rush and cut too much.

FAQ:

Q: Can I cut my hair with kitchen scissors?

A: While it’s technically possible, it’s not recommended. Kitchen scissors are often dull and can lead to an uneven cut. It’s best to invest in a pair of scissors specifically designed for cutting hair.

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It’s recommended to cut your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape and avoid split ends.

Q: What if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

A: Don’t panic! Mistakes happen, and they can often be fixed. If you cut too much off or create an uneven section, try to even it out with the rest of your hair. If all else fails, you can always visit a professional stylist to fix any mistakes.

Hair Care After Cutting:

After cutting your hair, it’s important to take care of it properly to maintain its shape and health. Be sure to use a shampoo and conditioner that’s appropriate for your hair type, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Regular trims every 4-6 weeks will also help to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, it’s possible to achieve a professional-looking cut from the comfort of your own home. Follow these steps and tips for best results, and remember to take your time and be patient.
