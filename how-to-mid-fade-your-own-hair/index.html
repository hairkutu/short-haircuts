How To Mid Fade Your Own Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Sep 13, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of paying a fortune to get your hair cut every few weeks? Mid fade haircuts have become increasingly popular over the years, and the good news is that you can do it yourself at home. In this article, we will guide you through the steps on how to mid fade your own hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Mid Fade Your Own Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for a Perfect Mid Fade
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After a Mid Fade

Description

A mid fade is a popular haircut that involves a gradual transition from longer hair at the top to shorter hair at the sides and back. This fade is usually done using clippers, and it can be done on any hair type or texture. The mid fade is a versatile haircut that can be styled in various ways, making it a popular choice among men of all ages.

How to Mid Fade Your Own Hair

Before you begin, make sure you have the right tools. You will need a pair of clippers, a comb, and scissors. It's also a good idea to have a mirror or two so you can see your hair from all angles.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to achieve a perfect mid fade:

  1. Start by washing your hair and towel-drying it until it's slightly damp. Dry hair is harder to work with, and you might end up cutting too much.
  2. Use a comb to part your hair into sections. Create a part that goes from the front of your head to the back, and another part that goes from ear to ear. This will create four sections.
  3. Begin with the longest section, which is usually the top section. Start cutting the hair with scissors, holding the hair between your fingers and cutting it to your desired length. Remember to cut with the grain of your hair.
  4. Next, use the clippers to start fading the hair on the sides and back. Start with a higher guard and work your way down to a lower guard, stopping at the point where you want the fade to end.
  5. Repeat this process on both sides and the back, gradually moving down the guard sizes until you achieve the desired fade.
  6. Clean up the edges around your ears and neckline using scissors and clippers. Be sure to blend the hair so that it looks natural.
  7. Once you're done trimming and fading, use a mirror to check your hair from all angles. Make any necessary adjustments and trim any stray hairs.
  8. Finally, wash your hair again to remove any loose hair and style it as desired.

Tips for a Perfect Mid Fade

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect mid fade:

  • Start with a higher guard to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Move slowly and carefully to avoid making mistakes.
  • Use a comb to help guide the clippers and ensure an even cut.
  • Blend the hair between the different guard sizes to create a natural-looking fade.
  • Check your hair from all angles using multiple mirrors.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about mid fades:

  • How long does a mid fade last? A mid fade can last anywhere from two to four weeks depending on how fast your hair grows.
  • Can I do a mid fade on curly hair? Yes, a mid fade can be done on any hair type or texture.
  • What's the difference between a mid fade and a low fade? A mid fade starts in the middle of the head and fades down to the sides and back, whereas a low fade starts at the hairline and fades down to the sides and back.

Hair Care After a Mid Fade

After you've achieved the perfect mid fade, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the look. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Visit a barber every few weeks to maintain the fade and keep your hair looking fresh.

Now that you know how to mid fade your own hair, you can save money and achieve a stylish look right at home. Follow these steps, and you'll be rocking a perfect mid fade in no time.
