How To Cut Curly Hair Dry A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Aug 11, 2023 · 8 min read

Cutting curly hair can be a daunting task, but cutting it dry can make it easier to see the natural curl pattern and ensure that the length and shape come out just right. In this article, we’ll go through the steps to cut curly hair dry, as well as provide tips and answers to frequently asked questions.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Curly Hair Dry
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair

How to Cut Curly Hair Dry

Cutting curly hair dry can help enhance the natural curl pattern, prevent excess shrinkage, and make it easier to see the final result. Here are the steps to follow:

1. Start with clean, detangled hair

Before beginning the cut, make sure the hair is clean and detangled. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, and gently comb through the hair with a wide-tooth comb or your fingers. Avoid using a brush, as it can disrupt the curl pattern.

2. Section the hair

Section the hair into four parts, using clips or hair ties to keep them separate. Start by parting the hair down the middle, then divide each half into two sections, from ear to ear.

3. Cut each section individually

Working with one section at a time, cut the hair at the desired length. Use the natural curl pattern as a guide, and avoid cutting too much at once. Remember, you can always go back and trim more, but you can’t add length back once it’s cut.

4. Blend the sections together

Once all four sections are cut, remove the clips or hair ties and blend the sections together. Use a mirror to check that the length and shape are even on both sides.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting curly hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Don’t pull the hair too tight when cutting, as this can cause the curls to bounce up and become shorter than intended.
  • Consider cutting the hair in its natural state, without stretching or straightening it, to get a more accurate length and shape.
  • Start by cutting a small amount of hair, then gradually cut more as needed.

FAQs

Can you cut curly hair wet?

While it is possible to cut curly hair wet, cutting it dry can help enhance the natural curl pattern and ensure that the length and shape come out just right.

What type of scissors should you use to cut curly hair?

Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends and breakage. It’s also a good idea to use scissors specifically designed for cutting curly hair, as they can help prevent frizz and maintain the natural curl pattern.

How often should you cut curly hair?

It’s recommended to get a trim every 8-12 weeks to get rid of split ends and maintain the hair’s health. However, the frequency of haircuts may vary depending on the individual’s hair type, length, and styling routine.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy, bouncy curls. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Use sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat tools frequently, as they can cause damage and frizz.
  • Use a microfiber towel or cotton t-shirt to dry the hair, as regular towels can cause frizz and breakage.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to enhance the natural curl pattern and prevent frizz.

With these tips and steps, cutting curly hair dry can be a breeze. Remember to take your time, use sharp scissors, and follow the natural curl pattern to achieve the best results. Happy cutting!
