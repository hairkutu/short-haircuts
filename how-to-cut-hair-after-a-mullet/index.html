How To Cut Hair After A Mullet A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Jul 22, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of your mullet? Maybe you've finally decided to cut it off, but you're not sure where to start. Don't worry, we've got you covered. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut your hair after a mullet. We'll cover everything from how to prepare your hair, to what tools you'll need, to how to style your new haircut. So, let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Hair After a Mullet
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Hair After a Mullet
  • FAQs

How to Cut Hair After a Mullet

The first step to cutting your hair after a mullet is to decide on the length you want. Do you want a short, medium, or long haircut? Once you've decided on the length, you'll need to gather the right tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Hair ties (optional)

Now that you have your tools, it's time to prepare your hair. Start by washing and drying your hair. Then, comb it out to remove any tangles. If your hair is long, you may want to tie it up to make it easier to work with.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that your hair is prepared, it's time to start cutting. Follow these steps:

  1. Start by cutting the back of your hair. Use scissors or clippers to trim the hair closest to your neck. Work your way up to the top of your head.
  2. Next, move to the sides of your head. Use scissors to cut the hair around your ears. Be careful not to cut too short. You can always go back and trim more later.
  3. Once the sides are done, move to the top of your head. Use scissors to cut your hair to the desired length. Start with small snips and work your way up to the longer lengths.
  4. Finally, trim the front of your hair. Use scissors to cut your bangs or fringe to the desired length.

Remember to take breaks and step back to check your progress. It's better to take your time and do it right than rush and make mistakes.

Tips for Cutting Hair After a Mullet

Here are some additional tips to help you cut your hair after a mullet:

  • Start with longer lengths and work your way down. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.
  • Use a comb to guide your scissors. This will help you make straighter cuts.
  • Invest in a good pair of scissors. Cheap scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to cut.
  • Ask a friend or family member to help you. It can be hard to cut the back of your hair by yourself.

FAQs

Here are some common questions about cutting hair after a mullet:

How long does it take to cut hair after a mullet?

It can take anywhere from 30 minutes to an hour or more, depending on the length and thickness of your hair.

Is it better to cut hair wet or dry?

It's generally better to cut hair when it's dry. Wet hair can be harder to work with and can shrink when it dries, causing uneven cuts.

Can I cut my hair myself?

Yes, you can cut your hair yourself, but it's usually easier and safer to have someone else help you.

Hair Care After Cutting a Mullet

Now that your mullet is gone, you'll need to take care of your new haircut. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair soft and hydrated.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Style your hair with a gel, mousse, or hairspray to keep it in place.

With these tips, you should be able to cut your hair after a mullet and enjoy your new look. Good luck!
