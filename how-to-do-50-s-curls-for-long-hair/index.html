How To Do 50 S Curls For Long Hair

Are you a fan of vintage hairstyles? Do you want to rock the classic 50's curls for your long hair? If you answered yes, then you're in the right place! In this article, we will teach you how to do 50's curls for long hair step by step. We'll also provide you with tips, FAQs, and hair care advice to help you achieve the perfect vintage look. Let's get started!

How to Do 50's Curls for Long Hair

The first step in achieving 50's curls for long hair is to make sure that your hair is properly washed and conditioned. This will help to make your hair more manageable and easier to curl. Once your hair is clean and dry, you can start the curling process.

Tools You'll Need

Before we begin, here are the tools you'll need to create 50's curls for long hair:

  • Curling iron
  • Hairspray
  • Sectioning clips
  • Heat protectant spray

Step by Step

Follow these steps to create the perfect 50's curls:

  1. Section your hair into small sections using sectioning clips.
  2. Apply heat protectant spray to each section of hair to prevent damage from the curling iron.
  3. Take one section of hair and wrap it around the curling iron, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends.
  4. Hold the hair on the curling iron for 10-15 seconds, then release the curl.
  5. Once the curl has been released, use your fingers to gently separate the curls and create volume.
  6. Repeat this process on all sections of hair, until your entire head is curled.
  7. Apply hairspray to set the curls in place.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best 50's curls:

  • Start with clean, dry hair.
  • Use a curling iron with a barrel size that is appropriate for your hair length.
  • Wrap the hair around the curling iron in the same direction for a more uniform look.
  • Don't brush out the curls too much, as this can cause them to lose their shape.
  • Use hairspray to set the curls in place, but don't overdo it.

FAQ

How long will the curls last?

The length of time that your curls will last depends on your hair type and the products you use. On average, 50's curls can last up to 3 days with proper care.

Can I create 50's curls without a curling iron?

Yes, you can create 50's curls without a curling iron by using hot rollers or flexi rods. These methods may take longer, but they can also be less damaging to your hair.

How do I care for my hair after curling?

After curling your hair, it's important to use a deep conditioning treatment to keep your hair healthy and hydrated. You should also avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage to your hair.

Hair Care

To keep your hair healthy and strong, it's important to follow a regular hair care routine. This includes washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner, using a heat protectant spray before using heat styling tools, and using a deep conditioning treatment at least once a week. Additionally, you should avoid using harsh chemicals or coloring your hair too often, as this can cause damage and breakage.

Now that you know how to create 50's curls for long hair, it's time to start curling! With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve the perfect vintage look in no time.
