How To Cut Long Layers In Thick Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Mar 25, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of your long, thick curly hair weighing you down? Adding layers to your hair can give it more movement and make it easier to manage. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting long layers in thick curly hair. Whether you want to do it yourself or take this guide to your hairstylist, we've got you covered.

How to Cut Long Layers in Thick Curly Hair

The first step in cutting long layers in thick curly hair is to make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will help you see the natural shape of your curls and make it easier to cut the right length. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Start by dividing your hair into four sections, two in the front and two in the back. Use hair clips to secure each section and keep it out of the way.

Step 2: Cut the First Layer

Start with the front section of your hair. Take a small section of hair, about 1-2 inches wide, and hold it straight out from your head. Use sharp hair cutting scissors to trim the ends of the hair at a slight angle. Make sure not to cut too much off at once, as you can always go back and trim more later.

Step 3: Repeat on the Other Side

Once you've finished the first layer on the front section, repeat the process on the other side. Be sure to match the length of the first layer so that your hair is even on both sides.

Step 4: Cut the Back Sections

Now it's time to move on to the back sections of your hair. Take down one of the sections and repeat the same process as before. Cut the hair at a slight angle to create a layered look. Continue until you've finished all four sections.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all of the layers, it's time to blend them together. Use a comb to comb through your hair and make sure there are no uneven parts. If you notice any areas that need to be trimmed, go back and do so.

Tips for Cutting Long Layers in Thick Curly Hair

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting long layers in thick curly hair:

  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Take your time and cut small sections at a time to avoid cutting too much off.
  • Make sure to match the length of the layers on both sides to keep your hair even.
  • When cutting the back sections, use a mirror to ensure you're cutting at the right angle.
  • If you're not comfortable cutting your own hair, seek the help of a professional hairstylist.

FAQs About Cutting Long Layers in Thick Curly Hair

Q: Can I cut long layers in my curly hair myself?

A: Yes, you can cut long layers in your curly hair yourself, but it's important to follow the proper steps and take your time. If you're not comfortable doing it yourself, seek the help of a professional hairstylist.

Q: How often should I cut my long layers in thick curly hair?

A: It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape of the layers and keep your hair healthy.

Q: Can I add long layers to my short curly hair?

A: Yes, you can add long layers to short curly hair, but it's important to consult with a professional hairstylist to determine the best length and style for your hair type.

Hair Care After Cutting Long Layers in Thick Curly Hair

After cutting long layers in your thick curly hair, it's important to maintain its health and shape. Here are a few hair care tips to follow:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking and feeling its best.

By following these tips, you can keep your long layers in thick curly hair looking great for weeks to come.

Final Thoughts

Cutting long layers in thick curly hair can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, it's easy to achieve a beautiful layered look. Whether you do it yourself or seek the help of a professional hairstylist, be sure to follow these steps and tips to ensure a perfect cut every time.
