Long Hairstyles For Fat Face Tips Tricks And Styles

Written by Frank May 07, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you tired of feeling self-conscious about your face shape? One way to boost your confidence is by choosing the right hairstyle that flatters your features. If you have a round or chubby face, long hairstyles can help elongate your face and create a slimming effect. In this article, we'll dive into how to choose the best long hairstyles for fat faces, step-by-step styling tips, and hair care advice to keep your locks healthy and shiny.

Choosing the Right Long Hairstyle for Fat Face

When it comes to long hairstyles for fat faces, there are a few key things to consider:

Length

Long hairstyles that fall below the chin or shoulder can help elongate your face shape. Avoid short hairstyles that end at the chin, as they can make your face appear wider.

Layers

Layered hairstyles can create movement and texture, which can help slim down your face. Opt for face-framing layers that start at your cheekbones or jawline to draw attention away from the fullest part of your face.

Bangs

If you're a fan of bangs, side-swept bangs can help create a slimming effect. Avoid blunt bangs that end at your eyebrows, as they can make your face appear wider.

Face-Framing Layers

Face-framing layers are a great option for long hairstyles for fat faces. These layers start at your cheekbones or jawline and help elongate your face shape. You can also add texture and movement to your hair with layers.

To style face-framing layers, blow dry your hair with a round brush to create volume and curl the ends of your hair with a curling iron. You can also create loose waves by braiding your hair and leaving it overnight.

Side-Swept Bangs

If you're a fan of bangs, side-swept bangs can help create a slimming effect on your face. These bangs start at your part and sweep across your forehead, creating a diagonal line that helps elongate your face shape.

To style side-swept bangs, blow dry your hair with a round brush and use a flat iron to smooth out any frizz. Use a light hairspray to hold your bangs in place.

Center Part

A center part is a simple yet effective way to elongate your face shape. This hairstyle works best with straight or wavy hair, as curly hair can make your face appear wider.

To style a center part, blow dry your hair with a round brush and use a flat iron to smooth out any frizz. You can also add texture to your hair with a sea salt spray.

Updo Styles

Updo styles can help create a slimming effect on your face by pulling your hair away from your face. You can try a low bun or a braided updo to add texture to your hair.

To style an updo, start by pulling your hair into a ponytail and securing it with a hair tie. Then, twist your hair into a bun or braid it and secure it with bobby pins.

Curls and Waves

Curls and waves can add volume and texture to your hair, which can help slim down your face. You can try loose waves, beachy curls, or voluminous curls.

To style curls or waves, use a curling iron or a flat iron to create loose curls. You can also create beachy waves by braiding your hair and leaving it overnight.

Hair Care Tips for Long Hairstyles

To keep your long hair looking healthy and shiny, follow these hair care tips:

Wash your hair every 2-3 days

Washing your hair too often can strip your hair of its natural oils, which can lead to dryness and breakage. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days to keep it clean and healthy.

Use a hair mask once a week

A weekly hair mask can help nourish and hydrate your hair, which can prevent breakage and split ends. Look for a hair mask that's formulated for your hair type.

Avoid using hot tools every day

Using hot tools like curling irons and flat irons every day can damage your hair and lead to breakage. Try to limit your use of hot tools to once or twice a week.

FAQs

What hairstyles should I avoid if I have a fat face?

Avoid short hairstyles that end at your chin or blunt bangs that end at your eyebrows. These styles can make your face appear wider.

Can I still wear my hair in a ponytail if I have a fat face?

Yes, you can still wear your hair in a ponytail if you have a fat face. Try pulling your hair back into a low ponytail or a side ponytail to create a slimming effect.

What hair colors work best for fat faces?

Dark hair colors like black, dark brown, and deep red can help create a slimming effect on your face. Avoid light hair colors like blonde and platinum, as they can make your face appear wider.

With these tips, tricks, and styles, you can choose the perfect long hairstyle for your fat face and feel confident and beautiful every day.
