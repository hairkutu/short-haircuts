How To Cut Stacked Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Aug 25, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you tired of your flat, lifeless hair? Do you want to add some volume and dimension to your locks? If so, you may want to consider getting a stacked haircut. This trendy style features layers that gradually increase in length towards the crown of the head, creating a stacked effect that adds volume and movement. But how do you cut stacked hair? In this guide, we’ll show you how to do it step-by-step.

Description

Before we dive into the how-to, let’s take a closer look at stacked haircuts. As mentioned, this style features layers that increase in length towards the crown of the head. The shortest layer is usually at the nape of the neck, while the longest layer is at the top of the head. This creates a tapered effect that adds volume and shape to the hair. Stacked haircuts can be done on a variety of hair lengths and textures, from short pixie cuts to long, flowing locks.

How to Cut Stacked Hair

Step 1: Section the Hair

Start by sectioning the hair into four parts: the top, left side, right side, and back. Use clips to hold each section in place.

Step 2: Cut the Back

Begin with the back section. Starting at the nape of the neck, take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the section between your fingers, and cut it straight across. Continue working your way up the head, taking small sections and cutting them straight across. As you move up, each section should be slightly longer than the previous one, creating a stacked effect.

Step 3: Cut the Sides

Once you’ve finished the back, move on to the side sections. Take a small section of hair from the bottom of one side, and comb it straight down. Hold it between your fingers, and cut it at a slight angle, following the length of the hair in the back. Repeat on the other side.

Step 4: Cut the Top

Finally, it’s time to cut the top section. Take a small section of hair from the crown of the head, and comb it straight up. Hold it between your fingers, and cut it at a slight angle, following the length of the hair in the back. Continue working your way towards the front of the head, taking small sections and cutting them at a slight angle.

Tips

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean, precise cut.
  • Don’t cut too much hair at once – it’s better to take small sections and work your way up.
  • Make sure each section is even before moving on to the next one.
  • Consult with your client to determine how much length and volume they want added to their hair.

FAQ

Q: Can stacked haircuts be done on any hair texture?

A: Yes, stacked haircuts can be done on straight, wavy, or curly hair.

Q: How often should stacked haircuts be trimmed?

A: It depends on the individual’s hair growth rate and desired length. Generally, it’s recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and volume of the cut.

Q: Can I do a stacked haircut on myself?

A: It’s not recommended to cut your own hair, especially if you’re attempting a complex style like a stacked haircut. It’s best to leave it to a professional stylist.

Hair Care

To keep your stacked haircut looking its best, it’s important to take good care of your hair. Use a shampoo and conditioner that’s suitable for your hair type, and avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons too frequently. Regular trims will also help maintain the shape and volume of the cut.

Cutting stacked hair may seem daunting at first, but with the right technique and some practice, it can be a great way to add volume and dimension to your client’s locks. Follow these steps, use the right tools, and don’t be afraid to ask for help if needed. With a little patience and skill, you’ll be able to create a beautiful, stacked haircut that your client will love.
