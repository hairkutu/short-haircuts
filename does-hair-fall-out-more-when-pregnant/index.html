Does Hair Fall Out More When Pregnant

Written by Christine Jul 18, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Many women experience changes in their hair during pregnancy, from increased thickness and shine to changes in texture and color. However, some women may also notice more hair loss than usual during this time. If you're asking yourself, "does hair fall out more when pregnant?" the answer is yes, it can. But don't worry, there are ways to manage it.

Why Does Hair Fall Out More When Pregnant?

During pregnancy, the body undergoes several hormonal changes that can affect hair growth and loss. The hormone estrogen, for example, can cause hair to remain in the growth phase longer than usual, leading to thicker and fuller hair. However, after giving birth, hormone levels drop, and hair can enter the shedding phase, resulting in more hair loss than usual.

How to Manage Hair Loss During Pregnancy

If you're experiencing more hair loss than usual during pregnancy, there are several things you can do to manage it:

1. Eat a Healthy Diet

A balanced and nutritious diet is essential for healthy hair growth. Make sure you're getting enough protein, iron, and vitamins, including biotin and vitamin D, which are essential for hair health.

2. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles, like braids and ponytails, can pull on the hair and cause breakage. Opt for looser hairstyles or let your hair down to reduce stress on the hair follicles.

3. Be Gentle When Brushing

Brushing your hair gently and using a wide-tooth comb can help prevent breakage and hair loss. Avoid brushing wet hair, as it is more prone to damage.

4. Manage Stress

Stress can contribute to hair loss, so make sure you're taking care of your mental health during pregnancy. Engage in relaxing activities like yoga or meditation to reduce stress levels.

FAQs

1. How much hair loss is normal during pregnancy?

It's normal to lose around 100 hairs per day, so if you're losing more than that, it's worth speaking to your healthcare provider.

2. Will my hair grow back after pregnancy?

Most women will experience some hair loss after pregnancy, but hair usually grows back within a few months.

3. Can pregnancy cause bald spots?

In rare cases, pregnancy can cause bald spots, but this is usually temporary, and hair will grow back once hormone levels return to normal.

Hair Care Tips for Pregnant Women

Here are some additional tips to help you care for your hair during pregnancy:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Choose a shampoo that is gentle and free from harsh chemicals, as they can strip the hair of its natural oils.

2. Condition Regularly

Conditioning your hair regularly can help keep it soft, smooth, and healthy.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

UV rays from the sun can damage your hair and cause it to become dry and brittle. Wear a hat or use a leave-in conditioner with SPF to protect your hair.

4. Stay Hydrated

Drinking plenty of water can help keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

In conclusion, hair loss during pregnancy is normal, but there are ways to manage it. By eating a healthy diet, avoiding tight hairstyles, being gentle when brushing, managing stress, and following these hair care tips, you can help keep your hair healthy and strong during this exciting time.
