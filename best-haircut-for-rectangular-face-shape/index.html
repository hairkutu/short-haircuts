Description

A rectangular face shape has a strong and angular jawline, with a forehead that is as wide as the jawline. The length of the face is longer than its width, and the cheekbones are not prominent. The best haircut for a rectangular face shape is one that softens the angles and adds width to the face.


How to

To determine if you have a rectangular face shape, measure your face from the hairline to the chin and from ear to ear. If the length is longer than the width, and the jawline and forehead are about the same width, you have a rectangular face shape.


Step by step

Step 1: Consult with a hairstylist who can help you choose the best haircut for your face shape.
Step 2: Choose a haircut that adds width to your face, such as layers, waves, or curls.
Step 3: Avoid haircuts that emphasize the angles of your face, such as blunt bangs or straight bobs.
Step 4: Experiment with different hairstyles to find the one that suits you best.


Tips

1. Bangs can be a great way to add width to a rectangular face shape, but make sure they are soft and wispy.
2. Soft waves and curls can also add width to the face and soften the angles.
3. Avoid center parts, as they can make the face appear longer.
4. Consider a hairstyle that falls just below the chin, as this can help balance the face.
5. If you have long hair, consider adding layers to create movement and softness.


FAQ

Q: What haircuts should I avoid if I have a rectangular face shape?

A: Avoid haircuts that emphasize the angles of your face, such as blunt bangs or straight bobs. Also, avoid hairstyles that are too long and straight, as they can make the face appear even longer.


Q: Can I wear my hair in a ponytail if I have a rectangular face shape?

A: Yes, you can wear your hair in a ponytail if you have a rectangular face shape. However, consider adding some volume to the top of your head to balance out the length of your face.


Hair Care

Regardless of your face shape, it is important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

- Use a shampoo and conditioner that are appropriate for your hair type.
- Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
- Use a heat protectant spray before using heat styling tools.
- Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
- Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
