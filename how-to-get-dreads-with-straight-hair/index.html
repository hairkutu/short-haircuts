How To Get Dreads With Straight Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Jul 18, 2023 · 7 min read

In recent years, dreadlocks have become a popular hairstyle choice for many people. While traditionally associated with certain cultures and ethnicities, anyone can rock a head full of dreads. However, if you have straight hair, you might be wondering if it’s possible to achieve this look. The answer is yes! In this article, we’ll walk you through the steps to get dreads with straight hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get Dreads with Straight Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Dreads
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care for Dreads

How to Get Dreads with Straight Hair

First things first, it’s important to note that getting dreads with straight hair is a different process than it is for those with curly or kinky hair. With straight hair, you’ll need to rely on a technique called “twist and rip” to create the knots that eventually turn into dreads.

Before you start the process, it’s important to have clean, dry hair. You’ll also need a comb, hair clips, and some patience.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, divide your hair into small, even sections. You can use hair clips to keep each section in place.

Step 2: Twist and Rip

Starting from the roots of each section, twist the hair in one direction. Then, using your fingers, rip the section in the opposite direction. This will create small knots in the hair.

Step 3: Repeat

Continue twisting and ripping each section of hair until your entire head is covered. This can take several hours, depending on the length and thickness of your hair.

Step 4: Let Your Hair Rest

After you’ve finished twisting and ripping, let your hair rest for a few days. During this time, avoid washing your hair or manipulating it too much. This will allow the knots to settle and start to form into dreads.

Step 5: Maintenance

Once your dreads have started to form, it’s important to maintain them regularly. This includes washing your hair with a residue-free shampoo, using a crochet hook to tighten loose knots, and separating any sections that start to merge together.

Tips for Maintaining Your Dreads

Here are a few tips to keep your dreads looking healthy and neat:

  • Use a residue-free shampoo to avoid buildup in your hair.
  • Avoid using conditioner, as it can loosen the knots in your hair.
  • Use a crochet hook to tighten any loose knots.
  • Separate any sections that start to merge together to prevent congos (large, tangled knots).
  • Be patient! Dreads take time to form and mature.

FAQ

Can I get dreads with straight hair?

Yes, you can! However, the process will be different than it is for those with curly or kinky hair. You’ll need to use the “twist and rip” technique to create knots in your hair.

How long does it take for dreads to form?

It can take several months for dreads to form, depending on the length and thickness of your hair. Be patient and don’t give up!

Do I need to cut off my hair to get dreads?

No, you don’t. However, if you have long hair, you might want to consider cutting it shorter to make the process easier and more manageable.

Hair Care for Dreads

When it comes to caring for your dreads, it’s important to use products that won’t leave residue in your hair. Look for shampoos and conditioners that are specifically designed for dreadlocks. You can also use oils, such as jojoba or coconut oil, to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

It’s also important to avoid using heat tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, as they can damage your hair and loosen the knots in your dreads. Instead, embrace your natural texture and let your dreads do their thing!

Conclusion

Getting dreads with straight hair is possible, but it takes time and patience. By following these steps and tips, you can achieve a head full of beautiful, healthy dreads. Remember to be gentle with your hair, use the right products, and embrace your unique style!
