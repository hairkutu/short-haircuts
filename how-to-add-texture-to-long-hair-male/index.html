How To Add Texture To Long Hair Male

Written by Bardi Jun 12, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you tired of your long hair looking flat and lifeless? Adding texture can give your long locks a new, edgy look. In this article, we’ll go over some tips and tricks on how to add texture to long hair male.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Add Texture to Long Hair Male
  3. Step by Step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description

Adding texture to long hair male involves creating depth and dimension in the hair. This can be achieved through various techniques like layering, thinning, and adding waves or curls. Texture can also be created through the use of hair products like sea salt spray, styling wax, and pomade.

How to Add Texture to Long Hair Male

Here are some steps on how to add texture to long hair male:

1. Get a Haircut

The first step to adding texture to long hair male is to get a haircut. Layers and thinning can create movement and dimension in the hair. Talk to your hairstylist about the best haircut for your hair type and face shape.

2. Use Hair Products

After washing your hair, apply a small amount of sea salt spray to create texture and volume. Styling wax or pomade can also be used to create definition and hold. Apply the product to your fingertips and work it through your hair, focusing on the ends.

3. Use Heat Tools

Heat tools like curling irons and straighteners can be used to create waves or curls in the hair. Use a heat protectant spray before using any heat tools to prevent damage to your hair.

Step by Step

Here is a step by step guide on how to add texture to long hair male:

  1. Wash and condition your hair.
  2. Towel dry your hair.
  3. Apply a small amount of sea salt spray to your hair.
  4. Blow dry your hair with a diffuser attachment to create volume.
  5. Use a curling iron or straightener to create waves or curls in your hair.
  6. Apply a small amount of styling wax or pomade to your fingertips.
  7. Work the product through your hair, focusing on the ends.
  8. Use your fingers to create texture and definition in your hair.
  9. Finish with a hairspray to hold your style in place.

Tips

Here are some additional tips on how to add texture to long hair male:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles to find what works best for you.
  • Use a clarifying shampoo once a week to remove buildup from hair products.
  • Get regular haircuts to prevent split ends and maintain healthy hair.

FAQ

What is the best haircut for adding texture to long hair male?

The best haircut for adding texture to long hair male is one with layers and thinning. This creates movement and dimension in the hair. Talk to your hairstylist about the best haircut for your hair type and face shape.

Can I add texture to long hair male without using products?

Yes, texture can also be created through the use of heat tools like curling irons and straighteners. However, using hair products like sea salt spray, styling wax, and pomade can enhance the texture and hold of your hairstyle.

Can I add texture to long hair male if my hair is naturally straight?

Yes, texture can still be added to naturally straight hair. Using a curling iron or straightener can create waves or curls, while layering and thinning can create movement and dimension in the hair.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is important for maintaining healthy, textured hair. Here are some tips on how to care for your hair:

  • Wash your hair every other day to prevent stripping natural oils.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to moisturize your hair.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can strip natural oils.
  • Avoid using excessive heat on your hair, as this can cause damage.

By following these tips and tricks, you can add texture to long hair male and achieve a new, edgy look. Experiment with different hairstyles and products to find what works best for you.
