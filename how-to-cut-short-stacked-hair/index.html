How To Cut Short Stacked Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Apr 07, 2023 · 7 min read

Short stacked hair is a trendy style that involves having your hair cut in layers that gradually get shorter towards the back of your head. This creates a stacked effect that looks great on both long and short hair. If you’re interested in trying out this style, read on for a step-by-step guide on how to cut short stacked hair.

Description

The short stacked haircut involves cutting the hair in layers, with the shortest layers at the nape of the neck and the longest layers at the crown of the head. This creates a stacked effect that adds volume and texture to the hair. The style is popular among women of all ages and hair types, and can be customized to suit your individual preferences.

How to Cut Short Stacked Hair: Step-by-Step

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Comb through it to remove any tangles, and section it off into four parts: the top of the head, the sides, and the back. Clip each section out of the way so you can work on one section at a time.

Step 2: Start Cutting

Begin by cutting the shortest layer at the nape of the neck. Use your fingers to lift a section of hair, and cut it straight across using your scissors. Work your way up the back of the head, cutting each section slightly longer than the one before it. When you reach the top of the head, make sure the layers are long enough to blend in with the longer hair on top.

Step 3: Cut the Sides

Once you’ve finished the back, move on to the sides. Start at the front and work your way towards the back, cutting each section slightly longer than the one before it. Make sure to blend the layers in with the back and top of the head.

Step 4: Finish with the Top

Finally, move on to the top of the head. Use your fingers to lift sections of hair and cut them at an angle, so that the shortest layers are at the back of the head and the longest layers are at the front. This will create a stacked effect that adds volume and texture to the hair.

Tips for Cutting Short Stacked Hair

  • Start with a longer length than you want, as you can always cut more off later.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Work slowly and methodically, cutting small sections at a time.
  • Use a comb to make sure each layer is even and straight.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask for help if you’re unsure about any of the steps.

FAQ

Is short stacked hair suitable for all face shapes?

Short stacked hair can be customized to suit any face shape. If you have a round face, you may want to add more layers to create a slimmer effect. If you have a square face, you may want to keep the layers longer at the front to soften the jawline.

How often should I get my short stacked haircut?

Short stacked haircuts require regular maintenance to keep the layers looking fresh. Plan on getting a trim every 4-6 weeks to keep the style looking its best.

Hair Care for Short Stacked Hair

To keep your short stacked hair looking its best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage the hair.
  • Use a lightweight styling product to add volume and texture to your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.

Now that you know how to cut short stacked hair, why not give it a try? With a little practice and patience, you can achieve a stylish and trendy look that’s perfect for any occasion.
