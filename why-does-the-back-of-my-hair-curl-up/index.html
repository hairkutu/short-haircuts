Why Does The Back Of My Hair Curl Up

Written by Petter Apr 24, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're someone who has straight hair and finds that the hair at the back of your head has a tendency to curl up, you're not alone. It's a common hair problem that many people face. In this article, we'll explore the reasons behind this hair issue and provide some tips and tricks to help manage it.

Description

The hair at the back of your head curls up because of the natural growth pattern of your hair. For most people, the hair at the back of the head grows in a different direction than the rest of the hair on the head. This makes it difficult for the hair to lay flat and instead causes it to curl up.

How to

There are several ways to manage the curling of the hair at the back of your head:

1. Blow-dry your hair

Blow-drying your hair is a great way to keep it straight and prevent it from curling up. Use a round brush to help guide the hair and create a smooth, flat style.

2. Use a straightening iron

A straightening iron is another great tool to use to keep the hair at the back of your head straight. Be sure to use a heat protectant spray before using the iron to prevent damage to your hair.

3. Apply a smoothing serum

A smoothing serum can help to tame frizz and keep your hair looking smooth and straight. Apply a small amount to the hair at the back of your head before blow-drying or using a straightening iron.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to manage the curling of the hair at the back of your head:

Step 1: Wash your hair

Start by washing your hair with a shampoo and conditioner that is designed for straight hair. This will help to keep your hair looking smooth and frizz-free.

Step 2: Apply a smoothing serum

Apply a small amount of smoothing serum to the hair at the back of your head. Use your fingers to work the serum through your hair, making sure to distribute it evenly.

Step 3: Blow-dry your hair

Using a round brush, blow-dry your hair, focusing on the hair at the back of your head. Use the brush to guide the hair and create a smooth, flat style.

Step 4: Use a straightening iron

If necessary, use a straightening iron to further smooth out the hair at the back of your head. Be sure to use a heat protectant spray before using the iron to prevent damage to your hair.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help manage the curling of the hair at the back of your head:

1. Use a silk pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help to prevent your hair from getting tangled and curled up while you sleep.

2. Avoid touching your hair

Avoid touching the hair at the back of your head throughout the day as this can cause it to curl up.

3. Use a leave-in conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help to keep your hair hydrated and prevent it from becoming frizzy and curly.

FAQ

Q: Can genetics play a role in the curling of the hair at the back of the head?

A: Yes, genetics can play a role in the natural growth pattern of your hair, including the direction in which it grows. This can make it difficult to keep the hair at the back of your head straight.

Q: Is it possible to permanently straighten the hair at the back of my head?

A: Yes, there are several hair treatments, such as a keratin treatment, that can help to permanently straighten your hair.

Hair Care

Managing the curling of the hair at the back of your head can be a challenge, but with the right tools and techniques, it's possible to keep your hair looking smooth and straight. Remember to use a shampoo and conditioner that is designed for straight hair, apply a smoothing serum, and use a round brush and straightening iron to help guide your hair into a smooth, flat style. Additionally, be sure to avoid touching your hair throughout the day and use a silk pillowcase to prevent tangling and curling while you sleep.
