What To Wear Hiking In 65 Degree Weather A Guide For 2023

Written by Robby Apr 15, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you planning to go hiking in 65 degree weather but unsure of what to wear? Look no further! This guide will cover everything you need to know about what to wear hiking in 65 degree weather. From head to toe, we’ve got you covered. Let’s dive in!

Description

Hiking is a great way to enjoy nature and stay active. However, it’s important to dress appropriately for the weather. In 65 degree weather, you want to wear clothes that will keep you comfortable without making you too hot or too cold. This guide will help you choose the right clothing for your hiking adventure.

How to

When it comes to dressing for hiking, it’s all about layering. Layers allow you to adjust your clothing based on the temperature and your activity level. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Start with a moisture-wicking base layer

Your base layer should be made of a material that wicks away sweat from your body. This will keep you dry and comfortable throughout your hike. Look for materials like polyester or merino wool. Avoid cotton, as it absorbs moisture and can make you feel clammy.

Step 2: Add an insulating layer

Your insulating layer should provide warmth without adding too much bulk. Fleece or a lightweight down jacket are good options for 65 degree weather. Make sure your insulating layer can be easily removed or added as needed.

Step 3: Wear a windproof and waterproof outer layer

Your outer layer should protect you from wind and rain. Look for a jacket made of a breathable, waterproof material like Gore-Tex. This will keep you dry without making you feel too hot.

Step 4: Choose the right pants

For 65 degree weather, you can wear lightweight pants or shorts. Look for pants made of a stretchy, breathable material that allows for freedom of movement. If you choose shorts, be sure to wear sunscreen on your legs.

Step 5: Choose the right footwear

Wear comfortable and sturdy hiking boots or shoes. Look for shoes with good traction and ankle support. Make sure your shoes are broken in before your hike to avoid blisters.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when dressing for a hike in 65 degree weather:

  • Bring a hat and sunglasses to protect your head and eyes from the sun.
  • Wear socks made of a moisture-wicking material to keep your feet dry and prevent blisters.
  • Carry a backpack to store extra layers, water, and snacks.
  • Check the weather forecast before you go and adjust your clothing accordingly.

FAQ

Q: Can I wear cotton clothing for hiking?

A: It’s best to avoid cotton clothing for hiking. Cotton absorbs moisture and can make you feel clammy and uncomfortable. Look for moisture-wicking materials like polyester or merino wool instead.

Q: Do I need to wear sunscreen?

A: Yes, it’s important to wear sunscreen on any exposed skin to protect yourself from the sun’s harmful rays.

Q: What kind of backpack should I bring?

A: Look for a backpack with a comfortable strap system and plenty of compartments to store your gear.

Hair Care

Don’t forget about your hair when you’re out hiking! Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy and manageable:

  • Wear a hat to protect your hair from the sun.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Bring a brush or comb to detangle your hair after your hike.
  • Use a dry shampoo to freshen up your hair if you don’t have access to a shower.

With these tips in mind, you’re ready to hit the trails in 65 degree weather. Happy hiking!
