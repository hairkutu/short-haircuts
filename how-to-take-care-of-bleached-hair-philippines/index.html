How To Take Care Of Bleached Hair In The Philippines Your Ultimate Guide

Written by Frank Jun 23, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Bleaching your hair can give you a new and unique look, but it also requires a lot of maintenance to keep it healthy and looking great. If you have bleached hair in the Philippines, you need to take extra care of it since our climate can be harsh on your locks. In this article, we will provide you with tips and tricks on how to take care of bleached hair in the Philippines.

Description

Bleaching hair is a chemical process that strips your hair of its natural pigments. This process can be quite harsh on the hair, and it requires a lot of maintenance to keep it healthy. If you have bleached hair in the Philippines, you need to take extra care of it since our climate can cause further damage to your hair.

How to Take Care of Bleached Hair in the Philippines

Step 1: Use a Moisturizing Shampoo

Bleached hair is more prone to dryness and damage, so it's important to use a moisturizing shampoo to keep it hydrated. Look for shampoos that contain natural oils like coconut, argan, or jojoba oil. These oils can help restore moisture to your hair and prevent it from becoming brittle.

Step 2: Condition Regularly

Conditioning is essential for bleached hair since it helps to restore moisture and prevent breakage. Use a conditioner that is specifically designed for bleached hair, and leave it on for at least 5 minutes before rinsing it off. You can also use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to give your hair an extra boost of moisture.

Step 3: Avoid Heat Styling

Bleached hair is more fragile than natural hair, and heat styling can cause further damage. Avoid using hot tools like straighteners, curling irons, and hair dryers. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

Step 4: Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Leave-in conditioners are great for bleached hair since they provide extra moisture and protection. Look for leave-in conditioners that contain keratin or other proteins, as these can help strengthen your hair and prevent breakage.

Step 5: Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun's UV rays can cause further damage to bleached hair, so it's important to protect your hair when you're out in the sun. Wear a hat or use a hair product that contains SPF to shield your hair from the sun's rays.

Tips for Taking Care of Bleached Hair in the Philippines

Here are some additional tips for taking care of your bleached hair in the Philippines:

  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, as this can prevent breakage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends and keep it looking healthy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How often should I wash my bleached hair?

A: It's best to wash your bleached hair every other day or every 2-3 days to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.

Q: Can I still dye my hair if it's already been bleached?

A: Yes, you can still dye your hair if it's been bleached. However, you should wait at least 2 weeks before dyeing your hair to give it time to recover from the bleaching process.

Q: How can I prevent my bleached hair from turning yellow?

A: Use a purple shampoo once a week to prevent your bleached hair from turning yellow. Purple shampoo contains purple pigments that neutralize yellow tones in your hair.

Hair Care

Taking care of bleached hair in the Philippines requires extra effort, but it's worth it to keep your locks healthy and looking great. Remember to use moisturizing shampoos and conditioners, avoid heat styling, use leave-in conditioners, protect your hair from the sun, and follow our additional tips for taking care of your bleached hair.
