How To Cut Top Layers In Hair Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia May 02, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you looking for a way to add volume and texture to your hair? Cutting top layers in your hair is a great way to achieve that look. In this article, we will provide a step-by-step guide on how to cut top layers in hair. We will also cover some tips, FAQs, and hair care advice to help you achieve the best results.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Top Layers in Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Top Layers in Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Advice

How to Cut Top Layers in Hair

Before we get started, it's important to note that cutting top layers in hair is best done on dry hair. This will give you a better idea of how the hair will fall and ensure that you don't cut too much off. Here are the steps:

Step 1: Section Your Hair

The first step is to section your hair. Divide your hair into three sections: top, middle, and bottom. Use hair clips to keep each section separate.

Step 2: Cut the Top Section

Start with the top section. Comb through the hair and hold it between your fingers at the desired length. Use scissors to cut the hair at an angle, starting from the back and working your way to the front. Be sure to cut small sections at a time, and don't cut too much off.

Tip: To add more volume to your hair, cut the top layer shorter than the middle and bottom layers.

Step 3: Cut the Middle and Bottom Sections

Next, move on to the middle and bottom sections. Use the top layer as a guide and cut the hair to the same length. Again, cut small sections at a time to avoid cutting too much off.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you have cut all three sections, it's time to blend the layers. Comb through your hair to ensure that the layers blend together seamlessly. Use scissors to trim any uneven ends.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a summary of the steps to cut top layers in hair:

  1. Section your hair into top, middle, and bottom sections.
  2. Cut the top section at an angle, starting from the back and working your way to the front.
  3. Cut the middle and bottom sections to the same length as the top section.
  4. Blend the layers together and trim any uneven ends.

Tips for Cutting Top Layers in Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the best results:

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Start with small sections and cut gradually to avoid cutting too much off.
  • Be conservative with your cutting. You can always go back and cut more if needed.
  • For a more natural look, use a razor instead of scissors to cut the hair.
  • Consider consulting a professional hairstylist if you are unsure about cutting your own hair.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting top layers in hair:

Can I cut my own hair?

Yes, you can cut your own hair. However, it's important to be cautious and start with small sections to avoid cutting too much off.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair and prevent split ends.

What should I do if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don't panic. You can always go back and cut more or visit a professional hairstylist to fix the mistake.

Hair Care Advice

Cutting top layers in hair can be a great way to add volume and texture to your hair. However, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and prevent damage. Here are some tips:

  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat tools such as straighteners and curling irons too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray.
  • Consider using a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair.

By following these tips and using our step-by-step guide, you can achieve beautifully layered hair that looks full and voluminous.
