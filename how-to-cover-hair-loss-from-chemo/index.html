How To Cover Hair Loss From Chemo

Written by Robby Jul 27, 2023 · 7 min read

Dealing with hair loss is one of the most challenging aspects of chemotherapy, but there are ways to cover it up and feel confident during treatment. In this article, we’ll go over how to cover hair loss from chemo, step by step. From headwear options to makeup tips, we’ll cover everything you need to know to feel your best during treatment.

Description

Hair loss is a common side effect of chemotherapy, and it can be a distressing experience for many patients. Some people choose to wear wigs, hats, or scarves to cover their hair loss, while others opt for makeup or other creative solutions. Regardless of your preferred method, the most important thing is to find a way to feel comfortable and confident in your own skin.

How to Cover Hair Loss from Chemo

Step 1: Choose a Headwear Option

The first step in covering hair loss from chemo is to choose a headwear option that suits your style and comfort level. Some popular options include:

  • Wigs: Wigs are a popular choice for many chemotherapy patients because they offer a natural-looking alternative to your own hair. You can choose from synthetic or human hair wigs, depending on your budget and preference.
  • Hats: Hats are a versatile option that can be worn indoors or outdoors. They come in a variety of styles, from baseball caps to berets to beanies.
  • Scarves: Scarves are a stylish and comfortable option that can be tied in a variety of ways to create different looks. They’re also a great way to protect your scalp from the sun.

Step 2: Experiment with Makeup

Makeup can be a powerful tool for covering hair loss and boosting your confidence during treatment. Here are some tips to help you get started:

  • Use eyebrow pencils or powder to fill in sparse eyebrows.
  • Apply a light layer of foundation or concealer to your scalp to create the illusion of hair.
  • Experiment with bold lipstick or eye makeup to draw attention away from your hair loss.

Step 3: Get Creative with Accessories

Accessories can also be a fun and creative way to cover hair loss during chemotherapy. Here are some ideas:

  • Try statement earrings or necklaces to draw attention away from your hair loss.
  • Wear headbands or hair clips to create the illusion of hair.
  • Experiment with temporary hair tattoos or glitter to add some sparkle to your scalp.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you cover hair loss from chemo:

  • Invest in a good quality wig or headwear option to ensure maximum comfort and confidence.
  • Experiment with different styles and colors to find the look that works best for you.
  • Consider working with a professional makeup artist to learn new techniques for covering hair loss.

FAQ

Q: Will my hair grow back after chemotherapy?

A: Yes, most people’s hair will grow back after chemotherapy. However, it may take several months for your hair to fully regrow.

Q: Can I color my hair during chemotherapy?

A: It’s generally not recommended to color your hair during chemotherapy, as it can cause damage to your scalp and hair follicles. However, you can talk to your doctor about your options.

Q: Are there any medications or treatments that can prevent hair loss during chemotherapy?

A: There are some medications and treatments that may help prevent or reduce hair loss during chemotherapy, but they’re not effective for everyone. Talk to your doctor about your options.

Hair Care

It’s important to take care of your scalp and any remaining hair during chemotherapy. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid using harsh shampoos or conditioners that can irritate your scalp.
  • Use a gentle detangling brush to avoid pulling out any remaining hair.
  • Massage your scalp regularly to stimulate hair growth.

With these tips and tricks, you can cover hair loss from chemo and feel confident during treatment. Remember, the most important thing is to find a solution that works for you and makes you feel comfortable and confident in your own skin.
