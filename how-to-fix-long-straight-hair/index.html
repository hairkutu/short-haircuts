How To Fix Long Straight Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Bardi Aug 26, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Long, straight hair is a classic look that never goes out of style. However, it can be challenging to maintain and style. If you're struggling with lifeless, dull hair, don't worry. In this article, we'll share tips and tricks on how to fix long straight hair for a healthy, shiny, and voluminous mane.

Table of Contents

  • Hair Care
  • How to Fix Long Straight Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs

Hair Care

Before we dive into styling techniques, it's essential to take care of your hair's health. Long straight hair is prone to breakage, split ends, and dryness. Therefore, incorporating a hair care routine is crucial to achieving healthy and manageable hair.

Here are some tips on how to care for long straight hair:

1. Wash Your Hair Less Frequently

Washing your hair every day can strip its natural oils, leaving it dry and brittle. Instead, aim to wash your hair two to three times a week. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and a nourishing conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.

2. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

After washing your hair, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle it instead of a brush. Combing your hair while wet can cause breakage and split ends. Start at the ends and work your way up to avoid tugging on your hair.

3. Apply Hair Masks

Once a week, use a deep conditioning hair mask to nourish your locks. Hair masks can help repair damage, boost shine, and add moisture to your hair. Apply the mask from the mid-lengths to the ends of your hair and leave it on for at least 20 minutes before rinsing.

How to Fix Long Straight Hair

Now that we've covered hair care let's move on to styling techniques. Here are some ways to fix long straight hair:

1. Add Layers

Adding layers to your hair can add volume and movement. Ask your hairstylist for long layers that frame your face and blend seamlessly with the rest of your hair.

2. Use Hot Rollers

Hot rollers are an easy and effective way to add volume and bounce to your hair. Roll small sections of your hair around the rollers and leave them in for about 10-15 minutes. Once you remove the rollers, run your fingers through your hair to loosen the curls and create a natural look.

3. Embrace Texturizing Products

Texturizing sprays, powders, and mousses can add grip and texture to your hair, making it easier to style. Apply the product to your roots or mid-lengths and scrunch your hair to create volume and movement.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to fix long straight hair:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and using a nourishing conditioner. Towel-dry your hair and apply a heat protectant spray to prevent damage from heat styling tools.

Step 2: Add Volume

Use a volumizing mousse and apply it to your roots. Blow-dry your hair using a round brush to add volume and lift at the roots.

Step 3: Curl Your Hair

Using a curling iron or hot rollers, curl small sections of your hair. Hold the curling iron or roller in place for about 10-15 seconds before releasing. Repeat until all of your hair is curled.

Step 4: Loosen the Curls

Once you've curled your hair, run your fingers through your hair to loosen the curls and create a natural look. Use a texturizing spray or powder to add grip and texture.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to keep in mind:

  • Use a dry shampoo to absorb excess oil and add volume to your hair between washes.
  • Avoid using too many styling products, as they can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.

FAQs

Can I fix my long straight hair without heat styling tools?

Yes, you can use texturizing sprays, powders, and mousses to add volume and texture to your hair without using heat styling tools.

How often should I wash my long straight hair?

Aim to wash your hair two to three times a week. However, if you have an oily scalp, you may need to wash your hair more frequently.

Can I add layers to my long straight hair?

Yes, adding layers to your hair can add volume and movement. Ask your hairstylist for long layers that frame your face and blend seamlessly with the rest of your hair.


In conclusion, fixing long straight hair requires a combination of hair care and styling techniques. Incorporating a hair care routine can help keep your hair healthy and manageable, while adding layers, using hot rollers, and embracing texturizing products can add volume and movement to your hair. Follow our tips and tricks, and you'll be on your way to a healthy, shiny, and voluminous mane.
