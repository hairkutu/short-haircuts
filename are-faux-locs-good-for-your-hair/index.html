Are Faux Locs Good For Your Hair Everything You Need To Know

Written by Frank Jul 05, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you're considering getting faux locs, you may be wondering if they're good for your hair. Faux locs have become increasingly popular in recent years, but there's still a lot of confusion around whether they're a healthy choice for your hair. In this article, we'll explore the pros and cons of faux locs, how to take care of them, and answer some frequently asked questions.

Table of Contents

  • Pros and Cons of Faux Locs
  • How to Get Faux Locs
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Installing Faux Locs

  • Hair Care Tips for Faux Locs

Pros and Cons of Faux Locs

Before we dive into whether faux locs are good for your hair or not, let's first explore the pros and cons of this hairstyle.

Pros

  • Faux locs can be a protective style for your hair, helping to prevent breakage and damage.
  • They're low maintenance, meaning you don't have to spend a lot of time styling your hair each day.
  • Faux locs can add volume and length to your hair, giving you a new and exciting look.

Cons

  • Getting faux locs can be time-consuming and expensive, especially if you have a lot of hair.
  • Some people may experience itching or discomfort while wearing faux locs, especially if they're done too tightly.
  • Removing faux locs can also be time-consuming and may require the use of chemicals, which can damage your hair.

Overall, faux locs can be a great choice for those looking for a protective hairstyle, but it's important to carefully consider the pros and cons before making a decision.

How to Get Faux Locs

If you've decided that faux locs are the right choice for you, the next step is to figure out how to get them. There are a few different methods for installing faux locs, including:

  • Crochet faux locs
  • Wrap faux locs
  • Braided faux locs

The method you choose will depend on your hair type, the look you're going for, and your personal preference. It's important to work with a stylist who is experienced in installing faux locs, as the process can be time-consuming and requires a certain level of skill.

Step-by-Step Guide to Installing Faux Locs

Here's a general step-by-step guide to installing faux locs:

  1. Wash and condition your hair.
  2. Section your hair and braid it into small, neat sections.
  3. Wrap or crochet the faux locs around each braid, securing them in place.
  4. Trim the faux locs to your desired length.
  5. Style the faux locs as desired.

It's important to take your time during each step to ensure that the faux locs are installed correctly and won't cause damage to your hair.

Tips for Maintaining Faux Locs

Once you have your faux locs installed, it's important to take proper care of them to ensure that they stay looking great and don't cause any damage to your hair. Here are some tips for maintaining your faux locs:

  • Keep your scalp clean and moisturized to prevent itching and dryness.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products on your scalp, as this can cause buildup and make your faux locs look greasy.
  • Protect your faux locs at night by wearing a satin or silk scarf or bonnet.
  • Avoid pulling or twisting your faux locs too tightly, as this can cause breakage and damage to your hair.
  • Be gentle when styling your faux locs, using a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to avoid pulling or tugging on them.

FAQs About Faux Locs

Do faux locs damage your hair?

When installed correctly and taken care of properly, faux locs should not cause damage to your hair. However, if they're done too tightly or if you don't take proper care of them, they can cause breakage and damage.

How long do faux locs last?

Faux locs can last anywhere from 4-8 weeks, depending on how well you take care of them and how quickly your hair grows.

Can you wash faux locs?

Yes, you can wash faux locs. It's important to be gentle and use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.

Can you swim with faux locs?

Yes, you can swim with faux locs, but it's important to protect them from chlorine and saltwater, which can cause damage and dryness. Wear a swim cap or cover your hair with a scarf to protect your faux locs while swimming.

Hair Care Tips for Faux Locs

Here are some additional hair care tips to keep in mind if you're considering getting faux locs:

  • Make sure your hair is healthy before getting faux locs installed. If your hair is already damaged or weak, faux locs can make the problem worse.
  • Drink plenty of water and eat a healthy diet to keep your hair and scalp healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your faux locs, as this can cause damage and dryness.
  • If you experience itching or discomfort while wearing faux locs, talk to your stylist about loosening them or removing them altogether.

Overall, faux locs can be a great choice for those looking for a low-maintenance protective hairstyle. As long as they're installed correctly and taken care of properly, they should not cause damage to your hair. If you're considering getting faux locs, be sure to work with an experienced stylist and follow proper hair care techniques to keep your hair looking and feeling healthy.
