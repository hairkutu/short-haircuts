Do Fringes Make Your Face Look Fatter

Written by Aprilia Apr 18, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you considering getting a fringe but worried it might make your face look rounder? This is a common concern among many people, especially those with round or chubby faces. In this article, we'll explore whether fringes really do make your face look fatter and provide some tips on how to choose the right fringe for your face shape.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Choose the Right Fringe for Your Face Shape
  3. Step-by-Step Guide to Cutting Your Own Fringe
  4. Tips for Styling Your Fringe
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your Fringe

Description

Firstly, let's address the question at hand: do fringes make your face look fatter? The answer is not a straightforward yes or no. It largely depends on the type of fringe you go for and your face shape. Some fringes can actually help to slim down the face, while others can accentuate roundness and make the face appear wider.

It's important to consider your face shape when choosing a fringe. If you have a round face, for example, you want to avoid fringes that fall straight across the forehead as these can make your face appear wider. Instead, opt for a side-swept fringe that falls at an angle to create the illusion of length and slimness.

How to Choose the Right Fringe for Your Face Shape

Here's a quick guide to choosing the right fringe for your face shape:

Round Face

  • Avoid straight-across fringes
  • Opt for a side-swept fringe that falls at an angle

Square Face

  • Soft, rounded fringes work well
  • Avoid blunt or geometric fringes

Heart-Shaped Face

  • Avoid heavy, full fringes
  • Opt for a wispy, layered fringe

Oval Face

  • Lucky you! Oval faces can pull off just about any fringe style
  • Consider your hair type and personal style when choosing a fringe

Step-by-Step Guide to Cutting Your Own Fringe

If you're feeling brave and want to cut your own fringe at home, here's a step-by-step guide:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair
  2. Section off the hair you want to cut into a triangle shape
  3. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and use them as a guide for the length you want to cut
  4. Cut the hair straight across, making small snips rather than one big cut
  5. Check the length by letting go of the hair and allowing it to fall naturally
  6. Repeat on the other side, making sure to keep the sections even

Tips for Styling Your Fringe

Once you've got your fringe cut, it's important to style it in a way that flatters your face shape:

  • Blow-dry your fringe with a round brush to add volume and bounce
  • Use a straightener or curling iron to add texture
  • Experiment with different parting styles to see what works best for you
  • Don't be afraid to trim your fringe between cuts to keep it looking fresh

FAQs

Q: Will a fringe make my face look fatter?

A: It depends on the type of fringe you go for and your face shape. Avoid straight-across fringes if you have a round face and opt for a side-swept fringe instead.

Q: Can I cut my own fringe at home?

A: Yes, but it's important to follow a guide and take your time to avoid any mistakes.

Q: How often should I trim my fringe?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your fringe starts to look messy. Generally, every 4-6 weeks is a good time frame.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your Fringe

To keep your fringe looking its best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly to prevent oil buildup
  • Use a dry shampoo in between washes to absorb any excess oil
  • Avoid touching your fringe too often to prevent it from becoming greasy
  • Protect your fringe from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray before styling

So, there you have it â fringes don't necessarily make your face look fatter, but it's important to choose the right style for your face shape. Follow our tips and advice to ensure you get the perfect fringe that flatters your features.
