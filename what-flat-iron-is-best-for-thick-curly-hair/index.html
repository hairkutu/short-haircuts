What Flat Iron Is Best For Thick Curly Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 01, 2023 · 9 min read

Are you tired of dealing with frizz, tangles and unmanageable hair? Do you have thick curly hair that just won't cooperate? If so, you're not alone. Many people with thick curly hair struggle to find a flat iron that can effectively straighten their hair without causing damage. In this article, we'll take a look at what flat iron is best for thick curly hair, as well as provide tips on how to properly care for your hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Choose the Best Flat Iron for Thick Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Straightening Thick Curly Hair
  • Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair

  • Hair Care for Thick Curly Hair

Description

Thick curly hair can be beautiful, but it can also be difficult to manage. One of the best ways to tame unruly curls is by using a flat iron. However, not all flat irons are created equal, and choosing the wrong one can cause damage to your hair. In this article, we'll help you find the best flat iron for your hair type and provide tips on how to properly care for your hair.

How to Choose the Best Flat Iron for Thick Curly Hair

When it comes to choosing the best flat iron for thick curly hair, there are several factors to consider:

Plate Material

The material of the plates on your flat iron can make a big difference in how well it straightens your hair. Ceramic, titanium, and tourmaline are all popular plate materials. Ceramic plates are great for evenly distributing heat, while titanium plates can heat up quickly and retain heat well. Tourmaline plates emit negative ions that can help reduce frizz and static.

Plate Width

The width of the plates on your flat iron will depend on the length and thickness of your hair. For thick curly hair, plates that are at least 1 inch wide are recommended. Wider plates can help straighten hair more quickly, but may not be as effective for short hair.

Temperature Settings

Thick curly hair requires a higher heat setting to effectively straighten it. Look for a flat iron with adjustable temperature settings so you can find the right temperature for your hair type. However, be careful not to use a temperature that is too high, as this can cause damage to your hair.

Brand and Price

There are many different brands of flat irons on the market, and prices can vary widely. While a higher price tag doesn't always mean better quality, it's important to invest in a good quality flat iron that will last for years. Look for a reputable brand with good customer reviews.

Step-by-Step Guide to Straightening Thick Curly Hair

Now that you've chosen the best flat iron for your hair type, it's important to know how to use it properly. Here's a step-by-step guide to straightening thick curly hair:

  1. Wash and condition your hair with products designed for thick curly hair.
  2. Towel-dry your hair, then apply a heat protectant spray.
  3. Divide your hair into sections and clip them up.
  4. Starting with the bottom section, take a 1-2 inch section of hair and clamp the flat iron down as close to the roots as possible.
  5. Slowly glide the flat iron down the length of the hair, taking care not to stop or hold the iron in one place for too long.
  6. Repeat this process on each section of hair, working from the bottom up.
  7. Once you've straightened all of your hair, apply a smoothing serum or oil to add shine and reduce frizz.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair

Using a flat iron regularly can cause damage to your hair, so it's important to take steps to keep your hair healthy. Here are some tips:

  • Use a heat protectant spray every time you use a flat iron.
  • Avoid using a flat iron on wet hair.
  • Don't use a flat iron on the same section of hair more than once.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week.

FAQs

Q. Can I use a flat iron on wet hair?

No, it's not recommended to use a flat iron on wet hair. Wet hair is more prone to damage, and using a flat iron on wet hair can cause breakage and split ends.

Q. How often should I use a flat iron on my hair?

It's best to limit the use of a flat iron to once or twice a week to prevent damage to your hair. Using a flat iron too often can cause dryness, breakage, and split ends.

Q. Can I use a flat iron on color-treated hair?

Yes, you can use a flat iron on color-treated hair. However, it's important to use a heat protectant spray and to avoid using a temperature that is too high, as this can cause damage to your hair.

Hair Care for Thick Curly Hair

Proper hair care is important for maintaining healthy, beautiful hair. Here are some tips for caring for thick curly hair:

  • Use products designed for thick curly hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools too often.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week.

By following these tips and choosing the right flat iron for your hair type, you can achieve smooth, straight hair without causing damage to your hair.
