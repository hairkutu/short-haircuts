How To Texturize Bangs With Thinning Shears A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Sep 06, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you tired of your straight-cut bangs and want to add some texture to them? Look no further than thinning shears! With this tool, you can create a choppy, layered look that adds dimension to your bangs. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps of how to texturize bangs with thinning shears, as well as provide some tips and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  • How to Texturize Bangs with Thinning Shears
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Texturizing Bangs
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care for Textured Bangs

How to Texturize Bangs with Thinning Shears

Texturizing with thinning shears involves cutting into the hair at an angle, which removes some of the bulk and creates a layered effect. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Thinning shears (also called blending shears or texturizing shears)
  • Hair clips
  • Comb
  • Scissors (optional)

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to texturize your bangs:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Comb your bangs forward and clip the rest of your hair back.
  2. Take a small section of your bangs and hold it between your fingers, about two inches from the roots.
  3. Use the thinning shears to cut into the hair at an angle, starting about an inch from the roots and working your way down to the ends. Don’t cut straight across—this will create blunt lines instead of texture.
  4. Repeat this process on different sections of your bangs, making sure to vary the length and angle of the cuts for a natural look.
  5. Check your progress by combing through your bangs and adjusting any uneven spots.
  6. If you want a more dramatic effect, use regular scissors to make deeper cuts into certain sections of your bangs.
  7. Repeat steps 2-6 until you’ve achieved your desired texture.

Tips for Texturizing Bangs

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind:

  • Start with small cuts—you can always go back and make more. It’s better to err on the side of caution than to accidentally cut too much off.
  • Don’t use thinning shears on wet hair, as this can cause the hair to clump together and create uneven results.
  • Try to avoid cutting into the same section of hair repeatedly, as this can create a hole or bald spot.
  • If you’re not confident in your ability to texturize your own bangs, consider going to a professional stylist.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about texturizing bangs with thinning shears:

  • Can I use regular scissors instead of thinning shears? While regular scissors can be used to texturize hair, they tend to create more blunt lines and may not produce the same layered effect as thinning shears.
  • How often should I texturize my bangs? This depends on how quickly your hair grows and how much texture you want. Some people prefer to texturize their bangs every few weeks, while others may only do it once every few months.
  • Will texturizing my bangs make them harder to style? Not necessarily. Texturizing can actually make your bangs easier to style, as it can help them hold their shape and add volume.

Hair Care for Textured Bangs

Now that you’ve texturized your bangs, it’s important to take care of them properly. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can dry it out and make it more prone to breakage.
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Use a small amount of styling product, such as mousse or texturizing spray, to help define your bangs and keep them in place.

Texturizing your bangs with thinning shears is a great way to add some dimension and style to your hair. With these tips and step-by-step instructions, you’ll be able to achieve the perfect textured look in no time!
