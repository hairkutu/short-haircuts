Why Won t My Hair Hold Curls

Are you tired of spending hours curling your hair only to have the curls fall out within an hour? It's frustrating, we know. But don't worry, you're not alone. Many people struggle with hair that won't hold curls. In this article, we'll explore the reasons why your hair may not be holding curls and provide tips and tricks to help you achieve the curly hairstyle you desire.

Reasons Why Your Hair Won't Hold Curls

Before we dive into how to make your curls last, it's important to understand why your hair may not be holding curls in the first place. Here are some common reasons:

1. Your hair is too clean or too dirty.

Believe it or not, having hair that is too clean or too dirty can both be problematic when it comes to holding curls. If your hair is too clean, it may be too slippery for the curling iron to grip onto. On the other hand, if your hair is too dirty, it may be weighed down by oils and product buildup, which can cause the curls to fall flat.

2. Your hair is too soft.

If your hair is naturally soft or has been chemically treated to be soft (such as with a relaxer), it may not hold curls well. Soft hair doesn't have the same structure and texture as coarse hair, which can make it difficult for curls to stay in place.

3. Your hair is damaged.

If your hair is damaged from heat styling, chemical treatments, or environmental factors, it may be too weak to hold curls. Damaged hair is more susceptible to breakage and doesn't have the strength to maintain a curly shape.

4. You're using the wrong products.

The products you use on your hair can have a big impact on how well your curls hold. Using heavy, oily products can weigh down your hair and cause the curls to fall flat. On the other hand, using products that are too lightweight may not provide enough hold to keep your curls in place.

How to Curl Your Hair to Make it Last

Now that you know some of the reasons why your hair may not be holding curls, let's talk about how to curl your hair to make it last. Follow these steps for best results:

Step-by-Step Guide for Curling Your Hair

1. Start with dry hair. If your hair is wet or damp, the curls won't hold as well. Blow dry your hair or let it air dry completely before curling.

2. Apply a heat protectant. This will help prevent damage to your hair from the curling iron.

3. Section your hair. Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to curl your hair and ensure that all of your hair gets curled.

4. Choose the right curling iron. The size of the curling iron you use will depend on the size of the curls you want. Generally, a smaller curling iron will create tighter curls, while a larger curling iron will create looser waves.

5. Curl your hair. Start at the bottom of each section and work your way up, wrapping small sections of hair around the curling iron. Hold for a few seconds, then release. Repeat until all sections are curled.

6. Let your curls cool. Once you've curled all of your hair, let the curls cool completely. This will help set the curl and make it last longer.

7. Finish with hairspray. Spray a light mist of hairspray over your curls to help hold them in place.

Tips for Making Your Curls Last Longer

In addition to following the steps above, there are some additional tips and tricks you can use to make your curls last longer:

  • Use a texturizing spray or dry shampoo before curling to add grip to your hair.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much after curling. The oils from your hands can weigh down your hair and cause the curls to fall flat.
  • Wrap your curls in a silk scarf or sleep on a silk pillowcase to help prevent frizz and maintain the shape of your curls overnight.
  • Avoid using too much hairspray or other heavy products, as this can cause your curls to fall flat.

FAQs About Hair That Won't Hold Curls

Why won't my hair hold curls even with hairspray?

If your hair still won't hold curls even with hairspray, there may be other factors at play. Make sure your hair is completely dry before curling, and consider using a texturizing spray or dry shampoo to add grip to your hair.

Can I curl my hair if it's been relaxed?

Yes, you can curl relaxed hair, but it may be more difficult to get the curls to hold. Be sure to use a heat protectant and avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and cause the curls to fall flat.

Is it bad to curl your hair every day?

Curling your hair every day can cause damage to your hair over time, especially if you're using high heat. To minimize damage, try to limit heat styling to a few times a week and use a heat protectant every time you curl your hair.

Hair Care Habits That Can Affect Your Curls

In addition to the tips and tricks above, there are some hair care habits that can affect how well your curls hold:

  • Wash your hair less frequently. Over-washing can strip your hair of its natural oils, which can make it more difficult for your curls to hold.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and strong.
  • Avoid using hot tools on a high heat setting. Use a lower heat setting to minimize damage to your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends, which can make it more difficult for your curls to hold.

By following these tips and tricks, you can achieve long-lasting, beautiful curls that won't fall flat. Remember to take care of your hair and use the right products and tools to keep your curls looking their best.
