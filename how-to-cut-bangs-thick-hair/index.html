How To Cut Bangs For Thick Hair A Step By Step Guide

If you have thick hair, you might be hesitant to cut your own bangs. But with the right technique and tools, it's actually quite easy to do. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps of cutting bangs for thick hair, as well as provide some tips and tricks to make the process even easier.

  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Cut Bangs for Thick Hair

Cutting bangs for thick hair requires a few different techniques than cutting thinner hair. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can cause split ends and make the hair harder to cut.
  • Start with dry hair. Wet hair can be deceptive and shrink up when dry, making your bangs shorter than you intended.
  • Go slow. Cutting hair is always easier to do in stages than all at once.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the basics, here's a step-by-step guide to cutting bangs for thick hair:

  1. Start by sectioning off your bangs. Use a comb to separate your bangs from the rest of your hair. Try to create a triangle shape that starts at the crown of your head and ends at your temples.
  2. Decide on the length of your bangs. Hold up the hair you want to cut and measure it against your forehead. Remember, it's always better to cut less than you think you need at first.
  3. Begin cutting. Use your scissors to cut into the bangs at an angle, starting from the center and working your way outward. This will help create a natural-looking curve.
  4. Trim the sides. Once you've cut the center, it's time to trim the sides of your bangs. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut straight across.
  5. Check your work. Shake out your bangs to see how they look. If they're too long, go back and make some small cuts until they're the right length.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to help you cut your bangs for thick hair:

  • Use a razor comb. A razor comb can help thin out thick hair and create a softer look.
  • Add layers. If you want to create a more textured look, try adding layers to your bangs.
  • Don't cut too much at once. Remember, it's always easier to cut more hair later than it is to try to grow it back quickly.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting bangs for thick hair:

  • Q: Can I cut my bangs while they're wet?
  • A: It's best to cut your bangs while they're dry to avoid any surprises once your hair dries. Wet hair can shrink up, making your bangs shorter than you intended.
  • Q: How often should I trim my bangs?
  • A: It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how short you like your bangs. As a general rule of thumb, you should trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks.
  • Q: Should I cut my bangs straight across or at an angle?
  • A: Cutting your bangs at an angle can help create a more natural-looking curve. However, if you prefer a blunt look, you can cut them straight across.

Hair Care After Cutting Bangs

After you've cut your bangs for thick hair, it's important to take care of them properly. Here are some tips to keep your bangs looking their best:

  • Use a heat protectant. If you're going to be using heat styling tools on your bangs, make sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.
  • Don't overwash your hair. Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, making it harder to manage.
  • Use a dry shampoo. If your bangs start to look greasy between washes, use a dry shampoo to absorb excess oil.

With these tips and tricks, you should be able to cut your own bangs for thick hair with confidence. Just remember to take it slow, use sharp scissors, and don't be afraid to make small cuts until you're happy with the length.
