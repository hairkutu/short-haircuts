Can I Start Dreadlocks With Relaxed Hair

Written by Christine Jul 20, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have relaxed hair and you're wondering if you can start dreadlocks, the answer is yes! It's entirely possible to start dreadlocks with relaxed hair, and it can be done in a way that's healthy for your hair. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about starting dreadlocks with relaxed hair, including how to do it, tips for maintaining your dreadlocks, and more.

Description

Dreadlocks are a popular hairstyle that has been around for centuries. They are often associated with Rastafarianism, but they have been worn by people of all cultures and backgrounds. Dreadlocks are created by twisting or matting the hair until it forms into long, rope-like strands. The process can take some time, but the end result is a unique and stylish hairstyle that can be customized to fit your personality.

How to Start Dreadlocks with Relaxed Hair

Starting dreadlocks with relaxed hair is a bit different than starting them with natural hair. Here's a step-by-step guide to help you get started:

Step 1: Stop Relaxing Your Hair

The first step in starting dreadlocks with relaxed hair is to stop relaxing your hair. Relaxers are designed to straighten and soften the hair, which makes it difficult for the hair to form into dreadlocks. If you continue to relax your hair, it will be harder to start and maintain your dreadlocks.

Step 2: Let Your Hair Grow Out

Next, you'll need to let your hair grow out for a few months. The longer your hair is, the easier it will be to form into dreadlocks. During this time, you can start to prepare your hair for dreadlocks by keeping it clean and moisturized.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Once your hair has grown out, you can start to section it into small, even sections. You can use a comb or your fingers to section your hair, depending on what works best for you.

Step 4: Twist Your Hair

After you've sectioned your hair, you can start to twist each section of hair until it forms into a cylinder shape. You can use a twisting gel or wax to help hold the twists in place.

Step 5: Maintain Your Dreadlocks

Once you've started your dreadlocks, you'll need to maintain them by keeping them clean and moisturized. You can use a special shampoo designed for dreadlocks to keep them clean, and you can use a moisturizing oil or cream to keep them from drying out.

Tips for Maintaining Your Dreadlocks

Here are some tips to help you maintain your dreadlocks:

  • Wash your dreadlocks regularly to keep them clean.
  • Use a special shampoo designed for dreadlocks.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Use a light moisturizing oil or cream to keep your dreadlocks from drying out.
  • Be gentle when styling your dreadlocks to avoid pulling or breaking your hair.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about starting dreadlocks with relaxed hair:

Can I start dreadlocks with relaxed hair?

Yes, you can start dreadlocks with relaxed hair. It's important to stop relaxing your hair and let it grow out before starting the process.

How long does it take to start dreadlocks with relaxed hair?

The length of time it takes to start dreadlocks with relaxed hair can vary depending on your hair type and how well you maintain your hair. It can take anywhere from a few months to a year or more to get fully formed dreadlocks.

Do I need to cut off my relaxed hair to start dreadlocks?

No, you don't need to cut off your relaxed hair to start dreadlocks. You can let your hair grow out and start the process from there.

Hair Care

Starting dreadlocks with relaxed hair requires some patience and care, but it can be done in a way that's healthy for your hair. By following the steps outlined above and taking good care of your hair, you can have beautiful, healthy dreadlocks that you'll love.
