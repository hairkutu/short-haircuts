Why Does My Hair Go Frizzy When I Brush It

Written by Bardi May 19, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Frizzy hair can be a real pain to deal with. It's frustrating when you spend time washing and brushing your hair, only to end up with a frizzy mess. So, why does your hair go frizzy when you brush it?

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to
  3. Step by step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description

Frizz occurs when the cuticle layer of your hair is lifted, allowing moisture to enter the hair shaft. When you brush your hair, you create friction which can cause the cuticle layer to lift even more. This leads to more moisture entering the hair shaft and causing frizz.

Other factors that can contribute to frizz include humidity, heat styling, and chemical treatments.

How to

If you're tired of dealing with frizzy hair every time you brush it, there are a few things you can do to help:

Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Using a wide-tooth comb can help to detangle your hair without causing as much friction as a brush. This can help to reduce the amount of frizz you experience.

Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Leave-in conditioner can help to add moisture to your hair, reducing the amount of frizz that occurs. Look for a lightweight formula that won't weigh down your hair.

Avoid Brushing Your Hair When It's Wet

Wet hair is more fragile than dry hair, and brushing it can cause damage to the cuticle layer. This can lead to more frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair.

Step by Step

If you're not sure how to best care for your hair to reduce frizz, here are some steps you can follow:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. This can help to add moisture to your hair and reduce frizz.

Step 2: Apply Leave-In Conditioner

After washing your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to help add moisture and reduce frizz.

Step 3: Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots.

Step 4: Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can cause damage to your hair, leading to more frizz. Try air-drying your hair or using a diffuser on your hair dryer.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help reduce frizz:

  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can also strip it of natural oils.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, as these can cause damage to the cuticle layer.

FAQ

Q: Can brushing your hair cause split ends?

A: Yes, brushing your hair can cause split ends if you're not careful. Be sure to use a wide-tooth comb and be gentle when detangling your hair.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: This can vary depending on your hair type and lifestyle. Some people may need to wash their hair every day, while others may only need to wash it once or twice a week.

Hair Care

Caring for your hair can help to reduce frizz and keep it looking healthy. Be sure to use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and try to avoid heat styling and harsh chemicals. Using a wide-tooth comb and a leave-in conditioner can also help to reduce frizz.

With a little bit of care and attention, you can keep your hair looking its best and avoid the frustration of frizz.
