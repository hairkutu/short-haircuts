Is It Best To Wear Your Hair Up Or Down For An Interview

Written by Petter May 12, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you have an upcoming job interview, you might be wondering how to style your hair. Should you wear it up or down? The answer is not always straightforward, as it depends on several factors, including the industry, the job position, and your personal style. In this blog post, we will discuss whether it is best to wear your hair up or down for an interview and provide tips on how to style your hair for success.

Consider the Industry

The industry you are interviewing for can play a significant role in deciding whether to wear your hair up or down. For example, if you are interviewing for a corporate job in finance or law, wearing your hair up in a neat bun or ponytail might be more appropriate. On the other hand, if you are interviewing for a creative industry, such as fashion or beauty, you might have more flexibility to wear your hair down or in a trendy style. Research the company and the industry to get a sense of the dress code and the expectations for hair and makeup.

Consider the Job Position

The job position you are applying for can also influence your hairstyle choice. If you are interviewing for a senior-level position, wearing your hair up can convey a sense of professionalism and competence. However, if you are interviewing for an entry-level or customer-facing role, wearing your hair down can help you appear approachable and friendly. Consider the job duties, responsibilities, and the level of interaction with clients or customers to determine the most appropriate hairstyle.

Consider Your Personal Style

While it is important to consider the industry and the job position, you should also feel comfortable and confident in your hairstyle. If you never wear your hair up and feel uncomfortable with a tight bun, it might not be the best choice for an interview. On the other hand, if you feel self-conscious with your hair down and prefer a sleek ponytail, go for it. The key is to strike a balance between professionalism and personal style.

How to Style Your Hair for an Interview

When it comes to styling your hair for an interview, the goal is to create a polished, neat, and sophisticated look. Here are some general tips:

  • Choose a hairstyle that complements your face shape and features.
  • Avoid hairstyles that are too elaborate, messy, or distracting.
  • Use hair products, such as hairspray, gel, or mousse, to keep your hair in place and prevent flyaways.
  • If you have long hair, consider wearing it in a low bun, a braided style, or a half-up, half-down style.
  • If you have short hair, consider adding volume with a blowout or styling it with hair accessories, such as clips or headbands.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Your Hair

If you need more specific guidance on how to style your hair for an interview, here is a step-by-step guide:

  1. Wash and condition your hair with a quality shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Towel-dry your hair and apply a heat protectant product.
  3. Blow-dry your hair with a round brush to add volume and smoothness.
  4. If you have long hair, create a low bun by gathering your hair at the nape of your neck and securing it with a hair tie and bobby pins. If you have short hair, style it with a curling iron or flat iron and add a hair accessory, such as a clip or headband.

Tips for a Successful Interview Hairstyle

Here are some additional tips to ensure your interview hairstyle is a success:

  • Don't try a new hairstyle on the day of the interview. Stick to a hairstyle you are comfortable with and have practiced before.
  • Avoid using too much hair product, as it can create a greasy or crunchy look.

FAQs About Hairstyles for Interviews

Here are some frequently asked questions about hairstyles for interviews:

  • Should I wear my hair up or down for a job interview? It depends on the industry, job position, and personal style. Generally, a neat and polished hairstyle, such as a low bun or a half-up, half-down style, can work well for most interviews.
  • Can I wear my hair in a ponytail for an interview? Yes, as long as it looks neat and professional. Avoid high ponytails or messy styles.
  • Is it okay to have colored hair for an interview? It depends on the industry and the company. If you work in a conservative field, such as finance or law, natural hair colors are usually preferred. However, if you work in a creative industry, such as advertising or design, colored hair can be acceptable as long as it looks well-maintained and professional.

Hair Care Tips for Interviews

Finally, here are some hair care tips to ensure your hair looks its best for the interview:

  • Wash your hair the night before the interview to avoid a greasy or frizzy look.

Now that you know whether to wear your hair up or down for an interview and how to style your hair for success, you can feel confident and prepared for your job interview. Remember, your hairstyle is just one aspect of your overall appearance and should complement your skills, experience, and personality. Good luck!
