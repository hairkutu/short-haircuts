How To Know If You Will Look Good With Shoulder Length Hair

Written by Robby Sep 02, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you thinking about getting a shoulder-length haircut but not sure if it will suit you? Well, you're not alone. Many women are hesitant to chop off their long locks because they fear that they won't look good with shorter hair. But don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll guide you through the steps to figure out if shoulder-length hair is the right style for you.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Shoulder-length hair is a versatile style that can suit many face shapes and hair types. It's a popular choice because it's not too short and not too long, making it easy to maintain and style. However, not everyone will look good with this length of hair. It's essential to consider your face shape, hair type, and lifestyle before making the chop.

How to

There are a few things to consider when determining if shoulder-length hair is right for you. The following factors can help you decide if this length will suit your face shape and hair type:

Face Shape

Consider your face shape when choosing a haircut. Shoulder-length hair tends to look good on oval, square, and heart-shaped faces. If you have a round face, it may not be the best choice, as this length can make your face appear wider. If you're unsure of your face shape, consult with your hairstylist to determine the best haircut for you.

Hair Type

Not all hair types are created equal. Consider the thickness and texture of your hair when choosing a haircut. Shoulder-length hair works well with medium to thick hair types. If you have thin hair, this length may not be the best choice, as it can make your hair look flat and lifeless. Additionally, if you have curly or wavy hair, this length can accentuate your natural texture.

Lifestyle

Your lifestyle is an essential factor to consider when choosing a haircut. If you're a busy mom or have a hectic work schedule, you may not have time to style your hair every day. Shoulder-length hair is a great choice for those who want a low-maintenance haircut that can be styled quickly and easily.

Step by Step

Now that you've considered the factors above, follow these steps to determine if shoulder-length hair is right for you:

  1. Research different shoulder-length haircuts and styles that you like.
  2. Consider your face shape, hair type, and lifestyle to determine if this length will suit you.
  3. Consult with your hairstylist to determine the best haircut for your face shape and hair type.
  4. Try on a wig or use a virtual hairstyle app to see how you'll look with shoulder-length hair.
  5. If you're still unsure, start with a shorter haircut and work your way up to shoulder-length hair.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when getting a shoulder-length haircut:

  • Bring pictures of hairstyles you like to your hairstylist.
  • Invest in hair products that are suited for your hair type.
  • Experiment with different styling techniques to find what works best for you.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and health of your haircut.

FAQ

Will shoulder-length hair make my face look wider?

It depends on your face shape. If you have a round face, shoulder-length hair may not be the best choice, as it can make your face appear wider. However, if you have an oval, square, or heart-shaped face, this length can be very flattering.

What hair types work best with shoulder-length hair?

Shoulder-length hair works well with medium to thick hair types. If you have thin hair, this length may not be the best choice, as it can make your hair look flat and lifeless. Additionally, if you have curly or wavy hair, this length can accentuate your natural texture.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential to maintain the health and shape of your shoulder-length haircut. Here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that are suited for your hair type.
  • Invest in high-quality styling tools, such as a blow dryer and flat iron.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and health of your haircut.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can damage the hair follicles.
  • Experiment with different styling techniques to find what works best for you.

Now that you know how to determine if shoulder-length hair is right for you, it's time to make the chop. Remember to consider your face shape, hair type, and lifestyle when choosing a haircut, and always consult with your hairstylist for advice. With the right haircut and proper hair care, you'll be rocking your new shoulder-length hair in no time!
