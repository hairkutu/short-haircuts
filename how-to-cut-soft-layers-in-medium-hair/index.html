How To Cut Soft Layers In Medium Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Aug 09, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking to add some dimension and movement to your medium-length hair, soft layers are the way to go. Not only do they add texture and volume, but they can also frame your face and enhance your features. However, cutting your own hair can be intimidating, especially if you're not a professional stylist. That's why we've put together this step-by-step guide to help you achieve the perfect soft layers for your hair type and face shape.

Description

Before we dive into the cutting process, let's talk about what soft layers actually are. Soft layers are layers that are cut with a razor or scissors to create a seamless, blended look. Unlike harsh, choppy layers, soft layers are meant to look natural and flowy, adding movement and depth to your hair.

How to Cut Soft Layers in Medium Hair

Now that you have a better understanding of what soft layers are, let's get started on the cutting process. Here's how to cut soft layers in medium hair:

Step 1: Wash and dry your hair

Start by washing your hair with your favorite shampoo and conditioner. Once you've towel-dried your hair, blow-dry it using a round brush to add volume and smoothness.

Step 2: Part your hair

Using a comb, create a part in your hair where you want your layers to start. For a natural look, try a side part or a middle part.

Step 3: Divide your hair into sections

Using clips, divide your hair into sections: one at the crown, one at the back, and one on each side of your head.

Step 4: Start cutting

Using a pair of sharp scissors, start cutting your hair in a downward motion, starting from the bottom of each section and working your way up. Be sure to cut at an angle to create soft, blended layers. Remember, it's always better to start with less hair and work your way up, rather than cutting too much at once.

Step 5: Blend your layers

Once you've cut all of your sections, use a razor to blend your layers together. This will create a seamless, natural look.

Step 6: Style your hair

After cutting your layers, style your hair as desired. For a natural look, try using a texturizing spray or a sea salt spray to enhance your layers and add volume.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting soft layers in medium hair:

  • Invest in a pair of high-quality scissors and a razor.
  • Start with dry hair to get a better idea of how your layers will look when styled.
  • Be patient and take your time - rushing can lead to uneven layers.
  • When in doubt, consult a professional stylist.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting soft layers in medium hair:

Can I cut soft layers in curly hair?

Absolutely! Soft layers can add dimension and shape to curly hair. Just be sure to cut your hair when it's dry, as cutting it when it's wet can lead to uneven layers.

How often should I cut my hair to maintain my soft layers?

It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain your soft layers and keep your hair healthy.

Can I cut soft layers in short hair?

Yes, you can! However, keep in mind that the shorter your hair is, the more difficult it may be to achieve soft, blended layers.

Hair Care

Maintaining healthy hair is key to achieving the perfect soft layers. Here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy:

  • Use a heat protectant before styling your hair with hot tools.
  • Limit your use of hot tools to prevent damage.
  • Apply a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes that can damage your hair.
  • Eat a balanced diet and stay hydrated to promote healthy hair growth.

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you'll be able to achieve soft, beautiful layers in your medium-length hair. Remember, practice makes perfect, so don't be afraid to experiment and find the perfect style for you!
