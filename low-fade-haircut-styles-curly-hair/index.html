Low Fade Haircut Styles For Curly Hair

Written by Eveline Sep 09, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of your curly hair and want to switch up your look? A low fade haircut might be just what you need. This style is versatile and can work with various lengths and textures of hair, making it a great option for those with curly hair. In this article, we'll discuss everything you need to know about low fade haircut styles for curly hair, including how to achieve the look, tips, and hair care.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Achieve the Look
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips for Maintaining Your Low Fade Haircut
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care for Curly Hair with a Low Fade Haircut

Description

A low fade haircut is a style that gradually fades from longer hair at the top of the head to shorter hair on the sides and back. This style is achieved using clippers and can be customized to fit your preferences. For curly hair, a low fade haircut can help to define your curls and add dimension to your overall look.

How to Achieve the Look

If you're interested in getting a low fade haircut, the first step is to find a stylist who specializes in this style. They will be able to guide you through the process and help you choose the right length and texture for your hair. Once you've found a stylist, you can discuss the following options:

Length

The length of your hair will determine how much of a fade you'll need. If you have longer hair, you may only need a slight fade, while shorter hair may require a more dramatic fade. Your stylist can help you determine the right length for your hair.

Texture

The texture of your hair will also help to determine the type of fade you need. If your hair is very curly, you may want a softer fade that blends into your curls. If your hair is less curly, you may be able to achieve a sharper, more defined fade.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to achieving a low fade haircut for curly hair:

  1. Wash and condition your hair to make sure it's clean and tangle-free.
  2. Blow-dry your hair using a diffuser to enhance your curls.
  3. Part your hair where you want the fade to begin.
  4. Use clippers to create the fade, starting with a longer guard and gradually switching to shorter guards as you move down the sides and back of your head.
  5. Blend the fade into your curls using scissors or clippers.
  6. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Maintaining Your Low Fade Haircut

Once you've achieved your low fade haircut, it's important to maintain it to keep it looking great. Here are some tips for maintaining your low fade:

  • Get regular trims to keep your fade looking fresh.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your curls moisturized and defined.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about low fade haircut styles for curly hair:

Can you get a low fade haircut if you have short curly hair?

Yes, a low fade can work with short curly hair. Your stylist can help you determine the right length and texture for your hair.

How often should you get a trim for your low fade?

You should get a trim every 4-6 weeks to keep your fade looking fresh.

Can you style your hair differently with a low fade?

Yes, a low fade provides versatility for styling your hair. You can experiment with different hairstyles, such as a slicked-back look or a messy, textured look.

Hair Care for Curly Hair with a Low Fade Haircut

When caring for your curly hair with a low fade, it's important to use products that will enhance your curls and keep them moisturized. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your curls moisturized and defined.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve a stylish low fade haircut that works with your curly hair. Remember to maintain your haircut and keep your hair healthy by using the right products and avoiding heat styling tools too often. With the right care, you can enjoy a great-looking low fade haircut for years to come.
