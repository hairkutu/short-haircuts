How To Hide Thinning Hair On Crown A Guide For Men And Women

Written by Bardi Aug 19, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Thinning hair can be a source of frustration for both men and women. It can make you feel self-conscious and affect your confidence. If you're struggling with thinning hair on your crown, don't worry. There are plenty of ways to hide it and boost your self-esteem. In this article, we'll show you some tips and tricks on how to hide thinning hair on crown.

Description

Thinning hair on the crown is a common problem for both men and women. It can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, age, and hormonal changes. Thinning hair on the crown can be difficult to hide, but there are several strategies you can use to make it less noticeable.

How to Hide Thinning Hair on Crown

Here are some ways you can hide thinning hair on your crown:

1. Change Your Hairstyle

Changing your hairstyle can be a simple and effective way to hide thinning hair on your crown. Try a shorter cut or a style with more volume on top to create the illusion of thicker hair.

2. Use Hair Fibers

Hair fibers are a temporary solution for thinning hair. They are made of keratin fibers that attach to your existing hair, making it look thicker and fuller. Apply the fibers to your crown area, and then style your hair as usual.

3. Try a Scalp Concealer

Scalp concealers are makeup products that can be used to cover up thinning hair on the crown. They come in a variety of shades and can be applied directly to your scalp to create the illusion of thicker hair.

4. Use Hair Extensions

Hair extensions are a more long-term solution for thinning hair on the crown. You can choose from a variety of extensions, including clip-ins, tape-ins, and sew-ins. Extensions can add volume and length to your hair, making it look fuller and thicker.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to hide thinning hair on your crown:

Step 1: Start with a clean slate

Wash your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body and texture to your hair.

Step 2: Apply a Scalp Concealer

Apply a scalp concealer to your crown area to cover up thinning hair. Use a brush or applicator to apply the product evenly.

Step 3: Use Hair Fibers

Apply hair fibers to your crown area to make your hair look thicker and fuller. Use a brush or applicator to apply the fibers evenly.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Style your hair as usual, using a blow dryer or styling tools to add volume and texture.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you hide thinning hair on your crown:

1. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles, like ponytails or braids, can pull on your hair and make thinning more noticeable. Instead, opt for looser hairstyles that don't put too much pressure on your hair.

2. Use a Thickening Shampoo

Thickening shampoos can add volume and body to your hair, making it look thicker and fuller. Look for shampoos that contain ingredients like biotin, keratin, and collagen.

3. Get Regular Haircuts

Regular haircuts can help keep your hair healthy and prevent breakage. Keeping your hair trimmed can also make it look thicker and fuller.

FAQ

Q: Can stress cause thinning hair on the crown?

A: Yes, stress can cause hair loss and thinning, including on the crown.

Q: Is thinning hair on the crown permanent?

A: Thinning hair on the crown can be permanent or temporary, depending on the cause.

Q: Are there any home remedies for thinning hair on the crown?

A: There are several home remedies that may help with thinning hair, including scalp massages, essential oils, and a balanced diet.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is essential for preventing thinning and promoting healthy hair growth. Here are some tips for hair care:

1. Avoid Harsh Chemicals

Harsh chemicals, like bleach and dye, can damage your hair and cause breakage. Avoid using these chemicals, or use them sparingly.

2. Use a Heat Protectant

Heat styling tools, like flat irons and curling irons, can damage your hair and cause breakage. Use a heat protectant spray or serum before using these tools to protect your hair.

3. Eat a Balanced Diet

Eating a balanced diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals can promote healthy hair growth. Make sure to include foods like leafy greens, nuts, and eggs in your diet.

In conclusion, thinning hair on the crown can be frustrating, but there are plenty of ways to hide it and boost your confidence. Try changing your hairstyle, using hair fibers or a scalp concealer, or using hair extensions. Remember to take care of your hair by avoiding harsh chemicals, using a heat protectant, and eating a balanced diet.
