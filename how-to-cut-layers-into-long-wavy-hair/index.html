How To Cut Layers Into Long Wavy Hair

Written by Daniel May 07, 2023 · 9 min read

Long wavy hair is beautiful, but it can sometimes lack shape and movement. Adding layers can give your hair the perfect amount of texture and volume. In this article, we will discuss how to cut layers into long wavy hair. We’ll cover everything from how to prepare your hair to the final touches. Let’s get started!

Table of Contents

  1. Preparing Your Hair
  2. Sectioning Your Hair
  3. Cutting Your Layers
  4. Blending Your Layers
  5. Texturizing Your Layers

  7. Tips for Cutting Layers into Long Wavy Hair
  8. FAQs
  9. Hair Care for Long Wavy Hair with Layers

Preparing Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, you need to make sure it is properly washed and dried. Clean hair will be easier to work with, and it will give you a more accurate cut. Make sure you use a good conditioner to keep your hair smooth and tangle-free.

Once your hair is dry, brush it out to remove any tangles. Then, part your hair down the middle to create two even sections. It’s important to have even sections so that your layers are symmetrical.

Sectioning Your Hair

Next, you need to section your hair into layers. Start by taking a small section of hair from the front of your head. This section should be about 1 inch wide and 1 inch deep. Comb through the section to make sure it is smooth and tangle-free.

Hold the section of hair straight up, and cut it straight across. This will be your guide for the rest of your layers. Now, take another section of hair directly underneath the first section. This section should be slightly longer than the first section. Use your guide to cut the second section of hair.

Continue this process, working your way towards the back of your head. You can make your layers as long or as short as you want. Keep in mind that shorter layers will add more volume to your hair.

Cutting Your Layers

Now that you have your sections, it’s time to start cutting your layers. Take a small section of hair from the first layer, and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your fingers, and cut it at an angle. The angle will depend on how long you want your layers to be. Repeat this process for each section of hair.

Remember to cut your layers slowly and cautiously. It’s better to cut less and go back for more than to cut too much and ruin your hair. You can always go back and cut more later.

Blending Your Layers

Once you’ve cut all of your layers, it’s time to blend them together. Take a comb and run it through your hair. This will help blend your layers and give your hair a more natural look.

If you notice any unevenness, you can go back and trim any stray hairs. Use caution when trimming, and only cut small amounts at a time.

Texturizing Your Layers

Texturizing your layers can give your hair even more volume and movement. To do this, take small sections of hair and cut into them with scissors. This will create small, choppy pieces that will add texture to your hair.

Texturizing can be done sparingly, as it is easy to overdo it. Start with small sections of hair, and work your way up to larger sections if you want more texture.

Finishing Touches

Now that you’ve cut and blended your layers, it’s time to add some finishing touches. You can style your hair however you like, but remember to use heat protectant if you’re using hot tools. This will help prevent damage to your hair.

You can also add some hairspray or mousse to give your hair extra hold and volume. Experiment with different products to find what works best for your hair type.

Tips for Cutting Layers into Long Wavy Hair

  • Start with small sections of hair, and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Use caution when cutting, and only cut small amounts at a time.
  • Remember to blend your layers for a natural look.
  • Texturizing can be done sparingly to avoid overdoing it.
  • Experiment with different products to find what works best for your hair type.

FAQs

How often should I cut my layers?

It’s recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and maintain your layers.

Can I cut layers into my hair at home?

Yes, you can cut layers into your hair at home. However, it’s important to use caution and start with small sections of hair.

Will layers work with all hair types?

Yes, layers can work with all hair types. However, it’s important to consult with a hairstylist if you have very curly or thick hair to ensure the layers are cut properly.

Hair Care for Long Wavy Hair with Layers

After cutting your layers, it’s important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Use a good shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized. Avoid using hot tools too often to prevent damage to your hair.

You can also use hair masks and deep conditioners to give your hair extra hydration. Remember to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain your layers and keep your hair healthy.

Cutting layers into long wavy hair can give your hair the perfect amount of texture and volume. With these tips and techniques, you can achieve a beautiful, layered look at home. Remember to start with small sections of hair, and use caution when cutting. Happy styling!
