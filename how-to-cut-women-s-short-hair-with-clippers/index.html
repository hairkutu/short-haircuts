How To Cut Women s Short Hair With Clippers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy May 26, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking to cut a woman's short hair with clippers, you've come to the right place! Whether you're a beginner or a pro, this step-by-step guide will help you achieve a chic, low-maintenance look that's perfect for any occasion. From choosing the right tools to executing the perfect cut, we've got you covered. Read on to learn more!

Table of Contents

  • Prepping the Hair
  • Sectioning the Hair
  • Cutting the Hair
  • Blending the Hair
  • Cleaning Up
  • Styling the Hair
  • Tips for a Successful Cut
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After the Cut

Tools You'll Need

To cut a woman's short hair with clippers, you'll need the following tools:

  • Clipper with guards
  • Shears
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Hair spray or water bottle
  • Cape or towel

Make sure your clippers are sharp and the guards are securely attached. You don't want to accidentally pull or tug on the hair.

Prepping the Hair

Before you begin cutting, make sure the hair is clean and dry. Wet hair can be more difficult to cut evenly. Comb through the hair to remove any tangles or knots.

Next, decide on the length you want to cut the hair. If you're not sure, start with a longer guard and work your way down. You can always go shorter, but you can't add hair back once it's cut.

Finally, drape a cape or towel around the person's shoulders to protect their clothing. Use hair clips to secure any loose hair out of the way.

Sectioning the Hair

Sectioning the hair ensures that you're cutting evenly and not missing any spots. Start by parting the hair down the middle, from forehead to nape of the neck. Then, create a horizontal part from ear to ear. You should now have four sections of hair.

Clip the top two sections out of the way, leaving the bottom two sections loose. Take a small section of hair from the bottom and comb it straight down. Choose the guard size you want to use and attach it to the clippers.

Cutting the Hair

Hold the clippers at a 45-degree angle, with the teeth pointing up. Start at the nape of the neck and work your way up, using smooth, even strokes. Use the comb to guide the clippers through the hair and keep it even.

Work your way around the head, using the same guard size for each section. If you're using multiple guard sizes, switch them out as you go. Pay attention to any cowlicks or uneven spots and adjust as needed.

Blending the Hair

After you've cut all the hair to the desired length, it's time to blend the layers. Remove the guard from the clippers and use the bare blade to trim any longer hairs that stick out. Use the shears to blend the hair around the crown of the head.

Cleaning Up

Use a brush or towel to remove any loose hair from the person's neck and shoulders. Use a damp cloth to wipe down the clippers and shears. Oil the blades to keep them in good condition.

Styling the Hair

Once the hair is cut and blended, it's time to style it. You can use a hair spray or water bottle to add volume or texture. Use your fingers or a comb to create the desired look.

Tips for a Successful Cut

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to avoid cutting too much too quickly.
  • Keep the clippers moving in smooth, even strokes to avoid choppy or uneven cuts.
  • Pay attention to any cowlicks or uneven spots and adjust as needed.
  • Blend the layers carefully to avoid any choppy or uneven spots.
  • Communicate with the person you're cutting to ensure they're happy with the length and style.

FAQs

Q: Can I use clippers on wet hair?

A: It's best to cut hair when it's dry to ensure an even cut. Wet hair can be more difficult to cut evenly.

Q: Can I use the same guard size for the entire head?

A: It's best to use a variety of guard sizes to achieve a more natural look. Using the same guard size can result in a choppy or uneven cut.

Hair Care After the Cut

After the cut, it's important to take care of the hair to maintain the style. Use a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair healthy and hydrated. Visit a stylist every 4-6 weeks for a touch-up and to keep the style looking fresh.

Cutting a woman's short hair with clippers can seem intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it's a breeze! Follow these steps for a successful cut every time. Happy cutting!
