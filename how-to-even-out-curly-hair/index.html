How To Even Out Curly Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Bardi Jun 24, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Curly hair can be a blessing or a curse, depending on how you manage it. Some people love their curls, while others struggle to keep them under control. If you're one of the latter, you might be wondering how to even out your curly hair. The good news is that there are several ways to do this, from haircuts to styling products. In this blog post, we'll show you how to even out curly hair and achieve a more uniform look.

  • Haircuts for Even Curly Hair
  • Styling Products for Even Curly Hair
  • Tips for Maintaining Even Curly Hair

How to Even Out Curly Hair

The first step in even out your curly hair is to determine whether your curls are uneven due to your hair's natural texture or because of damage or breakage. If it's the former, you can try a few different haircuts or styling techniques to even out your curls. If it's the latter, you may need to address the underlying issue before you can start working on evening out your curls.

Haircuts for Even Curly Hair

If you're looking to even out your curly hair, one of the easiest ways to do so is with a haircut. Here are a few haircuts that can help:

  • Layered cut: This cut can help to even out curls by removing weight from the bottom of your hair and adding volume to the top. This can help to create a more uniform look.
  • Long bob: A long bob can help to even out curls by creating a more structured shape. This cut can also help to add volume to the top of your hair.
  • Short bob: A short bob can help to even out curls by creating a more defined shape. This cut can also help to reduce frizz and create a more polished look.

Styling Products for Even Curly Hair

In addition to haircuts, styling products can also help to even out your curly hair. Here are a few products to consider:

  • Curl defining cream: A curl defining cream can help to define your curls and create a more uniform look. Look for a cream that is lightweight and doesn't weigh down your hair.
  • Gel: A gel can help to hold your curls in place and create a more defined shape. Look for a gel that is alcohol-free and doesn't leave your hair crunchy.
  • Mousse: A mousse can help to add volume and texture to your curls, which can help to even them out. Look for a mousse that is lightweight and doesn't leave your hair sticky.

Tips for Maintaining Even Curly Hair

Once you've evened out your curly hair, it's important to maintain it to keep it looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a wide-toothed comb: A wide-toothed comb can help to detangle your curls without causing breakage.
  • Avoid heat styling: Heat styling can damage your hair and cause your curls to become uneven. Instead, let your hair air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer.
  • Protect your hair: Use a heat protectant spray if you do use heat styling tools, and wear a hat or scarf to protect your hair from the sun and wind.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about even out curly hair:

  • Can I even out my curly hair without cutting it? Yes, you can use styling products and techniques to even out your curls without cutting your hair.
  • How often should I cut my curly hair? It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how quickly it becomes damaged. Generally, you should get a trim every 6-8 weeks.
  • Can I use a straightener to even out my curly hair? While a straightener can help to create a more uniform look, it can also cause damage to your hair. It's best to avoid using a straightener if possible.

Hair Care for Even Curly Hair

In addition to the tips above, there are a few things you can do to care for your even curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo: Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, which can make your curls uneven. Look for a shampoo that is sulfate-free and gentle on your hair.
  • Deep condition once a week: Deep conditioning can help to keep your hair hydrated and healthy, which can help to prevent breakage and uneven curls.
  • Avoid brushing your hair: Brushing your hair can cause breakage and make your curls uneven. Instead, use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair.

With these tips and tricks, you can even out your curly hair and achieve a more uniform look. Remember to be patient and experiment with different haircuts and styling products to find what works best for you.
