How To Fix Completely Damaged Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia May 31, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you dealing with hair that's completely damaged? This can be a frustrating and discouraging experience, but don't worry – there are steps you can take to help repair your hair and get it back to a healthy state. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to fix completely damaged hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Fix Completely Damaged Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Repairing Damaged Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care to Prevent Further Damage

Description

Completely damaged hair is hair that's been subjected to a lot of chemical treatments, heat styling, and other forms of damage. This can result in hair that's dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. If you have completely damaged hair, it's important to take steps to repair it as soon as possible to prevent further damage and promote healthy hair growth.

How to Fix Completely Damaged Hair

There are several steps you can take to fix completely damaged hair, including:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Cut off the damaged ends: If your hair is severely damaged, the best thing you can do is to cut off the damaged ends. This will help to prevent further damage and give your hair a fresh start.
  2. Use a protein treatment: Protein treatments can help to strengthen and repair damaged hair. Look for a product that contains hydrolyzed protein, which can penetrate the hair shaft and help to repair damage from the inside out.
  3. Moisturize your hair: Moisture is key when it comes to repairing damaged hair. Look for a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to help lock in moisture.
  4. Avoid heat styling: Heat styling can further damage already-damaged hair. Try to avoid using heat tools like flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers, or use them on the lowest heat setting.
  5. Avoid chemical treatments: Chemical treatments like hair dye, perms, and relaxers can also damage already-damaged hair. Avoid these treatments until your hair is healthy again.
  6. Protect your hair: Wear a hat or scarf when you're out in the sun to protect your hair from UV damage. Use a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent breakage while you sleep.

Tips for Repairing Damaged Hair

Here are some additional tips to help repair damaged hair:

  • Deep condition your hair once a week to help restore moisture and strength.
  • Avoid brushing wet hair, which is more prone to breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle.
  • Use a hair mask or hot oil treatment once a month to help nourish and repair your hair.
  • Take a break from tight hairstyles like braids and weaves, which can cause tension and breakage.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about fixing completely damaged hair:

  • Can damaged hair be repaired? Yes, damaged hair can be repaired with the right treatments and care.
  • How long does it take to fix completely damaged hair? The amount of time it takes to repair completely damaged hair depends on the severity of the damage and how well you care for your hair. It can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months to see significant improvement.
  • Is it possible to prevent hair damage? Yes, you can prevent hair damage by avoiding over-processing your hair, using heat tools sparingly, and protecting your hair from the sun and other environmental stressors.

Hair Care to Prevent Further Damage

Once you've repaired your damaged hair, it's important to continue taking good care of it to prevent further damage. Here are some tips:

  • Use a heat protectant spray before using heat tools.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, which can strip it of natural oils.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals that promote healthy hair growth.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.

By following these steps and taking good care of your hair, you can repair completely damaged hair and promote healthy hair growth.
