How To Cut Toddler Girl Hair With Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby May 30, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you a parent looking to save money on haircuts for your toddler girl? Or are you simply looking to take matters into your own hands when it comes to trimming her hair? Whatever the reason may be, cutting your toddler girl's hair with scissors can be a daunting task. But with the right tools and technique, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut from the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we'll take you through the steps on how to cut toddler girl hair with scissors.

Description

Cutting your toddler girl's hair with scissors can save you money and allow you to have control over her haircut. It can also be a fun bonding experience for you and your child. However, it's important to use the proper technique and tools to ensure a safe and successful haircut.

How To

Before getting started, gather the necessary tools: a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, a spray bottle, and hair clips. It's also helpful to have a mirror set up so you can see the back of your child's head.

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair

It's best to cut your toddler girl's hair when it's clean and dry. This will give you a better idea of the true length and texture of her hair. Use the spray bottle to lightly mist her hair if it's too dry.

Step 2: Section the hair

Use the comb to section off the hair. Start by parting down the middle of her head, then create a horizontal part at the back of her head. Clip the top section out of the way.

Step 3: Start cutting

Starting at the nape of her neck, comb out a small section of hair. Hold the hair between your fingers and use the scissors to cut straight across. Be sure to only cut a small amount at a time to avoid cutting too much off.

Step 4: Work your way up

Continue cutting small sections of hair, working your way up to the top section of her hair. Be sure to keep the sections even and consistent in length.

Step 5: Cut the top section

Unclip the top section of her hair and comb it straight up. Use your fingers to hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and cut straight across. This will create layers and add texture to her hair.

Step 6: Trim the bangs

If your toddler girl has bangs, trim them last. Comb the bangs straight down and cut them in a straight line, being careful not to cut them too short. You can always trim more later if needed.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting your toddler girl's hair with scissors:

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much off.
  • Take breaks if your child becomes fussy or restless.
  • Use hair clips to keep hair out of the way while you work.
  • Keep a steady hand and take your time.

FAQ

Here are a few frequently asked questions when it comes to cutting toddler girl hair with scissors:

Is it safe to cut my toddler girl's hair with scissors?

Yes, it's safe as long as you use proper technique and tools. Be sure to keep the scissors away from your child's face and ears.

How often should I cut my toddler girl's hair?

It depends on how fast her hair grows and how quickly it becomes unruly. Generally, it's recommended to trim hair every 6-8 weeks.

What if I make a mistake?

If you accidentally cut too much off, don't worry. Hair grows back and it's better to err on the side of caution. You can always take your child to a professional for a fix if needed.

Hair Care

After cutting your toddler girl's hair with scissors, be sure to give her a gentle shampoo and conditioning treatment. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle her hair and avoid using heat tools on her hair until it's fully grown out. Regular trims every 6-8 weeks will help keep her hair healthy and looking its best.
