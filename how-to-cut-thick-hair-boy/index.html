How To Cut Thick Hair Boy Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Sep 11, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Cutting a boy's hair can be a daunting task, especially if he has thick hair. However, with the right tools, technique and a little bit of patience, you can create a stylish and manageable haircut for your little one. In this article, we will provide a step-by-step guide on how to cut thick hair boy with ease.

Description

Thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it can be voluminous and full of texture, it can also be difficult to manage and style. That's why it's important to have a good haircut that will make your boy's hair more manageable and stylish. In this article, we'll show you how to cut thick hair boy in a way that will make it easier to style and maintain.

How to Cut Thick Hair Boy

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your boy's hair, you will need to gather the necessary tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, hair clippers, a comb, a spray bottle filled with water, and a cape or towel to protect your child's clothing.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Hair

Wash your boy's hair thoroughly and towel-dry it. Make sure it's not dripping wet, as this can make it difficult to cut. Comb the hair to remove any tangles.

Step 3: Determine the Length of the Haircut

Decide on the length of the haircut you want to give your boy. If you're not sure, start with a longer length and work your way down. Remember that you can always take more off, but you can't put it back on.

Step 4: Cut the Hair with Scissors

Use the scissors to cut the hair to the desired length. Start at the back of the head and work your way forward. Take small sections of hair and cut them at an angle, rather than straight across. This will create a more natural look.

Step 5: Blend the Hair with Clippers

Once you've cut the hair with scissors, use the clippers to blend the hair. Start at the bottom and work your way up, using different guard lengths to create a gradual fade. Make sure to blend the hair around the ears and neckline.

Step 6: Trim the Bangs

If your boy has bangs, trim them with scissors. Comb them straight down and cut them to the desired length. Make sure to cut them at an angle, rather than straight across.

Step 7: Check for Unevenness

Check the haircut for any unevenness. Use the scissors and clippers to make any necessary adjustments.

Step 8: Style the Hair

Once the haircut is complete, style the hair as desired. Use a little bit of hair product to create texture and hold.

Tips

  • Take breaks if your child becomes restless or uncomfortable.
  • Keep the hair damp as you cut to make it easier to manage.
  • Don't rush the haircut. Take your time and be patient.
  • Use a mirror to check the haircut from all angles.

FAQ

How often should I cut my boy's hair?

It depends on how quickly his hair grows and how long you want it to be. As a general rule, it's recommended to get a haircut every 4-6 weeks to maintain the style.

Can I cut my boy's hair myself?

Yes, you can! Just make sure to have the right tools and follow the steps carefully. It may take some practice, but you can save money and create a stylish haircut for your child.

What if I make a mistake?

Don't panic! Mistakes can happen, especially if you're new to cutting hair. Take a step back and assess the situation. You may be able to fix the mistake with a little bit of trimming. If not, you can always take your child to a professional stylist to fix the haircut.

Hair Care

After the haircut, it's important to take care of your boy's hair. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep it clean and healthy. Avoid using too much heat on the hair, as this can damage it. Encourage your child to brush and comb his hair regularly to prevent tangles and maintain the style.

Cutting thick hair boy is not as difficult as it may seem. With the right tools, technique, and a little bit of practice, you can create a stylish and manageable haircut for your little one. Follow the steps in this guide and don't forget to take care of your boy's hair after the haircut. Happy styling!
