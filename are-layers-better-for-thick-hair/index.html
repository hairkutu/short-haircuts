Are Layers Better For Thick Hair

Description

Thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it looks voluminous and full, it can also be difficult to manage and style. One solution that many people turn to is getting layers. But are layers better for thick hair?

The answer is yes! Layers can help to add movement and texture to thick hair, making it easier to style. They can also help to remove some of the weight from the hair, making it feel lighter and more manageable.

However, it's important to get the right kind of layers for your hair type and face shape. Keep reading to learn how to get layers in thick hair, step-by-step instructions, tips, FAQs, and hair care tips for layered thick hair.

How to Get Layers in Thick Hair

The best way to get layers in thick hair is to go to a professional stylist. They will be able to assess your hair type, face shape, and style preferences to create a customized layering plan. However, if you prefer to do it yourself, you can follow these steps:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

You will need a set of sharp hair cutting shears, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start with clean, dry hair. It's easier to see the natural texture and movement of your hair when it's not wet or weighed down by products.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Use the comb to create sections of your hair. You can section it off into four parts: top, sides, and back.

Step 4: Cut the Layers

Starting with the top section, take a horizontal subsection of hair and hold it straight up. Use the hair cutting shears to cut a small amount of hair at a time, working your way down the section. Repeat this process for each section of hair.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Use the comb to blend the layers together. This will help to create a seamless, natural look.

Tips for Layering Thick Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when layering thick hair:

  • Choose the right length of layers for your face shape.
  • Ask your stylist to use thinning shears to remove some of the weight from your hair.
  • Use a volumizing mousse or spray to add even more movement and texture to your hair.
  • Consider getting long layers if you have very thick hair. This will help to remove some of the bulk and make it easier to manage.

FAQs about Layering Thick Hair

Q: Will layers make my hair look thinner?

A: No, layers can actually make your hair look thicker and fuller by adding movement and texture.

Q: Can I still wear my hair straight with layers?

A: Yes, you can still wear your hair straight with layers. In fact, layers can help to make your hair look smoother and more polished.

Q: How often should I get my layers trimmed?

A: It's recommended to get your layers trimmed every 6-8 weeks to maintain their shape and prevent split ends.

Hair Care Tips for Layered Thick Hair

Here are some hair care tips to keep your layered thick hair looking its best:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Consider using a heat protectant spray when using hot tools like curling irons or flat irons.
  • Get regular trims to keep your layers looking fresh and healthy.

Overall, layers can be a great way to add movement and texture to thick hair. Just be sure to consult with a professional stylist and follow these tips to keep your hair looking its best.
