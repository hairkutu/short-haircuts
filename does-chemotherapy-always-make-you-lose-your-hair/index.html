Does Chemotherapy Always Make You Lose Your Hair

Written by Oliver May 14, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Chemotherapy is a type of cancer treatment that involves the use of strong drugs to kill cancer cells. While chemotherapy is effective in fighting cancer, it can also have side effects such as hair loss. In this article, we will answer the question, does chemotherapy always make you lose your hair? We will also provide tips on how to care for your hair during chemotherapy treatment.

Table of Contents

  • Does Chemotherapy Always Make You Lose Your Hair?
  • How Chemotherapy Affects Hair Loss
  • Factors Affecting Hair Loss
  • Tips for Hair Care During Chemotherapy Treatment
  • Frequently Asked Questions

How Chemotherapy Affects Hair Loss

Chemotherapy drugs target rapidly dividing cells, including cancer cells. However, hair follicle cells also divide rapidly, which is why chemotherapy can cause hair loss. Chemotherapy drugs can damage hair follicle cells, leading to hair loss. The amount of hair loss varies from person to person and depends on several factors.

Factors Affecting Hair Loss

Several factors can affect the amount of hair loss during chemotherapy treatment:

  • The type and dose of chemotherapy drugs
  • Your age and overall health
  • Your hair type and texture
  • The area of the body being treated

Some chemotherapy drugs cause more hair loss than others. For example, drugs such as doxorubicin and cyclophosphamide are more likely to cause hair loss than drugs such as paclitaxel and docetaxel. Younger people and those in good health tend to have less severe hair loss than older people or those with health problems. Fine hair and curly hair are less likely to fall out than thick, straight hair. Hair loss is most common on the scalp, but it can also occur on other parts of the body such as the eyebrows, eyelashes, and pubic hair.

Tips for Hair Care During Chemotherapy Treatment

While hair loss during chemotherapy treatment is common, there are some things you can do to care for your hair:

  • Gently wash your hair with a mild shampoo
  • Avoid using hair dryers, curling irons, and other heated styling tools
  • Use a soft-bristled brush or wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair
  • Avoid tight hairstyles such as braids, ponytails, and buns
  • Wear a hat or scarf to protect your scalp from the sun and cold
  • Consider using a wig or hairpiece

It's important to remember that your hair may grow back differently after chemotherapy treatment. It may be a different color or texture, or it may grow back thinner or thicker than before. Be patient and gentle with your hair as it grows back.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Does chemotherapy always make you lose your hair?

A: No, chemotherapy does not always cause hair loss. The amount of hair loss varies from person to person and depends on several factors.

Q: Will my hair grow back after chemotherapy treatment?

A: Yes, your hair will grow back after chemotherapy treatment. However, it may take several months for your hair to grow back fully.

Q: Can I color or perm my hair during chemotherapy treatment?

A: It is not recommended to color or perm your hair during chemotherapy treatment because the chemicals can irritate your scalp and damage your hair. Wait until after your treatment is complete to color or perm your hair.

Q: Is there anything I can do to prevent hair loss during chemotherapy treatment?

A: There is no guaranteed way to prevent hair loss during chemotherapy treatment. However, some studies suggest that scalp cooling may help reduce hair loss. Talk to your doctor to see if scalp cooling is an option for you.

In conclusion, chemotherapy does not always make you lose your hair. The amount of hair loss varies from person to person and depends on several factors. If you are undergoing chemotherapy treatment, it's important to care for your hair gently and consider using a wig or hairpiece. Remember that your hair may grow back differently after treatment, but with patience and care, you can have healthy hair again.
