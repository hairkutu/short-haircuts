How To Cut A Bob Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Mar 25, 2023 · 8 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking bob right at home. In this guide, we’ll show you how to cut a bob yourself step-by-step, including tips and hair care advice to make your new cut last.

How to Cut a Bob Yourself

A bob is a classic and versatile haircut that can be tailored to suit any face shape or hair type. Whether you want a sleek and straight bob or a tousled and textured look, cutting your own bob at home is easier than you might think.

What You Will Need

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • A comb
  • A hair clip or elastic band
  • A handheld mirror or a stationary mirror that allows you to see the back of your head

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these simple steps to cut your own bob at home:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Comb out any tangles and part your hair down the middle.
  2. Take a section of hair from the back of your head, starting at the nape of your neck. Use your fingers to measure the length you want your bob to be, and snip off the excess hair with your scissors.
  3. Continue taking sections of hair and cutting them to the length you want, making sure to keep the hair as straight and even as possible. Work your way up to the crown of your head.
  4. Once you reach the crown, divide your hair into two sections and bring them forward. Cut the hair in front of your ears to the desired length, making sure to keep the hair even on both sides.
  5. Release the hair in the back and check for any uneven sections. If necessary, trim any stray hairs until the length is even.
  6. For a textured look, use the point-cutting technique. Hold your scissors vertically and snip into the ends of your hair to create a choppy, layered effect.
  7. Blend any choppy or uneven sections by holding your scissors at a 45-degree angle and snipping into the hair to create soft, feathered layers.
  8. Once you’re happy with the overall length and shape of your bob, use your comb to check for any stray hairs or uneven sections. Trim as needed.
  9. Style your new bob as desired using a blow dryer, flat iron, or curling iron.
  10. Admire your handiwork and enjoy your new, fresh haircut!

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

Here are some additional tips to ensure a successful DIY bob:

  • Start with a longer length and trim gradually to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Use sharp, high-quality hair cutting scissors to avoid damaging your hair or creating jagged edges.
  • Don’t cut your hair when it’s wet, as wet hair can appear longer and you may end up cutting more than you intended.
  • Take breaks and step back from the mirror to avoid over-cutting or obsessing over small details.
  • Ask a friend or family member to help if you’re having trouble reaching certain areas or checking for uneven sections.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions and answers about cutting a bob yourself:

Can I cut a bob myself if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut a bob yourself if you have curly hair. However, keep in mind that curly hair can shrink when it dries, so it’s best to err on the side of caution and start with a longer length than you think you want.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don’t panic. You can always trim a little more to even out any uneven sections. If you’re really unhappy with the cut, you can always go to a professional stylist for correction.

How often should I trim my bob?

It’s recommended to trim your bob every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and keep split ends at bay.

Hair Care for Your New Bob

To keep your new bob looking its best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner that’s suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair and cause split ends.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and shiny.
  • Avoid using tight hair elastics or clips that can cause breakage or damage to your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain the shape and prevent split ends.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently cut a bob yourself at home and enjoy a fresh and stylish new look.
