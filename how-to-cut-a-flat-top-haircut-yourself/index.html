How To Cut A Flat Top Haircut Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Description

The flat top haircut is a classic style that has been around for decades. It is a versatile haircut that can be worn by men of all ages and hair types. The flat top haircut consists of a short, cropped top that is cut flat and square. The sides and back are usually tapered or faded.

If you are looking for a new haircut and want to save some money, cutting a flat top haircut yourself is a great option. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking flat top haircut right at home.

How to Cut a Flat Top Haircut Yourself

Cutting a flat top haircut yourself may seem daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it is actually quite simple. Here is what you will need:

  • Clippers
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Mirror
  • Hair spray

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut a flat top haircut yourself:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Use the clippers to trim the sides and back of your head. Use a #2 or #3 guard to create a tapered or faded look.

  2. Use the scissors to cut the top of your hair. Cut the hair in a square shape, making sure it is flat on top. Use a comb to keep the hair in place and cut it straight across.

  3. Blend the top and sides of your hair. Use the clippers to blend the hair where the top and sides meet. Use a #1 or #2 guard to create a smooth transition between the two areas.

  4. Trim the front of your hair. Use the scissors to trim the hair in the front of your hairline. Cut the hair straight across, making sure it is even.

  5. Use the clippers to create a line around the edges of your hair. Use a #1 or #2 guard to create a clean, sharp line around the edges of your hair.

  6. Check for any uneven spots or missed areas. Use the scissors or clippers to touch up any areas that need it.

  7. Apply hair spray to keep your hair in place.

Tips for a Perfect Flat Top Haircut

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect flat top haircut:

  • Use the right tools. Invest in a good pair of clippers and scissors to ensure a professional-looking haircut.

  • Choose the right guard size. Use a #2 or #3 guard to create a tapered or faded look on the sides and back of your head. Use a #1 or #2 guard to blend the top and sides of your hair.

  • Take your time. Cutting a flat top haircut yourself requires patience and attention to detail. Don't rush the process.

  • Use a mirror. Make sure you have a mirror in front of you and behind you to ensure an even haircut.

  • Practice makes perfect. The more you practice cutting your own hair, the better you will become.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting a flat top haircut yourself:

  • Can I cut a flat top haircut on curly hair?

    • Yes, you can cut a flat top haircut on curly hair. However, it may be more difficult to achieve a perfectly flat top. Consider consulting with a professional stylist if you have curly hair.


  • What if I make a mistake?

    • If you make a mistake, don't panic. Use the scissors or clippers to touch up the area. If the mistake is severe, consider consulting with a professional stylist to fix it.


  • How often should I cut my hair?

    • It is recommended to cut your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape of the flat top haircut.

Hair Care After the Cut

After cutting your flat top haircut, it is important to take care of your hair to maintain its shape and style. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.

  • Avoid heat styling tools, such as blow dryers and straighteners, that can damage your hair and cause it to lose its shape.

  • Use a hair spray or gel to keep your hair in place.

  • Visit a professional stylist every few months to maintain the shape of your flat top haircut.

Cutting a flat top haircut yourself is a great way to save money and achieve a professional-looking haircut. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a perfect flat top haircut right at home. Remember to take your time, use the right guard size, and practice regularly to improve your skills.
