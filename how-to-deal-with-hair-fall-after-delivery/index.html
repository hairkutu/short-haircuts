Dealing with hair fall after delivery can be a daunting task for new moms. The hormonal changes that occur during pregnancy and after delivery can cause hair to fall out in large clumps. You may also experience thinning hair, bald patches, and slower hair growth. However, there are several ways to deal with hair fall after delivery and promote healthy hair growth. How to Deal with Hair Fall After Delivery

The first step to dealing with hair fall after delivery is to understand the underlying causes. During pregnancy, your hormones change, and your body produces more estrogen, which can cause your hair to grow thicker and faster. After delivery, your hormone levels return to normal, and the excess hair that grew during pregnancy can fall out. In addition, stress, lack of sleep, and poor nutrition can also contribute to hair loss.

To manage hair fall after delivery, you need to take a multi-pronged approach. This includes:

  • Improving your diet
  • Taking supplements
  • Managing your stress levels
  • Taking care of your scalp and hair

Step-by-Step Guide

Hereâs a step-by-step guide to dealing with hair fall after delivery:

  1. Eat a healthy diet rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals. This includes foods like eggs, fish, nuts, leafy greens, and fruits.
  2. Take supplements like biotin, folic acid, and iron to promote healthy hair growth.
  3. Manage your stress levels by practicing relaxation techniques like yoga, meditation, and deep breathing.
  4. Take care of your scalp and hair by using gentle, sulfate-free shampoos, avoiding heat styling tools, and getting regular trims.

Tips for Managing Hair Fall After Delivery

Here are some additional tips for managing hair fall after delivery:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles like braids, buns, and ponytails that can pull on your hair and cause breakage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or brush to detangle your hair gently.
  • Avoid using hot water on your hair, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause damage.
  • Massage your scalp gently with your fingertips to increase blood flow and promote hair growth.
  • Consider using hair growth products like serums and oils that contain ingredients like castor oil, argan oil, and rosemary oil.

FAQs

Q: How long does hair fall last after delivery?

A: Hair fall after delivery can last up to six months, but it varies from person to person.

Q: Will my hair grow back after postpartum hair loss?

A: Yes, your hair will grow back after postpartum hair loss, but it may take several months for your hair to return to its pre-pregnancy thickness.

Q: Can breastfeeding cause hair loss?

A: Yes, breastfeeding can cause hair loss due to hormonal changes, but it is usually temporary.

Hair Care Tips for New Moms

Here are some additional hair care tips for new moms:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals like hair dyes and relaxers on your hair, especially if youâre breastfeeding.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protective hair product.
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water, which can help promote healthy hair growth.
  • Get plenty of rest and sleep to reduce stress and promote overall health.

By following these tips and tricks, you can manage hair fall after delivery and promote healthy hair growth. Remember to be patient, as it may take several months for your hair to return to its pre-pregnancy thickness. In the meantime, enjoy your new bundle of joy and all the joys and challenges that come with being a new mom!
