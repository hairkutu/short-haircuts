How Many Times Can You Bleach Asian Hair

Written by Daniel Aug 08, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Table of Content: Description, How to, Step by step, Tips, FAQ, Hair Care

Description

Many Asians have naturally dark hair, which makes it difficult to achieve lighter hair shades without bleaching. Bleaching is a chemical process that removes the natural pigment from hair, making it lighter in color. However, over-bleaching can severely damage the hair, leading to breakage and split ends. Therefore, it is important to know how many times you can bleach your Asian hair without causing damage.

How to

To bleach Asian hair, you will need a bleaching kit, which includes bleach powder, developer, gloves, and a mixing bowl. Follow the instructions on the kit carefully, and always do a patch test before applying the bleach to your entire head. Section your hair and apply the bleach evenly, starting from the ends and working your way up to the roots. Leave the bleach on for the recommended time, and then rinse it off with lukewarm water. Follow up with a deep conditioning treatment to restore moisture to your hair.

Step by step

1. Purchase a bleaching kit.

2. Do a patch test.

3. Section your hair and apply the bleach.

4. Leave the bleach on for the recommended time.

5. Rinse off the bleach with lukewarm water.

6. Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair.

Tips

- Use a lower volume developer if you have previously bleached your hair.

- Wait at least two weeks between bleaching sessions.

- Avoid overlapping the bleach on previously bleached hair.

- Use a toner to neutralize any yellow or orange tones in your hair after bleaching.

FAQ

Q: How many times can you bleach Asian hair?

A: It is recommended to bleach your hair no more than twice within a two-week period to avoid damage.

Q: Will bleaching my hair make it lighter?

A: Yes, bleaching removes the natural pigment from your hair, making it lighter in color.

Hair Care

After bleaching your Asian hair, it is important to take care of it properly to prevent damage. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated, and avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible. Apply a hair mask once a week to keep your hair moisturized, and trim the ends regularly to prevent split ends.

Overall, bleaching Asian hair can be done safely if done correctly and in moderation. Be sure to follow the instructions carefully, and always take good care of your hair after the bleaching process.
