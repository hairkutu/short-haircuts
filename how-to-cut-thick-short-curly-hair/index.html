How To Cut Thick Short Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jun 01, 2023 · 8 min read
How to Cut Thick Short Curly Hair | Step by Step Guide

Are you tired of dealing with thick short curly hair that is hard to manage? Do you want to learn how to cut your own hair at home? Look no further! In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting thick short curly hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Thick Short Curly Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Thick Short Curly Hair

Description of Thick Short Curly Hair

Thick short curly hair can be a blessing and a curse. It is voluminous, bouncy, and full of personality, but it can also be difficult to manage and style. Thick short curly hair tends to frizz easily and can be hard to tame.

When it comes to cutting thick short curly hair, it is important to consider the texture and curl pattern. Curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so it is important to cut it while it is wet to avoid cutting too much off.

How to Cut Thick Short Curly Hair

Cutting thick short curly hair can be intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done easily at home. Here are the steps to follow:

Step by Step Guide

  1. Start with freshly washed and conditioned hair. Apply a leave-in conditioner to help detangle and soften the hair.
  2. Section the hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back.
  3. Starting at the back of the head, take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Use a pair of sharp scissors to cut the hair at an angle, following the shape of the head. Cut less than you think you need to, as curly hair tends to shrink when it dries.
  4. Continue cutting small sections of hair, working your way around the head. Use your fingers to pull the hair straight down and cut at an angle, following the shape of the head.
  5. Once you have cut all the hair, check for any uneven spots and make small adjustments as needed.
  6. Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry the hair. This will help enhance the natural curl pattern and prevent frizz.
  7. Style as desired.

Tips for Cutting Thick Short Curly Hair

Cutting thick short curly hair requires some special considerations. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Invest in a pair of sharp scissors specifically designed for cutting hair. Dull scissors can damage the hair and cause split ends.
  • Cut less than you think you need to. Curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so it is important to cut it while it is wet.
  • Consider getting a dry cut from a professional stylist who is experienced with cutting curly hair.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to help detangle and soften the hair before cutting.
  • Don't be afraid to make small adjustments after the initial cut.

FAQs

Can I cut my own hair at home?

Yes, you can cut your own hair at home. However, it is important to use the right tools and techniques to ensure a good result.

Do I need to cut my curly hair differently than straight hair?

Yes, curly hair should be cut differently than straight hair. Curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so it is important to cut it while it is wet.

How often should I cut my thick short curly hair?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how quickly it loses its shape. As a general rule, it is recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep the shape and prevent split ends.

Hair Care for Thick Short Curly Hair

Caring for thick short curly hair requires some special considerations to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for curly hair to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing curly hair when it is dry, as this can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle when it is wet.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to help define and enhance the natural curl pattern.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage the hair and cause frizz. If you must use them, use a heat protectant and keep the temperature low.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent friction and breakage.

Now that you know how to cut thick short curly hair, you can confidently tackle your own hair at home. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a beautiful, healthy-looking style that shows off your natural curl pattern.
