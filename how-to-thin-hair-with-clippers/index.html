How To Thin Hair With Clippers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Aug 06, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Thinning hair with clippers is a great way to create a more manageable and stylish hairstyle. It's also an excellent option for those who want to reduce the thickness of their hair without sacrificing length. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to thin hair with clippers. We'll also share some tips and tricks to help you achieve the best results.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Thin Hair with Clippers
  2. Step-by-Step Guide

How to Thin Hair with Clippers

Thinning hair with clippers involves using a comb and a pair of clippers with a thinning blade attachment. The blade has teeth that cut the hair at different lengths, creating a more natural and textured look. The process is best done on dry hair, so make sure your hair is clean and free of any styling products.

What You'll Need

  • Clippers with a thinning blade attachment
  • Comb
  • Clip or hair tie

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to thin your hair with clippers:

  1. Start by sectioning your hair. Use a clip or hair tie to separate the top section of your hair from the bottom section.
  2. Take a small section of hair from the bottom section and comb it straight down.
  3. Hold the section of hair between your fingers and use the clippers to trim the hair from the ends. Hold the clippers vertically and cut into the hair using a scooping motion.
  4. Move the clippers up and down the section of hair, cutting at different lengths to create a textured look. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once.
  5. Repeat this process on all sections of the bottom layer of your hair.
  6. Release the top section of your hair and repeat the process on the top layer.
  7. Blend the two layers together by using the clippers to cut across the hair at a slight angle.
  8. Comb your hair to remove any loose hairs.
  9. Check your hair in a mirror to make sure it's evenly thinned.
  10. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Thinning Hair with Clippers

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when thinning your hair with clippers:

  • Start with a longer blade attachment and work your way down to a shorter one if needed.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. It's better to take small sections and work slowly.
  • Be careful not to cut too close to the scalp, as this can cause irritation and discomfort.
  • Use a comb to help guide the clippers and ensure an even cut.
  • Consider leaving the hair around your face slightly thicker for a more natural look.

FAQs

Can you thin your hair with regular clippers?

Yes, you can thin your hair with regular clippers by using a comb to guide the clippers and create a textured look. However, using clippers with a thinning blade attachment will give you more control and precision.

Can you thin your hair too much?

Yes, you can thin your hair too much, which can lead to a choppy or uneven look. It's better to take small sections and work slowly to achieve a natural and textured result.

How often should you thin your hair?

The frequency of thinning your hair will depend on your individual hair type and how quickly it grows. Generally, it's recommended to thin your hair every 6 to 8 weeks to maintain a healthy and manageable hairstyle.

Hair Care After Thinning

After thinning your hair with clippers, it's important to take extra care to keep your hair healthy and nourished. Here are some tips to help you care for your hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals to support healthy hair growth.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your hairstyle and prevent split ends.

Overall, thinning your hair with clippers is an easy and effective way to create a more manageable and stylish look. By following these steps and tips, you can achieve the best results and keep your hair healthy and nourished.
