Do Your Dreads Get Thicker Over Time

Written by Petter Jul 28, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you considering getting dreadlocks but wondering if they will get thicker over time? Or do you already have dreads and are curious about their growth? Here's everything you need to know about whether or not your dreads get thicker over time.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get Thicker Dreads
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Thick Dreads
  • FAQs about Dread Thickness
  • Hair Care for Healthy Dreads

How to Get Thicker Dreads

Yes, your dreads can get thicker over time, but you can also make them thicker intentionally. Here are some ways to do it:

  • Backcomb your dreads to create more volume and density.
  • Use a crochet hook to tighten and compact the hair in your dreads.
  • Add extensions to your dreads to increase their size and thickness.
  • Use a thicker gauge of hair when creating your dreads.

It's important to note that thicker dreads require more maintenance and can be heavier on your scalp. Be sure to take care of your scalp and hair to avoid any discomfort or damage.

Step by Step Guide

If you want to make your dreads thicker, here's a step by step guide:

  1. Wash your hair thoroughly to remove any product buildup or dirt.
  2. Backcomb your dreads using a comb or brush. Start at the tips of your dreads and work your way up to the roots.
  3. Use a crochet hook to tighten and smooth out the hair in your dreads.
  4. If you want to add extensions, attach them to the base of your dreads using a crochet hook or braiding method.
  5. Use a hair dryer or air dry your dreads until they are completely dry.

Repeat this process as necessary to achieve your desired thickness.

Tips for Maintaining Thick Dreads

To keep your dreads thick and healthy, follow these tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly to prevent buildup and maintain scalp health.
  • Avoid using heavy products or oils that can weigh down your dreads.
  • Use a residue-free shampoo to avoid buildup and maintain hair health.
  • Avoid using tight hair ties or headbands that can cause tension and breakage in your dreads.

FAQs about Dread Thickness

Do all dreads get thicker over time?

Yes, most dreads will naturally get thicker over time as the hair locks and compresses. However, the rate at which they thicken can vary depending on factors such as hair type, maintenance, and styling.

Can you thin out thick dreads?

Yes, you can thin out thick dreads by cutting or combing out some of the hair. However, this should only be done by a professional to avoid damaging your dreads or scalp.

Will adding extensions damage my natural hair?

If done properly, adding extensions to your dreads should not damage your natural hair. However, it's important to use high-quality extensions and have them attached by a professional to avoid any potential damage.

Hair Care for Healthy Dreads

Regardless of whether you want thick or thin dreads, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and appearance. Here are some tips for healthy dread care:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Use a dread-friendly conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heavy products or oils that can weigh down your dreads.
  • Protect your dreads from sun damage by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your dreads to prevent damage.

With proper care and maintenance, your dreads can be a beautiful and unique expression of your personal style. Whether you want thick or thin dreads, embrace your natural hair and enjoy the journey.
