How To Do Medium Length Hair Updos A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby May 26, 2023 Â· 10 min read

In this article, we'll be discussing how to do medium length hair updos. Whether you're going to a formal event or just want to change up your look for the day, medium length hair updos are a great option. We'll cover everything from the basics to more advanced techniques, so even if you're new to styling your hair, you'll be able to follow along. So let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • The Basics
  • Braids
  • Buns
  • Twists
  • Accessories
  • Tips for Success
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care

The Basics

The first thing to consider when doing a medium length hair updo is your hair type. If you have fine hair, you'll want to use products that add texture and volume. If you have thick hair, you may need to thin it out a bit to make the updo more manageable. Once you've got your hair prepped and ready to go, it's time to start styling!

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you start styling, make sure you have all the tools you need. You'll need a comb, hair ties, bobby pins, and any other accessories you want to use. It's also a good idea to have some hairspray on hand to keep your updo in place.

Step 2: Choose Your Updo

There are endless options when it comes to medium length hair updos, so it's important to choose one that works for you. Some popular choices include braids, buns, and twists. We'll cover each of these in more detail below.

Step 3: Prep Your Hair

Before you start styling, you'll want to prep your hair. This can vary depending on your hair type, but generally, you'll want to start with clean, dry hair. If you have fine hair, you may want to add some texture with a sea salt spray or texturizing powder. If you have thick hair, you may need to blow dry and straighten it to make it more manageable.

Braids

Braids are a great option for medium length hair updos. They're easy to do and can be dressed up or down depending on the occasion. Here's how to create a simple braided updo:

Step 1: Part Your Hair

Start by parting your hair down the middle. Then, take a small section of hair from the front on one side and braid it all the way down. Repeat on the other side.

Step 2: Pull the Braids Back

Take the two braids and pull them back, securing them with a hair tie. Make sure they're positioned where you want your updo to sit.

Step 3: Create the Updo

Take the loose ends of the braids and twist them together, creating a bun at the back of your head. Secure with bobby pins and hairspray to keep it in place.

Buns

Buns are another popular choice for medium length hair updos. They're easy to do and can be styled in a variety of ways. Here's one option:

Step 1: Pull Your Hair Back

Start by pulling your hair back into a low ponytail. Make sure it's positioned where you want your updo to sit.

Step 2: Create the Bun

Take the ponytail and twist it around itself, creating a bun. Secure with bobby pins and hairspray to keep it in place.

Twists

Twists are a great way to add some texture and dimension to your medium length hair updo. Here's how to do a simple twist updo:

Step 1: Part Your Hair

Start by parting your hair down the middle. Then, take a small section of hair from the front on one side and twist it all the way down. Repeat on the other side.

Step 2: Pull the Twists Back

Take the two twists and pull them back, securing them with a hair tie. Make sure they're positioned where you want your updo to sit.

Step 3: Create the Updo

Take the loose ends of the twists and tuck them under, creating a twisted bun at the back of your head. Secure with bobby pins and hairspray to keep it in place.

Accessories

Accessories can take your medium length hair updo to the next level. Here are a few ideas:

  • Headbands: Add a cute headband to your updo for some extra style.
  • Barrettes: Use a decorative barrette to hold your updo in place.
  • Bobby pins: Use bobby pins that match your hair color to keep your updo secure.

Tips for Success

Here are a few tips to help you achieve the perfect medium length hair updo:

  • Practice makes perfect! Don't be afraid to try different techniques and styles until you find one that works for you.
  • Use the right products for your hair type. Fine hair may need texturizing products, while thick hair may need to be thinned out a bit.
  • Don't be afraid to use accessories to enhance your updo.

FAQs

Can I do a medium length hair updo if my hair is curly?

Absolutely! In fact, curls can add some extra texture and dimension to your updo. Just make sure to use products that enhance your curls and keep them frizz-free.

How long does it take to do a medium length hair updo?

It depends on the style you choose and your level of experience. Simple updos like braids and buns can be done in just a few minutes, while more complex styles may take longer.

Hair Care

Finally, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a heat protectant spray when using hot tools like straighteners and curling irons.
  • Don't wash your hair every day, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.

That's it for our guide on how to do medium length hair updos. We hope you found it helpful and are inspired to try out some new styles!
