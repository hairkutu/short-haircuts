Does Hair Fall Out With Age

Written by Christine Sep 01, 2023 · 6 min read

As we age, many changes occur in our bodies - including changes to our hair. One common question people ask is, "Does hair fall out with age?" The answer is yes, but it's important to understand why and what you can do to prevent excessive hair loss.

Why Does Hair Fall Out with Age?

There are several reasons why hair may fall out as we age:

1. Hormonal Changes

As we age, our hormone levels change. This can lead to hair becoming thinner and more brittle. In particular, women may experience hair loss during and after menopause due to changes in estrogen levels.

2. Genetics

Genetics can play a role in hair loss. If your family has a history of baldness or thinning hair, you may be more likely to experience it as well.

3. Nutritional Deficiencies

If you're not getting enough nutrients, your hair may suffer. Nutritional deficiencies such as iron, zinc, and vitamin D can all contribute to hair loss.

4. Stress

Stress can cause hair loss in a few different ways. First, it can disrupt the normal hair growth cycle. Second, it can cause inflammation in the scalp, which can damage hair follicles.

How to Prevent Hair Loss with Age

While you can't completely prevent hair loss, there are several things you can do to minimize it:

1. Eat a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals can help keep your hair healthy. Make sure you're eating plenty of fruits, vegetables, and protein.

2. Manage Stress

Find ways to manage stress, such as meditation, exercise, or therapy. This can help reduce the risk of hair loss.

3. Use Gentle Hair Products

Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach or perms. Stick to gentle shampoos and conditioners that won't damage your hair.

4. See a Doctor

If you're experiencing excessive hair loss, it's a good idea to see a doctor. They can help determine the underlying cause and suggest treatments to help prevent further hair loss.

FAQ

1. Is hair loss a normal part of aging?

Yes, hair loss is a normal part of aging. However, excessive hair loss may be a sign of an underlying issue.

2. Can hair loss be reversed?

It depends on the underlying cause of the hair loss. In some cases, hair loss can be reversed with treatment.

3. Can stress cause hair loss?

Yes, stress can cause hair loss. Managing stress can help reduce the risk of hair loss.

Hair Care Tips

Here are a few additional tips to help keep your hair healthy:

1. Don't Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too frequently can strip it of its natural oils. Try to wash your hair every other day or every few days.

2. Use a Wide-Toothed Comb

Avoid using a brush on wet hair, as it can cause breakage. Instead, use a wide-toothed comb to gently detangle your hair.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools can damage your hair. Try to avoid using them or use them sparingly.


Overall, hair loss is a normal part of aging, but there are things you can do to minimize it. By eating a healthy diet, managing stress, and using gentle hair products, you can help keep your hair healthy and strong.
