What Is The Best Grade Of Human Hair Extensions

Written by Frank Aug 08, 2023 Â· 10 min read

When it comes to hair extensions, many people wonder what the best grade of human hair extensions is. There are a lot of options out there, and it can be overwhelming trying to figure out which one is right for you. In this article, we'll go over the different grades of human hair extensions, and help you determine which one is best for you.

  • How to Choose the Best Grade of Human Hair Extensions

What is the Best Grade of Human Hair Extensions?

The best grade of human hair extensions is typically considered to be Remy hair. Remy hair is made from 100% human hair, and is collected in a way that ensures all the hairs are aligned in the same direction. This helps to prevent tangling and shedding, and gives the hair a more natural look and feel.

There are different grades of Remy hair, which are based on the thickness and quality of the hair. The highest grade is typically considered to be 10A or 11A, which is the thickest and highest quality hair available. This type of hair is often used for professional extensions and wigs, and is designed to last for a long time.

Other Grades of Human Hair Extensions

Aside from Remy hair, there are other grades of human hair extensions available, including:

  • Non-Remy hair: This type of hair is collected from multiple sources, and the hair cuticles may not all be facing the same direction. This can lead to tangling and shedding, and the hair may not last as long as Remy hair.
  • Virgin hair: This is hair that has not been chemically processed, and is often considered to be the highest quality hair available. However, it can be difficult to find true virgin hair, and it is often more expensive than other types of human hair extensions.
  • Processed hair: This type of hair has been chemically treated to achieve a certain color or texture. While it can be less expensive than other types of human hair extensions, it may not last as long and may not look as natural.

How to Choose the Best Grade of Human Hair Extensions

When choosing the best grade of human hair extensions, there are several factors to consider:

  • Budget: Higher quality hair is typically more expensive, so you'll need to consider your budget when choosing the grade of hair you want.
  • Texture: Different grades of hair will have different textures, so you'll want to choose a grade that matches your natural hair texture as closely as possible.
  • Length: Some grades of hair may not be available in longer lengths, so if you have long hair, you'll want to choose a grade that is available in the length you want.
  • Color: If you plan to dye your extensions, you'll want to choose a grade of hair that can be easily dyed.

Step-by-Step Guide to Install Human Hair Extensions

Installing human hair extensions can be a bit tricky, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done at home. Here's a step-by-step guide to installing human hair extensions:

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair, and brushing it out to remove any tangles.
  2. Separate your hair into sections, and use clips to hold each section in place.
  3. Measure the extensions against your head to determine where each weft should be placed. Use a comb to create a part where the weft will be placed.
  4. Apply a small amount of hair glue to the weft, and attach it to the part in your hair. Press the weft firmly in place, and hold it for a few seconds to ensure it sticks.
  5. Repeat this process until all the wefts are in place.
  6. Blend your natural hair with the extensions using a brush or comb.

Tips for Caring for Your Human Hair Extensions

Proper care is essential for keeping your human hair extensions looking their best. Here are some tips for caring for your extensions:

  • Wash your extensions gently with a sulfate-free shampoo, and avoid using hot water or a hair dryer.
  • Brush your extensions daily to prevent tangling.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your extensions, or use a heat protectant spray if you must.
  • Store your extensions in a cool, dry place when not in use.

FAQs about Human Hair Extensions

What is the difference between Remy hair and non-Remy hair?

Remy hair is collected in a way that ensures all the hairs are aligned in the same direction, which helps to prevent tangling and shedding. Non-Remy hair is collected from multiple sources, and the hair cuticles may not all be facing the same direction. This can lead to tangling and shedding, and the hair may not last as long as Remy hair.

How long do human hair extensions last?

The lifespan of human hair extensions depends on the quality of the hair, how well they are cared for, and how often they are worn. Higher quality hair can last up to a year or more with proper care, while lower quality hair may only last a few months.

Can I dye my human hair extensions?

Yes, you can dye human hair extensions, but it's important to choose a grade of hair that can be easily dyed. It's also a good idea to have a professional stylist dye your extensions to ensure the best results.

Hair Care Tips for Human Hair Extensions

Proper hair care is essential for keeping your human hair extensions looking their best. Here are some additional hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or products on your extensions.
  • Don't sleep with wet hair, as this can cause tangling.
  • Avoid swimming or showering with your extensions in, as this can cause tangling and damage.
  • Consider using a silk pillowcase to prevent tangling and breakage.

By following these tips and choosing the best grade of human hair extensions for your needs, you can enjoy beautiful, natural-looking hair that lasts for months or even years.
