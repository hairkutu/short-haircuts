Big Chop Natural Hair Before And After A Comprehensive Guide

Are you considering doing the big chop on your natural hair? Or have you already taken the plunge and are curious about what to expect? Look no further! In this article, we will cover everything you need to know about the big chop natural hair before and after.

Description

The big chop is a term used in the natural hair community to describe cutting off all relaxed or chemically treated hair to start fresh with natural hair. This can be a big decision for many women, as it often means a significant change in appearance and requires some adjustment to a new hair care routine.

Many women choose to do the big chop because they are tired of dealing with the damage caused by chemical treatments, or they simply want to embrace their natural hair texture. Whatever the reason, it can be an empowering and transformative experience.

How to

The process of doing the big chop is relatively simple. You can either do it yourself at home or go to a professional stylist. If you choose to do it yourself, make sure you have sharp hair cutting scissors and a mirror that allows you to see the back of your head.

Start by sectioning your hair into small sections and cutting off the relaxed or chemically treated ends. You can either cut your hair to a short length or leave a bit of length if you prefer.

If you go to a professional stylist, they will likely use clippers to cut your hair to a short length. They may also provide advice on how to care for your new natural hair.

Step by step

Here is a step-by-step guide to doing the big chop at home:

  1. Wash and detangle your hair.
  2. Section your hair into small sections.
  3. Cut off the relaxed or chemically treated ends of each section.
  4. Continue until you have cut off all of the relaxed or chemically treated hair.
  5. Style your hair as desired.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you through the big chop process:

  • Invest in good hair care products specifically designed for natural hair.
  • Experiment with different hair styles to find what works best for you.
  • Be patient with your hair as it grows out.
  • Seek support from other women who have done the big chop.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about the big chop:

Is the big chop the only way to go natural?

No, it's not the only way. You can also transition to natural hair by gradually growing out your relaxed or chemically treated hair.

Will my hair grow back after the big chop?

Yes, your hair will grow back. Natural hair can grow up to six inches a year with proper care.

How do I care for my new natural hair?

Caring for natural hair involves using gentle, moisturizing hair care products, avoiding heat styling and chemical treatments, and protecting your hair at night with a satin or silk scarf or pillowcase.

Hair Care

Here are some tips for caring for your new natural hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a sulfate-free shampoo.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week.
  • Moisturize your hair daily with a leave-in conditioner or hair oil.
  • Protect your hair from heat styling and chemical treatments.

With these tips and a little patience, you can rock your natural hair with confidence and style. The big chop natural hair before and after can be a transformative experience that empowers you to embrace your natural beauty.
