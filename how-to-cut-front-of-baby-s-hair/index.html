How To Cut Front Of Baby s Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jun 14, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're a new parent, it's understandable to feel a little nervous about cutting your baby's hair for the first time. However, with the right tools and techniques, it can be a simple and stress-free process. In this article, we'll go through how to cut the front of your baby's hair, covering everything from preparation to aftercare.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Front of Baby's Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Baby's Hair
  • FAQs

How to Cut Front of Baby's Hair

Cutting your baby's hair is a personal choice, but if you decide to go ahead, it's important to do it safely and carefully. Here are the steps to cut the front of your baby's hair:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start, you'll need to gather the right tools. This includes a pair of baby hair scissors, a comb, and a spray bottle. You may also want to use a hair clip to keep the rest of your baby's hair out of the way.

Step 2: Prepare Your Baby

Choose a time when your baby is relaxed and comfortable, such as after a bath or during nap time. Make sure your baby is sitting still and secure, either in a high chair or with someone holding them.

Step 3: Wet the Hair

Spray the front section of your baby's hair with water to make it easier to cut. Be careful not to get water in your baby's eyes or face.

Step 4: Section the Hair

Use the comb to separate the front section of your baby's hair from the rest, and clip the rest of the hair out of the way. This will help you to see what you're doing and prevent accidentally cutting too much hair.

Step 5: Cut the Hair

Using the scissors, cut small amounts of hair at a time, following the shape of your baby's hairline. Start by cutting the hair in the middle and work your way outwards, cutting at an angle to give a natural-looking finish. Be sure to keep your fingers close to your baby's scalp to avoid accidentally cutting their skin.

Step 6: Check Your Progress

After cutting a small amount of hair, step back and check your progress in a mirror. This will help you to see if the hair is even and if you need to make any adjustments.

Step 7: Repeat as Necessary

If you need to take more hair off, repeat the process until you're happy with the length and shape of the hair.

Tips for Cutting Baby's Hair

Here are some additional tips to ensure a successful haircut:

  • Choose a time when your baby is calm and relaxed.
  • Use baby hair scissors, as these are designed to be safe and gentle.
  • Cut small amounts of hair at a time to avoid cutting too much.
  • Work slowly and carefully, taking breaks if necessary.
  • Keep your baby distracted with toys or songs to make the process more enjoyable.

FAQs

When should I cut my baby's hair for the first time?

There's no set time for cutting your baby's hair, but most babies will need a trim by around six months to a year old. However, some babies are born with more hair than others and may need a trim earlier.

Can I use regular scissors to cut my baby's hair?

No, regular scissors are not safe for cutting a baby's hair. Baby hair scissors are specially designed to be rounded and blunt, reducing the risk of injury.

What should I do if I accidentally cut too much hair?

If you accidentally cut too much hair, don't panic. Hair grows back quickly, and you can always take your baby to a professional hairdresser to fix any mistakes.

Hair Care for Your Baby

After cutting your baby's hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your baby's hair regularly with a gentle baby shampoo.
  • Use a soft brush or comb to detangle any knots.
  • Avoid using hair products, such as gel or hairspray, as these can be harsh on a baby's delicate skin.
  • Trim your baby's hair regularly to keep it looking neat and tidy.

With these tips and techniques, cutting the front of your baby's hair can be a simple and enjoyable experience for both you and your baby.
