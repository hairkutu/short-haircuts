How Should You Sleep With Curly Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Robby Apr 22, 2023 · 8 min read

If you have curly hair, you know that it can be a real struggle to maintain its shape and definition. One of the biggest challenges is figuring out how to sleep without ruining your curls. In this post, we’ll share some tips and tricks on how to sleep with curly hair, so you wake up with beautiful, defined curls every morning.

Table of Contents

  1. Sleep on a Silk or Satin Pillowcase
  2. Use a Silk or Satin Bonnet or Scarf
  3. Pineapple Your Curls
  4. Try the Medusa Clipping Method
  5. Use Curl-Defining Products
  6. Avoid Sleeping with Wet Hair
  7. Refresh Your Curls in the Morning
  8. Frequently Asked Questions
  9. Final Thoughts

Sleep on a Silk or Satin Pillowcase

If you want to maintain your curls overnight, the first thing you should do is switch to a silk or satin pillowcase. Unlike cotton, these materials don’t absorb moisture, which means your hair won’t dry out and frizz up as you sleep. Plus, they’re much gentler on your hair, so you’re less likely to wake up with tangles and knots.

Use a Silk or Satin Bonnet or Scarf

If you don’t want to switch out your pillowcase, you can also try wearing a silk or satin bonnet or scarf to bed. This will help protect your curls from friction and prevent them from getting flattened as you move around in your sleep. Just make sure to tie it loosely so it doesn’t create any creases in your hair.

Pineapple Your Curls

The pineapple method is a popular technique for preserving curls overnight. To do it, gather your hair into a high ponytail on the top of your head, but don’t pull it all the way through. Instead, leave the ends sticking up like a pineapple. This will help prevent your curls from getting squished as you sleep.

Try the Medusa Clipping Method

The Medusa clipping method can be a bit time-consuming, but it’s a great way to keep your curls intact overnight. To do it, section your hair into small sections and twist each one tightly. Then, clip each twist to your scalp with a bobby pin or hair clip. This will help your curls maintain their shape and definition as you sleep.

Use Curl-Defining Products

Using the right products can make a big difference in how your curls look in the morning. Look for curl-defining creams, gels, or mousses that will help enhance your curls and keep them looking bouncy and defined. Apply them to your hair before you go to bed, making sure to distribute them evenly throughout your hair.

Avoid Sleeping with Wet Hair

Sleeping with wet hair is a big no-no if you want to maintain your curls. Wet hair is more prone to breakage, and it can create frizz and tangles as you move around in your sleep. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you go to bed, either by air-drying or using a diffuser on low heat.

Refresh Your Curls in the Morning

Even if you follow all of these tips, your curls may still need a little TLC in the morning. To refresh them, spritz your hair with a mixture of water and leave-in conditioner, and scrunch your curls gently to revive them. You can also use a curl-enhancing spray or serum to add extra definition and shine.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how much product you use. Some people with curly hair can go up to a week without washing, while others may need to wash every other day. Experiment to find out what works best for you.

Q: Should I comb my curly hair before bed?

A: It’s generally not recommended to comb curly hair when it’s dry, as this can create frizz and breakage. Instead, use your fingers or a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it’s wet and use a leave-in conditioner to keep it smooth and hydrated.

Q: Can I sleep with my hair down?

A: It’s not ideal to sleep with your hair down if you want to maintain your curls, as they’re more likely to get tangled and flattened as you move around. Instead, try one of the methods we’ve outlined above to protect and preserve your curls overnight.

Final Thoughts

With the right techniques and products, sleeping with curly hair doesn’t have to be a nightmare. Experiment with different methods to find out what works best for you, and don’t be afraid to mix and match to create your own custom routine. With a little bit of effort and patience, you can wake up with beautiful, defined curls every morning.
