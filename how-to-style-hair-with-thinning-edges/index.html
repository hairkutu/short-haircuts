How To Style Hair With Thinning Edges A Complete Guide

Written by Aprilia Jun 26, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Thinning edges can be a major concern for many women, especially those with relaxed hair. But don't worry! There are plenty of ways to style your hair that can help conceal thinning edges while still looking fabulous. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about how to style hair with thinning edges.

Table of Contents

  • How to Style Hair with Thinning Edges
  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Style Hair with Thinning Edges

When it comes to styling hair with thinning edges, there are a few key things to keep in mind:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your edges and cause further damage.
  • Opt for styles that provide some coverage for your edges.
  • Use styling products that promote hair growth and help to strengthen your edges.

With these tips in mind, let's take a look at some of the best hairstyles for thinning edges.

Braids

Braids are a great option for those with thinning edges because they can provide full coverage for your hairline. However, it's important to avoid tight braids that can cause further damage. Instead, opt for looser styles like box braids or twist braids.

Twists

Twists are another great option for those with thinning edges. They provide a similar level of coverage to braids, but are generally gentler on your edges. You can opt for chunky twists or smaller twists depending on your preference.

Bantu Knots

Bantu knots are a great way to add some style to your hair while also protecting your edges. You can create small, tight knots or larger, looser knots depending on the look you're going for.

Wigs

If you're looking for a quick and easy way to switch up your hairstyle while also concealing thinning edges, wigs are a great option. You can choose from a variety of styles and colors to find the perfect look for you.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to styling your hair with thinning edges:

  1. Choose a hairstyle that provides some coverage for your edges, such as braids, twists, or Bantu knots.
  2. Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your edges and cause further damage.
  3. Use styling products that promote hair growth and help to strengthen your edges.
  4. If you're using a wig, make sure to properly secure it to prevent any damage to your edges.

Tips for Styling Hair with Thinning Edges

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when styling your hair with thinning edges:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools, which can cause further damage to your edges.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, rather than a brush.
  • Try to limit the use of hair extensions, which can put extra strain on your edges.
  • Be gentle when styling your hair, and avoid pulling or tugging on your edges.

FAQs

Can thinning edges be reversed?

While it's not always possible to fully reverse thinning edges, there are steps you can take to promote hair growth and strengthen your edges. This includes using hair growth products, avoiding tight hairstyles, and being gentle when styling your hair.

What are some hair growth products that can help with thinning edges?

There are a variety of hair growth products on the market that can help with thinning edges, including serums, oils, and shampoos. Look for products that contain ingredients like biotin, castor oil, and peppermint oil.

Hair Care for Thinning Edges

When it comes to caring for thinning edges, there are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as relaxers or dyes.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that won't strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Massage your scalp regularly to promote hair growth.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals that promote hair growth.

By following these tips and incorporating some of the hairstyles we've mentioned, you can style your hair with thinning edges while still looking fabulous.
