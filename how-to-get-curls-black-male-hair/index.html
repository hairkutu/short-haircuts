How To Get Curls In Black Male Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Apr 19, 2023 Â· 10 min read

Are you a black male looking to achieve curly hair? Whether you have naturally straight hair or relaxed hair, there are ways to get those coveted curls. In this guide, we will take you through the steps on how to get curls in black male hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Get Curls in Black Male Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Curls
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair

Description

Before we dive into the steps on how to get curls in black male hair, let's first discuss what we mean by "curls." In this guide, we are referring to tight, spiral curls that are often associated with black hair. While some black men may have naturally curly hair, others may need to use styling techniques to achieve this look.

It's important to note that getting curls in black male hair can be a time-consuming process, and it may require some trial and error to find the right method for your hair type. However, with patience and the right tools, you can achieve the curly hair you desire.

How to Get Curls in Black Male Hair

There are several methods you can use to get curls in black male hair. These include:

  • Twist-outs
  • Braid-outs
  • Finger coils
  • Roller sets

Each of these methods involves manipulating the hair in a certain way to encourage curl formation. We'll go into more detail on each of these methods in the step-by-step guide below.

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Start with clean, dry hair

Before you begin any of the curling methods, make sure your hair is clean and dry. You can wash and condition your hair as you normally would, but make sure it is completely dry before moving on to the next step.

2. Choose your method

As mentioned earlier, there are several methods you can use to get curls in black male hair. Choose the method that you think will work best for your hair type and the look you're trying to achieve.

3. Section your hair

Depending on the method you choose, you may need to section your hair into smaller sections. This will make it easier to manipulate and style your hair. Use hair clips or bands to secure each section.

4. Apply product

Apply a styling product to each section of your hair. The type of product you use will depend on the method you're using and your hair type. For example, if you're doing a twist-out, you may want to use a twisting cream or gel. If you're doing finger coils, you may want to use a curling cream or butter.

5. Twist, braid, coil, or roll

Now it's time to manipulate your hair to encourage curl formation. Follow the steps for your chosen method:

  • Twist-outs: Take a section of hair and split it into two. Twist each section around each other, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends. Repeat this process for each section of hair. You can leave the twists in overnight or until your hair is completely dry. Once your hair is dry, unravel the twists to reveal your curls.
  • Braid-outs: Take a section of hair and split it into three. Braid each section, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends. Repeat this process for each section of hair. You can leave the braids in overnight or until your hair is completely dry. Once your hair is dry, unravel the braids to reveal your curls.
  • Finger coils: Take a section of hair and apply a curling cream or butter. Use your fingers to coil the hair around itself, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends. Repeat this process for each section of hair. You can leave the coils in overnight or until your hair is completely dry. Once your hair is dry, unravel the coils to reveal your curls.
  • Roller sets: Take a section of hair and wrap it around a roller. Secure the roller in place and repeat this process for each section of hair. You can leave the rollers in overnight or until your hair is completely dry. Once your hair is dry, remove the rollers to reveal your curls.

6. Fluff and separate

Once your curls have been revealed, use your fingers to gently fluff and separate them. This will give your curls more volume and a more natural look.

Tips for Maintaining Curls

Now that you have your curls, it's important to know how to maintain them. Here are some tips:

  • Use a satin or silk pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage while you sleep
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your curls
  • Refresh your curls in the morning by lightly misting them with water and applying a small amount of styling product
  • Protect your curls from the elements by wearing a hat or scarf

FAQs

Can I get curls in relaxed hair?

Yes, you can get curls in relaxed hair. However, it may take more time and effort to get the curls to hold. Make sure to use a styling product that is specifically designed for relaxed hair.

How often should I wash my hair?

It's best to wash your hair once a week or every two weeks. Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, which can lead to dryness and breakage.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Caring for curly hair requires a different approach than caring for straight hair. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid using harsh shampoos that can strip your hair of its natural oils
  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair moisturized
  • Avoid brushing your hair, as it can disrupt your curls and cause frizz
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet

Now that you know how to get curls in black male hair, it's time to try it out for yourself. Remember to be patient and don't get discouraged if it doesn't work the first time. With practice and the right tools, you can achieve the curly hair you desire.
