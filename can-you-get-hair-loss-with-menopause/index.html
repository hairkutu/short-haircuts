Can You Get Hair Loss With Menopause

Written by Luffy Jul 05, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're going through menopause or approaching it, you may be wondering if hair loss is a common symptom. The short answer is yes, it is possible to experience hair loss during menopause. In fact, studies have shown that up to two-thirds of women will experience some degree of hair loss or thinning during this time.

What Causes Hair Loss During Menopause?

Hair loss during menopause can be attributed to several different factors, including hormonal changes, genetics, and aging. As women age, their levels of estrogen and progesterone decrease, which can lead to hair thinning and loss. Additionally, genetics can play a role in hair loss, as certain genes can make women more susceptible to hair loss during menopause.

How to Prevent Hair Loss During Menopause

While it may not be possible to completely prevent hair loss during menopause, there are steps you can take to minimize it. Here are some tips:

1. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a balanced diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals can help keep your hair healthy and strong. Foods that are particularly beneficial for hair health include leafy greens, nuts, and lean proteins like chicken and fish.

2. Exercise Regularly

Regular exercise can help improve blood flow to the scalp, which can promote healthy hair growth. Additionally, exercise can help reduce stress, which is another common cause of hair loss.

3. Take Supplements

Supplements like biotin, vitamin D, and iron can help support healthy hair growth. However, it's important to speak with your doctor before starting any new supplements, as they may interact with other medications you're taking or have other side effects.

4. Avoid Heat Styling and Chemical Treatments

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage hair and make it more prone to breakage. Additionally, chemical treatments like coloring and relaxing can weaken hair and make it more likely to fall out. Try to minimize your use of these types of styling tools and treatments, or avoid them altogether if possible.

FAQ About Hair Loss and Menopause

Q: Is hair loss during menopause permanent?

A: In most cases, hair loss during menopause is not permanent. As hormone levels stabilize and hair follicles recover, hair growth should return to normal.

Q: Can hair loss during menopause be reversed?

A: While hair loss during menopause can't always be reversed, there are steps you can take to promote healthy hair growth and minimize further loss. These include eating a healthy diet, exercising regularly, and avoiding heat styling and chemical treatments.

Q: Do all women experience hair loss during menopause?

A: No, not all women experience hair loss during menopause. However, it is a common symptom, with up to two-thirds of women experiencing some degree of hair loss or thinning.

How to Care for Thinning Hair During Menopause

If you are experiencing hair loss or thinning during menopause, there are steps you can take to care for your hair and keep it looking its best:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Choose a gentle shampoo that is free of sulfates and other harsh chemicals. Look for products that are specifically formulated for thinning hair, as these can help add volume and thickness.

2. Don't Brush Your Hair When It's Wet

Wet hair is more prone to breakage, so it's best to avoid brushing or combing it until it's dry. If you need to detangle your hair, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers.

3. Use a Thickening Spray or Mousse

Products like thickening sprays and mousses can help add volume and body to thinning hair. Apply these products to damp hair before blow-drying for best results.

4. Consider a New Hairstyle

If your hair loss is becoming more noticeable, it may be time to consider a new hairstyle. A shorter cut or a style with more layers can help disguise thinning hair and make it appear fuller.

In conclusion, hair loss during menopause is a common symptom that can be caused by hormonal changes, genetics, and aging. While it may not be possible to completely prevent hair loss, there are steps you can take to minimize it and care for your hair if it does occur. By eating a healthy diet, exercising regularly, and avoiding heat styling and chemical treatments, you can help keep your hair looking its best during this time of transition.
