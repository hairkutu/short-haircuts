How To Layer Thick Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Jul 14, 2023 · 8 min read

Having thick hair can be both a blessing and a curse. While it can be beautiful and voluminous, it can also be difficult to manage and style. One way to make thick hair more manageable is by layering it. Layering can add texture, movement, and shape to your hair, making it easier to style and maintain. In this article, we’ll be discussing how to layer thick hair in a relaxed and easy-to-understand language.

Table of Contents

  • How to Layer Thick Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Layering Thick Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Thick Hair

How to Layer Thick Hair

Layering thick hair can be a bit tricky, especially if you’re not familiar with the process. But don’t worry, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a beautiful layered look that complements your hair type and face shape.

The first step to layering thick hair is to decide what kind of layers you want. There are different types of layers, including:

  • Long Layers – these start at the chin or below and add volume and movement to the hair.
  • Short Layers – these start at the cheekbone or above and add texture and shape to the hair.
  • Face-Framing Layers – these are layers that frame the face and enhance your features.

Once you’ve decided on the type of layers you want, it’s time to gather the necessary tools. You’ll need a good pair of scissors, a comb, and clips to section your hair. It’s also a good idea to have a mirror or someone to help you with the back of your head.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have your tools ready, it’s time to start layering your hair. Here’s a step-by-step guide:

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair. It’s best to layer your hair when it’s dry, so you can see the length and texture of each strand.
  2. Section your hair into four parts – the top, left, right, and back. Use clips to secure each section.
  3. Start with the back section and divide it into smaller sections. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down.
  4. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and cut the ends at an angle. This will create a soft, layered look.
  5. Repeat this process for each section, working from the back to the front.
  6. Once you’ve finished the back section, move on to the sides. Take a small section from the front and comb it straight down.
  7. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut the ends at an angle, just like you did with the back section.
  8. Repeat this process for each section on both sides.
  9. Finally, move on to the top section. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight up.
  10. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut the ends at an angle, creating layers that blend with the rest of your hair.
  11. Once you’ve finished layering your hair, style it as desired.

Tips for Layering Thick Hair

Layering thick hair can be challenging, but with these tips, you can make the process easier:

  • Don’t cut too much hair at once. It’s better to cut small sections at a time to avoid making mistakes.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Start with longer layers and work your way up to shorter layers.
  • Consider your face shape when deciding on the type of layers you want.
  • Ask for help if you’re not confident in your skills.

FAQs

How often should I layer my thick hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it loses its shape. On average, you should layer your hair every six to eight weeks to maintain its shape and volume.

Can I layer my thick hair at home?

Yes, you can layer your thick hair at home, but it’s best to have someone help you, especially with the back of your head. If you’re not confident in your skills, it’s better to go to a professional stylist.

Will layering my thick hair make it thinner?

No, layering your thick hair will not make it thinner. In fact, layering can add volume and movement to your hair, making it appear fuller.

Hair Care for Thick Hair

Layering thick hair can make it easier to manage, but it’s still important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips for caring for your thick hair:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that are formulated for thick hair.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

Layering thick hair can be a bit intimidating, but with these tips and techniques, you can achieve a beautiful layered look that complements your hair type and face shape. Whether you do it yourself or go to a professional stylist, remember to take care of your hair properly to keep it healthy and beautiful.
