How To Ask For The Haircut You Want In Relaxed English Language

Written by William Apr 04, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you tired of leaving the hair salon feeling disappointed because you didn't get the haircut you wanted? It can be difficult to communicate what you want when you're not sure of the proper terminology. In this article, we'll provide you with tips on how to ask for the haircut you want using relaxed English language.

Table of Contents

  • How to Ask for the Haircut You Want
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Communicating with Your Stylist
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care After the Haircut

How to Ask for the Haircut You Want

When it comes to asking for the haircut you want, it's important to be clear and specific. Here are some tips on how to communicate with your stylist:

Bring a Picture

One of the best ways to ensure that you get the haircut you want is to bring in a picture. This will give your stylist a clear understanding of what you're looking for. Make sure the picture is clear and shows the hairstyle from different angles.

Use Relaxed English Language

If you're not familiar with the technical terms used in the hair industry, it's okay to use relaxed English language. For example, instead of saying you want a "bob with layers", you can say "I want a shorter cut with some volume and texture".

Be Specific

Be specific about the length you want, where you want the layers, and how much volume you want. This will help your stylist understand exactly what you're looking for.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to ask for the haircut you want:

  1. Bring in a picture of the hairstyle you want.
  2. Use relaxed English language to describe the style you want.
  3. Be specific about the length, layers, and volume.
  4. Ask your stylist for their opinion and if they have any suggestions.
  5. Confirm that you both have a clear understanding of the style you want.
  6. During the haircut, communicate with your stylist if you want any changes.
  7. Ask for styling tips and product recommendations.
  8. Leave the salon feeling confident and happy with your new haircut!

Tips for Communicating with Your Stylist

Here are some additional tips for communicating with your stylist:

  • Be honest about your hair's texture and any challenges you've had with past haircuts.
  • Ask questions if you're not sure of something.
  • Bring in multiple pictures to show different variations of the style you want.
  • Consider the maintenance required for the style you want.
  • Trust your stylist's expertise and be open to their suggestions.

FAQ

What if I'm not sure what style I want?

If you're not sure what style you want, ask your stylist for their opinion. They can recommend styles that will flatter your face shape and hair texture.

What if I don't like the haircut?

If you're not happy with the haircut, communicate with your stylist and let them know what changes you want. Most salons offer complimentary fixes within a certain timeframe.

How often should I get a haircut?

It depends on your hair type and the style you have. Generally, it's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair.

Hair Care After the Haircut

After getting a haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the style. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of the haircut.
  • Consider using styling products to enhance the style.
  • Avoid using too much product, as it can weigh down the hair.

By following these tips, you can confidently ask for the haircut you want using relaxed English language. Remember to communicate with your stylist and be specific about what you want. Happy hairstyling!
