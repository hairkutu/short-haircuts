What Male Hairstyle Suits Me A Guide For Men

Are you tired of your current hairstyle? Do you want to try something new but don't know what male hairstyle suits you? Look no further because we've got you covered! In this guide, we'll provide tips and tricks on how to determine the perfect hairstyle for your face shape, hair type, and personal style. Read on to learn more!

Table of Contents

  • Different Hair Types
  • Choosing a Hairstyle: Consider Your Face Shape and Hair Type

How to Determine Your Face Shape

The first step in determining what male hairstyle suits you is to identify your face shape. There are six basic face shapes: round, oval, square, heart, triangle, and diamond. To determine your face shape, follow these simple steps:

  1. Stand in front of a mirror and pull your hair back.
  2. Use a washable marker or lipstick to trace the outline of your face on the mirror.
  3. Step back and look at the shape you traced.

Now that you've identified your face shape, let's move on to the next step.

Different Hair Types

Another factor to consider when choosing a hairstyle is your hair type. There are several different hair types, including straight, wavy, curly, and kinky. Each hair type requires different styling techniques and products. For example, if you have curly hair, you may want to use a curl defining cream or gel to enhance your curls. If you have straight hair, you may want to use a pomade or wax to add texture and hold.

Choosing a Hairstyle: Consider Your Face Shape and Hair Type

Now that you know your face shape and hair type, it's time to choose a hairstyle that suits you. Here are some tips:

  • Round Face: If you have a round face, you may want to choose a hairstyle that adds height and volume to the top of your head. A short, choppy cut or a side-swept fringe can also help elongate your face.
  • Oval Face: Lucky you! Oval faces can pull off almost any hairstyle. Consider a classic short back and sides, a messy textured style, or a slicked-back look.
  • Square Face: If you have a square face, you may want to choose a hairstyle that softens your angular features. A textured crop, a side-parted slick back, or a messy fringe can help achieve this look.
  • Heart Face: A heart-shaped face is widest at the forehead and tapers down to a pointed chin. To balance out your features, consider a hairstyle that adds volume to the sides and back of your head. A messy quiff, a layered cut, or a side-swept fringe can work well.
  • Triangle Face: A triangle face is wider at the jawline and narrows at the forehead. To balance out your features, consider a hairstyle that adds volume to the top of your head. A classic side-parted cut or a messy textured style can work well.
  • Diamond Face: A diamond face is widest at the cheekbones and narrows at the forehead and jawline. To balance out your features, consider a hairstyle that adds volume to the top of your head. A messy textured style, a side-swept fringe, or a slicked-back look can work well.

Tips for Styling Your Hair

Now that you've chosen a hairstyle, it's important to know how to style it. Here are some tips:

  • Invest in high-quality styling products that work well with your hair type.
  • Use a hair dryer to add volume and texture to your hair.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and techniques.
  • If you're struggling to style your hair, consider visiting a barber or stylist for advice.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I have thinning hair?

If you have thinning hair, consider a shorter cut or a style that adds volume to the top of your head. Avoid heavy products that can weigh your hair down and make it look thinner.

What if I have a receding hairline?

If you have a receding hairline, consider a shorter cut or a style that adds volume to the top of your head. Avoid styles that require a lot of hair to be pulled forward, as this can accentuate your receding hairline.

What if I have curly hair?

If you have curly hair, consider a style that enhances your natural curls. Use a curl defining cream or gel to add definition and hold. Avoid heavy products that can weigh your curls down.

Hair Care Tips

Finally, it's important to take care of your hair to ensure it looks its best. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a shampoo and conditioner that work well with your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot tools like straighteners and curling irons too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors by wearing a hat or using a styling product with UV protection.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.

Now that you know what male hairstyle suits you, it's time to hit the barber shop and try out a new look!
