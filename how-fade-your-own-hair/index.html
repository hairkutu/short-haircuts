How To Fade Your Own Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Aug 31, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of spending money at the barber shop every time you need a fade? Learning how to fade your own hair can save you time and money in the long run. In this guide, we will walk you through the process of fading your own hair from start to finish. Read on for tips, tricks, and frequently asked questions about fading your own hair.

Description

Fading your own hair is a process of gradually blending the length of your hair down to a shorter length. This creates a clean, polished look that is popular among men of all ages. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking fade in the comfort of your own home.

How To Fade Your Own Hair

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you begin, make sure you have all the tools you need for the job. You'll need a pair of hair clippers with several guards, a comb, hair scissors, and a handheld mirror. It's also helpful to have a towel or cape to protect your clothing from hair clippings.

Step 2: Choose Your Fade

There are several types of fades to choose from, including high fades, low fades, and mid fades. Decide which type of fade you want and choose the appropriate guard for your clippers.

Step 3: Start With the Longest Guard

Begin by using the longest guard on your clippers to trim the hair on the top of your head. Work your way around your head, trimming the hair to an even length.

Step 4: Move to the Next Guard

Once you've trimmed the hair with the longest guard, switch to the next shortest guard and trim the hair around the sides and back of your head. Use the comb to blend the hair between the different lengths.

Step 5: Gradually Shorten the Length

Continue switching to shorter and shorter guards, gradually shortening the length of the hair around your head. Use the comb to blend the hair between the different lengths and create a smooth, even fade.

Step 6: Trim Any Stray Hairs

Once you've achieved your desired fade, use hair scissors to trim any stray hairs or uneven spots. Use the handheld mirror to check the back of your head for any missed areas.

Step 7: Clean Up

After you've finished fading your hair, brush away any loose hair clippings and clean up your tools. Use a damp towel to wipe away any hair stuck to your skin or clothing.

Tips for Fading Your Own Hair

  • Start with a longer guard and gradually work your way down to avoid cutting off too much hair at once.
  • Use the comb to blend the hair between different lengths for a smooth, even fade.
  • Take breaks if you feel fatigued or need to step away from the process for a moment.
  • Use a handheld mirror to check the back of your head for any missed areas.

FAQ

Can I fade my own hair if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can fade your own hair if you have curly hair. However, it may be more difficult to achieve a smooth, even fade. Take your time and use the comb to blend the hair between different lengths.

How often should I fade my own hair?

How often you fade your own hair depends on your personal preference and hair growth rate. Some people prefer to fade their hair every two weeks, while others may wait as long as four weeks between fades.

Can I use a razor to fade my hair?

We do not recommend using a razor to fade your hair, as it can cause irritation and ingrown hairs. Stick to using hair clippers with guards for best results.

Hair Care After Fading Your Own Hair

After fading your own hair, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain a healthy scalp and prevent dryness or breakage. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and soft. Avoid using heat tools, such as hair dryers or straighteners, and limit your use of hair products to prevent buildup.

Learning how to fade your own hair can be a game-changer for your grooming routine. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking fade in the comfort of your own home. Follow these steps, tips, and FAQs to achieve a perfect fade every time.
