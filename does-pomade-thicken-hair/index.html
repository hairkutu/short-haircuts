Does Pomade Thicken Hair

Written by Luffy Apr 05, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you are someone who is struggling with thinning hair or hair loss, then you might be wondering if pomade can help thicken your hair. Pomade is a popular hair styling product that has been used for decades to create sleek and shiny hairstyles. But, can it really help to thicken your hair? In this article, we will explore this topic in detail and provide you with all the information you need to know about does pomade thicken hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Use Pomade to Thicken Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Using Pomade to Thicken Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Thicker Hair

Description

Pomade is a hair styling product that is used to create various hairstyles, such as slicked-back looks, pompadours, and side parts. It is a waxy or greasy substance that is applied to the hair to give it a shiny, sleek appearance. Pomade is made from a combination of natural waxes, oils, and petroleum jelly. It is known for its ability to hold hair in place for long periods of time, and it is often used by men who have thick, unruly hair.

However, when it comes to thickening hair, pomade is not the best option. While it can make hair appear thicker by adding volume and texture, it does not actually thicken the hair shaft. In fact, using too much pomade can actually make hair look thinner and greasier, which can exacerbate the appearance of hair loss or thinning.

How to Use Pomade to Thicken Hair

If you still want to try using pomade to thicken your hair, there are a few things you can do to maximize its effectiveness. Here are some tips:

  • Choose a pomade that is designed for volumizing or thickening hair. Look for products that contain ingredients like keratin, biotin, and caffeine, which are known to promote hair growth and thickness.
  • Apply pomade to damp hair. This will help to distribute the product evenly and prevent clumping or greasiness.
  • Use a small amount of pomade. Too much product can weigh down your hair and make it look thinner.
  • Style your hair with a blow dryer. This will help to add volume and texture to your hair, which can make it appear thicker.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide for using pomade to thicken your hair:

  1. Wash your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Towel-dry your hair until it is damp.
  3. Take a small amount of pomade and rub it between your palms to warm it up.
  4. Apply the pomade to your hair, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends.
  5. Use a comb or your fingers to style your hair as desired.
  6. Blow dry your hair on a low heat setting, using your fingers to lift and separate the hair as you go.

Tips for Using Pomade to Thicken Hair

Here are some additional tips for using pomade to thicken your hair:

  • Use a clarifying shampoo once a week to remove any buildup from the pomade.
  • Avoid using pomade every day, as this can lead to product buildup and clogged hair follicles.
  • Massage your scalp regularly to promote blood flow and stimulate hair growth.
  • Eat a healthy diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals that are essential for hair growth, such as vitamin D, iron, and biotin.

FAQs

Q: Can pomade cause hair loss?

A: While pomade itself is not known to cause hair loss, using too much of it or not washing it out properly can lead to clogged hair follicles, which can inhibit hair growth and lead to thinning hair over time.

Q: Is pomade bad for your hair?

A: Pomade is not necessarily bad for your hair, but using too much of it or not washing it out properly can lead to product buildup, clogged hair follicles, and thinning hair over time.

Hair Care Tips for Thicker Hair

If you are looking for ways to thicken your hair, there are several things you can do to improve the overall health and appearance of your hair:

  • Eat a healthy diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals that are essential for hair growth, such as vitamin D, iron, and biotin.
  • Avoid harsh chemicals and heat styling tools that can damage your hair and inhibit growth.
  • Massage your scalp regularly to promote blood flow and stimulate hair growth.
  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner that are designed to add volume and thickness to your hair.
  • Consider using a hair growth supplement or treatment, such as minoxidil or finasteride, which can help to stimulate hair growth and thicken existing hair.

Overall, while pomade can add volume and texture to your hair, it is not a reliable solution for thickening hair. Instead, focus on improving your overall hair care routine and incorporating healthy habits into your daily routine to promote hair growth and thickness.
