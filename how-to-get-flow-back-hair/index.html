How To Get Flow Back Hair Step By Step Guide And Tips

Written by Aprilia Aug 25, 2023 · 11 min read
How to Get Flow Back Hair: Step-by-Step Guide and Tips | Hair Care

If you're struggling with dull, lifeless hair that lacks flow and movement, don't worry – you're not alone. Many people experience hair that's lost its natural bounce and shine, whether due to damage, age, or other factors. But the good news is that there are ways to get flow back hair and restore your locks to their former glory. In this article, we'll explore how to get flow back hair with a step-by-step guide and share some helpful tips along the way.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Get Flow Back Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Getting Flow Back Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Flowing Locks

How to Get Flow Back Hair

Getting flow back hair is all about restoring movement, volume, and shine to your locks. Here are some key steps to follow:

1. Start with a Deep Cleanse

The first step in getting flow back hair is to give it a deep cleanse. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove build-up and product residue that can weigh down your hair and make it look dull. Be sure to massage the shampoo into your scalp to stimulate blood flow and promote hair growth.

2. Condition with Care

After shampooing, it's important to condition your hair to restore moisture and nourishment. Choose a conditioner that's tailored to your hair type and apply it from mid-lengths to ends, avoiding the roots. Leave it on for a few minutes before rinsing thoroughly.

3. Use a Hair Mask

To give your hair an extra boost of hydration, use a hair mask once a week. Apply the mask to damp hair and leave it on for 10-15 minutes before rinsing thoroughly. Look for a mask that's infused with natural ingredients like avocado oil, coconut oil, or shea butter.

4. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like straighteners, curling irons, and hairdryers can damage your hair and make it look dull and lifeless. Try to avoid using these tools as much as possible, or at least use them sparingly. If you must use heat, be sure to apply a heat protectant spray first.

5. Get Regular Trims

Regular trims are essential for maintaining healthy, flowing hair. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and breakage. If you're growing out your hair, ask your stylist for a "dusting" – a light trim that removes only the very ends of your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to getting flow back hair:

Step 1: Deep Cleanse

Start by wetting your hair thoroughly, then apply a clarifying shampoo. Massage the shampoo into your scalp using your fingertips, then work it through your hair from roots to ends. Rinse thoroughly with warm water.

Step 2: Condition

Apply a generous amount of conditioner to your hair, focusing on the mid-lengths and ends. Leave the conditioner on for 2-3 minutes, then rinse thoroughly with cool water. Cool water helps to seal the hair cuticle and lock in moisture.

Step 3: Apply a Hair Mask

Once a week, apply a hair mask to give your locks an extra boost of hydration. Apply the mask to damp hair, then leave it on for 10-15 minutes before rinsing thoroughly with cool water.

Step 4: Air Dry

Avoid using heat styling tools if possible. Instead, let your hair air dry naturally. If you must blow-dry your hair, use a cool setting and hold the dryer at least 6 inches away from your hair.

Step 5: Style with Care

When styling your hair, be gentle and avoid pulling or tugging. Use a wide-toothed comb or a brush with soft bristles to detangle your hair. If you want to add some volume, try flipping your hair upside down and scrunching it with your hands.

Tips for Getting Flow Back Hair

Here are some additional tips for getting flow back hair:

1. Eat a Healthy Diet

What you eat can have a big impact on the health of your hair. Be sure to eat plenty of fruits, vegetables, and lean proteins to provide your locks with the nutrients they need to grow and shine.

2. Use a Satin Pillowcase

Cotton pillowcases can cause friction and damage to your hair while you sleep. Switch to a satin pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent tangles.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

UV rays from the sun can damage your hair and cause it to look dull and lifeless. Wear a hat or use a UV protectant spray to shield your locks from the sun's harmful rays.

4. Be Gentle When Brushing Wet Hair

Your hair is most vulnerable to damage when it's wet. Be gentle when brushing your hair after a shower, and use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your locks.

FAQs

Q: Can I get flow back hair if I have naturally curly hair?

A: Yes! The steps outlined in this article will work for all hair types, including curly hair. Just be sure to use products that are specifically formulated for curly hair to help define your curls and reduce frizz.

Q: How long does it take to get flow back hair?

A: The amount of time it takes to get flow back hair will depend on the current state of your locks and the steps you take to restore them. With consistent care and attention, you should start to see improvements in your hair's flow and movement within a few weeks.

Hair Care for Flowing Locks

Now that you know how to get flow back hair, it's important to maintain your locks with regular hair care. Here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy and flowing:

1. Wash with Care

Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's tailored to your hair type. Avoid washing your hair too frequently, as this can strip your locks of natural oils and cause them to look dull.

2. Protect from Heat

If you must use heat styling tools, be sure to apply a heat protectant spray first. Use a lower heat setting whenever possible, and limit your use of heat styling tools to avoid damage.

3. Get Regular Trims

Regular trims are essential for maintaining healthy, flowing hair. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and breakage.

4. Use Natural Products

Look for hair care products that are free from harsh chemicals and synthetic fragrances. Choose products that are formulated with natural ingredients like argan oil, coconut oil, or shea butter.

5. Stay Hydrated

Drinking plenty of water is essential for maintaining healthy hair. Aim to drink at least 8 glasses of water per day to keep your locks hydrated from the inside out.

By following these tips and using the step-by-step guide outlined in this article, you can get flow back hair and enjoy healthy, flowing locks that shine with vitality and vibrancy.
