How To Measure Your Face For Eyeglasses A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jun 24, 2023 Â· 8 min read

When it comes to buying eyeglasses, it's important to get the right fit. One of the most important factors in achieving the perfect fit is measuring your face correctly. In this article, we'll show you how to measure your face for eyeglasses in easy-to-understand language. From finding your face shape to measuring your pupillary distance, we've got you covered.

Table of Contents

  1. Finding Your Face Shape
  2. Measuring Your Pupillary Distance
  3. Measuring Your Face Width
  4. Measuring Your Temple Length
  5. Tips for Measuring Your Face for Eyeglasses
  6. FAQs
  7. Bonus: Hair Care Tips for Eyeglass Wearers

Finding Your Face Shape

The first step in measuring your face for eyeglasses is to determine your face shape. There are six basic face shapes: round, oval, square, heart, diamond, and triangle. To find your face shape, stand in front of a mirror and pull your hair back. Use a washable marker or lipstick to outline the shape of your face on the mirror. Compare your outline to the six basic face shapes and determine which one is closest to your own.

Once you've determined your face shape, you can use it to guide your eyeglass frame selection. Different frame shapes and styles flatter different face shapes, so it's important to choose a frame that complements your face shape.

Measuring Your Pupillary Distance

Your pupillary distance (PD) is the distance between the centers of your pupils. This measurement is important because it determines where the optical center of your lenses should be placed for optimal vision. You can have your PD measured by an optometrist or you can measure it yourself at home.

To measure your PD at home, you'll need a ruler and a friend to help you. Stand facing your friend with your eyes level and your face straight ahead. Have your friend place the ruler against your brow, with the zero mark at the center of your left pupil. Ask your friend to measure the distance between the centers of your pupils in millimeters. Repeat the measurement a few times to ensure accuracy.

Measuring Your Face Width

Measuring your face width is important because it helps you determine the appropriate frame size for your face. To measure your face width, use a ruler to measure the distance between your temples. Start at the top of your left temple and measure to the top of your right temple. Record the measurement in millimeters.

Measuring Your Temple Length

Your temple length is the distance from the hinge of your glasses to the earpiece. To measure your temple length, use a ruler to measure the distance from the hinge of your glasses to the back of your ear. Record the measurement in millimeters.

Tips for Measuring Your Face for Eyeglasses

  • Measure your face when it's relaxed and in a natural position, with your eyes looking straight ahead.
  • Use a flexible measuring tape or a string to measure your face width if you don't have a ruler.
  • Ask a friend to help you measure your PD to ensure accuracy.
  • Take your time and measure each parameter a few times to ensure accuracy.
  • Use your measurements to guide your eyeglass frame selection, but also remember to choose a frame that you feel comfortable and confident in.

FAQs

How often should I measure my face for eyeglasses?

You should measure your face for eyeglasses whenever you're buying new glasses or if you notice that your current glasses don't fit well.

Is it better to have my PD measured by an optometrist or to measure it myself?

Having your PD measured by an optometrist is the most accurate method, but measuring it yourself at home can be a good option if you don't have access to an optometrist or if you want to save money.

What if my measurements fall between two sizes?

If your measurements fall between two sizes, it's best to choose the larger size. You can always have the frames adjusted to fit your face more comfortably.

Bonus: Hair Care Tips for Eyeglass Wearers

If you wear eyeglasses, you know that they can sometimes cause your hair to get tangled or flattened. Here are a few tips to keep your hair looking great while wearing glasses:

  • Choose frames that don't have nose pads to avoid hair getting caught.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or detangler to keep hair smooth and tangle-free.
  • Try pulling your hair back into a low ponytail or bun to keep it out of the way.
  • Use a volumizing spray or dry shampoo to add volume to hair that's been flattened by glasses.

By following these simple tips, you can keep your hair looking great while wearing glasses and enjoy clear, comfortable vision.
