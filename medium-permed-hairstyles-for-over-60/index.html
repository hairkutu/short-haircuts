Medium Permed Hairstyles For Over 60 A Guide To Styling Your Hair

Written by Frank Jun 15, 2023 Â· 7 min read

As we age, our hair tends to lose its volume and texture, making it difficult to style. However, getting a medium perm can add bounce and curl to your hair, giving it a youthful and vibrant look. If you're over 60 and looking for a new hairstyle, medium permed hairstyles might be a great option for you. In this article, we'll discuss everything you need to know about medium permed hairstyles for over 60.

Description

Medium permed hairstyles for over 60 are haircuts that involve getting a perm on hair that is shoulder-length or shorter. A perm is a chemical treatment that adds curls or waves to your hair, giving it a bouncy and voluminous look. Medium permed hairstyles can be styled in many different ways, from loose waves to tight curls, making them versatile and easy to maintain.

How to Get a Medium Perm

The first step to getting a medium perm is to consult with a hairstylist. They can help you choose the right type of perm for your hair type and face shape. Once you've decided on a style, your hair will be washed and wrapped around perm rods. A perm solution will then be applied to your hair, which will break down the protein bonds in your hair, allowing it to take on its new shape. After a few minutes, the perm solution will be rinsed out, and a neutralizing solution will be applied to set the curls in place.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Your Medium Permed Hair

Styling your medium permed hair is easy and can be done in a few simple steps:

  1. Start by washing your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent your curls from getting frizzy.
  2. Apply a curl-enhancing cream or gel to your damp hair, then use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair while scrunching it with your hands.
  3. If you want tighter curls, use a curling iron on small sections of your hair, then scrunch them with your fingers to loosen them up.
  4. Finish off your look with a hairspray or anti-frizz serum to set your curls in place.

Tips for Maintaining Your Medium Permed Hair

To keep your medium permed hair looking its best, follow these tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent your curls from getting frizzy.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause your curls to lose their shape.
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair while scrunching it with your hands.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

FAQs

Q: Will a perm damage my hair?

A: Perming your hair can cause some damage, but if done correctly, it shouldn't be too severe. To minimize damage, make sure to get your perm done by a professional stylist and use a deep conditioner regularly.

Q: How long will my perm last?

A: The lifespan of your perm will depend on your hair type and the type of perm you get. Typically, perms last anywhere from two to six months.

Q: Can I color my hair after getting a perm?

A: Yes, you can color your hair after getting a perm, but it's best to wait a few weeks before doing so to avoid damaging your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Medium Permed Hair

To keep your medium permed hair looking its best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent your curls from getting frizzy.
  • Avoid using heating tools like straighteners and curling irons too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause your curls to lose their shape.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

In conclusion, medium permed hairstyles for over 60 can add bounce and curl to your hair, giving it a youthful and vibrant look. By following the tips and hair care advice in this article, you can keep your medium permed hair looking its best for months to come.
