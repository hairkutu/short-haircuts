What Is Progressed Thinning Hair Mean

Written by Daniel Jul 17, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Progressed thinning hair is a condition where an individual experiences excessive hair loss, leading to thinning of the hair. This condition can be caused by various factors, such as genetics, hormonal changes, stress, and certain medical conditions.

Description

Progressive thinning hair is a gradual process that takes place over time. It usually starts with a few strands of hair falling out, which gradually increases with time. As the hair follicles weaken, the hair becomes thinner and more fragile, leading to breakage and hair loss.

How to Identify Progressed Thinning Hair

There are various signs that can help you identify if you are experiencing progressed thinning hair. Some of the common signs include:

  • Increased hair shedding during washing or combing
  • Thinning of the hair on the crown or top of the head
  • Receding hairline
  • Bald spots or patches on the scalp
  • Visible scalp through the hair

Step by Step Guide on How to Deal with Progressed Thinning Hair

Step 1: Identify the Cause of Your Thinning Hair

The first step in dealing with progressed thinning hair is to identify the underlying cause. This can be done by visiting a dermatologist or a trichologist, who can examine your scalp and hair and recommend appropriate treatment.

Step 2: Change Your Diet

Your diet plays a significant role in the health of your hair. Eating a balanced diet rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals can help promote hair growth and prevent hair loss.

Step 3: Use Hair Growth Products

There are various hair growth products available in the market that can help promote hair growth and prevent hair loss. These include shampoos, conditioners, and serums that contain ingredients such as biotin, caffeine, and minoxidil.

Step 4: Consider Hair Transplant

If your hair loss is severe, you may consider hair transplant surgery. This involves removing hair follicles from a donor area and implanting them into the balding areas of the scalp.

Tips for Dealing with Progressed Thinning Hair

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on the hair
  • Avoid using heat styling tools frequently
  • Protect your hair from harsh chemicals and environmental factors
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair

FAQs About Progressed Thinning Hair

Q: Is progressed thinning hair reversible?

A: In some cases, progressed thinning hair can be reversed with appropriate treatment. However, in severe cases, hair transplant surgery may be the only option.

Q: Can stress cause progressed thinning hair?

A: Yes, stress can cause progressed thinning hair. When you are stressed, your body produces a hormone called cortisol, which can lead to hair loss.

Q: Can hair loss be prevented?

A: While hair loss cannot be completely prevented, you can reduce the risk by maintaining a healthy diet, avoiding tight hairstyles, and protecting your hair from harsh chemicals and environmental factors.

Hair Care Tips for Preventing Progressed Thinning Hair

  • Eat a balanced diet rich in protein and vitamins
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on the hair
  • Protect your hair from harsh chemicals and environmental factors
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair
  • Avoid frequent use of heat styling tools

In conclusion, progressed thinning hair is a common condition that affects both men and women. While it cannot be completely prevented, appropriate treatment and hair care can help promote hair growth and prevent hair loss.
