How To Cut Bangs To Make Them Look Fuller A Step By Step Guide

Description

Bangs are a great way to switch up your hairstyle and add a fresh new look to your face. However, not all bangs are created equal. Some bangs can look thin and lifeless, while others look full and voluminous. If you're looking to cut your bangs to make them look fuller, you've come to the right place. In this article, we'll give you a step-by-step guide on how to cut bangs to make them look fuller, as well as tips and tricks to make sure you get the best results.

How to Cut Bangs to Make Them Look Fuller

The first step in cutting bangs to make them look fuller is to choose the right bangs for your face shape. If you have a round face, you should opt for side-swept bangs that are longer on the sides. If you have a square face, you should choose bangs that are longer in the middle and shorter on the sides. If you have an oval face, you can choose any type of bangs that you like.

Once you've chosen the right bangs for your face shape, it's time to start cutting. Here are some tips to help you get started:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Wash and dry your hair. It's important to start with clean, dry hair so that you can see the natural fall of your bangs.
  2. Section off your bangs. Using a comb, part your hair where you want your bangs to start.
  3. Start cutting. Using a pair of sharp scissors, cut your bangs in a straight line. Make sure to cut them slightly longer than you want them to be, as they will shorten when they dry.
  4. Texture your bangs. Using thinning scissors or a razor, add texture to your bangs. This will make them look fuller and more voluminous.
  5. Style your bangs. Blow-dry your bangs using a round brush. This will help to add volume and shape to your bangs.
  6. Finish with hairspray. Spray your bangs with a light hairspray to help them hold their shape.

Tips for Cutting Bangs

Here are some additional tips to help you cut your bangs to make them look fuller:

  • Don't cut too much at once. It's better to cut a little bit at a time and keep checking your progress, rather than cutting too much and having to start over.
  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make your bangs look uneven.
  • Start longer than you want. Your bangs will shorten when they dry, so it's better to start with longer bangs and trim them as needed.
  • Don't cut your bangs when they're wet. Wet hair can stretch and shrink, so it's best to cut your bangs when they're dry.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting bangs to make them look fuller:

1. How often should I cut my bangs?

You should trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh and healthy.

2. What if I mess up my bangs?

If you accidentally cut too much off your bangs, don't panic. You can always pin them back or wear a headband to hide them while they grow out.

3. Can I cut my own bangs?

Yes, you can cut your own bangs, but it's best to start with small cuts and work your way up.

Hair Care for Bangs

Once you've cut your bangs to make them look fuller, it's important to take care of them to keep them looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your bangs every other day to keep them clean.
  • Blow-dry your bangs using a round brush to add volume.
  • Use a small amount of styling cream to tame flyaways and add shine.
  • Avoid touching your bangs too much, as this can make them look greasy.

With these tips and tricks, you can cut your bangs to make them look fuller and achieve the perfect new look for your face shape. Happy cutting!
