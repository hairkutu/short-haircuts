Which Dog Breed Has Less Hair

Written by Bardi Jul 18, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you tired of constantly cleaning up dog hair from your furniture, clothes, and floors? Maybe you have allergies and are looking for a dog breed that sheds less. Whatever your reasons, there are certain dog breeds that are known for having less hair than others. In this article, we will explore which dog breed has less hair and provide tips for hair care.

  • Which Dog Breed Has Less Hair?

Which Dog Breed Has Less Hair?

There are several dog breeds that are known for shedding less than others. These breeds include:

  • Poodle
  • Bichon Frise
  • Maltese
  • Portuguese Water Dog
  • Shih Tzu
  • Yorkshire Terrier
  • Australian Terrier
  • West Highland White Terrier
  • Chinese Crested
  • Havanese

Each of these breeds has a unique personality and appearance, so it's important to research them further to see which one would be the best fit for your lifestyle.

Description of Low-Shedding Dog Breeds

Low-shedding dog breeds typically have hair instead of fur, which means they don't shed as much. These breeds require regular grooming to prevent matting and to keep their hair from getting too long. Some low-shedding breeds also have hypoallergenic qualities, making them a good choice for people with allergies.

Poodle

Poodles are a popular low-shedding breed that come in three sizes: standard, miniature, and toy. They have curly hair that requires regular grooming to prevent matting. Poodles are highly intelligent and trainable, making them a great choice for families.

Bichon Frise

The Bichon Frise is a small, fluffy breed that has a thick, curly coat. They are known for their friendly personalities and are often used as therapy dogs. Bichon Frises require regular grooming to keep their coat in good condition.

Maltese

The Maltese is a small breed with long, silky hair. They are known for their affectionate personalities and are great lap dogs. Maltese require daily grooming to keep their hair from matting.

Portuguese Water Dog

The Portuguese Water Dog is a medium-sized breed that was originally bred to help fishermen. They have curly, non-shedding hair and require regular grooming. Portuguese Water Dogs are highly intelligent and active, making them a good choice for families who enjoy outdoor activities.

How to Choose a Low-Shedding Dog Breed

When choosing a low-shedding dog breed, there are several factors to consider:

  • Your lifestyle: Do you have a lot of free time to devote to grooming and exercise?
  • Your living situation: Do you live in an apartment or a house with a yard?
  • Your allergies: Do you have allergies to dogs?
  • Your family: Do you have children or other pets?

Researching different breeds and talking to breeders or rescue organizations can help you make an informed decision.

Step-by-Step Guide to Grooming a Low-Shedding Dog

Grooming a low-shedding dog requires regular maintenance to prevent matting and to keep their hair at a manageable length. Here are the steps to grooming a low-shedding dog:

  1. Brush your dog's hair daily to prevent matting.
  2. Trim your dog's hair every 4-6 weeks, depending on the breed.
  3. Bathe your dog as needed, using a gentle shampoo.
  4. Check your dog's ears and clean them as needed.
  5. Trim your dog's nails regularly to prevent them from getting too long.

Following these steps can help keep your low-shedding dog's hair in good condition.

Tips for Hair Care

Here are some additional tips for caring for your low-shedding dog's hair:

  • Use a high-quality brush to prevent matting.
  • Trim your dog's hair regularly to prevent it from getting too long.
  • Use a gentle shampoo to avoid irritating your dog's skin.
  • Consider using a detangler spray to make brushing easier.

Following these tips can help keep your dog's hair healthy and looking great.

FAQs About Low-Shedding Dog Breeds

Are low-shedding dogs hypoallergenic?

Not all low-shedding dogs are hypoallergenic, but some breeds are less likely to cause allergies than others. Breeds such as the Poodle and the Bichon Frise are often recommended for people with allergies.

Do low-shedding dogs require less grooming than other breeds?

No, low-shedding dogs require regular grooming to prevent matting and to keep their hair in good condition. In some cases, they may require more grooming than other breeds.

Can low-shedding dogs be left alone for long periods of time?

No, low-shedding dogs require regular exercise and human interaction. They are not recommended for people who are away from home for long periods of time.

What is the lifespan of low-shedding dogs?

The lifespan of low-shedding dogs varies depending on the breed. On average, they can live anywhere from 10-15 years.

Conclusion

If you're looking for a dog breed that sheds less, there are several options to choose from. Low-shedding breeds require regular grooming to keep their hair in good condition, but they can make great pets for people with allergies or for those who are tired of constantly cleaning up dog hair. Use the tips and information in this article to help you make an informed decision.
