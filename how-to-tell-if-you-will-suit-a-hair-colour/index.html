How To Tell If You Will Suit A Hair Colour

Written by Robby Aug 13, 2023 Â· 11 min read

Are you thinking of changing your hair colour but not sure if it will suit you? Choosing the right hair colour can be a daunting task, but it doesn't have to be. With a few tips and tricks, you can easily tell if a hair colour will suit you or not. In this article, we will guide you on how to tell if you will suit a hair colour and provide you with some useful tips to help you make the right decision.

Description

Choosing the perfect hair colour that suits your skin tone and personality is essential to look and feel great. The right hair colour can enhance your features, brighten your skin, and make you feel confident. However, selecting the wrong hair colour can make you look washed out, dull, or even older than your age.

How to Tell If You Will Suit a Hair Colour

Here are some tips on how to tell if you will suit a hair colour:

1. Determine Your Skin Tone

The first step in choosing the right hair colour is to determine your skin tone. Your skin tone is the underlying pigmentation of your skin. It can be warm, cool or neutral. To determine your skin tone, look at the veins on your wrist. If they appear blue, you have a cool skin tone. If they appear green, you have a warm skin tone. If you can't decide, you probably have a neutral skin tone.

2. Consider Your Eye Colour

Another factor to consider when choosing a hair colour is your eye colour. Your eye colour can help you determine which hair colour will suit you. For example, if you have blue eyes, a cool hair colour such as ash blonde, platinum blonde, or cool brown will suit you. If you have brown eyes, warm hair colours such as golden blonde, warm brown, or copper will suit you.

3. Look at Your Natural Hair Colour

Your natural hair colour can also help you determine which hair colour will suit you. If you have a warm natural hair colour such as red, orange, or yellow, warm hair colours such as golden blonde, warm brown, or copper will suit you. If you have a cool natural hair colour such as blue, violet, or green, cool hair colours such as ash blonde, platinum blonde, or cool brown will suit you.

4. Consider Your Personality

Your personality is also an important factor to consider when choosing a hair colour. If you are outgoing and adventurous, you may want to opt for a bold and bright hair colour. If you are more reserved, you may want to choose a more natural hair colour.

Step by Step Guide

Follow these simple steps to determine if a hair colour will suit you or not:

Step 1: Determine Your Skin Tone

Look at the veins on your wrist. If they appear blue, you have a cool skin tone. If they appear green, you have a warm skin tone. If you can't decide, you probably have a neutral skin tone.

Step 2: Consider Your Eye Colour

If you have blue eyes, a cool hair colour such as ash blonde, platinum blonde, or cool brown will suit you. If you have brown eyes, warm hair colours such as golden blonde, warm brown, or copper will suit you.

Step 3: Look at Your Natural Hair Colour

If you have a warm natural hair colour such as red, orange, or yellow, warm hair colours such as golden blonde, warm brown, or copper will suit you. If you have a cool natural hair colour such as blue, violet, or green, cool hair colours such as ash blonde, platinum blonde, or cool brown will suit you.

Step 4: Consider Your Personality

If you are outgoing and adventurous, you may want to opt for a bold and bright hair colour. If you are more reserved, you may want to choose a more natural hair colour.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you choose the right hair colour:

1. Consult a Professional

If you are unsure which hair colour will suit you, consult a professional hairstylist. They can help you choose the right hair colour based on your skin tone, eye colour, and personality.

2. Try a Temporary Hair Colour

If you are not ready to commit to a permanent hair colour, try a temporary hair colour. This will allow you to experiment with different hair colours without damaging your hair.

3. Consider Your Lifestyle

Consider your lifestyle when choosing a hair colour. If you have a busy lifestyle, choose a low-maintenance hair colour that requires minimal upkeep.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about how to tell if you will suit a hair colour:

Q: Can I dye my hair if I have sensitive skin?

A: Yes, but be sure to choose a hair dye specifically designed for sensitive skin. Also, do a patch test before applying the hair dye to your hair.

Q: How often should I dye my hair?

A: It depends on the type of hair colour you choose. Permanent hair colour typically lasts 4-6 weeks, while semi-permanent hair colour lasts 2-4 weeks.

Q: Can I dye my hair if I have damaged hair?

A: It is not recommended to dye your hair if it is damaged. Wait until your hair is healthy before applying hair dye.

Hair Care

After dyeing your hair, it is important to take care of it to maintain its health and vibrancy. Here are some tips to care for coloured hair:

1. Use a Colour-Safe Shampoo and Conditioner

Use a shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for coloured hair. This will help prevent the colour from fading and keep your hair looking vibrant.

2. Avoid Hot Styling Tools

Avoid using hot styling tools such as flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage coloured hair. Instead, opt for heat-free styling methods such as air-drying or using rollers.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Exposure to the sun can cause coloured hair to fade. Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a hair product with SPF.

Conclusion

Choosing the right hair colour can be a game changer. By following these tips and tricks, you can easily tell if a hair colour will suit you or not. Remember to consider your skin tone, eye colour, natural hair colour, and personality when choosing a hair colour. Consult a professional hairstylist if you are unsure which hair colour will suit you. Take care of your coloured hair by using a colour-safe shampoo and conditioner, avoiding hot styling tools, and protecting your hair from the sun.
