How To Prevent Hair Falling Out In Shower Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Aug 26, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're dealing with hair loss, you're not alone. Unfortunately, one of the most common times for hair to fall out is in the shower. The good news is that there are some simple steps you can take to reduce the amount of hair that falls out during your shower. In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks to help you keep your hair healthy and prevent hair loss in the shower.

How to Prevent Hair Falling Out in Shower: Step by Step

Step 1: Choose the Right Shampoo and Conditioner

The type of shampoo and conditioner you use can make a big difference in the health of your hair. Look for products that are specifically designed for your hair type, whether that's oily, dry, curly, or straight. Avoid shampoos and conditioners that contain harsh chemicals, like sulfates and parabens, as these can damage your hair and make it more prone to falling out.

Step 2: Detangle Your Hair Before You Shower

Before you step into the shower, take a few minutes to detangle your hair. Use a wide-tooth comb or a brush with soft bristles to gently remove any knots or tangles. This will help prevent hair from getting caught in your hair during shampooing and conditioning.

Step 3: Don't Use Hot Water

Hot water can be damaging to your hair, causing it to become dry and brittle. Instead, use lukewarm water when you're washing your hair. This will help keep your hair healthy and prevent it from falling out.

Step 4: Massage Your Scalp

When you're shampooing your hair, take the time to massage your scalp. This will help increase blood flow to your hair follicles, which can promote healthy hair growth. Be gentle, though, as too much pressure can damage your hair.

Step 5: Rinse Thoroughly

Make sure you rinse your hair thoroughly after shampooing and conditioning. Any leftover product can cause buildup and make your hair more prone to falling out. Rinse until the water runs clear.

Tips for Preventing Hair Falling Out in Shower

Tip 1: Use a Shower Filter

Hard water can be damaging to your hair, causing it to become dry and brittle. One way to combat this is to use a shower filter. These are easy to install and can help remove minerals and other impurities from your water.

Tip 2: Use a Silk Pillowcase

Using a silk pillowcase can help prevent hair loss while you sleep. Unlike cotton pillowcases, silk doesn't create friction against your hair, which can cause it to fall out. Silk also helps keep your hair moisturized, which can reduce breakage.

Tip 3: Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles, like braids and ponytails, can put strain on your hair and cause it to fall out. Instead, opt for looser hairstyles that don't pull on your hair. If you do wear your hair in a tight hairstyle, make sure to take breaks and let your hair down frequently.

Hair Care FAQ

Q: Can stress cause hair loss?

A: Yes, stress can be a contributing factor to hair loss. When you're under stress, your body produces cortisol, which can interfere with hair growth. If you're experiencing hair loss, take steps to manage your stress, such as practicing yoga or meditation.

Q: Can diet affect hair loss?

A: Yes, diet can play a role in the health of your hair. Eating a balanced diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals, like iron and biotin, can help promote healthy hair growth. If you're experiencing hair loss, consider talking to a doctor or nutritionist about your diet.

Q: Should I brush my hair when it's wet?

A: No, it's best to avoid brushing your hair when it's wet. Wet hair is more prone to breakage, so it's better to wait until your hair is dry before brushing. If you need to detangle your hair when it's wet, use a wide-tooth comb or a brush with soft bristles.

Conclusion

Preventing hair loss in the shower doesn't have to be difficult. By taking a few simple steps, like choosing the right products and avoiding tight hairstyles, you can keep your hair healthy and reduce the amount of hair that falls out. If you're experiencing excessive hair loss, consider talking to a doctor or dermatologist for more personalized advice.
