How To Fix Brassy Orange Hair At Home A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Petter Jun 17, 2023 · 7 min read

If you've recently dyed your hair and ended up with brassy orange tones instead of the desired hair color, don't worry. It happens to the best of us. The good news is that you can fix brassy orange hair at home without damaging your hair further or spending a lot of money at the salon. In this article, we'll take you through the steps on how to fix brassy orange hair at home, and provide you with some tips and tricks to prevent this from happening in the future.

Description

Brassy orange hair is a common problem that occurs when you try to lighten your hair or dye it blonde. This happens because the hair color doesn't lift evenly, and some parts of your hair turn out to be brassy or orange. This can be frustrating, especially if you were going for a different color. However, fixing brassy orange hair is possible, and with the right techniques and products, you can get the color you want.

How to Fix Brassy Orange Hair at Home

Here are the steps you can take to fix brassy orange hair at home:

Step 1: Use a Purple Shampoo

Purple shampoo is a great way to tone down brassy orange tones in your hair. The purple pigments in the shampoo neutralize the orange tones and give your hair a more cool-toned look. You can use purple shampoo once or twice a week, depending on how brassy your hair is.

Step 2: Apply a Toner

A toner is another great way to fix brassy orange hair. Toners come in different shades, and you'll need to choose one that's opposite to the brassy tones in your hair. For example, if your hair is brassy orange, you'll need to choose a toner with blue or green pigments. Toners are easy to apply and can be done at home.

Step 3: Use a Hair Mask

Using a hair mask can help to restore moisture to your hair and prevent further damage. Look for a hair mask that's specifically formulated for color-treated hair, and leave it on for the recommended time. Hair masks can be used once a week to keep your hair healthy and shiny.

Tips

Here are some tips to prevent brassy orange hair:

  • Choose a hair dye that's suitable for your hair type and color
  • Do a strand test before dyeing your hair
  • Use a hair dye with cool tones if you have warm undertones
  • Don't leave the dye on for too long
  • Use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often

FAQ

Can I fix brassy orange hair without using toner?

Toners are the most effective way to fix brassy orange hair, but you can also use purple shampoo or a hair mask to tone down the brassiness.

How often should I use purple shampoo?

You can use purple shampoo once or twice a week, depending on how brassy your hair is. Don't use it too often, as it can dry out your hair.

Can I fix brassy orange hair without damaging my hair?

Yes, you can fix brassy orange hair without damaging your hair further. Use gentle products that are specifically formulated for color-treated hair, and avoid using hot tools on your hair too often.

Hair Care

It's important to take care of your hair after you've fixed the brassy orange tones. Here are some tips for healthy hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often
  • Eat a healthy diet with plenty of vitamins and minerals
  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends

By following these tips, you can maintain healthy, shiny hair and prevent brassy orange tones in the future.

Conclusion

Fixing brassy orange hair at home is possible, and with the right techniques and products, you can get the color you want without damaging your hair. Use a purple shampoo, toner, and hair mask to fix the brassiness, and follow our tips to prevent it from happening in the future. By taking care of your hair, you can maintain healthy, shiny locks that look great all year round.
