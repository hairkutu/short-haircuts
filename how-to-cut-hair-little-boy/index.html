How To Cut Hair Little Boy A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Sep 07, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you tired of paying a fortune for your little boy's haircuts? Do you want to learn how to cut hair little boy at home? You've come to the right place! In this blog post, we'll guide you through the process of cutting your little boy's hair from start to finish. We'll cover everything you need to know, from the tools you'll need to the techniques you'll use. So without further ado, let's get started!

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your little boy's hair, you'll need to gather some tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Scissors
  • Clippers
  • Comb
  • Haircutting cape or towel
  • Spray bottle
  • Clip or hair tie (if your little boy has longer hair)

Step by Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Little Boy's Hair

Start by washing your little boy's hair and towel drying it. You want the hair to be damp, but not soaking wet. This will make it easier to cut.

Step 2: Comb Out Any Tangles

Use a comb to gently comb out any tangles in your little boy's hair. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that the hair is even.

Step 3: Decide on the Length You Want

Decide on the length you want for your little boy's hair. If you're not sure, start with a longer length and work your way shorter. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Start cutting your little boy's hair using scissors or clippers. If you're using clippers, start with a longer guard and work your way shorter. If you're using scissors, start at the bottom and work your way up. Cut small sections at a time and be careful not to cut too much off at once.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Blend the hair by using a comb and scissors to remove any harsh lines. This will create a more natural look and ensure that the hair is even.

Step 6: Cut the Bangs

If your little boy has bangs, cut them last. Use scissors to cut the bangs to the desired length. Be sure to cut them straight across.

Step 7: Clean Up

Once you're done cutting, use a brush or vacuum to clean up any hair that's fallen on the floor or stuck to your little boy's clothes.

Tips

  • Start with a longer length and work your way shorter.
  • Use scissors for longer hair and clippers for shorter hair.
  • Cut small sections at a time.
  • Be patient and take your time.
  • Use a spray bottle to keep the hair damp while cutting.

FAQ

How Often Should I Cut My Little Boy's Hair?

It depends on how fast your little boy's hair grows. On average, most little boys need a haircut every 4-6 weeks.

What Do I Do If I Make a Mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic! It's just hair and it will grow back. You can try to fix the mistake or take your little boy to a professional to have it fixed.

Hair Care

After cutting your little boy's hair, be sure to wash it again to remove any loose hairs. You can also use a leave-in conditioner to keep the hair soft and manageable. Finally, be sure to trim your little boy's hair regularly to keep it looking neat and tidy.
