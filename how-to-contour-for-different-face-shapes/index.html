How To Contour For Different Face Shapes A Ultimate Guide

Written by Luffy Apr 29, 2023 · 8 min read

Contouring is a makeup technique that can transform your face by highlighting and shadowing certain areas. Whether you want to slim down your nose or define your cheekbones, contouring can help you achieve the look you desire. However, the key to successful contouring is understanding your face shape and knowing how to contour accordingly. In this guide, we’ll show you how to contour for different face shapes, including round, square, heart, and oval faces.

Description

Before we get started, let’s go over some basic terminology. Contouring involves using a darker shade of makeup to create shadows and a lighter shade to highlight certain areas. By doing this, you can create the illusion of depth and dimension on your face. The goal is to create a natural-looking shadow that blends seamlessly with your skin tone.

How to Contour for Different Face Shapes

Round Face

If you have a round face, you’ll want to focus on creating angles and definition. To do this, start by applying a darker shade of makeup along your hairline and jawline. Then, use a lighter shade to highlight your cheekbones and the center of your forehead. Blend everything together for a seamless look.

Square Face

If you have a square face, your goal is to soften your angles and create a more oval shape. Start by applying a darker shade of makeup along your hairline and jawline, but be sure to blend it out to create a softer look. Then, use a lighter shade to highlight the center of your forehead and the tops of your cheekbones.

Heart Face

If you have a heart-shaped face, your focus should be on creating balance between your forehead and chin. Start by applying a darker shade of makeup along your hairline and jawline, but be sure to blend it out towards your chin. Then, use a lighter shade to highlight your cheekbones and the center of your forehead.

Oval Face

If you have an oval face, you’re in luck – this face shape is considered the most balanced and versatile. To contour, start by applying a darker shade of makeup along your hairline and jawline. Then, use a lighter shade to highlight your cheekbones and the center of your forehead. Blend everything together for a seamless look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the basics, let’s go over a step-by-step guide to contouring:

  1. Start by applying your foundation and concealer as usual.
  2. Choose a darker shade of makeup that is one to two shades darker than your skin tone.
  3. Apply the darker shade along your hairline and jawline using a contour brush or sponge.
  4. Blend the darker shade out towards your cheeks using a blending brush or sponge.
  5. Apply the lighter shade of makeup to the tops of your cheekbones, the center of your forehead, and the bridge of your nose using a highlighting brush or sponge.
  6. Blend the lighter shade into the darker shade for a seamless look.
  7. Finish with a setting spray to lock your makeup in place.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the perfect contour:

  • Choose a matte finish for your contour makeup to create a natural-looking shadow.
  • Blend, blend, blend! The key to successful contouring is seamless blending.
  • Use a light hand when applying your makeup – it’s easier to add more than to take it away.
  • Practice makes perfect – don’t be afraid to experiment with different techniques and products.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about contouring:

What products do I need for contouring?

You’ll need a darker shade of makeup (such as a contour stick or powder) and a lighter shade of makeup (such as a highlighter or illuminator).

Can I contour every day?

Yes, you can contour every day if you want to. Just be sure to choose a natural-looking shade and blend everything together for a seamless look.

What if I make a mistake?

Don’t worry – mistakes happen! If you apply too much makeup, simply blend it out with a clean brush or sponge. If you apply the wrong shade, you can always start over with a clean slate.

Hair Care

Lastly, it’s important to take care of your hair when contouring. Make sure to tie your hair back or use a headband to keep it out of your face. You can also use a shower cap to protect your hair from any stray makeup.

Contouring can seem intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, anyone can do it. By following this guide, you can contour for different face shapes and achieve the look you desire. Remember to practice and have fun – after all, makeup should be a form of self-expression!
