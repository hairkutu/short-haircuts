How To Grow Out A Pixie Cut With Curly Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Daniel Jul 25, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you have curly hair and recently chopped off your locks into a pixie cut, you might be wondering how to grow it out without sacrificing your curls. Growing out a pixie cut can be a challenging process, but with the right approach and hair care routine, you can make the transition smoother and less stressful. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to grow out a pixie cut with curly hair.

Hair Care

Before we dive into the tips on growing out a pixie cut, it's important to establish a good hair care routine. Curly hair tends to be drier and more prone to breakage, so you need to make sure you're keeping it moisturized and healthy.

Here are some basic hair care tips for curly hair:

  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo once or twice a week.
  • Use a conditioner that's formulated for curly hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible.
  • Detangle your hair gently with a wide-tooth comb or your fingers.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to your damp hair to define your curls.

By taking care of your hair, you'll make the process of growing out a pixie cut with curly hair much easier.

How to Grow Out a Pixie Cut with Curly Hair

Growing out a pixie cut with curly hair can be a frustrating process, but it's important to be patient and stick to a plan. Here are some general steps to follow:

Step 1: Decide on a Haircut Plan

Before you start growing out your pixie cut, you need to decide on a haircut plan. This will help you avoid awkward stages and ensure that your hair grows out evenly. You can consult with a hairstylist to come up with a plan that works for your hair type and face shape.

Step 2: Be Patient

It's important to be patient when growing out a pixie cut with curly hair. It can take several months or even a year to grow out your hair to the desired length. Don't get discouraged if your hair doesn't grow as quickly as you'd like.

Step 3: Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trimming your hair regularly is essential when growing out a pixie cut. It helps to get rid of split ends and keep your hair healthy. You don't have to trim a lot of hair each time; even a quarter of an inch can make a difference.

Step 4: Experiment with Hairstyles

As your hair grows out, you can experiment with different hairstyles to keep it looking fresh and stylish. Try braids, updos, and other styles that work with your curly hair texture. You can also use accessories like headbands and hair clips to add some flair to your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to grow out a pixie cut with curly hair:

Step 1: Start with a Good Haircut

Before you start growing out your hair, get a good haircut that works with your hair type and face shape. This will help to ensure that your hair grows out evenly and avoids awkward stages.

Step 2: Be Patient

Be patient and don't expect your hair to grow out overnight. It can take several months or even a year to grow out a pixie cut.

Step 3: Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends and keep your hair healthy. Even a quarter of an inch can make a difference.

Step 4: Experiment with Hairstyles

Experiment with different hairstyles to keep your hair looking fresh and stylish. Try braids, updos, and other styles that work with your curly hair texture.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks for growing out a pixie cut with curly hair:

  • Use hair products that are specifically formulated for curly hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like straighteners and curling irons.
  • Consider getting a deep conditioning treatment once a month to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.

FAQ

Q: How long does it take to grow out a pixie cut with curly hair?

A: It can take several months or even a year to grow out a pixie cut with curly hair. It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows.

Q: Can I still curl my hair while growing out a pixie cut?

A: Yes, you can still curl your hair while growing out a pixie cut. Use a curl cream or mousse to define your curls and avoid using heat styling tools.

Q: How often should I trim my hair while growing out a pixie cut?

A: You should trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to get rid of split ends and keep your hair healthy.

Growing out a pixie cut with curly hair can be a challenging process, but with the right approach and hair care routine, you can make the transition smoother and less stressful. Remember to be patient and experiment with different hairstyles to keep your hair looking fresh and stylish. By following these tips and tricks, you'll be on your way to long, luscious curls in no time.
