How To Figure Out Your Face Shape For Glasses

Written by Eveline Aug 05, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Choosing the right pair of glasses can be a daunting task, especially if you're not sure what shape of glasses will flatter your face. But don't worry, we've got you covered! In this article, we'll walk you through step by step on how to figure out your face shape for glasses. We'll also provide you with tips on how to choose the perfect glasses for your face shape, and answer some frequently asked questions about glasses and face shapes.

Table of Contents

  • How to Figure Out Your Face Shape
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips on Choosing the Perfect Glasses
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Tips for Glasses Wearers

How to Figure Out Your Face Shape

The first step in choosing the right glasses is to figure out your face shape. There are six basic face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and triangular. To determine your face shape, follow these steps:

Step 1: Trace Your Face

Take a mirror and a lipstick, and stand in front of it. Pull your hair back tightly, and trace the outline of your face onto the mirror using the lipstick. Make sure your hand is steady and the line is smooth.

Step 2: Identify Your Face Shape

Compare the shape you traced on the mirror with the six basic face shapes. Look for the shape that most closely matches the outline you traced. If you're having difficulty deciding which shape your face falls into, ask a friend for their opinion.

Now that you've identified your face shape, it's time to choose the perfect pair of glasses!

Step by Step Guide

Now that you know your face shape, it's time to choose the perfect glasses. Here's a step by step guide:

Step 1: Determine Your Style

Before you start shopping for glasses, think about your personal style. Do you prefer classic, timeless designs or do you like to make a statement with bold, trendy frames? Knowing your style will help you narrow down your choices.

Step 2: Choose the Right Size

When it comes to glasses, size matters. Make sure the frame is proportional to your face. If you have a small face, choose smaller frames. If you have a larger face, choose larger frames.

Step 3: Consider Your Face Shape

Choose glasses that complement your face shape. For example, if you have an oval face, choose glasses with a strong bridge and rectangular frames. If you have a round face, choose glasses with angular frames to add definition to your features.

Step 4: Think About Your Skin Tone

Choose glasses that complement your skin tone. If you have warm undertones, choose glasses with warm colors like gold and brown. If you have cool undertones, choose glasses with cool colors like silver and black.

Tips on Choosing the Perfect Glasses

Here are some additional tips to help you choose the perfect glasses:

  • Try on as many pairs of glasses as you can to find the perfect fit.
  • Consider your lifestyle. If you're active, choose glasses that are durable and lightweight.
  • Make sure the glasses sit comfortably on your nose and ears.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and colors.

FAQ

What if I have a combination of face shapes?

It's common to have a combination of face shapes. In this case, choose glasses that complement the dominant shape of your face.

What if I have a narrow face?

If you have a narrow face, choose glasses with a strong bridge and rectangular frames to add width to your face.

What if I have a wide face?

If you have a wide face, choose glasses with a narrow bridge and round frames to balance out your features.

Hair Care Tips for Glasses Wearers

Wearing glasses can sometimes cause your hair to tangle or get caught in the frames. Here are some hair care tips for glasses wearers:

  • Choose a hairstyle that complements your glasses.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to prevent your hair from getting caught in the frames.
  • Avoid wearing your glasses on top of your head, as this can cause your hair to tangle.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.

Now that you know how to figure out your face shape for glasses, it's time to start shopping for the perfect pair! Remember to consider your personal style, face shape, and skin tone when choosing glasses, and don't be afraid to try on different styles and colors. With these tips, you'll be sure to find the perfect glasses that flatter your face and enhance your personal style.
