What Makes Babies Hair Fall Out

Written by Oliver Jul 15, 2023 Â· 6 min read

As a new parent, you may be concerned if you notice that your baby's hair is falling out. Rest assured, this is a common occurrence and there are several reasons why it may be happening. In this article, we will explore what causes babies' hair to fall out, how to prevent it, and how to take care of your baby's hair.

Table of Content

  • Causes of Baby Hair Loss
  • Prevention of Baby Hair Loss
  • Taking Care of Your Baby's Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions

Causes of Baby Hair Loss

One of the most common causes of baby hair loss is cradle cap. This is a condition where the scalp becomes dry and flaky, which can cause hair to fall out. Other causes of baby hair loss include:

  • Friction from rubbing against bedding or clothing
  • Pulling or twisting of the hair
  • Hormonal changes after birth
  • Iron deficiency
  • Genetics

If you notice that your baby's hair is falling out in clumps or there are bald patches, it is important to speak with your pediatrician to rule out any underlying medical conditions.

Prevention of Baby Hair Loss

While some causes of baby hair loss cannot be prevented, there are steps you can take to minimize the risk. Here are some tips:

  • Be gentle when washing your baby's hair
  • Use a soft brush or comb
  • Avoid tight hairstyles
  • Change your baby's bedding often
  • Provide proper nutrition to prevent iron deficiency

It is important to note that hair loss in babies is usually temporary and hair growth will resume on its own.

Taking Care of Your Baby's Hair

Even though your baby's hair may be falling out, it is still important to take care of it. Here are some tips:

  • Wash hair with a gentle baby shampoo
  • Use a soft brush or comb to avoid pulling on the hair
  • Avoid using hair products or styling tools
  • Keep your baby's scalp moisturized

Remember to be gentle when handling your baby's hair, as their scalp is delicate and sensitive.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is it normal for babies to lose hair?

A: Yes, it is normal for babies to lose hair during the first six months of life. This is due to hormonal changes and is usually temporary.

Q: Will my baby's hair grow back?

A: Yes, your baby's hair will grow back on its own. Hair loss in babies is usually temporary and hair growth resumes after a few months.

Q: Should I be concerned if my baby's hair is falling out in clumps?

A: Yes, if your baby's hair is falling out in clumps or there are bald patches, it is important to speak with your pediatrician to rule out any underlying medical conditions.

Q: Can cradle cap cause hair loss?

A: Yes, cradle cap can cause hair loss if the scalp becomes dry and flaky. However, this is usually temporary and hair growth resumes on its own.

Q: How often should I wash my baby's hair?

A: You should only wash your baby's hair once or twice a week. Washing too frequently can dry out their scalp and cause irritation.

In conclusion, baby hair loss is a common occurrence and usually temporary. By taking proper care of your baby's hair and speaking with your pediatrician if you notice any concerning symptoms, you can ensure that your baby's hair grows back healthy and strong.
