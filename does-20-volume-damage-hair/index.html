Does 20 Volume Damage Hair Get The Facts

Are you considering using 20 volume developer on your hair but worried about the potential damage it can cause? In this article, we'll dive into the science behind hair damage and answer the question: does 20 volume damage hair?

Table of Contents:

  • Description
  • How to Use 20 Volume Developer
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Minimizing Damage
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

Description

20 volume developer is a common strength used in hair dye and bleach. It contains 6% hydrogen peroxide, which helps to lift the hair's cuticle and allow for the color to penetrate. However, the use of hydrogen peroxide can also cause damage to the hair if not used properly.

When the hair's cuticle is lifted, it leaves the cortex exposed, which can lead to breakage, split ends, and dryness. The amount of damage caused by 20 volume developer depends on various factors, such as the hair's health, porosity, and previous damage.

How to Use 20 Volume Developer

It's important to follow the instructions on the hair dye or bleach product you are using, as the amount of developer needed can vary. In general, the ratio of developer to hair dye or bleach is 1:1.5 or 1:2. This means that for every ounce of hair dye or bleach, you'll need 1.5 to 2 ounces of 20 volume developer.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a simple guide to using 20 volume developer:

  1. Mix the hair dye or bleach with the developer according to the instructions on the product.
  2. Apply the mixture to the hair, starting at the roots and working your way to the ends.
  3. Leave the mixture on for the recommended amount of time, usually 30 to 45 minutes.
  4. Rinse the hair thoroughly with cool water and apply a conditioner.

Tips for Minimizing Damage

To minimize the damage caused by 20 volume developer, follow these tips:

  • Do a strand test before applying the mixture to your entire head.
  • Avoid overlapping the bleach or dye on previously treated hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment after using 20 volume developer.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on the hair immediately after using 20 volume developer.

FAQs

1. Can 20 volume developer be used on all hair types?

Yes, 20 volume developer can be used on all hair types. However, it may cause more damage to already damaged or porous hair.

2. Can 20 volume developer be used to lighten black hair?

Yes, 20 volume developer can be used to lighten black hair, but it may take multiple sessions to achieve the desired result.

3. How often can I use 20 volume developer on my hair?

It's best to wait at least 4-6 weeks between uses of 20 volume developer to give your hair time to recover.

Hair Care Tips

To keep your hair healthy after using 20 volume developer, follow these hair care tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on a daily basis.
  • Trim the ends of your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioning treatment once a week.

So, does 20 volume damage hair? The answer is yes, it can cause damage if not used properly. However, by following the instructions on the product and taking care of your hair after using 20 volume developer, you can minimize the damage and achieve beautiful results.
