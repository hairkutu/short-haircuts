How To Cover Gray Hair In Braids A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Sep 05, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Gray hair is a natural part of aging, and many people choose to embrace it. However, some people prefer to cover their gray hair, especially when they are getting braids. If you're looking for a way to cover your gray hair in braids, you've come to the right place. In this article, we'll give you a step-by-step guide on how to cover gray hair in braids, as well as some tips and hair care advice to help you maintain your braids.

  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Cover Gray Hair in Braids

Covering gray hair in braids is a simple process that requires a few extra steps. The key is to make sure that the hair is properly prepped and that you choose the right braiding hair color to match your natural hair color.

Choose the Right Braiding Hair Color

The first step to covering gray hair in braids is to choose the right braiding hair color. You want to choose a color that matches your natural hair color as closely as possible. If your hair is mostly gray, you may want to choose a darker color to help cover the gray.

Prep Your Hair

Before you start braiding, it's important to prep your hair. This means washing and conditioning your hair thoroughly to remove any dirt, oil, or product buildup. You should also detangle your hair using a wide-tooth comb or your fingers.

Once your hair is clean and detangled, you may want to apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to help moisturize your hair.

Braid Your Hair

Now it's time to start braiding your hair. You can use any braiding technique that you prefer, such as box braids, cornrows, or twists. When braiding, make sure to use the braiding hair color that you chose earlier.

As you braid your hair, make sure to cover your gray hair as much as possible. You can do this by making sure that the braids are tight and close together. You may also want to use a crochet hook to pull your natural hair through the braids to help cover any gray hairs.

Secure Your Braids

Once you've finished braiding your hair, you'll need to secure the ends of the braids. You can do this by using a rubber band or by burning the ends of the braids with a lighter (if you're using synthetic hair).

Now that you know how to cover gray hair in braids, let's take a look at a step-by-step guide to help you through the process.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to help you cover gray hair in braids:

  1. Choose the right braiding hair color.
  2. Prep your hair by washing, conditioning, and detangling it.
  3. Braid your hair using your preferred braiding technique.
  4. Make sure to cover your gray hair as much as possible.
  5. Use a crochet hook to pull your natural hair through the braids to help cover any gray hairs.
  6. Secure the ends of the braids with a rubber band or by burning them with a lighter (if you're using synthetic hair).

Tips for Covering Gray Hair in Braids

Now that you know how to cover gray hair in braids, here are some tips to help you achieve the best results:

  • Choose a braiding hair color that matches your natural hair color as closely as possible.
  • Make sure to properly prep your hair before braiding it.
  • Use a crochet hook to help cover any gray hairs.
  • Make sure that your braids are tight and close together to help cover your gray hair.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to help moisturize your hair.

FAQs

Can I use regular hair dye to cover my gray hair before getting braids?

No, it's not recommended to use regular hair dye on your hair before getting braids. This can cause damage to your hair and scalp, and it may also affect the longevity of your braids.

How often should I wash my braids?

You should wash your braids every 2-3 weeks to help maintain their cleanliness and to prevent buildup.

Hair Care Tips for Braids

Here are some hair care tips to help you maintain your braids:

  • Wash your braids every 2-3 weeks.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to help moisturize your hair.
  • Avoid using heavy products or oils on your braids, as this can cause buildup and make your braids look dull.
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on your braids, as this can cause breakage and damage to your hair.

Now that you know how to cover gray hair in braids, you can confidently get your hair braided without worrying about your gray hair showing. By following these tips and hair care advice, you can help your braids last longer and look beautiful.
