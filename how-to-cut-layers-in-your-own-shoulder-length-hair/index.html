How To Cut Layers In Your Own Shoulder Length Hair

Written by Bardi May 08, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of your shoulder length hair looking flat and lifeless? Adding layers can give your hair a new dimension and make it look more voluminous. But going to a salon can be expensive, and you might not have the time to go. Don't worry, you can learn how to cut layers in your own shoulder length hair. In this blog post, we will guide you through the process step by step.

  2. How to Cut Layers in Your Own Shoulder Length Hair

Description

Layered hair has been a popular trend for many years. It's a great way to add volume, texture, and movement to your hair. But going to a salon to get your hair layered can be expensive, and you might not have the time or money to go. That's why we're going to show you how to cut layers in your own shoulder length hair. With just a few tools and some patience, you can achieve a salon-worthy look from the comfort of your own home.

How to Cut Layers in Your Own Shoulder Length Hair

The first step in cutting layers in your own shoulder length hair is to gather your tools. You will need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips or hair ties

Once you have these tools, you can start the process of layering your hair.

Step by Step

Follow these steps to cut layers in your own shoulder length hair:

  1. Wash and dry your hair. Make sure that your hair is completely dry before you start cutting.
  2. Divide your hair into sections. Use clips or hair ties to separate your hair into as many sections as you want layers.
  3. Start with the bottom layer. Take one section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold it between your fingers, and cut the hair at an angle. Repeat this step for all the sections in the bottom layer.
  4. Move on to the next layer. Take another section of hair from the layer above the one you just cut. Pull it straight down and cut it at an angle, making sure that it is shorter than the layer below it. Repeat this step for all the sections in this layer.
  5. Repeat steps 3 and 4 for all the remaining layers.
  6. Blend the layers. Comb through your hair to make sure that the layers are all blended together. If you notice any unevenness, you can go back and trim any sections that are too long.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting layers in your own shoulder length hair:

  • Start with small cuts. You can always cut more hair off, but you can't put it back on.
  • Make sure that your scissors are sharp. Dull scissors can damage your hair and create split ends.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your hair. It's important to make sure that your layers are even all around your head.
  • Take your time. Cutting your own hair can be nerve-wracking, but rushing can lead to mistakes.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting layers in your own shoulder length hair:

Can I cut my hair wet or dry?

You should always cut your hair when it's dry. Wet hair can be deceiving and can lead to uneven cuts.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Can I cut my hair into a certain style?

If you're not a professional, it's best to stick to simple layers. Cutting your hair into a specific style can be tricky and requires more skill.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and style. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that is appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet and drink plenty of water to promote hair growth and health.

Now that you know how to cut layers in your own shoulder length hair, you can give yourself a new look without leaving your home. Remember to be patient and take your time, and you'll end up with beautiful, layered hair.
