What To Do For Broken Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by William May 22, 2023 · 9 min read

If you're dealing with broken hair, you're not alone. Whether it's from heat styling, chemical treatments, or just general wear and tear, many people experience hair breakage at some point in their lives. But the good news is that there are plenty of things you can do to help repair and prevent further damage to your hair. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about what to do for broken hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Repair Broken Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Preventing Hair Breakage
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Healthy Hair

Description

Broken hair can be caused by a variety of factors, including heat styling, chemical treatments, and even rough brushing or combing. When hair becomes damaged, it can become brittle, dry, and prone to breakage. This can lead to split ends, frizz, and an overall unhealthy appearance. Fortunately, there are many things you can do to help repair broken hair and prevent further damage.

How to Repair Broken Hair

When it comes to repairing broken hair, the key is to focus on nourishing and strengthening your strands. Here are some of the most effective ways to do this:

1. Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

A deep conditioning treatment can help to nourish and hydrate your hair, which can help to prevent breakage. Look for a treatment that contains ingredients like keratin, biotin, and vitamin E, which can help to strengthen and protect your strands.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers can cause a lot of damage to your hair, especially if you use them frequently. Try to avoid heat styling as much as possible, and when you do use these tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray first.

3. Get Regular Trims

Regular trims can help to prevent split ends and breakage, so be sure to visit your stylist every 6-8 weeks for a trim. This will help to keep your hair healthy and prevent damage from spreading up the hair shaft.

4. Use a Protein Treatment

Protein treatments can help to strengthen your hair and prevent breakage. Look for a treatment that contains hydrolyzed keratin, collagen, or silk protein, which can help to rebuild and strengthen your strands.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to repairing broken hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo. Be sure to massage your scalp and work the shampoo through your hair to remove any buildup or debris.

Step 2: Apply a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair, focusing on the ends and any areas that are particularly damaged. Leave the treatment on for the recommended amount of time (usually 5-10 minutes), then rinse thoroughly.

Step 3: Use a Protein Treatment

Apply a protein treatment to your hair, following the instructions on the package. Be sure to focus on the areas of your hair that are most damaged. Leave the treatment on for the recommended amount of time, then rinse thoroughly.

Step 4: Avoid Heat Styling

Try to avoid heat styling as much as possible while you're repairing your hair. If you do need to use heat tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray first.

Step 5: Get Regular Trims

Visit your stylist every 6-8 weeks for a trim to prevent split ends and breakage.

Tips for Preventing Hair Breakage

Here are some tips for preventing hair breakage:

1. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush, which can be too harsh and cause breakage.

2. Be Gentle

Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair, and avoid pulling or tugging on your strands.

3. Use a Silk Pillowcase

Switch to a silk or satin pillowcase, which can help to prevent breakage and frizz.

4. Protect Your Hair Overnight

If you have long hair, consider putting it in a loose braid or bun overnight to prevent tangles and breakage.

FAQs

Q: Can broken hair be repaired?

A: Yes, broken hair can be repaired with the right treatment and care.

Q: How long does it take to repair broken hair?

A: It can take several weeks or even months to fully repair broken hair, depending on the severity of the damage.

Hair Care for Healthy Hair

In addition to repairing broken hair, it's important to take care of your hair on a daily basis to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some tips for healthy hair:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.

2. Use a Conditioner

Use a conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and moisturized.

3. Eat a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet with plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals can promote healthy hair growth.

4. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Wear a hat or use a UV protectant spray to protect your hair from the damaging effects of the sun.

By following these tips and taking care of your hair, you can help to prevent and repair broken hair.
