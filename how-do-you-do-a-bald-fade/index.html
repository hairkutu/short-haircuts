How To Do A Bald Fade A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 07, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking for a sharp and clean haircut, a bald fade might be the perfect option for you. This style is versatile and can work for any hair type or texture. In this guide, we'll go over the steps to achieve a bald fade, as well as some tips and hair care advice. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Do a Bald Fade
  • Tips for a Perfect Bald Fade
  • FAQs about Bald Fades
  • Hair Care for Bald Fades

How to Do a Bald Fade

A bald fade is a gradual haircut that fades from a longer length at the top to a shorter length on the sides and back until the hair disappears completely. Here's how to achieve this style:

Step 1: Choose Your Clipper Size

Start by selecting the right clipper size. For a bald fade, you'll need three different clipper sizes: a longer one for the top, a medium one for the middle, and a shorter one for the bottom. For example, you might use a #4 clipper for the top, a #2 for the middle, and a #1 for the bottom.

Step 2: Start with the Top

Begin by cutting the top of your hair to the desired length. Use the longer clipper size you selected and cut the hair evenly across the top of your head.

Step 3: Move to the Sides

Next, use the medium clipper size to start fading the sides. Start at the hairline and work your way up, gradually blending the longer hair on top with the shorter hair on the sides.

Step 4: Use the Shortest Clipper Size at the Bottom

Finally, use the shortest clipper size to fade the hair at the bottom of your head. This will create a seamless transition from the longer hair on top to the completely shaved hair at the bottom.

Step 5: Refine and Blend

After you've completed the main steps, go back and refine any areas that need more blending or trimming. Use your clippers or scissors to create a smooth and even look.

Tips for a Perfect Bald Fade

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when doing a bald fade:

  • Use a comb to help guide your clippers and create even lines
  • Start with a higher clipper size and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair
  • Take your time and make small, gradual adjustments rather than trying to fix a mistake all at once
  • Don't forget to blend the hair at the back of your head for a seamless look

FAQs about Bald Fades

What hair types are best for a bald fade?

A bald fade can work for any hair type or texture. However, it's important to consider your hairline and any bald spots before deciding on this style.

How often should I touch up my bald fade?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how sharp you want your haircut to look. On average, you might touch up your bald fade every 2-4 weeks.

Can I do a bald fade on my own?

While it's possible to do a bald fade at home, it can be difficult to achieve a professional-looking result. We recommend going to a licensed barber or stylist for the best results.

Hair Care for Bald Fades

Once you've achieved your perfect bald fade, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the look. Here are some tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your scalp and hair hydrated
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair looking healthy and shiny
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers or straighteners, as these can damage your hair and scalp
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your bald fade

With these tips and steps, you'll be able to achieve a sharp and clean bald fade. Remember to take your time and be patient, and don't hesitate to ask a professional for help if needed.
