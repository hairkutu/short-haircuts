How To Start Locs On Short Natural Hair A Step By Step Guide

Starting locs on short natural hair can seem like a daunting task, but with the right tools and knowledge, it can be a simple and rewarding process. In this guide, we will walk you through the steps to start locs on short natural hair, including tips for maintaining healthy locs and answers to frequently asked questions.

How to Start Locs on Short Natural Hair

Before starting your locs, it is important to understand what they are and how they form. Locs are a hairstyle that results from the natural matting and tangling of hair. They can be started on any length of hair, but starting them on short natural hair can be a bit more challenging.

The first step in starting locs on short natural hair is to decide on the method you will use. There are two main methods: the freeform method and the palm rolling method. The freeform method allows your hair to naturally form into locs without any manipulation, while the palm rolling method involves using your palms to roll your hair into locs.

Freeform Method

To start locs using the freeform method, all you need to do is stop combing or brushing your hair. Over time, your hair will begin to form into locs naturally. This method is great for those who want a more organic and low-maintenance look.

Palm Rolling Method

The palm rolling method involves using your palms to roll your hair into locs. Here are the steps:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Section your hair into small sections.
  3. Apply a small amount of styling gel to each section.
  4. Using your palms, roll each section of hair in a clockwise motion until it begins to form into a loc.
  5. Repeat this process on each section of hair.
  6. After you have rolled each section, allow your hair to air dry.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to starting locs on short natural hair:

  1. Decide on the method you will use: freeform or palm rolling.
  2. Start with clean, dry hair.
  3. Section your hair into small sections.
  4. If using the palm rolling method, apply a small amount of styling gel to each section.
  5. If using the palm rolling method, roll each section of hair in a clockwise motion until it begins to form into a loc.
  6. Repeat the rolling process on each section of hair.
  7. After you have rolled each section, allow your hair to air dry.
  8. Be patient! It can take several months for your locs to fully form.
  9. Continue to care for your locs using the tips below.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Locs

Here are some tips for maintaining healthy locs:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep your locs clean and healthy.
  • Use a residue-free shampoo and avoid using heavy oils and products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Avoid using a hair dryer or heat on your locs.
  • Retwist your locs regularly to keep them looking neat.
  • Protect your locs at night by wearing a silk or satin scarf or bonnet.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about starting locs on short natural hair:

How long does it take for locs to form?

It can take several months for your locs to fully form. Be patient and continue to care for your hair to encourage healthy loc formation.

Can I start locs on relaxed hair?

Yes, you can start locs on relaxed hair. However, it may take longer for your locs to form and you may need to be more gentle with your hair to avoid breakage.

Do I need to use special products for my locs?

No, you do not need to use special products for your locs. However, it is important to use a residue-free shampoo and avoid heavy oils and products that can weigh down your hair.

Hair Care for Locs

Caring for your locs is essential for maintaining healthy and beautiful hair. Here are some tips for caring for your locs:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using heavy oils and products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Retwist your locs regularly to keep them looking neat.
  • Protect your locs at night by wearing a silk or satin scarf or bonnet.
  • Avoid using a hair dryer or heat on your locs.
  • Avoid using too much tension on your hair, as this can cause breakage.

Starting locs on short natural hair can be a simple and rewarding process with the right tools and knowledge. Whether you choose the freeform or palm rolling method, be patient and continue to care for your hair to encourage healthy loc formation.
