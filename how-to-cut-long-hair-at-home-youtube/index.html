How To Cut Long Hair At Home Youtube A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Jul 30, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you're tired of spending a fortune at the salon and want to learn how to cut long hair at home, YouTube is a great resource to turn to. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut without ever leaving your house. In this article, we'll walk you through the process of cutting long hair at home using YouTube tutorials.

Description

Cutting long hair at home can be intimidating, but it's actually easier than you might think. By following a few simple steps and watching YouTube tutorials, you can achieve a salon-worthy haircut in the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we'll cover everything you need to know to get started, from selecting the right tools to finishing touches.

How to Cut Long Hair at Home YouTube

The first step to cutting long hair at home is to find a YouTube tutorial that suits your needs. There are thousands of videos available, so take your time to find one that speaks to you. Look for a tutorial that matches your hair type, length, and desired style.

Once you've found a tutorial you like, gather your tools. You'll need a pair of sharp hair-cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair. It's also a good idea to have a spray bottle filled with water to keep your hair damp while cutting.

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Comb through your hair to remove any tangles or knots, and then section it off into manageable pieces. Use hair clips to secure each section.

Follow the instructions in your YouTube tutorial to cut your hair. It's important to go slowly and carefully, making small cuts at a time. Remember that you can always cut more hair off, but you can't put it back on! Take breaks as needed to rest your hands and check your progress.

Once you've finished cutting, remove the hair clips and style your hair as desired. You may want to blow-dry or flat iron your hair to achieve a polished look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Find a YouTube tutorial that matches your hair type, length, and desired style.

Take your time to find a tutorial that speaks to you. Watch the video several times to make sure you understand the steps.

Step 2: Gather your tools.

You'll need a pair of sharp hair-cutting scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a spray bottle filled with water.

Step 3: Prep your hair.

Step 4: Follow the instructions in your YouTube tutorial to cut your hair.

Cut your hair slowly and carefully, making small cuts at a time. Take breaks as needed to rest your hands and check your progress.

Step 5: Style your hair.

Remove the hair clips and style your hair as desired. You may want to blow-dry or flat iron your hair to achieve a polished look.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you get the best results when cutting long hair at home:

  • Invest in a good pair of hair-cutting scissors. Cheap, dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to achieve a clean cut.
  • Watch several YouTube tutorials before you start cutting to get a better understanding of the process.
  • Work in a well-lit area with a clean, flat surface to cut your hair on.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you're struggling to cut the back of your head. It can be tricky to do on your own.
  • Take your time and don't rush the process. It's better to make small cuts and check your progress frequently.

FAQ

Hair Care

After you've cut your hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it looking healthy and shiny. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and flat irons too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using hot tools.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently cut your long hair at home using YouTube tutorials. Remember to take your time and go slow, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. Happy cutting!
