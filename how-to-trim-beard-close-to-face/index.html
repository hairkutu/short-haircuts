How To Trim Your Beard Close To Your Face A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Jun 10, 2023 · 8 min read

Keeping your beard looking neat and tidy is an essential part of any good grooming routine. However, achieving a close and precise trim can be challenging, especially when it comes to trimming your beard close to your face. In this article, we’ll be sharing some tips and tricks on how to trim your beard close to your face, so you can achieve the perfect look every time.

Table of Contents

  • How to Trim Your Beard Close to Your Face
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for a Perfect Trim
  • FAQs
  • Beard Hair Care

How to Trim Your Beard Close to Your Face

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, it’s essential to understand the tools you’ll need. Firstly, invest in a good quality trimmer with adjustable settings to ensure you can achieve the desired length. A pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a beard oil or balm also come in handy for a smooth and precise trim.

Now, let’s get into the steps to achieve a close and sleek trim:

Step 1: Prepare Your Beard

Start by washing your beard with warm water and a mild cleanser to remove any dirt or debris. Dry your beard with a towel and comb it in the direction of your hair growth to detangle any knots or tangles.

Step 2: Choose Your Trimming Length

Decide on the length you want your beard to be and adjust your trimmer setting accordingly. Start with a longer setting and gradually decrease the length until you achieve the desired look.

Step 3: Trim Your Neckline

Using a trimmer or razor, shave the hair below your Adam’s apple to create a defined neckline. Start by outlining the neckline with a comb and trim the hair below it in a downward motion to avoid creating any harsh lines.

Step 4: Trim Your Cheek Line

Using the comb as a guide, trim the hair above your cheek line in an upward motion. Be sure to follow your natural hairline and avoid trimming too high, as this can create an unnatural look.

Step 5: Trim Your Mustache and Goatee

Use scissors to trim any stray hairs in your mustache and goatee area. Comb the hair upward and snip any hairs that stick out above the rest. Be sure to cut in small increments and check your progress regularly to avoid any mistakes.

Step-by-Step Guide

To make the trimming process even easier, follow these step-by-step instructions:

  1. Wash and dry your beard
  2. Comb your beard in the direction of hair growth
  3. Choose your desired trimming length and adjust your trimmer settings accordingly
  4. Trim your neckline below your Adam’s apple
  5. Trim your cheek line using a comb as a guide
  6. Trim your mustache and goatee area with scissors
  7. Apply beard oil or balm to moisturize and add shine

Tips for a Perfect Trim

Here are some additional tips to ensure your beard trim is perfect:

  • Trim your beard when it’s dry, as wet hair appears longer than it actually is
  • Use a mirror to check your progress regularly
  • Use a light hand and cut in small increments to avoid mistakes
  • Don’t forget to trim your sideburns to match the length of your beard
  • Moisturize your beard regularly to keep it healthy and prevent breakage

FAQs

How often should I trim my beard?

You should trim your beard every two to four weeks to maintain its shape and length.

Can I trim my beard with scissors?

Yes, scissors can be used to trim your beard, but it’s essential to use a sharp pair and cut in small increments to avoid mistakes.

Should I use beard oil or balm?

Both beard oil and balm can be used to moisturize and add shine to your beard. However, beard balm provides a stronger hold than oil and can be used to style your beard.

Beard Hair Care

Trimming your beard is only one part of a good grooming routine. To keep your beard looking and feeling its best, it’s essential to take care of it with a few simple steps:

  • Wash your beard with a mild cleanser to remove any dirt or debris
  • Moisturize your beard with beard oil or balm to prevent breakage and add shine
  • Brush or comb your beard regularly to detangle any knots or tangles
  • Avoid using hot water or harsh chemicals on your beard, as this can cause damage and dryness

By following these simple steps, you can keep your beard looking its best and achieve a close and precise trim every time.
