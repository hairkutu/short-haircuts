How Do You Do A Low Fade Haircut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jul 12, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of your old hairstyle and looking for a change? A low fade haircut might just be what you need. This haircut has become increasingly popular in recent years and is a great way to freshen up your look. In this article, we will guide you through the process of how to do a low fade haircut.

Description

A low fade haircut is a type of haircut where the hair gradually tapers down to the skin, starting from the temple area and ending at the back of the head. This haircut is suitable for all hair types and is a perfect choice for those who want a stylish yet low-maintenance haircut.

How To

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to do a low fade haircut:

Step 1: Prepare the Hair

Before starting the haircut, the hair should be washed and dried. This will make it easier to work with and will give you a better finish.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Using a comb, section the hair into two parts: the top and the bottom. The top part will be clipped away to make it easier to work with the bottom part.

Step 3: Start the Fade

Using clippers with a guard, start the fade at the temple area. The guard size will depend on the desired length of the fade. A good starting point is a #3 guard. Work your way around the head, gradually decreasing the guard size to create a smooth transition from the longer hair to the shorter hair.

Step 4: Blend the Fade

Once you have reached the desired length, remove the guard and blend the fade using an upward motion with the clippers. This will create a seamless transition between the faded hair and the longer hair on top.

Step 5: Cut the Top

Unclip the top section of the hair and cut it to the desired length. This can be done using scissors or clippers, depending on the desired look.

Step 6: Blend the Top

Using scissors or clippers, blend the top section of the hair with the faded hair. This will create a seamless transition between the two sections.

Step 7: Finish the Haircut

Once you have finished cutting and blending the hair, style it as desired using a hair product.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when doing a low fade haircut:

  • Use a comb and a pair of scissors to blend the hair for a more natural look.
  • Take your time and work slowly to avoid any mistakes.
  • Experiment with different guard sizes to find the perfect length for your fade.
  • Use a mirror to check your work from different angles.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about low fade haircuts:

Q: Can I do a low fade haircut at home?

A: Yes, you can do a low fade haircut at home if you have the right tools and some experience with haircuts. However, if you are unsure, it is always best to go to a professional hairstylist.

Q: How often should I get a low fade haircut?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how long you want to keep the haircut. On average, a low fade haircut should be done every 4-6 weeks.

Q: Can I style my hair differently after a low fade haircut?

A: Yes, a low fade haircut is versatile and can be styled in many ways. You can use hair products like pomade, wax, or gel to achieve different styles.

Hair Care

Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy and looking great:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using hot tools like hair dryers and straighteners too often.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth.

Now that you know how to do a low fade haircut, you can try it out for yourself. Remember to take your time and be patient, and you will end up with a stylish and trendy new look.
