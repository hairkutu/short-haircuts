How To Cut Your Own Fringe At Home Uk

Written by William Apr 18, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the hair salon just to maintain your fringe? Cutting your own fringe at home is a great way to save money and keep your hair looking fresh. In this article, we will provide you with step-by-step instructions on how to cut your own fringe at home in the UK, as well as tips for maintaining your new look.

How to Cut Your Own Fringe at Home UK

To start, gather the necessary tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and clips to section off your hair. It's also a good idea to have a spray bottle filled with water to help keep your hair damp.

Step 1: Determine Your Desired Length

Before you start cutting, decide on the length of your fringe. Use a ruler or measuring tape to make sure both sides are even. It's always better to start longer and gradually cut more if needed.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use clips to section off your hair, leaving only the fringe area exposed. This will help you avoid accidentally cutting the wrong section of hair.

Step 3: Dampen Your Hair

Spray your fringe with water to help keep it damp and easier to work with. This will also help prevent split ends.

Step 4: Cut Your Fringe

Start cutting your fringe from the center and work your way outwards. Use the comb to help guide your scissors and make sure you are cutting straight. Remember to cut small sections at a time and constantly check your progress in the mirror.

Step 5: Check for Evenness

Once you have finished cutting, check your fringe in the mirror to make sure it is even. If necessary, make small adjustments by trimming off any uneven pieces.

Tips for Maintaining Your Fringe

Now that you have successfully cut your own fringe at home, here are some tips for maintaining your new look:

  • Trim your fringe every 4-6 weeks to keep it looking fresh.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using any heating tools such as straighteners or curling irons.
  • Consider using dry shampoo to help keep your fringe looking clean in between washes.
  • Avoid cutting your fringe too short, as it may take longer to grow back if you are unhappy with the results.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own fringe at home in the UK:

Can I cut my fringe if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can still cut your own fringe if you have curly hair. Just make sure to cut it when it is dry to avoid any surprises.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always adjust your fringe by trimming it shorter or styling it differently. And remember, hair grows back!

Do I need to use special scissors?

You don't necessarily need special scissors, but it's important to use sharp ones to avoid damaging your hair.

Hair Care

After cutting your own fringe at home, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heating tools too often, as they can cause damage and split ends.
  • Consider getting a trim every 8-12 weeks to maintain healthy hair.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage.

Cutting your own fringe at home in the UK can be a fun and cost-effective way to change up your look. Just remember to take your time, use sharp scissors, and follow our tips for maintaining healthy hair. Happy cutting!
