How To Treat A Bald Spot From Braids

Written by Eveline Jun 05, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you've ever had braids, then you know how they can sometimes lead to hair loss or thinning in certain areas of your scalp. This is known as a bald spot from braids, and it can be frustrating and embarrassing. However, there are ways to treat and prevent bald spots from braids. In this article, we'll go over how to treat a bald spot from braids in easy-to-understand language.

Table of Contents

  • How to Identify a Bald Spot from Braids
  • How to Treat a Bald Spot from Braids
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Treating a Bald Spot from Braids
  • FAQ about Bald Spots from Braids
  • Hair Care for Preventing Bald Spots from Braids

How to Identify a Bald Spot from Braids

A bald spot from braids is usually a small, round patch of hair loss on your scalp. It's often caused by the tension and weight of the braids pulling on your hair follicles. You may notice the bald spot after you remove your braids or after a few weeks of wearing them.

Some common signs of a bald spot from braids include:

  • A circular or oval-shaped patch of hair loss
  • An itchy or irritated scalp
  • Redness or inflammation around the bald spot
  • Thinning hair around the bald spot

How to Treat a Bald Spot from Braids

There are several ways to treat a bald spot from braids, including:

  • Applying topical treatments
  • Massaging your scalp
  • Taking supplements
  • Using hair growth products
  • Seeing a dermatologist

Applying Topical Treatments

Topical treatments can help stimulate hair growth and reduce inflammation on your scalp. Some popular topical treatments for bald spots from braids include:

  • Aloe vera gel
  • Jamaican black castor oil
  • Peppermint oil
  • Tea tree oil

To use a topical treatment, apply a small amount to your bald spot and massage it into your scalp. Do this once or twice a day for best results.

Massaging Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can improve blood flow to your hair follicles and stimulate hair growth. Use your fingertips to gently massage your scalp for a few minutes each day.

Taking Supplements

Taking supplements can help support healthy hair growth. Some supplements that may be helpful for treating bald spots from braids include:

  • Biotin
  • Vitamin D
  • Iron
  • Zinc

Talk to your doctor before taking any new supplements.

Using Hair Growth Products

There are many over-the-counter hair growth products that can help stimulate hair growth and reduce inflammation on your scalp. Look for products that contain ingredients like minoxidil, saw palmetto, or ketoconazole.

Seeing a Dermatologist

If your bald spot from braids doesn't improve with at-home treatments, it's a good idea to see a dermatologist. They can help diagnose the cause of your hair loss and recommend the best treatment plan for you.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide for treating a bald spot from braids:

  1. Identify the bald spot
  2. Apply a topical treatment
  3. Massage your scalp
  4. Take supplements
  5. Use hair growth products
  6. See a dermatologist if necessary

Tips for Treating a Bald Spot from Braids

Here are some additional tips for treating a bald spot from braids:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair
  • Use a satin or silk scarf when sleeping
  • Wash your hair regularly
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair
  • Eat a healthy diet

FAQ about Bald Spots from Braids

How long does it take for a bald spot from braids to grow back?

It can take several weeks or even months for a bald spot from braids to grow back, depending on the severity of the hair loss. Consistent treatment and good hair care practices can help speed up the growth process.

Can you prevent bald spots from braids?

Yes, you can prevent bald spots from braids by avoiding tight hairstyles that pull on your hair, using a satin or silk scarf when sleeping, and practicing good hair care habits.

Hair Care for Preventing Bald Spots from Braids

To prevent bald spots from braids, it's important to practice good hair care habits. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair
  • Use a satin or silk scarf when sleeping
  • Wash your hair regularly
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair
  • Eat a healthy diet

By following these tips and treating any bald spots from braids as soon as they appear, you can maintain healthy hair and avoid further hair loss.
