How To Sleep Comfortably With Tape In Extensions A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver May 15, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're a fan of tape in extensions, you know just how transformative they can be for your hair. Not only do they add length and volume, but they also give you the opportunity to experiment with different styles and colors. However, one of the challenges of having tape in extensions is figuring out how to sleep comfortably with them. In this article, we'll provide a step-by-step guide on how to do just that.

Table of Contents

  • How to Sleep Comfortably with Tape In Extensions
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How to Sleep Comfortably with Tape In Extensions

The first thing you'll want to do is make sure your extensions are secure. If they're loose, they can get tangled or even fall out while you're sleeping. You can use a silk scarf or a sleep cap to help keep your extensions in place.

Another important factor to consider is your pillowcase. Cotton pillowcases can cause friction and tangles, which can damage your extensions. Instead, opt for a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and keep your extensions smooth.

You'll also want to avoid sleeping with wet hair. Wet hair is more prone to tangling and can cause your extensions to become matted. Make sure your hair is completely dry before going to bed.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to sleep comfortably with tape in extensions:

  1. Make sure your extensions are secure.
  2. Use a silk or satin pillowcase.
  3. Avoid sleeping with wet hair.
  4. Brush your hair before bed.
  5. Create a loose braid or bun.
  6. Use a sleep cap or silk scarf to keep your extensions in place.
  7. Avoid sleeping on your back or stomach.
  8. Use a travel pillow or cushion to support your head and neck.
  9. Don't toss and turn too much.
  10. Take care when removing your sleep cap or scarf in the morning.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to help you sleep comfortably with tape in extensions:

  • Avoid using heavy products on your extensions before bed. This can weigh them down and make them more prone to tangling.
  • Use a detangling spray or serum to help keep your extensions smooth.
  • Try sleeping with a silk or satin bonnet instead of a scarf or sleep cap.
  • If you're a restless sleeper, consider using a sleep tracker to monitor your movements at night.
  • If you have trouble sleeping with your extensions, try taking them out before bed and reapplying them in the morning.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about sleeping with tape in extensions:

Q: Can I sleep with my extensions down?

A: It's not recommended to sleep with your extensions down. This can cause them to tangle and become damaged.

Q: Can I use a regular pillowcase?

A: While you can use a regular pillowcase, it's not recommended. Cotton pillowcases can cause more friction and tangles, which can damage your extensions.

Q: How often should I wash my extensions?

A: It's recommended to wash your extensions every 2-3 days.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is crucial when it comes to tape in extensions. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your extensions too often.
  • Brush your hair gently, starting at the ends and working your way up.
  • Be gentle when brushing your extensions, especially when they're wet.
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using hot tools.

By following these tips, you'll be able to sleep comfortably with your tape in extensions and keep them looking great for longer.
