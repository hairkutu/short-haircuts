How To Get Rid Of Brassy Blonde Hair At Home

Written by Aprilia Jun 30, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're a blonde, you know the struggle of keeping your hair color looking fresh and vibrant. Over time, blonde hair can become brassy and yellow, leaving you with a less-than-perfect color. But fear not! There are ways to get rid of brassy blonde hair at home without having to go to the salon.

Description

Brassy blonde hair is when your blonde hair takes on a yellow or orange hue. This can happen for a variety of reasons including exposure to the sun, hard water, and using hair products that contain sulfates. While going to the salon is always an option, it can be expensive and time-consuming. Fortunately, there are things you can do at home to get rid of brassy blonde hair.

How to Get Rid of Brassy Blonde Hair at Home

Getting rid of brassy blonde hair at home is easier than you might think. Here are a few things you can do:

Use a Purple Shampoo

Purple shampoo is a must-have for any blonde. The purple pigments in the shampoo will help neutralize the yellow tones in your hair, leaving you with a cooler, more ashy shade. Use the shampoo once or twice a week to keep your blonde looking fresh.

Try a Toning Treatment

If your hair is really brassy, you might want to try a toning treatment. These treatments are usually purple or blue and will help neutralize the yellow and orange tones in your hair. Apply the treatment to your hair and leave it on for the recommended amount of time. Rinse it out and style as usual.

Use a Color-Depositing Conditioner

If you're looking for a longer-lasting solution, try a color-depositing conditioner. These conditioners will deposit color into your hair, helping to neutralize any brassy tones. Use the conditioner once a week to keep your blonde looking fresh.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to getting rid of brassy blonde hair at home:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo. This will help remove any buildup in your hair, which can contribute to brassy tones.

Step 2: Apply Purple Shampoo

Apply a generous amount of purple shampoo to your hair. Massage it in and leave it on for 5-10 minutes. Rinse it out and follow up with a regular conditioner.

Step 3: Try a Toning Treatment

If your hair is still brassy, try a toning treatment. Apply the treatment to your hair and leave it on for the recommended amount of time. Rinse it out and style as usual.

Step 4: Use a Color-Depositing Conditioner

If you're looking for a longer-lasting solution, use a color-depositing conditioner. Apply the conditioner to your hair and leave it on for the recommended amount of time. Rinse it out and style as usual.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep your blonde hair looking fresh:

  • Use a heat protectant when styling your hair
  • Avoid using hot tools on wet hair
  • Avoid over-washing your hair
  • Avoid using harsh hair products
  • Protect your hair from the sun

FAQs

Q: Can I use regular shampoo instead of purple shampoo?

A: While regular shampoo will clean your hair, it won't help neutralize brassy tones. Purple shampoo is specifically designed for blondes.

Q: How often should I use a toning treatment?

A: It depends on your hair and the toning treatment you're using. Follow the instructions on the package and adjust as needed.

Q: Will a color-depositing conditioner damage my hair?

A: No, as long as you're using a quality product and following the instructions, a color-depositing conditioner shouldn't damage your hair.

Hair Care

Keeping your blonde hair looking fresh and vibrant takes effort, but it's worth it. Use quality hair products and avoid over-washing and over-styling your hair. Protect your hair from the sun and try to avoid using hot tools on wet hair. With a little care and attention, you can keep your blonde hair looking beautiful.
