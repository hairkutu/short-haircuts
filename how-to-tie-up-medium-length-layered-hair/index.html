How To Tie Up Medium Length Layered Hair A Relaxed Guide

Written by Daniel Sep 06, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Medium length layered hair is versatile and stylish, but sometimes you just need to get it out of your face. Whether you're heading to the gym, running errands, or just want a relaxed hairstyle for the day, there are plenty of ways to tie up medium length layered hair. In this guide, we'll show you how to tie up your hair in a variety of styles, from messy buns to sleek ponytails.

  • How to Tie Up Medium Length Layered Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Tying Up Medium Length Layered Hair
  • FAQs

How to Tie Up Medium Length Layered Hair

Before we get into the specific styles, here are a few tips for tying up medium length layered hair:

  • Use hair ties and bobby pins that match your hair color to avoid any obvious hair accessories.
  • Don't tie your hair up too tightly, as this can cause breakage and damage to your hair.
  • Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you and your hair type.

Messy Bun

The messy bun is a classic hairstyle that's perfect for medium length layered hair. Here's how to do it:

  1. Gather your hair into a high ponytail and secure it with a hair tie.
  2. Twist your ponytail around the hair tie to create a bun shape.
  3. Secure the bun with bobby pins, tucking in any loose strands.
  4. Pull out a few face-framing pieces of hair to create a relaxed, effortless look.

Sleek Ponytail

A sleek ponytail is a great option for a more polished look. Here's how to achieve it:

  1. Brush your hair back into a high ponytail and secure it with a hair tie.
  2. Take a small section of hair from the bottom of the ponytail and wrap it around the hair tie to hide it.
  3. Secure the wrapped section of hair with a bobby pin.
  4. Smooth down any flyaways with a small amount of hair gel or hairspray.

Half Up Half Down

The half up half down style is perfect for those who want to keep some of their hair down while still getting it out of their face. Here's how to do it:

  1. Take a section of hair from the top of your head and pull it back, securing it with a hair tie.
  2. Leave the rest of your hair down or style it as desired.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to tying up your medium length layered hair:

  1. Brush your hair to remove any tangles or knots.
  2. Decide on the style you want to create.
  3. Gather your hair into a ponytail or section as needed.
  4. Secure your hair with a hair tie or bobby pins.
  5. Adjust your hair as needed to create the desired shape and style.
  6. Add any hair accessories, such as headbands or clips, as desired.
  7. Finish with a spritz of hairspray or hair gel to keep your style in place.

Tips for Tying Up Medium Length Layered Hair

Here are a few additional tips for tying up medium length layered hair:

  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Try different hair ties and bobby pins to find ones that work well with your hair type.
  • Don't tie your hair up in the same style every day to avoid breakage and damage.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about tying up medium length layered hair:

Can I tie up my medium length layered hair if it's thin?

Yes, you can still tie up your hair even if it's thin. Just be sure to use hair ties and bobby pins that are gentle on your hair and don't pull too tightly.

What's the best way to tie up my hair for a workout?

A high ponytail or bun is a great option for working out. Be sure to use hair ties and bobby pins that are sturdy and won't slip out of your hair during exercise.

Hair Care for Medium Length Layered Hair

To keep your medium length layered hair healthy and looking its best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or brush to detangle your hair after washing.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

With these tips and styles, you'll be able to tie up your medium length layered hair in a variety of ways. Experiment with different styles and find what works best for you and your hair type.
