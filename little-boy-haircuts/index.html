Little Boy Haircuts How To Choose The Best Style

Written by Aprilia May 05, 2023 · 9 min read

As a parent, you want your little boy to look his best, and a great haircut is the perfect way to achieve that. But with so many different little boy haircuts to choose from, how do you know which one is right for your child? In this article, we will discuss how to choose the best little boy haircut, provide step-by-step instructions on how to achieve popular styles, offer tips for maintaining the cut, and answer some frequently asked questions about little boy haircuts.

Table of Contents

  • How to Choose the Best Little Boy Haircut
  • Step-by-Step Instructions for Popular Little Boy Haircuts
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Child's Little Boy Haircut
  • Frequently Asked Questions About Little Boy Haircuts
  • Hair Care Tips for Little Boys

How to Choose the Best Little Boy Haircut

When choosing a little boy haircut, it’s important to consider your child’s hair type, face shape, and personal style. Some popular options include:

Crew Cut

The crew cut is a classic, low-maintenance style that’s perfect for active little boys. It’s also versatile enough to work with most face shapes and hair types. To achieve this style, the hair on top is cut short and tapered, while the sides and back are buzzed with clippers.

Side Part

The side part is a timeless style that can work for any occasion. To achieve this look, the hair is cut short on the sides and back, while the top is left longer and styled to the side with a part. This style works well for boys with round or oval face shapes.

Undercut

The undercut is a trendy style that’s popular among little boys and adults alike. To achieve this look, the sides and back are shaved very short, while the hair on top is left longer and styled in a variety of ways. This style works best for boys with thick hair.

Step-by-Step Instructions for Popular Little Boy Haircuts

While it’s always best to leave hair cutting to the professionals, these step-by-step instructions can help you achieve some popular little boy haircuts at home:

Crew Cut

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Use clippers to buzz the sides and back of the head, starting with a longer guard and working your way down to a shorter one.
  3. For the top of the head, use scissors to cut the hair short and tapered, leaving it slightly longer in the front.
  4. Blend the top and sides with scissors or clippers.
  5. Style with a bit of hair gel if desired.

Side Part

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Use clippers to cut the sides and back short, leaving the top longer.
  3. Create a part on one side of the head.
  4. Use scissors to cut the top of the hair to the desired length.
  5. Blend the top and sides with scissors or clippers.
  6. Style by combing the hair to the side with a bit of hair gel.

Undercut

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Use clippers to shave the sides and back very short, leaving the top longer.
  3. Cut the top of the hair to the desired length with scissors.
  4. Style with a bit of hair gel or pomade.

Tips for Maintaining Your Child's Little Boy Haircut

To keep your child’s little boy haircut looking its best, follow these tips:

  • Visit the barber every four to six weeks for a trim.
  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep hair clean and healthy.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers or straighteners on your child’s hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or brush to detangle hair after washing.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to the ends of the hair to prevent split ends.

Frequently Asked Questions About Little Boy Haircuts

How often should I take my child for a haircut?

It’s generally recommended to have your child’s hair trimmed every four to six weeks to keep it looking neat and healthy.

What if my child doesn’t want to sit still during a haircut?

Some children may be nervous or fidgety during haircuts. You can try distracting them with a toy, book, or video, or ask the barber to take breaks as needed.

Are there any haircuts that are not recommended for little boys?

While there are no hard and fast rules, some haircuts may not be appropriate for very young children, such as styles that require a lot of maintenance or that might be uncomfortable for the child to wear (such as a tight, slicked-back style).

Hair Care Tips for Little Boys

In addition to getting regular haircuts, there are some things you can do to keep your child’s hair healthy and looking great:

  • Encourage your child to eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals.
  • Limit exposure to heat and sun, which can damage hair.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes on your child’s hair.
  • Teach your child to be gentle when brushing or combing their hair to prevent breakage.
  • Use a hat or scarf to protect your child’s hair on windy or cold days.

With these tips and tricks, you can help your child look and feel their best with a trendy and age-appropriate little boy haircut. Whether you opt for a classic crew cut or a trendy undercut, your child is sure to turn heads with their new look!
