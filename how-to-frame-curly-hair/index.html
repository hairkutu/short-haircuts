How To Frame Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Mar 27, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Curly hair is beautiful, but it can be challenging to style. One of the best ways to enhance the beauty of curly hair is to frame it. Framing curly hair involves shaping the hair around the face to highlight its natural texture and create a flattering look. In this article, we will discuss how to frame curly hair in relaxed English language.

Description

Framing curly hair is a technique that involves trimming the hair around the face to create a flattering shape. This technique is particularly useful for those with curly hair, as it can help to enhance the natural texture of the hair and create a more balanced look. Framing curly hair can be done on any length of hair, from short bobs to long curls. The key is to create a shape that complements the natural texture of the hair and flatters the face shape.

How to Frame Curly Hair

There are a few different techniques that can be used to frame curly hair. One popular technique is to create layers around the face, which can help to create movement in the hair and soften the overall look. Another technique is to create face-framing curls, which involves styling the hair around the face in loose, bouncy curls. Whatever technique you choose, it's important to work with the natural texture of your hair and create a shape that complements your face shape.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to framing curly hair:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products designed for curly hair.
  2. Towel dry your hair gently, being careful not to rub or tangle the curls.
  3. Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl-enhancing product to help define the curls and reduce frizz.
  4. Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your hair, using a low heat setting.
  5. Once your hair is dry, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently separate the curls and create volume.
  6. Decide on the shape you want to create around your face. You can create layers, face-framing curls, or a combination of both.
  7. Use sharp hair cutting scissors to trim the hair around your face, being careful not to cut too much at once.
  8. Work in small sections, cutting the hair at an angle to create a natural-looking shape.
  9. Take breaks to step back and assess the shape you're creating, making adjustments as needed.
  10. Finish by styling your hair as desired, using a curling iron or other styling tools to enhance the shape and texture.

Tips for Framing Curly Hair

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when framing curly hair:

  • Work with the natural texture of your hair, rather than fighting against it.
  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Take breaks to step back and assess the shape you're creating.
  • Consider consulting a professional stylist for help creating the perfect shape.
  • Use products designed for curly hair to help define and enhance the curls.

FAQs

Here are a few frequently asked questions about framing curly hair:

  • Can I frame curly hair if it's short? Yes, framing curly hair can be done on any length of hair, from short bobs to long curls.
  • What's the best technique for framing curly hair? The best technique will depend on your hair type and face shape. Consider consulting a professional stylist for advice.
  • How often should I trim my curly hair? It's recommended to trim curly hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and health.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Keeping curly hair healthy and well-maintained is essential for creating a beautiful, defined shape. Here are a few tips for caring for curly hair:

  • Use products designed for curly hair, such as sulfate-free shampoos and rich conditioners.
  • Avoid brushing curly hair, which can cause damage and frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle the curls.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl-enhancing product to help define the curls and reduce frizz.
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your hair, using a low heat setting.
  • Consider using a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent tangles while you sleep.

With these tips and techniques, you can create a beautiful, flattering shape for your curly hair. Whether you opt for layers, face-framing curls, or a combination of both, framing your curly hair can help to enhance its natural texture and create a more balanced look.
