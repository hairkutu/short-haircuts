How To Make My Perm Curlier A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Apr 23, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you looking to give your perm a bit more curl? Maybe you’re not quite happy with the results of your last perm, or perhaps you just want to switch things up. Whatever the reason, making your perm curlier is easier than you might think.

What You’ll Need

Before we get started, let’s take a look at what you’ll need:

  • Perm rods
  • Setting lotion or mousse
  • Wide-toothed comb
  • Blow dryer
  • Hairspray

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo. This will help to remove any buildup from previous styling products or oils that may be weighing down your curls.

Step 2: Apply Setting Lotion or Mousse

Apply a generous amount of setting lotion or mousse to your hair, making sure to distribute it evenly from roots to ends.

Step 3: Comb Through Your Hair

Using a wide-toothed comb, comb through your hair to ensure that the setting lotion or mousse is evenly distributed.

Step 4: Roll Your Hair Using Perm Rods

Starting at the top of your head, take small sections of hair and roll them tightly onto perm rods. Make sure to roll your hair in the direction that you want your curls to go.

Step 5: Dry Your Hair

Once all of your hair is rolled, use a blow dryer to dry your hair completely. This will help to set the curls in place.

Step 6: Remove the Perm Rods

Once your hair is completely dry, carefully remove the perm rods. You should be left with tight, bouncy curls.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

You can leave your curls as they are, or you can style them further using a curling iron or styling wand. Just be sure to use a heat protectant spray to avoid damaging your hair.

Step 8: Set Your Curls

Once you’ve achieved your desired style, use a light hold hairspray to set your curls in place.

Tips for Making Your Perm Curlier

  • Use smaller perm rods for tighter curls.
  • Apply a bit of hair oil to your curls to add shine and reduce frizz.
  • Don’t be afraid to experiment with different styling products to find what works best for your hair.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can cause your curls to become limp and lifeless.
  • Be gentle with your curls when styling, as rough handling can cause them to frizz and lose their shape.

FAQ

Can I make my perm curlier without using perm rods?

Perm rods are the best way to achieve tight, bouncy curls with a perm. However, you can try using a curling iron or styling wand to add more curl to your perm.

How often should I wash my permed hair?

It’s best to avoid washing your permed hair too often, as this can cause your curls to become limp and lifeless. Aim to wash your hair no more than every other day.

How can I keep my permed hair looking healthy?

Be sure to use a deep conditioning treatment on your permed hair once a week to keep it looking healthy and shiny. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage to your hair.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

If you have curly hair, it’s important to take proper care of your locks to keep them looking their best. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to your curls to keep them hydrated and frizz-free.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle your curls to avoid breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage to your hair.

With these tips and tricks, you can make your perm curlier and keep your curly hair looking its best.
