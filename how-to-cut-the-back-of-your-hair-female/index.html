How To Cut The Back Of Your Hair Female A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 25, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking to save money and cut your own hair at home, one of the trickiest parts can be cutting the back of your hair. It's not always easy to see what you're doing, and it can be hard to reach. But with the right tools and some guidance, you can achieve a professional-looking cut from the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to cut the back of your hair female step-by-step.

Description

Cutting the back of your hair can be a daunting task, but it doesn't have to be. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a beautiful cut that looks like you just stepped out of the salon. This guide will give you all the information you need to get started, including what tools you'll need, how to section your hair, and step-by-step instructions for cutting the back of your hair.

How to Cut the Back of Your Hair Female: Step-by-Step

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you begin, you'll need to gather some essential tools. These include:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • A comb
  • Clips to section your hair
  • A handheld mirror or a mirror that you can place behind you

Make sure your scissors are sharp, as dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to achieve a clean cut.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair. Use clips to pull the top section of your hair up and away from the back of your head. This will allow you to focus on the back of your hair without getting in the way of the top section.

Step 3: Trim the Bottom

Take a small section of hair from the bottom of your hairline and comb it straight down. Use your scissors to trim the ends, working from the center outwards. Be sure to cut only a small amount at a time, and check your progress frequently in the mirror.

Step 4: Work in Sections

Continue working in small sections, moving up your head towards the crown. As you work, take care to cut your hair evenly and to keep your sections neat and tidy. Use your comb to guide your scissors and to ensure that you're cutting straight lines.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

As you work, use your handheld mirror to check the back of your hair. Angle the mirror so that you can see the back of your head, and use it to check that your cut is even and symmetrical.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut the back of your hair, you may notice that the layers don't blend seamlessly. To fix this, gently run your scissors over the edges of the layers, blending them together to create a more natural look.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

Once you're happy with your cut, style your hair as desired. You can blow-dry it straight, curl it, or leave it natural. Just be sure to use heat protectant if you're using heat tools, as this will help prevent damage to your hair.

Tips for Cutting the Back of Your Hair Female

  • Start with small sections and work your way up.
  • Check your progress frequently in the mirror.
  • Be patient and take your time.
  • Use sharp scissors to achieve a clean cut.
  • If you're unsure, start with a longer length and gradually work your way shorter.

FAQ

How often should I cut the back of my hair?

This depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. As a general rule, it's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and looking its best.

Can I cut the back of my hair myself?

Yes, you can! It may take some practice, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking cut from the comfort of your own home.

Hair Care

Now that you've cut the back of your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking healthy and beautiful. Be sure to use a good quality shampoo and conditioner, and consider using a hair mask or treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.

Cutting the back of your hair female can be a bit intimidating, but with these tips and step-by-step instructions, you can achieve a beautiful cut that looks like you just stepped out of the salon. So grab your scissors and get ready to give yourself a fabulous new look!
