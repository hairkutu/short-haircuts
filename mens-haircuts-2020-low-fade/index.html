Mens Haircuts 2020 Low Fade The Ultimate Guide

Written by Luffy Jun 14, 2023 · 2 min read

Welcome to our ultimate guide on mens haircuts 2020 low fade. In this article, we’ll cover everything you need to know about this popular hairstyle that has been trending for the past few years. From what it is, how to get it, to tips on how to take care of it, we’ve got you covered. So, whether you’re looking for a new hairstyle or just curious about the low fade, keep reading.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Get Mens Haircuts 2020 Low Fade

Description

The low fade is a popular haircut for men that involves cutting the hair short on the sides and back, while gradually tapering it down to the skin around the ears and the nape of the neck. This creates a clean, modern look that is suitable for all hair types and face shapes.

The low fade can be paired with a variety of hairstyles, such as a quiff, a pompadour, or a slicked-back style. It is a versatile haircut that can be dressed up or down depending on the occasion, making it an ideal choice for men who want a stylish, low-maintenance look.

How to Get Mens Haircuts 2020 Low Fade

If you’re interested in getting a low fade haircut, the first step is to find a skilled barber who has experience cutting this style. It’s important to choose a barber who understands your hair type and face shape, as well as your personal style preferences.

Once you’ve found a barber you trust, the next step is to discuss the specifics of the low fade haircut you want. This includes the length of the hair on top, the type of fade you prefer, and the overall shape of the haircut. Your barber will be able to guide you through these decisions and make recommendations based on your hair type and face shape.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to get mens haircuts 2020 low fade:

  1. Choose your desired length for the hair on top.
  2. Select the type of fade you want (low, mid, or high).
  3. Determine the shape of the fade (taper, bald, or skin).
  4. Ask your barber to cut the sides and back of your hair to the desired length.
  5. Ask your barber to use scissors to cut the hair on top to your desired length and style.
  6. Have your barber use a straight razor to clean up the edges of your haircut.
  7. Ask your barber for styling tips and product recommendations to maintain your new low fade haircut.

Tips for Maintaining Mens Haircuts 2020 Low Fade

Here are some tips for maintaining your mens haircuts 2020 low fade:

  • Get regular trims every 2-3 weeks to keep your haircut looking fresh.
  • Use a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Apply a styling product, such as pomade or wax, to keep your hair in place and add texture.
  • Avoid over-styling your hair, as this can damage the hair and make it look greasy.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles to find the one that suits you best.

FAQs

What face shape is best for mens haircuts 2020 low fade?

The low fade is a versatile haircut that can be adapted to suit any face shape. However, it is particularly well-suited to men with oval or round faces, as it helps to elongate the face and create a more defined jawline.

What type of hair is best for mens haircuts 2020 low fade?

The low fade works well on all hair types, from fine to thick and curly to straight. However, it may require more maintenance for men with curly or thick hair, as the hair can become unruly and difficult to manage.

Hair Care Tips for Mens Haircuts 2020 Low Fade

To keep your mens haircuts 2020 low fade looking its best, it’s important to follow a regular hair care routine. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days with a quality shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as curling irons or straighteners, as these can damage the hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid washing your hair with hot water, as this can strip the hair of its natural oils.
