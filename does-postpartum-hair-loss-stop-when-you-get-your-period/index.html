Does Postpartum Hair Loss Stop When You Get Your Period

Written by Eveline Sep 10, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Postpartum hair loss is a common condition that affects many women after giving birth. It can be quite distressing and can lead to a loss of self-confidence. Many women wonder if postpartum hair loss stops when they get their period. In this article, we will explore whether or not this is true and provide tips on how to manage postpartum hair loss.

  • Tips for Managing Postpartum Hair Loss

Description

Postpartum hair loss is a common condition that affects many women after giving birth. It is caused by hormonal changes in the body that occur during pregnancy and after delivery. During pregnancy, the hormone estrogen increases, which can cause hair to grow thicker and fuller. However, after delivery, estrogen levels drop, and the hair that was gained during pregnancy can fall out. This can lead to hair loss and thinning.

Many women wonder if postpartum hair loss stops when they get their period. The answer is no. Postpartum hair loss is not related to menstruation or the menstrual cycle. It is a separate condition that can last for several months after giving birth.

How to Manage Postpartum Hair Loss

While postpartum hair loss is a natural and temporary condition, there are several things that you can do to manage it:

Step by Step Guide

  1. Be gentle with your hair. Avoid pulling, tugging, or brushing too hard.
  2. Avoid hairstyles that pull on your hair, such as tight braids or ponytails.
  3. Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals that promote hair growth, such as iron, zinc, and biotin.
  4. Take a postnatal vitamin supplement that contains these vitamins and minerals.
  5. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is free of harsh chemicals.
  6. Avoid using hot tools, such as hair dryers, curling irons, and straighteners.
  7. Wash your hair every other day to avoid over-washing.
  8. Avoid stress, which can exacerbate hair loss.

Tips for Managing Postpartum Hair Loss

Here are some additional tips for managing postpartum hair loss:

  • Consider getting a shorter haircut to make your hair appear fuller.
  • Use volumizing products, such as mousse or root lifters.
  • Try hair extensions or a hairpiece to add volume and length.
  • Consider talking to your doctor about using minoxidil, a topical medication that can promote hair growth.
  • Remember that postpartum hair loss is a temporary condition that will resolve on its own.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about postpartum hair loss:

  • How long does postpartum hair loss last? Postpartum hair loss can last for several months after giving birth. It typically resolves on its own within six to twelve months.
  • Can breastfeeding cause postpartum hair loss? Yes, breastfeeding can contribute to postpartum hair loss due to hormonal changes in the body.
  • Is postpartum hair loss permanent? No, postpartum hair loss is a temporary condition that will resolve on its own.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some general hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle wet hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools on wet hair, as it can cause damage.
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using hot tools.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as it can strip natural oils.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

In conclusion, postpartum hair loss is a common condition that affects many women after giving birth. It is not related to menstruation or the menstrual cycle and can last for several months after delivery. However, with proper hair care and management, you can minimize the effects of postpartum hair loss and promote healthy hair growth.
