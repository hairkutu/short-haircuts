How To Layer Your Own Hair Around Your Face A Step By Step Guide

Layering your hair is a great way to add volume and dimension to your locks. Not to mention, it's a great way to frame your face and accentuate your features. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to layer your own hair around your face. We'll cover everything from how to prepare your hair to styling tips and hair care. Let's get started!

How to Layer Your Own Hair Around Your Face

Layering your hair around your face can be tricky, but with the right tools and techniques, it's totally doable. Here's what you'll need:

  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Clip or hair tie
  • Flat iron or curling iron (optional)

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and towel drying it. You want your hair to be slightly damp for easier cutting. If you have curly or wavy hair, you can apply a curl-enhancing product to help define your curls.

Step 2: Part Your Hair

Using a comb, create a center part in your hair. Then, use the comb to section off the front portion of your hair on both sides of your face. Clip or tie back the rest of your hair.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Starting with one side of your hair, take a small section of hair and hold it with your fingers. Use the scissors to cut the hair at an angle, starting from your chin and working downwards. Make sure to cut small sections at a time to avoid any mistakes. Repeat on the other side.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you've finished cutting both sides, take down the rest of your hair and blend the layers together. Use the scissors to trim any uneven parts or stray hairs.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Finally, style your hair as desired. You can use a flat iron or curling iron to add some waves or straighten your hair. Play around with different styles to see what works best for you.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide to help you layer your own hair around your face:

  1. Wash and towel dry your hair.
  2. Part your hair down the center.
  3. Section off the front portion of your hair on both sides of your face.
  4. Clip or tie back the rest of your hair.
  5. Starting with one side, take a small section of hair and hold it with your fingers.
  6. Using the scissors, cut the hair at an angle, starting from your chin and working downwards.
  7. Repeat on the other side.
  8. Take down the rest of your hair and blend the layers together.
  9. Trim any uneven parts or stray hairs.
  10. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Layering Your Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when layering your hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid any mistakes.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Make sure to blend the layers together for a natural look.
  • Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you.

FAQs

Can I layer my hair if it's already short?

Yes, you can still layer your hair if it's already short. Just make sure to start with small sections of hair and blend the layers together.

Do I need to use a specific type of scissors?

It's best to use sharp hair cutting scissors for a clean cut. Regular household scissors may damage your hair.

How often should I trim my layered hair?

It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and style of your layers.

Hair Care for Layered Hair

Here are some tips to keep your layered hair looking its best:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot tools too often to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Apply a hair serum or oil to help tame any flyaways or frizz.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and style of your layers.

Layering your own hair around your face may seem daunting at first, but with these tips and techniques, you'll be able to achieve a beautiful, layered look. Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and have fun with your new 'do!
