How To Cut Curly Hair At Home For Girls A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jun 03, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're a girl with curly hair, you know how difficult it can be to maintain your curls. Going to the salon can be expensive, and finding a stylist who understands your hair can be a challenge. But with the right tools and techniques, you can cut your curly hair at home and achieve the look you want. In this step-by-step guide, we'll show you how to cut curly hair at home for girls.

How to Prepare for Cutting Curly Hair at Home

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to prepare your hair and your tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • A pair of sharp haircutting scissors
  • A wide-tooth comb
  • A spray bottle filled with water
  • A mirror

Once you have all your tools ready, it's time to start preparing your hair. Here's what you need to do:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as you normally would.
  2. Blot your hair dry with a towel, but don't rub it vigorously.
  3. Apply a leave-in conditioner or detangler to your hair.
  4. Comb your hair with a wide-tooth comb to remove any tangles.

Step-by-Step Guide to Cutting Curly Hair at Home

Now that you're ready to start cutting your hair, here's a step-by-step guide to follow:

  1. Section your hair into four or five sections using hair ties or clips.
  2. Start with one section at a time, and spray it lightly with water to make it easier to cut.
  3. Use your wide-tooth comb to gently comb through the section of hair to remove any tangles.
  4. Take a small section of hair from the bottom of the section you're working on, and hold it between your fingers.
  5. Use your scissors to cut the ends of the hair straight across, following your fingers as a guide.
  6. Continue working your way up the section of hair, taking small sections and cutting the ends straight across.
  7. Repeat this process on each section of hair.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair at Home

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting your curly hair at home:

  • Don't cut your hair when it's dry. Curly hair can shrink up to 50% when it's dry, so you may end up cutting more than you intended.
  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair, causing split ends and breakage.
  • Don't cut too much at once. It's better to take small sections of hair and cut a little bit at a time.
  • Be patient. Cutting curly hair can take time, so don't rush it.

FAQs About Cutting Curly Hair at Home

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting curly hair at home:

Can I cut my hair when it's wet?

It's best to cut curly hair when it's damp, not wet. Wet hair can be more difficult to cut, and it can also shrink up to 50% when it dries, which can make it difficult to achieve the look you want.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to cut curly hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Should I cut my hair myself or go to a professional?

If you're not confident in your ability to cut your own hair, it's best to go to a professional. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can cut your curly hair at home.

How to Care for Curly Hair After Cutting It

After cutting your curly hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and frizz.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible, as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to your hair to keep it moisturized.
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your hair, as this will help define your curls.

Cutting your curly hair at home can be a great way to save money and achieve the look you want. With the right tools and techniques, you can cut your hair with confidence and keep it healthy and beautiful.
