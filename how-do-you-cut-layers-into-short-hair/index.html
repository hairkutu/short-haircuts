How Do You Cut Layers Into Short Hair

Written by Petter Aug 10, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you've got short hair, you may be wondering if you can still have layers. The answer is yes, you definitely can! Layers can add movement and texture to short hair, making it look fuller and more voluminous. In this article, we'll show you how to cut layers into short hair step by step.

Description

Cutting layers into short hair can be a bit tricky, especially if you're doing it yourself. But with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a layered look that suits your hair type and face shape. Layers can be added to any short hairstyle, from a pixie cut to a bob, and can be cut in different lengths and angles to create different effects.

How To

If you're cutting your own hair, start by washing and drying it. If you're cutting someone else's hair, make sure their hair is clean and dry. Then, follow these steps:

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. Start at the back of your head and work your way forward, leaving the hair at the crown of your head untouched. This will be your guide for the length of your layers.

Step 2: Cut the Back

Start at the back of your head and cut upward at a slight angle, following the shape of your head. Take small sections of hair and cut them to the length of your guide. Make sure you're cutting straight across and not at an angle. Continue cutting upward until you've reached the desired length.

Step 3: Cut the Sides

Now it's time to cut the sides. Take a small section of hair from the front and pull it forward. Cut at an angle, starting at the length of your guide and working your way down. Keep the angle consistent to create a gradual slope. Repeat on the other side.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Blend the layers by using scissors to cut into the ends of the hair at a slight angle. This will create a soft, layered look that blends the different lengths together.

Step 5: Add Finishing Touches

Once you've cut your layers, you can add some finishing touches. Use a razor or thinning shears to remove any bulk or add texture. You can also use a straightening iron or curling wand to style your hair and show off your new layers.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting layers into short hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut
  • Take breaks if you need to, especially if you're cutting your own hair
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different lengths and angles to find the perfect look for you

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting layers into short hair:

Can you add layers to any short hairstyle?

Yes, layers can be added to any short hairstyle, from a pixie cut to a bob.

Can you cut your own layers into short hair?

Yes, but it can be tricky. Make sure you have the right tools and follow the steps carefully.

What's the best way to blend layers in short hair?

Use scissors to cut into the ends of the hair at a slight angle. This will create a soft, layered look that blends the different lengths together.

Hair Care

Once you've cut your layers, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and shiny. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can damage the ends
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends

Cutting layers into short hair can be a fun and creative way to change up your look. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a layered look that suits your hair type and face shape. Just remember to take your time, start with small sections of hair, and experiment with different lengths and angles to find the perfect look for you.
