How To Use Thinning Scissors On Yourself

Written by Petter May 30, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Thinning scissors are an essential tool for anyone who wants to maintain their hair at home. They are used to remove bulk from thick hair, resulting in a more manageable and natural-looking style. In this article, we will guide you on how to use thinning scissors on yourself.

Description

Thinning scissors are a type of hair cutting scissors that have teeth on one blade. These teeth help to remove hair in a subtle and controlled manner. They are perfect for thinning out thick hair or adding texture to a haircut. Thinning scissors come in different sizes and tooth spacing, so it's essential to choose one that fits your hair type and desired outcome.

How To

Before using thinning scissors, make sure your hair is clean and dry. It's also best to use them on freshly washed hair to avoid any tangles or knots. Here's how to use thinning scissors on yourself:

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair into manageable sections. You can use clips to secure the hair you're not working on to avoid cutting more than you intended.

Step 2: Hold the Scissors Correctly

Hold the thinning scissors with the teeth facing upwards and the stationary blade in your dominant hand. Your other hand should hold the hair you want to thin out.

Step 3: Cut the Hair

Start by slowly cutting the hair in a back-and-forth motion with the thinning scissors. Make sure you don't cut too much hair at once, as it can result in an uneven cut. It's best to start with small sections and work your way up.

Step 4: Check Your Progress

After each section, check your progress by running your fingers through your hair. This will help you see if you need to thin out more or if you've cut enough.

Step 5: Repeat the Process

Repeat the process on all the sections of your hair until you achieve your desired look. Remember to take breaks and step back to see your hair from different angles.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you use thinning scissors on yourself:

  • Start with small sections and work your way up to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Use clips to secure the hair you're not working on to avoid cutting more than you intended.
  • Take breaks and step back to see your hair from different angles.
  • Do not use thinning scissors on wet hair as it can result in an uneven cut.
  • Choose thinning scissors that fit your hair type and desired outcome.

FAQ

Can I Use Thinning Scissors on Any Hair Type?

Yes, you can use thinning scissors on any hair type. However, it's essential to choose the right type of thinning scissors for your hair. For example, if you have fine hair, you may want to use thinning scissors with wider gaps to avoid cutting too much hair.

Can I Use Thinning Scissors on Wet Hair?

No, it's not recommended to use thinning scissors on wet hair as it can result in an uneven cut. It's best to use them on freshly washed and dry hair.

How Often Should I Use Thinning Scissors on My Hair?

It depends on your hair type and desired look. If you have thick hair, you may need to use thinning scissors more often to maintain a manageable hairstyle. However, it's best to consult with a hair stylist for a personalized recommendation.

Hair Care

After using thinning scissors on yourself, it's essential to take care of your hair to maintain a healthy and natural look. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as blow dryers and flat irons.
  • Use a hair mask or oil treatment once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to avoid split ends and maintain a healthy look.
  • Consult with a hair stylist for personalized hair care recommendations.

Now that you know how to use thinning scissors on yourself, you can achieve a natural and manageable hairstyle at home. Remember to choose the right type of thinning scissors for your hair and take care of your hair regularly to maintain a healthy look.
