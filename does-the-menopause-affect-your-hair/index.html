Does The Menopause Affect Your Hair

Written by Frank Apr 30, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Menopause is a natural biological process that occurs in women typically around the age of 51. It is a time when a woman's body undergoes many changes, including hormonal changes that can affect her hair. In this article, we will discuss how menopause affects your hair and what you can do to keep it healthy and looking its best.

Table of Contents

  • How does menopause affect hair?
  • What are the symptoms of menopause affecting hair?
  • How to take care of hair during menopause?
  • Tips for healthy hair during menopause
  • FAQ
  • Conclusion

How does menopause affect hair?

Menopause affects hair by causing changes in hormone levels. The decrease in estrogen levels and increase in androgen levels can cause hair to become thin and brittle. This is because estrogen helps to keep hair in the growth phase, while androgens can cause hair to enter the resting phase, which leads to hair loss.

Additionally, menopause can cause the hair to become dry, dull, and prone to breakage. This is because the sebaceous glands, which produce oil to keep the hair moisturized, become less active during menopause.

Finally, menopause can cause changes in the texture of the hair. Some women may experience a change in curl pattern or an increase in frizz.

What are the symptoms of menopause affecting hair?

The symptoms of menopause affecting hair include:

  • Hair loss or thinning
  • Dry, brittle hair
  • Change in hair texture
  • Increased frizz

How to take care of hair during menopause?

There are several things you can do to take care of your hair during menopause:

  1. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is designed for your hair type.
  2. Avoid using hot styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, which can damage the hair.
  3. Avoid pulling your hair back tightly, as this can cause breakage.
  4. Eat a healthy diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals, which can help to promote healthy hair growth.
  5. Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water, which can help to keep your hair moisturized.

Tips for healthy hair during menopause

To keep your hair healthy during menopause, try the following tips:

  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to help moisturize your hair.
  • Avoid using chemical treatments, such as perms and relaxers, which can damage the hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or brush to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.
  • Consider taking a hair supplement, such as biotin or vitamin D, to help promote healthy hair growth.

FAQ

Q: Can menopause cause hair loss?

A: Yes, menopause can cause hair loss or thinning due to changes in hormone levels.

Q: Will my hair grow back after menopause?

A: It is possible for hair to grow back after menopause, but it may take time and require changes in hair care habits and diet.

Q: What can I do to prevent hair loss during menopause?

A: To prevent hair loss during menopause, you can try using gentle hair care products, eating a healthy diet, and avoiding hot styling tools and chemical treatments.

Conclusion

Menopause can affect your hair in many ways, but there are things you can do to keep it healthy and looking its best. By using gentle hair care products, avoiding hot styling tools and chemical treatments, and eating a healthy diet, you can help to promote healthy hair growth and prevent hair loss during menopause.
