How Do I Know If I ll Suit Black Hair

Written by William Aug 13, 2023 Â· 10 min read

Are you thinking about changing up your hair color and considering black? It can be a bold and dramatic choice, but it's important to make sure it will suit you before taking the plunge. In this article, we'll go over how to determine if black hair will suit you and give you some tips on how to care for it once you've made the switch.

Table of Contents

  • How to Determine if Black Hair Will Suit You
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Trying Black Hair
  • Tips for Maintaining Black Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Tips for Black Hair

How to Determine if Black Hair Will Suit You

There are a few factors to consider when determining if black hair will suit you:

1. Skin Tone

Your skin tone is one of the most important factors to consider when choosing a hair color. If you have a warm skin tone, which means you have yellow or golden undertones, black hair can look stunning on you. If you have a cool skin tone, which means you have pink or blue undertones, black hair can still look great, but you may want to consider a softer shade of black, such as dark brown or espresso.

2. Eye Color

Your eye color can also play a role in determining if black hair will suit you. If you have dark eyes, such as brown or black, black hair can create a beautiful contrast that will really make your eyes pop. If you have lighter eyes, such as blue or green, black hair can still look great, but you may want to consider adding some highlights or lowlights to break up the darkness.

3. Personal Style

Finally, your personal style is an important factor to consider. If you have a bold and edgy style, black hair can be the perfect complement. If you prefer a more classic and sophisticated look, black hair can also work, but you may want to consider a sleek and polished hairstyle to go with it.

Step-by-Step Guide to Trying Black Hair

Once you've determined that black hair will suit you, here's a step-by-step guide to trying it:

1. Consult with a Professional

Before making any drastic changes to your hair, it's always best to consult with a professional stylist. They can help you determine the best shade of black for your skin tone and can also give you tips on how to care for your new hair color.

2. Prepare Your Hair

To prepare your hair for the color, your stylist will likely need to bleach it first. This will remove any existing color and create a blank canvas for the black dye. Depending on the condition of your hair, your stylist may also recommend a deep conditioning treatment before and after the color to keep it healthy.

3. Apply the Color

Your stylist will then apply the black hair dye to your hair. Depending on the brand and formula, the dye may need to sit on your hair for a certain amount of time before being rinsed out.

4. Maintain Your Color

Once you've got your new black hair, it's important to maintain it properly to keep it looking its best. This means using a color-safe shampoo and conditioner, avoiding heat styling tools as much as possible, and protecting your hair from the sun and other environmental factors that can cause fading.

Tips for Maintaining Black Hair

Here are a few tips for maintaining your black hair:

1. Use a Color-Safe Shampoo and Conditioner

Regular shampoos and conditioners can strip your hair of its color, so it's important to use products that are specifically designed for color-treated hair. Look for shampoos and conditioners that are sulfate-free and contain ingredients like argan oil or keratin to help keep your hair healthy.

2. Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can cause damage to your hair and also cause your color to fade more quickly. If you must use heat styling tools, make sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature as low as possible.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun can also cause your hair color to fade, so it's important to protect your hair when you're outside. You can wear a hat or scarf, or use a hair product that contains SPF.

FAQ

Q: Will black hair make me look older?

A: Black hair can be a sophisticated and timeless look, but it can also make you look older if it's not done right. To avoid looking too harsh, consider opting for a soft black shade or adding some highlights or lowlights to break up the darkness.

Q: Can I dye my hair black at home?

A: While it's possible to dye your hair black at home, it's always best to consult with a professional stylist. They can help you choose the right shade of black for your skin tone and can also give you tips on how to care for your new hair color.

Hair Care Tips for Black Hair

Here are a few additional hair care tips for black hair:

1. Deep Condition Regularly

Black hair can be prone to dryness and damage, so it's important to deep condition your hair regularly to keep it healthy. You can use a store-bought deep conditioning treatment or make your own using ingredients like avocado or coconut oil.

2. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular trims can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends, which can make your hair look dull and lifeless. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair looking its best.

3. Be Gentle with Your Hair

Black hair can be fragile, so it's important to be gentle with it. Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage, and use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead. You can also use a silk or satin pillowcase to help prevent breakage while you sleep.

Now that you know how to determine if black hair will suit you, you can make an informed decision about whether it's the right choice for you. Just remember to take care of your hair properly to keep it looking its best!
