How To Cut Your Own Hair Into A Choppy Bob

Written by Robby Aug 27, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of your long hair and craving a change? A choppy bob might be the perfect option for you! Not only is it trendy and chic, but it's also easy to maintain. Plus, you can save some money by cutting it yourself. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of cutting your own hair into a choppy bob step by step.

Description

A choppy bob is a textured, layered haircut that falls between the chin and shoulders. It's perfect for those who want a low-maintenance hairstyle that still looks stylish. The choppy layers add volume and movement to the hair, making it perfect for those with fine or thin hair. This haircut can be customized to suit different face shapes and hair textures.

How to Cut Your Own Hair into a Choppy Bob

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips. It's also a good idea to have a handheld mirror to check the back of your head.

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and letting it air dry. Don't use any styling products, as this can affect the way your hair falls. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use your comb to section your hair into four parts: two at the front and two at the back. Clip the sections to keep them out of the way.

Step 3: Cut the Back

Start with the back section. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, starting at the bottom and working your way up. Repeat this process until you've cut all the hair in the back section.

Step 4: Cut the Sides

Move on to the side sections. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Cut the hair at an angle, starting at the bottom and working your way up. Make sure the length is even on both sides.

Step 5: Cut the Front

Finally, it's time to tackle the front sections. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Cut the hair at an angle, starting at the bottom and working your way up. You can choose to leave the front sections longer or shorter than the rest of your hair, depending on your preference.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the sections, it's time to blend the layers. Use your scissors to cut into the hair, creating a choppy, textured look. Make sure you blend the layers evenly throughout your hair.

Step 7: Check the Back

Use your handheld mirror to check the back of your head. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure the length is even.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

Once you're happy with the length, it's time to style your hair. You can use a straightener or curling iron to add texture and volume. Finish with a texturizing spray for a messy, tousled look.

Tips

- Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much off at once.

- Cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is.

- Use a comb to guide your scissors and ensure a straight cut.

FAQ

Can I cut my hair into a choppy bob with curly hair?

Absolutely! A choppy bob can work with all hair textures, including curly hair. Just make sure you cut the hair when it's dry and use a texturizing product to enhance the curls.

Do I need to cut my hair straight across?

No, a choppy bob is all about texture and layers. You can cut your hair at an angle to create a more textured look.

Can I cut my hair into a choppy bob if I have a round face?

Yes, a choppy bob can be customized to suit different face shapes. If you have a round face, opt for a longer bob that falls just below the chin.

Hair Care

Now that you've cut your hair into a choppy bob, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type, and avoid using too much heat on your hair. Regular trims will also help keep your choppy bob looking fresh and stylish.
