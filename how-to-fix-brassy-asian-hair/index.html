How To Fix Brassy Asian Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Mar 28, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you tired of dealing with brassy asian hair? Brassy hair can be a common problem for many people, especially those with lighter hair shades. It can be frustrating to spend time and money on getting your hair colored, only to have it turn brassy after a few weeks. In this guide, we’ll walk you through how to fix brassy asian hair, so you can get back to feeling confident and happy with your hair.

Description

Brassy asian hair is hair that has turned yellow or orange due to the oxidation of hair color. This can happen due to a variety of reasons, such as exposure to sunlight, chlorine, or hard water. If you’ve recently colored your hair, it can also turn brassy over time as the color fades.

How to Fix Brassy Asian Hair

If you’re dealing with brassy asian hair, don’t worry. There are several ways you can fix it. Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to fix brassy asian hair:

Step 1: Use a Purple Shampoo

Purple shampoo is a great way to tone down brassy hair. The purple pigment in the shampoo neutralizes yellow and orange tones in your hair, leaving it looking cooler and brighter. Use it once a week to maintain your hair color.

Step 2: Try a Toning Treatment

If you need something stronger than a purple shampoo, try a toning treatment. These are typically more potent and can be left on your hair for longer periods of time. Look for a toning treatment that is specifically designed for your hair color and type.

Step 3: Use a Hair Mask

Applying a hair mask once a week can help restore moisture to your hair and prevent it from turning brassy. Look for a hair mask that is designed for your hair type and color. Leave it on for at least 10-15 minutes before rinsing it out.

Step 4: Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage your hair and make it more prone to turning brassy. Try to avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons as much as possible. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

Step 5: Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Exposure to sunlight can cause your hair color to fade and turn brassy. Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a hair product with SPF.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you fix brassy asian hair:

  • Use a clarifying shampoo to remove buildup from your hair. This can help prevent your hair from turning brassy.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach. These can damage your hair and make it more prone to turning brassy.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Consider getting a professional color correction if you’re having trouble fixing your brassy hair on your own.

FAQ

Q: Can I use regular shampoo to fix brassy hair?

A: Regular shampoo may not be effective at fixing brassy hair. However, you can use a clarifying shampoo to remove buildup and prevent your hair from turning brassy in the future.

Q: How often should I use a toning treatment?

A: This will depend on the toning treatment you choose. Follow the instructions on the product packaging for best results.

Q: Can I use a toning treatment on colored hair?

A: Yes, you can use a toning treatment on colored hair. Look for a toning treatment that is specifically designed for your hair color and type.

Hair Care

Finally, taking good care of your hair can help prevent it from turning brassy. Here are some tips for healthy hair:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s wet, as this can cause breakage.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to restore moisture to your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

In conclusion, brassy asian hair can be frustrating, but it’s not impossible to fix. By using the right products and following a good hair care routine, you can prevent your hair from turning brassy and keep it looking healthy and vibrant.
