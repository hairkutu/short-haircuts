How To Make Dark Roots Look Good With Blonde Hair

Written by William May 24, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you tired of constantly dyeing your hair to cover up those pesky dark roots? If you have blonde hair, you might be wondering how to make dark roots look good without resorting to constant touch-ups. Fortunately, there are several ways to embrace your roots and make them work for you. In this article, we'll explore different techniques and products to help you rock the blonde-with-roots look.

  • How to Make Dark Roots Look Good with Blonde Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Blonde Hair with Dark Roots
  • FAQs

How to Make Dark Roots Look Good with Blonde Hair

Firstly, it is important to embrace your roots and understand that they are a natural part of your hair growth cycle. Instead of trying to hide them, you can use different techniques to make them look intentional and chic. Here are some ways to make dark roots look good with blonde hair:

1. Add Lowlights

Lowlights are a great way to blend dark roots with blonde hair. They involve adding darker strands of hair to your natural blonde, creating a more natural look. This technique can also add dimension and depth to your hair, making it look fuller and healthier. You can ask your stylist to add lowlights in a way that complements your hair color and skin tone.

2. Try Balayage

Balayage is a technique that involves painting individual strands of hair to create a natural-looking gradient effect. It is a low-maintenance technique that can work well with dark roots and blonde hair. By adding lighter shades to your roots and gradually blending them with your blonde hair, you can create a sun-kissed look that is perfect for summer.

3. Use Root Touch-Up Spray

If you want a quick fix for your dark roots, you can use root touch-up spray. This product comes in different shades and can be applied directly to your roots to cover them up. It is a temporary solution that can last until your next hair wash. Just make sure to choose a shade that matches your hair color and blend it well to avoid any visible lines.

4. Try Root Shadowing

Root shadowing is a technique that involves adding darker shades to your roots and gradually blending them with your blonde hair. It can create a soft and natural-looking transition between your roots and blonde hair. This technique works well for people who want to maintain a natural-looking hair color without frequent touch-ups.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to making dark roots look good with blonde hair:

Step 1: Choose a Technique

Decide which technique you want to use to blend your dark roots with your blonde hair. You can try lowlights, balayage, root touch-up spray, or root shadowing.

Step 2: Consult with a Stylist

If you're not sure which technique would work best for you, consult with a stylist. They can assess your hair color and skin tone and recommend the best technique to achieve the look you want.

Step 3: Schedule an Appointment

Once you've decided on a technique, schedule an appointment with your stylist. Make sure to discuss the details of the technique and any aftercare instructions.

Step 4: Maintain Your Hair Color

To maintain your hair color, use products that are specifically designed for color-treated hair. Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often, as they can damage your hair and cause your color to fade faster.

Tips for Maintaining Blonde Hair with Dark Roots

Here are some tips for maintaining blonde hair with dark roots:

1. Use a Color-Protecting Shampoo

Using a color-protecting shampoo can help preserve your hair color and prevent it from fading. Look for shampoos that are specifically designed for blonde hair to keep your hair looking vibrant and healthy.

2. Avoid Over-Washing Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and cause your color to fade faster. Try to wash your hair every other day or every three days to maintain your color.

3. Use a Deep-Conditioning Treatment

Using a deep-conditioning treatment once a week can help keep your hair healthy and hydrated. It can also prevent your color from fading and make your hair look shinier and smoother.

FAQs

Q: Can I dye my roots blonde instead of covering them up?

A: Yes, you can dye your roots blonde if you want a more uniform hair color. However, it might require more frequent touch-ups to maintain the color.

Q: How often should I touch up my roots?

A: It depends on the rate of your hair growth and the technique you used to blend your roots with your blonde hair. Consult with your stylist to determine the best touch-up schedule for your hair.

Hair Care for Blonde Hair with Dark Roots

Here are some tips for taking care of blonde hair with dark roots:

1. Avoid Hot Tools

Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons too often, as they can damage your hair and cause your color to fade faster.

2. Use a Heat Protectant

When you do use hot tools, use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your hair.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Wear a hat or use a hair sunscreen to protect your hair from the sun's harmful rays, which can cause your color to fade faster.

By following these tips and techniques, you can make dark roots look good with blonde hair and embrace your natural hair color with confidence.
