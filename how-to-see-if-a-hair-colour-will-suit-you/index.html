How To See If A Hair Colour Will Suit You

Written by Petter Apr 15, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you're thinking about changing your hair colour, it's important to consider whether or not the new shade will suit you. After all, there's nothing worse than spending time and money on a new hair colour that just doesn't look right. Fortunately, there are several ways you can determine whether or not a hair colour will suit you. In this article, we'll go over everything you need to know.

Determining Your Skin Tone

The first step in determining whether a hair colour will suit you is to determine your skin tone. There are two main types of skin tones: warm and cool. Warm skin tones have a yellow or golden undertone, while cool skin tones have a pink or blue undertone.

To determine your skin tone, take a look at the veins on your wrist. If they appear green, you have a warm skin tone. If they appear blue, you have a cool skin tone. Another way to determine your skin tone is to look at the jewellery you wear. If you look best in gold jewellery, you have a warm skin tone. If you look best in silver jewellery, you have a cool skin tone.

Choosing the Right Hair Colour

Once you've determined your skin tone, it's time to choose the right hair colour. As a general rule of thumb, warm skin tones look best with warm hair colours, such as golden blonde, honey brown, and copper red. Cool skin tones look best with cool hair colours, such as platinum blonde, ash brown, and burgundy.

However, there are exceptions to this rule. For example, if you have a warm skin tone but want to try a cool hair colour, you can still make it work by choosing a shade with warm undertones. Similarly, if you have a cool skin tone but want to try a warm hair colour, you can choose a shade with cool undertones.

Trying on Wigs or Hair Extensions

If you're not sure whether a hair colour will suit you, one of the best ways to find out is to try on a wig or hair extensions in the desired shade. This will give you a good idea of how the colour will look against your skin tone without committing to a permanent change.

When trying on wigs or hair extensions, make sure to choose a style that is similar to your current hair length and texture. This will give you the most accurate idea of how the new colour will look on you.

Consulting with a Professional

If you're still unsure about which hair colour will suit you, it's always a good idea to consult with a professional stylist. They will be able to take into account your skin tone, eye colour, and even your personality to help you choose the perfect shade.

During the consultation, make sure to bring pictures of the hair colours you're considering. This will give the stylist a visual reference to work with.

Tips for Choosing a Hair Colour

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when choosing a hair colour:

  • Consider your lifestyle. If you're low-maintenance, choose a hair colour that requires minimal upkeep.
  • Take into account your natural hair colour. If you have dark hair, it may take multiple sessions to achieve a lighter shade.
  • Choose a hair colour that complements your wardrobe. If you wear a lot of warm colours, choose a warm hair colour.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I do if I don't like my new hair colour?

If you don't like your new hair colour, the first step is to talk to your stylist. They may be able to make adjustments to the colour or suggest a different shade that will better suit you. If you're really unhappy with the colour, you can always go back to your natural shade or try a different colour.

How often do I need to touch up my roots?

This will depend on the hair colour you choose and how fast your hair grows. As a general rule of thumb, most people need to touch up their roots every 4-6 weeks.

Will colouring my hair damage it?

Colouring your hair can cause damage if it's not done properly. However, if you take care of your hair and use the right products, you can maintain healthy, coloured hair.

Hair Care Tips for Coloured Hair

Here are some tips for taking care of your coloured hair:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for coloured hair
  • Avoid washing your hair every day to prevent colour from fading
  • Use a heat protectant when styling your hair to prevent damage
  • Avoid chlorine and salt water, as they can cause colour to fade

By following these tips, you can help your coloured hair look its best for as long as possible.
