How To Cut The Back Of Your Hair Straight A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jun 16, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you tired of paying salon prices to have your hair cut and styled? Or are you simply looking to maintain your haircut between appointments? Cutting the back of your hair can be a daunting task, but it doesn't have to be. With the right tools and technique, you can easily cut the back of your hair straight at home. In this guide, we'll show you how to achieve a professional-looking haircut from the comfort of your own home.

Description

Cutting the back of your hair can be tricky, especially if you can't see what you're doing. However, with the right tools and technique, you can achieve a straight and even cut. This guide will provide you with step-by-step instructions on how to cut the back of your hair straight, as well as tips on how to maintain your haircut and keep your hair healthy.

How to Cut the Back of Your Hair Straight

To cut the back of your hair straight, you will need the following tools:

  • Haircutting scissors
  • A comb
  • A handheld mirror
  • Clips to section off your hair

Follow these steps to cut the back of your hair straight:

Step 1: Wash and Dampen Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and allowing it to air-dry until it is damp. This will make it easier to cut and will also ensure that your hair is clean and free of debris.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, create a part in the middle of your hair and section it off into two parts. Clip one section to the side and focus on the other section for now.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Starting at the back of your head, take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut the hair straight across to the desired length. Repeat this process for the entire back section of your hair.

Step 4: Cut the Top Layer

Release the clipped section of your hair and let it fall over the cut bottom layer. Take another small section of hair from the back of your head and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut the hair straight across to the desired length. Repeat this process for the entire top section of your hair.

Step 5: Check for Evenness

After you have cut both layers, use your handheld mirror to check the back of your hair for evenness. If you notice any uneven sections, simply trim them until the entire back of your hair is even.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the perfect haircut:

  • Start by cutting a little bit of hair at a time. You can always cut more, but you can't undo a bad haircut.
  • Make sure to use sharp scissors to prevent split ends.
  • Consider investing in a haircutting cape to protect your clothes from hair clippings.
  • Don't forget to trim the ends of your hair every six to eight weeks to keep your hair healthy.

FAQ

Is it easy to cut the back of your hair?

Cutting the back of your hair can be tricky, especially if you can't see what you're doing. However, with the right tools and technique, you can achieve a straight and even cut.

What tools do I need to cut the back of my hair?

To cut the back of your hair, you will need haircutting scissors, a comb, a handheld mirror, and clips to section off your hair.

How often should I cut the back of my hair?

You should trim the ends of your hair every six to eight weeks to keep your hair healthy. However, you can cut the back of your hair as often as you like to maintain your desired length and style.

Hair Care

Cutting your hair is just one part of a healthy hair care routine. To keep your hair looking its best, it is important to:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Eat a balanced diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair health.
  • Use hair masks and treatments to keep your hair moisturized and nourished.

By following these tips and incorporating them into your hair care routine, you can achieve healthy, strong, and beautiful hair.
