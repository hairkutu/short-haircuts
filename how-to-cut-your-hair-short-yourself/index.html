How To Cut Your Hair Short Yourself

Written by Bardi Jun 17, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking to cut your hair short yourself, you've come to the right place. Cutting your own hair can be a bit daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy cut from the comfort of your own home. In this article, we'll go over everything you need to know about how to cut your hair short yourself.

Description

Cutting your hair short yourself can be both fun and rewarding. Not only can it save you time and money, but it can also give you a sense of empowerment and independence. However, it's important to note that cutting your own hair can be tricky, especially if you're new to it. That's why it's crucial to have the right tools and techniques at your disposal.

How to Cut Your Hair Short Yourself

Before we dive into the step-by-step process of cutting your hair short yourself, let's go over the tools you'll need. Here's what you'll need to get started:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Comb
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Handheld mirror (optional)

Once you have all of your tools ready, it's time to get started. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to cut your hair short yourself:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

The first step to cutting your hair short yourself is to make sure it's clean and dry. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that you get an accurate cut.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, use your comb to section your hair into manageable sections. The number of sections you create will depend on how thick your hair is, but generally, you'll want to create four to six sections. Use your hair ties or clips to hold each section in place.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Once your hair is sectioned, it's time to start cutting. Start with the back of your head and work your way forward. Use your scissors to cut small sections of hair at a time, making sure to cut straight across. If you want a layered look, use your scissors to cut at an angle.

Step 4: Use Clippers (Optional)

If you want an even shorter cut, you can use clippers to shave the sides and back of your head. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one to achieve your desired length.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

Once you've finished cutting, use your handheld mirror to check the back of your head. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure that your hair is even.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Finally, style your hair as desired. You can blow-dry it or let it air dry, and use styling products to add volume or texture.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting your hair short yourself:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Take breaks if you need to, especially if you're new to cutting your own hair.
  • Don't be afraid to ask a friend or family member for help if you need it.

FAQ

Here are a few frequently asked questions about cutting your hair short yourself:

1. Can I cut my hair short myself?

Yes, you can definitely cut your hair short yourself. However, it's important to have the right tools and techniques to ensure a successful cut.

2. What tools do I need to cut my hair short myself?

You'll need sharp scissors, clippers (optional), a comb, hair ties or clips, and a handheld mirror (optional).

3. What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always make adjustments or go to a professional stylist for help.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair short yourself, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and tidy.
  • Eat a healthy diet and stay hydrated to promote healthy hair growth.

Cutting your hair short yourself can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a great cut from the comfort of your own home. Just remember to take your time, start with small sections, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it.
