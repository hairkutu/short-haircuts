Diy Layered Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 03, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle, consider a DIY layered short hair cut. This edgy and versatile look can be achieved at home with a few simple steps. In this guide, we'll cover everything you need to know about cutting your own hair, including tips, tricks, and frequently asked questions.

Table of Contents:

  • Description
  • How To
  • Step-by-Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

A layered short hair cut is a popular hairstyle that features shorter layers on top and longer layers on the bottom. This style is great for adding volume and texture to your hair, while still maintaining a chic and polished look. With a DIY layered short hair cut, you can save money and have complete control over your hairstyle.

How To

Before you begin cutting your hair, you'll need a few essential tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Mirror

Once you have your tools, you can start cutting your hair.

Step-by-Step

Follow these steps to achieve a layered short hair cut:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Divide your hair into sections using hair clips.
  3. Start cutting the back of your hair first.
  4. Using your comb, take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers at the desired length.
  5. Cut the hair straight across with your scissors.
  6. Repeat this process for each section of hair, working your way to the front.
  7. Once you've finished cutting the back and sides of your hair, move on to the top.
  8. Take small sections of hair and cut them at an angle, creating layers.
  9. Continue cutting until you've achieved your desired length and layers.
  10. Use your comb to blend the layers together.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting your own hair:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to longer cuts.
  • Use your fingers to measure the length of your hair instead of a ruler.
  • Don't be afraid to make mistakes. Hair grows back!
  • Take breaks if you feel tired or overwhelmed.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your hair, consider watching tutorials or consulting with a professional stylist.

FAQ

Can I cut my own hair if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your own hair if you have curly hair. However, keep in mind that curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so be conservative with your cuts.

Do I need to wet my hair before cutting it?

It's recommended to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can stretch and appear longer than it actually is.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain healthy hair and prevent split ends.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and style.

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

With these tips and tricks, you can achieve a DIY layered short hair cut that's both stylish and easy to maintain. Happy cutting!
