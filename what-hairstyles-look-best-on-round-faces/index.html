What Hairstyles Look Best on Round Faces: A Comprehensive Guide

If you have a round face, finding the perfect hairstyle can be challenging. You want to find a style that flatters your face shape and enhances your features. In this guide, we will provide you with tips and ideas for what hairstyles look best on round faces.

Table of Contents

  • How to Determine Your Face Shape
  • Hairstyles that Flatter Round Faces
  • Tips for Styling Round Faces
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Round Faces

How to Determine Your Face Shape

Before we dive into hairstyles, it's essential to determine your face shape. Round faces have a soft, circular shape with full cheeks and a rounded chin. To check if you have a round face, measure the width of your forehead, cheeks, and jawline. If all three measurements are similar in size, you likely have a round face.

Hairstyles that Flatter Round Faces

Now that you know your face shape let's explore hairstyles that flatter round faces. The goal is to create angles and elongate the face shape.

1. Long Layers

Long layers work well for round faces as they create a lengthening effect. The layers should start below the chin and gradually get longer towards the ends. Avoid layers that start above the chin or are too short as they can accentuate the roundness of your face.

2. Side-Swept Bangs

Side-swept bangs add angles to round faces and create a slimming effect. Avoid blunt bangs as they can make your face appear shorter and rounder.

3. Bob with Volume

A bob with volume at the crown can elongate a round face. The volume should be at the top of the head, not the sides. The length of the bob should be at the chin or below.

4. Pixie Cut

A pixie cut can work well for round faces if it's styled with volume on top. This adds height to the face and creates angles. Avoid a flat pixie cut as it can accentuate the roundness of your face.

5. Asymmetrical Cut

An asymmetrical cut with longer layers on one side can create angles and elongate a round face. The longer layers should be on the side opposite your fuller cheek.

Tips for Styling Round Faces

Now that you have some hairstyle ideas let's explore some tips for styling round faces.

1. Add Volume

Volumizing products can help add height to the crown of your head, creating a lengthening effect.

2. Avoid Center Parts

Avoid center parts as they can make your face appear rounder. Instead, opt for a side part to create angles.

3. Curl Away from Face

Curl your hair away from your face to create angles and slim your face. This technique adds volume and creates a lengthening effect.

4. Experiment with Accessories

Experiment with accessories such as headbands, scarves, and pins to add height and angles to your hairstyle.

FAQs

What is the best haircut for a round face?

The best haircut for a round face is one that creates angles and elongates the face shape. Long layers, side-swept bangs, bobs with volume, and asymmetrical cuts are all great options.

What hairstyles should I avoid with a round face?

Avoid hairstyles that add volume to the sides of your face, such as blunt bobs or straight-across bangs. These can accentuate the roundness of your face.

Hair Care Tips for Round Faces

1. Keep Your Hair Healthy

Healthy hair looks better and is easier to style. Use a deep conditioner once a week and get regular trims to prevent split ends.

2. Don't Overwash

Overwashing your hair can strip it of natural oils, leading to dryness and breakage. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days.

3. Protect Your Hair from Heat

Heat styling tools can damage your hair, leading to dryness and breakage. Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools.

4. Use the Right Products

Use products specifically formulated for your hair type to keep it healthy and looking its best. Avoid products that contain sulfates and silicones as they can be harsh on your hair.

Now that you have some ideas for what hairstyles look best on round faces, it's time to experiment and find a style that works for you. Remember to consider your face shape and features when choosing a hairstyle and have fun with it!
