How To High Taper Fade A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Apr 23, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you looking for a stylish and modern haircut that is easy to maintain? Look no further than the high taper fade. This popular haircut features a gradual fade from short hair on the sides and back to longer hair on top. It is a versatile style that can be worn in a variety of ways, from a slicked-back look to a messy, textured style. In this guide, we will walk you through the steps to achieve a high taper fade.

What You Will Need

Before you get started, make sure you have the following items:

  • Clippers
  • Clipper guards in various sizes
  • A comb
  • Scissors
  • A mirror

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Determine the Length of the Fade

The first step in achieving a high taper fade is to determine the length of the fade. This will depend on your personal preference and the shape of your head. A general rule of thumb is to start with a longer guard on the clippers and work your way down to a shorter guard as you move towards the bottom of the head.

Step 2: Start with the Sides

Begin by using the longest clipper guard to trim the hair on the sides and back of the head. Use a comb to guide the clippers and create a straight line around the head.

Step 3: Blend the Fade

Next, switch to a shorter clipper guard and blend the fade by trimming the hair around the edges of the longer hair. Use a comb to guide the clippers and create a smooth transition from short to long hair.

Step 4: Gradually Shorten the Hair

Continue to switch to shorter clipper guards and gradually shorten the hair as you move towards the bottom of the head. Use scissors to trim any longer hairs that are sticking out and creating an uneven look.

Step 5: Trim the Top

Once you have achieved the desired length on the sides and back, it's time to trim the hair on top. Use scissors to trim the hair to the desired length, taking care to create a smooth transition between the shorter hair on the sides and the longer hair on top.

Step 6: Style as Desired

Finally, style your hair as desired using a hair product such as pomade or wax. You can slick it back for a classic look or create a messy, textured style for a more modern look.

Tips for Achieving the Perfect High Taper Fade

  • Start with a longer clipper guard and work your way down to a shorter guard to achieve a gradual fade.
  • Use a comb to guide the clippers and create a straight line around the head.
  • Blend the fade by gradually shortening the hair around the edges of the longer hair.
  • Use scissors to trim any longer hairs that are sticking out and creating an uneven look.
  • Take your time and be patient. Achieving the perfect high taper fade may take some practice.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a high taper fade?

A high taper fade is a popular haircut that features a gradual fade from short hair on the sides and back to longer hair on top.

How do I achieve a high taper fade?

To achieve a high taper fade, start with a longer clipper guard and work your way down to a shorter guard as you move towards the bottom of the head. Use a comb to guide the clippers and create a smooth transition from short to long hair.

How long does a high taper fade last?

A high taper fade can last anywhere from a few weeks to a few months depending on how fast your hair grows and how well you maintain the style.

Hair Care for a High Taper Fade

To keep your high taper fade looking its best, it's important to take care of your hair. Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and healthy. Avoid using too much heat when styling your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage. Finally, be sure to get regular haircuts to maintain the shape and length of your high taper fade.

Now that you know how to achieve a high taper fade, it's time to give it a try. With a little practice and patience, you can achieve a stylish and modern look that is sure to turn heads.
