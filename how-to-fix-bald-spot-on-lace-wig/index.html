How To Fix Bald Spot On Lace Wig A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Aug 15, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're new to wearing lace wigs, you may be experiencing some bald spots on the lace. This can be frustrating and may make you want to give up on wearing lace wigs altogether. However, there are several ways to fix bald spots on lace wigs without having to buy a new one. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of fixing bald spots on lace wigs in a relaxed and easy-to-understand language. So, let's get started!

Description

A lace wig is a type of wig that is made with a sheer lace base that covers the scalp. The hair is hand-tied onto the lace base, creating a natural-looking hairline. However, sometimes the hair on the wig can thin out or fall out, leaving bald spots on the lace. This can be caused by several factors, including excessive pulling, heat damage, or improper maintenance. But don't worry, there are ways to fix these bald spots and restore your wig to its former glory.

How to Fix Bald Spot on Lace Wig

Step 1: Clean Your Wig

The first step in fixing bald spots on your lace wig is to clean it thoroughly. Use a wig shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for lace wigs to remove any dirt, oil, or product buildup. Rinse the wig thoroughly with cool water and gently pat dry with a towel. Avoid rubbing the wig as this can cause further damage to the lace.

Step 2: Apply a Wig Cap

To protect the lace while you work on the bald spots, apply a wig cap over the top of the wig. This will prevent any glue or adhesive from getting on the lace and causing further damage.

Step 3: Apply Glue or Adhesive

Apply a small amount of wig glue or adhesive to the bald spot on the lace. Use a toothpick or small brush to spread the glue evenly over the area. Be sure to apply the glue to the underside of the lace as well to ensure a secure hold.

Step 4: Apply Hair Netting

Once the glue has been applied, place a small piece of hair netting over the bald spot. This will help to hold the hair in place and prevent it from falling out again. Use a small brush to smooth any flyaways and ensure that the hair is evenly distributed over the netting.

Step 5: Style Your Wig

Once the hair netting is in place, style your wig as desired. You can use a flat iron or curling iron to create waves or curls, or you can simply brush it out for a sleek and smooth look.

Tips

  • Be gentle when working with your lace wig to avoid further damage.
  • Choose a wig glue or adhesive that is specifically designed for use on lace wigs.
  • Use a wig stand or mannequin head to hold your wig in place while you work on it.
  • When styling your wig, use heat protectant to prevent damage from hot tools.

FAQ

How long does the glue or adhesive hold?

The length of time that the glue or adhesive holds will depend on several factors, including the type of adhesive used, the amount applied, and how well the wig is maintained. Typically, the glue or adhesive will hold for several days to a week.

Can I use hair growth products to help fill in the bald spots?

While hair growth products may help to stimulate hair growth on your natural hair, they are not recommended for use on lace wigs. These products can cause damage to the lace and may not be effective in filling in the bald spots.

How often should I clean my lace wig?

You should clean your lace wig after every 6-8 wears or when it becomes visibly dirty or oily. Over-washing your wig can cause damage, so it's important to follow the manufacturer's instructions for cleaning and maintenance.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining the health and longevity of your lace wig. Be sure to use a wig brush or wide-toothed comb to detangle your wig gently. Avoid using heat on your wig unless absolutely necessary, and always use a heat protectant product. Store your wig on a wig stand or mannequin head to help maintain its shape and prevent tangling. And always follow the manufacturer's instructions for cleaning and maintenance to ensure that your wig lasts as long as possible.
