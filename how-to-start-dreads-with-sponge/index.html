How To Start Dreads With Sponge A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Apr 09, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you're looking for a simple and easy way to start dreadlocks, using a sponge may be the perfect solution for you. This method is becoming increasingly popular, as it doesn't require any harsh chemicals or tools, and is suitable for all hair types. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to start dreads with sponge, from preparation to hair care.

How to Prepare for Starting Dreads

Before you start dreading your hair with a sponge, there are a few things you should do to prepare your hair.

First, make sure your hair is clean and free of any product buildup. You can use a clarifying shampoo to help remove any residue from your hair.

Second, make sure your hair is completely dry before you start. Wet hair can be difficult to work with, and may not hold the dread as well.

Finally, make sure you have all the tools you need. You'll need a sponge with large holes, a spray bottle filled with water, and a hair tie or clip to keep your hair out of the way.

How to Use a Sponge for Dreads

Using a sponge for dreads is very simple. The sponge has large holes that are designed to help your hair lock together. To use it, you simply rub the sponge over your hair in a circular motion, using a twisting motion to help the hair lock together.

The sponge can be used on any hair type, but it works best on hair that is at least 2 inches long. If your hair is shorter than this, you may want to consider using a different method for starting your dreads.

Step-by-Step Guide to Starting Dreads with Sponge

Here's a step-by-step guide to starting your dreads with a sponge:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Divide your hair into sections using a hair tie or clip.
  3. Spray one section of hair with water until it is damp.
  4. Take the sponge and rub it over the damp section of hair in a circular motion.
  5. Use a twisting motion to help the hair lock together.
  6. Repeat steps 3-5 for each section of hair.
  7. Once all sections are done, use a hair tie or clip to keep the hair in place.
  8. Leave the hair tied up for at least a day or two to give the dreads time to set.
  9. After the dreads have set, you can take out the hair tie or clip and let your hair down.

Tips for Maintaining Your Dreads

Once you've started your dreads with a sponge, there are a few things you can do to help maintain them:

  • Avoid using products that contain silicone, as this can cause buildup in your dreads.
  • Wash your hair regularly, but be gentle. Use a residue-free shampoo and avoid rubbing your hair vigorously.
  • Use a crochet hook to tidy up loose hairs that may be sticking out of your dreads.
  • Avoid using a hair dryer, as this can cause your dreads to dry out and become brittle.

Frequently Asked Questions About Starting Dreads with Sponge

Q. Can I use a sponge to start dreads if I have relaxed hair?

A. Yes, you can use a sponge to start dreads even if you have relaxed hair. However, you should be aware that the process may take longer and require more maintenance than it would for natural hair.

Q. How long does it take for dreads to form using a sponge?

A. The length of time it takes for dreads to form using a sponge can vary depending on your hair type and how often you maintain your dreads. On average, it can take anywhere from 6 months to a year for your dreads to fully form.

Q. Can I wash my hair after starting dreads with a sponge?

A. Yes, you can wash your hair after starting dreads with a sponge. However, you should avoid using products that contain silicone, as this can cause buildup in your dreads.

How to Care for Your Dreads

Once you have your dreads, it's important to take good care of them to keep them looking their best. Here are a few tips for caring for your dreads:

  • Wash your hair regularly, but be gentle. Use a residue-free shampoo and avoid rubbing your hair vigorously.
  • Use a crochet hook to tidy up loose hairs that may be sticking out of your dreads.
  • Avoid using a hair dryer, as this can cause your dreads to dry out and become brittle.
  • Cover your hair when you sleep to help prevent frizz and loose hairs.
  • Be patient. Dreads take time to mature and develop, so don't get discouraged if they don't look perfect right away.

Starting dreads with a sponge is a simple and easy way to achieve the look you want without using harsh chemicals or tools. With a little patience and care, you can have beautiful, healthy dreads that you'll love for years to come.
