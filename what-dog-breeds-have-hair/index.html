What Dog Breeds Have Hair

Written by Bardi Jun 07, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Welcome to our blog post about what dog breeds have hair. If you're a dog lover, you may have noticed that some dogs have hair instead of fur. This can be a great option for people who have allergies or prefer a non-shedding dog. In this article, we'll explore what dog breeds have hair, how to care for their hair, and answer some frequently asked questions.

Table of Contents

  • Dog Breeds with Hair
  • How to Care for Hair
  • Tips for Maintaining Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions

Dog Breeds with Hair

There are several dog breeds that have hair instead of fur. Here are some of the most popular:

Poodle

The Poodle is probably the most well-known breed with hair. Their curly, dense coat is often styled in a variety of ways, from the classic "poodle cut" to more modern styles. Poodles come in three sizes: toy, miniature, and standard.

Yorkshire Terrier

The Yorkshire Terrier, or "Yorkie," is a small breed with a long, silky coat. While their hair is fine and delicate, it can grow quite long if not trimmed regularly. Yorkies are popular lap dogs and make great companions.

Maltese

The Maltese is another small breed with a long, silky coat. Their hair is often styled in a "puppy cut" to make it easier to maintain. Maltese dogs are known for their sweet temperament and love of attention.

Shih Tzu

The Shih Tzu is a small breed with a long, flowing coat. Their hair can be quite thick and requires daily brushing to prevent matting. Shih Tzu dogs are loyal and affectionate companions.

Havanese

The Havanese is a small breed with a wavy or curly coat. Their hair is often left long and can be styled in a variety of ways. Havanese dogs are known for their playful personalities and love of people.

How to Care for Hair

Caring for a dog with hair requires a bit more effort than caring for a dog with fur. Here are some tips:

Brushing

Dogs with hair require daily brushing to prevent matting and tangles. Use a slicker brush or comb to gently remove any knots or mats.

Bathing

Regular baths are important to keep your dog's hair clean and healthy. Use a mild shampoo and conditioner designed for dogs to prevent dryness and irritation.

Trimming

Regular trimming is necessary to keep your dog's hair at a manageable length. Depending on the breed, you may need to visit a professional groomer every 4-6 weeks.

Tips for Maintaining Hair

Here are some additional tips for maintaining your dog's hair:

Diet

A balanced diet is important for healthy hair. Make sure your dog is getting enough protein and essential fatty acids.

Exercise

Regular exercise can help promote healthy hair growth and reduce shedding.

Sun Protection

Just like humans, dogs can get sunburned. If your dog spends a lot of time outdoors, consider using a dog-specific sunscreen or providing shade.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do all dogs with hair have non-shedding coats?

No, not all dogs with hair are non-shedding. While they may shed less than dogs with fur, there is no guarantee that they won't shed at all.

Can I cut my dog's hair myself?

You can, but it's generally recommended to leave it to a professional groomer. They have the tools and experience to trim your dog's hair safely and evenly.

What should I do if my dog's hair becomes matted?

If your dog's hair becomes matted, don't try to cut it out yourself. Take your dog to a professional groomer who can safely remove the matting without hurting your dog.

Are there any health concerns associated with long hair?

Long hair can sometimes cause health issues such as skin irritation or infections. Make sure to keep your dog's hair clean and well-groomed to prevent these issues.

We hope this article has been helpful in answering your questions about what dog breeds have hair. Remember, caring for a dog with hair requires some extra effort, but the love and companionship they provide is well worth it!
