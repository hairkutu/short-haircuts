Will Bleached Hair Look Good On Me Tips Faq And Hair Care

Written by Petter Aug 23, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you considering bleaching your hair but wondering if it will look good on you? This is a common concern for many people who want to try out a new hair color. In this article, we will discuss the factors that determine if bleached hair will look good on you, how to prepare for the process, and how to take care of your bleached hair. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  1. Factors that Determine if Bleached Hair Will Look Good on You
  2. Preparation for Bleaching Your Hair
  3. Bleaching Your Hair: Step-by-Step
  4. Toning Your Hair
  5. Aftercare for Bleached Hair
  6. FAQ

Factors that Determine if Bleached Hair Will Look Good on You

There are several factors that determine if bleached hair will look good on you, including:

  • Your skin tone
  • Your natural hair color
  • Your hair texture
  • Your personal style

If you have warm undertones in your skin, blonde hair may complement you well. If you have cool undertones, platinum blonde or ash blonde may be a better choice. If you have naturally dark hair, you may need to bleach your hair several times to achieve the desired shade. If you have fine hair, bleaching may weaken your hair and cause breakage. If you have a bold and edgy personal style, bleached hair may be a great fit for you.

Preparation for Bleaching Your Hair

Before you bleach your hair, there are several steps you should take to ensure the best results:

  • Do a strand test to see how your hair will react to bleach
  • Deep condition your hair to strengthen it
  • Avoid washing your hair for a few days to allow your natural oils to protect your scalp
  • Protect your skin with petroleum jelly or a barrier cream

Bleaching Your Hair: Step-by-Step

Bleaching your hair can be a challenging process, but it can be done at home with the proper tools and knowledge. Here is a step-by-step guide:

  1. Section your hair into four parts
  2. Mix your bleach according to the instructions
  3. Apply the bleach to your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up
  4. Wrap your hair in foil or plastic wrap to trap heat and speed up the process
  5. Check your hair every 10-15 minutes to ensure it is not over-processed
  6. Rinse your hair thoroughly with cool water

Toning Your Hair

After bleaching your hair, you may need to tone it to achieve your desired shade. Toning helps to neutralize any unwanted brassy tones in your hair. Here are the steps to toning your hair:

  1. Mix your toner according to the instructions
  2. Apply the toner to your hair and let it sit for the recommended amount of time
  3. Rinse your hair thoroughly with cool water

Aftercare for Bleached Hair

Bleached hair requires special care to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips for aftercare:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid heat styling tools
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair
  • Avoid chlorine and salt water
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends

FAQ

Q: Will bleaching my hair damage it?

A: Bleaching can be damaging to your hair, but with proper care, you can minimize the damage.

Q: How often should I wash my bleached hair?

A: It is best to wash your bleached hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.

Q: Can I bleach my hair at home?

A: Yes, you can bleach your hair at home, but it is important to follow the instructions carefully and take the necessary precautions to protect your skin and hair.

Now that you have all the information you need, you can make an informed decision about whether bleached hair will look good on you. Remember to take care of your hair before and after bleaching, and enjoy your new look!
