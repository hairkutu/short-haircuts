How To Cut Toddler Boy Hair With Clippers At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Aug 11, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you tired of paying for expensive haircuts for your toddler boy? With the right tools and a little bit of practice, you can easily cut your toddler's hair at home with clippers. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to achieve a great haircut for your little one.

How to

Before you begin cutting your toddler's hair, it's important to gather the necessary tools. You'll need:

  • Clipper with guard attachments
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Haircutting cape or old towel
  • Spray bottle with water

Once you have your tools ready, follow these steps:

Step 1: Prep

Start by washing your toddler's hair and towel-drying it. Then, put on the haircutting cape or old towel to protect their clothing.

Step 2: Choose Guard

Decide on the length you want for your toddler's hair and choose the appropriate guard for your clippers. It's best to start with a longer guard and work your way down if needed.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Begin by using the clippers to cut the hair on the sides and back of your toddler's head. Start at the bottom and work your way up, going against the direction of hair growth. Use the comb to lift the hair and trim any longer strands with scissors.

Step 4: Cut Top

Next, move to the top of your toddler's head. Use the comb to lift the hair and cut it to the desired length with the clippers. Again, use scissors to trim any longer strands.

Step 5: Blend

To blend the top and sides, use the clippers without a guard to trim the hair around the ears and neckline. Use the comb to blend any harsh lines.

Step 6: Finish

Once you're done cutting, use the spray bottle to wet the hair and comb it to check for any uneven spots. Make any necessary adjustments with the clippers or scissors.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve a great haircut for your toddler:

  • Start with clean hair to make it easier to cut
  • Use a steady hand and go slow to avoid any accidents
  • Use scissors to trim any longer strands that the clippers missed
  • Keep your toddler distracted with toys or videos to make the process easier

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting toddler boy hair with clippers at home:

Is it safe to cut my toddler's hair at home?

Yes, as long as you have the proper tools and take your time, it's safe to cut your toddler's hair at home.

How often should I cut my toddler's hair?

It depends on how quickly their hair grows, but most toddlers need a haircut every 4-6 weeks.

What if my toddler won't sit still during the haircut?

Try distracting them with toys, videos, or snacks. If that doesn't work, consider cutting their hair in shorter sessions over a few days.

Hair Care

After the haircut, make sure to wash your toddler's hair to remove any loose hairs. Use a mild shampoo and conditioner to keep their hair healthy and soft.

Cutting your toddler boy's hair with clippers at home may seem daunting at first, but with a little practice, you'll be able to achieve great results. Follow these steps, use the proper tools, and take your time to ensure a successful haircut.
