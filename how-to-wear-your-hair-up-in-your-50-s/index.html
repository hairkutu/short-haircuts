How To Wear Your Hair Up In Your 50 S A Guide To Stylish And Comfortable Hairdos

Written by Christine Sep 01, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of the same old hairstyle and looking for ways to elevate your look? Wearing your hair up can be a great way to add sophistication and elegance to your appearance, and it's a practical choice for busy women who don't have time to fuss with their hair. In this guide, we'll show you how to wear your hair up in your 50's and provide tips on how to keep your hair healthy and vibrant.

Table of Contents

  • How to Wear Your Hair Up in Your 50's
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Your Hair Up
  • Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair

How to Wear Your Hair Up in Your 50's

Wearing your hair up doesn't mean you have to sacrifice style for comfort. There are many chic and sophisticated hairstyles that are perfect for women in their 50's. Here are some ideas to get you started:

Bun

A classic bun is a timeless hairstyle that works for any age. To make it more modern, add some texture to your hair before styling. You can use a curling iron or texturizing spray to create loose waves or curls. Then, gather your hair into a loose bun at the nape of your neck and secure it with bobby pins or a hair tie.

French Twist

A French twist is an elegant and sophisticated hairstyle that's perfect for special occasions. To create this look, gather your hair at the nape of your neck and twist it upwards, tucking the ends into the twist. Secure the twist with bobby pins and add some hairspray to keep it in place.

Braided Updo

A braided updo is a stylish and practical hairstyle that's perfect for busy women. To create this look, start by braiding your hair into a loose or tight braid. Then, twist the braid into a low bun and secure it with bobby pins. You can also add some volume to your hair by teasing the roots before braiding.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Your Hair Up

Now that you have some ideas for hairstyles, let's go over the steps for styling your hair up:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. If your hair is oily or greasy, it will be harder to style and hold the shape.
  2. Apply some styling product to your hair, such as mousse or texturizing spray. This will help add volume and texture to your hair.
  3. Use a comb or brush to smooth out any tangles or knots in your hair.
  4. Gather your hair into a ponytail or twist it into a bun at the nape of your neck.
  5. Secure your hair with bobby pins or a hair tie.
  6. Add some finishing touches, such as hairspray or hair accessories, to complete your look.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair

Wearing your hair up can be a great way to protect it from damage and keep it healthy. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as curling irons or straighteners, too often.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather by wearing a hat or scarf.
  • Eat a healthy diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth and strength.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I wear my hair up if it's short?

A: Yes, you can still wear your hair up if it's short. Try a half-up, half-down style or a braided updo.

Q: How do I keep my hair up all day without it falling out?

A: Use bobby pins or a hair tie to secure your hair tightly. You can also use hairspray or texturizing spray to add grip to your hair.

Hair Care Products and Tools

Here are some recommended hair care products and tools to help you achieve your desired hairstyle:

  • Texturizing spray
  • Curling iron
  • Bobby pins
  • Hair tie
  • Hairspray

With these tips and ideas, you can confidently wear your hair up in your 50's and look stylish and sophisticated. Remember to take care of your hair by using gentle products and protecting it from damage, and you'll have healthy and vibrant hair for years to come.
