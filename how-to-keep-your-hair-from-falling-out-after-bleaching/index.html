How To Keep Your Hair From Falling Out After Bleaching

Written by Daniel Jun 27, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're a fan of bold hair colors, you know that bleaching is the first step to achieving the perfect shade. Unfortunately, bleaching can be incredibly damaging to your hair, and if you're not careful, it can even cause your hair to fall out. In this guide, we'll show you how to keep your hair healthy and strong after bleaching.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Keep Your Hair from Falling Out After Bleaching
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Keeping Your Hair Healthy
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care Routine

How to Keep Your Hair from Falling Out After Bleaching

The first step to keeping your hair from falling out after bleaching is to make sure that you're using the right products. Look for products that are specifically designed for color-treated or bleached hair, as these will contain ingredients that can help repair and protect your hair.

Another important step is to avoid over-processing your hair. This means that you should wait at least a few weeks between bleaching and any other chemical treatments, such as coloring or perming. Additionally, try to limit your use of heat-styling tools like flat irons and curling irons, as these can also cause damage to your hair.

Finally, make sure that you're taking good care of your hair overall. This means maintaining a healthy diet, getting enough sleep, and staying hydrated. All of these factors can impact the health of your hair, so it's important to pay attention to them.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're new to bleaching your hair, it can be helpful to follow a step-by-step guide to make sure that you're doing it correctly. Here are the basic steps:

  1. Choose the right bleach and developer for your hair type and the level of lift that you want to achieve.
  2. Divide your hair into sections and apply the bleach mixture evenly, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  3. Check your hair regularly to make sure that it's not over-processing. You can do this by wiping away some of the bleach mixture and checking the color of your hair. If it's not light enough yet, you can reapply the bleach.
  4. Rinse out the bleach mixture thoroughly and shampoo your hair with a color-safe shampoo.
  5. Apply a deep conditioner to your hair and leave it on for at least 10 minutes.
  6. Rinse out the conditioner and style your hair as desired.

Tips for Keeping Your Hair Healthy

Here are some additional tips for keeping your hair healthy after bleaching:

  • Use a hair mask once a week to help repair and nourish your hair.
  • Avoid using hot water when you wash your hair, as this can strip away natural oils and cause damage.
  • Try to air-dry your hair whenever possible, and use a heat protectant spray if you do need to use a blow dryer or other heat-styling tool.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray.

FAQs

How often should I bleach my hair?

You should wait at least a few weeks between bleaching sessions to give your hair time to recover. Additionally, you should only bleach your hair as often as necessary to achieve your desired level of lightness.

Can I bleach my hair if it's already damaged?

If your hair is already damaged, it's best to wait until it's in better condition before bleaching. Otherwise, you risk causing even more damage and potentially causing your hair to fall out.

Hair Care Routine

After bleaching your hair, it's important to maintain a healthy hair care routine to keep your hair strong and healthy. Here's a basic routine to follow:

  • Wash your hair with a color-safe shampoo and conditioner.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to help repair and nourish your hair.
  • Avoid using heat-styling tools whenever possible, and use a heat protectant spray if you do need to use them.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and other damage.

Following these tips and steps can help you keep your hair healthy and prevent it from falling out after bleaching. Remember to be patient and gentle with your hair, and give it time to recover between chemical treatments.
