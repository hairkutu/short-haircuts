How To Do A 70S Flick Hairstyle A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Aug 31, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're looking to add a touch of retro glamour to your hairstyle, why not try a 70s flick? This iconic hairstyle was made popular by celebrities like Farrah Fawcett and has remained a classic look ever since. With a little bit of practice and the right tools, you can easily recreate this look at home. In this article, we'll walk you through how to do a 70s flick hairstyle.

  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Do a 70s Flick Hairstyle

The 70s flick is characterized by its feathered layers and flipped-out ends. Here's what you'll need to get started:

  • A heat protectant spray
  • A curling iron with a 1-inch barrel
  • A paddle brush
  • Hairspray

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to achieve the perfect 70s flick:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Apply a heat protectant spray to protect your hair from the heat of the curling iron.
  2. Section your hair into layers. Use a paddle brush to brush each layer smooth.
  3. Take a 1-inch section of hair and curl it away from your face using the curling iron. Hold the curl for 10-15 seconds, then release.
  4. Repeat step 3 for each section of hair, working your way up from the bottom layer to the top.
  5. Once you've curled all of your hair, use a paddle brush to brush through the curls. This will create a softer, more feathered look.
  6. To create the flick, use the curling iron to curl the ends of your hair outwards. Hold the curl for 10-15 seconds, then release.
  7. Finish with a light mist of hairspray to hold the style in place.

Tips for Perfecting the 70s Flick

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect 70s flick:

  • Practice makes perfect! It may take a few tries to get the hang of curling your hair in the right direction.
  • Choose the right size curling iron for your hair length. If you have shorter hair, a smaller barrel will give you tighter curls.
  • Brush through your curls to create a softer, more natural look.
  • If you have bangs, curl them away from your face to create a more cohesive look.
  • Have patience! This style takes a bit of time and effort, but the end result is worth it.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about the 70s flick hairstyle:

  • Can I do a 70s flick on short hair? Absolutely! Just use a smaller barrel curling iron to create tighter curls.
  • Can I do a 70s flick with a straightener? While it's possible to create curls with a straightener, a curling iron will give you a more defined and polished look.
  • How long does the 70s flick hairstyle last? The 70s flick can last for several days with proper care and maintenance.

Hair Care for the 70s Flick

To keep your 70s flick looking its best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Use a heat protectant spray before using any hot tools.
  • Avoid washing your hair every day, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils. Instead, try washing every other day or using dry shampoo in between washes.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, especially when it's wet.
  • Invest in a good quality hairspray to hold your style in place without weighing your hair down.

And that's it! With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to rock a 70s flick hairstyle like a pro. Remember to have fun with your hair and experiment with different styles to find what works best for you.
