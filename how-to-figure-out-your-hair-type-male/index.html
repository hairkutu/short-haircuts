How To Figure Out Your Hair Type Male A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Aprilia May 11, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Knowing your hair type is crucial in determining the right hair care products and styling methods that work best for you. However, figuring out your hair type can be challenging, especially for men who are not familiar with hair terminologies and styling techniques. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of determining your hair type, along with tips for hair care and styling.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Figure Out Your Hair Type
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Hair Care and Styling
  4. Frequently Asked Questions

How to Figure Out Your Hair Type

Understanding your hair type involves identifying your hair's texture, density, and porosity. These three factors determine how your hair behaves, what products work best, and what styling methods suit your hair.

Texture: This refers to the thickness or diameter of each hair strand. Hair texture is classified into three types:

  • Straight hair - fine, smooth, and shiny hair with no bends or curves
  • Wavy hair - hair that forms a loose S-shape, with a slight bend or curl
  • Curly hair - hair that forms a spiral or coil shape with tight curls

Density: This refers to the number of hair strands on your head. Hair density is classified into three types:

  • Low density - thin hair with fewer strands
  • Medium density - hair with an average number of strands
  • High density - thick hair with many strands

Porosity: This refers to your hair's ability to absorb and retain moisture. Hair porosity is classified into three types:

  • Low porosity - hair that is resistant to moisture absorption
  • Medium porosity - hair that absorbs and retains moisture easily
  • High porosity - hair that absorbs moisture quickly but loses it just as fast

Step-by-Step Guide

To determine your hair type, follow these steps:

  1. Observe your hair texture - is it straight, wavy, or curly?
  2. Check your hair density - are there few, average, or many strands of hair on your head?
  3. Assess your hair porosity - does your hair easily absorb and retain moisture?
  4. Based on the observations, you can now classify your hair type using the following categories:
    • Straight hair: Type 1
    • Wavy hair: Type 2
    • Curly hair: Type 3
    • Kinky hair: Type 4

Each hair type is further classified into subcategories based on the hair's texture, density, and porosity. For example, Type 2 hair is classified into 2A, 2B, and 2C, with 2A being the loosest wave pattern and 2C being the tightest wave pattern.

Tips for Hair Care and Styling

Once you have identified your hair type, you can now choose the appropriate hair care products and styling methods that work best for you. Here are some tips:

  • Choose hair care products that are specifically designed for your hair type.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals and heat styling tools that can damage your hair.
  • For curly and kinky hair types, consider using the LOC (liquid, oil, cream) or LCO (liquid, cream, oil) method to moisturize your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, especially for curly and kinky hair types.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and promote hair growth.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can my hair type change over time?

A: Yes, your hair type can change due to hormonal changes, aging, and other factors. It's essential to re-evaluate your hair type regularly and adjust your hair care routine accordingly.

Q: Do I need to use different hair care products for each hair type?

A: Yes, using hair care products that are specifically designed for your hair type can help improve your hair's health and appearance.

Q: Can I use the same styling method for different hair types?

A: No, different hair types require different styling methods to achieve the desired look. It's essential to research and learn about styling techniques that work best for your hair type.

In conclusion, identifying your hair type is essential in achieving healthy and stylish hair. By following the steps and tips outlined in this article, you can now determine your hair type and choose the appropriate hair care products and styling methods that work best for you.
