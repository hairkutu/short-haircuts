How Long Do Box Braids Last On Straight Hair

Written by Robby Mar 26, 2023 · 8 min read

Box braids are a popular protective hairstyle for many women with natural hair, but what about those with straight hair? If you're considering getting box braids, you may be wondering how long they will last on straight hair. In this article, we'll explore the answer to that question and provide some tips for caring for your box braids.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Get Box Braids on Straight Hair
  2. How Long Do Box Braids Last on Straight Hair?
  3. Tips for Caring for Box Braids on Straight Hair
  4. FAQs About Box Braids on Straight Hair
  5. How to Take Care of Your Hair After Box Braids

How to Get Box Braids on Straight Hair

Before we dive into how long box braids last on straight hair, let's first discuss how to get them. The process is similar to getting box braids on natural hair, but there are a few key differences.

First, you'll need to make sure your hair is clean and free of any product buildup. This will help the braids to lay flat and last longer. Next, you'll need to section your hair into small, even parts. The size of the sections will depend on how thick you want your braids to be.

Once your hair is sectioned, you'll need to braid each section tightly from the root to the end. If you have straight hair, you may need to add some synthetic hair to each braid to give it enough length for the box braids.

After all of your hair is braided, you'll need to dip the ends in hot water to seal them. This will prevent the braids from unraveling and help them to last longer.

How Long Do Box Braids Last on Straight Hair?

Now, let's get to the main question: how long do box braids last on straight hair? The answer to this question depends on a few factors, such as the size of the braids, the length of your hair, and how well you care for your braids.

On average, box braids can last anywhere from 4-8 weeks on straight hair. However, if you take good care of your braids, they can last up to 12 weeks or longer.

If you're looking to keep your box braids for an extended period of time, there are a few things you can do to help them last:

Tips for Caring for Box Braids on Straight Hair

  • Wrap your hair in a silk scarf or bonnet at night to prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Avoid getting your braids wet, as this can cause them to unravel and shorten their lifespan.
  • When washing your hair, use a diluted shampoo and focus on your scalp, not the braids themselves.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or oil to your scalp and braids to keep them moisturized.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products, as these can weigh down your braids and make them look frizzy.

FAQs About Box Braids on Straight Hair

Here are some frequently asked questions about box braids on straight hair:

1. Can I get box braids on relaxed hair?

Yes, you can get box braids on relaxed hair. However, you may need to add some synthetic hair to your braids to give them enough length.

2. How long does it take to get box braids on straight hair?

The amount of time it takes to get box braids on straight hair depends on the size of the braids and the length of your hair. On average, it can take anywhere from 4-8 hours.

3. How much do box braids cost?

The cost of box braids depends on a few factors, such as the size of the braids and the length of your hair. On average, you can expect to pay anywhere from $100-$300.

How to Take Care of Your Hair After Box Braids

Once you've had your box braids for a few weeks, it's time to start thinking about taking them out. To prevent any damage to your hair, it's important to remove your braids carefully.

Start by using a pair of scissors to cut the ends of each braid. Then, use a hair tie to secure the braid and gently unravel it from the bottom up. Once all of your braids are removed, wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup.

After your hair is clean, apply a deep conditioner to help restore moisture and prevent breakage. It's also a good idea to trim any split ends or damaged hair.

Overall, box braids can be a great protective hairstyle for those with straight hair. With proper care, they can last for several weeks and help to keep your hair healthy and strong.
