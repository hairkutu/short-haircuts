What Is The Least Common Hair Type

Written by Christine Jun 05, 2023 Â· 5 min read

When it comes to hair, there are a lot of different types out there. From straight to curly, thick to thin, and everything in between, everyone's hair is unique. However, there is one hair type that is considered to be the least common. In this blog post, we'll take a closer look at what this hair type is and what makes it so unique.

What is the Least Common Hair Type?

The least common hair type is known as Type 1A. This hair type is the straightest and thinnest of all the hair types, and it is characterized by its lack of volume and texture. According to the Andre Walker Hair Typing System, Type 1A hair is "very fine and straight" and "hard to hold a curl."

How to Identify Type 1A Hair

If you're not sure what type of hair you have, here are some characteristics of Type 1A hair to look out for:

  • Straight texture
  • No natural curl or wave
  • Fine diameter
  • Lacks volume

Why is Type 1A Hair Considered the Least Common?

There are a few reasons why Type 1A hair is considered to be the least common. For one, it is estimated that only about 2% of the population has Type 1A hair. Additionally, this hair type is often seen as less desirable than other hair types because it lacks volume and texture, which can make it difficult to style and can lead to a "flat" appearance.

How to Care for Type 1A Hair

While Type 1A hair may require a bit more effort to style, it is still important to take care of it properly in order to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips for caring for Type 1A hair:

  • Use a lightweight shampoo and conditioner to avoid weighing down the hair
  • Avoid using heavy styling products, which can make the hair look greasy or flat
  • Blow dry the hair using a round brush to add volume
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons or curling irons, which can damage the fragile hair

FAQ

Can Type 1A Hair Ever Have Volume?

While Type 1A hair may naturally lack volume, there are ways to add volume to this hair type. Blow drying the hair with a round brush, using volumizing products, and getting a layered haircut can all help to add volume and texture to Type 1A hair.

Is Type 1A Hair More Prone to Damage?

Due to its fine texture, Type 1A hair can be more prone to damage from heat styling, chemical treatments, and even brushing. It is important to be gentle with this hair type and to avoid over-styling or over-processing it.

The Bottom Line

While Type 1A hair may be the least common hair type, it is still a beautiful and unique hair type that deserves proper care and attention. By using the right products and styling techniques, you can keep your Type 1A hair looking healthy and voluminous.
