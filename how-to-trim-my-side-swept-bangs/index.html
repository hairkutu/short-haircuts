How To Trim My Side Swept Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Apr 07, 2023 · 9 min read

Side swept bangs are a great way to add some style and dimension to your hair. However, keeping them trimmed and looking their best can be a challenge. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps to trim your side swept bangs at home. Here’s what we’ll cover:

Table of Contents

  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Trimming Side Swept Bangs
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Side Swept Bangs

How to Trim My Side Swept Bangs

Trimming your side swept bangs can be a simple process if you follow these basic steps:

  1. Wash and dry your hair. Start with clean, dry hair so you can see the true length of your bangs.
  2. Section your bangs. Use a comb to create a triangle-shaped section of hair for your bangs. Make sure the point of the triangle is at the center of your forehead.
  3. Comb your bangs straight down. Use a fine-tooth comb to comb your bangs straight down over your forehead.
  4. Decide on the length. Decide on the length you want your bangs to be. It’s always better to start with a longer length and trim gradually, so don’t go too short at first.
  5. Start trimming. Use hair cutting scissors to cut your bangs in a straight line across. Start at the center of your forehead and work your way outward.
  6. Check your work. Take a step back from the mirror and make sure your bangs are even. If not, trim any uneven pieces carefully.
  7. Blend the edges. Use your scissors to blend the edges of your bangs into the rest of your hair for a natural look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the basic steps, let’s take a closer look at how to trim your side swept bangs:

Step 1: Wash and dry your hair

Start with clean, dry hair. This will help you see the true length of your bangs and make it easier to cut them evenly.

Step 2: Section your bangs

Use a comb to create a triangle-shaped section of hair for your bangs. Make sure the point of the triangle is at the center of your forehead.

Step 3: Comb your bangs straight down

Use a fine-tooth comb to comb your bangs straight down over your forehead. This will make it easier to see the length you want to cut.

Step 4: Decide on the length

Decide on the length you want your bangs to be. It’s always better to start with a longer length and trim gradually, so don’t go too short at first.

Step 5: Start trimming

Use hair cutting scissors to cut your bangs in a straight line across. Start at the center of your forehead and work your way outward. Make sure to cut in small sections to avoid any mistakes.

Step 6: Check your work

Take a step back from the mirror and make sure your bangs are even. If not, trim any uneven pieces carefully.

Step 7: Blend the edges

Use your scissors to blend the edges of your bangs into the rest of your hair for a natural look. This will help your bangs look more natural and less choppy.

Tips for Trimming Side Swept Bangs

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when trimming your side swept bangs:

  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors. Dull scissors can lead to uneven cuts and damage your hair.
  • Start with a longer length. It’s easier to cut more hair off than it is to add it back on.
  • Take your time. Cutting your bangs too quickly can lead to mistakes and uneven cuts.
  • Keep your hair dry. Wet hair can lead to uneven cuts and make it harder to see the true length of your bangs.
  • Don’t cut your bangs too short. It’s better to err on the side of caution and cut less hair off at first.

FAQs

How often should I trim my side swept bangs?

You should trim your side swept bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking their best.

Can I trim my side swept bangs myself?

Yes, you can trim your side swept bangs yourself with the proper tools and technique. Just make sure to take your time and follow the steps outlined in this guide.

What should I do if I make a mistake while trimming my bangs?

If you make a mistake while trimming your bangs, don’t panic. You can always visit a professional stylist to fix the problem. In the meantime, try to blend any uneven pieces into your hair for a more natural look.

Hair Care for Side Swept Bangs

To keep your side swept bangs looking their best, it’s important to take good care of your hair. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to nourish your hair.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or paddle brush to detangle your hair gently.
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair soft and shiny.

Trimming your side swept bangs at home can be a simple process if you follow these steps and tips. Just remember to take your time, start with a longer length, and use a sharp pair of scissors. With a little practice, you’ll be able to keep your bangs looking stylish and fresh between salon visits.
