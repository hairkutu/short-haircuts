How To Cut Short Layered Bangs A Complete Guide

Are you tired of your boring hairstyle and want to switch it up? Short layered bangs might just be the perfect solution for you! Not only do they add a fun and edgy look to your hair, but they're also easy to maintain. In this article, we'll walk you through step by step on how to cut short layered bangs. Let's get started!

How to Cut Short Layered Bangs

Before we get into the details, it's important to note that cutting your own bangs can be tricky. It's always best to seek the help of a professional stylist if you're not confident in your abilities. However, if you're determined to give it a try, follow these steps:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

You'll need a good pair of scissors, a comb, and hair clips to hold your hair in place. Make sure your scissors are sharp and clean to ensure a clean cut.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair. Use a comb to create a triangle section at the front of your head. The base of the triangle should be at your hairline, and the tip of the triangle should be at the crown of your head.

Step 3: Cut the Bangs

Hold the section of hair straight up with your fingers and cut across the bottom. Cut slowly and in small increments to avoid cutting too much hair off at once. Remember, you can always cut more, but you can't add hair back.

Step 4: Layer the Bangs

To create a layered effect, take another small section of hair from underneath the first section and cut it slightly shorter than the first. Continue this process until you've cut all the hair in the triangle section.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Blend the layers by taking small sections of hair between your fingers and gently pulling them upward. Use your scissors to trim any uneven ends until you achieve the desired look.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a more detailed guide on how to cut short layered bangs:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Make sure your hair is clean and dry before cutting it. Wet hair tends to look longer than it actually is, which can result in uneven cutting.

Step 2: Comb Your Hair

Use a comb to smooth out any tangles and make sure your hair is straight. This will ensure a clean and even cut.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Create a triangle section at the front of your head, as mentioned in the previous section.

Step 4: Cut the Bangs

Hold the first section of hair straight up with your fingers and cut across the bottom. Make sure to cut in small increments to avoid cutting too much hair off at once.

Step 5: Layer the Bangs

Take another small section of hair from underneath the first section and cut it slightly shorter than the first. Continue this process until you've cut all the hair in the triangle section.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Use your scissors to blend the layers by taking small sections of hair between your fingers and gently pulling them upward. Trim any uneven ends until you achieve the desired look.

Tips for Cutting Short Layered Bangs

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting short layered bangs:

  • Start by cutting less hair than you think you need. You can always cut more later.
  • Use sharp and clean scissors to avoid jagged ends.
  • Take breaks if you feel tired or frustrated. Cutting your own hair can be a tedious process.
  • Don't cut your bangs too short. They'll look shorter once they dry and you don't want to end up with bangs that are too short for your face shape.

FAQs

Can I cut my own short layered bangs?

Yes, you can cut your own short layered bangs. However, it's always best to seek the help of a professional stylist if you're not confident in your abilities.

How often should I cut my short layered bangs?

It's recommended to trim your short layered bangs every 4-6 weeks to maintain their shape and length.

What face shapes look best with short layered bangs?

Short layered bangs look great on oval, heart-shaped, and square-shaped faces. However, it's important to consider your hair texture and personal style when deciding on a haircut.

Hair Care After Cutting Short Layered Bangs

Now that you've cut your short layered bangs, it's important to take care of them. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools.
  • Trim your bangs regularly to maintain their shape and length.
  • Avoid using too much product in your hair as it can weigh down your bangs and make them look oily.
  • Consider using a dry shampoo to keep your bangs looking fresh in between washes.

Cutting short layered bangs can be a fun and exciting way to switch up your hairstyle. Just remember to take your time, use the right tools, and seek the help of a professional if necessary. With these tips and tricks, you'll be rocking your new look in no time!
