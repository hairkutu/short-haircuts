How To Low Taper Fade Your Own Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter May 29, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking for a fresh new haircut, a low taper fade might be the perfect choice for you. This haircut is characterized by short hair on the sides and back of your head that gradually fades into longer hair on top. Not only is it stylish, but it's also low maintenance and easy to style. In this article, we'll provide a step-by-step guide on how to low taper fade your own hair at home.

Description: What is a Low Taper Fade?

A low taper fade is a type of haircut that gradually fades from short hair on the sides and back of your head to longer hair on top. It's a popular choice for men of all ages and works well with a variety of hairstyles. The low taper fade blends the hair seamlessly, creating a natural look that's easy to maintain. This haircut is perfect for anyone who wants a stylish, low maintenance look.

How to Low Taper Fade Your Own Hair

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you begin, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of clippers, scissors, a comb, and a mirror. It's also helpful to have a spray bottle filled with water.

Step 2: Start with the Sides

Begin by trimming the sides of your hair with the clippers. Start with a longer guard and gradually work your way down to a shorter guard. Use the comb to guide the clippers and create a straight, even line. Remember to start at the bottom and work your way up.

Step 3: Create the Fade

Once you've trimmed the sides, it's time to create the fade. Switch to a shorter guard and use the clippers to fade the hair from the sides to the top. Use a longer guard at first and gradually work your way down to a shorter guard. Blend the hair seamlessly, making sure there are no harsh lines.

Step 4: Trim the Top

Once you've created the fade, it's time to trim the top. Use the scissors to cut the hair on top to your desired length. Use the comb to guide the scissors and create a uniform cut. You can also use the scissors to blend the hair at the top with the fade.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Once you've finished cutting your hair, it's time to style it. Apply a small amount of hair product to your hair and use your fingers to style it. You can also use a comb or brush to create a more polished look.

Tips for Low Taper Fading Your Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect low taper fade:

  • Start with a longer guard and gradually work your way down to a shorter guard.
  • Use the comb to guide the clippers and create a straight, even line.
  • Blend the hair seamlessly, making sure there are no harsh lines.
  • Use the scissors to blend the hair at the top with the fade.
  • Apply a small amount of hair product to your hair and use your fingers to style it.

FAQs

What is the difference between a low taper fade and a regular taper fade?

A low taper fade starts with shorter hair on the sides and gradually fades into longer hair on top. A regular taper fade is more gradual, with the hair gradually getting shorter towards the top.

Can I low taper fade my own hair?

Yes, you can low taper fade your own hair at home with the right tools and technique.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some tips to keep your hair looking its best:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a mild shampoo.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Use hair products that are suitable for your hair type.

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to achieve the perfect low taper fade at home. Remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun! Experiment with different styles and find the one that works best for you.
