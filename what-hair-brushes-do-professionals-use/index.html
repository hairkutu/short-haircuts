What Hair Brushes Do Professionals Use The Ultimate Guide

When it comes to achieving a perfect hairstyle, a good hairbrush can make all the difference. But with so many options out there, it can be overwhelming to know which one to choose. That's why we've compiled a guide on what hair brushes professionals use and why.

Boar Bristle Brushes

Boar bristle brushes are a favorite among professionals because they distribute natural oils throughout the hair, leaving it looking shiny and healthy. The bristles are also gentle on the scalp, making it a great option for those with sensitive skin. These brushes work well on all hair types, but are especially beneficial for those with fine or thin hair.

Vent Brushes

Vent brushes are designed to help speed up the drying process by allowing air to circulate through the brush. They are a popular choice for professionals because they are lightweight and easy to maneuver. Vent brushes work best on short to medium-length hair and can also be used for creating volume at the roots.

Paddle Brushes

Paddle brushes are great for detangling and smoothing long, straight hair. They have a large, flat surface area that makes it easy to brush through thick sections of hair. Paddle brushes can also be used for creating sleek and straight styles.

Round Brushes

Round brushes are a versatile tool that can be used for creating a variety of styles, from loose waves to tight curls. They come in a range of sizes, with smaller brushes being best for creating tighter curls and larger ones for looser waves. Round brushes are also great for adding volume to the hair at the roots.

Teasing Brushes

Teasing brushes, also known as backcombing brushes, are used for creating volume and texture in the hair. They have tightly packed bristles that make it easy to backcomb the hair at the roots. Teasing brushes are best for short to medium-length hair and can be used to create everything from subtle volume to dramatic updos.

Wide-Tooth Combs

Wide-tooth combs are a gentle option for detangling wet hair. They are designed to glide through the hair without causing damage or pulling. Wide-tooth combs are a great choice for those with curly or textured hair, as they help to maintain the shape of the curls and prevent frizz.

Rat Tail Combs

Rat tail combs, also known as tail combs, are a staple tool for creating precise parts and sections in the hair. They have a long, thin tail that makes it easy to section the hair and create clean lines. Rat tail combs are also great for creating updos and intricate styles.

How to Choose the Right Brush

Choosing the right brush depends on your hair type and the style you want to achieve. Here are some tips for selecting the right brush:

  • If you have fine or thin hair, opt for a boar bristle brush or a vent brush.
  • If you have long and straight hair, a paddle brush is a great option for detangling and smoothing.
  • If you have curly or textured hair, use a wide-tooth comb or a round brush to maintain the shape of your curls.
  • If you want to create volume or texture, a teasing brush or a round brush is the way to go.

Hair Brush FAQ

How often should I clean my hairbrush?

You should aim to clean your hairbrush at least once a week to remove any buildup of hair products or oils. Use a comb or brush cleaner to remove the hair and then soak the brush in warm water with a little shampoo or baking soda. Rinse thoroughly and let it air dry.

Can I use a brush on wet hair?

It's best to use a wide-tooth comb on wet hair to prevent damage and breakage. However, if you need to use a brush, opt for a vent brush or a paddle brush with gentle bristles.

Can I use the same brush for different hair types?

It's best to use a brush that is suited to your hair type to avoid damage and breakage. However, some brushes, such as vent brushes and wide-tooth combs, can be used on all hair types.

Hair Care Tips

Using the right hairbrush is just one part of a healthy haircare routine. Here are some other tips for keeping your hair looking its best:

  • Use a heat protectant spray when using hot tools
  • Avoid using tight hair ties that can cause breakage
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends
  • Avoid washing your hair every day to prevent stripping natural oils
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy

By following these tips and using the right hairbrush, you can achieve a salon-worthy hairstyle from the comfort of your own home.
