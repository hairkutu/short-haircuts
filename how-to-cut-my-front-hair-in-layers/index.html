How To Cut My Front Hair In Layers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel May 04, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking to change up your hairstyle without going for a full chop, consider adding some layers to your front hair. Layering your hair can add volume, texture, and movement to your look, and it's relatively easy to do at home. In this step-by-step guide, we'll walk you through how to cut your front hair in layers, along with tips for styling and caring for your new 'do.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut My Front Hair in Layers
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Layering Your Front Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Layered Hair

How to Cut My Front Hair in Layers

Before you start cutting, it's important to have a clear idea of the type of layers you want. Do you want long, face-framing layers or shorter, choppy layers? Once you have a plan in mind, follow these steps:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. Brush out any knots or tangles, and make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, section off the front portion of your hair that you want to cut into layers. Use a comb to create a horizontal part from ear to ear, and clip the back section out of the way.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Take a small section of hair from the front, and hold it between your fingers. Use a pair of scissors to cut the hair at an angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up. This will create a gradual, layered effect.

Continue cutting small sections of hair, working your way from one side of your head to the other. Be sure to keep your fingers parallel to the angle of your cut, so that your layers are even.

Step 4: Check Your Progress

Once you've finished cutting, release the back section of your hair and check your progress in the mirror. Make any adjustments as needed to create a balanced, layered look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a quick recap of the steps involved in cutting your front hair in layers:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Section off the front portion of your hair.
  3. Start cutting small sections of hair at an angle, working your way from one side to the other.
  4. Check your progress in the mirror, and make any necessary adjustments.

Tips for Layering Your Front Hair

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting your front hair in layers:

  • Start with a small amount of hair, and cut slowly. You can always take more off, but you can't put it back on!
  • Use sharp scissors designed for cutting hair, not kitchen scissors or craft scissors.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet; it's harder to control and may result in uneven layers.
  • Consider using a razor comb for a softer, more textured look.

FAQs

Here are a few frequently asked questions about cutting front hair in layers:

How often should I cut my layers?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your layers lose their shape. As a general rule, you should get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your layers looking fresh.

Can I layer my own hair?

Yes, you can layer your own hair at home. Just be sure to follow the steps above and take your time!

What if I mess up?

If you make a mistake and cut your hair too short or unevenly, don't panic. You can always book an appointment with a professional stylist to fix it, or try styling your hair in a different way to disguise any mistakes.

Hair Care Tips for Layered Hair

Once you've cut your front hair in layers, you'll want to take good care of your new 'do to keep it looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner designed for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair and cause split ends.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your locks hydrated and healthy.
  • Get regular trims to keep your layers looking fresh.

With these tips and techniques, you can easily cut your front hair in layers at home and enjoy a fresh, new look. Just remember to take your time, use the right tools, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it!
