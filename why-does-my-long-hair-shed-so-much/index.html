Why Does My Long Hair Shed So Much Understanding The Causes And Solutions

Written by Frank Jul 03, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Long hair has always been associated with beauty, femininity, and glamour. However, when it comes to hair shedding, long hair can be a nightmare. If you're one of those people who have been wondering "why does my long hair shed so much," you're not alone.

Table of Contents

  • Causes of Hair Shedding
  • Solutions to Hair Shedding
  • Hair Care Tips to Prevent Shedding
  • FAQs About Hair Shedding

Causes of Hair Shedding

There are several reasons why your long hair may be shedding more than usual:

Hormonal Changes

Hormonal changes can affect the hair growth cycle and lead to shedding. For women, hormonal changes during pregnancy, menopause, and thyroid disorders can all cause hair loss.

Stress

Stress can affect the hair growth cycle and cause hair to shed. If you're going through a stressful period, you may notice more hair shedding than usual.

Poor Nutrition

A diet lacking in essential nutrients can lead to hair shedding. Make sure you're eating a balanced diet with plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.

Over-Styling

Over-styling your hair with heat tools, chemical treatments, and tight hairstyles can damage the hair follicles and cause hair to shed.

Solutions to Hair Shedding

If you're experiencing excessive hair shedding, there are several solutions you can try:

Consult a Doctor

If you suspect that a medical condition is causing your hair shedding, consult a doctor. They can diagnose and treat any underlying conditions that may be contributing to your hair loss.

Reduce Stress

Reducing stress through exercise, meditation, or other relaxation techniques can help reduce hair shedding.

Eat a Balanced Diet

Eating a diet rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals can help promote healthy hair growth and reduce shedding.

Avoid Over-Styling

Avoid using heat tools, chemical treatments, and tight hairstyles that can damage your hair and cause shedding.

Hair Care Tips to Prevent Shedding

Here are some hair care tips to prevent shedding:

Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair instead of a brush that can pull and tug on your hair.

Avoid Over-Washing

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and brittle, leading to shedding. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days.

Use a Gentle Shampoo

Use a gentle shampoo that's free of sulfates and harsh chemicals that can strip your hair of its natural oils.

Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid hairstyles that pull on your hair, such as tight braids, ponytails, and buns, as they can damage your hair and cause shedding.

FAQs About Hair Shedding

Q: Is it normal to shed hair every day?

A: Yes, it's normal to shed 50-100 hairs a day as part of the hair growth cycle.

Q: Can stress cause hair shedding?

A: Yes, stress can affect the hair growth cycle and cause hair to shed.

Q: How long does it take for hair to grow back after shedding?

A: It can take several months for hair to grow back after shedding, depending on the cause of the shedding.

Q: Can over-styling cause hair shedding?

A: Yes, over-styling your hair with heat tools, chemical treatments, and tight hairstyles can damage the hair follicles and cause hair to shed.

By understanding the causes of hair shedding and implementing the solutions and tips outlined in this article, you can minimize hair shedding and promote healthy hair growth.
