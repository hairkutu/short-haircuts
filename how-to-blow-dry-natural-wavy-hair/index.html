How to Blow Dry Natural Wavy Hair

Before we get into the specifics of how to blow dry natural wavy hair, it's important to know that the process will vary depending on your hair type, length, and texture. However, the following general steps can be applied to most types of natural wavy hair:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner that's designed for wavy hair. After washing, gently pat your hair dry with a towel to remove excess water. Avoid rubbing your hair vigorously with the towel, as this can cause frizz.

Step 2: Apply Heat Protectant

Apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair to protect it from heat damage. This will also help to reduce frizz and flyaways.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using hair clips. This will make it easier to blow dry and ensure that all sections are evenly dried.

Step 4: Choose Your Blow Dryer

Choose a high-quality blow dryer with a diffuser attachment. A diffuser will help to distribute heat evenly and enhance your natural waves.

Step 5: Start Blow Drying

Start blow drying your hair on a low heat setting, using the diffuser attachment. Hold the diffuser close to your head and cup your hair in the diffuser, gently scrunching as you go. Continue this process until your hair is about 80% dry.

Step 6: Finish with High Heat

Once your hair is about 80% dry, switch to a high heat setting and continue blow drying. Use your fingers to scrunch your hair as you go, and focus on the areas that need extra attention. Be careful not to over-dry your hair, as this can cause frizz.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Now that we've gone through the general steps for blow drying natural wavy hair, let's break it down into a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner that's designed for wavy hair. Rinse thoroughly and gently pat your hair dry with a towel to remove excess water.

Step 2: Prep Your Hair

Apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair to protect it from heat damage. Brush your hair gently to remove any tangles.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using hair clips. Start at the bottom of your hair and work your way up, clipping each section as you go.

Step 4: Choose Your Blow Dryer

Choose a high-quality blow dryer with a diffuser attachment. This will help to enhance your natural waves and reduce frizz.

Step 5: Start Blow Drying

Start blow drying your hair on a low heat setting, using the diffuser attachment. Hold the diffuser close to your head and cup your hair in the diffuser, gently scrunching as you go. Continue this process until your hair is about 80% dry.

Step 6: Finish with High Heat

Once your hair is about 80% dry, switch to a high heat setting and continue blow drying. Use your fingers to scrunch your hair as you go, and focus on the areas that need extra attention. Be careful not to over-dry your hair, as this can cause frizz.

Tips for Blow Drying Natural Wavy Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you blow dry natural wavy hair:

  • Use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle your hair before blow drying.
  • Blot your hair with a microfiber towel to remove excess water before blow drying.
  • Don't use a brush on your hair while blow drying, as this can cause frizz.
  • Set your blow dryer to a cool setting for a few minutes at the end of your blow dry to help set your waves.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about blow drying natural wavy hair:

Q: Can I blow dry my hair every day?

A: Blow drying your hair every day can cause damage and dryness. It's best to let your hair air dry occasionally, and only blow dry when necessary.

Q: Can I blow dry my hair without a diffuser?

A: While a diffuser can help to enhance your natural waves, it's not necessary. You can still blow dry your hair without a diffuser, but be sure to use a low heat setting and avoid over-drying your hair.

Q: How can I prevent frizz while blow drying my hair?

A: To prevent frizz, use a heat protectant spray or serum, and avoid rubbing your hair with a towel. You can also try using a microfiber towel to blot your hair dry.

Hair Care for Natural Wavy Hair

Blow drying natural wavy hair is just one part of a good hair care routine. Here are some additional tips for caring for your natural wavy hair:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner that's designed for wavy hair.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to your hair to keep it moisturized.

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to blow dry natural wavy hair with ease. Remember to take care of your hair with a good hair care routine, and it will reward you with beautiful, healthy waves.
