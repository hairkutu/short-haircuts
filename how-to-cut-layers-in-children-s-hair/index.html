How To Cut Layers In Children s Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter May 11, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you tired of your child's hair looking dull and lifeless? Adding layers to their hair can give it volume and texture, making it look healthier and more vibrant. Cutting layers in children's hair may seem like a daunting task, but with the right tools and technique, it can be done easily at home. In this guide, we'll take you through the steps of how to cut layers in children's hair, along with some tips and tricks to make the process smoother.

How to Cut Layers in Children's Hair

Step 1: Gather the Right Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You will need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Make sure your scissors are sharp and clean. Dull scissors can damage the hair and create split ends.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Divide the hair into sections. You can do this by parting the hair down the middle and then from ear to ear. Use hair clips to secure the sections you are not working on.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Starting from the back, take a small section of hair and comb it straight. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it at an angle. The shorter the hair you cut, the steeper the angle should be. Repeat this process for each section of hair.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you have cut all the sections, comb through the hair to blend the layers. This will create a natural-looking finish.

Step 5: Check the Layers

Once you have finished cutting, check the layers to make sure they are even. If you notice any unevenness, go back and trim the hair until it is even.

Tips for Cutting Layers in Children's Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting layers in children's hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up. It's better to cut too little than too much.
  • Use a spray bottle to keep the hair damp. This will make it easier to cut and prevent flyaways.
  • Don't cut the hair too short. Leave some length so that the layers are visible.
  • Use a comb to lift the hair and cut at an angle. This will create a natural-looking finish.

FAQ

Can I cut layers in my child's hair if they have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut layers in curly hair. However, make sure you cut the hair when it is dry and in its natural state. This will help you see the curl pattern and avoid cutting too much.

How often should I cut my child's hair?

It depends on how quickly their hair grows. On average, children's hair grows about half an inch per month. You can cut their hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the layers.

Hair Care

After cutting layers in your child's hair, make sure to take good care of it. Use a mild shampoo and conditioner and avoid using hot styling tools too often. Trim the hair regularly to maintain the layers and prevent split ends.

Cutting layers in children's hair can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and technique, you can give your child's hair a new lease on life. Follow these steps and tips to create a beautiful and natural-looking finish.
