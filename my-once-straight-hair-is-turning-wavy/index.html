My Once Straight Hair Is Turning Wavy Understanding The Changes And How To Deal With Them

Written by Petter Jul 08, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you are someone who used to have straight hair but has noticed it turning wavy, you are not alone. This phenomenon is quite common, and it can be attributed to a variety of factors. In this blog post, we will explore why your once straight hair is turning wavy, how to deal with this change, and some tips for taking care of your new wavy locks.

Why is My Hair Turning Wavy?

There are several reasons why your hair may be turning wavy. One of the most common reasons is hormonal changes. As we age, our hormone levels fluctuate, and this can affect the texture of our hair. Another reason may be changes in the environment. For example, if you move to a more humid location, your hair may start to curl or wave. Finally, changes in hair care routines or styling habits can also impact the texture of your hair.

How to Deal with Wavy Hair

Dealing with wavy hair can be a bit of a challenge, especially if you are used to having straight hair. Here are some tips to help you manage your new texture:

1. Embrace the Waves

The first step in dealing with wavy hair is to embrace it. Rather than fighting against your hair's natural texture, work with it. This may mean changing your hair care routine or trying out new styling techniques that enhance your waves.

2. Use the Right Products

Using the right hair care products can make a big difference in the texture and appearance of your hair. Look for products that are specifically designed for wavy hair, such as shampoos and conditioners that provide moisture and definition.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling, such as blow drying and straightening, can damage wavy hair and make it frizzy. If you must use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

4. Get Regular Haircuts

Getting regular haircuts is important for keeping your hair healthy and maintaining its shape. For wavy hair, it's best to get a cut that enhances your natural texture. Ask your stylist for recommendations.

Step-by-Step Guide: Styling Wavy Hair

Now that you know how to deal with wavy hair, let's take a look at a step-by-step guide for styling your new texture:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a shampoo and conditioner designed for wavy hair. Be sure to rinse thoroughly.

Step 2: Apply Styling Products

Apply a styling product, such as a curl cream or mousse, to damp hair. Be sure to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

Step 3: Scrunch Your Hair

Scrunch your hair with your hands to encourage your natural waves. Avoid using a towel to dry your hair, as this can cause frizz.

Step 4: Air Dry or Diffuse

Allow your hair to air dry or use a diffuser on your hair dryer to dry your hair. Avoid using a regular hair dryer, as this can cause frizz.

Step 5: Finish with a Serum

Finish off your wavy hairstyle with a serum or oil to add shine and tame frizz.

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about wavy hair:

Q: Can I straighten my wavy hair?

A: Yes, you can straighten your wavy hair. However, be sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low to avoid damaging your hair.

Q: How often should I wash my wavy hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have oily hair, you may need to wash it more frequently. However, washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, so aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days.

Q: Can I still use a straightening iron on my wavy hair?

A: Yes, you can still use a straightening iron on your wavy hair. However, be sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low to avoid damaging your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Wavy Hair

Here are some additional hair care tips for wavy hair:

1. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

When detangling your hair, use a wide-tooth comb to avoid causing damage or breakage.

2. Sleep on a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help prevent frizz and breakage.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun can damage your hair, so be sure to wear a hat or use a hair product with SPF when spending time outdoors.

4. Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims can help prevent split ends and keep your hair healthy.

5. Experiment with Different Styles

Wavy hair can be versatile, so don't be afraid to experiment with different styles, such as braids, buns, and updos.

With these tips and tricks, you can embrace your new wavy texture and rock your locks with confidence!
