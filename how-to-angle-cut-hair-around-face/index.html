How To Angle Cut Hair Around Face A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Apr 09, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you looking for a way to add some volume and dimension to your hair without sacrificing length? Angled haircuts around the face can be a great option. This style creates layers that frame your face, giving you a fresh and youthful look. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps of how to angle cut hair around the face in a relaxed and easy-to-understand language.

How to Angle Cut Hair Around Face

Before we start, it's important to note that if you're not experienced with cutting hair, you may want to consider going to a professional stylist. However, if you're confident in your skills and want to try it yourself, here's how to angle cut hair around the face:

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair

Wash your hair and let it fully dry before you begin cutting. This will give you a more accurate idea of how your hair will fall naturally.

Step 2: Section off your hair

Use a comb to create a middle part and section off your hair into two parts. Then, use hair ties or clips to secure each section.

Step 3: Angle your scissors

Hold your scissors at a slight angle, with the longer edge facing downward. This will create a softer, more natural look than cutting straight across.

Step 4: Cut the first section

Start with the section of hair on one side of your head. Take a small section of hair from the front, near your face, and hold it between your fingers. Use your angled scissors to cut at a slight angle, starting at the bottom and working your way up.

Step 5: Check your work

Let the hair fall naturally and check your work. If you're happy with the length and angle, continue cutting the rest of the section. If not, make adjustments as needed.

Step 6: Repeat on the other side

Once you've finished one side, repeat the process on the other side.

Step 7: Blend the layers

Use your scissors to blend the layers together. This will create a more natural, seamless look.

Step 8: Style your hair

Once you've finished cutting, style your hair as desired. You can use a flat iron or curling iron to add waves or curls.

Tips for Angle Cutting Hair Around Face

Here are some tips to help you get the best results:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends or uneven cuts.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help or advice from a friend or stylist.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting hair can be tiring and may require multiple sessions.

FAQ about Angle Cutting Hair Around Face

Q: Will an angled haircut work for all hair types?

A: Yes, an angled haircut can work for all hair types, including straight, wavy, and curly hair.

Q: How often should I trim my angled haircut?

A: It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and prevent split ends.

Q: Can I angle cut my own hair?

A: If you're comfortable with cutting your own hair, you can angle cut your own hair. However, it's always a good idea to seek advice from a professional stylist if you're unsure.

Hair Care Tips for Angled Haircuts

Now that you've angle cut your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. Here are some hair care tips for angled haircuts:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and damage.
  • Avoid using hot tools too often, as they can cause damage and breakage.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray.

With these tips and tricks, you can angle cut your hair around the face and enjoy a fresh, new look. Happy cutting!
