How To Cut The Back Of Your Hair Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Description

Have you ever tried to cut your own hair and ended up with a botched job? Cutting your own hair can be tricky, especially when it comes to the back. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can easily cut the back of your hair yourself and achieve a professional-looking cut.

How to Cut the Back of Your Hair Yourself

If you're looking to cut the back of your hair yourself, there are a few things you'll need to keep in mind. First, you'll need to invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors. You'll also need a handheld mirror and a larger, stationary mirror that allows you to see the back of your head.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these simple steps to cut the back of your hair yourself:

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair. This will make it easier to see the natural shape of your hair and ensure that you don't miss any stray hairs.
  2. Stand in front of a larger, stationary mirror and hold a handheld mirror behind your head so that you can see the back of your hair.
  3. Section off the hair at the back of your head into two parts, using a comb to create a straight line down the middle.
  4. Clip one section of hair up and out of the way, leaving the other section down.
  5. Take a small section of hair from the bottom of the section that is down and comb it straight down towards the floor.
  6. Use your hair cutting scissors to trim the hair to the desired length. Be sure to cut in a straight line and avoid cutting too much at once.
  7. Continue taking small sections of hair from the bottom of the section that is down and trimming until you've cut all of the hair in that section.
  8. Release the clipped section of hair and repeat the process on the other side of your head.
  9. Once you've finished cutting both sides, use the handheld mirror to check the back of your hair and make any final adjustments.
  10. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

When cutting your own hair, it's important to keep these tips in mind:

  • Invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors. Don't use regular household scissors, as they can damage your hair.
  • Take your time and cut in small sections. Avoid cutting too much at once, as this can result in a choppy, uneven cut.
  • Use the right tools. In addition to hair cutting scissors, you'll need a comb and a handheld mirror.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet. Wet hair appears longer than it actually is, so you may end up cutting more than you intended.
  • Be patient. Cutting your own hair can be challenging, so don't get discouraged if it doesn't turn out perfect the first time.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions people have about cutting the back of their own hair:

Can I cut my own hair with regular household scissors?

No, it's best to invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors. Regular household scissors can damage your hair.

How often should I cut the back of my hair?

This depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. Generally, it's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

Is it easier to cut the back of my hair if I have short hair?

Yes, cutting the back of your hair is generally easier if you have shorter hair. However, the same techniques can be used for longer hair as well.

Hair Care Tips

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain a healthy, shiny appearance. Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools (like curling irons and straighteners) too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

By following these simple steps and tips, you can easily cut the back of your hair yourself and achieve a professional-looking cut. Just remember to take your time, invest in the right tools, and be patient!
