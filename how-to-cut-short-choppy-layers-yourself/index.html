How To Cut Short Choppy Layers Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Jul 12, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking for an edgy and modern haircut, short choppy layers can be a great choice. Not only do they add texture and movement to your hair, but they can also make your face look slimmer and more youthful. And the best part is, you don't need to visit a salon to get this trendy haircut. In this article, we'll show you how to cut short choppy layers yourself at home, with simple and easy-to-follow steps.

How to Cut Short Choppy Layers Yourself

Cutting short choppy layers may seem intimidating at first, but with the right tools and techniques, anyone can do it. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Sectioning clips
  • Water spray bottle

Once you have everything you need, you're ready to start cutting. Keep in mind that it's always better to start with longer layers and work your way up, as you can always cut more hair if needed.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair

Make sure your hair is clean and completely dry before you start cutting. If your hair is wet, it may look longer than it actually is, and you may end up cutting more hair than you intended.

Step 2: Section your hair

Using your comb, create a part in the middle of your head, and then create two more parts on each side of your head, creating three equal sections. Secure each section with a sectioning clip.

Step 3: Cut the bottom layer

Starting with the bottom layer, take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers, pulling it downwards. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, creating a slight V-shape. Repeat this step on all sections of the bottom layer, making sure to cut each section to the same length.

Step 4: Cut the middle layer

Release the middle section of your hair and repeat the same process as the bottom layer, cutting each section to the same length.

Step 5: Cut the top layer

Release the top section of your hair and repeat the same process as the previous two layers. However, for the top layer, cut the hair at a slightly steeper angle, creating more texture and movement.

Step 6: Blend the layers

Once you've cut all the layers, use your scissors to blend the ends of the layers together, creating a seamless and natural look.

Step 7: Final touches

Take a look at your hair and make any final adjustments if needed. If you want your layers to be more choppy, you can use your scissors to create small, random cuts throughout your hair to add more texture.

Tips for Cutting Short Choppy Layers

  • Start with longer layers and work your way up, as you can always cut more hair if needed.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair or creating split ends.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you're unsure about cutting your hair yourself.
  • Take breaks throughout the cutting process to avoid making mistakes due to fatigue.
  • Experiment with different angles and techniques to create a unique and personalized look.

FAQ

Can I cut my hair wet?

We recommend cutting your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can be misleading and make it difficult to achieve the desired length and style.

How often should I cut my short choppy layers?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your layers lose their shape. Generally, we recommend getting a trim every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and texture of your layers.

Can I cut short choppy layers on curly hair?

Absolutely! Short choppy layers can look great on curly hair, adding volume and definition to your curls. Just make sure to cut your hair when it's dry and use your fingers to separate your curls as you cut.

Hair Care After Cutting Short Choppy Layers

After cutting your short choppy layers, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and texture. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair and make your layers look frizzy.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair hydrated and shiny.
  • Get regular trims to remove split ends and maintain the shape of your layers.

Cutting short choppy layers yourself can be a fun and rewarding experience, and with these simple steps and tips, you can achieve a stylish and modern haircut at home. Give it a try and see how you can transform your look!
