How Do I Thin Out Thick Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Apr 15, 2023 · 8 min read

Thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it’s great to have a full head of healthy hair, it can be overwhelming to style and manage. If you’re struggling to manage your thick hair or want to add some texture and volume, thinning it out might be the solution. In this article, we’ll guide you through the process of how to thin out thick hair, step by step.

Table of Contents

  • How to Thin Out Thick Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Thinning Out Thick Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Thick Hair

How to Thin Out Thick Hair

Thinning out thick hair can be done in a few different ways, depending on your desired outcome. Here are some common methods:

1. Razor Cutting

Razor cutting involves using a razor to create thinning and texture in the hair. This technique is best done by a professional stylist who can determine how much hair needs to be removed and where.

2. Point Cutting

Point cutting involves using scissors to create texture in the hair. The stylist will cut into the hair at different angles to remove bulk and create movement.

3. Thinning Shears

Thinning shears are specially designed scissors that have teeth on one blade to remove hair without creating blunt lines. This technique is best for achieving a natural, blended look.

Step by Step Guide

If you want to thin out your thick hair at home, here’s a step by step guide:

1. Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start with clean, dry hair. This will make it easier to see how much hair needs to be removed.

2. Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. This will help you work on one section at a time.

3. Choose Your Thinning Method

Decide which thinning method you want to use. Thinning shears are the easiest to use at home, but razor cutting and point cutting should be done by a professional.

4. Use Thinning Shears

If you’re using thinning shears, start by holding a small section of hair in your fingers. Hold the thinning shears vertically and cut into the hair, removing small sections at a time. Repeat this process throughout your hair, working on one section at a time.

5. Blend Your Hair

Once you’ve thinned out your hair, use a comb to blend any harsh lines. Be careful not to remove too much hair, as this can create unevenness.

Tips for Thinning Out Thick Hair

Thinning out thick hair can be tricky, so here are some tips to help you achieve the best results:

1. Don’t Overdo It

It’s important not to remove too much hair at once. Start with small sections and work your way up to larger sections as you get more comfortable.

2. Use the Right Tools

Invest in good quality thinning shears if you plan to do it at home. Using regular scissors can create blunt lines and remove too much hair.

3. Consult a Professional

If you’re unsure about how to thin out your hair, it’s best to consult a professional stylist. They can help you determine the best method and how much hair needs to be removed.

FAQs

1. Will Thinning Out My Hair Make It Look Thinner?

No, thinning out your hair will not make it look thinner. It will remove bulk and create texture, making it easier to manage and style.

2. Can I Thin Out My Hair at Home?

Yes, you can thin out your hair at home using thinning shears. However, it’s best to consult a professional stylist if you’re unsure about the process.

Hair Care for Thick Hair

Thick hair requires special care to keep it healthy and manageable. Here are some tips:

1. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Thick hair can be prone to dryness, so use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep it hydrated.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage thick hair, so try to air dry your hair whenever possible. If you must use heat tools, use a heat protectant spray.

3. Get Regular Trims

Regular trims can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

Thinning out thick hair can be a game changer when it comes to managing and styling it. Whether you choose to do it at home or consult a professional, follow these tips and steps to achieve the best results. Remember to take care of your thick hair with the right hair care routine to keep it healthy and beautiful.
