How To Fix Patches In Bleached Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Frank Aug 07, 2023 · 8 min read

Bleaching your hair can be a fun and exciting way to change up your look. However, it can also lead to patches of uneven color or even breakage. If you're dealing with patches in your bleached hair, don't worry – there are ways to fix it. In this post, we'll discuss how to fix patches in bleached hair and provide you with tips and tricks to prevent it from happening again in the future.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Fix Patches in Bleached Hair
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Preventing Patches in Bleached Hair
  4. Frequently Asked Questions
  5. Hair Care for Bleached Hair

How to Fix Patches in Bleached Hair

If you have patches in your bleached hair, the first step is to determine the cause. If the patches are due to breakage, you may need to trim your hair or use a protein treatment to strengthen it. If the patches are due to uneven bleaching, there are several ways to fix it:

  • Toning: If you have patches of yellow or orange in your hair, toning can help neutralize the color. Use a toner with a violet or blue base to cancel out the warm tones.
  • Balayage: If you have patches of darker color, balayage can help blend the color. This technique involves painting lighter color onto the hair in a sweeping motion, creating a natural-looking blend.
  • Color Correction: If the patches are more severe, you may need to visit a professional stylist for color correction. They can use a color remover to remove the uneven color and then apply a new color to create a more even look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide for fixing patches in bleached hair:

  1. Assess the damage: Determine the cause of the patches and decide which technique will work best for your hair.
  2. Prepare your hair: Wash and dry your hair thoroughly before applying any treatments.
  3. Apply the treatment: Follow the instructions for your chosen technique, whether it's toning, balayage, or color correction.
  4. Rinse and condition: Rinse out any products thoroughly and apply a deep conditioning treatment to nourish your hair.
  5. Style as usual: Once your hair is dry, style it as you normally would.

Tips for Preventing Patches in Bleached Hair

Prevention is key when it comes to avoiding patches in your bleached hair. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Choose a professional stylist: If you're new to bleaching your hair, it's best to visit a professional stylist who can guide you through the process.
  • Use quality products: Invest in high-quality hair care products, such as a deep conditioning treatment or a protein treatment, to keep your hair healthy and strong.
  • Take it slow: Bleaching your hair too quickly or too often can lead to damage and patches. Take it slow and give your hair time to recover between treatments.
  • Protect your hair: Use a heat protectant when styling your hair with heat tools, and avoid excessive brushing or pulling on your hair.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can patches in bleached hair be fixed?

Yes, patches in bleached hair can be fixed using toning, balayage, or color correction techniques.

How long does it take to fix patches in bleached hair?

The time it takes to fix patches in bleached hair depends on the severity of the patches and the technique used. Toning and balayage can be done in a single appointment, while color correction may require multiple appointments.

How can I prevent patches in bleached hair?

To prevent patches in bleached hair, choose a professional stylist, use quality hair care products, take it slow with bleaching treatments, and protect your hair from heat damage.

Hair Care for Bleached Hair

Bleaching your hair can be damaging, so it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some tips for caring for bleached hair:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment: Apply a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid excessive heat: Limit your use of heat tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, and use a heat protectant when you do use them.
  • Trim regularly: Regular trims can help prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Use a protein treatment: Use a protein treatment once a month to strengthen your hair and prevent breakage.

With these tips and techniques, you can fix patches in your bleached hair and keep it healthy and strong. Remember to take it slow with bleaching treatments and to always care for your hair to prevent damage.
