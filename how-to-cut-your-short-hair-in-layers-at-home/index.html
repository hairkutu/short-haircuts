How To Cut Your Short Hair In Layers At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 18, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you tired of your short hair looking flat and lifeless? Cutting your hair in layers can give it more volume and texture. While it may seem daunting to cut your own hair, it's actually quite simple with the right tools and technique. In this article, we'll walk you through how to cut your short hair in layers at home.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Prepping Your Hair
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Texturizing Your Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • Full-length mirror

Prepping Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and letting it air dry or blow drying it. You want your hair to be clean and free of any tangles or knots. Once your hair is dry, use a comb to part it down the middle.

Sectioning Your Hair

Use hair clips to separate your hair into four sections: two on top and two on the bottom. The top sections should start at the temples and end at the crown of your head. The bottom sections should start at the nape of your neck and end at the earlobes. Make sure each section is even and secure with hair clips.

Cutting Your Hair

Start with one of the bottom sections. Take a small section of hair and hold it straight up with your fingers. Use the scissors to cut the ends at an angle, starting from the middle and working your way down to the tips. Repeat this process for all the sections in the bottom half of your hair.

Once you're done with the bottom sections, move on to the top sections. This time, hold the hair straight up and cut it at a 45-degree angle, starting from the middle and working your way down to the tips. Make sure you're cutting each section evenly.

Texturizing Your Hair

To add more texture and volume to your hair, use the scissors to create small notches in the ends of your hair. This technique is called point cutting and it helps to soften the edges of your hair.

FAQs

Can I cut my hair in layers if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut your curly hair in layers. However, it's important to cut it while it's dry so you can see how the curls will fall. Use the same technique as described above, but be careful not to cut off too much length or you may end up with uneven curls.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

If you accidentally cut off too much hair, don't panic. You can always go to a professional stylist to fix it. However, it's best to take your time and cut small sections at a time to avoid any major mistakes.

Hair Care Tips

  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the layers.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using hair products with alcohol, as they can dry out your hair.

Cutting your short hair in layers at home is a great way to give it more texture and volume. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a salon-worthy look without leaving your house. Remember to take your time and cut small sections at a time to avoid any major mistakes. Happy cutting!
