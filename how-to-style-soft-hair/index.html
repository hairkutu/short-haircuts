How To Style Soft Hair Tips Tricks And Techniques

Written by Aprilia Mar 30, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Soft hair can be a blessing and a curse. On one hand, it's silky smooth and easy to comb through. On the other hand, it can be difficult to style and hold a shape. But fear not, there are plenty of ways to style soft hair that will make it look voluminous, bouncy, and full of life. In this guide, we'll cover everything you need to know about how to style soft hair, including tips, techniques, and step-by-step instructions.

Table of Contents

  • Hair Care
  • How To Style Soft Hair
  • Step-by-Step Instructions
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs

Hair Care

The first step in styling soft hair is to take good care of it. This means using high-quality shampoo and conditioner that are designed for your hair type. Look for products that are specifically formulated for soft or fine hair, as these will help to add volume and texture. Avoid using heavy, oily products that can weigh down your hair and make it difficult to style.

It's also important to avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils and make it even softer and more difficult to style. Aim to wash your hair no more than every other day, and use dry shampoo in between washes to keep your hair looking fresh and clean.

How To Style Soft Hair

When it comes to styling soft hair, there are a few key techniques that can help to add volume, texture, and hold. Here are some of our favorites:

Blow Drying

Blow drying is a great way to add volume and texture to soft hair. Start by applying a volumizing mousse or spray to your roots, then use a round brush to lift your hair as you blow dry it. Once your hair is dry, use a curling iron or straightener to add some waves or curls.

Teasing

Teasing, or backcombing, is another great way to add volume and texture to soft hair. Start by sectioning off the top layer of your hair, then use a fine-tooth comb to backcomb the underside of each section. Once you've teased all of the sections, smooth the top layer of your hair over the teased sections and style as desired.

Braiding

Braiding is a simple and stylish way to add texture and hold to soft hair. Start by braiding your hair while it's still damp, then let it dry completely before unraveling the braid. This will create soft waves and curls that will hold their shape throughout the day.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Ready to style your soft hair? Here's a step-by-step guide to some of our favorite techniques:

Blow Drying

  1. Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Towel dry your hair to remove excess moisture.
  3. Apply a volumizing mousse or spray to your roots.
  4. Use a round brush to lift your hair as you blow dry it.
  5. Once your hair is dry, use a curling iron or straightener to add waves or curls.

Teasing

  1. Section off the top layer of your hair.
  2. Use a fine-tooth comb to backcomb the underside of each section.
  3. Smooth the top layer of your hair over the teased sections.
  4. Style as desired.

Braiding

  1. Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Towel dry your hair to remove excess moisture.
  3. Braid your hair while it's still damp.
  4. Let your hair dry completely before unraveling the braid.
  5. Style as desired.

Tips and Tricks

Here are a few more tips and tricks for styling soft hair:

  • Use a volumizing powder or dry shampoo at your roots to add texture and hold.
  • Avoid using heavy, oily products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Try using hot rollers to add volume and texture to your hair.
  • Experiment with different styles and techniques to find what works best for you.

FAQs

Q: Can I use hairspray on soft hair?

A: Yes, but be sure to use a light-hold hairspray that won't weigh down your hair.

Q: How often should I wash my soft hair?

A: Aim to wash your hair no more than every other day to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.

Q: Can I use a straightener on soft hair?

A: Yes, but be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your hair.

Q: What should I do if my hair is too soft to hold a style?

A: Try using a volumizing powder or dry shampoo to add texture and hold to your hair.

Conclusion

Styling soft hair can be a challenge, but with the right techniques and products, it's possible to create a wide range of beautiful and stylish looks. Whether you prefer blow drying, teasing, braiding, or something else, there are plenty of ways to add volume, texture, and hold to your soft hair. Just remember to take good care of your hair and experiment with different styles and techniques to find what works best for you.
