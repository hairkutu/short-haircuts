Can Hypothyroidism Make You Lose Hair

Written by Christine Aug 24, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Hypothyroidism is a condition where the thyroid gland does not produce enough thyroid hormones. These hormones are crucial for the body's metabolism, growth, and development. A common symptom of hypothyroidism is hair loss. In this article, we will explore how hypothyroidism can cause hair loss and what you can do to prevent it.

Table of Content:

  • Description of Hypothyroidism
  • How Hypothyroidism Can Cause Hair Loss
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Prevent Hair Loss
  • Tips for Hair Care with Hypothyroidism
  • Frequently Asked Questions

Description of Hypothyroidism

Hypothyroidism is a condition where the thyroid gland does not produce enough thyroid hormones. The thyroid gland is located in the neck and produces hormones that regulate the body's metabolism, growth, and development. When the thyroid gland does not produce enough thyroid hormones, it can lead to a variety of symptoms, including:

  • fatigue
  • weight gain
  • dry skin
  • hair loss

Hypothyroidism can be caused by a variety of factors, including autoimmune disorders, radiation therapy, and certain medications. It is important to consult with your healthcare provider if you suspect that you have hypothyroidism.

How Hypothyroidism Can Cause Hair Loss

Hair loss is a common symptom of hypothyroidism. When the thyroid gland does not produce enough thyroid hormones, it can disrupt the hair growth cycle. Hair growth occurs in three stages:

  • anagen (the active growth phase)
  • catagen (the transition phase)
  • telogen (the resting phase)

When the thyroid gland does not produce enough thyroid hormones, it can cause more hairs to enter the telogen phase, leading to hair loss. Additionally, hypothyroidism can lead to dry, brittle hair that is more prone to breakage.

Step-by-Step Guide to Prevent Hair Loss

If you have hypothyroidism and are experiencing hair loss, there are steps you can take to prevent further hair loss:

  1. Consult with your healthcare provider. Your healthcare provider can help you manage your hypothyroidism and provide recommendations for preventing hair loss.
  2. Eat a balanced diet. A diet rich in nutrients, including protein, iron, and vitamins, can help promote hair growth.
  3. Manage stress. Stress can contribute to hair loss, so it is important to manage stress through exercise, meditation, or other relaxation techniques.
  4. Avoid harsh hair treatments. Chemicals and heat can damage hair and contribute to hair loss. Avoid harsh hair treatments, including coloring, perming, and straightening.
  5. Use gentle hair care products. Use gentle, sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners that are designed for dry, damaged hair.

Tips for Hair Care with Hypothyroidism

If you have hypothyroidism, it is important to take extra care of your hair. Here are some tips for hair care with hypothyroidism:

  • Avoid brushing wet hair. Wet hair is more prone to breakage, so avoid brushing wet hair.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles. Tight hairstyles, including braids, buns, and ponytails, can pull on the hair and contribute to hair loss.
  • Avoid excessive heat. Excessive heat from blow dryers, curling irons, and flat irons can damage hair and contribute to hair loss.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb. A wide-toothed comb can help detangle hair without causing breakage.
  • Avoid over-shampooing. Over-shampooing can strip hair of its natural oils and contribute to dry, brittle hair.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Will treating hypothyroidism prevent hair loss?

A: Yes, treating hypothyroidism can prevent hair loss. Your healthcare provider can help you manage your hypothyroidism and provide recommendations for preventing hair loss.

Q: Can hypothyroidism cause hair thinning?

A: Yes, hypothyroidism can cause hair thinning. When the thyroid gland does not produce enough thyroid hormones, it can disrupt the hair growth cycle, leading to hair loss and hair thinning.

Q: Can hypothyroidism cause permanent hair loss?

A: In some cases, hypothyroidism can cause permanent hair loss. However, with proper management of hypothyroidism, hair loss can be prevented or minimized.

In conclusion, hypothyroidism can cause hair loss due to disruption of the hair growth cycle. If you have hypothyroidism and are experiencing hair loss, consult with your healthcare provider and take steps to prevent further hair loss. By taking extra care of your hair and managing your hypothyroidism, you can maintain healthy, beautiful hair.
