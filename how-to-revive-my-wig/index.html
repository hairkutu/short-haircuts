How to Revive My Wig

Reviving your wig is easier than you might think. All you need are a few basic tools and some patience, and you'll be able to restore your wig to its former glory. Here's what you'll need:

  • Wig brush or wide-tooth comb
  • Mild shampoo
  • Conditioner
  • Towel
  • Wig stand or mannequin head

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to revive your wig:

  1. Brush or comb your wig gently to remove any tangles or knots.
  2. Fill a sink or basin with lukewarm water and add a small amount of mild shampoo.
  3. Place the wig in the water and gently swirl it around for a few minutes to distribute the shampoo.
  4. Rinse the wig thoroughly with lukewarm water until all the shampoo is gone.
  5. Apply conditioner to the wig, being careful not to apply too much.
  6. Let the conditioner sit on the wig for a few minutes, then rinse it out with lukewarm water.
  7. Blot the wig gently with a towel to remove excess water.
  8. Place the wig on a wig stand or mannequin head to air dry.
  9. Once the wig is completely dry, brush or comb it again to style it as desired.

Tips for Reviving Your Wig

Here are a few additional tips to help you revive your wig:

  • If your wig is particularly tangled, you can use a wig detangler spray before brushing or combing it.
  • Be gentle when washing and conditioning your wig, as rough handling can cause damage.
  • Never use hot water on your wig, as this can cause the hair fibers to melt or become damaged.
  • Don't be afraid to trim your wig if necessary. This can help get rid of split ends and make the wig look healthier.

FAQs

Here are a few frequently asked questions about reviving wigs:

  • Can I use regular shampoo and conditioner on my wig? It's best to use products specifically designed for wigs, as regular shampoo and conditioner can be too harsh and cause damage.
  • How often should I revive my wig? It depends on how often you wear your wig and how much styling you do. As a general rule, you should aim to revive your wig every 4-6 weeks.
  • Can I use heat styling tools on my wig? Yes, but be sure to use a heat protectant spray and never use high heat, as this can cause damage to the wig.

Hair Care Tips for Your Wig

Here are a few additional tips for taking care of your wig:

  • Store your wig on a wig stand or mannequin head when you're not wearing it to help maintain its shape.
  • Avoid exposing your wig to direct sunlight, as this can cause the color to fade.
  • Don't sleep in your wig, as this can cause tangles and matting.
  • Use a wig cap to help keep your wig in place and protect your natural hair.
