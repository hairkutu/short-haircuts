How To Get Waves With Bad Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Apr 04, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have bad hair but want to achieve waves, don't worry! You can still achieve the look you want with a few simple steps. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of getting waves with bad hair.

Description

Waves are a popular hairstyle for men with curly or wavy hair. However, if you have bad hair, it can be difficult to achieve the look you want. Bad hair can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, heat damage, and chemical treatments. But with the right techniques and products, you can still achieve waves with bad hair.

How to Get Waves with Bad Hair

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

The first step to getting waves with bad hair is to start with clean, damp hair. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to clean and moisturize your hair. Avoid using hot water, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause dryness.

Step 2: Apply Wave Cream

Once your hair is clean and damp, apply a wave cream to your hair. Wave cream helps to define and hold your waves in place. Apply the cream evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the areas where you want to create waves.

Step 3: Brush Your Hair

Use a wave brush to brush your hair in the direction of the waves. Brush from the crown of your head to the nape of your neck, using long, sweeping motions. Make sure to brush your hair evenly, and don't forget to brush the sides and back of your head.

Step 4: Wear a Durag

After you've brushed your hair, wear a durag to help hold your waves in place. A durag is a type of headwrap that helps to compress your hair and create defined waves. Wear the durag for at least 30 minutes to an hour to help set your waves.

Step 5: Remove the Durag

After you've worn the durag for the recommended time, remove it and brush your hair again. Use a soft-bristled brush to brush your hair in the direction of the waves.

Step 6: Apply Wave Pomade

Once your hair is brushed and dry, apply a wave pomade to your hair. Wave pomade helps to define and hold your waves in place, and also adds shine to your hair. Apply the pomade evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the areas where you want to create waves.

Step 7: Brush Your Hair Again

Use a wave brush to brush your hair again in the direction of the waves. Brush from the crown of your head to the nape of your neck, using long, sweeping motions. Make sure to brush your hair evenly, and don't forget to brush the sides and back of your head.

Tips

- Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.

- Avoid using hot water, as it can cause dryness and damage to your hair.

- Use a wave brush to help define and create waves in your hair.

- Wear a durag to help compress your hair and create defined waves.

- Apply wave cream and pomade evenly throughout your hair to help hold your waves in place.

FAQs

Can I get waves with bad hair?

Yes, you can still achieve waves with bad hair. Use the tips and techniques outlined in this article to help create defined waves in your hair.

What products should I use to get waves with bad hair?

Use a wave cream and pomade to help define and hold your waves in place.

How often should I brush my hair to get waves?

Brush your hair at least twice a day to help create and define your waves.

Hair Care

To maintain your waves, it's important to take care of your hair. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to clean and moisturize your hair, and avoid using hot water. Use a wave brush to help define and create waves in your hair, and wear a durag to help hold your waves in place. Apply wave cream and pomade evenly throughout your hair to help hold your waves in place, and brush your hair at least twice a day to help create and define your waves.

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve waves with bad hair and maintain the look you want.
