Are you looking for a new hairstyle to switch up your look? With so many options out there, it can be overwhelming to know which one to choose. In this guide, we'll go over some of the most popular men's hairstyles with images, so you can find the perfect one for you.

Buzz Cut

The buzz cut is a classic, low-maintenance hairstyle that involves cutting the hair very short all over the head. It's perfect for those who want a fuss-free style that doesn't require a lot of upkeep.

To achieve the perfect buzz cut, use clippers to cut the hair to a uniform length all over the head. You can choose how short you want to go, but a 1 or 2 guard length is typical for a classic buzz cut.

Tip: If you want a more textured look, you can ask your barber to use scissors instead of clippers.

Crew Cut

The crew cut is another classic hairstyle that involves cutting the hair short on the sides and back, with slightly longer hair on top. It's a versatile style that can be worn in a variety of ways, from messy and textured to sleek and polished.

To achieve a crew cut, ask your barber for a short taper on the sides and back, with about 1-2 inches of length on top. You can style the top however you like, whether that's with a side part, messy texture, or slicked back.

Quiff

The quiff is a stylish, retro-inspired hairstyle that involves longer hair on top that's styled upwards and back. It's a great option for those who want a more polished look that's still easy to maintain.

To achieve a quiff, ask your barber for longer hair on top that's gradually tapered on the sides and back. You can style the top by blow-drying it upwards and back, using a round brush to add volume. Finish with a pomade or wax to hold the style in place.

Slick Back

The slick back is a classic, sophisticated hairstyle that involves combing the hair back and away from the face. It's perfect for those who want a more polished look that's still easy to maintain.

To achieve a slick back, start with damp hair and use a comb to create a side part. Comb the hair back and away from the face, using a pomade or wax to hold the style in place.

Side Part

The side part is a classic hairstyle that involves creating a part on one side of the head and combing the hair over to the other side. It's a versatile style that can be worn in a variety of ways, from a polished, professional look to a more casual, messy style.

To achieve a side part, use a comb to create a part on one side of the head. Comb the hair over to the other side, using a pomade or wax to hold the style in place. You can choose how neat or messy you want the style to be.

Pompadour

The pompadour is a bold, statement-making hairstyle that involves longer hair on top that's styled upwards and back. It's a great option for those who want to stand out and make a statement with their hair.

To achieve a pompadour, ask your barber for longer hair on top that's gradually tapered on the sides and back. You can style the top by blow-drying it upwards and back, using a round brush to add volume. Finish with a pomade or wax to hold the style in place.

Man Bun

The man bun is a trendy, laid-back hairstyle that involves pulling the hair back into a bun at the crown of the head. It's a great option for those with longer hair who want to keep it out of their face.

To achieve a man bun, gather your hair at the crown of your head and secure it with an elastic band. You can choose how high or low you want to place the bun, and whether you want a more polished or messy look.

Undercut

The undercut is a bold, edgy hairstyle that involves shaving the hair short on the sides and back, with longer hair on top. It's a great option for those who want to make a statement with their hair.

To achieve an undercut, ask your barber to shave the sides and back of your head short, leaving 2-3 inches of length on top. You can style the top however you like, whether that's with a quiff, slick back, or messy texture.

Fade

The fade is a popular, modern hairstyle that involves a gradual transition from short hair on the sides and back to longer hair on top. It's a versatile style that can be worn in a variety of ways, from a classic crew cut to a modern pompadour.

To achieve a fade, ask your barber for a gradual fade on the sides and back of your head, with shorter hair at the bottom and longer hair at the top. You can style the top however you like, depending on your personal preference.

Comb Over

The comb over is a classic hairstyle that involves creating a part on one side of the head and combing the hair over to the other side. It's a versatile style that can be worn in a variety of ways, from a polished, professional look to a more casual, messy style.

To achieve a comb over, use a comb to create a part on one side of the head. Comb the hair over to the other side, using a pomade or wax to hold the style in place. You can choose how neat or messy you want the style to be.

Hair Care Tips

No matter what hairstyle you choose, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips to help you maintain healthy, stylish hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a high-quality shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage it over time.
  • Use a comb or brush that's appropriate for your hair type and style.
  • Invest in high-quality styling products, such as pomades, waxes, and gels.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and products to find what works best for you.
