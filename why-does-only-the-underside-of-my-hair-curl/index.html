Why Does Only The Underside Of My Hair Curl

Written by Frank Aug 28, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Have you ever noticed that only the underside of your hair seems to curl, while the top remains stubbornly straight? This can be frustrating, especially if you're trying to achieve a full head of curls. In this article, we'll explore the reasons why this happens, and offer some tips on how to get your whole head of hair to curl.

Why Does Only the Underside of My Hair Curl?

The reason why the underside of your hair curls more easily is due to the natural structure of your hair. The top layer of your hair is exposed to more sunlight, heat, and environmental factors, which can cause it to become drier and more damaged over time. This makes it harder for the top layer of your hair to hold a curl.

The underside of your hair, on the other hand, is protected by the top layer and is less exposed to these factors. As a result, it tends to be healthier and more hydrated, which makes it easier for it to curl.

How to Get Your Whole Head of Hair to Curl

If you're tired of only having the underside of your hair curl, there are some things you can do to encourage your whole head of hair to curl:

1. Use the Right Products

Using the right products can make a big difference in how easily your hair curls. Look for products that are designed specifically for curly hair, such as curl-enhancing shampoos, conditioners, and styling products.

2. Use Heat Styling Tools

Using heat styling tools, such as curling irons and hot rollers, can help you achieve a full head of curls. Be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your hair.

3. Experiment with Different Techniques

There are many different techniques for curling your hair, so don't be afraid to experiment until you find one that works for you. Some popular techniques include using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer, using foam rollers, and using a curling wand.

4. Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims can help keep your hair healthy, which can make it easier for it to hold a curl. Aim to get a trim every six to eight weeks to keep split ends at bay.

Tips for Maintaining Curls

Once you've achieved a full head of curls, it's important to take steps to maintain them. Here are some tips:

1. Use a Satin Pillowcase

Sleeping on a satin pillowcase can help prevent your curls from getting flattened overnight.

2. Don't Overbrush Your Hair

Brushing your hair too much can cause your curls to become frizzy and flat. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair.

3. Avoid Heat Styling Too Often

Using heat styling tools too often can damage your hair, so try to limit their use to once or twice a week.

FAQs

Q: Can I still achieve a full head of curls if I have naturally straight hair?

A: Yes, you can still achieve a full head of curls if you have naturally straight hair. Follow the tips outlined in this article, and experiment with different techniques until you find one that works for you.

Q: How long will my curls last?

A: The length of time your curls will last depends on your hair type, the products you use, and how you style your hair. On average, curls should last anywhere from a few hours to a few days.

Hair Care

Remember to take care of your hair by using products that are designed for your hair type, getting regular trims, and avoiding excessive heat styling. With the right care and styling techniques, you can achieve a full head of curls that lasts all day.
