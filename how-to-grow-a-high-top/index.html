How To Grow A High Top Step By Step Guide And Hair Care Tips

If you're interested in growing a high top, you've come to the right place! In this article, we'll show you how to grow a high top step-by-step, provide you with some tips and answer some frequently asked questions. So, let's get started!

Table of Contents

How to Grow a High Top

Growing a high top requires patience, dedication and the right hair care routine. Here are the steps you can follow to grow a high top:

Step 1: Start with the Right Haircut

The first step in growing a high top is to get the right haircut. You'll want to ask your barber for a high top fade, which means that the hair on the sides and back of your head will be shaved or cut short, while the hair on the top of your head will be left longer. This will create the foundation for your high top.

Step 2: Let Your Hair Grow

Next, you'll need to let your hair grow. This can take several months, depending on how fast your hair grows. During this time, you'll want to avoid cutting your hair, except for trimming the sides and back to maintain the fade.

Step 3: Train Your Hair

As your hair grows, you'll need to train it to stand up. You can do this by using a brush or comb to push your hair up and towards the center of your head. You can also use a hair dryer and a round brush to add volume and shape to your high top.

Step 4: Maintain Your High Top

Once your high top has reached your desired length and shape, you'll need to maintain it. This means regularly washing and conditioning your hair, using a moisturizing cream or oil to keep it hydrated, and avoiding harsh chemicals or heat styling tools that can damage your hair.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some hair care tips to help you grow and maintain your high top:

Tip 1: Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and healthy. Look for products that contain natural ingredients like argan oil, coconut oil, or shea butter.

Tip 2: Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Once a week, use a deep conditioning treatment to nourish your hair and prevent breakage. You can use a store-bought product or make your own using ingredients like avocado, honey, or olive oil.

Tip 3: Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons or curling wands, as they can damage your hair and cause breakage. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

Tip 4: Protect Your Hair at Night

Use a satin or silk pillowcase and a hair wrap or bonnet to protect your hair while you sleep. This will prevent breakage and help your high top maintain its shape.

FAQ

Q: How long does it take to grow a high top?

A: Growing a high top can take several months, depending on how fast your hair grows. It's important to be patient and stick to a consistent hair care routine.

Q: Can I grow a high top if my hair is naturally curly?

A: Yes, you can grow a high top if your hair is naturally curly. You'll need to use a styling product or gel to help your hair stand up and maintain its shape.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: You should wash your hair every 2-3 days, or as needed. Over-washing can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and brittle.

Q: Can I cut my hair while growing a high top?

A: You should avoid cutting your hair, except for trimming the sides and back to maintain the fade. Cutting the top of your hair will disrupt the growth and shape of your high top.

Q: What should I do if my high top starts to flatten?

A: If your high top starts to flatten, you can use a brush or comb to push it back up and towards the center of your head. You can also use a hair dryer and a round brush to add volume and shape to your high top.

With these tips and steps, you'll be well on your way to growing a high top. Remember to be patient, stay consistent with your hair care routine, and avoid damaging your hair with harsh chemicals or heat styling tools. Good luck!
