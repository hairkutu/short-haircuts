How Much Does It Cost To Get A Lace Front Wig Put On A Comprehensive Guide

Are you tired of constantly styling your hair every morning? Or are you experiencing hair loss and want to try out a new hairstyle? A lace front wig might be the perfect solution for you. But before you make a decision, one of the most important questions you should ask yourself is: how much does it cost to get a lace front wig put on? In this article, we'll break down the costs and everything you need to know about getting a lace front wig installed.

  • How to

Description

A lace front wig is a type of wig that has a sheer lace panel in the front of the wig that covers the hairline. The rest of the wig is made of a more durable material that is less likely to break or tear. Lace front wigs are popular because they look more natural than traditional wigs and can be styled in a variety of ways.

How to Get a Lace Front Wig Put On

Getting a lace front wig put on can be done in a salon or by a professional hairstylist. The process usually takes around 2-3 hours and involves the following steps:

Step 1: Choosing the Right Wig

The first step to getting a lace front wig put on is to choose the right wig. There are many different types of lace front wigs, so it's important to choose one that matches your natural hair color and texture. You can either buy a wig online or in a store, or you can have a custom wig made for you.

Step 2: Preparing Your Hair

Before the wig can be put on, your natural hair needs to be prepared. This involves washing and conditioning your hair and then braiding it into cornrows. The cornrows will serve as a base for the wig to be attached to.

Step 3: Applying Adhesive

The next step is to apply adhesive to the perimeter of the wig. The adhesive is what will hold the wig in place, so it's important to use a high-quality adhesive that won't damage your natural hair. The adhesive is usually applied using a brush or a small applicator.

Step 4: Placing the Wig

Once the adhesive has been applied, the wig can be placed on your head. The wig should be positioned so that the front of the wig lines up with your natural hairline. The wig can be secured in place using clips or more adhesive.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step by step guide to getting a lace front wig put on:

  1. Choose the right wig that matches your natural hair color and texture.
  2. Wash and condition your natural hair and braid it into cornrows.
  3. Apply adhesive to the perimeter of the wig using a brush or small applicator.
  4. Place the wig on your head, lining up the front of the wig with your natural hairline.
  5. Secure the wig in place using clips or more adhesive.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when getting a lace front wig put on:

  • Choose a high-quality wig and adhesive to avoid damaging your natural hair.
  • Make sure to prepare your natural hair properly before putting on the wig.
  • Consider having a professional hairstylist put on the wig for the best results.
  • Follow a proper hair care routine to keep your wig looking its best.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about getting a lace front wig put on:

How long does it take to get a lace front wig put on?

The process usually takes around 2-3 hours.

Can I put on a lace front wig by myself?

While it's possible to put on a lace front wig by yourself, it's recommended to have a professional do it for the best results.

How long will a lace front wig last?

A lace front wig can last anywhere from a few months to a year depending on how well it's cared for.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is important to keep your lace front wig looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a wig brush or wide-tooth comb to detangle the wig.
  • Wash the wig every 2-3 weeks using a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on the wig.
  • Store the wig on a wig stand or mannequin head when not in use.

Now that you know everything there is to know about getting a lace front wig put on, you can make an informed decision on whether it's the right choice for you. Just remember to choose a high-quality wig and adhesive and follow a proper hair care routine to keep your wig looking its best.
