How To Cut Short Layers In Long Hair Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Jun 17, 2023 · 6 min read

If you're looking for a change in your hairstyle, cutting short layers into your long hair can add volume, texture, and movement. But, heading to the salon for a haircut can be expensive and time-consuming. Instead, learn how to cut short layers in long hair yourself with our easy-to-follow guide.

How to Cut Short Layers in Long Hair Yourself

Before you begin, it's important to have the right tools on hand. You'll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair.

Step 1: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Begin by washing and drying your hair. This will ensure that your hair is clean, tangle-free, and easy to work with.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use your comb to create a center part in your hair, separating it into two sections. Then, use hair clips to secure each side.

Step 3: Cut the First Layer

Starting on one side, take a small section of hair from the front and hold it up at a 45-degree angle. Use your scissors to cut the hair to the desired length, keeping the scissors parallel to the hair for a blunt cut. Continue working your way around the head, cutting small sections of hair at a time.

Step 4: Repeat on the Other Side

Once you have finished cutting one side, repeat the process on the other side of your head.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

After you have cut all of the layers, use your comb to blend them together. Take small sections of hair and run your comb through them, holding the hair at a 45-degree angle. Use your scissors to cut any sections that need to be blended further.

Step 6: Check Your Work

Once you have finished cutting, check your work in a mirror. Make any additional cuts or adjustments as needed.

Tips for Cutting Short Layers in Long Hair Yourself

Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, but with these tips, you can achieve a professional-looking cut at home.

  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is.
  • Cut small sections of hair at a time to ensure accuracy.
  • Take breaks if you need to, to avoid fatigue and mistakes.

FAQs

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to get a haircut every 4-6 weeks to maintain healthy hair and prevent split ends.

Can short layers work with any hair type?

Yes, short layers can work with any hair type, including straight, wavy, and curly hair.

Hair Care After Cutting Layers into Long Hair

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and style.

  • Use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair soft and hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your haircut and prevent split ends.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to add extra hydration and nourishment.

With these tips and tricks, cutting short layers in long hair yourself can be a fun and rewarding experience. Give it a try and see how it can transform your hairstyle!
