How To Cut African American Hair Short A Complete Guide

Written by Oliver Apr 17, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you thinking of cutting your African American hair short? Short haircuts for African American women are trendy and can give you a fresh new look. However, before you grab the scissors and start snipping away, there are a few things you should keep in mind to ensure a successful haircut. In this article, we'll provide you with a complete guide on how to cut African American hair short.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut African American Hair Short
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting African American Hair Short
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting African American Hair Short

Description

African American hair is unique, and it requires special care and attention when it comes to cutting. When cutting African American hair short, it's essential to choose a style that complements your face shape and hair type. Short haircuts can range from a pixie cut to a tapered cut, and the key to a successful haircut is to have a clear vision of what you want before you start.

How to Cut African American Hair Short

The first step in cutting African American hair short is to prepare the hair for cutting. This involves washing the hair and conditioning it to detangle any knots or tangles. Next, you should section the hair, starting from the back and moving to the front. This will help you to cut the hair evenly and ensure that you don't miss any sections.

Tools You Will Need

Here are some of the tools you will need to cut African American hair short:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Clipper or trimmer
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Mirror

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step by step guide on how to cut African American hair short:

Step 1: Section the Hair

Start by sectioning the hair. Use hair clips to secure the sections and ensure that the hair is tangle-free.

Step 2: Choose the Length

Decide on the length you want to cut your hair. If you're not sure, consider going to a professional stylist who can help you choose the perfect length based on your face shape and hair type.

Step 3: Cut the Hair

Using sharp scissors, cut the hair in small sections. Remember to cut slowly and carefully to ensure that the hair is even. For shorter styles, use a clipper or trimmer to cut the hair close to the scalp.

Step 4: Check the Length

After you've finished cutting the hair, check the length in the mirror to ensure that it's even. Make any necessary adjustments to achieve your desired length.

Step 5: Style the Hair

Finally, style the hair as desired. You can use a styling product to add volume or texture to your new short haircut.

Tips for Cutting African American Hair Short

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting African American hair short:

  • Choose a style that complements your face shape and hair type.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Start cutting from the back and move to the front.
  • Take your time and cut the hair in small sections.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help from a professional stylist if you're not sure.

FAQs

How often should I cut my African American hair?

African American hair should be cut every six to eight weeks to maintain its shape and health.

Can I cut my own African American hair?

Yes, you can cut your own African American hair, but it's important to have the right tools and follow the proper techniques to ensure a successful haircut.

What should I do if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don't panic. You can always visit a professional stylist who can help you fix the mistake.

Hair Care After Cutting African American Hair Short

After cutting your African American hair short, it's essential to take care of it properly to maintain its shape and health. Here are some tips:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly to prevent breakage and split ends.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair gently.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and environmental damage by wearing a hat or scarf.

In conclusion, cutting African American hair short can be a fun and exciting change. With the right tools, techniques, and tips, you can achieve a beautiful and stylish new look that complements your face shape and hair type.
