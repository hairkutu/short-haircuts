Why Does My Hair Feel Spiky? | Hair Care Tips and FAQ

If you've ever run your hand through your hair and felt a prickly sensation, you might be wondering why your hair feels spiky. There are a few reasons why this might be happening, and in this article, we'll go over the most common causes of spiky hair and what you can do to resolve the issue.

Table of Contents

  • Dry Hair
  • Product Buildup
  • Split Ends
  • Hard Water
  • How to Fix Spiky Hair
  • Hair Care Tips
  • FAQ

Dry Hair

Dry hair is one of the most common causes of spiky hair. When your hair is dry, the cuticles on each hair strand stand up, making your hair feel rough and prickly. This can be caused by a variety of factors, including:

  • Over-washing your hair
  • Using hot styling tools
  • Exposure to sun and wind

To combat dry hair, try using a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and avoid over-washing your hair. You can also use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to hydrate your hair and keep it soft and smooth.

Product Buildup

Using too much hair product or not washing it out properly can also cause spiky hair. When product builds up on your hair, it can create a sticky residue that makes your hair feel rough and prickly.

To prevent product buildup, try using less product and washing your hair thoroughly after each use. You can also use a clarifying shampoo once a week to remove any buildup that may have accumulated.

Split Ends

Split ends occur when the ends of your hair become frayed and split into two or more strands. This can happen when your hair is damaged by heat or chemicals, or when it's not getting enough moisture.

To prevent split ends, avoid using hot styling tools too often and make sure to get regular trims to remove any damaged ends. You can also use a hair mask or deep conditioning treatment to hydrate your hair and prevent further damage.

Hard Water

If you live in an area with hard water, it could be contributing to your spiky hair. Hard water contains minerals that can build up on your hair and scalp, making your hair feel rough and prickly.

To combat hard water, try using a clarifying shampoo or installing a water softener in your home. You can also rinse your hair with apple cider vinegar to remove any mineral buildup.

How to Fix Spiky Hair

If your hair is feeling spiky, there are a few things you can do to fix the problem:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid over-washing your hair
  • Use less hair product
  • Wash your hair thoroughly after each use
  • Get regular trims to remove split ends
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioning treatment
  • Install a water softener if you have hard water

Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional hair care tips to keep your hair feeling soft and smooth:

  • Avoid using hot styling tools too often
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind
  • Eat a healthy diet with plenty of vitamins and nutrients
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water
  • Get enough sleep to promote healthy hair growth

FAQ

Why does my hair feel rough?

Your hair may feel rough due to dryness, product buildup, split ends, or hard water. Try using a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, washing your hair thoroughly, and getting regular trims to prevent roughness.

Can spiky hair be fixed?

Yes, spiky hair can be fixed by using a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, avoiding over-washing your hair, and getting regular trims to remove split ends. You can also use a hair mask or deep conditioning treatment to hydrate your hair and keep it soft.

How often should I wash my hair?

It depends on your hair type and personal preference. Generally, it's recommended to wash your hair every 2-3 days, but if you have dry hair, you may want to wash it less often.


By following these hair care tips and addressing any underlying issues, you can keep your hair feeling soft and smooth, and avoid the prickly sensation of spiky hair.
