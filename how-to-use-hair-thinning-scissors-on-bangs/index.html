How To Use Hair Thinning Scissors On Bangs A Step By Step Guide

If you're looking to add some texture and volume to your bangs, using hair thinning scissors can be a great option. However, it's important to know how to properly use them to avoid any mishaps. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps of how to use hair thinning scissors on your bangs, as well as provide some tips and answer some frequently asked questions.

How to Use Hair Thinning Scissors on Bangs

Hair thinning scissors are designed to remove some of the hair from a section, creating a more natural, textured look. To use them on your bangs, follow these steps:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Wet hair can make it difficult to see where you are cutting, and can also cause the hair to clump together.
  2. Section off the bangs. Use a comb to create a clean, straight part, separating the bangs from the rest of your hair. Clip the rest of your hair out of the way.
  3. Hold the thinning scissors parallel to your bangs. The teeth of the scissors should be pointing towards the ends of your hair.
  4. Starting at the tips of your bangs, gently close the scissors and slide them up towards the roots. Repeat this motion, working in small sections, until you have thinned out the desired amount of hair.
  5. Check your work in the mirror. Make sure that the hair is still even and that there are no noticeable gaps or uneven areas.
  6. Repeat on the other side.

Remember, it's always better to start with a small amount of hair and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.

Tips for Using Hair Thinning Scissors on Bangs

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when using hair thinning scissors on your bangs:

  • Invest in a good pair of hair thinning scissors. Cheap scissors can cause damage to your hair and can also be difficult to use.
  • Practice on a mannequin head or a willing friend before attempting to cut your own hair.
  • Don't overdo it. It's easy to get carried away with thinning scissors, but remember that a little goes a long way.
  • Use a comb to keep the hair taut while you are cutting. This will help to prevent any uneven areas.
  • Consider the overall shape of your face before deciding how much to thin your bangs. Thinning too much can make your face look rounder, while not thinning enough can make your forehead look too small.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about using hair thinning scissors on bangs:

Can I use regular scissors instead of thinning scissors?

You can, but regular scissors can create a blunt, choppy look. Thinning scissors are designed to create a more natural, textured look.

Can I use hair thinning scissors on curly hair?

Yes, but be careful not to thin too much, as this can cause the curls to lose their shape.

Do I need to use any special products after thinning my bangs?

Not necessarily, but using a texturizing spray or dry shampoo can help to create more volume and texture.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your Bangs

Now that you've successfully thinned your bangs, it's important to take good care of them to keep them looking their best. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep your bangs clean and free of oil.
  • Blow dry your bangs with a round brush to create volume.
  • Consider using a heat protectant spray before using any hot tools on your bangs.
  • Trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to maintain their shape.
  • Avoid touching your bangs too much, as this can cause them to become greasy.

By following these tips, you'll be able to keep your bangs looking great between cuts.

With these steps and tips, you can easily use hair thinning scissors on your bangs to create a more natural, textured look. Just remember to take your time, start with small sections, and practice before attempting to cut your own hair. Happy cutting!
