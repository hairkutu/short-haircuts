Should You Have Layers In Your Hair

Description

If you're considering changing up your hairstyle, you may be wondering whether or not to get layers in your hair. Layers can add volume, movement, and texture to your hair, but they may not be right for everyone. In this article, we'll explore the pros and cons of layered hair and help you decide if it's the right choice for you.

How to Get Layers in Your Hair

Getting layers in your hair is a simple process that can be done at a salon or at home with the right tools. If you're not experienced with cutting hair, we recommend having a professional stylist do it for you to ensure the best results. Here's what to expect:

At the Salon

A stylist will typically start by washing and drying your hair. Then, they will section your hair and use scissors or a razor to cut into the layers. They may use a variety of techniques to create different types of layers, such as face-framing layers or choppy layers. The process usually takes around 30-45 minutes.

At Home

If you're comfortable with cutting your own hair, you can create layers at home with a pair of sharp scissors. Here's how:

  1. Start with dry, clean hair.
  2. Section your hair into layers using hair ties or clips.
  3. Hold each section of hair straight up and cut into it at a slight angle.
  4. Repeat on each section of hair, cutting at different angles and lengths to create texture and movement.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're getting layers at a salon, here's what you can expect:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Section your hair into layers using clips or hair ties.
  3. Consult with your stylist about the type of layers you want and where they should be placed.
  4. Your stylist will use scissors or a razor to cut into each layer, creating texture and movement.
  5. After all the layers are cut, your stylist will style your hair to your desired look.

Tips for Layered Hair

If you decide to get layers in your hair, here are some tips to keep them looking their best:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo to add body to your hair.
  • Blow dry your hair upside down to add volume at the roots.
  • Use a texturizing spray or dry shampoo to add texture and hold.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and length of your layers.
  • Consider using a heat protectant spray before using hot tools like curling irons or straighteners.

FAQ

What face shape is best for layered hair?

Layered hair can work well with almost any face shape, but it's particularly flattering for those with round or square faces. The layers can help to soften the angles of the face and create a more balanced look.

How often should I get my layers trimmed?

We recommend getting your layers trimmed every 6-8 weeks to maintain their shape and prevent split ends.

Can I get layers if I have thin hair?

Yes, layers can actually help to add volume and texture to thin hair. Just make sure to consult with your stylist about the best type of layers for your hair type.

Hair Care for Layered Hair

Layered hair requires some extra care to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. Here are some tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Avoid using heavy products like pomades or waxes, as these can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to add shine and moisture to your hair.

In conclusion, getting layers in your hair can be a great way to add volume, movement, and texture to your hair. With the right care and styling, layered hair can look beautiful and vibrant. We hope this article has helped you decide if layers are right for you!
