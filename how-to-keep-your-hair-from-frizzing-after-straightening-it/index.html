How To Keep Your Hair From Frizzing After Straightening It

Written by Daniel Mar 26, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have ever straightened your hair, you know that frizz can be a huge problem. Frizz can ruin your hairstyle and make it look less polished. But, don't worry! There are many ways to keep your hair from frizzing after straightening it. In this article, we will give you some tips and tricks to keep your hair smooth and shiny.

Table of Contents

  • How to Keep Your Hair from Frizzing After Straightening It
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips to Prevent Frizz
  • FAQs

How to Keep Your Hair from Frizzing After Straightening It

Frizz occurs when the hair cuticle opens up and allows moisture to pass through, causing the hair to expand and become frizzy. To keep your hair from frizzing after straightening it, you need to seal the cuticle and prevent moisture from entering the hair shaft.

Here are some ways to do that:

  • Use a heat protectant spray before straightening your hair. This will help to protect your hair from heat damage and seal the cuticle, preventing frizz.
  • Avoid using high heat settings on your straightening iron. High heat can damage your hair and cause frizz. Use a lower heat setting and take your time to straighten each section of hair.
  • Use a serum or oil to add shine and smoothness to your hair. Apply a small amount to your hair after straightening it to seal the cuticle and prevent frizz.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase at night to prevent your hair from rubbing against cotton, which can cause frizz.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much after straightening it. The oils from your hands can cause frizz.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to keeping your hair from frizzing after straightening it:

  1. Wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair before straightening it.
  3. Use a straightening iron on a low to medium heat setting.
  4. Take your time to straighten each section of hair.
  5. Apply a serum or oil to your hair after straightening it.
  6. Avoid touching your hair too much.
  7. Use a silk or satin pillowcase at night.

Tips to Prevent Frizz

Here are some additional tips to prevent frizz:

  • Avoid using too much product in your hair, as this can weigh it down and make it look greasy.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, rather than a brush. A brush can cause breakage and frizz.
  • Don't wash your hair too often, as this can strip it of its natural oils and cause frizz.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Don't use hot water to wash your hair, as this can open up the cuticle and cause frizz. Use lukewarm water instead.

FAQs

Can I straighten my hair if it's already frizzy?

Yes, but you will need to use a heat protectant spray and a serum or oil to tame the frizz.

How often should I straighten my hair?

It's best to limit heat styling to once or twice a week to prevent damage and frizz.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional hair care tips to keep your hair healthy and frizz-free:

  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth and strength.
  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach or relaxers.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

By following these tips and tricks, you can keep your hair looking smooth and frizz-free after straightening it. Remember to use a heat protectant spray, avoid high heat settings, and use a serum or oil to seal the cuticle and prevent frizz.
