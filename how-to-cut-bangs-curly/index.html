How To Cut Bangs Curly A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Apr 19, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Curly bangs can be a great addition to any hairstyle, adding texture and personality. However, cutting curly bangs can be a bit tricky, especially if you've never done it before. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to cut bangs curly, step by step, and give you some tips and hair care advice along the way.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Bangs Curly
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Bangs Curly
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Curly Bangs

How to Cut Bangs Curly

Cutting curly bangs is different than cutting straight bangs. Curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so you need to cut it longer than you want it to be. Here are the basic steps for cutting curly bangs:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start with clean, dry hair. This will help you see the natural curl pattern of your hair and ensure that your bangs are the right length when they dry.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Separate your bangs from the rest of your hair. Use a comb or your fingers to create a triangle-shaped parting at the front of your head. The base of the triangle should start at the top of your head, and the point should be at the middle of your forehead.

Step 3: Cut Your Bangs

Hold your scissors at a slight angle and cut your bangs in a straight line. Be sure to cut them longer than you want them to be, as they will shrink when they dry. You can always go back and trim them later if needed.

Step 4: Check the Length

Once you've cut your bangs, let them dry naturally. This will give you a better idea of how short they will look when they're dry. If they're too long, you can always trim them a little shorter.

Step by Step Guide

Now that you know the basic steps for cutting curly bangs, let's go through them in more detail:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a curly hair-friendly shampoo and conditioner. Gently towel-dry your hair, being careful not to rub it too hard. If you have time, let your hair air-dry naturally. If you're in a rush, you can use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use a comb or your fingers to create a triangle-shaped section of hair at the front of your head. The base of the triangle should start at the top of your head, and the point should be at the middle of your forehead. Clip the rest of your hair out of the way.

Step 3: Cut Your Bangs

Take a small section of your bangs between your fingers and hold them straight out from your head. Hold your scissors at a slight angle and cut your bangs in a straight line. Repeat this process with small sections of your bangs until you've cut them all. Be sure to cut your bangs longer than you want them to be, as they will shrink when they dry.

Step 4: Check the Length

Once you've cut your bangs, let them dry naturally. This will give you a better idea of how short they will look when they're dry. If they're too long, you can always trim them a little shorter.

Tips for Cutting Bangs Curly

Here are some tips to help you cut your curly bangs:

  • Always cut curly hair when it's dry, as it will shrink when it dries.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Cut your bangs longer than you want them to be, as they will shrink when they dry.
  • Take your time and cut small sections of your bangs at a time.
  • If you're unsure, it's always best to start with a longer length and trim them later if needed.

FAQs

How often should I cut my curly bangs?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how short you want your bangs to be. Generally, you should cut your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh.

Can I cut curly bangs myself?

Yes, you can cut your curly bangs yourself. However, it's always best to have a professional stylist cut your hair if you're unsure or want a more complex style.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic! You can always go to a professional stylist and have them fix it. Alternatively, you can try to style your hair differently to hide the mistake.

Hair Care for Curly Bangs

Curly hair requires special care to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips for caring for your curly bangs:

  • Use a curly hair-friendly shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using a brush on your hair, as it can cause frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair, as they can damage your curls.
  • Sleep on a satin or silk pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to your hair to keep it moisturized and frizz-free.

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you should be able to cut your curly bangs with confidence. Remember, cutting curly hair can be a bit tricky, but with practice, you'll get the hang of it. Happy cutting!
