What Is Neutral Shade In Foundation A Complete Guide

Written by Robby Apr 08, 2023 Â· 7 min read

When it comes to choosing the right foundation shade, it can be a daunting task. With so many options available, it can be challenging to find the perfect match for your skin tone. One of the most popular choices is a neutral shade. But what exactly is a neutral shade in foundation? In this guide, we will explore everything there is to know about neutral shades in foundation.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Find Your Neutral Shade
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Finding Your Neutral Shade
  • Tips for Choosing a Neutral Shade
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Neutral Shades

Description

A neutral shade in foundation is a shade that is neither too warm nor too cool. It's a balance of both, making it a versatile shade that can work for a range of skin tones. Neutral shades typically have equal amounts of yellow and pink undertones, creating a neutral base.

Neutral shades are a great option for those who have difficulty finding the right shade for their skin tone. They can also be a good choice for those who have a combination of warm and cool undertones in their skin.

How to Find Your Neutral Shade

The first step in finding your neutral shade is to determine your skin's undertone. This can be done by looking at the veins on your wrist. If your veins appear blue, you have cool undertones. If they appear green, you have warm undertones. If you can't tell if they are blue or green, you likely have neutral undertones.

Once you know your undertone, you can start looking for a neutral shade that matches. It's important to test the foundation on your jawline to ensure it matches your skin tone. You may also want to try different lighting, as the color can appear different in different lighting conditions.

Step-by-Step Guide to Finding Your Neutral Shade

Here's a step-by-step guide to finding your neutral shade:

  1. Determine your skin's undertone by looking at the veins on your wrist.
  2. Look for foundations that have a neutral undertone.
  3. Test the foundation on your jawline to ensure it matches your skin tone.
  4. Try the foundation in different lighting conditions.
  5. If you're still having trouble finding your perfect match, consider mixing two shades together for a custom blend.

Tips for Choosing a Neutral Shade

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when choosing a neutral shade:

  • Choose a shade that matches your skin tone, not your desired skin tone.
  • Consider the coverage you want. If you want full coverage, you may need to go a shade lighter to avoid looking too heavy.
  • Remember that your skin tone may change throughout the year, so you may need to adjust your foundation shade accordingly.
  • If you have trouble finding a neutral shade, consider getting color-matched at a makeup counter or store.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about neutral shades in foundation:

Q: Can I use a neutral shade if I have warm or cool undertones?

A: Yes, neutral shades can work for a range of undertones. However, if you have a strong warm or cool undertone, you may want to consider a foundation with a stronger undertone to complement your skin.

Q: How do I know if a foundation has a neutral undertone?

A: Look for foundations that are labeled as neutral, or have equal amounts of yellow and pink undertones.

Q: Can I mix a neutral shade with another shade?

A: Yes, mixing shades is a great way to create a custom blend that matches your skin perfectly.

Hair Care for Neutral Shades

When it comes to hair care, those with neutral shades in their foundation can typically wear a range of hair colors. However, it's important to consider your skin's undertone when choosing a hair color. If you have cool undertones, you may want to consider cooler hair colors like ash blonde or cool brown. If you have warm undertones, warmer colors like honey blonde or golden brown may be a better option.

Overall, a neutral shade in foundation is a great option for those who have difficulty finding the right shade for their skin tone. By following these tips and tricks, you can find a neutral shade that matches your skin perfectly.
