How Do You Get Dreadlocks With Short Hair

Written by Daniel Apr 29, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of your short hair and want to try something new? Dreadlocks can be a great way to change up your look, but many people believe that you need long hair to get dreadlocks. That's not true! In this article, we'll go over how to get dreadlocks with short hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get Dreadlocks with Short Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Getting and Maintaining Short Hair Dreadlocks
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Dreadlocks

How to Get Dreadlocks with Short Hair

First things first, it's important to understand that you won't be able to achieve the same length or thickness of dreadlocks with short hair as you would with long hair. However, that doesn't mean you can't create a unique and stylish look with short hair dreadlocks.

The key to getting dreadlocks with short hair is to start with clean, dry hair. You'll also need some products to help the hair lock together, such as beeswax, locking gel, or a locking powder.

Step by Step Guide

Follow these steps to get dreadlocks with short hair:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Section your hair into small, even parts using a comb or your fingers.
  3. Apply a small amount of locking gel, beeswax, or locking powder to each section of hair. Be careful not to use too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it harder for the locks to form.
  4. Using a comb or your fingers, twist each section of hair in a clockwise or counterclockwise direction to create a tight coil. Repeat this process for each section of hair.
  5. Once you've twisted all of your hair, you can either leave the coils as is or use a crochet hook to pull the ends of the hair through the base of the twist. This will help to tighten the lock and make it more secure.
  6. Repeat this process every few days to maintain the locks.

Tips for Getting and Maintaining Short Hair Dreadlocks

Here are some tips to help you get and maintain short hair dreadlocks:

  • Be patient. It takes time for dreadlocks to form, especially with short hair. Don't get discouraged if your locks don't look perfect right away.
  • Avoid using too much product. This can weigh down your hair and make it harder for the locks to form.
  • Wash your hair regularly, but be gentle when shampooing. Use a residue-free shampoo and avoid rubbing your hair too vigorously.
  • Avoid using a hair dryer, as the heat can cause damage and breakage.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and accessories, such as beads or wraps.

FAQs

Can you get dreadlocks with really short hair?

Yes, you can get dreadlocks with really short hair. However, it may take longer for the locks to form and they may not be as thick as longer dreadlocks.

How often should you wash your hair with dreadlocks?

You should wash your hair with dreadlocks once a week or every two weeks. Use a residue-free shampoo and avoid rubbing your hair too vigorously.

Can you undo dreadlocks?

Yes, you can undo dreadlocks, but it can be a time-consuming process. You'll need to use a lot of conditioner and patience to gently comb out each lock.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Once you have dreadlocks, it's important to take care of them to keep them healthy and looking great. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or oil to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can cause buildup and make it harder for the locks to form.
  • Avoid using a hair dryer, as the heat can cause damage and breakage.
  • Be gentle when styling your hair and avoid pulling or tugging on the locks.

Now that you know how to get dreadlocks with short hair, it's time to get started! Remember to be patient, take care of your hair, and have fun experimenting with different styles and accessories.
