How To Find Out What Type Of Natural Hair You Have A Comprehensive Guide

Are you struggling to figure out what type of natural hair you have? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many people are unaware of their hair type, which can make it challenging to take care of their hair properly. In this article, we'll show you how to determine your hair type and provide some tips for taking care of it.

Definition of Hair Types

Before we dive into how to determine your hair type, let's first define the four main hair types:

  • Type 1: Straight Hair
  • Type 2: Wavy Hair
  • Type 3: Curly Hair
  • Type 4: Coily Hair

It's also essential to note that each hair type has subcategories, which we'll discuss in more detail below.

How to Determine Your Hair Type

The easiest way to determine your hair type is by examining the curl pattern. To do this, follow these steps:

  1. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup.
  2. Avoid using any hair products, including conditioner, after washing your hair.
  3. Let your hair air dry without brushing or combing it.
  4. Examine your hair in a mirror and determine the curl pattern.

Once you've determined your curl pattern, you can use the following guide to identify your hair type.

Type 1: Straight Hair

If your hair is straight and lacks any curl or wave, you have type 1 hair. Type 1 hair is typically shiny and reflects light easily. It's also the most resilient hair type and can withstand heat styling and chemical treatments.

Subcategories of type 1 hair include:

  • 1A: Fine and Thin Hair
  • 1B: Medium and Straight Hair
  • 1C: Thick and Straight Hair

Type 2: Wavy Hair

If your hair has a slight wave or curl, you have type 2 hair. Type 2 hair is typically frizz-prone and can be challenging to manage. It's also often susceptible to heat damage.

Subcategories of type 2 hair include:

  • 2A: Loose Waves
  • 2B: Medium Waves
  • 2C: Tight Waves

Type 3: Curly Hair

If your hair has defined curls, you have type 3 hair. Type 3 hair is susceptible to frizz, and its curl pattern can be disrupted by humidity. It's also often dry and requires plenty of moisture.

Subcategories of type 3 hair include:

  • 3A: Loose Curls
  • 3B: Tight Curls
  • 3C: Kinky Curls

Type 4: Coily Hair

If your hair has a tight curl or coil pattern, you have type 4 hair. Type 4 hair is the most delicate hair type and requires plenty of moisture to prevent breakage. It's also often prone to shrinkage and can be challenging to detangle.

Subcategories of type 4 hair include:

  • 4A: Tightly Coiled
  • 4B: Z-Shaped Coils
  • 4C: Tight Coils

Tips for Taking Care of Your Hair Type

Once you've determined your hair type, it's essential to take care of it correctly. Here are some tips for taking care of each hair type:

  • Type 1: Use lightweight products that won't weigh down your hair. Avoid heavy oils and butters.
  • Type 2: Use products that enhance your waves without weighing them down. Scrunch your hair when applying products to encourage your natural wave pattern.
  • Type 3: Use products that hydrate your curls and prevent frizz. Avoid using a hairbrush, as it can disrupt your curl pattern.
  • Type 4: Use products that provide plenty of moisture and prevent breakage. Finger detangle your hair instead of using a comb or brush.

FAQs

Can my hair type change over time?

Yes, your hair type can change over time. Factors that can affect your hair type include age, hormones, and chemical treatments.

What's the best way to determine my hair type?

The best way to determine your hair type is by examining your curl pattern. Follow the steps outlined above to determine your hair type.

Do I need to use different products for each hair type?

Yes, using products that are formulated for your hair type can help you achieve the best results. Using products that aren't suitable for your hair type can lead to dryness, breakage, and other issues.

Hair Care Products for Your Hair Type

Now that you've determined your hair type, it's time to find products that work for you. Here are some hair care products that are formulated for each hair type:

  • Type 1: Paul Mitchell Awapuhi Wild Ginger Moisturizing Lather Shampoo
  • Type 2: Shea Moisture Coconut & Hibiscus Curl Enhancing Smoothie
  • Type 3: Ouidad Curl Quencher Moisturizing Conditioner
  • Type 4: Carol's Daughter Black Vanilla Moisture & Shine Leave-In Conditioner

Remember, finding the right products for your hair type may take some trial and error. Don't be afraid to experiment until you find what works for you.

Conclusion

Determining your hair type is an essential first step in taking care of your hair. By following the steps outlined in this article, you can identify your hair type and find products that work for you. Remember to take care of your hair, and it will reward you with healthy, beautiful locks.
