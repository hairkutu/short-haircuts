How To Cut Out Layers In Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Jul 13, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking for a way to add some dimension and movement to your hair, layers are a great option. But if you're not keen on heading to the salon and would rather try your hand at cutting your own layers, it can be a bit tricky. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to cut out layers in hair, step by step.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Out Layers in Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Layers in Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting Layers

How to Cut Out Layers in Hair

Before you start cutting, it's important to have a plan in mind. Think about how many layers you want, where you want the shortest layers to fall, and what type of layers you want (long, choppy, etc.). It's also important to have the right tools on hand:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Spray bottle with water

Once you have your plan and tools ready, you can start cutting your layers.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair. Towel dry your hair until it's damp, but not dripping wet. Comb out any tangles.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use a comb to part your hair down the middle. Then, make another part from the top of one ear to the top of the other ear. You should now have four sections of hair: two in the front and two in the back. Clip the sections you're not working on out of the way.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Starting with the bottom section in the back, take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, starting at the length you want the shortest layer to be. Repeat this process, working your way up to the top section of hair in the back.

Once you're done with the back, move on to the front sections. When cutting the front sections, make sure to angle the scissors away from your face to create a framing effect.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the layers, it's time to blend them together. Take a comb and comb through your hair, making sure the layers flow seamlessly into each other. If necessary, go back in and make any additional cuts to blend the layers together.

Tips for Cutting Layers in Hair

  • Start with a little bit of hair and work your way up. It's easier to cut more hair off than to try to fix a mistake.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure clean cuts.
  • If you're not sure how short to cut your layers, start with a longer length and work your way shorter.
  • When cutting the front sections, angle the scissors away from your face to create a framing effect.
  • Take breaks to step back and look at your progress. This will help you avoid over-cutting.

FAQs

How often should you cut layers in your hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly you want to maintain your layers. On average, every 8-12 weeks is a good time frame to trim your hair and maintain your layers.

Can you cut layers in curly hair?

Yes! Layers can add definition and movement to curly hair. However, it's important to take your curls into account when cutting your layers. Curly hair tends to shrink up when dry, so make sure to cut your layers longer than you want them to be.

Should you cut your hair wet or dry?

It's best to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can shrink up when it dries and alter the length of your layers.

Hair Care After Cutting Layers

Once you've cut your layers, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and shape. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a heat protectant when styling with hot tools.
  • Avoid using too much product, as it can weigh down your layers.
  • Trim your hair every 8-12 weeks to maintain your layers.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it hydrated and healthy.

And that's it! With a little bit of planning and the right tools, you can achieve a fresh, layered look without ever leaving your home.
