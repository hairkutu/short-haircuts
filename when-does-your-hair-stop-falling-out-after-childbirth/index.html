When Does Your Hair Stop Falling Out After Childbirth A Complete Guide

Written by Robby Aug 10, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you a new mom wondering when your hair fall will stop? If yes, then this post is for you. Postpartum hair loss is a common experience for many new moms. It happens due to hormonal changes that occur during pregnancy and after childbirth. In this post, we will discuss when your hair will stop falling out after childbirth and how you can manage this hair loss.

When Does Your Hair Stop Falling Out After Childbirth?

Postpartum hair loss usually starts around three months after childbirth. It can last for up to six months or longer. The extent of hair loss varies from person to person. Some women may experience mild hair loss, while others may lose a significant amount of hair.

The good news is that postpartum hair loss is temporary, and your hair will eventually grow back. However, it may take several months to a year for your hair to return to its pre-pregnancy state.

How to Manage Postpartum Hair Loss?

While postpartum hair loss is a natural process, there are a few things you can do to manage it:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as hair dyes and relaxers.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to prevent further damage.
  • Avoid styling your hair with heat tools, such as flat irons and curling wands.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth.
  • Take a multivitamin supplement to ensure you are getting all the necessary nutrients.

Step-by-Step Guide to Stop Hair Fall After Childbirth

Here is a step-by-step guide to help you manage postpartum hair loss:

  1. Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Avoid using hot water to wash your hair, as it can damage your hair follicles.
  3. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair gently.
  4. Avoid using a hairbrush, as it can pull out more hair.
  5. Avoid styling your hair with heat tools, such as flat irons and curling wands.
  6. If you need to style your hair, use a low-heat setting and a heat protectant spray.
  7. Avoid wearing tight hairstyles, such as braids and ponytails, as they can pull out more hair.
  8. Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth.
  9. Take a multivitamin supplement to ensure you are getting all the necessary nutrients.
  10. Be patient and give your hair time to grow back.

Tips to Prevent Hair Loss After Childbirth

Here are a few tips to help prevent hair loss after childbirth:

  • Take care of your hair by washing and conditioning it regularly.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as hair dyes and relaxers.
  • Avoid styling your hair with heat tools, such as flat irons and curling wands.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth.
  • Take a multivitamin supplement to ensure you are getting all the necessary nutrients.
  • Get enough rest and sleep to reduce stress and promote hair growth.
  • Avoid wearing tight hairstyles, such as braids and ponytails, as they can pull out more hair.

FAQs

1. Is postpartum hair loss permanent?

No, postpartum hair loss is temporary, and your hair will eventually grow back.

2. How long does it take for hair to grow back after childbirth?

It may take several months to a year for your hair to return to its pre-pregnancy state.

3. Can I use hair growth products to stop postpartum hair loss?

While hair growth products can help promote hair growth, it's best to consult your doctor before using them.

Hair Care Tips for New Moms

Here are a few hair care tips for new moms:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as hair dyes and relaxers.
  • Avoid styling your hair with heat tools, such as flat irons and curling wands.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth.
  • Take a multivitamin supplement to ensure you are getting all the necessary nutrients.
  • Get enough rest and sleep to reduce stress and promote hair growth.
  • Avoid wearing tight hairstyles, such as braids and ponytails, as they can pull out more hair.
  • Be patient and give your hair time to grow back.

In conclusion, postpartum hair loss is a common experience for new moms. It usually starts around three months after childbirth and can last for up to six months or longer. While it's a natural process, there are a few things you can do to manage it, such as using a gentle shampoo and conditioner, avoiding heat tools, and eating a healthy diet. Remember to be patient and give your hair time to grow back to its pre-pregnancy state.
