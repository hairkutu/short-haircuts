How To Cut Mid Length Layered Haircuts A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby May 29, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Mid length layered haircuts are one of the most versatile styles out there. They suit almost every face shape, hair type, and age group. With the right approach, cutting mid length layered haircuts can be a breeze. Here's everything you need to know to get started.

Description

Mid length layered haircuts are haircuts that are shoulder-length or slightly longer, with layers that start at the chin or cheekbones. These layers add volume and texture to the hair, making it look fuller and more dynamic. The layers can be cut at different lengths and angles, depending on the desired effect.

How to

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a spray bottle filled with water. Here's how to proceed:

Step 1: Divide the Hair

Start by dividing the hair into four sections: two at the front and two at the back. Use hair clips to keep each section separate.

Step 2: Cut the Back

Start with the back sections. Comb the hair straight down and cut it straight across at the desired length. Then, take a section of hair from the bottom and cut it at a slight angle, creating the first layer. Continue cutting sections at different angles until you reach the desired amount of layers.

Step 3: Cut the Front

Move on to the front sections. Comb the hair straight down and cut it straight across at the desired length. Then, take a section of hair from the side and cut it at a slight angle, creating the first layer. Repeat on the other side.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the layers, use the scissors to blend them together. Cut into the hair at a slight angle, working from the bottom up. This will create a seamless transition between the layers.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results:

  • Don't cut too much hair at once. It's better to cut small sections and build up the layers gradually.
  • Keep the scissors sharp. Dull scissors can damage the hair and create split ends.
  • Use the comb to hold the hair taut while you cut. This will ensure a clean, even cut.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting hair can be tiring, so don't push yourself too hard.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting mid length layered haircuts:

Q: How often should I get my mid length layered haircut trimmed?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how well you maintain it. As a general rule, aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

Q: Can I cut my own mid length layered haircut at home?

A: Yes, you can. However, it's important to have the right tools and approach. If you're not confident, it's best to leave it to a professional.

Hair Care

To keep your mid length layered haircut looking its best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that suits your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, and always use a heat protectant.
  • Trim the ends regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair nourished and hydrated.

With these tips and techniques, you can cut mid length layered haircuts like a pro. Whether you're going for a classic or trendy look, this style is sure to turn heads.
