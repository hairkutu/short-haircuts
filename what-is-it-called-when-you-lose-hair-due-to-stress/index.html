What Is It Called When You Lose Hair Due To Stress

Written by Christine Aug 16, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Have you been experiencing hair loss lately? If so, you might be wondering what is causing it. One possible reason for hair loss is stress. In this blog post, we'll explore what it's called when you lose hair due to stress, how to prevent it, and what you can do to promote healthy hair growth.

Table of Contents:

  1. Description
  2. How to Identify Hair Loss Due to Stress
  3. Step by Step Guide to Preventing Hair Loss Due to Stress
  4. Tips for Promoting Healthy Hair Growth
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care Routine for Preventing Hair Loss Due to Stress

Description

When you lose hair due to stress, it's called telogen effluvium. This condition occurs when your body experiences a lot of stress, and your hair follicles go into a resting phase. During this phase, your hair might fall out more easily than usual.

Most of the time, telogen effluvium is temporary. Once your body recovers from the stress, your hair will start growing back. However, if you continue to experience a lot of stress, your hair might not grow back as quickly or as thickly as it used to.

How to Identify Hair Loss Due to Stress

If you're experiencing hair loss, it's important to figure out what's causing it. Here are some signs that your hair loss might be due to stress:

  • You've been under a lot of stress lately
  • You're losing more hair than usual when you brush or wash it
  • You're noticing bald patches or thinning areas on your scalp
  • You're shedding hair all over your body, not just on your scalp

If you're experiencing any of these symptoms, it's a good idea to talk to your doctor or a dermatologist. They can help you figure out what's causing your hair loss and recommend treatment options.

Step by Step Guide to Preventing Hair Loss Due to Stress

If you're experiencing hair loss due to stress, there are several things you can do to prevent it:

  1. Identify the source of your stress and try to reduce it. Whether it's work, family, or financial stress, finding ways to manage it can help reduce your hair loss.
  2. Practice stress-reducing activities like yoga, meditation, or deep breathing exercises.
  3. Eat a healthy diet full of vitamins and minerals that promote hair growth, such as iron, zinc, and biotin.
  4. Take a multivitamin or hair supplement that contains these vitamins and minerals.
  5. Avoid harsh hair treatments like coloring, perming, or using hot tools.
  6. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that won't damage your hair.
  7. Avoid tight hairstyles like braids, ponytails, or buns that can pull on your hair.
  8. Get enough sleep every night to help your body recover from stress.
  9. If you're experiencing severe hair loss, talk to your doctor about prescription medications or hair transplant treatments.

By following these steps, you can help prevent hair loss due to stress and promote healthy hair growth.

Tips for Promoting Healthy Hair Growth

In addition to preventing hair loss, there are several things you can do to promote healthy hair growth:

  • Eat a healthy diet full of protein, vitamins, and minerals
  • Massage your scalp daily to increase blood flow to your hair follicles
  • Avoid using hot tools like curling irons or straighteners
  • Use a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush to avoid pulling on your hair
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair like bleach or relaxers
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner with SPF

By following these tips, you can promote healthy hair growth and prevent hair loss due to stress.

FAQs

Q: Is hair loss due to stress permanent?

A: In most cases, hair loss due to stress is temporary. Once your body recovers from the stress, your hair will start growing back. However, if you continue to experience a lot of stress, your hair might not grow back as quickly or as thickly as it used to.

Q: Can stress cause baldness?

A: In rare cases, stress can cause a condition called alopecia areata, which causes bald patches on the scalp. However, this condition is not usually caused by everyday stressors and is more commonly linked to autoimmune disorders.

Q: Can I use hair products to promote hair growth?

A: There are many hair products on the market that claim to promote hair growth, but it's important to be cautious when using them. Some products may contain harsh chemicals that can damage your hair or scalp. It's best to talk to your doctor or a dermatologist before using any hair growth products.

Hair Care Routine for Preventing Hair Loss Due to Stress

Here's a simple hair care routine you can follow to prevent hair loss due to stress:

  1. Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner every other day
  2. Avoid using harsh chemicals or hot tools on your hair
  3. Eat a healthy diet full of protein, vitamins, and minerals
  4. Massage your scalp daily to increase blood flow to your hair follicles
  5. Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair, like braids or ponytails
  6. Get enough sleep every night to help your body recover from stress

By following this routine, you can keep your hair healthy and prevent hair loss due to stress.

Conclusion

Losing hair due to stress can be a frustrating and emotional experience, but it's important to remember that it's usually temporary. By taking steps to manage your stress, eating a healthy diet, and following a gentle hair care routine, you can promote healthy hair growth and prevent hair loss. If you're experiencing severe hair loss, talk to your doctor or a dermatologist for personalized treatment options.
