Should I Use Thinning Scissors

Written by Christine Sep 09, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you've ever wondered whether thinning scissors are a good choice for your hair, you're not alone. With so many different types of hair cutting tools available, it can be difficult to know which ones are right for you. In this article, we'll explore the pros and cons of thinning scissors, as well as provide some tips for using them effectively.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ

Description

Thinning scissors, also known as texturizing shears, are a type of hair cutting tool that are designed to remove bulk from the hair. Unlike regular scissors, which simply cut the hair straight across, thinning scissors have teeth or notches on one or both blades. These teeth or notches help to remove some of the hair as it is being cut, resulting in a more textured and layered look.

Thinning scissors are often used to create a more natural-looking haircut, especially for people with thick or heavy hair. They can also be used to thin out the ends of the hair, which can help to reduce split ends and breakage.

How to

Using thinning scissors is not difficult, but it does require some practice and skill. Here are some basic steps to get you started:

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair as usual.
  2. Section your hair into manageable sections using hair clips or elastics.
  3. Hold the thinning scissors at a slight angle and make small, gentle cuts into the hair, working from the top of the section down to the ends. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once, as this can result in uneven or choppy layers.
  4. Repeat this process on each section of hair, blending the layers as you go.
  5. Once you have finished cutting, style your hair as usual.

Step by step

For a more detailed guide on how to use thinning scissors, follow these step-by-step instructions:

Step 1: Wash and dry your hair

Before using thinning scissors, it's important to start with clean, dry hair. This will make it easier to see the texture of the hair and ensure that the scissors cut smoothly.

Step 2: Section your hair

Using hair clips or elastics, divide your hair into manageable sections. The size of the sections will depend on the thickness and length of your hair.

Step 3: Hold the scissors correctly

Hold the thinning scissors at a slight angle, with the teeth or notches facing upwards. This will help to ensure that the hair is cut evenly and smoothly.

Step 4: Make small cuts

Starting at the top of the section, make small, gentle cuts into the hair, working from the roots down to the ends. Be sure to cut only a small amount of hair at once, as cutting too much can result in uneven or choppy layers. Repeat this process on each section of hair, working from the top down.

Step 5: Blend the layers

As you cut each section of hair, be sure to blend the layers together by cutting slightly into the previously cut sections. This will help to create a more natural and seamless look.

Step 6: Style your hair

Once you have finished cutting, style your hair as usual. You may notice that your hair feels lighter and more textured than before.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when using thinning scissors:

  • Practice on a small section of hair before trying to cut your entire head.
  • Use the scissors sparingly, as overuse can result in a choppy or uneven look.
  • Always cut in small sections, working from the top of the section down to the ends.
  • Be sure to blend the layers together as you go, for a more natural look.
  • Consider consulting a professional hairstylist before attempting to use thinning scissors on your own.

FAQ

What is the difference between thinning scissors and regular scissors?

Thinning scissors have teeth or notches on one or both blades, which helps to remove some of the hair as it is being cut. Regular scissors cut the hair straight across, without removing any of the bulk.

Can thinning scissors be used on all hair types?

Thinning scissors are generally best suited for people with thick or heavy hair. They can be used on other hair types as well, but may not be as effective.

How often should I use thinning scissors?

Thinning scissors should be used sparingly, as overuse can result in a choppy or uneven look. It's best to consult with a professional hairstylist before attempting to use thinning scissors on your own.

Hair Care

Using thinning scissors can be a great way to add texture and depth to your hair. However, it's important to take care of your hair to ensure that it stays healthy and strong. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage the hair.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to help nourish the hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of fruits, vegetables, and protein.

By following these simple tips, you can keep your hair looking and feeling healthy, no matter what type of haircut or styling tools you use.
