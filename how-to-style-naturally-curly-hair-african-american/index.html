How to Style Naturally Curly Hair African American

Naturally curly hair can be challenging to style, but it also offers endless possibilities. The key to styling African American curly hair is to embrace its natural texture and find the best products and techniques that work for you.

Before you start styling your hair, make sure to have the right tools on hand. You'll need a wide-tooth comb, hair clips, a diffuser, and products such as a leave-in conditioner, curl defining cream, and styling gel.

1. Start with Clean Hair

Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and follow up with a moisturizing conditioner. Gently detangle your hair with a wide-tooth comb while the conditioner is still in your hair, then rinse thoroughly.

2. Apply Leave-In Conditioner

After washing and rinsing your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to help detangle and moisturize your curls. Use a dime-sized amount and distribute it evenly throughout your hair using your fingers or a wide-tooth comb.

3. Apply Curl Defining Cream

Next, apply a curl defining cream to your hair. Use a quarter-sized amount and work it through your hair from roots to ends. Scrunch your hair with your hands to encourage your curls to form.

4. Scrunch with Styling Gel

After applying the curl defining cream, scrunch your hair with a styling gel to hold your curls in place. Use a dime-sized amount and work it through your hair, scrunching as you go.

5. Diffuse Your Hair

Attach a diffuser to your hair dryer and set it to a low heat and speed setting. Flip your head over and diffuse your hair by cupping your curls in the diffuser and lifting it towards your scalp. This will help enhance your curls and reduce frizz.

6. Finish with Hair Oil

Once your hair is dry, finish it off with a hair oil to add shine and lock in moisture. Use a dime-sized amount and apply it to the ends of your hair, working your way up towards your scalp.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to styling your naturally curly hair as an African American:

  1. Start with clean hair
  2. Apply leave-in conditioner
  3. Apply curl defining cream
  4. Scrunch with styling gel
  5. Diffuse your hair
  6. Finish with hair oil

Tips for Styling Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for styling your naturally curly hair:

  • Avoid using a brush on your curls, as it can cause frizz and breakage
  • Use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to dry your hair, as regular towels can cause frizz
  • Try different product combinations and techniques to find what works best for your hair
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray before using styling tools
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends

Frequently Asked Questions

What are some good products for styling curly hair?

Some good products for styling curly hair include leave-in conditioners, curl defining creams, styling gels, and hair oils. Look for products that are specifically formulated for curly hair and contain moisturizing ingredients.

How often should I wash my curly hair?

How often you wash your curly hair depends on your hair type and personal preference. Some people with curly hair prefer to wash it once a week, while others may wash it more frequently. However, it's important not to over-wash your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils and cause dryness.

How can I prevent frizz in my curly hair?

To prevent frizz in your curly hair, avoid using a regular towel to dry it and instead use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt. You can also apply a hair oil or serum to the ends of your hair to help seal in moisture and prevent frizz. Additionally, avoid using a brush on your hair, as this can cause frizz and breakage.

Hair Care for Naturally Curly Hair

In addition to styling your curly hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and moisturizing conditioner
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or swim cap
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends

By following these tips and techniques, you can style your naturally curly hair as an African American with confidence and ease.
