How To Fix Dry Damaged Hair After Bleaching

Written by Christine Jun 09, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you dealing with dry, damaged hair after bleaching? Don't worry, you're not alone. Bleaching can cause significant damage to your hair, leaving it dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. But there are steps you can take to repair and restore your hair to its former glory. In this article, we'll show you how to fix dry damaged hair after bleaching, step by step.

How to Fix Dry Damaged Hair After Bleaching

The first step to fixing dry, damaged hair after bleaching is to assess the extent of the damage. If your hair is severely damaged, it may be best to seek professional help from a hair stylist. However, if your hair is moderately damaged, you can try these steps to repair and restore it:

1. Trim your hair

The first step to repairing damaged hair is to get rid of the damaged ends. Trim your hair regularly to remove split ends and prevent further damage.

2. Use a protein treatment

Bleaching can strip your hair of its natural proteins, making it weak and prone to breakage. Use a protein treatment to help restore the strength and elasticity of your hair.

3. Deep condition your hair

Deep conditioning can help restore moisture to your hair and prevent further damage. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.

4. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling can further damage your hair, making it more prone to breakage. Avoid using heat styling tools like hair dryers, curling irons, and flat irons, and let your hair air dry instead.

5. Use a leave-in conditioner

Use a leave-in conditioner to help protect your hair from further damage and keep it hydrated throughout the day.

6. Limit washing your hair

Washing your hair too frequently can strip it of its natural oils, making it more prone to dryness and damage. Limit washing your hair to once or twice a week, and use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid further damage.

Step by Step

If you're not sure where to start, here's a step-by-step guide to fixing dry, damaged hair after bleaching:

Step 1: Trim your hair

Trim your hair to remove split ends and prevent further damage.

Step 2: Use a protein treatment

Use a protein treatment to help restore the strength and elasticity of your hair.

Step 3: Deep condition your hair

Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.

Step 4: Avoid heat styling

Avoid using heat styling tools like hair dryers, curling irons, and flat irons.

Step 5: Use a leave-in conditioner

Use a leave-in conditioner to help protect your hair from further damage and keep it hydrated throughout the day.

Step 6: Limit washing your hair

Limit washing your hair to once or twice a week, and use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid further damage.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you fix dry, damaged hair after bleaching:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, as it's gentler on your hair than a brush.
  • Avoid using hair elastics or hair accessories that can pull on your hair and cause breakage.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, like hair dyes and relaxers, as they can further damage your hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.

FAQ

Q: Can damaged hair be repaired?

A: Yes, damaged hair can be repaired with the right care and treatment.

Q: How long does it take to repair damaged hair?

A: The length of time it takes to repair damaged hair depends on the extent of the damage. It can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months to see results.

Q: Can I bleach my hair again after it's been damaged?

A: It's best to wait until your hair has fully recovered before bleaching it again, to avoid further damage.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy, beautiful hair. Here are some tips for taking care of your hair:

  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.
  • Use a conditioner to help hydrate and nourish your hair.
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair, as it can dry it out.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like hair dryers, curling irons, and flat irons, or use them on a low heat setting.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.

By following these tips and taking the necessary steps to repair and restore your hair, you can fix dry damaged hair after bleaching and achieve healthy, beautiful hair once again.
