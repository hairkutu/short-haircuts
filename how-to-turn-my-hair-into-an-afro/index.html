How To Turn Your Hair Into An Afro

Written by Aprilia Jun 11, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you tired of your straight hair and want to switch things up with an afro? Look no further! In this blog post, I will show you step by step how to turn your hair into an afro. From the basics of what an afro is to tips and tricks for maintaining your new look, I’ve got you covered.

Description

An afro is a hairstyle that is typically associated with people of African descent. It is characterized by curly or tightly-coiled hair that is styled to stand up and out from the head. Afro hair can be worn in a variety of lengths and styles, from short and cropped to long and braided.

How to

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

The first step in turning your hair into an afro is to make sure it is clean. Wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and prevent breakage.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

After washing your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and soft. This will make it easier to style and prevent damage from heat styling tools.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Using a blow dryer, dry your hair until it is about 80% dry. Make sure to use a diffuser attachment to prevent frizz and maintain your hair’s natural texture.

Step 4: Use a Pick Comb

Once your hair is mostly dry, use a pick comb to create volume and shape. Starting at the roots, gently comb your hair in an upward motion. This will help create the signature round shape of an afro.

Step 5: Define Your Curls

If you have naturally curly hair, you can skip this step. If your hair is straight or has a looser curl pattern, use a curl defining cream or gel to enhance your curls. Work the product through your hair, scrunching it upwards as you go. This will help create a more defined curl pattern.

Step 6: Finish With a Hair Oil

To keep your hair hydrated and prevent frizz, finish with a hair oil. Apply a small amount to the palms of your hands and work it through your hair, focusing on the ends.

Tips

- Use a satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage while you sleep.

- Don’t over-wash your hair. Afro hair is naturally dry, so washing it too often can strip it of its natural oils.

- Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

FAQ

Q: Can I turn my straight hair into an afro?

A: Yes! Follow the steps above to create an afro on straight hair.

Q: Can I wear my afro in different styles?

A: Yes! Afros can be worn in a variety of styles, from a simple round shape to more elaborate braided styles.

Hair Care

It is important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and prevent breakage. Here are some tips:

- Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.

- Avoid using heat styling tools too often.

- Moisturize your hair regularly with a leave-in conditioner or hair oil.

- Trim your ends regularly to prevent split ends.

With these tips and tricks, you’ll be rocking your new afro in no time!
