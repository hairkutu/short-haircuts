Our Top Picks: Best Middle Aged Fashion Bloggers

These stylish women are redefining what it means to be fashionable in middle age. Whether you're looking for casual outfits or formal wear, these bloggers have got you covered.

1. Lyn Slater of Accidental Icon

Lyn Slater is a professor, model, and fashion blogger who started her blog, Accidental Icon, in her 60s. Her style is edgy and eclectic, with a focus on black and white pieces and bold accessories.

2. Catherine Summers of Not Dressed As Lamb

Catherine Summers is a UK-based blogger who started her blog, Not Dressed As Lamb, in her 40s. Her style is vibrant and colorful, with a focus on mixing patterns and textures.

3. Deborah Boland of Fabulous After 40

Deborah Boland is a fashion expert who started her blog, Fabulous After 40, to help women over 40 look and feel their best. Her style is classic and timeless, with a focus on quality over quantity.

4. Beth Djalali of Style at a Certain Age

Beth Djalali is a blogger who started her blog, Style at a Certain Age, in her 50s. Her style is preppy and polished, with a focus on classic pieces like blazers and loafers.

How to Find Your Own Style in Middle Age

As we age, our bodies and lifestyles change, and so do our fashion needs. Here are some tips for finding your own unique style in middle age:

1. Take inventory of your closet. Before you start shopping, take a look at what you already own. Which pieces make you feel confident and comfortable? Which ones do you never wear? This will give you a better idea of what to look for when shopping.

2. Experiment with different styles. Don't be afraid to try something new! You might be surprised by what looks good on you. Play around with different styles and silhouettes until you find what works for you.

3. Invest in quality pieces. As we get older, it's important to invest in high-quality pieces that will last. Look for classic pieces that won't go out of style, like a well-tailored blazer or a pair of leather boots.

4. Accessorize. Accessories can take an outfit from basic to chic. Invest in a few statement pieces, like a bold necklace or a colorful scarf, to add some personality to your outfits.

Step-by-Step Guide to Building a Chic Wardrobe

Building a chic wardrobe doesn't have to be overwhelming. Follow these simple steps to create a wardrobe you love:

1. Create a color palette. Choose a few colors that you love and that look good on you. This will make it easier to mix and match pieces in your wardrobe.

2. Invest in basics. Every wardrobe needs a few basic pieces, like a white t-shirt, a pair of jeans, and a black blazer. These pieces can be dressed up or down and will never go out of style.

3. Mix and match. Don't be afraid to mix and match pieces in your wardrobe. A blazer can be worn with jeans or a dress, and a statement necklace can dress up a basic t-shirt.

4. Add some personality. Accessories are a great way to add some personality to your outfits. Invest in a few statement pieces, like a bold necklace or a colorful scarf.

Fashion Tips for Women in Their 40s and Beyond

Here are some fashion tips for women in their 40s and beyond:

1. Embrace your body. As we age, our bodies change, and that's okay! Embrace your body and dress in a way that makes you feel confident and comfortable.

2. Don't be afraid to stand out. As we get older, we tend to blend in more. Don't be afraid to stand out and wear something bold and eye-catching.

3. Invest in comfortable shoes. Comfort is key as we age, so invest in comfortable shoes that you can wear all day.

4. Dress for your lifestyle. Your wardrobe should reflect your lifestyle. If you're a busy mom, focus on comfortable and practical pieces. If you work in an office, invest in some classic workwear.

Frequently Asked Questions About Middle Aged Fashion

Q: Can I still wear trendy pieces in middle age?

A: Absolutely! There's no age limit on fashion. Just make sure to balance trendy pieces with classic pieces to create a timeless look.

Q: What should I wear to a job interview in my 50s?

A: It's always best to err on the side of caution when dressing for a job interview. Stick to classic pieces like a blazer and slacks.

Hair Care Tips for Middle Aged Women

As we age, our hair can become thinner and more brittle. Here are some hair care tips for middle aged women:

1. Use a gentle shampoo. Look for a shampoo that's gentle on your hair and scalp. Avoid shampoos with harsh chemicals that can strip your hair of its natural oils.

2. Invest in a good conditioner. Conditioning is key for keeping your hair healthy and moisturized. Look for a conditioner that's formulated for your hair type.

3. Protect your hair from heat. Heat styling can damage your hair, so use a heat protectant spray before using a curling iron or straightener.

4. Get regular trims. Regular trims can help prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy and full.

Now that you have some inspiration and tips, it's time to start building your own chic wardrobe. Remember, fashion is all about expressing yourself and feeling confident in your own skin. Happy shopping!
