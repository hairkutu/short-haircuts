Which Glue Is Best For Lace Front Wigs A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Jun 23, 2023 Â· 11 min read

When it comes to wearing lace front wigs, one of the most important things to consider is the type of adhesive you use. A good glue not only keeps your wig securely in place, but also ensures that it looks natural and seamless. With so many different types of wig glue available on the market, it can be overwhelming to figure out which one is best for your needs. That's why we've put together this comprehensive guide to help you find the perfect glue for your lace front wig.

  2. How to Apply Wig Glue

Types of Wig Glue

There are several types of wig glue available on the market, each with its own unique properties and benefits. Here are some of the most popular types:

Liquid Adhesive

Liquid adhesive is a popular choice for many wig wearers because it provides a strong hold and is easy to apply. It typically comes in a small bottle with a brush applicator, making it easy to control the amount of glue you use. This type of glue can be water-based or solvent-based, and can either be clear or colored.

Tape

Tape is another popular option for securing lace front wigs. It comes in a variety of shapes and sizes, including strips and rolls, and can be either double-sided or single-sided. Tape is generally easier to apply than liquid adhesive, but may not provide as strong of a hold.

Cream Adhesive

Cream adhesive is a thick, creamy substance that is applied to the scalp with a brush or spatula. This type of glue provides a strong hold and is ideal for people with sensitive skin, as it is less likely to cause irritation than liquid or tape adhesives.

How to Apply Wig Glue

Before applying wig glue, it's important to thoroughly clean your scalp and the area around your hairline. This will help ensure that the glue adheres properly and lasts as long as possible. Here's a basic rundown of how to apply wig glue:

Step 1: Apply Scalp Protector

Before applying wig glue, it's a good idea to apply a scalp protector to the area around your hairline. This will help prevent irritation and make it easier to remove the glue later on.

Step 2: Apply Glue

Using a brush or spatula, apply a thin layer of glue to your scalp, starting at the hairline and working your way back. Be careful not to apply too much glue, as this can cause the wig to look bulky and unnatural.

Step 3: Wait for Glue to Dry

After applying the glue, wait a few minutes for it to dry. This will help ensure that the wig adheres properly and lasts as long as possible.

Step 4: Attach Wig

Carefully place the wig onto your head, starting at the hairline and pressing down firmly to secure it in place. Use a comb or brush to smooth out any bumps or lumps in the wig.

Step-by-Step Guide to Applying Wig Glue

Here's a more detailed guide to help you apply wig glue:

Step 1: Prepare Your Scalp

Start by thoroughly cleaning your scalp and the area around your hairline with a mild shampoo and warm water. Rinse your scalp thoroughly and let it air dry.

Step 2: Apply Scalp Protector

Using a cotton ball or swab, apply a scalp protector to the area around your hairline. This will help prevent irritation and make it easier to remove the glue later on.

Step 3: Apply Glue

Using a brush or spatula, apply a thin layer of glue to your scalp, starting at the hairline and working your way back. Be sure to apply the glue evenly and avoid getting it on your hair.

Step 4: Wait for Glue to Dry

After applying the glue, wait a few minutes for it to dry. You'll know the glue is dry when it feels tacky to the touch.

Step 5: Attach Wig

Carefully place the wig onto your head, starting at the hairline and pressing down firmly to secure it in place. Use a comb or brush to smooth out any bumps or lumps in the wig.

Tips for Using Wig Glue

Here are some tips for using wig glue:

  • Always do a patch test before using a new glue to make sure you're not allergic.
  • Apply glue in thin, even layers to avoid a bulky or unnatural look.
  • Don't apply too much pressure when attaching the wig, as this can cause the glue to shift and create gaps.
  • Use a wig cap to help protect your natural hair and make it easier to apply the wig.

FAQs About Wig Glue

Q: How long does wig glue usually last?

A: The length of time wig glue lasts depends on several factors, including the type of glue used, the amount applied, and the wearer's skin type. Generally, wig glue can last anywhere from a few days to a few weeks.

Q: Is wig glue safe to use?

A: Most wig glues are safe to use, but it's important to do a patch test before using a new product to make sure you're not allergic. If you experience any itching, redness, or irritation, remove the wig immediately and consult a dermatologist.

Q: Can wig glue damage my natural hair?

A: Wig glue is generally safe for your natural hair, but it's important to use it properly to avoid damaging your hairline or scalp. If you experience any hair loss or damage, stop using the glue immediately and consult a hair care professional.

Hair Care Tips for Wearing Wigs

Wearing a wig can be a great way to change up your look, but it's important to take care of your natural hair and scalp while wearing it. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair while wearing a wig:

  • Wash your natural hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products or heat styling tools on your natural hair.
  • Moisturize your scalp regularly to prevent dryness and itching.
  • Remove your wig at night to give your scalp a chance to breathe.
  • Store your wig properly to prevent tangling and damage.

By following these tips and using the right wig glue, you can enjoy a natural-looking, comfortable wig that lasts as long as you need it to.
