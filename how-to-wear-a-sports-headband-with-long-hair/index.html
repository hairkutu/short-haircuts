How To Wear A Sports Headband With Long Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 20, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you tired of your long hair getting in the way during your workout? A sports headband can be a great solution to keep your hair out of your face while you exercise. However, if you have long hair, it can be tricky to figure out how to wear a headband without it slipping or falling off. In this article, we'll provide a step by step guide on how to wear a sports headband with long hair, along with some tips and FAQ.

Table of Contents

  • How to Wear a Sports Headband with Long Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Wearing a Sports Headband with Long Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Tips for Active Women

How to Wear a Sports Headband with Long Hair

Before we dive into the step by step guide, let's talk about what type of headband to choose. Look for a headband that is made of moisture-wicking material, such as polyester or spandex, to keep sweat away from your skin. A wide headband with a silicone strip on the inside is also a good choice, as it will help keep the headband in place.

Step by Step Guide

1. Start with clean, dry hair. If your hair is wet or sweaty, it will be harder to keep the headband in place.

2. Brush your hair to remove any tangles.

3. Put the headband on your head, positioning it about an inch back from your hairline.

4. Pull the headband down towards the nape of your neck.

5. Take a small section of hair from the front of your head and tuck it into the headband.

6. Repeat on the other side, taking a small section of hair and tucking it into the headband.

7. Continue tucking small sections of hair into the headband, working your way towards the back of your head.

8. Once you've tucked all the hair into the headband, adjust the position of the headband as needed.

Tips for Wearing a Sports Headband with Long Hair

1. Experiment with different headband styles and sizes to find what works best for you.

2. If your headband keeps slipping, try spraying some hairspray on the inside of the headband for extra grip.

3. If you have thick hair, you may need to use bobby pins to secure the headband in place.

4. If you're doing a high-impact workout, such as running, choose a headband with a wider band and a silicone strip for extra grip.

FAQ

Q: Can I wear a sports headband with any type of hairstyle?

A: While a sports headband can work with a variety of hairstyles, it's best suited for straight or wavy hair. If you have curly hair, you may find that the headband doesn't stay in place as well.

Q: How often should I wash my sports headband?

A: It's a good idea to wash your sports headband after every use to prevent sweat and bacteria buildup. Follow the care instructions on the label to ensure the headband lasts as long as possible.

Hair Care Tips for Active Women

1. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to prevent damage and breakage.

2. Avoid using hot tools such as flat irons and curling irons, as they can cause further damage to your hair.

3. Use a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.

4. Try to avoid tight hairstyles, such as high ponytails, as they can cause strain on your scalp and lead to hair loss.

By following these tips, you can wear a sports headband with long hair and still look and feel great during your workout!
