Is It Easier To Wax Long Or Short Hair

Written by Christine Jun 07, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Waxing is one of the most popular hair removal methods, but have you ever wondered if it's easier to wax long or short hair? In this blog post, we'll explore the pros and cons of waxing long and short hair, as well as provide tips for hair care and answer frequently asked questions.

Description

Waxing is a hair removal method that involves applying hot or cold wax to the skin, followed by a quick removal to pull the hair out from the root. This method is popular because it provides longer-lasting results compared to shaving or using hair removal creams. However, one question that often arises is whether it's easier to wax long or short hair.

How to

The answer to this question is that it's easier to wax short hair. This is because the wax can adhere better to shorter hair, making it easier to remove. When hair is too long, it can be more difficult to remove, and the wax may not be able to grip the hair properly. Additionally, longer hair can also cause more pain and discomfort during waxing.

If you do have long hair, it's recommended that you trim it to a shorter length before waxing. This will make the process easier and less painful. Use a pair of scissors or a trimmer to cut the hair to about a quarter of an inch in length.

Step by Step

If you're new to waxing, here's a step-by-step guide for how to wax short hair:

  1. Cleanse the area to be waxed with soap and water.
  2. Apply talcum powder or cornstarch to the area to absorb excess moisture and oils.
  3. Heat the wax according to the instructions on the packaging.
  4. Apply the wax to the area in the direction of hair growth.
  5. Place a wax strip over the wax and press down firmly.
  6. Hold the skin taut with one hand, and use the other hand to quickly pull off the wax strip in the opposite direction of hair growth.
  7. Repeat until all the desired hair has been removed.
  8. Cleanse the area with a gentle cleanser to remove any leftover wax residue.
  9. Apply a soothing lotion to calm the skin.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when waxing:

  • Exfoliate the area a few days before waxing to remove any dead skin cells and prevent ingrown hairs.
  • Avoid waxing if you have sunburn or any skin irritations.
  • Apply a numbing cream or take a pain reliever 30 minutes before waxing to minimize pain and discomfort.
  • Avoid hot showers, saunas, and swimming pools for 24 hours after waxing to prevent infections.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about waxing:

Q: How long does waxing last?

A: Waxing can last anywhere from 2-6 weeks, depending on your hair growth cycle.

Q: Is waxing painful?

A: Yes, waxing can be painful, but the pain usually subsides quickly.

Q: Can I wax at home?

A: Yes, you can wax at home using waxing kits that are available at drugstores or online. However, it's recommended that you get waxed by a professional if you're a first-timer.

Hair Care

After waxing, it's important to take care of your skin to prevent any infections or irritations. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Avoid tight clothing or anything that may rub against the area.
  • Avoid exposure to direct sunlight or tanning beds.
  • Apply aloe vera gel or a cooling lotion to soothe the skin.
  • Avoid touching the area with unwashed hands.

Conclusion

In conclusion, it's easier to wax short hair compared to long hair. However, if you do have long hair, make sure to trim it to a shorter length before waxing. Follow the tips and step-by-step guide we've provided to ensure a successful waxing experience. Remember to take care of your skin after waxing to prevent any infections or irritations.
