How to Get Cornrows for White Guys in 2023: A Step-by-Step Guide

If you're a white guy looking to get cornrows, you may be wondering where to start. The good news is that with a little bit of patience and the right tools, you can achieve this trendy look. In this guide, we'll walk you through everything you need to know about how to get cornrows for white guys.

How to Get Cornrows for White Guys

Cornrows are a popular hairstyle that involves braiding the hair into a series of small, tight braids that lay flat against the scalp. While traditionally associated with African American hair, cornrows have become popular with people of all backgrounds in recent years. Here's what you need to know to get cornrows:

Tools You'll Need

To get cornrows, you'll need a few basic tools:

  • Rat-tail comb
  • Hair clips
  • Hair ties
  • Leave-in conditioner
  • Gel or pomade

Choosing the Right Hair Length

The length of your hair will determine how long your cornrows will be. Generally, your hair should be at least 2-3 inches long to achieve a good cornrow look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Before you start braiding, make sure your hair is clean and moisturized. Use a leave-in conditioner to prevent your hair from drying out during the braiding process.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use a rat-tail comb to divide your hair into sections. The size of the sections will depend on the thickness of your hair and the size of the braids you want. Use hair clips to hold each section in place.

Step 3: Start Braiding

Starting at the front of your head, take a small section of hair and divide it into three equal parts. Cross the right strand over the middle strand, then cross the left strand over the middle strand. Add a little bit of hair to the right and left strands each time you cross them over the middle strand.

Step 4: Continue Braiding

Continue braiding until you reach the end of the section, then secure the braid with a hair tie. Repeat this process for each section of hair until your entire head is braided.

Step 5: Apply Gel or Pomade

To keep your cornrows looking neat and tidy, apply a small amount of gel or pomade to each braid. This will help to smooth down any flyaways and keep the braids in place.

Tips for Maintaining Cornrows

Once you have your cornrows, there are a few things you can do to keep them looking their best:

  • Wear a silk or satin scarf or sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage while you sleep.
  • Avoid pulling your cornrows too tight, as this can cause damage to your hair and scalp.
  • Apply a little bit of oil to your scalp and braids every few days to keep your hair moisturized.

FAQ

Can white guys get cornrows?

Yes, anyone can get cornrows regardless of their race or ethnicity.

Is it painful to get cornrows?

The braiding process can be uncomfortable, but it should not be painful. If you experience pain or discomfort, let your hairstylist know so they can adjust their technique.

How long do cornrows last?

Cornrows can last anywhere from 2-6 weeks depending on how well you take care of them.

Hair Care for Cornrows

To keep your hair and scalp healthy while you have cornrows, here are a few tips:

  • Wash your scalp and braids every 1-2 weeks with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can clog your pores and cause breakouts.
  • Take breaks between braiding styles to give your hair and scalp time to rest and recover.

With these tips and a little bit of patience, you can achieve the cornrow look you've been dreaming of. Good luck!
