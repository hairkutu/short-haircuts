How To Cut Bangs Shorter In The Middle A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy May 19, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you've decided that your bangs need a little trim, you may be wondering how to cut them shorter in the middle. This can be a bit tricky, but with the right techniques and tools, you can achieve the look you want. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to cut bangs shorter in the middle, along with some tips and tricks to make the process easier.

How to Cut Bangs Shorter in the Middle

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your bangs, you'll need to gather a few tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • A comb
  • A mirror
  • Clips or bobby pins to hold your hair in place

Step 2: Section Your Bangs

Using your comb, separate your bangs from the rest of your hair. Then, clip or pin back the sections of your hair that you're not going to cut. This will make it easier to see what you're doing and prevent you from accidentally snipping the wrong section of hair.

Step 3: Determine How Much to Cut

Decide how much you want to take off your bangs. If you're not sure, start with a small amount and work your way up. Remember that you can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once you've cut it!

Step 4: Cut the Middle Section

Starting at the middle of your bangs, use your scissors to make small, vertical cuts. Be careful not to cut too much at once. Instead, take small snips until you've achieved the desired length.

Step 5: Blend the Edges

After you've cut the middle section, use your scissors to blend the edges. This will make your bangs look more natural and prevent harsh lines from forming.

Step 6: Check Your Work

Use a mirror to check your work. Make sure your bangs are even and that you've achieved the desired length. If you need to make any adjustments, go back and do so carefully.

Tips for Cutting Bangs Shorter in the Middle

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the perfect cut:

  • Always use sharp scissors to prevent split ends and frayed edges.
  • Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up to prevent cutting too much at once.
  • Take breaks if your arms get tired or your hand starts to cramp.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you're not sure what you're doing.

FAQs About Cutting Bangs Shorter in the Middle

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting bangs shorter in the middle:

Can I cut my bangs shorter in the middle if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your bangs shorter in the middle if you have curly hair. However, keep in mind that curly hair can be more difficult to cut evenly. It's best to cut curly hair when it's dry, so you can see how it will lay.

How often should I trim my bangs?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how long you want your bangs to be. As a general rule, you should trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh.

What should I do if I accidentally cut too much?

If you accidentally cut too much, don't panic! You can always trim a little bit more to even things out. Alternatively, you can style your bangs in a way that hides the mistake until they grow out.

Hair Care After Cutting Bangs Shorter in the Middle

After you've cut your bangs, it's important to take care of them to keep them looking their best. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly to prevent oil buildup.
  • Use a heat protectant if you use hot tools on your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid touching your bangs too much, as this can cause them to become oily.

Now that you know how to cut bangs shorter in the middle, you can give yourself a fresh new look whenever you want! Just remember to take your time, use sharp scissors, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it.
