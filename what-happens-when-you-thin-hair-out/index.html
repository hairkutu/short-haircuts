What Happens When You Thin Hair Out A Complete Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 04, 2023 · 8 min read

Thinning hair out is a common practice among both men and women. It involves removing some of the hair strands to make the hair appear less dense. While thinning hair out can make your hair appear more manageable, there are some things you need to know before you take the plunge. In this article, we’ll explore everything you need to know about what happens when you thin hair out.

Table of Contents

  • How to Thin Hair Out
  • What Happens When You Thin Hair Out
  • Tips for Thinning Hair Out
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Thinning Hair

How to Thin Hair Out

Before we dive into what happens when you thin hair out, let’s first take a look at the process of thinning hair out. There are different methods you can use to thin out your hair, including:

  • Thinning shears: These are scissors specifically designed to remove some of the hair strands. They have teeth-like blades that cut some of the strands without removing them completely.
  • Razor: You can use a razor to thin out your hair. The razor cuts the hair at an angle, creating different lengths of strands.
  • Clipper: Clippers are another tool you can use to thin out your hair. They work by cutting the hair to a specific length, making it less dense.

Depending on the method you choose, you can thin out your hair evenly or leave some areas thicker than others. It’s important to note that thinning hair out too much can lead to hair damage and breakage. It’s best to have a professional stylist do it for you.

What Happens When You Thin Hair Out

Now that you know how to thin hair out, let’s take a look at what happens to your hair when you thin it out.

Hair appears less dense

The primary outcome of thinning hair out is that it appears less dense. This can be a good thing if you have thick hair that’s difficult to manage. Thinning hair out can make it easier to style and manage.

Hair becomes more prone to damage

Thinning hair out can make it more prone to damage. This is because there are fewer hair strands to protect the hair from external factors such as heat, chemicals, and environmental factors. As a result, thinning hair out too much can lead to hair breakage and damage.

Hair may grow back thicker

Contrary to popular belief, thinning hair out does not lead to hair loss. In fact, it can stimulate hair growth. Thinning hair out can stimulate the hair follicles, leading to the growth of new hair strands. As a result, you may notice that your hair grows back thicker and healthier than before.

Tips for Thinning Hair Out

Thinning hair out can be a good way to manage thick hair, but it’s important to do it right. Here are some tips for thinning hair out:

  • Don’t thin hair out too much: Thinning hair out too much can lead to hair damage and breakage. It’s best to have a professional stylist do it for you.
  • Choose the right method: There are different methods you can use to thin out your hair. Choose the one that’s best for your hair type and texture.
  • Use the right tools: Make sure you use the right tools, such as thinning shears or a razor, to thin out your hair.

FAQs

Does thinning hair out lead to hair loss?

No, thinning hair out does not lead to hair loss. In fact, it can stimulate hair growth.

Can thinning hair out cause damage?

Thinning hair out can make your hair more prone to damage and breakage. It’s important to do it right and not thin hair out too much.

Can I thin out my hair at home?

While it’s possible to thin out your hair at home, it’s best to have a professional stylist do it for you. They have the skills and experience to thin out your hair evenly and without causing damage.

Hair Care Tips for Thinning Hair

Thinning hair out can make your hair more prone to damage and breakage. Here are some hair care tips to keep your hair healthy:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner: Choose a gentle shampoo and conditioner that won’t strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid heat styling: Heat styling can damage your hair, especially when it’s already thin. Try to avoid it as much as possible.
  • Protect your hair from the sun: The sun’s UV rays can damage your hair. Wear a hat or use a hair product with UV protection.

Thinning hair out can be a good way to manage thick hair, but it’s important to do it right. Follow these tips to keep your hair healthy and looking its best.
