What Causes Hair To Start Thinning

Written by Aprilia Mar 31, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you experiencing hair thinning or hair loss? You’re not alone. Hair loss affects both men and women of all ages. Thinning hair can be caused by a variety of factors including genetics, hormonal changes, and certain medical conditions. In this article, we will discuss what causes hair to start thinning and provide tips for maintaining healthy hair.

Table of Contents

  • Genetics
  • Hormonal Changes
  • Medical Conditions
  • Diet
  • Stress
  • Hair Care
  • Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions

Genetics

One of the most common causes of hair thinning is genetics. If your parents or grandparents had thinning hair, you may be more likely to experience it as well. Male pattern baldness and female pattern baldness are both genetic conditions that often result in hair thinning or loss.

Hormonal Changes

Hormonal changes can also cause hair thinning. This is especially true for women who have recently given birth, gone through menopause, or are experiencing hormonal imbalances. Hormones like testosterone, estrogen, and thyroid hormones can all affect hair growth and cause thinning.

Medical Conditions

Certain medical conditions can also cause hair thinning. Conditions like alopecia areata, lupus, and thyroid disorders can all result in hair loss. Additionally, certain medications like chemotherapy drugs can also cause hair thinning or loss.

Diet

A poor diet can also contribute to hair thinning. If you’re not getting enough nutrients like iron, zinc, and biotin, your hair may become weaker and more prone to breakage.

Stress

Stress can also be a contributing factor to hair thinning. When you’re under a lot of stress, your body may divert resources away from hair growth to other areas of the body. This can result in weaker hair that’s more prone to breakage.

Hair Care

Finally, the way you care for your hair can also contribute to hair thinning. If you’re using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools on a regular basis, your hair may become damaged and more prone to breakage. Additionally, tight hairstyles like braids and ponytails can also cause hair thinning over time.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair

If you’re experiencing hair thinning, there are a few things you can do to help promote healthy hair growth:

  • Eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of vitamins and minerals
  • Avoid harsh chemicals and heat styling tools
  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair
  • Consider using a hair growth product like minoxidil

Frequently Asked Questions

Can stress cause hair thinning?

Yes, stress can be a contributing factor to hair thinning. When you’re under a lot of stress, your body may divert resources away from hair growth to other areas of the body. This can result in weaker hair that’s more prone to breakage.

Can a poor diet cause hair thinning?

Yes, a poor diet can contribute to hair thinning. If you’re not getting enough nutrients like iron, zinc, and biotin, your hair may become weaker and more prone to breakage.

Can using heat styling tools cause hair thinning?

Yes, using heat styling tools on a regular basis can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage. Try to limit your use of heat styling tools and use a heat protectant spray when you do use them.

Can tight hairstyles cause hair thinning?

Yes, tight hairstyles like braids and ponytails can cause hair thinning over time. If you’re experiencing hair thinning, try to avoid tight hairstyles and opt for looser styles that don’t pull on your hair.

What can I do if I’m experiencing hair thinning?

If you’re experiencing hair thinning, there are several things you can do to promote healthy hair growth. Eat a balanced diet, avoid harsh chemicals and heat styling tools, wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo, and consider using a hair growth product like minoxidil.
