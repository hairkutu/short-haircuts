Is It Possible To Keep Your Hair During Chemo

Written by Robby Apr 18, 2023 · 8 min read

Dealing with cancer treatment can be a challenging experience, and losing your hair can be one of the hardest parts of it. However, there are ways to keep your hair during chemotherapy. In this article, we’ll discuss the possibility of keeping your hair during chemo and provide you with some tips on how to do it.

Description

Chemotherapy is a common cancer treatment that involves the use of drugs to destroy cancer cells. Unfortunately, chemotherapy also affects healthy cells, including hair cells, which can result in hair loss. This can be a traumatic experience for many cancer patients, and some may avoid chemotherapy due to the fear of losing their hair.

How to Keep Your Hair During Chemo

Although hair loss during chemotherapy is common, it is not inevitable. You can take steps to minimize hair loss and even keep your hair during chemo. Here are some tips:

1. Use a Cold Cap

A cold cap is a device that helps to reduce hair loss during chemotherapy. The cap is placed on the head before and after chemotherapy treatment, and it helps to cool the scalp, which reduces the flow of blood to the hair follicles. This reduces the amount of chemotherapy drugs that reach the hair cells, which can help to prevent hair loss.

2. Opt for a Milder Chemo Regimen

Some chemotherapy drugs are more likely to cause hair loss than others. If possible, choose a milder chemotherapy regimen that is less likely to cause hair loss. Your doctor can help you choose the best chemotherapy regimen for your specific type of cancer.

3. Take Hair Supplements

Taking hair supplements can help to strengthen your hair and prevent hair loss during chemotherapy. Some supplements that may be helpful include biotin, vitamin D, and iron.

4. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage. During chemotherapy, it’s important to avoid heat styling and let your hair air dry instead. You can also use gentle styling products that are designed for fragile hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to keep your hair during chemo:

Step 1: Talk to Your Doctor

Before you start chemotherapy, talk to your doctor about your concerns regarding hair loss. Your doctor can provide you with information on the best ways to prevent hair loss and keep your hair during chemo.

Step 2: Use a Cold Cap

If you decide to use a cold cap, talk to your doctor about the best type of cold cap to use and how to properly use it.

Step 3: Take Hair Supplements

Talk to your doctor about which hair supplements are best for you, and how much to take.

Step 4: Avoid Heat Styling

During chemotherapy, avoid heat styling and use gentle styling products instead.

Step 5: Be Patient

It may take several weeks or months after chemotherapy for your hair to grow back. Be patient and take care of your hair during this time.

Tips for Hair Care During Chemo

Here are some tips for taking care of your hair during chemotherapy:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Use a gentle shampoo that is designed for fragile hair. Avoid harsh chemicals and ingredients that can damage your hair.

2. Be Gentle When Brushing

Be gentle when brushing your hair, and use a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush. This can help to prevent hair breakage.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

During chemotherapy, your scalp may be more sensitive to the sun. Wear a hat or scarf to protect your hair and scalp when you’re outside.

4. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a healthy diet can help to promote hair growth and prevent hair loss. Eat plenty of fruits, vegetables, and protein-rich foods.

FAQ

1. Does chemotherapy always cause hair loss?

No, not all chemotherapy drugs cause hair loss. The likelihood of hair loss depends on the specific drugs used and the dosage.

2. Are there any side effects to using a cold cap?

The most common side effect of using a cold cap is a headache. However, this can usually be managed with over-the-counter pain medication.

3. Will my hair grow back after chemotherapy?

Yes, your hair will usually grow back after chemotherapy. However, it may take several weeks or months for your hair to grow back to its normal length.

Conclusion

While hair loss during chemotherapy is common, it is not inevitable. By using a cold cap, opting for a milder chemo regimen, taking hair supplements, avoiding heat styling, and taking care of your hair, you can minimize hair loss and even keep your hair during chemo. Talk to your doctor for more information on the best ways to keep your hair during chemotherapy.
