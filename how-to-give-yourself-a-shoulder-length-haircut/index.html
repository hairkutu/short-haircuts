How To Give Yourself A Shoulder Length Haircut The Ultimate Guide

Written by Frank Apr 01, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're tired of your long locks and want to try something new, why not give yourself a shoulder length haircut? Not only is it a trendy and versatile look, but it's also easy to maintain. In this guide, we'll show you step-by-step how to give yourself a shoulder length haircut in the comfort of your own home. So, grab your shears and let's get started!

  • How to Give Yourself a Shoulder Length Haircut
  • Step-by-Step
  • Tips for a Successful Haircut
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Your Haircut

How to Give Yourself a Shoulder Length Haircut

Before we begin, it's important to have the right tools on hand. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair. Make sure to also have a mirror or two so you can see the back of your head. Now, let's get started!

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or product residue. Then, towel dry your hair and blow dry it straight using a round brush. This will make it easier to cut your hair evenly.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, section your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Clip each section out of the way so you can focus on one section at a time.

Step 3: Cut Your Hair

Starting with the back section, take a small piece of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut straight across, making sure to keep your fingers steady. Repeat this process with each section of hair, making sure to cut evenly.

Step 4: Blend Your Layers

Once you've cut all of your hair to the desired length, it's time to blend your layers. Take small sections of hair and hold them straight up. Use your scissors to cut into the ends at a slight angle, creating soft layers. Repeat this process throughout your hair until you achieve the desired level of layering.

Tips for a Successful Haircut

- Start with small snips and work your way up to the desired length. It's easier to cut more hair off than to try and fix a mistake.

- Use a sharp pair of scissors to ensure a clean cut.

- Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it will appear longer than it actually is.

- Take breaks to step back and assess your progress. This will help you avoid any uneven cuts.

FAQs

Q: Can I give myself a shoulder length haircut if I have curly hair?

A: Yes, you can! Just make sure to cut your hair when it's dry so you can see the natural curl pattern.

Q: How often should I trim my hair?

A: It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Q: What if I make a mistake?

A: Don't panic! You can always cut more hair off or visit a professional stylist to fix any mistakes.

Hair Care After Your Haircut

After your haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great. Use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated, and try to avoid using heat styling tools too often. You can also use a hair mask once a week to give your locks an extra boost of moisture.

There you have it! With these simple steps and tips, you can give yourself a shoulder length haircut that's both stylish and easy to maintain. Remember to take your time and be patient, and you'll have a fabulous new look in no time.
