How To French Braid Someone Else s Hair For Beginners

Written by Daniel Mar 31, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're someone who loves experimenting with hairstyles, then you must have heard the term 'French braid'. French braids are a classic and timeless hairstyle that looks good on everyone, regardless of their hair type or length. But, if you're a beginner, French braiding someone else's hair can be a daunting task. However, with some practice and patience, you can master the art of French braiding someone else's hair. In this blog post, we'll guide you through the process of French braiding someone else's hair for beginners.

How to French Braid Someone Else's Hair for Beginners

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you start French braiding someone else's hair, it's important to have all your supplies ready. You'll need a comb, hair clips, and hair ties.

Step 2: Brush the Hair

The next step is to brush the hair and remove any tangles. This will make it easier for you to French braid the hair.

Step 3: Divide the Hair

Divide the hair into three equal sections, and hold them in your non-dominant hand.

Step 4: Start Braiding

To start braiding, cross the right section over the middle section, then cross the left section over the middle section.

Step 5: Add Hair

Once you've completed one round of braiding, start adding hair to each section before crossing it over. Take a small section of hair from the right side of the head and add it to the right section of the braid. Cross the right section over the middle section, then take a small section of hair from the left side of the head and add it to the left section of the braid. Cross the left section over the middle section.

Step 6: Continue Braiding

Continue adding hair and braiding until you reach the nape of the neck.

Step 7: Finish the Braid

Once you've reached the nape of the neck, you can finish the braid by braiding the hair without adding any more sections.

Step 8: Secure the Braid

Use a hair tie to secure the end of the braid.

Tips

Here are some tips that can help you French braid someone else's hair:

  • Practice on a mannequin head before trying it on someone else's hair.
  • Use a detangling spray to make the hair easier to work with.
  • Divide the hair into equal sections to create a neat and even braid.
  • Take your time and be patient.

FAQ

Can French braids be done on short hair?

Yes, French braids can be done on short hair. However, you may need to use hair extensions to make the braid longer.

How long does it take to French braid someone else's hair?

The time it takes to French braid someone else's hair depends on the length and thickness of the hair, as well as your skill level. It can take anywhere from 10 minutes to an hour.

Hair Care

French braids can be a great hairstyle for any occasion, but it's important to take care of your hair to prevent damage. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can cause damage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, especially when it's wet.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair.

With these tips and steps, you can now confidently French braid someone else's hair. Remember, practice makes perfect, so don't be afraid to try it out on yourself or a friend. Happy braiding!
