What Underlying Medical Conditions Can Cause Your Eyebrows To Fall Out

Written by Luffy Jun 30, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you experiencing thinning or patchy eyebrows? You're not alone. Eyebrow loss can occur for many reasons, including medical conditions. In this article, we'll discuss the underlying medical conditions that can cause your eyebrows to fall out and what you can do to prevent it.

Table of Contents

  1. Hypothyroidism
  2. Alopecia Areata
  3. Atopic Dermatitis
  4. Trichotillomania
  5. Vitamin Deficiencies
  6. Frequently Asked Questions

Hypothyroidism

Hypothyroidism is a condition in which the thyroid gland doesn't produce enough thyroid hormone. This hormone controls the body's metabolism, and when it's low, it can cause a range of symptoms, including hair loss. Eyebrow loss is a common symptom of hypothyroidism, along with thinning hair on the scalp and other parts of the body.

If you suspect you have hypothyroidism, you should see your doctor for a blood test. Treatment may involve taking thyroid hormone replacement medication.

Alopecia Areata

Alopecia areata is an autoimmune disorder that causes hair loss on the scalp, face, and other parts of the body. It occurs when the immune system attacks hair follicles, causing hair to fall out. Eyebrow loss is a common symptom of alopecia areata, and it can be patchy or complete.

There is no cure for alopecia areata, but there are treatments to help manage the condition. Your doctor may recommend topical or oral medications, or in severe cases, immunosuppressive therapy.

Atopic Dermatitis

Atopic dermatitis, also known as eczema, is a chronic skin condition that causes dry, itchy, and inflamed skin. It can occur anywhere on the body, including the eyebrows. When atopic dermatitis affects the eyebrows, it can cause hair loss.

Treatment for atopic dermatitis may involve topical or oral medications, as well as lifestyle changes to avoid triggers that can cause flare-ups.

Trichotillomania

Trichotillomania is a disorder that causes people to pull out their hair, including eyebrow hair. It's often a coping mechanism for stress or anxiety, and it can lead to noticeable hair loss.

Treatment for trichotillomania may involve therapy or medication to address the underlying emotional issues that contribute to hair pulling.

Vitamin Deficiencies

Vitamin deficiencies can also cause eyebrow loss. Specifically, a lack of vitamins B and D can contribute to hair loss. Vitamin B is essential for hair growth, and vitamin D is important for overall hair health.

If you suspect you have a vitamin deficiency, talk to your doctor about taking supplements or making dietary changes to address the issue.

Frequently Asked Questions

How can I prevent eyebrow loss?

To prevent eyebrow loss, it's important to take care of your eyebrows. Avoid over-plucking or waxing, and be gentle when grooming your eyebrows. Additionally, make sure you're getting enough nutrients, including vitamins B and D, to support hair growth.

Can eyebrow loss be reversed?

Whether eyebrow loss can be reversed depends on the underlying cause. In some cases, such as vitamin deficiencies, addressing the issue can help promote hair growth. In other cases, such as alopecia areata, there is no cure, but treatments can help manage the condition and promote hair growth.

Is eyebrow loss a sign of cancer?

Eyebrow loss can be a symptom of certain types of cancer, such as lymphoma, but it's not a common symptom. If you're experiencing eyebrow loss, it's important to see your doctor to determine the underlying cause.

Can stress cause eyebrow loss?

Stress can contribute to hair loss, including eyebrow loss. If you're experiencing stress, it's important to take steps to manage it, such as practicing relaxation techniques or talking to a therapist.

Hair Care Tips

While there are many medical conditions that can cause eyebrow loss, taking care of your eyebrows can help promote hair growth and prevent further loss. Here are some hair care tips for healthy eyebrows:

  • Avoid over-plucking or waxing
  • Be gentle when grooming your eyebrows
  • Use a soft-bristled brush to comb your eyebrows
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your eyebrows
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and nutrients
  • Consider taking supplements to support hair growth

By taking care of your eyebrows and addressing any underlying medical conditions, you can promote hair growth and maintain healthy, full eyebrows.
