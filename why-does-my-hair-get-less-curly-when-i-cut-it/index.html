Why Does My Hair Get Less Curly When I Cut It

Written by Petter Mar 31, 2023 · 10 min read

If you have curly hair, you may have noticed that your curls tend to become less defined after you get a haircut. This can be frustrating, especially if you love your curls and want to maintain their shape. In this article, we'll explore why this happens and what you can do to keep your curls looking their best.

Why Does My Hair Get Less Curly When I Cut It?

When you have curly hair, the curls are created by the shape of the hair follicle. The more oval the follicle, the curlier the hair. When you cut your hair, you are essentially removing the weight that was pulling your curls down. This can cause your curls to bounce up and become less defined.

Additionally, if you get a haircut that is too short or if your stylist uses thinning shears, this can also cause your curls to become less defined. Thinning shears can create uneven layers and remove too much weight from your hair, which can cause your curls to fall flat.

How to Maintain Your Curls After a Haircut

The key to maintaining your curls after a haircut is to keep them hydrated and defined. This means using the right products and techniques to enhance your curls and prevent them from becoming frizzy or flat.

Use a Curl-Enhancing Shampoo and Conditioner

Start by using a shampoo and conditioner that are specifically designed for curly hair. These products will help to hydrate your curls and enhance their natural shape. Look for products that contain ingredients like shea butter, coconut oil, and argan oil, which are known for their moisturizing properties.

Avoid Over-Washing Your Hair

While it's important to keep your hair clean, over-washing can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and frizzy. Try washing your hair every two or three days instead of every day, and use a dry shampoo in between washes to keep your hair looking fresh.

Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

When you're styling your hair, use a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush. This will help to prevent breakage and keep your curls defined. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up, gently detangling as you go.

Step-by-Step Guide to Maintaining Your Curls

Here's a step-by-step guide to maintaining your curls after a haircut:

  1. Start by washing your hair with a curl-enhancing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Gently blot your hair with a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to remove excess water. Avoid rubbing your hair with a regular towel, as this can cause frizz.
  3. Apply a curl-enhancing cream or gel to your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.
  4. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.
  5. Flip your head upside down and use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your hair. This will help to enhance your curls and prevent them from becoming flat.
  6. Once your hair is dry, use your fingers to gently scrunch your curls to enhance their shape.
  7. Finish with a hairspray or oil to help hold your curls in place and add shine.

Tips for Keeping Your Curls Defined

Here are some additional tips for keeping your curls defined:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as these can damage your hair and cause your curls to become frizzy.
  • Try sleeping on a silk or satin pillowcase, as these materials are gentler on your hair and can help to prevent frizz.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause your curls to lose their shape.
  • If you have long hair, try using a hair clip or a silk scrunchie to pull your hair back while you sleep.

FAQs

Can I still cut my curly hair?

Yes, you can still cut your curly hair. Just make sure to communicate with your stylist about your desired length and the shape you want your curls to take. Avoid thinning shears and opt for a blunt cut or long layers instead.

What's the best way to dry curly hair?

The best way to dry curly hair is to use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer. This will help to enhance your curls and prevent them from becoming frizzy. You can also air dry your hair, but be sure to avoid touching it too much while it's drying.

What's the best type of brush to use on curly hair?

It's best to avoid using a brush on curly hair, as this can cause your curls to become frizzy and lose their shape. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for caring for curly hair:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your curls hydrated.
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol, as these can dry out your hair and cause it to become frizzy.
  • Invest in a silk or satin scarf or bonnet to wear while you sleep, as this can help to prevent breakage and maintain your curls' shape.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends, which can cause your curls to become frizzy and lose their shape.

By following these tips and techniques, you can maintain your curls and keep them looking their best after a haircut. Remember to be patient and gentle with your hair, and don't be afraid to experiment with different products and styles to find what works best for you.
