How to Start Dreadlocks with Long Hair

Starting dreadlocks with long hair can seem daunting, but it's actually quite simple. The first step is to decide on the type of dreadlocks you want. There are various types, including freeform, traditional, and interlocked. Once you've decided on the type of dreadlocks you want, you can start the process.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to start dreadlocks with long hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

It's important to start with clean hair. Wash your hair thoroughly with a residue-free shampoo.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using a comb. The size of the sections depends on the type of dreadlocks you want. For traditional dreadlocks, sections should be about an inch wide. For freeform dreadlocks, sections can be bigger.

Step 3: Backcomb Your Hair

Take one section of your hair and backcomb it using a small-toothed comb. Start at the roots and work your way down to the tips. Repeat this process for all sections of your hair.

Step 4: Twist Your Hair

Twist each section of your hair in the same direction. This will help the hair lock together.

Step 5: Secure Your Dreadlocks

Use a hairband or elastic to secure each dreadlock. You can also use a crochet hook to tighten and shape your dreadlocks.

Tips for Starting Dreadlocks with Long Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when starting dreadlocks with long hair:

  • Be patient. Dreadlocks take time to form, especially with long hair.
  • Don't use wax or other products that can cause buildup in your hair.
  • Keep your hair clean and moisturized.
  • Avoid using a hair dryer, as the heat can damage your hair.
  • Consider getting your hair professionally done if you're unsure about starting dreadlocks on your own.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about starting dreadlocks with long hair:

How long does it take for dreadlocks to form?

It can take anywhere from six months to a year for dreadlocks to fully form.

Can I wash my hair with dreadlocks?

Yes, you can wash your hair with dreadlocks. It's important to use a residue-free shampoo and to be gentle when washing your hair.

Can I start dreadlocks with straight hair?

Yes, you can start dreadlocks with straight hair. Backcombing and twisting your hair will help it lock together.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks with Long Hair

Here are some tips for caring for your dreadlocks with long hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can cause buildup in your hair.
  • Use a crochet hook to tighten and shape your dreadlocks.
  • Be gentle when handling your hair to avoid breakage.
  • Consider getting your hair professionally maintained if you're unsure about caring for your dreadlocks on your own.

Starting dreadlocks with long hair is a fun and unique way to express yourself. With these tips and a little patience, you can have beautiful dreadlocks in no time. Happy locking!
