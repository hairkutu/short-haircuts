Can Orthodontics Change Face Shape

Written by William Apr 15, 2023 · 6 min read

Orthodontics is a branch of dentistry that deals with the diagnosis, prevention, and treatment of dental and facial irregularities. It involves the use of braces, aligners, and other dental appliances to correct misaligned teeth and jaws. But can orthodontics change face shape?

Description

Orthodontic treatment can change the shape of your face by correcting the alignment of your teeth and jaws. When your teeth and jaws are properly aligned, they can improve the overall appearance of your face. Orthodontic treatment can also improve your bite, which can contribute to better facial symmetry.

How To

Orthodontic treatment involves several steps. The first step is a consultation with an orthodontist, who will evaluate your teeth and jaws and recommend the best treatment option for your needs. The next step is the placement of braces, aligners, or other dental appliances. These appliances will gradually apply pressure to your teeth and jaws, which will move them into the correct position over time. You will need to visit your orthodontist regularly to have your appliances adjusted and to monitor your progress.

Step by Step

The steps involved in orthodontic treatment are as follows:

  1. Consultation with an orthodontist
  2. Placement of braces, aligners, or other dental appliances
  3. Regular adjustments and monitoring of progress
  4. Removal of appliances
  5. Retention phase

The retention phase involves the use of retainers to maintain the new position of your teeth and jaws. This phase is crucial to prevent relapse and to ensure that your new smile lasts a lifetime.

Tips

If you are considering orthodontic treatment, here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Choose an experienced and qualified orthodontist
  • Follow your orthodontist's instructions carefully
  • Maintain good oral hygiene throughout treatment
  • Avoid hard and sticky foods that can damage appliances
  • Wear your retainers as directed

FAQ

Q: Can orthodontics change the shape of my face?

A: Yes, orthodontic treatment can change the shape of your face by correcting the alignment of your teeth and jaws.

Q: How long does orthodontic treatment take?

A: The length of orthodontic treatment varies depending on the severity of your dental and facial irregularities. Most treatments take between 12 and 36 months.

Q: Will I need to wear braces?

A: It depends on your specific needs. Your orthodontist may recommend braces, aligners, or other dental appliances to correct your dental and facial irregularities.

Q: Is orthodontic treatment painful?

A: Orthodontic treatment may cause some discomfort, especially during the first few days after appliances are placed or adjusted. However, the discomfort is usually mild and can be managed with over-the-counter pain relievers.

Hair Care

During orthodontic treatment, it is important to take good care of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid using hair ties that can get tangled in your appliances
  • Use a hair dryer on low heat to avoid melting or warping your appliances
  • Keep your hair away from your face to avoid getting it caught in your appliances
  • Wash your hair regularly to prevent buildup of bacteria and food particles
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to avoid damaging your appliances

In conclusion, orthodontic treatment can change the shape of your face by correcting the alignment of your teeth and jaws. If you are considering orthodontic treatment, consult with an experienced and qualified orthodontist to determine the best treatment option for your needs.
