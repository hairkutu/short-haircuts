How To Cut Shaggy Bangs Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jul 19, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle without spending a ton of money at the salon, cutting your own shaggy bangs can be a great option. Not only is it cost-effective, but it's also a fun way to experiment with your hair. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to cutting shaggy bangs yourself, as well as provide tips and hair care advice to help you achieve the perfect look.

How to Cut Shaggy Bangs Yourself

Cutting shaggy bangs is a relatively simple process, but it does require some patience and attention to detail. Here are the basic steps you'll need to follow:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. You don't want to cut your bangs when they're wet, as they'll appear longer once they dry.
  2. Section off the hair you want to cut. Use a comb to create a triangle-shaped section at the front of your head, starting at the center of your forehead and extending towards your temples.
  3. Decide on the length of your bangs. Use your fingers to pinch the hair where you want to cut it, and use hair cutting scissors to trim the ends. Remember, you can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.
  4. Use point cutting technique to add texture to your bangs. This involves holding the scissors vertically and snipping into the hair at an angle, creating a jagged effect. You can also use thinning shears to remove excess bulk.
  5. Repeat the process on the other side of your head, making sure to keep the length and texture consistent.
  6. Blend your bangs into the rest of your hair. Use a comb to section off the rest of your hair, and use scissors to feather the ends of your bangs so they blend seamlessly into the rest of your hair.
  7. Double check your work. Make sure both sides are even and that you're happy with the length and texture of your bangs.

Tips for Cutting Shaggy Bangs

Here are a few tips to keep in mind while cutting your shaggy bangs:

  • Start with longer bangs than you want, as you can always cut more. It's better to err on the side of caution.
  • Use hair cutting scissors, not regular scissors. Regular scissors can damage the hair and create split ends.
  • Be patient and take your time. Rushing can lead to uneven or choppy bangs.
  • Don't cut straight across. This can create a harsh, blunt look. Instead, opt for a slightly curved or jagged edge.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress from all angles.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting shaggy bangs:

Can you cut bangs with regular scissors?

We don't recommend it. Regular scissors can damage the hair and create split ends, which can lead to frizz and breakage.

How often do shaggy bangs need to be trimmed?

This depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. On average, bangs should be trimmed every 4-6 weeks to maintain their length and shape.

Can I cut my bangs if I have curly hair?

Yes, but keep in mind that curly hair can shrink up when it dries, so it's best to cut your bangs a little longer than you want them to be.

Hair Care Tips for Shaggy Bangs

Once you've cut your shaggy bangs, it's important to take care of them properly to keep them looking their best. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to prevent your bangs from getting too oily.
  • Blow dry your bangs in the direction you want them to lay, using a round brush to create volume and texture.
  • Avoid touching your bangs too much, as this can transfer oils from your fingers onto your hair, making it greasy.
  • Use a dry shampoo in between washes to keep your bangs looking fresh.
  • Consider using a hairspray or hair wax to help hold your bangs in place.

With these tips and techniques, you should be able to cut your own shaggy bangs with confidence. Remember to take your time, be patient, and don't be afraid to experiment!
