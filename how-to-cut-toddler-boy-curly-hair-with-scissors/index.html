How To Cut Toddler Boy Curly Hair With Scissors

Written by Christine Aug 23, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you a parent struggling to cut your toddler boy’s curly hair with scissors? You’re not alone. Cutting curly hair requires a different approach than straight hair. In this article, we’ll walk you through the steps of how to cut toddler boy curly hair with scissors. We’ll also address some common questions and provide tips for hair care.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Toddler Boy Curly Hair with Scissors
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Toddler Boy Curly Hair with Scissors
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Toddler Boy Curly Hair

How to Cut Toddler Boy Curly Hair with Scissors

The first step to cutting your toddler boy’s curly hair with scissors is to gather the necessary tools. You’ll need a pair of hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips. It’s important to use hair cutting scissors, as regular scissors can cause split ends and uneven cuts.

Next, choose a quiet and well-lit area to cut your toddler’s hair. You may also want to have distractions like toys or a tablet to keep your toddler occupied during the haircut.

When cutting curly hair, it’s important to cut the hair dry. This allows you to see the natural curl pattern and avoid cutting too much off. Start small and gradually cut more if needed.

Step by Step Guide

Now that you have your tools and a designated area, let’s walk through the steps of how to cut toddler boy curly hair with scissors:

  1. Start by combing your toddler’s hair in the direction of the curl pattern. This will help you see any tangles or knots that need to be removed before cutting.
  2. Section off the hair you want to cut with hair clips. You can start with the back and work your way to the front.
  3. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use the comb to comb through the hair, starting from the bottom and working your way up.
  4. Trim the hair with scissors, following the natural curl pattern. Start by cutting small amounts and gradually cut more if necessary. Be sure to cut straight across and avoid thinning the hair.
  5. Repeat this process for each section of hair, working from the back to the front.
  6. Once you’ve finished cutting, comb through the hair to make sure it’s even. You may need to make some additional cuts to even out any uneven spots.

Tips for Cutting Toddler Boy Curly Hair with Scissors

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your toddler boy’s curly hair with scissors:

  • Start small and gradually cut more if necessary.
  • Use hair cutting scissors, not regular scissors.
  • Cut the hair dry, not wet.
  • Follow the natural curl pattern.
  • Cut straight across, avoiding thinning the hair.

FAQs

Here are some common questions about cutting toddler boy curly hair with scissors:

Can I cut curly hair with clippers?

While you can cut curly hair with clippers, it’s not recommended. Clippers can create an uneven cut and cause the hair to look too short. It’s best to use scissors and follow the natural curl pattern.

How often should I cut my toddler boy’s curly hair?

It’s recommended to cut your toddler boy’s curly hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

How can I prevent my toddler’s curly hair from tangling?

To prevent tangling, use a leave-in conditioner or detangler after washing your toddler’s curly hair. You can also comb through the hair gently with a wide-tooth comb before cutting.

Hair Care for Toddler Boy Curly Hair

Now that you know how to cut your toddler boy’s curly hair with scissors, it’s important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your toddler’s hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner designed for curly hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle the hair while it’s wet.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like blow dryers or straighteners.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or detangler to keep the hair soft and manageable.

By following these tips and the steps outlined in this article, you can successfully cut your toddler boy’s curly hair with scissors. Remember to start small, follow the natural curl pattern, and use the right tools for the job.
